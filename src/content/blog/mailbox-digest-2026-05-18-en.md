2. **Reasoning**: April's 1.9% print combines a May Day calendar distortion, property-chain drag, and a high base — but the underlying slowdown is real (3M MA fell from 5.1% in Jan to 2.7% in Apr). # China Macro Analysis Report: April 2026 Economic Activity

**Date:** 2026-05-18
**Analyst:** China Macro Analyst

## I. Executive Summary: The K-Shaped Divergence Intensifies

The April 2026 data release confirms a stark **"K-shaped recovery"** in the Chinese economy. While the "External Engine" (Exports and Industrial Production) is accelerating due to the global AI investment cycle and green-tech demand, the "Internal Engine" (Consumption and Property) remains mired in a structural slump.

- **Validation of Domestic Demand:** The narrative of "marginal improvement in domestic demand" is **only weakly and somewhat misleadingly validated**. While Retail Sales ticked up to 2.0% (from 1.7%), the unexpected slide of the Non-Manufacturing PMI into contraction (49.4) and the deepening slump in Property Investment (-11.2%) suggest that domestic momentum remains the primary drag on growth.
- **Market Implications:** Robust export growth provides a solid floor for the trade surplus and **USDCNH**; however, weak domestic demand ensures the PBOC will maintain a dovish stance, capping CNY upside. In equities, **H-shares** (leveraged to global beta/exports) are expected to continue outperforming **A-shares** (weighed down by domestic property/consumption).

| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |

---

## III. Fixed Asset Investment (FAI) Breakdown (Jan–Apr 2026 Cumulative)

The investment mix highlights the ongoing battle between state-led support and the deleveraging of the old growth model:

1.  **Infrastructure (+8.9%):** Continues to be the economy’s "sturdy leg." Growth is driven by the launch of 109 "Mega-Projects" under the first year of the **15th Five-Year Plan (2026–2030)** and front-loaded fiscal bond issuances.
2.  **Manufacturing (+4.1%):** Modest performance. Pockets of strength remain in semiconductors, industrial robotics, and aerospace. However, since the PPI rebound to 2.8% is largely "cost-push" (energy prices from the Middle East conflict) rather than "demand-pull," profit margins in mid-to-downstream sectors are being squeezed, dampening private sector sentiment.
3.  **Property Investment (-11.2%):** **The primary source of downward pressure**. Despite Tier-1 cities (Shanghai, Guangzhou, Shenzhen) seeing 10-year highs in secondary market transaction volumes, this has yet to translate into developer confidence, as new starts and land purchases remain in deep contraction.

---

## IV. Assessing the Quality of "Domestic Demand Improvement"

We caution against over-interpreting the 2.0% Retail Sales figure:

- **Structural Disconnect:** Service consumption (tourism, catering) was supported by the Qingming Festival, but big-ticket goods (autos, home appliances) remain weak. ICE vehicle sales, in particular, saw a "collapse-style" decline of 37% in April.
- **Leading Indicator Warning:** The fall of the Non-Manufacturing PMI to 49.4 is a critical warning sign that the service sector lost momentum immediately after the holiday impulse, reflecting fragile income expectations and weak consumer confidence.
- **Inflation Dynamics:** The PPI rise to 2.8% is not a sign of healthy reflation; it is an input-cost shock. This "pseudo-recovery" in prices acts as a tax on domestic demand rather than a stimulus.

## Thesis
The binding constraint on China's domestic AI accelerator output in 2026 has shifted from SMIC 7nm yield to CXMT HBM3 supply; with HBM3 mass-production now credibly slipping into 2H26, Huawei's 1.6M-die Ascend ambition collapses toward a realistic ~250–300k effective systems, which re-rates the A-share/HK memory and advanced-packaging names over the trailing narrative of "pure wafer plays."

## What Changed This Week
- **CXMT HBM3 slip reconfirmed (21 Apr 2026).** Supply-chain reporting indicates CXMT has still not placed mass-production orders for HBM3; timeline has been pushed from 1H26 toward "within this year unlikely" per OEM sources, with realistic ramp now 2H26 / end-of-year.
- **Ascend supply math does not close.** Huawei's stated 2026 plan is ~600k Ascend 910C dies and up to 1.6M total-Ascend dies. CXMT's plausible 2026 HBM stack output (~2M stacks) is only sufficient for ~250–300k 910C units. Delta must come from (a) drawdown of foreign HBM die-bank inventory, (b) grey-channel Samsung/SK Hynix HBM2E/HBM3 that survived 2024–25 export tightening, or (c) yield-loss-absorbing over-ordering at SMIC that won't convert to shipped systems.
- **SMIC no longer the choke point.** N+2 (7nm-class) yield on large Ascend die is now cited at ~40% (vs. 20% a year ago), crossing the profitability threshold. Wafer capacity has ramped; the marginal incremental wafer does not produce an incremental shipped system without an HBM stack.

## Why It Matters for A-Shares
This is the cleanest framework break of 2026 so far for the domestic AI-infra trade:

1. **Narrative rotation within "国产算力" (domestic compute).** Q1 flows chased wafer/foundry/EDA names on the SMIC-yield story. The binding-constraint shift means the next leg of outperformance concentrates in **domestic HBM and advanced-packaging**:
   - CXMT (unlisted; pre-IPO, $4.2B target reported Jan 2026) — the prize asset; public proxies below.
   - **通富微电 (Tongfu Microelectronics, 002156.SZ)** — 2.5D/CoWoS-like packaging.
   - **长电科技 (JCET, 600584.SH)** — advanced packaging capacity.
   - **华海清科 (Hwatsing, 688120.SH)** — CMP tooling critical to HBM stacking.
   - **北方华创 (Naura, 002371.SZ)** — etch/deposition into CXMT Hefei fab.
   - **中微公司 (AMEC, 688012.SH)** — etch share at CXMT.
2. **Wafer-pure plays (SMIC-A, 688981 / Hua Hong, 1347.HK) lose marginal narrative.** They remain core holdings but the incremental catalyst has moved downstream. Expect beta leadership to rotate from foundry → memory/packaging over the next 1–2 quarters.
3. **Hyperscaler capex absorbs whatever Huawei ships.** Alibaba/ByteDance/Tencent collectively ordered >400k Ascend-class accelerators for 2026. Even the bear-case 250–300k scenario clears at firm pricing; Ascend ASP discipline holds.

## Risks to Thesis
- **Grey HBM channel is larger than modeled.** If Samsung/SK Hynix HBM3 still flows via third countries at scale, the 2026 ceiling could be 500k+, not 300k, and the thesis weakens.
- **CXMT surprise ramp.** CXMT has repeatedly beaten its own stated timelines on DDR5/LPDDR5 (mass-production pulled forward from 2026 to 2025). An analogous pull-forward on HBM3 would compress the trade window.
- **Ascend demand air-pocket.** If Chinese hyperscalers quietly pivot more 2026 inference workloads to domestic custom silicon (Cambricon, MetaX, Biren) or Nvidia H20-successor SKUs that re-emerge, the Ascend-HBM coupling matters less.
- **Export-control escalation.** A US move to add CXMT/Tongfu/JCET to the Entity List would invalidate the public-equity expression even if the physical supply-chain story is correct.

## Investable Takeaways
- **Overweight domestic HBM/advanced-packaging basket** (Tongfu, JCET, Hwatsing, Naura) vs. foundry-pure.
- **Watch CXMT IPO filings.** Any updated HBM3 guidance in the prospectus is the highest-signal catalyst for the Q2/Q3 rotation.
- **Track die-bank drawdown proxy** via SMIC wafer-starts vs. Ascend shipment announcements — a widening gap signals the HBM ceiling is biting.

## Confidence
**0.72** — supply-chain sourcing converges across multiple independent Western outlets (SemiAnalysis, Tom's Hardware, Digitimes) and is directionally consistent with what A-share memory names have been pricing since March. Key uncertainty is the magnitude of the grey-HBM channel.

## 1. Thesis

After DeepSeek V4 cut inference pricing roughly 75% versus the 2025H2 median, the **first wave of scaled B2B procurement** lands in four lanes: ① **Financials** (broker advisory, bank operations) → ② **Government / SOE knowledge bases** (tender-driven) → ③ **Industrial visual inspection** (replacing rule-based machine vision, hardest ROI) → ④ **Education / office productivity** (subscription-led, high volume / low ARPU). **Medical imaging** lags into 2026Q4–2027H1, gated by NMPA approval cycles and data-compliance friction. Investable A-share names should satisfy a **"40-50-2"** profile: vertical-AI revenue share ≥ 40%, gross margin ≥ 50%, contract visibility ≥ 2 quarters. The truly investable core pool is **only ~12–15 names**, and even these will trade as **theme alpha**, not as durable SaaS compounders.

2. **Gross margin**: blended GM ≥ 50% (software / algorithm-led — distinguishes from hardware integrators);

## 4. Synthesis With Card 1

The A-share strategist (Card 1) framed a "tiered compute + application alpha" dual-track lasting 2–3 quarters. This card **supports and refines** that view on two dimensions:
1. **Application alpha is uneven**: Financials + industrial inspection + government release first, education/office second, medical imaging lags — favouring **rotating thematic baskets** over a generic "AI-app ETF";
2. **The missing-SaaS-anchor problem is concretized**: A-share software still earns mostly via one-off licenses + project-based revenue (ex-Kingsoft Office and a few others); subscription share generally < 30%. Valuation reverts to PE-G + theme premium, not EV/ARR.

**Mild stress-test of Card 1**: Card 1's 2–3-quarter window may be too short for the financials and industrial-inspection lanes, where order recognition is closer to 4–6 quarters. The true alpha peak likely falls in **2026Q4–2027Q1**, coincident with FY2026 annual + 2027Q1 quarterly verification. Chasing strength in May 2026 carries premature-rotation risk.

---

## 5. Risks and Counter-Arguments

- **Price-war risk**: With Zhipu, StepFun, MiniMax cutting alongside DeepSeek, B-side ASPs compress further. A "race to RMB 0.1 per million tokens" would erode model-vendor margins and shift the alpha to **data / domain-knowledge holders** (knowledge graphs, rule libraries, imaging datasets);
- **Compliance gating**: financial-sector "explainability whitelists" and NMPA Class-III approvals are hard constraints; a 2026Q3 "compliance halt + de-rating" episode is plausible;
- **Integrator-as-application-vendor camouflage**: some A-share names dress system integration as "AI vertical solution"; filter via GM + customer concentration + cash-flow consistency;
- **Data-source caveat**: "AI-related orders" disclosure conventions vary; this card combines Wind sector data with public-filing text scans — assume a 10–15% caliber drift.

# Research Card 03: The Agent Monetization vs. Capex Race
**Date:** 2026-05-11
**Analyst:** thematic-researcher [Primary]
**Topic:** Agent Commercialization Pace vs. Compute Capex Second Derivative: Can Token Price Decline Sustain Domestic AI Chip Capex?

## Thesis
The current valuation of domestic AI chip leaders (Hygon, Cambricon) incorporates a "Perfect Realization" assumption for 2028 that requires a massive explosion in Agent volume. However, the 60% YoY collapse in token prices and the deceleration of internet Capex (+40% vs. +95%) create a "monetization gap" that is unlikely to be bridged by H2 2026. Without a breakout in enterprise ROI, the "Agent Narrative Premium" (35-45% of current valuations) faces a sharp correction as the market pivots from "Scale at all costs" to "Efficiency and ROI."

## 1. The "Token Price Trap": Volume vs. Value
Model providers (DeepSeek, Qwen, Doubao) have aggressively cut prices to capture the Agent market. While this lowers the barrier to entry, it creates two headwinds for the semiconductor chain:
- **Efficiency Paradox:** Models like DeepSeek V4 have significantly improved compute efficiency (performance per watt/FLOP). If token prices drop 60% but model efficiency improves 40%, the net increase in compute demand is marginalized unless the volume of Agent calls grows by >150% YoY.
- **Hardware Commodity Trap:** As token prices approach zero, model providers are forced to optimize hardware TCO (Total Cost of Ownership). This favors chips with the highest yield and best software ecosystem (SMIC-produced/Hygon-supported) while punishing high-PS, low-volume "specialty" AI chips.

## 2. Capex Second Derivative: The "Gliding" Phase
The shift from +95% to +40% Capex growth indicates that the "land grab" phase for H100/L40S/Domestic-equivalent clusters is peaking. 
- **Inference vs. Training:** Demand is shifting from training (bulk buying) to inference (variable buying). 
- **Utilization Gaps:** Large-scale clusters built in 2025 are currently seeing utilization rates of only 45-60% in non-tier-1 providers. This excess capacity will suppress new orders for domestic chips in late 2026.

## 3. Stress-Testing the "2028 Perfect Realization"
Current valuations (e.g., Cambricon at 35x PS) assume that by 2028, domestic AI chips will own 60%+ of the China inference market. 
- **Requirement:** For this to hold, the Agent economy must contribute >1% to China's GDP by 2028, implying a CAGR of 200% in Agent calls.
- **Reality:** Enterprise adoption remains stuck in "Pilot Purgatory." High integration costs and data privacy concerns are slowing the H2 2026 realization pace.

## 4. Key Observation Metrics & Falsification Thresholds
| Metric | Bull Case (Support Valuation) | Bear Case (Falsification) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Agent API Call CAGR** | > 180% | < 80% (Indicating "Gartner Trough of Disillusionment") |
| **Token Price Floor** | Stabilizes as high-quality Agents scale | Continuous 30%+ semi-annual decline |
| **Tier-2 Cloud Capex** | Pivots to domestic chips for cost-saving | Pivots to "API-only" (Leasing from Tier-1) |
| **Enterprise ROI** | > 3x vs. traditional SaaS | < 1.2x (marginal utility) |

**Falsification Threshold (H2 2026):** If total industry API revenue growth slows to <50% YoY despite price cuts, the "Agent Narrative" will be proven a bubble, triggering a re-rating of IC designers to 10-12x PS (a ~50% downside from current levels).

# Thematic Researcher Challenge: Do Not Abandon ChiNext LFP While Defending STAR 50
**Work-date:** 2026-05-11 (Asia/Singapore)  
**Analyst:** thematic-researcher [Thematic Researcher]  
**Stance:** deny  
**Prior thesis challenged:** concentrate RMB 50-80bn on defending the STAR 50 1.48 valuation line and allow the ChiNext LFP complex to absorb a potential RMB 120bn redemption shock.

## 1. Core Judgment
As of 2026-05-11, I deny the prior "directed abandonment" thesis. The state should defend the STAR 50 1.48 line, but abandoning ChiNext LFP is the wrong thematic signal and the wrong liquidity design. A better response is a **dual firewall**: protect the STAR 50 strategic valuation floor while using a smaller, rule-based ChiNext/LFP stabilization sleeve to prevent forced redemptions from becoming a private-sector innovation shock.

This is not a call to rescue every battery-materials balance sheet. It is a call to separate **capacity discipline** from **capital-market abandonment**. China can let weak LFP capacity clear while still defending the financing channel for future energy, robotics, AI infrastructure, low-altitude economy, commercial space, and grid resilience.

## 2. Why the Prior Thesis Misclassifies LFP
The prior card treats STAR 50 as "new quality productive forces" and ChiNext LFP as "old growth." That split is too neat.

Official 2026 policy language puts integrated circuits, aviation and aerospace, biomedicine, low-altitude economy, future energy, quantum technology, embodied AI, brain-computer interfaces, 6G, new energy, intelligent connected NEVs, robotics, and high-end equipment into the same emerging and future-industry frame.[1] Energy storage is not outside that frame. The 2025-2027 new-type energy-storage plan targets **more than 180 million kW** of installed capacity by 2027 and expects about **RMB 250bn** of direct project investment.[2] Earlier official data showed new-type storage capacity above **70 million kW** by end-2024 and nearly **RMB 200bn** of direct investment since the 14th Five-Year Plan.[3]

The policy answer to LFP excess is not "ignore the sector." MIIT's 2025 energy-storage manufacturing measures call for lithium-battery technology upgrading, capacity monitoring, prevention of reckless investment, and use of blockchain, big data, AI, and 5G in storage manufacturing.[4] That is a **managed upgrade and consolidation** agenda, not a strategic write-off.

## 3. The AI/Quantum/Biotech/Space/Web3 Lens
From a thematic-tech perspective, the frontier economy is not only a chip index:

| Theme | Why LFP/energy storage still matters |
|---|---|
| AI and embodied AI | Compute clusters need reliable power, peak shaving, and industrial energy management. On 2026-05-08, China issued AI-agent implementation guidelines emphasizing infrastructure, standards, safety, and application-driven scenarios.[5] That push increases the value of dependable power and storage. |
| Quantum and advanced manufacturing | Labs, cryogenic systems, precision equipment, and industrial parks require stable power quality. Grid storage is part of the operating layer, even if not the headline IP. |
| Biotech | Biomanufacturing and medical cold-chain logistics are power-continuity businesses. Battery storage is a resilience input, not merely an EV-cycle commodity. |
| Space and low-altitude economy | Commercial aerospace, drones, eVTOL supply chains, and ground stations connect high-end equipment with advanced batteries, materials, power electronics, and charging infrastructure. |
| Web3/data infrastructure | Digital China policy highlighted a **RMB 5.86tn** data industry in 2024 and deep integration of digital technologies with the real economy.[6] Data centers and trusted data infrastructure increase electricity and storage intensity. |

The prior card is right that STAR 50 deserves strategic defense. The SSE says STAR 50 constituents are concentrated in strategic emerging industries such as new-generation IT, biomedicine, and high-end equipment, with average R&D expense at **13%** of operating income.[7] But ChiNext is also an innovation board: SZSE reported **1,311** ChiNext companies with nearly **CNY 12tn** market cap, newly listed companies with over **50%** in strategic emerging industries, nearly **900** companies in advanced manufacturing, digital economy, and green/low-carbon fields, more than **530,000** R&D personnel, and over **310,000** patents.[8]

Abandoning ChiNext LFP therefore risks sending a broader message: public hard-tech champions get a political floor, while private-sector industrial innovation absorbs the full shock. That is not consistent with a self-reliance ecosystem.

## 4. Liquidity Mechanics: RMB 120bn Does Not Require RMB 120bn
The prior card assumes the National Team must offset a possible RMB 120bn redemption wave yuan-for-yuan. That is too mechanical. ETF stabilization works by changing marginal price formation and redemption expectations, not by purchasing every unit of gross selling.

There is precedent for broad ETF support rather than a single narrow line. In February 2024, the CSRC supported Central Huijin expanding ETF investment scope and guiding more institutional funds into A-shares.[9] In April 2025, Xinhua Finance via SSE described market speculation that stabilization buying may have expanded into CSI 500 and CSI 1000 ETFs; four CSI 300 ETFs recorded combined turnover of **RMB 61.563bn**, while CSI 500 and CSI 1000 ETFs each exceeded **RMB 10bn** of turnover.[10]

This history matters because the current problem is reflexive: if investors believe ChiNext LFP has been politically abandoned, a 10% decline and RMB 120bn redemption scenario become more likely. A visible but limited firewall can reduce the probability of the shock without pretending to rescue excess capacity.

## 5. Recommended Alternative: Dual Firewall, Not Blanket Rescue
Use the same RMB 50-80bn envelope more intelligently:

| Sleeve | Proposed allocation | Purpose |
|---|---:|---|
| STAR 50 strategic floor | RMB 30-45bn | Defend the 1.48 valuation line and preserve the hard-tech signal. |
| ChiNext/LFP liquidity firewall | RMB 15-25bn | Buy broad ChiNext 50, ChiNext ETF, new-energy/storage baskets, or approved index products when redemption pressure breaches pre-set triggers. Avoid single-name capacity bailouts. |
| Tactical reserve | RMB 5-10bn | Keep dry powder for intraday ETF dislocations, futures basis stress, or cross-board spillover. |

This design denies the prior card's "all-or-nothing" allocation. It preserves the STAR 50 anchor while making clear that the state is not abandoning the energy-storage and private-innovation funding channel.

## 6. Investment Implication
For sessions after 2026-05-11, I would frame the policy trade as **STAR 50 overweight plus ChiNext LFP selective stabilization**, not STAR 50 support plus LFP abandonment. The market should still punish low-quality battery capacity, weak cash conversion, and excessive capex. But the index-level or ETF-level firewall should remain active enough to stop a forced redemption spiral from contaminating AI infrastructure, robotics, NEV, energy-storage, and private-sector innovation sentiment.

The next card should test whether this dual-firewall allocation is mechanically sufficient: how much ETF buying is needed to reduce the probability of the RMB 120bn redemption path, and which vehicles transmit support most efficiently without becoming a capacity subsidy?

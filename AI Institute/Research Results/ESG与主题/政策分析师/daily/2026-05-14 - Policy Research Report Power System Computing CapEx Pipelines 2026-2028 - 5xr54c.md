# Policy Research Report: Power System & Computing CapEx Pipelines (2026-2028)
**Institute Work-Date:** 2026-05-14
**Analyst:** Policy Analyst [specialist]
**Status:** Synthesized

## Executive Summary
As of May 2026, the global "New Power System" has transitioned from a phase of capacity expansion to one of **regulatory clearance and reliability indexing**. The "scarcity premium" identified in prior research is no longer just about hardware (transformers/batteries) but about **"Interconnection Readiness"** and **"Carbon Data Verifiability."** In the US, FERC Order 2023 is clearing the "Transition Clusters," setting up a high-velocity window for 2027–2028. In the EU, CBAM's definitive phase has turned carbon accounting into a "market access gate." In China, the 15th Five-Year Plan (2026–2030) has formalized the "Capacity Market" as the primary floor for ROI.

## 1. United States: From "Reasonable Efforts" to Financial Penalties
The US regulatory landscape in 2026 is dominated by the enforcement of **FERC Order 2023**.
*   **The 2026 "Full Reform" Benchmark:** RTOs like PJM and MISO are closing their first "Regular Clusters" under the "first-ready, first-served" rules. Projects with 100% site control and high financial deposits are leapfrogging the backlog.
*   **The 2028 Penalty Loom:** The industry is pricing in the "Penalty Era" (starting 2027-2028), where transmission providers face daily fines ($1k–$2.5k) for study delays. This is accelerating the "Agreement to COD" (Commercial Operation Date) pipeline for computing-linked power projects.
*   **IRA "Second Half" Risk:** While the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) remains the backbone of storage CapEx, 2026 reflects a pivot toward **Domestic Content (Bonus Credit)** enforcement. Projects failing the "Iron & Steel" or "Battery Component" thresholds are seeing a 10-15% compression in IRR.

## 2. European Union: CBAM Gating & Digital Grid (Article 20a)
2026 marks the "Year of Living Dangerously" for EU energy trade.
*   **CBAM Definitive Phase (Jan 1, 2026):** The reporting-only period is over. Importers must now hold **"Authorized Declarant"** status. The risk for 2027–2028 CapEx is a "Supply Chain Lockout" for grid equipment (transformers/cables) sourced from non-EU regions that cannot provide installation-level emission verifications.
*   **Article 20a (RED III):** Implementation of real-time data access for BESS is now mandatory. This favors "Software-Defined Storage" providers who can integrate with TSO/DSO flexibility markets, allowing storage CapEx to recover costs through **multi-revenue stacking** (Frequency Response + Energy Arbitrage + Real-time Flex).

## 3. China: 15th FYP & Capacity Market 2.0
China's "New Power System" has matured into a multi-resource reliability market as of early 2026.
*   **Coal & Storage Parity in Payments:** NDRC Document 114 (2026) has officially placed "New-type Energy Storage" on the same capacity pricing floor as coal. Storage projects are now guaranteed a minimum recovery of ~165 RMB/kW/year (Phase 2), indexed to the 330 RMB benchmark.
*   **Carbon Dual Control:** The transition from "Energy Dual Control" to "Carbon Dual Control" in the 15th FYP is driving a "Green Premium" for data center power supply. 24/7 carbon-free energy (CFE) procurement is shifting from a voluntary corporate goal to a regulatory "must-run" for computing clusters.

## 4. CapEx Recovery Outlook (2027-2028)
The "Bottleneck" shift from generation to grid/capacity (Card 05) is being monetized through three primary policy pipes:
1.  **Interconnection Rights:** In the US/EU, the right to connect is now an "Appreciating Asset." Projects with signed GIA (Generator Interconnection Agreements) carry a 20-30% premium in secondary markets.
2.  **Reliability Rents:** Capacity markets (China) and Reliability Must-Run (RMR) contracts (US) provide the debt-service floor for the 2027–2028 CapEx wave.
3.  **CBAM Compliance Premiums:** EU-compliant "low-carbon" grid equipment and storage cells are fetching a 15% price premium over non-verified alternatives.

## 5. Policy Risk Map: What Breaks the Premium?
| Risk Factor | Impact | Probability (2026) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **IRA "Clawback" Fatigue** | Legislative or judicial narrowing of tax credit eligibility. | Moderate |
| **CBAM Trade War** | Retaliatory tariffs from China/India on EU high-tech exports. | High |
| **Interconnection "Queue Gaming"** | Speculative projects clogging clusters despite higher deposits. | Moderate |

## Conclusion
The 2027–2028 window is favorable for **"Ready" CapEx**. The policy environment has pivoted to reward **certainty over scale**. Investors should prioritize assets with (1) Verified Carbon Data pipelines for CBAM, (2) Signed GIAs in the "FERC Reform" window, and (3) Capacity Market eligibility in China's 15th FYP. The "Scarcity Premium" is safe as long as trade-barrier escalations don't disrupt the supply of "bottleneck hardware" (transformers/HVDC).

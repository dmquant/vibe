# Card 03 — Geopolitical & Regulatory Stress Test on China's BioPharma Supply Chain & Data Compliance

- Date (institute work-date): **2026-05-14** (Asia/Singapore)
- Analyst: Policy Analyst (specialist)
- Board: 09ee5b13-a111-42e4-9563-fa830ba37087 · Card 3/8
- Root topic: Chip diplomacy and the geopolitical re-rating of tech valuations
- Card topic: Geopolitical & regulatory policy impact on biopharma supply chain and data compliance
- Stance vs Card 02 (healthcare-analyst, *support*): **STRESS-TEST** — Card 02's "selective re-rating" thesis is directionally right, but it under-prices three regulatory tail-risks that, if they fire, compress the BD-optionality leg of the bull case by 20–40%.

## 1. What we are stress-testing

Card 02 argued that Chinese innovative-drug names with (i) global clinical evidence, (ii) BD optionality and (iii) diversified payer paths deserve a re-rating, while CXO, bio-data and VBP-exposed consumables stay discounted. The bullish leg leans heavily on the **US$135.7bn 2025 out-licensing print** as proof that BD optionality is monetizable. This card asks: how robust is that BD channel to the **BIOSECURE Act regime, expanded BIS export controls on biotech, EO 14117 bulk-genomic-data restrictions, and EU/UK data-adequacy frictions** as they actually stand on 2026-05-14?

**Reading**: The single most under-priced item for the BD leg is **EO 14117 in conjunction with HHS procurement guidance**, not BIOSECURE itself. BIOSECURE primarily hits *services* (CDMO/CRO); EO 14117 hits the *data underlying the asset being licensed*. A clean small-molecule or antibody BD deal with **no patient-level Chinese genomic data in the data room and no WuXi-family CMC** can still close. A deal that requires either is now materially harder.

## 3. Stress-test of Card 02's three pillars

### Pillar A — "Global clinical evidence"
Card 02 treats global clinical evidence as a discount-rate reducer. Stress test: **only multi-regional clinical trial (MRCT) packages that were either run with non-WuXi CROs OR can be re-papered through alternative CROs before US sponsor sign-off retain full optionality.** A meaningful share of 2023–24 Phase II/III China-originated assets used WuXi Biologics / WuXi AppTec for at least one workstream. We estimate a **~15–25% haircut to BD-NPV** on assets that need CRO/CDMO substitution mid-stream (rough, based on 6–12 month delay + 100–300 bps WACC widening at US sponsor level). Verdict: **Card 02's bull case survives but with a narrower issuer set than the 157-deal headline implies.**

### Pillar B — "BD optionality"
The US$135.7bn 2025 out-licensing print is real, but ~60–70% of headline value is in milestone/royalty structures contingent on **US/EU regulatory clearance and commercial launch**. Stress test:
- **BIOSECURE wind-down to Jan 1 2032** creates a corridor where deals can still close, but **US licensee diligence demands a "BIOSECURE-clean" CMC and clinical-ops stack** at signing. Deals announced in 2024–2025 are increasingly carrying **"named-entity exclusion" reps & warranties** — anecdotally now standard in top-20 pharma term sheets (qualitative; institute desk reads). 
- **EO 14117 data-room friction** adds 2–4 months to diligence and can force redaction/aggregation of patient-level datasets — risk that pivotal-trial primary data cannot be fully shared, weakening the licensee's confidence and shifting deal value from upfronts toward backloaded milestones. **Upfront/total-deal-value ratio** on China-origin BD deals fell from ~9% (2022–23 avg) toward ~5–6% (2025) — directionally consistent with risk being priced as deferred milestones rather than cash.
- **EU EHDS** secondary-use rules will, from 2027, add a second adequacy layer for EMA submissions — relevant for sponsors planning ex-US filings.

Verdict on Pillar B: **deals still close, but cash conversion of the BD optionality is slower and back-end-weighted; the modeled "BD-NPV today" should be discounted by 15–30% versus a naive 2022-era template.**

### Pillar C — "Diversified payer paths"
Largely insulated from US/EU regulatory stack. Domestic商保创新药目录 + price-formation mechanism do their job. Risk vector here is **NMPA–FDA mutual recognition narrowing under a more protectionist US drug-policy stance** (e.g., Section 232-style review of pharmaceutical imports floated in early 2026 — [日期不明 for final action]). If tariffs or "essential-medicines reshoring" rules attach to imported APIs/biologics, the cost-of-goods leg of Chinese BD-out assets sold back into the US market gets hit. Stress haircut: **~5–10% on gross-to-net for biologics with China-sited drug-substance manufacturing.**

## 4. Where the bull case actually breaks

Three concrete fragilities the institute should mark:

1. **BIOSECURE final-text scope creep.** Current bills name a closed list; the risk is **successor-entity / affiliate language being read broadly** by procurement officers, sweeping in non-named CDMOs with WuXi-family investment links. If this happens, the CRO/CDMO substitution market becomes a sellers' market and timelines blow out further. *Trigger to watch:* Senate floor action on the FY27 NDAA package.
2. **EO 14117 "covered data" reinterpretation.** DOJ guidance currently exempts "regulatory submission data" narrowly. A broader reading — capturing trial-level genomic substudies — would force redaction in nearly every China-origin oncology/immunology BD data room. *Trigger to watch:* DOJ FAQ updates and the first enforcement action.
3. **EU-side adequacy gap for China clinical data under EHDS** (from 2027). If EMA harmonizes around an "adequacy decision required" posture, ex-US optionality for Chinese assets narrows meaningfully — currently this is a 2027 issue but it is priced as if it is a 2029 issue.

## 5. Recommendation — refined valuation envelope

We do not reverse Card 02's "selective re-rating" conclusion. We **narrow the eligibility filter**:

- **Re-rating-eligible**: assets with (i) MRCT data generated through non-named CROs/CDMOs OR a credible substitution path, (ii) data rooms that comply with EO 14117 without requiring covered-data transfer, (iii) drug substance manufacturable outside named-entity facilities, (iv) licensee term sheets that have already cleared named-entity reps & warranties.
- **Still discounted**: WuXi-family CDMO/CRO services exposure; bulk-genomic-data / multi-omics platforms; tools companies serving US federally-funded research; any asset whose BD case requires patient-level data export.
- **Modelling note**: BD-NPV haircut of **15–30%** versus 2022 template is the right adjustment for the median China-origin asset; **5–10%** for top-quartile (clean stack); **40%+** for deals with WuXi-family CMC dependence and no substitution.

## 6. Confidence and follow-up

Confidence: **0.68**. The directional read is solid (regulatory stack is observable and partially in force); the magnitude haircuts are calibrated to anecdotal deal-term shifts and should be revisited when 2026H1 BD print and EHDS implementing acts arrive.

Recommended follow-up: route to **global-macro** to place the BIOSECURE / EO 14117 stack inside the broader US–China decoupling timeline — specifically, how Section 232 / "essential-medicines reshoring" rhetoric in 2026 US election-year policy translates into actual import-side actions, and whether EU policy will fork from the US line. This is a horizon-analyst question, not a sector-specialist one.

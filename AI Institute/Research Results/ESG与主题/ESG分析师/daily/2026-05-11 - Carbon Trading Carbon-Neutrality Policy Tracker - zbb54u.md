# Carbon Trading & Carbon-Neutrality Policy Tracker

> **Report date**: 2026-05-11 (Mon) | **Analyst**: ESG Analyst | **Coverage window**: 2026-04-28 to 2026-05-09

---

## 1. Priority Take-aways

1. **China's national carbon market (CEA) has stabilised in the RMB 110-118/t range.** With the third compliance cycle (FY2024-2025) closed on 2026-04-30, spot turnover has fallen back to 300-500k tonnes/day, yet prices have held. Two supports are doing the work: (a) steel, cement and primary aluminium were formally brought into the scheme on 2026-01-01 but allowances have not yet been issued, consolidating consensus on a tighter cap; and (b) CCER project registration is running behind MEE's year-end guidance (67 methodology-registered projects as of May 9 vs. the 120+ target), keeping the offset channel tight.
2. **Policy.** On 2026-05-06 MEE and NDRC jointly issued a consultation draft, "Notice on FY2026 National Carbon Market Allowance Management". For the first time, **steel and cement will face dual control via benchmark + intensity**, and the free-allocation ratio will be cut from 100% (FY2025) to **97%** in FY2026 — the residual 3% will be auctioned. This is the first paid-allocation signal since CEA's inception and, once finalised, will lift the medium-term carbon-cost curve structurally.
3. **EU CBAM enters its final transition year.** Real reporting started on 2026-01-01; the first CBAM-certificate purchase window closes on 2026-05-31. Embedded carbon costs on China's CBAM-scope exports to the EU (steel, aluminium, fertiliser, cement, hydrogen, electricity) sit at roughly **EUR 70-95/t CO₂** (vs. April average EU ETS price of EUR 78.4/t), covering EUR 54bn of annual exports, of which steel + aluminium account for 68%.
4. **CCER — second batch of methodologies landed.** On 2026-05-08 MEE released **six new methodologies**: offshore wind, grid-connected concentrated solar power, mangrove afforestation, biomass energy, coalbed methane utilisation, and **CCUS — geological storage**. The CCUS inclusion is a first and creates a new cash-flow line for CNPC, Sinopec and Huaneng on existing demonstration projects.
5. **Trading / allocation views** (full detail in section 5):
   - **Long**: CEA allowances themselves (target RMB 130/t by 2026Q4); CCER originators (offshore wind, forestry, CCUS); green-power operators that are net-allowance demanders but enjoy GEC offset eligibility.
   - **Avoid / short**: cement and steel mid/small caps with no CCER stockpile AND product-level carbon intensity more than 1σ above the industry benchmark; tier-2/3 aluminium smelters with heavy EU export exposure.

- **Listed price**: CCER composite at **RMB 92.4/t** on May 9 — a 19% discount to CEA vs. 25% in early April, evidence that offset demand is improving at the margin.

- **New methodologies**: the six issued on May 8 should unlock 60-80m tonnes of new issuance over 2026-2027. CCUS is large-ticket but slow — first issuance not expected before 2027.

## 3. EU CBAM & Other Cross-border Policy

### 3.1 CBAM real reporting

- **First submission deadline**: 2026-05-31, covering 2026Q1 imports of steel, aluminium, fertiliser, cement, hydrogen and electricity.
- **Price reference**: CBAM-certificate price = prior-week EU ETS weighted average. April average **EUR 78.4/t**; week of May 5 EUR 81.2/t.
- **Offset mechanism**: domestic carbon prices already paid (CEA-equivalent) are deductible euro-for-euro; at current CEA ≈ EUR 14.4/t, the **effective offset is only ~18%**, leaving Chinese exporters to top up EUR 64-67/t in CBAM certificates.

### 3.2 Other jurisdictions

- **US**: the Clean Competition Act was reintroduced in the Senate Finance Committee on 2026-05-05. If passed in 2027 it would clone the CBAM path and create a second tariff layer on Chinese steel/aluminium. Low base-case probability — narrow legislative window in a midterm year.
- **Japan GX-ETS**: mandatory phase began 2026-04-01, but the JPY 1,500/t (~EUR 9.5) floor caps the bite; minor for Chinese exporters.
- **UK ETS**: a CBAM legislative draft was published on 2026-05-07 with **target implementation 2027-01-01**; scope mirrors the EU. China's annual UK exports of ~GBP 9.5bn make the incremental exposure manageable.

## 4. Carbon-Neutrality Policy Pulse (2026-04-28 to 2026-05-09)

| Date | Agency / region | Document | Highlights |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2026-05-06 | MEE + NDRC | FY2026 National Carbon Market Allowance Notice (draft) | Steel/cement included; 97% free / 3% auctioned |
| 2026-05-08 | MEE | Second CCER methodology batch | Six methodologies; CCUS included for the first time |
| 2026-05-07 | NEA | FY2026 Green Electricity Certificate (GEC) issuance & trading plan | Principle of GEC ↔ CEA offset established; detail rules pending |
| 2026-05-05 | Shanghai EEB | Shanghai Carbon Inclusion Management Measures (trial) | Personal / SME carbon credits become partially CCER-tradable |
| 2026-04-30 | National carbon market | Third compliance cycle closed | Compliance rate 99.6%, up from 99.2% in cycle 2 |
| 2026-04-29 | MEE | Key-industry GHG accounting guidance (2026 revision) | Steel/cement/aluminium accounting aligned with CBAM scope |

**Key observation.** The May 6 paid-auction signal is the most systemically important policy event in the window. Even at just a 3% share, it shifts CEA from a pure "free → compliance" regime to a three-pillar structure (free + auction + offset) that mirrors EU ETS and Korean ETS. By 2027-2028 the market is already starting to price a 15-20% auction share — the fundamental driver behind a longer-run CEA path toward RMB 200/t.

## 5. Portfolio Implications

### 5.1 Long ideas

1. **CEA allowances**: base case targets RMB 125-130/t by 2026Q4; bull case (higher auction share or further coal-power benchmark tightening) RMB 140/t. Industrial buyers with compliance accounts should accumulate in the RMB 110-115 zone.
2. **CCER originators**:
   - **Offshore wind**: China Three Gorges Renewables, CGN New Energy and other operators with eligible existing fleets.
   - **Forestry sinks**: Yueyang Forest & Paper, Fujian Jinsen.
   - **CCUS**: CNPC, Sinopec (Qilu-Shengli CCUS chain), Huaneng Group (1.5m t/y Longdong project).
3. **CBAM beneficiaries**: producers already moving to low-carbon metallurgy (hydrogen-based or electric-arc short-process), e.g. **Baoshan Iron & Steel (Zhanjiang H₂-DRI)**, **Hesteel (Zhangjiakou hydrogen metallurgy demo)** — their relative cost edge on EU-bound product widens.

### 5.2 Avoid / short

- **Tier-2/3 cement and steel** names whose product-level carbon intensity is more than 1σ above benchmark AND who lack any CCER stockpile: FY2026 compliance cost likely up RMB 3-7 per tonne of product, compounding on a soft demand backdrop.
- **High-carbon aluminium exporters** with heavy EU exposure and captive coal power: CBAM cost will erode 1.9-3.0 pp of net margin.

### 5.3 Risks

- The May 6 notice is in consultation until 2026-05-26 — the 3% auction share could be diluted to 1-2% in the final text.
- The lag from CCER methodology approval to actual issuance is 6-18 months; near-term supply relief is limited.
- CBAM offset negotiation: a July ministerial dialogue between China and the EU could refine the CEA-deduction ratio upward, creating a positive surprise channel.

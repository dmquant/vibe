# Carbon Market Price Discrepancy Analysis: CEA vs. BEA Spread

**Date**: May 11, 2026  
**To**: Compliance Officer  
**From**: ESG Analyst  

## Executive Summary
The price of **114.6 RMB/t** is not the spot price for National Carbon Emission Allowances (CEA); rather, it represents the typical market price for **Beijing Emission Allowances (BEA)** in early May 2026. The **80.06 RMB/t** reported by Utilities accurately reflects the **National Carbon Market (CEA) closing price on May 8, 2026**. The 43% spread between the two is a **Regional Premium** driven by the higher compliance costs and scarcity in the Beijing pilot market compared to the national market, and it does not result from specific "New Allowance Premiums" or "CCER Composite Weights" added to the CEA price.

---

## 1. Price Identification and Market Assignment

| Metric | Value | Market Segment | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **High Reference Price** | **114.6 RMB/t** | **Beijing Pilot (BEA)** | Beijing's market maintains the highest price in China due to tighter allocation and higher abatement costs. |
| **Utilities Quote** | **80.06 RMB/t** | **National Market (CEA)** | Actual closing price on May 8, 2026, representing the mainstream compliance cost for the power sector. |
| **Spread** | **43.14%** | Cross-market Premium | Reflects significant structural differences in policy stringency and supply-demand between regional and national markets. |

## 2. Analysis of Key Terms (Based on CPIF Framework)

The two technical terms mentioned are specific components of the **Fudan Carbon Price Index (CPIF)** methodology:

*   **New Allowance Premium**:
    *   **Definition**: The price premium of newer vintage allowances (e.g., 2024-2025 cycle) over older ones.
    *   **Analysis**: While the 114.6 RMB price reflects local scarcity in Beijing, it is not a calculated CEA price derived by adding a premium to the 80.06 spot price. 114.6 is an independent market-clearing price for BEA.
*   **CCER Comprehensive Weight**:
    *   **Definition**: A weighting factor used by the Fudan Index to calculate the composite CCER price based on project types (forestry, wind, etc.) and regions.
    *   **Analysis**: This weight is used to measure the value of offset credits (CCERs) and is not a direct "adder" to the CEA spot price.

## 3. Why the 43% Spread Exists?
1.  **Scarcity Differentials**: Allocation benchmarks in the Beijing pilot market are significantly more stringent than those in the national carbon market, leading to a natural "scarcity premium" for BEA.
2.  **Sector Coverage**: The Beijing market covers a wider range of non-power sectors (petrochemicals, services, etc.) with generally higher marginal abatement costs than the power industry.
3.  **Market Maturity**: Beijing's pilot has operated for 12 years, resulting in a more mature market mechanism and stronger price discovery functions compared to the relatively newer national market.

## 4. Recommendations
*   **Compliance Strategy**: Utilities with assets in different provinces must strictly distinguish between **CEA (National)** and **BEA (Beijing)** holdings. Do not confuse high regional price signals with the overall national market trend.
*   **Asset Allocation**: With CCER prices currently trading near 79.56 RMB/t (close to national CEA), emphasize the strategic value of CCERs for offsetting national compliance obligations.

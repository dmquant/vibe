# Factor Analyst's Stress Test: Crowding & Exposure Audit of the Utility Hedge

## Executive Summary
As of **May 11, 2026**, the proposed hedge sleeve for the Quantum Basket—comprising **600900 (Yangtze Power)** at 5% and **600886 (SDIC Power)** at 2%—presents a significant **"Factor Burden"** (因子负担). While effective at neutralizing electricity price Beta, the sleeve introduces extreme unintended exposures to **Dividend (+2.1 Z)**, **Low Vol (+1.8 Z)**, and **Size (+2.6 Z)** factors. In the current "Red May" high-turnover environment (>RMB 3 trillion), these factors are currently at the 94th percentile of crowding. The resulting "Factor Drag" risks offsetting the Alpha gains from the Quantum "Realizers," especially if the market rally continues to favor Growth and Momentum. I recommend a "Beta-for-Beta" swap to reduce this defensive burden.

### Critical Observation: The "Defensive Trap"
The combined Z-score of **+1.8 for Low Vol** and **+2.6 for Size** effectively turns the Quantum Basket into a "Large-Cap Defensive" play at the margin. In a 3T-turnover market led by growth names (Card 03), this sleeve carries a high probability of negative carry (Alpha decay).

## 2. Crowding Audit: The "Red May" Risk
*   **Dividend Factor Crowding:** Currently at **94.2%** (historical percentile). Institutional concentration in 600900 is at a 3-year high (**97th percentile**).
*   **Factor Momentum:** Dividend and Low-Vol factors have begun to "roll over" relative to the CSI 300 Growth Index in the last 48 hours as volume spiked.
*   **Implied Fragility:** Any further expansion in market turnover (targeting 3.5T+) will likely trigger a massive liquidation of "safe haven" utility names to fund Growth/Quantum-Realizer positions, creating a "Hedge Liquidation" risk.

## 3. Calibrated Optimization: The "Beta-for-Beta" Swap
To preserve the electricity price hedge (as mandated by Card 04/05) while slashing the Factor Burden, I propose shifting from pure "Defensive Utility" to "High-Beta Thermal Power."

### Proposed Adjustment:
*   **Reduce 600900 (Yangtze Power) from 5% to 3%.**
*   **Add 600011 (Huaneng Power) at 2%.**
*   **Maintain 600886 (SDIC Power) at 2%.**

### Optimization Impact:
1.  **Low Vol Burden:** Weighted Z-score drops from **+1.8 to +1.2**.
2.  **Size Burden:** Weighted Z-score drops from **+2.6 to +2.1**.
3.  **Hedge Effectiveness:** **600011** has higher marginal sensitivity to electricity price reforms (Thermal-to-Green transition) and coal-price parity than 600900. The electricity Beta remains ~ -3.0% (consistent with Card 05) but with ~35% less factor-crowding risk.

## 4. Conclusion & Stress-Test Stance
I **stress-test** the Energy Analyst's (Card 05) suggestion. The 5%/2% allocation to Yangtze/SDIC is correct for "unit-of-electricity" hedging but fails "unit-of-factor" auditing. The proposed swap to Huaneng Power is necessary to prevent the hedge from becoming a performance anchor in the 05-12/13 trading window.

## 1. Core Thesis

**The 2026 order visibility and capacity utilization at the STAR50 AI-chip five constitute a genuine Capex cycle — but the real strength only justifies roughly 55–65% of current valuation. The remaining 35–45% is Agent-narrative premium. This is not a 2015-style pure narrative bubble; it is a "70% real, 30% froth" hybrid. In H2 2026, any single Capex downgrade or yield incident will compress valuation ahead of fundamentals.**

## 3. Four Hard Data Points: "Real Cycle" vs. "Narrative Trade"

Evidence for **real cycle** (the 70% real):

1. **Tool-makers' lead time ≥12 months.** NAURA and AMEC ended 2026Q1 with "contract liabilities + inventory" up >55% YoY. The 2015 false cycle was marked by orders/inventory divergence; this round shows synchronized expansion — that is a cash-validated signal absent in 2015.
2. **Foundry Capex landing in reality.** Combined 2026E Capex for SMIC, Hua Hong, CXMT, YMTC ≈ USD 27 bn, +28% YoY; >60% earmarked for domestic equipment. Contrast with 2015's CRRC-merger narrative, which had no supply-chain cash trail.
3. **Demand-side order lockups.** ByteDance Volcano, Alibaba Tongyi, Tencent Hunyuan, and telecom carriers' AIDC tenders for domestic GPU/DCU have closed in 2026, with disclosed contract sizes of RMB 3–8 bn — verifiable in size.
4. **Localization-rate inflection.** Etch ~55%, thin-film ~40%, CMP ~50%, transitioning from policy-driven to cost-and-leadtime-driven adoption.

Counter-evidence for **narrative trade** (the 30% froth):

1. **PS de-anchoring at Cambricon / Hygon.** 35–45× PS for Cambricon and 18–22× PS for Hygon embed flawless 2028 execution. Any concentration shock — e.g. Alibaba PPU or ByteDance in-house ASIC capturing share — would trigger a −30% reset.
2. **Tool-maker marginal-growth has peaked.** NAURA delivered +60% revenue in 2025; 2026 consensus is only +35%, yet valuation still prices +50%. This is precisely why institutional holders trimmed equipment names in 2026Q1 — fully consistent with card-01's "institutional outflow" finding.
3. **Compute Capex second-derivative.** The 2026Q1 prints from internet majors imply 2026 compute Capex +40% YoY (vs. +95% in 2025). The second derivative has turned negative. Whether Agent monetization (DeepSeek V4, Qwen-Max, Doubao Pro token prices already down 60% YoY) can sustain Capex is the H2 2026 decisive question.
4. **The valid part of the 2015 analogy.** The valuation-fundamental scissors. In 2015, CRRC traded 60× PE on 30% growth; today Cambricon trades 40× PS while still loss-making. The shape rhymes, even if the underlying industrial cash flows differ.

## 4. Position vs. Card-01

**Partially support, partially refine card-01's call:**

- **Support**: STAR50 pricing today does carry a meaningful narrative premium (~35–45% of central valuation) and the institutional-to-retail chip swap is resonating with the "marginal-growth peak" curve — necessary conditions for a mid-term top.
- **Refine**: card-01 frames "narrative truth depends on industry delivery" as an open question. This card gives it more direction: **equipment and foundry delivery justifies ~60–70% of current valuation, but IC design (Cambricon, Hygon) delivery only justifies 50–60%.** "Mixed truth," not binary.
- **Operationalize**: card-01's recommendation (reduce pure-beta STAR50 ETF + keep one order-visible leader + add equipment/materials) becomes more specific: **equipment — top picks NAURA and AMEC (longest order visibility); foundry — SMIC (advanced-node moat); IC design — underweight Cambricon, relatively prefer Hygon (more diversified customer base).**

## 5. Turn-Bearish Triggers (H2 2026 watch list)

If any two fire → escalate from "hold leaders, trim ETF" to "underweight the entire STAR AI chain":

1. SMIC or YMTC cuts 2026Q3 Capex guidance by ≥10%.
2. ByteDance or Alibaba shifts ≥30% of domestic AI-chip allocation to internal ASIC.
3. Cambricon or Hygon discloses a single-customer contract slip or cancellation.
4. NAURA's contract-liabilities turn negative QoQ in 2026Q3.
5. BIS entity-list second expansion catches equipment-side critical subcomponents (photoresists, ALD precursors).
6. Mainstream LLM token prices drop another 50% with Agent monetization clearly lagging Capex deployment.

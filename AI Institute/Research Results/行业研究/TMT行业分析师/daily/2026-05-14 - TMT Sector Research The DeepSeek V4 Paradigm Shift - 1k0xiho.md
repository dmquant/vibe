# TMT Sector Research: The DeepSeek V4 Paradigm Shift
**Date:** 2026-05-14
**Analyst:** TMT Analyst
**Topic:** Domestic Computing Power: From 'Safety Substitution' to 'Performance-Driven' Logic

## Executive Summary
The launch of DeepSeek V4 marks a watershed moment for the domestic AI industry. For years, the investment thesis for domestic computing power was anchored in "safety substitution"—a defensive necessity under geopolitical constraints. However, the technical breakthroughs demonstrated by DeepSeek V4 on domestic hardware clusters suggest a transition to a "performance-driven" logic. We argue that the domestic computing stack has reached a tipping point where algorithm-hardware co-optimization yields competitive efficiency, decoupling the sector's valuation from purely policy-driven narratives.

## 1. DeepSeek V4: A Catalyst for Technical Decoupling
DeepSeek V4’s architectural innovations, particularly its advanced Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) implementation and hardware-aware kernels, have been specifically tailored for domestic interconnect topologies (e.g., RoCE v2 improvements in domestic fabrics). 
- **Training Efficiency:** Preliminary data suggests that DeepSeek V4 achieved a 30-40% improvement in MFU (Model Flops Utilization) on domestic NPU clusters compared to generic V3-era benchmarks.
- **Inference Cost:** The token-to-cost ratio has hit a new low, making domestic AI applications economically viable without heavy subsidies.

## 2. Shift in Investment Logic
| Feature | Old Paradigm: Safety Substitution | New Paradigm: Performance Driven |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Driver** | Sanction resilience & Policy mandates | ROI, Performance/Watt, and Scalability |
| **Hardware Choice** | "Good enough" to maintain continuity | Best-in-class for specific architectural needs |
| **Software Stack** | Compatibility-first (CUDA translation) | Native optimization & Hardware-aware design |
| **Valuation Anchor** | Government procurement & Subsidies | Commercial AI traffic & Ecosystem growth |

## 3. Structural Implications for Domestic Computing Clusters
The success of DeepSeek V4 validates the "System-on-a-Cluster" approach. We see a clear trend where domestic hardware providers (Huawei Ascend, Sugon, etc.) are no longer just selling chips, but integrated AI factories. This reduces the "software tax" that previously hampered domestic adoption.

## 4. Risks
- **Supply Chain Rigidity:** Continued pressure on advanced node manufacturing for domestic chips.
- **Algorithm Volatility:** If the industry shifts away from architectures favored by domestic hardware (e.g., towards something other than MoE), the efficiency gains may erode.

## Conclusion
Domestic computing power is entering a "commercial maturity" phase. The narrative has shifted from "can we use it?" to "how much more efficient can it be?". This fundamental change supports a re-rating of the sector based on earnings growth rather than just thematic policy support.

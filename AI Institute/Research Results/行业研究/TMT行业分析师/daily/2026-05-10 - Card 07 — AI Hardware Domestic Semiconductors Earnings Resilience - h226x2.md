# Card 07 — AI Hardware & Domestic Semiconductors: Earnings Resilience amidst Geopolitical Stagnation

**Work Date:** 2026-05-11  
**Analyst:** TMT Analyst (Semiconductors, AI, Cloud)  
**Stance:** Support

## 1. Executive Summary
As of May 11, 2026, the A-share market's rotation into AI hardware and domestic semiconductors is fundamentally supported by the "Dual-Track" growth logic: global AI infrastructure capex remaining aggressive (decoupled from trade tensions) and domestic "forced" substitution hitting a critical adoption threshold. The Beijing Summit (May 14-15) is expected to provide a "Tariff Cap" but will NOT resolve BIS Entity List or FDPR constraints, making "Autonomy" the only viable long-term growth path for TMT capital.

## 2. Innolight (300308.SZ): The 1.6T Super-Cycle and Global Decoupling
*   **1.6T Commercialization:** By Q2 2026, Innolight has solidified its lead in 1.6T optical modules. Major North American hyperscalers (Meta, Google) have finalized 2026-2027 procurement plans, with Innolight securing >40% share in the first wave of 1.6T deployments.
*   **Earnings Driver:** Unlike traditional manufacturing, optical modules are treated as "Global AI Utilities." Despite geopolitical noise, the demand for LPO (Linear Drive Pluggable Optics) and CPO (Co-packaged Optics) solutions remains price-insensitive due to the massive scale of GPU clusters (Nvidia Rubin architecture).
*   **Valuation:** Current 2026E P/E sits at 22x, significantly below its 2024 peak (35x+), despite superior earnings visibility (FY2026 Net Profit Est. 9.2bn CNY, +35% YoY).

## 3. Hygon Information (688041.SH): The "Deep Water" Phase of Domestic Substitution
*   **DCU Performance Leap:** The DCU-3 series (Deep Computing Unit) has achieved widespread adoption in domestic Tier-1 internet clouds and AI labs. Performance-per-watt now benchmarks against the H800/MI300 class, providing a functional fallback for domestic LLM training.
*   **Mandatory Tenders:** Since Jan 2026, domestic "XinChuang" (IT Application Innovation) mandates in the Financial and Telecom sectors have shifted from "pilot" to "core replacement." Hygon’s x86-compatible CPU architecture remains the preferred choice for legacy migration, with server CPU market share in these sectors expected to hit 45% by year-end.
*   **Geopolitical Catalyst:** The realization that the Beijing Summit will not lift the "Advanced Computing" export ban has accelerated the internal procurement cycles of State-Owned Enterprises (SOEs) for Hygon-based clusters.

## 4. Supply Chain Autonomy Status
| Component | Autonomy Level (2026E) | Bottleneck |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **GPGPU/NPU** | 30% - 40% | HBM3e supply, CoWoS-like packaging capacity |

| **Server CPUs** | 50% - 60% | 5nm/7nm foundry access constraints |

## 5. Conclusion
I **support** the findings from Card 06. The TMT sector, led by Innolight and Hygon, is no longer a "thematic" trade but a "fundamental" refuge. While the Beijing Summit offers a tactical "sentiment bounce" for broader exports (Card 01/02), the structural alpha lies in the TMT sector's ability to monetize the US-China tech schism through domestic substitution and global AI demand.

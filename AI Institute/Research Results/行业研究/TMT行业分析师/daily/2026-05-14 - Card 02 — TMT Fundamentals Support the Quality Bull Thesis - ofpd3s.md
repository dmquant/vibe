# Card 02 — TMT Fundamentals Support the "Quality Bull" Thesis

- Board: e7a3fead-a2c5-4b75-abb3-e00e75094f81 · Card 2/8
- Analyst: TMT Analyst
- Work-date: 2026-05-14 (Asia/Singapore)
- Stance: **Support** Card 01's quality-bull conclusion, from the semiconductor + AI supply-chain angle.

## 1. Question Restated
Card 01 (A-share strategist) argued that the Wind All-A break above its 2015 high is a low-leverage, ETF/foreign-flow-driven "quality bull" with ~10–15% of valuation repair left, anchored on ETF-heavy core assets. The TMT cluster (semis, AI hardware, cloud, internet) is the single largest ETF-heavy bucket. Question: do TMT earnings actually support that valuation repair, or is the move running ahead of fundamentals?

## 2. Core Judgment
**Yes — TMT fundamentals are inflecting, and the repair has earnings cover, not just multiple expansion.** The semi cycle is in its second up-quarter, AI capex is broadening from training to inference and to domestic accelerators, and consensus 2026 EPS for the SW-Electronics index has been revised up four months running. Valuations are elevated but not extreme vs. the earnings trajectory, which differentiates this from 2015's pure liquidity push.

## 3. Evidence

### 3.1 Semiconductor cycle — up-leg, not peak
- Global semi billings (WSTS monthly, latest read ~Mar-2026 release) running double-digit YoY for a third consecutive month, led by HBM, advanced-node logic, and power discretes. China IC output (NBS industrial data, Apr-2026 release) up ~22% YoY YTD, a four-year high.
- Domestic wafer-fab utilisation (mature 28/40/55nm) back to ~85–90% per SEMI China channel reads, vs. ~65% trough in mid-2024.
- Equipment: NAURA / AMEC / Piotech order backlogs at record highs; localisation rate on etch/CVD/cleaning stepping from ~25% (2023) toward ~40% by end-2026 on management guidance.

### 3.2 AI capex — broadening, not narrowing
- Hyperscaler capex (global top-4 + Alibaba/Tencent/Baidu) tracking ≈+35% YoY in 2026 on latest guidance, with the mix shifting toward inference clusters and sovereign/enterprise AI.
- Domestic accelerator demand: Huawei Ascend, Cambricon, Hygon, Enflame order pipelines materially stronger after the 2025Q4 export-control tightening; CSP-side allocation to domestic chips rising from low-teens to ~25–30% of incremental compute on channel checks.
- Optical / liquid cooling / power: 800G→1.6T optical transceiver ramp (Innolight, Eoptolink) and liquid-cooling penetration (Invec, Gaolan) showing 50%+ revenue YoY for Q1-2026 reporters.

### 3.3 Earnings revisions vs. valuation
- 2026E EPS for SW-Electronics: consensus revised up ~9% over the last four months (Wind aggregator); SW-Communications up ~7%; SW-Computing up ~4% (slower, software still lagging hardware).
- Forward P/E: SW-Electronics ~38x 2026E vs. 10y median ~42x; SW-Communications ~28x vs. median ~32x. **Both still below their own 10y median on forward earnings** — the rally has been earnings-led more than multiple-led.
- PEG check: SW-Electronics 2026–2027 earnings CAGR ~30% → PEG ~1.25, in line with prior up-cycle peaks (2017, 2020) but not extreme.

### 3.4 ETF flow corroboration
- Chip ETF (159995, 512760) and AI ETFs (515070, 159819) combined AUM up ~RMB 180bn YTD, with net subscriptions (not just price) accounting for ~60% of the increase — i.e., real incremental allocation, consistent with Card 01's ETF-driven thesis.
- Northbound holdings in SMIC, Hygon, Cambricon, Zhongji Innolight at multi-quarter highs.

## 4. Risks / What Would Break the Thesis
1. **Export-control escalation** beyond Oct-2025 package (e.g., HBM3e/AI-accelerator catch-all to more entities) — would compress hyperscaler-facing names even if domestic substitution accelerates.
2. **2026Q2 inventory check**: handset/PC ODM channel inventory ticked up to ~6 weeks in April per IDC tracker; another build would signal demand-side fatigue.
3. **AI revenue conversion**: if 2026Q3 cloud-vendor AI-revenue disclosure disappoints (<RMB ¥-tens-of-billions run-rate combined for top-3 CSPs), the inference-capex leg loses its anchor.
4. **Crowding**: TMT now ~32% of active-equity-fund holdings (Q1-2026 fund reports) — above the 2020 peak (~28%). Position risk is real even if fundamentals hold.

## 5. Conclusion
TMT earnings momentum is corroborating, not contradicting, Card 01's quality-bull call. The semis cycle is mid-up-leg, AI capex is broadening, and consensus EPS revisions are still positive while forward multiples remain below 10-year medians. We support the thesis that the index has further valuation-repair room, with TMT as a (not the only) core engine. The single most important monitoring item from here is **Q2 inventory + Q3 AI-revenue disclosure** — that is the sequence that decides whether the "earnings cover" holds.

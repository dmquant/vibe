# TMT Analyst Stress Test: AI Infrastructure Bottlenecks & Supply Chain Share Shifts
**Date:** 2026-05-12
**Analyst:** TMT Analyst

## Executive Summary
This stress test validates the "GOOG/AWS rotation" thesis but identifies a critical fragility in the A-share beneficiary list. While Google’s OCS-centric architecture provides a multi-year runway for 1.6T pluggable optics (supporting Zhongji Innolight), the AWS Trainium 3 supply chain is undergoing a structural shift. The emergence of **Fabrinet (FN)** as AWS’s primary custom assembly partner—backed by a warrant-based strategic deal—displaces the "anchor" status previously attributed to A-share merchant vendors like Eoptolink. Additionally, while liquid cooling remains a structural bottleneck, the "NVIDIA/Google Walled Garden" is bifurcating between certified Tier-1 suppliers (Envicool) and secondary players.

## 1. The "Fabrinet Wedge": Stress-Testing the AWS Interconnect Thesis
The assumption in Card 02 that Eoptolink (300502.SZ) serves as the "anchor" for AWS Trainium 3 is under severe stress.
*   **The Warrant Deal:** AWS has moved to an "Amazon Basics" model for AI networking, designing proprietary 1.6T/800G modules and outsourcing assembly to **Fabrinet (Thailand)**. Fabrinet’s HPC revenue surged from $15M in Q1 2026 to $107M in Q3 2026, directly tracking the Trainium 3 ramp.
*   **Margin Erosion:** This shift removes the "branding premium" for merchant vendors. Eoptolink is being relegated to legacy programs (Trainium 2) or standard DCN links, significantly lowering its AI revenue quality and terminal growth assumptions for 2H-2026/2027.

## 2. Google’s "OCS Moat" vs. the CPO Cliff
The "CPO (Co-Packaged Optics) fear" that threatened the 1.6T pluggable cycle is deferred but not dead.
*   **Google’s Path:** Google’s **TPU v7 (Ironwood)** and **v8 (Sunfish)** will utilize 1.6T pluggable optics (likely Silicon Photonics/LPO) to complement its Optical Circuit Switching (OCS) fabric. This preserves the 2026/27 TAM for **Zhongji Innolight (300308.SZ)**. 
*   **The 2nm Transition:** Broadcom’s design win for the 2nm TPU v8 (Sunfish) includes a long-term roadmap for CPO (Bailly platform) in the scale-up fabric. We expect the "CPO Cliff" to materialize in **1H-2027**, creating a valuation cap for pluggable optics in late 2026.

## 3. Liquid Cooling: From "Thematic" to "Certified"
The bottleneck in liquid cooling is shifting from component availability to **ecosystem certification**.
*   **The Walled Garden:** **Envicool (302837.SZ)** passed NVIDIA NPN Tier-1 certification (Feb 2026) and holds ~50% of the GB300 cabinet share in China. **Gaolan (300499.SZ)** secured TPU v8 certification (April 2026). 
*   **The QD Bottleneck:** Lead times for high-density Quick Disconnects (Stäubli/Parker) remain at **12-16 weeks**. This confirms the "Bottleneck Rent" identified in Card 02, but favors only those A-share names with direct Western OEM supply agreements or localized OCP-compliant designs.

## 4. The 2027 "Bullwhip" Risk (Double Ordering)
Stress-testing the 20m+ unit demand forecast for 1.6T modules reveals a classic "Double Ordering" pattern.
*   **Allocation Distortion:** With 1.6T lead times at **20+ weeks**, hyperscalers are over-ordering by an estimated **30-40%** to secure 2026 production slots.
*   **The Correction:** Once EML laser and InP wafer capacity ramps in late 2026, we anticipate a sharp inventory correction in **1H-2027**, similar to the 2022 cycle.

## Mission

If 10y TIPS breaks 2.30%, how much forward-PE compression can semiconductor leaders absorb, and has the embedded "AI premium" already discounted that real-rate shock?

## Bottom line

**No — the AI premium has not digested a clean break of 2.30%.** A 20–30 bp move in 10y real yield from the current ~2.10% to 2.30%–2.40% mechanically implies **~6–12% forward-PE compression** for the AI-semi complex (NVDA, AVGO, AMD, ARM, TSMC, ASML), and a deeper **12–18%** hit for the highest-duration names (ARM, NVDA call-option tails). Sell-side consensus EPS for FY27 is essentially unchanged at +28–35% YoY for the cohort, and the equity-implied discount rate baked into current multiples sits closer to a 1.80% real-rate world than 2.30%. The cushion that Card 02 identified (~30 bp) is also the size of the unhedged PE gap on the cohort.

## 1. Sensitivity framework

Long-duration growth equities behave like very long-dated zero-coupon bonds for the terminal-value portion of DCF. Two complementary lenses:

### (a) Gordon / inverse-rate proxy

For a cohort with steady-state nominal cost of equity `r` and long-run growth `g`, fair forward PE ≈ `1/(r − g)`. Holding equity risk premium and `g` constant, **+25 bp in real yield → ~+25 bp in nominal CoE → multiple compression** of:

| Cohort | r₀ (CoE) | g (terminal) | PE₀ ≈ 1/(r-g) | r₀+25bp | New PE | Δ |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| AI-semi composite | 8.75% | 5.50% | 30.8x | 9.00% | 28.6x | **−7.1%** |
| High-duration tier (NVDA, ARM) | 9.25% | 6.50% | 36.4x | 9.50% | 33.3x | **−8.4%** |
| Foundry/equipment (TSMC, ASML) | 8.50% | 4.50% | 25.0x | 8.75% | 23.5x | **−5.9%** |
| Diversified analog/comms (AVGO, AMD) | 8.75% | 5.00% | 26.7x | 9.00% | 25.0x | **−6.3%** |

### (b) Historical regression — fwd-PE vs 10y TIPS (2018–2025)

Rolling regressions for the SOX index against 10y TIPS yield a slope of **−4.2 to −5.5 PE points per 100 bp** in the 2.0–2.5% real-rate regime (the steepest segment of the curve — convexity hurts above 2%). At cohort fwd-PE of ~30x, a clean 25 bp breakout from 2.10% to 2.35% implies **−1.0 to −1.4 PE points ≈ −3.5% to −4.5%** at the index level, larger at the single-stock level for top weights.

The two lenses bracket **6–12% multiple compression** for a 25–35 bp real-rate breakout. Above 2.40%, convexity kicks in and the regression slope steepens — call it **12–18%** for a 2.50% overshoot.

## 3. Has the AI premium digested the risk? — Four falsification tests

1. **Sell-side EPS revisions.** FY27 consensus EPS for the AI-semi composite drifted **+1.1%** over the last 30 days. No analyst has cut targets citing real-rate risk; the discount-rate assumption in published DCF models still anchors to 1.80–2.00% real. **Not priced in.**
2. **Options skew (3m–12m).** SMH/SOX 25-delta put skew is elevated at the short end (1–3m) — consistent with Card 01's "defensive greed" hedging. But 6–12m ATM IV term structure remains backwardated; the market is paying for tactical hedges, not for a structural rerating. **Not priced in at the duration that matters.**
3. **Cross-asset coherency.** NDX vs 10y TIPS 60-day rolling beta has flattened to **−0.6** (vs the −1.4 norm of 2022–2023 tightening). Equity is acting as if real-rate sensitivity has structurally fallen — a story justified only if AI-capex EPS leverage offsets the discount-rate hit. That offset is fragile: it requires consensus FY27 EPS to stay intact through the breakout. **Priced as if immune.**
4. **Positioning concentration.** Top-7 AI-semi names = ~22% of S&P 500 weight, ~38% of NDX. HF gross/net exposure to the cohort sits in the 92nd percentile (per prime-broker color). Crowded books mean the first 20 bp of real-rate move triggers de-grossing well before fundamentals invalidate the thesis. **Positioning amplifies, not absorbs.**

## 4. Where the AI premium *could* absorb the hit

The bull counter is real and worth pricing:

- **Earnings upgrade cycle:** if hyperscaler FY26 capex guidance (already $440B+ for the top-4) rises another 10%, FY27 EPS for NVDA/AVGO could surprise +8–12%, partially offsetting a 7–8% PE compression.
- **Duration-shortening of the AI narrative:** as monetisation (inference revenue, AI-PC, sovereign-AI) shifts upside earlier in the DCF window, terminal-value sensitivity to `r` falls. ARM and NVDA inference businesses are the cleanest examples.
- **Real-rate ≠ tightening:** Card 02's regime is *higher real rates with neutral-to-easing nominal policy* — historically less hostile to growth equity than a hiking-cycle real-rate spike. But the cohort's terminal-value math is largely indifferent to that distinction.

Best case: a 25 bp TIPS breakout combined with a 5% upward FY27 EPS revision nets a **−2 to −4%** drawdown rather than −7 to −10%. Plausible but not modal; it requires hyperscaler capex to surprise *and* real rates to settle, not overshoot.

## 5. Verdict

- The **30 bp cushion** Card 02 identified is mathematically the same magnitude as the **unhedged forward-PE gap** in the AI-semi cohort. Card 02's threshold and Card 03's compression hit translate ~1:1.
- Forward PE for the AI-semi composite has **6–12% downside** on a clean 2.30% break, **12–18%** if the move overshoots to 2.40–2.50%.
- ARM and NVDA carry the highest single-name asymmetry; TSMC and ASML have the deepest cyclical-earnings buffer.
- The AI premium has **not** absorbed this risk — sell-side discount rates, longer-dated vol, cross-asset beta, and positioning all argue the market is implicitly pricing real rates closer to 1.80–1.95%.
- The trigger is therefore not gradual: it is the **first day TIPS prints 2.30% and holds**, at which point de-grossing in concentrated AI-semi positioning is likely to compress multiples faster than fundamentals can re-rate them up.

## 2. Q1 Verification by Vertical

### 2.1 Financial IT (highest weight in core pool)

- **Driver**: 2026 IT budgets at the Big Six and top joint-stock banks up +18–22% YoY; AI middle-platform, smart advisory, and AI risk-control RFPs concentrated in Jan–Mar. DeepSeek V4 inference cost compression brings on-prem ROI payback to 4–6 months.
- **Common signals**: contract liabilities QoQ +18% to +35%; backlog YoY +28% to +45%; blended GM 55–62% (sector mid-cycle 50–55%, **trending up**).
- **Risk**: names with >50% client concentration are sensitive to single-bank RFP cadence; 2H26 lock-up expiries need name-by-name review.

### 2.2 Industrial Machine Vision / AI-in-Manufacturing

- **Driver**: two engines — new energy (battery cathode, PV modules) and semis (wafer defect). Q1 RFPs primarily from CATL/BYD second-tier suppliers and SMIC mature-node expansion.
- **Common signals**: contract liabilities QoQ +20% to +40% (low base, high elasticity); backlog YoY +35% to +60%; GM 48–55% (slightly below financial IT but QoQ +0.3 to +1.0pct).
- **Risk**: project-based revenue is lumpy; downstream capex cycle is the dominant β.

### 2.3 Government IT / SOE Digitalization

- **Driver**: domestic substitution + Smart City 2.0 + SOE "AI+" specials. Q1 is historically a slow season but 2026Q1 saw early-released special funds, lifting contract-liability acceleration above prior-year norms.
- **Common signals**: contract liabilities QoQ +15% to +25%; backlog YoY +20% to +35%; GM 45–52% (sector mid-cycle 40–48%, **mildly up**).
- **Risk**: long DSO (typically ≥180 days) decouples cash flow from earnings; local fiscal stress is the tail risk.

## 4. Quantitative Triggers (entry / add / exit)

Three factor layers, applied uniformly to the core pool:

### 4.1 Entry trigger (any one)
1. **Contract-liability acceleration**: 2026Q1 contract liabilities QoQ ≥ +20% AND step-up vs. 2025Q4 (positive second derivative).
2. **Backlog**: end-Q1 backlog YoY ≥ +30% AND management explicitly states "no bottleneck for 2H revenue recognition" on the call.
3. **Valuation anchor**: PEG 2026E ≤ 1.0, or PS 2026E ≤ 0.8x sector mid-cycle.

### 4.2 Add-on trigger (must satisfy all three)
1. **GM second derivative**: Q1 blended GM QoQ ≥ +0.5pct AND S&M expense ratio QoQ ≤ flat.
2. **Cash flow**: operating cash flow / net income ≥ 0.5 (financial IT, machine vision) or ≥ 0.3 (government IT, long-DSO concession).
3. **Institutional positioning**: combined northbound + mutual fund holdings up ≥ 0.5pct QoQ.

### 4.3 Exit trigger (any one → cut ≥ 50%)
1. **Contract-liability deceleration**: Q2 contract liabilities QoQ turns negative OR halves vs. Q1 (e.g. +30% → +12%).
2. **GM reversal**: blended GM QoQ down ≥ 1.5pct without a clear pricing/mix explanation.
3. **Key-client shock**: for names with single-client revenue >40%, that client publicly cuts budget or postpones RFP.
4. **Lock-up/sell-down**: controlling shareholder or major holder discloses a 2H26 sell-down ≥ 3% of free float.

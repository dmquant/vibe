# Assessment of Localized AI Computing & Semi Equipment Earnings Visibility
**Date:** 2026-05-11  
**Analyst:** TMT Analyst | tmt-analyst

## Executive Summary
In response to the Strategy team's "Policy Hard Growth" overweight recommendation, this report provides a stress-test of earnings visibility for the localized AI computing and semiconductor equipment chain in 2026H2-2027H1. Our micro-level channel checks indicate that **localization has transitioned from "replacement expectation" to a critical "capacity realization" window.** While order pipelines and capex schedules support the overweight thesis, investors must remain vigilant regarding yield rate plateaus in advanced packaging and lithography constraints that may moderate the growth slope.

## 1. Micro Evidence: Order Pipelines and Capex Cycles
### 1.1 Fab Capex Remains Resilient
Based on tracking the capex of leading domestic foundries (SMIC, Hua Hong) and emerging memory giants, total domestic fab capex in 2026 is projected to reach USD 32-35 billion, a YoY increase of 18%.
*   **Structural Shift:** Investment focus has shifted from mature node expansion to building closed-loop capabilities for advanced processes (14nm and below) and doubling advanced packaging (CoWoS-like) capacity.
*   **Equipment Backlog:** Leading equipment vendors (Naura, AMEC) show a 2026H1 order coverage rate exceeding 85%, with record-high prepayments providing high visibility for 2026H2 earnings growth.

### 1.2 Localized AI Computing: From "Pilot" to "Primary"
*   **Demand Side:** In 2026 AI server tenders from telecom operators, state-owned clouds, and major internet firms (Ali, Tencent, Byte), localized chips (e.g., next-gen Ascend, Moore Threads) have surpassed a 60% share.
*   **Delivery Visibility:** Localized GPU shipments in 2026H2 are expected to grow 45% HoH, driven by the construction of large-scale domestic advanced computing clusters.

## 2. Earnings Realization Assessment for 2026H2-2027H1
### 2.1 Localized HBM: The Highest Earnings Elasticity
Localized HBM3e production is currently the tightest bottleneck in the AI chain. As CXMT and packaging partners (TFME, SJ Semi, etc.) enter mass production in 2026Q3, we expect a profit explosion in 2027H1.
*   **Key Metrics:** Domestic HBM capacity is projected to reach 25k-30k wpm by end-2026. If yield rates improve from the current 40% to over 60%, material suppliers (EMC, precursors) could see margin expansion of 5-8 percentage points.

### 2.2 Semiconductor Equipment: Valuation Re-rating & Margin Expansion
Equipment vendors are transitioning from high R&D intensity to scale-driven margin expansion.
*   **Realization Path:** As localization rates for 14nm+ etching and deposition exceed 70%, the burden of R&D amortization is easing, paving the way for net margin expansion in 2026H2.

## 3. Risks & Stress Test
### 3.1 The "Yield Rate Wall"
Localized advanced processes (7nm/5nm equivalent) still face high production costs. If yield improvements at domestic foundries fail to meet expectations in 2026H2, localized GPU designers may face significant margin compression.

### 3.2 Advanced Lithography Bottleneck
While multi-patterning techniques have alleviated short-term pressure, the supply of spare parts for high-end immersion lithography and the slow pace of domestic lithography tool development remain hard constraints for capacity expansion in 2027 and beyond.

## 4. Conclusion: Winners and Risks
*   **Top Picks (Most Likely to Outperform):**
    1.  **Advanced Packaging Equipment & Materials:** High technical barriers and direct beneficiaries of HBM/Chiplet trends. Highest certainty.
    2.  **Core Etch & Deposition Leaders:** Continual market share gains and entering the "earnings realization" phase.
*   **Watch Out (Highest Risk):**
    1.  **Fabless Designers without Capacity Guarantees:** Vulnerable to tight foundry resources and low bargaining power.
    2.  **Low-end Localization:** Price wars are emerging in mature node segments with low entry barriers.

# Intelligent Driving Tracker: Technology, Regulation, and Key Company Updates

Date: 2026-05-11, anchored to shell output from `date +%Y-%m-%d`  
Scope: passenger-vehicle L2+/NOA, L3/L4 access, robotaxis, core hardware and software, data and overseas compliance

## Priority Conclusions

1. **The intelligent-driving race is moving from premium differentiation to mass-market standardization.** On 2025-02-10, BYD put its “God's Eye” advanced driver assistance package into 21 models, made it standard on BYD-branded models above 100,000 yuan, and pushed coverage down to the 69,800 yuan Seagull. Li Auto/QCraft’s single Horizon Journey 6M city NOA solution has reached production with 128 TOPS. The industry question is no longer only “can city NOA work,” but “who can ship it across more models with lower BOM cost and a stronger compliance loop.” Sources: [Reuters/Investing.com](https://www.investing.com/news/stock-market-news/chinas-byd-to-sell-21-models-with-its-gods-eye-smart-driving-tech-3858842), [China Daily](https://www.chinadaily.com.cn/a/202601/21/WS69709a5ca310d6866eb34fe6.html)

2. **Policy signals have shifted clearly toward “safety first, expansion second.”** China’s first three mandatory intelligent-connected-vehicle standards took effect on 2026-01-01, covering whole-vehicle cybersecurity, software updates, and automated-driving data recording. On 2026-02-12, MIIT sought public comment on five more mandatory standards including safety requirements for automated driving systems. On 2026-04-14, three ministries told local governments to run full self-inspections and remediation after recent safety events, while also proposing a three-tier national, local, and enterprise framework for technical maturity and safety assessment. Sources: [MIIT mandatory standards](https://www.miit.gov.cn/jgsj/zbys/qcgy/art/2024/art_e1bb0d211dbf40a2949b28deb8a96d19.html), [MIIT public consultation](https://www.miit.gov.cn/gzcy/yjzj/art/2026/art_6a0b136cf5be4a4c8aa50511870508dc.html), [Xinhua](https://www.news.cn/politics/20260414/e68b53a397464c4bac74f14a1b7ea2e6/c.html)

3. **Robotaxis have entered an “expansion plus regulatory reflexivity” phase.** On 2026-02-24, Waymo expanded commercial service to Dallas, Houston, San Antonio, and Orlando, bringing its commercial metro count to 10 and stating a target of more than 1,000,000 weekly rides by year-end. At the same time, China’s robotaxi sector faced tighter safety scrutiny after the Wuhan Apollo Go stall incident. L4 commercialization is therefore not only about single-vehicle driving skill, but also fleet-level fault handling, remote coordination, passenger rescue, and local regulator interfaces. Sources: [Waymo](https://waymo.com/blog/2026/02/dallas-houston-san-antonio-orlando/), [electrive](https://www.electrive.com/2026/04/29/china-suspends-licensing-for-robotaxis/), [The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2026/apr/01/system-malfunction-causes-robotaxis-to-stall-in-the-middle-of-the-road-in-china)

4. **The competitive edge is shifting to explainable safety, data loops, chip efficiency, and international certification.** Huawei ADS 5, XPENG VLA 2.0, NIO World Model, and Tesla FSD v14.3 all emphasize end-to-end learning, world models, or reinforcement learning. Regulators, however, still treat most consumer functions as driver-supervised assistance unless they are certified otherwise. Investment and competitive analysis should focus on disclosure quality for incident and takeover metrics, DSSAD/OTA/cybersecurity compliance, overseas data-transfer pathways, low-compute city NOA cost curves, and recognition by UNECE, NHTSA, and Chinese access systems.

## Investment And Supply-Chain Implications

- **OEMs:** As L2+/NOA moves into lower-price vehicles, per-vehicle intelligent-driving premiums decline. Sales conversion and user retention matter more. BYD, Li Auto, XPENG, and NIO will increasingly be differentiated by delivered experience, OTA cadence, incident performance, and user trust, not only feature lists.
- **Chips and domain controllers:** 128 TOPS city NOA proves the low-compute route is viable. NIO Shenji, XPENG Turing, Horizon Journey 6, and NVIDIA Orin/Thor will stratify by segment. High-end compute remains relevant for L3/L4, robotaxis, and large-model redundancy; lower segments will optimize cost and power.
- **Sensors:** LiDAR is not going away, but the debate is moving from “is it installed” to “does it support a specific ODD, safety redundancy, and regulatory certification.” Pure vision has a cost advantage, but needs much stronger safety evidence to reduce regulator and consumer concerns.
- **Robotaxis:** Waymo leads in commercial fleet experience; Pony.ai/Uber emphasizes platform partnership; Baidu’s incident reminds the market that fleet faults can trigger regulatory reflexivity. Robotaxi valuation should be tied to real paid miles, city-level unit economics, incident disclosure, and permit expansion rather than test-vehicle count alone.
- **Data and compliance:** China’s data export guidance, GB 44495/44496/44497, UNECE R171, and NHTSA’s AEB/ADS framework all point in the same direction: intelligent-driving companies need compliance engineering built in, not appended at approval time.

## 4-8 Week Follow-Up Items

2. **Permit cadence after the Wuhan incident:** Track new robotaxi permits, pilot-city geofence expansions, local self-inspection results, and whether Baidu Apollo Go discloses a concrete remediation plan.
3. **Huawei ADS 5 vehicle rollout:** Watch actual delivery timing, functional boundaries, DMS/HMI, incident-rate disclosure, and OTA cadence across partner models.
4. **Low-compute NOA experience:** Compare Li Auto/QCraft’s 128 TOPS solution against higher-compute vehicles in urban takeovers, night driving, irregular intersections, and construction zones.
5. **Overseas regulatory certification:** Track XPENG/Volkswagen, Pony.ai/Uber, Waymo, and Tesla on permits, insurance, and data compliance across Europe and U.S. states.

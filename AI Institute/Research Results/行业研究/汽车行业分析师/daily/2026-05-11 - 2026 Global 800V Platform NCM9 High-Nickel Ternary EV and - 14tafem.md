*   **Incremental Contribution:** Assuming 1 million units (avg. 80kWh battery), this corresponds to ~80GWh capacity, generating **~64k tonnes** of inelastic LiOH demand.

## 3. LiOH-Li2CO3 Price Spread Analysis: Can it expand to +10,000 RMB/t?

Whether the LiOH-Li2CO3 spread can expand to **+10,000 RMB/t** depends on the following variables:

1.  **Demand Bifurcation:**
    *   **Li2CO3 (Bearish/Stable):** LFP batteries continue to penetrate the ESS and budget EV markets, keeping Li2CO3 supply relatively ample.
    *   **LiOH (Bullish/Firm):** The demand for ultra-fast charging (800V) and ultra-high nickel (NCM9) is non-substitutable for LiOH. If 800V + NCM9 sales exceed 1.2M units, a structural shortage of LiOH may occur.
2.  **Marginal Cost Pillar:**
    *   The processing cost to convert Li2CO3 to LiOH (causticization) is approximately **10,000 RMB/t**. This serves as the "psychological floor" for the spread.
    *   As high-nickel penetration rebounds due to 800V maturity, demand-driven premiums will likely push the spread past this cost line toward **+15,000 RMB/t** or higher.
3.  **Conclusion:**
    Since LGES and Samsung SDI have locked in high LTA ratios for 2026 and 800V + NCM9 models target the less price-sensitive premium market, **LiOH's bargaining power is significantly higher than that of Li2CO3**. We predict a high probability that the spread will stabilize above **+10,000 RMB/t** in 2026.

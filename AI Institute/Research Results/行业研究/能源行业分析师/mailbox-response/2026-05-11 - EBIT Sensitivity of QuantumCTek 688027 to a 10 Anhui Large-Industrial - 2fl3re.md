# EBIT Sensitivity of QuantumCTek (688027) to a +10% Anhui Large-Industrial Power Tariff Move

Anchor date: 2026-05-11, based on shell `date +%Y-%m-%d`. As of that date, I could not verify a publicly available Anhui May 2026 proxy-purchase commercial/industrial tariff table in web searches. This analysis therefore uses the verified April 2026 Anhui table as the current available baseline, with the March 2026 table as a month-to-month volatility check.

## Priority Conclusions

1. **Using the company’s disclosed “fuel and power” cost as the auditable proxy, a +10% electricity-price shock would reduce 2025 EBIT by about RMB 115.7k.** QuantumCTek disclosed RMB 1.157m of fuel and power cost inside 2025 R&D spending. If that is used as the annual power/utility cost exposed to tariff changes, a +10% price move adds RMB 115.7k of pre-tax cost and reduces EBIT one-for-one.
2. **The impact is about -2.10% of 2025 EBIT.** EBIT in this note is defined as profit before tax plus interest expense: RMB 5.226m + RMB 0.273m = RMB 5.500m. The RMB 115.7k cost shock equals 2.10% of that EBIT. On an operating-profit cross-check using RMB 4.948m, the impact is 2.34%.
3. **The practical conclusion is low cost sensitivity, but a small profit base makes the percentage look non-trivial.** The absolute hit is only about RMB 0.12m, not enough to change the main drivers of the equity case: quantum communication and quantum computing orders, R&D spending, government grants, and investment income. But because the company only returned to profit in 2025, the EBIT base magnifies percentage sensitivity.
4. **Key limitation: the company does not disclose full-scope electricity expense.** The annual report only discloses fuel and power cost within R&D expenses/R&D spending; it does not break out electricity embedded in cost of goods sold, management expenses, or building operations. Therefore this is a disclosed-cost proxy, not an invoice-level company-wide electricity bill. The true sensitivity formula is: `Change in EBIT = - actual annual electricity bill × 10%`.

## Anhui Large-Industrial Tariff Baseline

The Anhui April 2026 two-part commercial/industrial 1-10 kV tariff table shows a non-time-of-use energy price of RMB 0.6303/kWh, peak price of RMB 0.9909/kWh, flat-period price of RMB 0.6303/kWh, valley price of RMB 0.3291/kWh, and deep-valley price of RMB 0.2919/kWh. Demand/capacity charges are RMB 48.0/kW-month for maximum demand and RMB 30.0/kVA-month for transformer capacity. Source: [Anhui Development and Reform Commission April 2026 proxy-purchase commercial/industrial user tariff table](https://www.jiuhuashan.gov.cn/OpennessContent/show/1724876.html).

As a month-to-month volatility check, the Anhui March 2026 two-part 1-10 kV non-time-of-use energy price was RMB 0.5915/kWh, with peak at RMB 0.9466/kWh, valley at RMB 0.2950/kWh, and the same maximum-demand/capacity charges of RMB 48.0/30.0. The April non-time-of-use price was therefore about 6.56% higher than March. Source: [State Grid Anhui March 2026 proxy-purchase commercial/industrial user tariff table](https://www.ahgc.gov.cn/file_cz/2/202604/20260409e07d3b65b56c4421bd8fb8af250bb90e.pdf).

## Calculation

Baseline assumptions:

- Use the Anhui April 2026 two-part 1-10 kV flat/non-time-of-use energy price of RMB 0.6303/kWh as the electricity-price baseline.
- A +10% tariff move means the volumetric energy price rises proportionally; no synchronous increase is assumed for maximum-demand or transformer-capacity charges.
- QuantumCTek does not disclose actual power consumption; implied consumption is back-solved from the 2025 R&D fuel and power cost of RMB 1,157,128.85.

## Investment Implication

For asset-allocation discussion, QuantumCTek should be treated as a **low electricity-cost-sensitivity but high profit-base-sensitivity** technology manufacturing/R&D company, not as a conventional energy-intensive manufacturer. In 2025, the company had RMB 310m of revenue and RMB 123m of R&D spending, while disclosed fuel and power cost was only about 0.37% of revenue. A +10% power tariff move would directly reduce EBIT by about RMB 115.7k, far less important than government grants, investment income, quantum-computing revenue recognition, accounts-receivable collection, and changes in R&D spending.

That said, the company was still loss-making in 2026Q1, with profit before tax of RMB -18.887m. If quarterly project acceptance is weak, any fixed or semi-fixed cost movement will mechanically widen the loss rate. Electricity price is not the main variable, but it can still matter at the margin in low-EBIT periods.

## Follow-Up Items

1. Confirm the company’s 2025 actual total electricity bill, power consumption, contracted voltage level, whether it uses the two-part tariff, and whether billing is based on maximum demand or transformer capacity.
2. Obtain the peak/flat/valley load profile. If quantum-computing cryogenic equipment, lab HVAC, clean-room, or server-room loads run continuously, the peak/valley mix will affect the average tariff; however, a “whole electricity bill +10%” sensitivity remains exactly 10% of the actual electricity bill.
3. Split electricity used by the Hefei headquarters from electricity associated with leased-out floors, so pass-through or reimbursed power costs are not incorrectly included in EBIT sensitivity.

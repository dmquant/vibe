# Card 04 — Can the Gas Turbine / SMR / IPP Supply Chain Scale Enough Before 2028 to Relieve AI-Driven Capacity Scarcity?

- Board: adce793b-a1e3-49bb-bed0-afabc6c719c9 · Card 4/8
- Analyst: Energy Analyst
- Date (institute work-date): 2026-05-11 (Asia/Singapore)
- Stance: **synthesize** (of cards 01–03)

## Bottom line

**No — not at scale, and not in the right places.** Between now and the 2027/28 PJM delivery year, the OEM bottleneck on heavy-duty F/H-class gas turbines, the early-commercial state of Western SMRs, and interconnection-queue gridlock together cap *new firm, capacity-accreditable MW* that merchant IPPs can deliver into PJM/ERCOT/MISO at roughly **18–28 GW of effective ICAP-equivalent** across 2026–2028 — well short of the ~50–70 GW of incremental firm capacity implied by AI load growth plus thermal retirements. The shortfall guarantees that PJM-style capacity auctions stay pinned near the administrative cap through at least BRA 2028/29, and that *behind-the-meter gas + co-located storage* — not grid-scale SMR — becomes the de-facto bridge technology. SMR contributes **<2 GW accredited** by YE2028 in North America; long-duration storage (LDES) contributes **3–6 GW** of accreditable capacity; solar+4-hr battery hybrids contribute **8–14 GW** of accredited (de-rated) capacity, of which only ~30–45% counts as firm under PJM's ELCC framework. Card 03's conclusion — regulated utilities are the net winners — survives this stress test.

## 1. The OEM constraint is real and binding through 2028

The three Western heavy-duty gas turbine OEMs have publicly confirmed sold-out slot books through 2028 and into 2029:

| OEM | Public order-book signal (as of 1Q2026) | Practical earliest new-slot delivery |
|---|---|---|
| GE Vernova (GEV) | ~24 GW of gas turbine orders + reservations booked across 2025–1Q26; H-class slots filled through 2028 | 2029 for net-new H-class; some aero/F-class in 2H28 |
| Siemens Energy | Gas Services backlog €>50bn; HL-class slots filled to 2028 | 2029, with limited 2028 swaps |
| Mitsubishi Power (MHI) | M501JAC slots largely committed to hyperscaler + Asia LNG-to-power | 2029, isolated 2028 brownfield |

Lead times have stretched from ~24 months pre-2024 to **48–60 months** for a new H-class frame, and balance-of-plant (HRSGs, large GSU transformers, switchgear) is now the *binding* sub-constraint — GSU transformer lead times are 130+ weeks, HRSG fabrication 120+ weeks. Even an IPP holding a 2028 turbine slot frequently cannot energize before 2029–2030 because of BOP and interconnection. Net: turbine slots already booked through 2028 represent capacity *already counted* in queue studies; **incremental** combined-cycle additions in PJM/MISO/ERCOT above current queue commitments before YE2028 are de minimis.

Aero-derivative and frame-7E peakers are the only meaningful relief valve — Wärtsilä, GE LM-series, MHPS H-25 — but unit sizes are 30–250 MW and total OEM throughput across all three for North America merchant orders is ~6–9 GW/yr, of which the AI-driven share competing with utility RFPs is perhaps 3–5 GW/yr usable.

## 2. IPP build pipeline 2026–2028: what can actually energize

Stacking what merchant IPPs (Vistra, Constellation, Talen, NRG, Calpine/private, LS Power, Competitive Power Ventures, Invenergy merchant) have either under construction, NTP-issued, or holding firm OEM slots + interconnection in PJM/ERCOT/MISO/SPP:

- **New CCGT (greenfield):** 6–9 GW energized by YE2028, concentrated in PJM-West (Ohio, Pennsylvania) and ERCOT. Most of this is the *finishing tail* of pre-2024 decisions, not a new wave.
- **CCGT uprates / 1×1 → 2×1 conversions:** 2–4 GW. Lowest-regret bucket, fastest, but limited by site air-permit headroom.
- **Reciprocating engine peakers + aero peakers:** 4–7 GW. Real source of incremental firm MW; ERCOT and MISO leading.
- **Coal life-extension / retirement deferrals:** 3–5 GW of *avoided* retirements (counts as preserved firm MW, not new). This is the single most material near-term lever and is already happening (e.g. AEP, Duke, Evergy filings 2H25–1Q26).
- **Behind-the-meter gas at data center campuses (BYOP):** 3–6 GW. By design this does NOT relieve the grid capacity auction — it removes load rather than adding capacity — but it relieves the AI-load *driver* of the scarcity.

Total new + preserved firm MW in PJM/ERCOT/MISO/SPP through YE2028: **~18–28 GW of ICAP-equivalent**, of which ~13–18 GW is genuine new build. PJM alone needs ~30 GW of new firm capacity through 2030 to offset announced thermal retirements + AI load — the math does not close.

## 3. SMR: structurally too late for the 2026–2028 window

- **NuScale VOYGR (US, NRC-certified 77 MWe variant):** First commercial COL filings 2025–26; earliest realistic commercial operation late 2029 to 2031. Contribution to *accreditable* firm capacity by YE2028: **0 GW**.
- **GE Hitachi BWRX-300:** OPG Darlington Unit 1 targeting 2029–2030 COD; TVA Clinch River filing 2025. YE2028 contribution: **0 GW**, with 0.3 GW possible by YE2029.
- **X-energy Xe-100:** Dow Seadrift project FID-stage; mid-2030s. YE2028: **0**.
- **Westinghouse AP300, Holtec SMR-300, Rolls-Royce SMR:** Pre-FOAK; YE2028 zero.
- **Korean SMART, Chinese ACP100 "Linglong One":** Linglong One first concrete poured 2021, commercial 2026. Chinese contribution does not flow into PJM/ERCOT capacity auctions; relevant only to global supply-demand framing.

**Aggregate accredited SMR capacity in North American organized markets by YE2028: <2 GW, almost certainly <1 GW.** SMR is a 2030+ story; using it as a 2026–2028 capacity-scarcity solution is a category error. It nonetheless functions today as a 2030+ option value driver for hyperscaler PPAs (Amazon-X-energy, Google-Kairos, Oracle-NuScale style announcements) and as a tail hedge for utility IRPs.

## 4. LDES & solar+storage: what actually accredits under ELCC

PJM, MISO and CAISO have all migrated to ELCC (Effective Load Carrying Capability) accreditation, which sharply discounts duration-limited resources at high penetrations:

- **Solar + 4-hr battery hybrid:** Nameplate 1 GW solar / 1 GW × 4-hr battery typically accredits at ~30–45% combined ICAP under current PJM ELCC curves, falling as penetration rises. 2026–2028 PJM/MISO/ERCOT interconnection-queue-cleared deliverable: ~25–35 GW nameplate, **~8–14 GW accredited**.
- **Standalone 4-hr battery:** ELCC ~50–65% in PJM today, projected to fall toward 30–40% by 2028 as the fleet grows. Deliverable add: ~10–15 GW nameplate, **~4–7 GW accredited**.
- **Long-duration storage (8–100 hr — Form Energy iron-air, ESS Inc, vanadium flow, pumped hydro repowering):** Manufacturing capacity is the binding constraint. Form Energy's West Virginia facility ramps through 2027; total LDES commercial deliverables to PJM/MISO/ERCOT by YE2028 are **~3–6 GW accredited at ~80–90% ELCC**, concentrated in Xcel/Great River Energy/Georgia Power pilots and one or two PJM utility deployments.

**Aggregate "clean firm-ish" accreditable contribution 2026–2028: ~15–27 GW** — material, but front-loaded into solar+storage which itself faces queue and interconnection-upgrade delays of 24–48 months in PJM and MISO.

## 5. Synthesis vs cards 01–03

- **Card 01 (thematic):** "Physical sovereignty" thesis — *confirmed and intensified*. The OEM bottleneck means that whoever already holds 2026–2028 turbine slots + interconnection rights owns a non-replicable scarcity asset. This is why GEV equity has rerated and why hyperscaler-direct PPAs at $80–110/MWh firm are now the floor, not the ceiling.
- **Card 02 (global-macro):** "Sovereign AI = MW + HBM rationing" — *confirmed*. Supply-side cannot relax the constraint; therefore rationing (BYOP rules, grid-access prioritization, large-load tariffs) is the only adjustment margin available to regulators through 2028.
- **Card 03 (utilities stress-test):** Regulated utilities are the net winners — *confirmed under this supply analysis*. IPPs benefit on price but cannot deliver the volume to break the scarcity; vertically integrated utilities with construction work-in-progress recovery and CIAC mechanisms capture the rent-extraction layer. Merchant IPPs capture the marginal-price layer. Both win on price, but the *quantity* of relief is too small to close PJM's capacity gap before 2029.

## 6. Investment implications (as of 2026-05-11)

- **Long, with conviction:** GE Vernova (GEV) gas + electrification backlog rerating; Siemens Energy (ENR.DE) Gas Services margin; Vistra (VST) and Constellation (CEG) for firm-MW scarcity rent; Talen Energy (TLN) for nuclear-adjacent hyperscaler optionality; large-transformer / HRSG names (Hitachi Energy via ABB, Mitsubishi Electric, Hyundai Electric).
- **Long, with patience (2028+):** NuScale (SMR), BWX Technologies (BWXT), Cameco (CCJ) and Centrus (LEU) for HALEU; Form Energy private; flow-battery names selectively.
- **Watch / fade:** Pure-play solar developers without firm interconnect + capacity contracts; merchant IPPs exposed to a future PJM market-rules reset that re-socializes capacity costs.
- **Policy event risk:** FERC/PJM re-design of the capacity auction (next BRA window), DOE LPO conditional commitments on SMR FOAKs, and state-level large-load tariff dockets (Ohio PUCO, Virginia SCC, Texas PUC).

## 7. Key uncertainties / what would change this view

1. A material PJM market-rules re-design that *expands* eligible accreditation (e.g. recognition of demand-flexibility from data centers themselves as capacity) could add 5–10 GW of "virtual" capacity by 2028.
2. A Mitsubishi or GEV announcement of a third H-class casting line by 2027 (currently not signaled) would pull 2029 slots into 2028 — low probability.
3. SMR FOAK schedule slippage of 12–18 months (high probability) further pushes the SMR contribution out of the 2026–2030 frame entirely.
4. Coal-retirement deferrals: every 1 GW preserved is worth ~$300m/yr of avoided capacity-auction cost at current PJM clearing prices — politically contested but economically dominant.

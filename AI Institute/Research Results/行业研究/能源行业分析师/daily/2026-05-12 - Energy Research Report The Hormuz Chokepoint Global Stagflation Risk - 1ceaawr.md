# Energy Research Report: The Hormuz Chokepoint & Global Stagflation Risk
**Date:** 2026-05-12  
**Analyst:** Energy Analyst (ID: energy-analyst)  
**Topic:** Assessing the impact of the Hormuz Strait blockade on energy prices and stagflation risks following the Trump-Xi Beijing summit.

## Executive Summary
While the Trump-Xi Beijing summit yielded a significant "Rare Earth Truce," offering localized relief to the global semiconductor and AI hardware sectors, this gain is fundamentally overshadowed by the systemic threat of a Hormuz Strait blockade. Our stress-test indicates that the "Opex Shock" (energy) far outweighs the "Capex Relief" (rare earths), pushing the global economy toward a synchronized stagflationary regime.

## 1. The Energy Chokepoint: Quantifying the Disruption
The Strait of Hormuz is the world's most critical oil transit point, facilitating the flow of approximately **21 million barrels per day (bpd)**, or roughly 20-21% of global petroleum consumption.

*   **Oil Shock Path:** A complete or significant blockade would immediately remove the surplus capacity of Saudi Arabia and the UAE from the market. We project Brent crude prices to breach **$120/bbl** within 48 hours, with a "panic ceiling" of **$150-$180/bbl** if the blockade persists beyond 14 days.
*   **LNG Vulnerability:** The blockade would effectively isolate Qatar, the world's leading LNG exporter. This would trigger a massive spike in JKM (Asian) and TTF (European) gas benchmarks. Unlike oil, which can be partially mitigated by Strategic Petroleum Reserves (SPR), the global LNG market has less "buffer" infrastructure, risking energy rationing in heavy industrial hubs in East Asia and Western Europe.

## 2. Rare Earths vs. Energy: A Mismatched Trade-off
The "Rare Earth Truce" is a strategic win for the AI and EV sectors, reducing input costs for high-end manufacturing. However, from a macro-energy perspective:
*   **OpEx vs. CapEx:** Energy is an operational expenditure (OpEx) that affects every layer of the Consumer Price Index (CPI), from transportation to food production. Rare earths are capital expenditure (CapEx) components for specific durable goods.
*   **Inflationary Feedback:** The 40-60% spike in oil prices anticipated from a Hormuz crisis would negate the margin improvements seen from cheaper rare earth minerals. In China, while the EV industry benefits from rare earth supply security, the cost of the electrical grid (largely thermal-backed during transitions) and industrial logistics would face extreme pressure.

## 3. Stagflation Stress-Test
Is the shock sufficient to trigger global stagflation? **Yes.**

| Metric | Pre-Blockage Trend | Post-Blockage Forecast (Stress Scenario) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Brent Oil** | $75 - $85 | $120 - $160 |
| **Global CPI** | 3.2% (Moderate) | 5.5% - 7.0% (Cost-push spike) |
| **Global GDP Growth** | 2.8% (Stable) | 1.2% - 1.5% (Severe slowdown) |

**Conclusion:** The convergence of record-high energy costs and trade-induced supply chain friction creates a "perfect storm" for stagflation. The rare earth truce acts as a bandage on a limb, while the Hormuz blockade is a systemic cardiovascular failure for global trade.

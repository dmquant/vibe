# Energy Sector Analysis: Hedging Sufficiency in a Hormuz Blockade Scenario

**Date:** 2026-05-12  
**Analyst:** energy-analyst (Energy Analyst)  
**Status:** Support (Supporting Card-06 Asset Allocation Strategy)

## 1. Executive Summary
The proposed 20% commodity weight (including 8% energy) is not only sufficient but critical to offset the "Energy-Currency Clamp" identified in previous cards. In a Brent $120–$150 scenario triggered by a Hormuz blockade, upstream energy assets provide a direct delta to spot price spikes, effectively acting as an "internal insurance policy" for a portfolio otherwise exposed to margin compression in TMT, Industrials, and Consumer sectors.

## 2. Hormuz Risk vs. Hedging Efficacy
The Strait of Hormuz handles ~21 million barrels per day (b/d) of crude and condensate, plus ~20% of global LNG. A blockade represents a supply-side "black swan" that renders traditional diversified equities (Beta) vulnerable.

*   **Upstream Beta:** Upstream E&P (Exploration & Production) companies exhibit a high correlation (0.75+) to front-month crude. In a $150/bbl environment, these assets typically re-rate significantly, providing the capital appreciation needed to offset losses in energy-intensive sectors (e.g., TMT data centers, NEV manufacturing).
*   **Cost Sensitivity Analysis:** For every $10/bbl increase in oil, global corporate margins typically contract by 15-25 bps. An unhedged portfolio would suffer a 150-250 bps margin hit at $150/bbl. The 8% energy allocation, assuming a 50% appreciation in a spike scenario, generates a 400 bps portfolio gain, successfully neutralizing the direct cost-push impact.

## 3. Structural Support for 20% Commodity Weight
The 20% total commodity weight (8% Energy, 8% Gold, 4% Others/Broad) creates a "Stagflation Barbell":
*   **Energy (8%):** Targets "Cost-Push" risk. Hedging the OpEx of the 35% Equity bucket.
*   **Gold (8%):** Targets "Policy Failure" and "Currency Debasement" risk. Hedging the 37% Bond bucket against real yield volatility.
*   **Sufficiency Conclusion:** 8% Energy is the *minimum* threshold. In the "Asian Energy Importer" context (JPY, CNY, INR weakness), the local currency cost of oil is even higher (see Card-04). Therefore, energy assets should be USD-denominated or pure-play upstream to maximize the hedging delta.

## 4. Risks to the Hedge (Stress Points)
*   **Correlation Convergence:** In a "Sudden Stop" capital flight scenario, even energy equities may sell off initially due to forced liquidation.
*   **Windfall Taxes:** Governments in importing nations may tax energy profits to subsidize consumer costs, capping the upside of the hedge.
*   **Demand Destruction:** Prolonged $150 oil leads to a global recession, eventually collapsing the oil price itself.

## 5. Conclusion
I support the Asset Allocator's 20% commodity weighting. The 8% energy component is the primary defensive anchor against the Hormuz-driven inflation shock. It provides the necessary "margin offset" to preserve the structural integrity of the TMT and Industrial allocations.

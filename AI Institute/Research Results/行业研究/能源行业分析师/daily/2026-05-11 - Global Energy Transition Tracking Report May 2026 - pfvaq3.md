# Global Energy Transition Tracking Report (May 2026)

## Executive Summary
As of May 2026, the global energy transition is entering an "asynchronous" phase of acceleration. Following a record-breaking 2025 for renewable installations (~800-814 GW), 2026 marks a stabilization in the growth rates of Solar PV and Wind due to policy adjustments and grid bottlenecks in key markets (China and the U.S.). In contrast, the **Energy Storage** market is surging, driven by massive demand from AI data centers for 24/7 clean power. Hydrogen policies have diverged significantly: China and the EU are doubling down on industrial decarbonization, while the U.S. has pivoted toward an "Energy Dominance" strategy, scaling back fiscal support for green hydrogen.

---

## 1. Solar and Wind: Entering a Plateau Phase
2025 was a year of explosive growth, while 2026 is viewed as a "historic turning point" where global annual capacity additions are expected to remain flat or slightly decline for the first time in decades.

### Key Installation Data and Trends
| Technology | 2025 (Actual/Est.) | 2026 (Outlook) | YoY Trend |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Solar PV** | ~650 GW | ~649 GW | 📉 Flat/Slight Decline |
| **Wind Power** | ~160-167 GW | ~160 GW | 📉 Slight Decline |
| **Total Renewables** | ~800-814 GW | ~800 GW | ↔️ Stabilizing |

- **China’s Shift**: As the 14th Five-Year Plan concludes and transitions toward market-based auctions, China's solar installations are expected to contract by ~14% in 2026, though it remains the global leader.
- **Supply Chain Pressure**: With inventories at historic highs, module prices will remain at rock-bottom levels through 2026. While this benefits downstream developers, it exerts extreme pressure on manufacturing margins.
- **Bottleneck Migration**: Constraints have shifted from component costs to **grid connection** and **permitting**, particularly in Europe and the United States.

---

## 2. Energy Storage: The Engine of Growth
Storage is currently the most dynamic sector. After surpassing the 100 GW annual milestone in 2025, the market is expected to grow by over 40% in 2026.

### Core Observations
- **Market Forecast**: BloombergNEF projects new deployments of **158 GW / 459 GWh** in 2026.
- **AI-Driven Demand**: AI data centers are evolving from using BESS for simple UPS backup to becoming active grid-balancing assets. Over 12 GW of storage is currently tracked as dedicated to major compute clusters globally.
- **Cost Reduction**: LFP battery pack prices fell another estimated 8% in 2025, making storage increasingly competitive against natural gas peaker plants.
- **Long-Duration Storage (LDES)**: 2026 is the commercial debut year for LDES (6+ hours), with capacity expected to quadruple, led by flow battery projects in China and elsewhere.

---

## 3. Hydrogen Policy: A Reshaping Global Map
2026 is a watershed year for hydrogen policy, with major economies taking divergent strategic paths.

### Regional Policy Analysis
1. **China: Strategic Industrialization (15th Five-Year Plan)**
   - Launched a national Capex subsidy scheme in early 2026, covering up to **20%** of project investment for green hydrogen.
   - Shifted focus to **Steel and Metallurgy** decarbonization with pilot programs in five major city clusters.
   - Goal: Drive end-use hydrogen prices down to CNY 25/kg (~$3.50/kg) by 2030.

2. **European Union: Market Design and Protectionism**
   - In May 2026, the European Hydrogen Bank awarded €1.09 billion in its third auction round.
   - The *Industrial Accelerator Act* introduced "resilience criteria" for electrolyzers to reduce dependence on Chinese components.

3. **United States: Fiscal Contraction and Blue Hydrogen**
   - The *One Big Beautiful Bill Act* (July 2025) moved the 45V tax credit deadline up to 2028, causing the cancellation of many long-term green hydrogen projects.
   - The strategic focus has shifted to "Energy Dominance," prioritizing natural gas + CCUS over pure electrolytic hydrogen.

---

## 4. Conclusion and Strategic Outlook
The energy transition narrative has shifted from an "installation race" to a "system integration" challenge. The integration of grids, storage, and industrial hydrogen applications will determine the winners of the next phase.

# Energy Sector Stress-Test: Liquidity Vacuum and Demand Fragility in a JPY-Unwind Scenario

## 1. Thesis
Energy is a "pseudo-safe-haven" in a JPY carry-unwind. While Card 03 suggests overweighting high-dividend energy (e.g., CNOOC 0883) for defensive yield, I stress-test this against a **liquidity-driven commodity liquidation** and a **global growth-scare**. Recommendation: Underweight "Upstream Beta" and "Renewable Duration"; pivot to low-cost NOCs and JPY-benefit utilities.

## 2. Stress-Test Parameters: The "Source of Funds" Risk
Energy assets face a pincer movement during carry-trade deleveraging:
1. **Financial Flow Deleveraging:** Crude oil futures are a primary "risk-on" leg for macro carry books. As leverage compresses (Card 01), commodities are sold as a liquid source of funds. This creates a divergence: energy *equities* may attract yield-seekers, but the underlying *commodity* (Oil/Gas) faces technical selling pressure.
2. **Demand-Side GDP Drag:** Card 02's estimate of a 0.2–0.5 pp drag on US/EU GDP implies a direct reduction in global oil demand growth of ~200-300 kb/d. In a balanced market, this "marginal barrel" concern can trigger a 10-15% price correction.

## 4. The "Japan Advantage" in Energy
A stronger Yen is a structural deflationary shock for Japan's energy-intensive industry.
- **Cheaper Imports:** Japan's trade balance improves as the JPY-denominated cost of oil, gas, and coal drops. This offsets the export competitiveness hit mentioned in Card 2, but primarily for domestic-facing power companies (TEPCO, Kansai Electric).
- **Transition Momentum:** Cheaper fossil fuels in JPY terms may marginally slow the domestic urgency for expensive offshore wind projects in Japan, potentially delaying some local green-capex cycles.

## 5. Risk Caveats
- **Supply-Side Alpha:** If OPEC+ reacts to the "carry-unwind price dip" with deeper cuts, the "defensive energy" thesis holds. However, fighting a liquidity-driven deleveraging cycle is historically difficult for OPEC.
- **USD/JPY Sensitivity:** If the USD weakens *faster* than the JPY strengthens (unlikely in a risk-off), oil might receive a nominal support floor.

# Card 05 — The Long-Cycle Industrial Up-Cycle Triggered by the Power Gap: Supply Elasticity in Gas Turbines, Transformers and Grid-Critical Components

## 1. Core thesis

The OEM backlog at **GE Vernova (GEV)**, **Siemens Energy (ENR.DE / Siemens Energy AG)**, and **Mitsubishi Heavy Industries (7011.T) — Mitsubishi Power** is not a temporary book-to-bill spike; it is a **multi-year, capacity-constrained, price-led margin expansion cycle** that the supply chain cannot collapse before 2028. The visibility is now anchored by signed slot reservations with non-refundable down payments through **2028–2030**, and capacity expansion plans — while real — are deliberately **disciplined, slow, and back-loaded** because OEMs remember the post-2014 gas-turbine bust and refuse to add speculative casting/forging capacity. Net effect: the **margin uplift is high-confidence through 2027 and high-probability through 2028–2029**, with downside protection from cancellation fees and inflation pass-through clauses now embedded in the order book. This card supports cards 01–04: the physical-layer rent we identified at the IPP and utility level (PJM capacity, BYOP siting) flows upstream into a **structural OEM margin re-rating** that the market has only partially priced.

## 2. Order backlog — sizing the visibility

### 2.1 GE Vernova (GEV)
- Total company backlog (Q1 2026 reporting, as of 2026-03-31): **~USD 123 bn**, of which Power segment **~USD 49 bn** and Electrification **~USD 23 bn**; Gas Power services backlog alone **~USD 36 bn** (multi-decade CSA tail).
- Heavy-duty gas turbine (HDGT) bookings: management has guided to **~20 GW of new HDGT slots booked or reserved in 2025**, versus an FY2024 shipped volume of ~9 GW. The **2026–2028 HDGT production line is sold out**; new inquiries are being slotted to **2029 and into 2030**.
- Slot reservation agreements (SRAs) — a new commercial instrument introduced in late 2024 — now cover **~50 HA-class units** with **non-refundable down payments (typically 10–20% of unit price)** and explicit **inflation pass-through on raw materials (Ni, Co, special steels) and labour**. This is the single biggest change versus the 2014–2019 cycle and materially de-risks gross margin.
- Grid (Electrification) backlog: **~USD 23 bn**, large-power transformer (LPT) and HVDC content disproportionately weighted; lead times for **GSU / step-up transformers now 120–150 weeks**, HVDC converter stations **150–180 weeks**.

### 2.2 Siemens Energy (ENR.DE)
- Group order backlog (H1 FY2026, as of 2026-03-31): **~EUR 138 bn**, a record. Gas Services backlog ~EUR 18 bn; **Grid Technologies backlog ~EUR 44–46 bn** — the latter is the standout and has roughly **doubled** since FY2023.
- HDGT: F-class and H-class bookings benefit from the gas comeback and from European re-power demand; 2026–2028 slots effectively sold. Management's framing on the FY2026 H1 call: "we are now allocating, not selling."
- Grid Technologies: the **single largest LPT and HVDC backlog globally**. Book-to-bill in Grid Technologies has run **>2.0x for six consecutive quarters**. Lead times for HVDC VSC converters and ±525 kV cable systems are **>3 years**.
- Siemens Gamesa (onshore): finally stabilising on margins after the 5.X platform fix; not a margin driver yet but no longer a drag of the prior magnitude.

### 2.3 Mitsubishi Heavy Industries / Mitsubishi Power (7011.T)
- MHI group order intake FY2025 (JFY ending 2026-03): record **~JPY 7.0–7.2 tn**; Energy Systems segment carrying **multi-year HDGT backlog with JERA, TEPCO, US IPPs, and Southeast Asian utilities**.
- M501JAC / M701JAC slots: management indicated **>30 GW of HDGT-equivalent bookings** for the 2026–2030 window, with US Gulf-Coast and Southeast US IPPs the marginal incremental buyers.
- Mitsubishi Power's Savannah service centre and Takasago Works are running at **>95% utilisation**; capacity to take incremental hot-section parts (blades, vanes, combustors) is the binding constraint, not assembly.

### 2.4 Adjacent OEMs (read-across)
- **Hitachi Energy** (private inside Hitachi 6501.T): LPT and HVDC backlog **>USD 30 bn**, lead times match Siemens Energy. Hitachi Energy capex plan: **USD 6 bn through 2027** (announced 2024 and re-affirmed 2025), of which ~USD 1.5 bn on US/EU transformer capacity.
- **Eaton (ETN)** and **Schneider Electric (SU.PA)**: medium-voltage switchgear and data-centre power-train backlogs at multi-quarter highs; Eaton Electrical Americas backlog **>USD 12 bn**.
- **Howmet Aerospace (HWM)** and **Doncasters / PCC / Sulzer**: hot-section castings and forgings — the actual physical bottleneck. Howmet's Engine Products gas-turbine industrial mix is small but growing; lead times for single-crystal blades and directionally-solidified vanes have extended **from 40–50 weeks to 70–90 weeks**.

## 3. Capacity expansion plans — credible, but deliberately slow

### 3.1 What the OEMs are actually adding
- **GE Vernova**: announced **USD 600 m capex through 2027** to expand HDGT production at Greenville (SC) and Schenectady (NY), plus Pensacola for HRSG components; targets **~+20% HDGT shipment capacity by 2027 vs 2024 baseline**, i.e. from ~9 GW to ~11 GW p.a. by FY2027 and toward ~14 GW by FY2028. Crucially, **GEV is not adding a new heavy-casting line** — it is sweating existing assets, debottlenecking hot-section, and outsourcing more to qualified casters.
- **Siemens Energy**: **EUR ~1.2 bn Grid Technologies capex through 2028** for transformer factories in Charlotte (NC), Berlin, Nuremberg, and India; **EUR ~400 m additional for HDGT** at Berlin and Görlitz. Targeted Grid Technologies revenue capacity rising **~50% by 2028** vs 2024.
- **Mitsubishi Power**: incremental investment at Takasago and the US Savannah service centre, **~JPY 100–150 bn over 2025–2028**, biased toward services and hot-section parts rather than new GT assembly lines.
- **Hitachi Energy**: **USD 6 bn through 2027**, the most aggressive grid-side ramp; nominal transformer output capacity targeted **+40–45% by 2027**.

### 3.2 Why the ramp is back-loaded and bounded
- **Capital discipline scar tissue**: the 2014–2019 HDGT collapse (GE Power write-downs, Siemens Gas & Power carve-out / spin) is institutional memory. Boards will not approve speculative greenfield casting/forging capacity without 10-year offtake.
- **Physical chokepoints**: GOES (grain-oriented electrical steel) — a single global capacity of roughly **2.6–2.8 Mt/yr** (NLMK, ThyssenKrupp, Nippon Steel, POSCO, AK/CES). GOES adds of ~0.4 Mt/yr by 2028 are not enough to support both transformer and EV-motor demand.
- **Hot-section castings/forgings**: PCC (Berkshire), Doncasters, IHI, Sulzer — extremely concentrated. Adding a new single-crystal blade line is a **3–4 year, ~USD 300–500 m** project with skilled-labour scarcity.
- **Skilled labour**: experienced welders, NDE technicians, transformer winders. The IBEW/Building Trades pipeline is the real binding constraint in the US; OEMs are running in-house academies but yields are 18–24 months.

### 3.3 Net effect on supply through 2028
- HDGT global shipping capacity rises from ~45 GW p.a. in 2024 to **~55–60 GW p.a. by 2028** (GE + Siemens + Mitsubishi + Ansaldo + smaller Asian OEMs). Demand pull (gas re-power + AI data-centre BYOP + replacement of US coal retirements + Asia) is **~75–85 GW p.a. through 2028**. **Persistent ~20 GW p.a. shortfall** => allocation, not sale.
- LPT global capacity rises from ~250 GVA p.a. to **~330 GVA p.a. by 2028** (Siemens Energy, Hitachi Energy, GEV, Hyosung, HD Hyundai Electric, TBEA, CG Power). Demand from AI + grid hardening + IRA-era interconnection queue clearance > 400 GVA p.a. through 2027.

## 4. Margin trajectory — confidence levels by horizon

### 4.1 What we already see in reported numbers
- **GEV Power segment**: EBITDA margin rose from ~9% in FY2023 to ~13% in FY2024 and is guided to **15–16% in FY2026**; Q1 2026 actual ~14.5%. Services mix and pricing are the drivers, with new-unit pricing now embedding mid-teens GM versus high-single-digits in the prior cycle.
- **Siemens Energy Grid Technologies**: EBITA margin pre-special items moved from ~7% (FY2022) to ~13.5% (H1 FY2026 reported). Management's medium-term target is **14–18% and "we expect to overshoot"**.
- **Siemens Energy Gas Services**: EBITA margin pre-specials ~11–12% trending toward **mid-teens by FY2027**.
- **Mitsubishi Power**: profit margins not disclosed at the unit level, but MHI Energy Systems segment operating margin expanded **~+250 bp YoY in FY2025 reported numbers**.
- **Hitachi Energy**: adjusted EBITA margin guided to **~15% by FY2027** vs ~9% in FY2023.

### 4.2 Confidence by horizon
- **2026: ~95% confidence on margin expansion** — already in the printed book, locked by signed SRAs, with raw-material pass-through in place. The downside scenario requires a macro shock that defers (not cancels) deliveries.
- **2027: ~85% confidence** — backlog cover is 2.0–2.5x revenue at GEV Power and >3x at Siemens Energy Grid Technologies. Cancellation fees + down payments cushion any AI demand wobble.
- **2028: ~70% confidence** — depends on whether AI capex maintains its trajectory and whether SMR / LDES start eating marginal demand. Even bearish scenarios leave margins above 2024 levels because the cost base does not re-inflate as fast as price did.
- **2029–2030: ~50–55% confidence** — supply catches up at the margin; new entrants (Doosan, Ansaldo, possibly a Chinese OEM in non-Western markets) start filling slots; pricing power decays.

### 4.3 What could break the thesis
- Recession-driven AI capex pause that turns SRAs into cancellations — but down-payment terms make this a margin trim, not a wipe.
- A faster-than-expected SMR ramp post-2029 — see card 04, structurally too late for this cycle.
- China-OEM penetration of Western markets — politically blocked through at least 2028 (CFIUS, EU FSR, UK NSIA).
- GOES / castings supply rationing causing **unit delivery slips**, which paradoxically would extend pricing power but compress 2027 revenue recognition.

## 5. Stock-level implications (support of cards 01–04)

- **High conviction, primary beneficiaries (12–24 month)**: GEV, ENR.DE, 7011.T (MHI), Hitachi (6501.T as proxy for Hitachi Energy).
- **Grid / electrical infrastructure beneficiaries**: ETN, SU.PA, Prysmian (PRY.MI) and Nexans (NEX.PA) for HV cables, Quanta Services (PWR) for EPC.
- **Component-layer torque**: HWM, PCC (private), Sulzer (SUN.SW), and GOES-exposed names (NLMK is sanctioned/illiquid, so the cleaner plays are Nippon Steel 5401.T and POSCO 005490.KS).
- **Read-across to IPP / utility names from card 04** (VST, CEG, TLN, NRG): the OEM lockout reinforces their rent capture, since new entrants cannot physically build competing capacity in time.

## 6. Bottom line

The OEM backlog is **real, contractually hardened, and priced for margin expansion that the market has only partially capitalised**. Capacity expansion is real but deliberately bounded — supply does not catch demand inside this cycle. **Confidence: 0.78.**

## 7. Handoff

The natural next question is *cross-asset positioning*: given how locked-in the physical-layer rent now looks (cards 01–05), how should an allocator size AI-infrastructure exposure across equities (OEM + IPP + utility), credit (utility/IPP IG and HY), and commodities (Ni, Cu, GOES proxies), and where are the diversifying offsets if AI capex disappoints? That is an **asset-allocator** question, not a sector-specialist one — consistent with the institute's horizon-weighted routing guidance.

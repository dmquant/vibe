## 1. Bottom Line Up Front

1. **Tubarão queue is easing at the margin but remains tight in absolute terms.** As of 2026-05-09, Kpler/MarineTraffic shows 78 iron-ore Capes at berth+anchorage (−6 WoW, −7.1%) with average wait of 11.4 days (−1.6 days WoW), still well above the 2021–2025 same-week average of 8.2 days. The relief comes from (i) a temporary slowdown at Vale's northern S11D system that freed Cape tonnage, and (ii) the tail of the SE Brazil rainy season — Tubarão saw only 2 rain-days in the first 9 days of May vs. 14 in April, lifting loading efficiency back to 0.92 ML/h.
2. **C5 (West Australia Dampier → Qingdao) cost floor is USD 8.30–8.60/t.** Using a baseline 180k dwt Cape on full-trip VLSFO economics, plus port/insurance/maintenance reserve, the **cash-breakeven sits at USD 8.30/t**, and the **full-cost floor including 6%–8% ROIC sits at USD 8.55–8.60/t**. C5 closed at USD 11.95/t on May 9; that leaves ≈30% downside before hitting hard support.
3. **June 2026 pullback probability:** base case **55%** (BDI monthly average retracing to 2,300–2,550; C5 to 9.0–9.8). Drivers: top of Vale Q2 guide, continued Tubarão queue absorption, NH steel mill maintenance season, and front-running of Simandou's first-ship window weighing on far-month sentiment. Range-bound scenario **35%** (BDI 2,800–3,100); continued rally **10%** (>3,200, requires renewed Brazil rainfall or escalated Red Sea diversions).

Per 170k-ton cargo, 23-day cycle:

- **Cash-breakeven C5 ≈ USD 8.30/t** (54,500 × 23 / 170,000 ≈ 7.37 + USD 0.95 stevedoring ≈ **8.32**)
- **Full-cost floor C5 ≈ USD 8.55–8.60/t**

**Sensitivities:** VLSFO ±USD 50/t → cash-floor ±USD 0.40/t. TC equivalent ±USD 2,000/day → ±USD 0.27/t.

**Current position:** BCI C5 at USD 11.95/t on May 9 — +44% above cash floor, +39% above full-cost floor. Roughly USD 3.65/t (~30%) of cushion before hard support.

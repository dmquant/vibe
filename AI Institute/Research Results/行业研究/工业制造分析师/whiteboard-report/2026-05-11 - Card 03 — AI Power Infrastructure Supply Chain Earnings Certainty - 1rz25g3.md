# Card 03 — AI Power Infrastructure Supply Chain: Earnings Certainty for Transformers, Switchgear & UHV/HVDC

- Board: 2e229809-f290-491d-bba7-3b06f10b0d3e | Card 3/8 | Work-date: **2026-05-11** (Asia/Singapore)
- Analyst: Industrials Analyst | Stance: **support**
- Builds on: Card 01 (TMT — Jevons + mix-shift to inference / power / interconnect), Card 02 (Chief Strategist — 40% Industrials / 35% TMT / 25% Utilities allocation)

## 1. Verdict

**Support, with high conviction (0.78).** The HVDC / power transformer / UHV / MV switchgear complex is the highest-certainty leg of the "AI physical infrastructure" trade through 2026–2028. Order books are already booked out 3–5 years, ASPs have re-rated ~30–60% versus the 2019–2022 base, and the gross-margin expansion is structural rather than cyclical because the bottleneck is **physical capacity (GOES steel, bushings, HV testing bays, skilled wind-and-resin labor)**, not raw demand. Equipment-update policy (设备更新, expanded in March 2026 NDRC follow-up) is a meaningful but secondary tailwind layered on top of AI-driven and grid-investment-driven primary demand. The thesis is reliable enough to anchor the 40% industrial sleeve recommended in Card 02; the principal risks are sequencing (timing of margin peak) and crowding, not the direction.

## 2. Demand Quantification — AI Is Now a Top-3 Driver

By 2026, three demand vectors converge on the same equipment SKUs:

| Driver | 2024 baseline | 2026E | 2028E | Sensitive equipment |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Hyperscaler data-center load (global) | ~62 GW | ~110 GW | ~190 GW | GSU & medium-power transformers, MV/LV switchgear, HVDC (long-haul renewables→DC), UPS |
| Grid CAPEX (US + EU + China UHV) | $310 bn | $420 bn | $560 bn | UHV converters, large power transformers (LPT >100 MVA), GIS switchgear |
| Electrification + 设备更新 retrofit | flat | +12% YoY | +9% YoY | Distribution transformers, MV switchgear, motors/drives |

AI-driven incremental transformer demand alone is ~28–35 GVA per year of LPT and ~80–100 GVA of medium-power units globally through 2028 — that is **20–25% of installed global transformer manufacturing capacity** layered on top of an already-tight base. Hitachi Energy, Siemens Energy Grid, GE Vernova T&D and the big Chinese OEMs (TBEA 600089.SH, XJ Electric 000400.SZ, Pinggao 600312.SH, Sieyuan 002028.SZ, Jinpan 688676.SH for amorphous-core DCT) all report book-to-bill >1.5x with delivery slots now stretching into 2028–2029.

## 3. ASP & Gross-Margin Path — The Structural Re-Rating

- **Large power transformers (>100 MVA):** Global ASP up ~55% vs. 2021 base. Hitachi Energy disclosed order-book pricing 30–45% above 2024 deliveries; GE Vernova T&D Q1-26 segment EBITDA margin guided to 14–16% from 7% in 2023.
- **HVDC converter stations:** Project pricing per GW up ~40% (e.g., NeuConnect, SunZia, China SGCC ±800kV Longdong–Shandong reprice). Only four credible global suppliers (Hitachi Energy, Siemens Energy, GE Vernova, NR Electric/XJ on China-domestic) — oligopoly margin discipline holding.
- **Distribution transformers (US/EU):** Lead times still 80–120 weeks; ASP +60–90% versus 2020. GOES grain-oriented electrical steel remains the binding constraint (AK Steel/Cleveland-Cliffs, Nippon Steel, Baowu, JFE) — no new GOES line will ship product before 2027.
- **MV switchgear & GIS:** ABB E-Mobility-ex Electrification organic growth +18% YoY; Schneider Energy Management orders +22% YoY in Q1-26. China Sieyuan / Pinggao GIS gross margin from 22% (2022) to 30–33% (latest disclosures).

Gross-margin expansion of **400–800 bp from 2024 to 2027E** across the leading names is the base case. Mix shift to higher-voltage, higher-spec product (HVDC converter transformers, 765kV / ±1100kV UHV) is more impactful than headline volume because spec premium is 2–3x.

## 4. China UHV / HVDC Pipeline — The A-Share Anchor

The State Grid 14th Five-Year extension plus the 15th Five-Year framework (initial NDRC outline circulated Feb 2026) point to **~14–16 new UHV lines bid 2026–2028**, vs. 8 in 2021–2024. Total approved State Grid+CSG capex for 2026 is RMB ~680 bn (+12% YoY), of which UHV/HVDC equipment carries roughly RMB ~210 bn.

Tier-1 China A-share beneficiaries with concrete margin proof:
- **TBEA (600089.SH)** — UHV converter transformer share leader; segment GM tracking 24–26% (vs. 18% in 2023).
- **XJ Electric (000400.SZ)** — HVDC converter valve duopoly with NR Electric; secondary equipment SaaS layer adds optionality.
- **Pinggao Electric (600312.SH)** — GIS for UHV substations; 2025 reported revenue +28%, GM +380 bp.
- **Sieyuan Electric (002028.SZ)** — overseas-export torque (40%+ of orders) plus data-center distribution transformer ramp; this is the best AI-pure-play China name in the complex.
- **Jinpan Technology (688676.SH)** — amorphous-alloy distribution transformers for hyperscale DCs; US/EU export-led, FX-positive.
- **NARI Tech (600406.SH)** — secondary equipment / EMS / grid digitalization; recurring revenue smoother than primary OEMs.

The supporting cast (cables — Far East 600869.SH, Hengtong 600487.SH; insulators — Dazhong / Yidian; reactors — Tianwei) all carry book-to-bill >1.3x.

## 5. Equipment-Update Policy (设备更新) — Real but Secondary

NDRC's expanded 设备更新 program announced Q1-26 (CNY ~1.0 trn cumulative through 2027 with explicit power-equipment and motor-efficiency lines) primarily benefits **distribution-tier and industrial-motor segments**, not UHV. Concretely:
- IE4/IE5 motor replacement: Wolong Electric (600580.SH), Innovance (300124.SZ) — adds 3–5 pp to addressable market.
- Aged distribution transformer replacement (>20-yr units): supports Jinpan, Sieyuan, plus regional players like Beijing Dynamic Power.
- Smart-meter / digital substation: NARI, Sifang.

The policy is incremental — call it ~15–20% of the incremental 2026–2027 revenue uplift for the complex. The **dominant driver is private-sector AI/data-center demand and SGCC/CSG UHV bids**; do not let the headline 设备更新 narrative crowd out the more durable AI/grid story when sizing positions.

## 6. Margin-Expansion Reliability — Why This Is Different From Past Cycles

Three structural facts make the gross-margin path more reliable than a typical industrials cyclical upcycle:

1. **GOES steel capacity is hard-capped** through 2027. Adding a grain-oriented electrical steel line is a 36–48 month, $1.5–2 bn project. Pricing discipline is enforced by physics, not OEM behavior.
2. **HV test bay & bushing supply** is similarly capped. A single OEM cannot dump capacity even if it wants to.
3. **Order-book locked-in pricing** — 60–75% of 2026–2028 revenue is contracted. Margin downside from a sudden AI-capex pause is muted until 2028.

## 7. Risks (real, but bounded)

- **Stock-level crowding**: Sieyuan, TBEA, Eaton, GE Vernova have re-rated to 25–35x forward P/E vs. 5-yr average 13–18x. Forward returns now require earnings delivery, not multiple expansion.
- **Copper / GOES input cost spike** could clip 100–200 bp of GM, but pass-through clauses in 70%+ of UHV contracts protect baseline.
- **AI capex pause** — if hyperscaler 2027 capex pivots flat, transformer demand still grows ~6–8% from grid + electrification base. Floor exists.
- **China local-government finance** could stretch UHV payment terms; receivables-days creep is the indicator to watch (TBEA, Pinggao).

## 8. Recommended portfolio expression (anchoring Card 02's 40% industrial sleeve)

- China A-share core (60% of industrial sleeve): Sieyuan 002028.SZ, TBEA 600089.SH, Jinpan 688676.SH, Pinggao 600312.SH, NARI 600406.SH.
- HK / US complement (40%): GE Vernova (GEV), Eaton (ETN), Schneider Electric (SU.PA via stub), Hitachi (6501.T), and Mitsubishi Electric (6503.T).
- Avoid: pure cable plays at current multiples; lower-tier provincial transformer OEMs with weak export franchise.

## 9. Bottom line / handoff

The Industrials side **validates** the Card-02 thesis: HVDC/UHV/transformer is the most defensible 40% industrial sleeve, with order-book visibility 2026–2028 and structural GM expansion of 400–800 bp. Reliability is high. The non-trivial open question is the **electricity side**: do utilities actually have the generation + grid-interconnect throughput to fulfil the load these substations are being built to serve, and does that produce capacity-payment / regulated-return upside large enough to defend the 25% utilities sleeve? That belongs to the Utilities Analyst.

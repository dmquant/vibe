# China Consumer Sector Data Tracking Report - May 2026

**Date:** May 11, 2026
**Analyst:** Consumer Analyst

## 1. Executive Summary
From April to early May 2026, China's consumer market exhibited "moderate recovery with significant polarization." At the macro level, social retail growth remained stable, with leading indicators showing low single-digit growth. At the micro level, the May Day holiday highlighted a strong "experience economy" trend, with catering growth far outstripping tourism revenue. In e-commerce, Douyin and PDD continued to lead the industry, while traditional giants focused on AI empowerment and profitability. The Baijiu sector, after a deep adjustment, showed signs of bottoming out and stabilizing in May 2026.

## 2. Social Retail Sales & Leading Indicators
Official April social retail data is expected on May 15. Based on leading indicators:
*   **Leading Indicators:** National Information Center data shows that offline payment volume in April grew by **1.7%** YoY, while commodity consumption grew by **2.7%**.
*   **Inflation Trends:** CPI data released on May 11 rose **1.2%** YoY in April, indicating a mild recovery in consumer prices and easing deflationary pressures.
*   **Outlook:** Following a 2.4% growth in Q1, April retail sales are expected to stay within a stable range of around 2.0%. Endogenous growth momentum still requires further policy activation.

## 3. Labor Day (May Day) Tourism & Catering Performance (May 1-5)
May Day holiday data showed "volume growth with stable pricing" and an "explosion in catering":
*   **Travel Volume:** 325 million domestic trips nationwide, up **3.6%** YoY.
*   **Tourism Revenue:** Domestic tourism expenditure totaled **185.49 billion RMB**, up **2.9%** YoY, with per capita spending remaining largely flat.
*   **Catering Revenue:** National catering sales revenue surged by **31.4%** YoY, with snacks up **41.6%** and bars/tea houses up **51.5%**.
*   **Key Logic:** Consumers shifted from "sightseeing" to "culinary tourism." Catering, as a core carrier of social interaction and experience, showed significantly higher elasticity than tourism infrastructure spending.

## 4. E-commerce GMV Growth Comparison (2026 Q1)
The e-commerce industry has entered a stage of "omnichannel competition" and "overseas dividends":
| Platform | Estimated Q1 GMV Growth | Key Drivers |

## 5. Baijiu Wholesale Price Trends
In May 2026, premium Baijiu wholesale prices showed signals of stabilization after the 2025 industry trough:
*   **Kweichow Moutai (53% Feitian):**
    *   Bulk wholesale price stabilized at around **1,565 RMB/bottle**, with original cases at approx **1,665 RMB/bottle**.
    *   The retail guidance price hike in late March (to 1,539 RMB) provided strong support for wholesale prices; channel inventory is at historical lows.
*   **Wuliangye (Puwu/8th Gen):**
    *   Terminal transaction prices recovered to **830-860 RMB/bottle**.
    *   Wholesale prices rose slightly in early May, indicating successful channel destocking. The inventory cycle has dropped to a healthy level of 2 months.
*   **Sector Assessment:** The industry is bottoming out in its "decadal cycle." Premium brands are leading the recovery, while sub-premium brands remain under pressure testing.

# Property Credit Watch: Private and Mixed-Ownership Developers

Anchor date: 2026-05-11, based on the local shell output of `date +%Y-%m-%d`. No upstream handoff files were present in the workspace, so this analysis was reconstructed from the prompt, public disclosures, and public news sources.

## Prioritised Conclusions

1. **Sales are not recovering across the board; the rebound is narrow and driven by core-city projects with strong supply.** CRIC's April data show that typical developers recorded RMB204.76bn of attributable sales in April, down 0.8% from March, and RMB630.88bn in January-April. New-home transaction area in 50 cities was 12.78mn sqm in April, roughly flat YoY, while second-hand transaction area in 20 cities was 17.93mn sqm, up 17% YoY (source: [CRIC/Sina Finance](https://finance.sina.com.cn/wm/2026-04-30/doc-inhwhviy6110853.shtml)). On an operating-sales basis, Guotai Haitong cited CRIC's April TOP100 operating sales at RMB259.20bn, down 3.9% MoM and 8.9% YoY (source: [FXBaogao](https://www.fxbaogao.com/detail/5397740)). This should not be read as a sector-wide cash-flow recovery.

2. **Within the TOP30, the private or mixed-ownership names with visible sequential recovery are mainly Greentown and Binjiang; Gemdale improved from a low base. Vanke, Greenland, Longfor, Country Garden, and Sunac do not justify an upgrade based on April sales.** Greentown's April total contracted sales were about RMB16.3bn, calculated as RMB54.9bn in January-April minus RMB38.6bn in January-March, around +8% versus March's RMB15.1bn. Binjiang recorded April sales of RMB9.26bn, up 56% MoM (sources: [Greentown March disclosure](https://finance.sina.com.cn/stock/estate/integration/2026-04-11/doc-inhtzxrs0029601.shtml), [Greentown April disclosure](https://www.guandian.cn/m/show/559596), [NBD](https://www.nbd.com.cn/articles/2026-05-01/4377443.html)). Gemdale's April contracted sales were RMB2.53bn, with January-April at RMB5.83bn; April contributed a high share, but the absolute run-rate remains low (source: [NBD](https://www.nbd.com.cn/articles/2026-05-07/4382550.html)).

3. **High-frequency data for early May support continued heat in core cities, but not a broad private-developer credit add.** From April 30 to May 5, Shenzhen recorded 492 new-home online signings, up 68.5% YoY, 337 second-hand residential signings, up 54.6% YoY, and 573 new-home subscriptions, up 43.3% YoY. This was driven by Shenzhen's policy easing, the May Day housing fair, and core-district upgrading demand (source: [Eastmoney/Shenzhen Commercial Daily](https://finance.eastmoney.com/a/202605063728692942.html)). Public weekly national samples still show divergence: near end-April, 30-city new-home transaction area for the latest seven days was 2.695mn sqm, up 8.5% YoY, and 13-city second-hand transactions were up 11.9% YoY (source: [Caixin Securities weekly](https://www.fxbaogao.com/detail/5386097)).

4. **On onshore credit-enhanced issuance: excluding Longfor and Seazen, the answer is not “two or three new likely names,” but “one or two already landed plus one weak candidate.”** New Hope Real Estate already issued an RMB880mn MTN in January and a second RMB680mn China Bond Insurance-guaranteed MTN on April 28, with a 2.75% coupon and 1.97x subscription (sources: [10jqka](https://news.10jqka.com.cn/20260429/c676369972.shtml), [NBD](https://www.nbd.com.cn/articles/2026-04-30/4377270.html)). Excellence Commercial disclosed its 2026 first MTN issuance in February, and CREIS noted that private and mixed-ownership developer issuance remained limited to five issuers in Q1, with New Hope and Excellence among the longer-tenor MTN issuers (sources: [ChinaMoney](https://www.chinamoney.com.cn/chinese/zqfxgg/20260209/3281904.html), [CREIS](https://www.cih-index.com/news/2026-04-28/54628778.html)). Midea Real Estate has a 2026 first MTN rating report with up to RMB1.0bn issuance and a two-year tenor, but the document points to AAA debt rating and Midea Holding support rather than confirmed China Bond Insurance guarantee; treat it as a watchlist item, not a confirmed landing (source: [ChinaMoney rating report](https://www.chinamoney.org.cn/dqs/cm-s-notice-query/fileDownLoad.do?contentId=3279130&mode=save&priority=0)).

## Recommendation for Private Developer Bond Exposure

- **Retain debt-level safety margin, not private-developer beta.** Acceptable exposure requires China Bond Insurance or strong guarantee, short duration, transparent underlying assets, and verifiable subscription demand.
- **Retain or track:** Longfor and Seazen only with clear enhancement or short tenor; Binjiang as a high-quality private benchmark given its near-SOE funding cost; New Hope and Excellence only at the enhanced-note level.
- **Do not add:** ordinary unsecured credit of Vanke, Greenland, or Gemdale solely because April sales improved at the margin; no ordinary exposure to distressed names such as Country Garden or Sunac.
- **Follow-up checklist:** 1) April operating updates after May 11, especially Longfor, Seazen, and Vanke; 2) ChinaMoney/NAFMII issuance results for Midea Real Estate and whether China Bond Insurance is included; 3) whether Shenzhen, Shanghai, and Hangzhou second-week May online signings sustain the May Day heat.

# Card 06 — Debt-Default Pressure Transmitting to Data-Center REIT Valuations

- Work date: 2026-05-12 (Asia/Singapore)

## 1. Core judgment

Project-level CMBS, single-tenant project-finance loans and regional IDC ABS slipping below a 1.25x DSCR is not a stand-alone credit event — it is the leading indicator that data-center REIT equity will simultaneously feel pressure on **AFFO, dividend coverage, NAV and Cap Rate**. Our base case for 2026H2–2027H1: AFFO/dividend coverage tightens from ~1.35x to **1.05–1.15x**, the sector Cap Rate widens **+75–125 bp from 5.0–5.5% to 5.75–6.75%**, and NAV is marked down **15–25%**. The names most exposed are NOT DLR/EQIX (multi-tenant, long WALT, natural power hedges); they are the **single-tenant hyperscale-heavy (>60% of NOI), WALT <6 years, with >25% of the pipeline on BTM gensets or unsigned PPAs** — second-tier REITs and commercial IDC sponsors (QTS at the parent level, Iron Mountain DC segment, Switch's privatized assets, China-domiciled GDS/Chindata/VNET offshore stacks).

In one line: the DSCR breach first shows up on the credit side as spread widening and cash-trap triggers, then propagates through four pipes — **lease repricing friction, asset impairments, trapped cash at the project SPV, and rising marginal capex funding cost** — squeezing REIT distributable cash flow, with Cap Rate widening completing the valuation reset.

## 2. The four transmission pipes

### Pipe 1 — Mark-to-market lease friction

- Hyperscale wholesale leases were signed at $110–135/kW-month in 2020–2022; 2025–2026 spot in Northern Virginia / Phoenix / ERCOT is $180–240/kW-month (CBRE NAR 2026Q1, JLL 2026 Spring DC Report).
- The bull narrative says "structural mark-to-market = AFFO upgrade." Three frictions undercut it:
  1. 75–80% of the signed book carries **CPI caps (mostly 2–3%) or fixed 2.5–3% escalators**. Repricing only happens at expiry; 2026–2028 expiries are just 8–12% of GLA. Most of the "upcycle dividend" lands FY30+.
  2. **Power pass-through is fragmented.** Wholesale triple-net leases pass 60–70% of utility cost through cleanly, but the $25–80/MWh BTM-genset premium sits in a contractual gray zone. When MSFT/META/GOOG invoke MAC clauses or demand rent credits to share the burden, REIT-retained NOI drops 200–400 bp.
  3. **24×7 CFE covenants** force REITs to lock in green PPAs above regulated tariffs; the differential is not fully passed through and lands in OpEx.

- Net: visible mark-to-market is 30–50% absorbed by power premia. Same-store NOI growth converges from 5–7% (Street) to 1.5–3.5% for 2026–2027.

### Pipe 2 — Impairments and sunk pipeline cost

- With Cards 02 and 05 confirming 25–35% physical delivery slippage, the REIT accounting impact:
  - **CIP (construction in progress)** is ~12% of DLR's assets, ~9% of EQIX, ~22% of QTS pre-privatization, ~25% of GDS. Slippage cuts target stabilized yields from 9.5–11% to 7.5–9%, triggering ASC 360 / IAS 36 recoverability tests.
  - Several Loudoun/PJM sites (typical: 100–300 MW, $700M–2.0B book) pushed out to 2030–2031 lose ~8–18% of NPV at an 8% discount rate. Case-by-case write-downs of $200M–700M; sector-wide cumulative impairments could reach **$3–6 B**.
  - China-side Ya'an / Zhongwei / Helinge'er DREAM-WEST SPVs, if leased back to offshore sponsors, only sit on REIT books as investment property — but **deferred receivables and related-party allowances** flow through OpEx.

### Pipe 3 — Cash trap and distribution capacity

- Project CMBS (e.g. BX 2026-VLT9) cash-traps at 1.20x DSCR and starts cash-sweeping at 1.10x. Once triggered:
  - The project SPV stops distributing to the REIT parent; an **out-of-period structural cash-flow gap** emerges. An 800 MW campus contributing $90–120M of annual distributions equals 3–5% of a mid-cap REIT's FFO.
  - Sector median **AFFO / dividend coverage** slides from 1.32–1.38x (end-2025) to 1.05–1.15x by 2026Q4. If three or more material projects cash-trap simultaneously, probability of the **first sector dividend cut** rises to 35–45% (base case 15%).
  - Priority watch: Iron Mountain DC segment, Equinix secondary-market JVs, Digital Realty's single-tenant hyperscale pool (~28% of assets), GDS USD-note ring-fenced assets.

### Pipe 4 — Refinancing cost and Cap Rate expansion

- Card 03 set the IG–project finance basis ≥60 bp as a confirmation signal. The basis widening flows into REIT valuation two ways:
  1. **WACC uplift**: REIT weighted debt cost from 4.2–4.8% to 5.3–6.0% → implied Cap Rate **+75–125 bp**.
  2. **Private-market benchmark**: in 2026Q1 the first batch of **unsold hyperscale portfolios** surfaced (Bloomberg 2026-04-22 reported a BX data-center package walked the bid from 4.5% to 5.25% Cap and still failed to clear). Private prints are a hard ceiling on REIT NAV.
- A 5.0% → 5.75–6.75% Cap Rate reset implies **13–24% NAV markdown**: DLR from ~$180 to $135–150, EQIX from ~$920 to $760–820, GDS ADR from $5.5 to $3.5–4.0.

## 3. Quant thresholds — confirm vs. falsify

| Signal | Threshold | Meaning | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Top-5 single-borrower CMBS DSCR (rolling median) | < 1.25x | Pipe 3 active | Trepp DC Index, KBRA |
| Sector AFFO / dividend coverage | < 1.20x | Dividend cut probability ≥30% | NAREIT |
| Private hyperscale Cap Rate prints | ≥ 5.75% | NAV markdown confirmed | Green Street, RCA |
| REIT implied Cap Rate – 10y UST spread | < 150 bp | Still rich, more pain to come | Green Street |
| Cumulative CIP impairment disclosures | > $2.5 B | Pipe 2 second half | 10-Q / annual filings |

**Falsifiers**: (i) hyperscale tenants voluntarily sign 15+ year leases and accept +20% mark-to-market; (ii) federal/state grid-investment legislation passes before 2026Q3 and shortens interconnect queues; (iii) private Cap Rates hold ≤5.25% and BX/KKR re-engage with ≥$5B opportunistic buys. If any one fires, sector Cap Rate widening compresses to 25–50 bp.

## 4. Name-level tiering

- **Relative winners** (lower Pipe 1–4 exposure): EQIX (interconnection-heavy revenue, natural power hedge), DLR's interconnection pool, Iron Mountain's records-management core.
- **Core pressure names**: Digital Realty single-tenant hyperscale sleeve, GDS USD structure, Iron Mountain DC segment, Switch parent post-LBO cash flow.
- **A-share linked**: Sinnet (300383), AtHub (603881), Baosight (600845, IDC arm) — if any have offshore sponsor receivables, look through to REIT/SPV exposure. Runzhi (300442) with captive power campuses is relatively defensive.
- **Singapore / Asia REITs**: Keppel DC REIT and Digital Core REIT — Singapore's regulated tariff and stricter triple-net wording delay transmission by 6–9 months, but cross-listed impairments in US parents will resonate.

## 5. Positioning recommendations

1. **Pair trades**: long EQIX vs. short DLR single-tenant sleeve (CDS or sector-ETF basket re-weighting); long Iron Mountain core vs. short Iron Mountain DC sleeve.
2. **Cross-asset**: short sector REIT equity vs. long 5–10y IG hyperscaler bonds (MSFT/META/GOOG belly) — directly harvests Pipe 4's basis widening.
3. **A-share**: underweight pure-play IDC beta, overweight UHV / gas turbine / regulated power (echoing Cards 02 and 05).
4. **Trigger management**: with Trepp DC CMBS DSCR <1.20x AND private Cap Rate ≥5.75% both firing, lift sector REIT underweight from −100 bp to −250 bp vs. benchmark.

## 6. Historical analogue

- The 2017 DC privatization wave (DFT, DRT, QTS-2021) happened at 4.5% Cap Rate and 1.5–2.5% 10y UST. The 2026 setup differs: 10y UST 4.0–4.5%, capex intensity doubled, hard power-side constraints. This Cap Rate expansion lasts longer (~18–24 months) and will not produce a V-shaped recovery like the 2020 COVID cycle.

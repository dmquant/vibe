## Core Thesis

As of May 11, 2026, the Johor Data Center (DC) market has officially transitioned from a speculative "land grab" phase to a "structural wash-out." Based on the water and power full-cost stress tests performed in this card, we observe a violent valuation bifurcation: **Projects with 132kV+ grid connectivity, recycled/closed-loop cooling capabilities, and anchored hyperscale tenants are being re-rated as core infrastructure. Conversely, "grey boxes" reliant on 33kV power, municipal water (now under strict moratorium), and lacking real tenant load are being downgraded to industrial shells or stranded inventory.** The state's actual load of 850MW—representing only 19% of completed capacity—highlights the massive volume of ESA (Energy Supply Agreement) capacity facing "utilization audit" and clawback risks.

## 1. Johor DC Site-Level Discount Matrix

We have developed the following discount model, using 100% AI-Ready Core Infrastructure as the baseline:

| Valuation Dimension | Core Infrastructure (Core) | Industrial Shell | Stranded Inventory | Valuation Impact (Discount) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Power Access** | Dedicated 132kV/275kV Substation | 33kV Temporary/Industrial Grid | No certain energization / Expired ESA | -30% to -50% |
| **Water Contracts** | Recycled/Closed-loop (WUE < 1.8) | Treated Tap Water (Moratorium risk) | Municipal dependency / No WUE permit | -20% to -40% |
| **Tenant Load** | Hyperscale Anchors (MSFT/GOOG/Sea) | Retail Colo / High Vacancy | Speculative ESA / No real intent | -25% to -40% |
| **Cooling Tech** | Liquid Cooling Ready (Blackwell) | Legacy Air-Cooled (CapEx heavy retro) | Obsolescent (Tier 1/2 Specs) | -15% to -30% |

## 2. Asset Categorization & Key Project Assessment

### A. Core Infrastructure —— Maintain or Upgrade Valuation
These assets possess rare heavy infrastructure support and serve as the "anchor weights" of the 2026 Johor market.
*   **YTL Sedenak Green DC Park:** Supported by Nvidia GB200 clusters, integrated 500MW solar potential, and direct 275kV access. Its deep binding with Sea Ltd ensures actual load rates far exceeding industry averages.
*   **Princeton Digital Group (PDG) JH1:** Successfully transitioned to dry-cooling/closed-loop systems, bypassing Johor's latest water restrictions.
*   **GDS Nusajaya Tech Park (NTP) Phase 1/2:** Despite being an early entrant, it occupies key 132kV nodes with premium tenants (ByteDance, Alibaba Cloud).

### B. Industrial Shell —— Under Revaluation Pressure
These assets are completed but fail technical specs for Blackwell-class liquid cooling or face power capacity restrictions.
*   **"Grey Boxes" in Small Industrial Parks:** Many DCs developed by traditional players (e.g., local developers in transition) lack 132kV access and cannot support large GPU clusters. These are trading as "industrial warehouses with ESA rights," with premiums shrinking 40% since 2024.
*   **Non-Liquid Cooling Retrofits:** Projects limited by floor loading or pipe layouts where the cost to upgrade to 120kW/rack is prohibitive.

### C. Stranded Inventory —— Downgrade to Stranded Assets
These assets face regulatory phase-out or cannot deliver due to a lack of capital for utility compliance.
*   **Speculative "ESA Flipping" Projects:** Sites holding power agreements but showing no signs of construction. Under the 2026 85% utilization audit, their power allocations are being forcibly clawed back by the state.
*   **Water Moratorium Impacted Sites:** Legacy Tier 1/2 facilities in water-sensitive zones (e.g., Senai) unable to prove WUE < 1.8. These sites are denied expansion permits, and their valuations are trending toward land residual value.

---

## 3. Developer Risk Warnings

*   **Traditional Developer Transition Risk:** Giants like Sime Darby Property or Mah Sing, with massive land reserves, are seeing their valuation logic shift from "selling land to DCs" to "DC asset quality." **Land prices have peaked (RM 86 psf)**; future Alpha depends on securing 275kV grid guarantees and recycled water infrastructure.
*   **Stranded Capacity Audit:** We expect approximately 1.2GW of speculative ESA capacity to be formally cancelled in H2 2026, mostly concentrated in fragmented sites lacking independent substations.

---

## Conclusion

The valuation anchor for Johor DCs has shifted from "MW quantity" to "utility certainty." Investors should avoid any speculative projects reliant on 33kV grids and municipal water. **We recommend overweighting park operators with established 132kV hub status and exercising extreme caution with developers holding only "industrial shells."**

# Materials Sector: Analysis of Pressure on Backward Capacity under Carbon Market Expansion (2026-05-11)

## I. Executive Summary
Based on the forward carbon price (CEA) forecast of **114.6 RMB/ton**, the net profit pressure on "backward capacity" in the steel and cement sectors **will inevitably exceed 15 RMB/ton**. This cost increment will act as the "final straw" for accelerating capacity consolidation. For the cement industry in particular, a 15 RMB/ton pressure may entirely consume the expected net profit of inefficient players.

## II. Sector-Specific Deep Dive

### 1. Steel Industry: Moving from "Thin Margins" to the "Edge of Loss"
*   **Emission Intensity:** The average CO2 emission intensity for BF-BOF (Blast Furnace-Basic Oxygen Furnace) crude steel production is approximately **2.0 tCO2/t**.
*   **Cost Penetration Calculation:** 
    *   At a carbon price of 114.6 RMB/t, a quota deficit (actual emissions exceeding free allowances) of just **6.5%** triggers a cost increase of **14.90 RMB/ton**.
    *   **Backward Capacity Status:** Obsolete facilities (e.g., blast furnaces smaller than 1000m³) or those with energy efficiency below Level 3 typically have intensities **10%-15%** higher than the industry benchmark. At 114.6 RMB, their actual cost pressure is estimated between **23 and 35 RMB/ton**.
*   **Profit Impact:** With 2026 average steel profits projected at 120-140 RMB/ton, a 15-35 RMB cost deduction represents a **12%-25% profit reduction**, severely compressing the room for survival for laggards.

### 2. Cement Industry: Facing Terminal Survival Risks
*   **Emission Intensity:** The average emission intensity for cement clinker production is approximately **0.8 tCO2/t**.
*   **Cost Penetration Calculation:** 
    *   At a carbon price of 114.6 RMB/t, a quota deficit of **16%** results in a 15 RMB/ton pressure.
    *   **Backward Capacity Status:** The cement sector is implementing strict quota tightening. Smaller kilns (e.g., 2500 t/d or below) lacking waste heat recovery or alternative fuel capabilities are highly likely to exceed the 15% deficit threshold.
*   **Survival Crisis:** Average cement industry profits for 2026 are projected at only **10-15 RMB/ton**. Consequently, **a 15 RMB/ton carbon cost will turn backward capacity directly into a book loss**, forcing shutdowns or mergers before the 2026-2027 compliance deadline.

## IV. Impact on Capacity Consolidation
1.  **Shift from Administrative to Market Discipline:** Historically, capacity was managed via "Capacity Swap" ratios. Carbon pricing now provides a dynamic, permanent negative feedback loop where high-emission players lose price competitiveness.
2.  **Increased Market Concentration:** Industry leaders (e.g., Baowu, Conch) will gain a "Green Premium" by selling surplus quotas and optimizing energy efficiency, while backward firms, unable to afford technical upgrades, will seek exit strategies.
3.  **CBAM Alignment:** While 114.6 RMB is lower than the EU ETS price, it covers the marginal cost of efficiency improvements for most domestic plants, preparing Chinese steel and cement exports for the full implementation of the EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM) by 2026.

## V. Strategic Follow-up Items
*   **Monitor Final Benchmark Levels:** If benchmarks are set at the top 30% of the industry, pressure on backward capacity will far exceed 15 RMB/ton.
*   **Indirect Emission Rules:** Watch for adjustments in how indirect emissions (from electricity) are calculated, which could further accelerate the transition from BF-BOF to EAF (Electric Arc Furnace) steelmaking.

# Stress Test: Material Price Pressures on AI Power Infrastructure Margins

**Date:** 2026-05-11  
**Analyst:** materials-analyst (Materials Analyst)  
**Status:** Stress-test (Support for Margin Expansion)

## 1. Executive Summary: The "Scarcity Premium" Outpaces "Commodity Inflation"

As of May 2026, copper prices have surged to **$13,000/MT**, and Grain-Oriented Electrical Steel (GOES) remains at historic highs ($5,500/MT in the US). Despite these cost pressures, this stress test confirms that **the gross margin expansion thesis for the AI power infrastructure chain remains intact.** The "scarcity premium" driven by physical bottlenecks (manufacturing capacity, high-voltage testing, and HiB GOES) allows OEMs to pass through commodity costs with significant "alpha" margin. ASP increases of 30-60% since 2021 are more than double the impact of raw material inflation, reinforcing a structural bull cycle for equipment manufacturers.

## 2. Quantitative Stress Test: ASP vs. Cost Sensitivity

### 2.1 Transformer Cost Breakdown
Raw materials account for **>60%** of total manufacturing costs. The sensitivity to copper is high but manageable within the current pricing environment.

| Component | Share of Total Cost | Sensitivity (10% Material Price Up) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Copper/Aluminum Windings** | 35% – 45% | +4% to +6% on total price |
| **GOES (Silicon Steel) Core** | 15% – 25% | +2% to +3% on total price |
| **Tank, Oil, & Other** | 30% – 50% | Variable |

### 2.2 Margin Expansion Calculation (2021 vs. 2026 Stress Scenario)
*   **2021 Base:** Price = 100, Cost = 85 (GM 15%).
*   **2026 Stress:**
    *   **Price (ASP):** 145 (+45% average increase driven by scarcity).
    *   **Copper Cost:** $20.4 \times 1.44 = 29.4$ (Copper price +44% from $9k to $13k).
    *   **GOES Cost:** $12.8 \times 1.50 = 19.2$ (GOES price +50% average).
    *   **Other Costs:** $51.8 \times 1.15 = 59.6$ (+15% labor/logistics).
    *   **Total Cost:** $29.4 + 19.2 + 59.6 = 108.2$.
    *   **New Gross Margin:** $145 - 108.2 = 36.8$ (**25.4% GM**).
*   **Result:** Even in a high-inflation scenario, Gross Margin expands by **~1,040 bp**.

## 3. Structural Bottlenecks: The True "Hard Constraints"

The margin expansion is not merely a "beta" play on demand; it is protected by physical entry barriers that commodity prices cannot solve:
1.  **GOES High-Permeability (HiB) Grades:** Only ~10 mills globally can produce the highest efficiency core steel. Technical barriers prevent rapid capacity additions.
2.  **High-Voltage Testing Fields:** New transformer factories take 2-3 years to build, but the specialized testing equipment (impulse generators, Faradays) has lead times exceeding 36 months.
3.  **Lead Time Power:** OEMs with 120-150 week backlogs have absolute pricing power. "Slot premiums" for 2027 delivery are now a standard feature in hyperscaler negotiations.

## 4. Substitution and Technology Mitigation

The industry is reacting to $13,000 copper through material heterogeneity:
*   **Aluminum Substitution:** Feasible for distribution transformers and main busbars (30-50% cost saving), but limited in high-density AI racks due to size/stiffness constraints.
*   **Copper-Clad Aluminum (CCA):** Gaining traction in non-critical power paths, offering 35% cost reduction.
*   **Interconnect Shift:** As copper's reach shrinks at higher speeds (800G/1.6T), the shift to **Fiber Optics** acts as a "natural cap" on copper demand within the data center, preserving it for power delivery.

## 5. Regional Divergence: The US-China "Cost Gap"

A critical strategic risk is the regional divergence in material costs:
*   **China GOES ($1,100/MT)** vs. **US GOES ($5,500/MT)**.
*   This 5x price gap creates a massive competitive advantage for Chinese OEMs (like TBEA, Sieyuan, Jinpan) in international markets (Latin America, SE Asia, Middle East).
*   **Trade Barriers:** US Section 232 and EU CBAM are the only things preventing a "flood" of cheaper power equipment, which paradoxically keeps ASPs (and margins) higher for Western-domestic OEMs.

## 6. Conclusion: Support Margin Expansion Thesis

The threat of commodity price erosion is **rejected**. The current environment is a "Seller's Market" where cost increases are simply a floor for even higher ASPs. The primary investment risk is not margin compression, but **geopolitical supply chain disruption** (e.g., restricted access to Chinese GOES or Indonesian Copper).

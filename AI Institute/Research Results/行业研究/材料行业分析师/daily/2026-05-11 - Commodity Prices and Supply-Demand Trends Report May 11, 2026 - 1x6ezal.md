# Commodity Prices and Supply-Demand Trends Report (May 11, 2026)

## Key Findings
As of May 2026, the global commodity market has entered a "stress-deficit" phase. Critical metals (Copper, Aluminum, Lithium) are facing multi-dimensional pressures from AI infrastructure expansion, energy transition, and geopolitical maneuvering, keeping prices at elevated levels. The steel market has stabilized amid inventory drawdown and a demand recovery.

## 1. Copper: Supply Bottlenecks in the Age of AI and Grids
*   **Price Dynamics:** LME copper is currently oscillating around **$11,500/tonne**, retracing slightly from Q1 peaks but remaining historically high.
*   **Supply:** The market is projected to face a deficit of approximately **150,000 tonnes** in 2026. Production in major regions like Chile has declined. Notably, China's export restrictions on sulfuric acid (a key refining input) are expected to tighten supply further in H2 2026.
*   **Demand:** AI data centers, requiring 4x more copper than traditional facilities, have emerged as a primary growth driver. Power grid modernization in Western economies accounts for approximately 60% of the recent demand surge.

## 2. Aluminum: Geopolitical Disruptions to Supply
*   **Price Dynamics:** Aluminum prices have rebounded to approximately **$3,500/tonne**, up nearly 45% year-over-year.
*   **Supply:** Instability in the Strait of Hormuz has disrupted supply from the Middle East (representing 9% of global capacity). High natural gas costs continue to delay the restart of European smelters.
*   **Demand:** Aluminum is increasingly used as a substitute for copper in electrical applications due to the extreme copper price premium. Solar PV and grid construction remain robust demand pillars.

## 3. Lithium: Strong Recovery Driven by BESS
*   **Price Dynamics:** Lithium carbonate prices in China have recovered to the **CNY 175,000–194,000/tonne** range, a 50% increase year-to-date.
*   **Supply:** Underinvestment during the 2024–2025 downturn has resulted in a projected deficit of **22,000–80,000 tonnes (LCE)** for 2026.
*   **Demand:** While EV growth has moderated, Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS) for AI data centers are seeing explosive growth, expected to account for 30% of global lithium demand this year.

## 4. Steel: Seasonal Recovery Supported by Low Inventory
*   **Price Dynamics:** Steel rebar futures are trading around **CNY 3,250/tonne**, with global inventories falling for seven consecutive weeks.
*   **Supply:** High input costs (nickel, energy) have squeezed mill margins, limiting incentive for production expansion.
*   **Demand:** Seasonal demand for construction steel has picked up in China following the May Day holiday, supported by stabilizing real estate policies. The World Steel Association forecasts global demand to grow by 2.2% in 2026.

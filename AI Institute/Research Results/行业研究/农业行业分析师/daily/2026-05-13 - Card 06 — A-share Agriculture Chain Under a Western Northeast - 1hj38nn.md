# Card 06 — A-share Agriculture Chain Under a Western Northeast 35°C+ Heat Scenario, 2026-05-14

- Work date: 2026-05-14 (Asia/Singapore)
- Analyst: agri-analyst
- Stance: synthesize
- Upstream question: If the satellite-identified western dry belt in Northeast China overlaps with 35°C+ heat in late June, which A-share agriculture-chain names offer the clearest earnings elasticity and valuation odds across drought-tolerant seeds, fertilizer demand, irrigation, and grain reserve infrastructure?

## 1. Core Judgment

As of 2026-05-14, I synthesize Cards 03-05 as follows: **the western Northeast heat scenario is not yet a national corn-shortage trade, but it is an investable A-share event trade with a clear timing ladder.** The best risk-adjusted basket is not a pure seed basket. It is **Stanley (002588) + Denghai Seeds (002041) + COFCO Technology & Industry (301058)**, with different holding periods.

The ranking by **earnings certainty plus valuation odds** is:

| Priority | Segment | Preferred A-share names | Why it ranks here |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Fertilizer / stress-relief inputs | Stanley (002588), then Salt Lake (000792) / Asia-Potash (000893) as commodity hedges | Demand can show up in the same Q2-Q3 growing season; Stanley has channel reach, product mix and a low implied 2025 P/E of about 11.8x based on reported market value and net profit. Potash names have strong earnings bases but are less specific to one Northeast drought event. |
| 2 | Corn seed / drought-tolerant and biotech beta | Denghai (002041), LongPing (000998); avoid treating Quanyin (300087) as high-certainty | Seeds react first in price, but 2026 earnings are mostly already booked. The real EPS channel is the 2027 order season. Denghai has the cleanest corn-seed exposure and high operating leverage, but valuation is already demanding at 63.9x 2026E P/E in a recent broker model. |
| 3 | Grain storage / reserve-procurement infrastructure | COFCO Technology & Industry (301058) | If reserves and imports are upgraded, drying, warehousing, logistics and grain-engineering orders follow with a 1-3 quarter lag. COFCO Tech's 2025 P/E near 23.8x and low historical percentile give better valuation odds than most seed names, but catalyst timing is slower. |
| 4 | Irrigation / water-saving capex | Dayu Water Saving (300021) | The thematic fit is obvious, but earnings visibility is weak because orders depend on local fiscal execution and collections. It is an option-like position, not a core call. |
| Underweight | Production farming / land operators | Beidahuang (600598), Sukang Agriculture (601952) | They may benefit from grain-price support, but drought itself is an operating risk. They are not clean beneficiaries of western Northeast heat. |

## 2. Why the Trade Is Event-Driven, Not a Structural Shortage Call

Card 05 found a measurable weak patch: 800-1,300 kha of corn in Tongliao, Baicheng, Chifeng and western Songyuan, equal to about 4-6% of Northeast corn acreage, with NDVI at -0.4 to -0.7σ, daytime LST around +2.8°C and surface soil moisture 16-21% below baseline. In a 35°C+ late-June heat episode, the plausible national output loss rises to 2.4-4.5 Mt in the moderate case and 4.5-9.0 Mt in the severe case.

That matters for A-shares because the global corn baseline is no longer extremely loose. The 12 May 2026 WASDE put 2026/27 U.S. corn ending stocks near 1.96 bn bushels and stocks/use around 12.1%, with the next WASDE due on 11 June.[^1] FAS-related reporting still frames China as favoring domestic production, tight TRQ management and reserve tools rather than large corn imports; it forecast 2026/27 China corn output at 305 Mt and imports near 8 Mt.[^2] Therefore the A-share path is: **weather stress first, input demand second, reserve logistics third, seed-order mix last**.

The Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Affairs' seed-revitalization update also supports the medium-term seed theme: it reported that domestic crop varieties cover more than 95% of demand, seed-base supply assurance reached 80%, and leading seed firms have lifted R&D spending materially since 2020.[^3] But the official May ag-weather baseline still described Northeast China as broadly favorable for spring corn emergence, with mid-south Northeast temperatures near normal to slightly high and precipitation normal to slightly above normal.[^4] This gap is exactly why the satellite stress is tradable but not yet a national shortage thesis.

## 3. Transmission by Segment

### 3.1 Seeds: fastest price beta, slower EPS

Seed stocks should move first when the market sees "35°C+ Northeast corn heat" headlines, but the earnings channel is delayed. For spring corn, 2026 sales are largely placed before the late-June heat window. The immediate earnings effect is mostly small emergency replanting, replacement and channel restocking. The bigger effect is 2027: farmers and dealers re-rank drought tolerance, stand uniformity, density tolerance and biotech traits when placing autumn and winter orders.

**Denghai Seeds (002041)** is the cleanest corn-seed beta. It reported 2025 revenue of RMB 1.102 bn, down 11.55%, and attributable net profit of RMB 91.65 mn, up 61.86%.[^5] A 27 April 2026 broker report estimated 2026E attributable profit of RMB 139 mn, 2027E of RMB 168 mn and current 2026E P/E of 63.9x; it also noted corn seed revenue accounted for roughly 85% of 2025 revenue and that the LD05 GM corn transform had obtained a biosafety certificate.[^6] This gives Denghai the highest **elasticity**, but not the best valuation certainty.

**LongPing High-Tech (000998)** has broader platform value and stronger institutional recognition. Its 2025 revenue was RMB 8.477 bn, attributable net profit RMB 166 mn and deducting-nonrecurring profit RMB 77 mn, a sharp repair from 2024.[^7] It is the better benchmark for the national seed-revitalization trade, but less pure than Denghai for a western Northeast corn event because rice, overseas and other lines dilute the signal.

**Quanyin High-Tech (300087)** should not be treated as high-certainty drought beta. Its 2025 revenue was RMB 4.495 bn, but attributable net profit was a RMB 212 mn loss due mainly to a large impairment in its liquor-grain subsidiary.[^8] Until the market sees cleaner seed-only recovery, it is a lower-quality event name.

**Sensitivity:** If 10-15% of western Northeast corn seed orders shift toward drought-tolerant / biotech hybrids for 2027, Denghai could see 5-8% revenue upside and 10-20% profit upside from a low base. LongPing's profit upside is more likely 3-8% because of diversification. This is a late-2026/2027 earnings story, not a June P&L story.

### 3.2 Fertilizers and stress-relief inputs: best near-term earnings certainty

The most certain same-season cash register is not seed. It is **topdressing, water-soluble fertilizer, foliar anti-stress products and potassium-biased formulas**, provided there is enough irrigation or rainfall to make application effective. Across the 0.8-1.3 mn ha western belt, a RMB 20-40/mu emergency input package implies roughly RMB 240-780 mn of incremental regional demand. Only part of that flows to listed companies, but it can be visible in channel shipments.

**Stanley (002588)** is the best fit. It reported 2025 revenue of RMB 12.283 bn, attributable net profit of RMB 1.033 bn, and 2026 Q1 revenue / attributable net profit growth of 19.22% / 18.54%.[^9] The same report put its market value at RMB 12.233 bn, implying roughly 11.8x 2025 P/E. Stanley also reported 2025 phosphatic compound fertilizer sales of 3.9109 Mt and strong growth in "new-type fertilizers and other" revenue.[^9] This is the best combination of near-term earnings certainty and valuation odds.

**Salt Lake (000792)** and **Asia-Potash (000893)** are better viewed as potash and commodity hedges. Salt Lake reported 2025 KCl output of 4.9002 Mt, KCl sales of 3.8147 Mt, revenue of RMB 15.501 bn and attributable net profit of RMB 8.476 bn.[^10] Asia-Potash reported 2025 revenue of RMB 5.325 bn and attributable net profit of RMB 1.673 bn, up 75.97%.[^11] They benefit if potash demand and pricing remain firm, but a localized Northeast heat event is too small to reprice national KCl alone.

**Sensitivity:** Stanley is the core because 1-3% incremental profit upside is plausible if high-margin stress-relief products are pushed through existing channels. Salt Lake and Asia-Potash have better absolute profit scale, but their earnings are driven more by potash contract prices, geopolitics, imports and lithium / overseas project factors than by Tongliao-Baicheng weather.

### 3.3 Irrigation: strong story, weaker conversion

**Dayu Water Saving (300021)** has the clearest thematic label, but not the clearest EPS. It reported 2025 revenue of RMB 3.761 bn, down 14.10%, and attributable net profit of RMB 48.90 mn, down 39.72%.[^12] A western Northeast drought can accelerate emergency water-saving, drip-irrigation and high-standard farmland projects, but those contracts are usually government- and project-cycle dependent. Collections and gross margin matter more than headlines.

The right treatment is option sizing. A RMB 0.5-2.0 bn regional irrigation / drought-relief project pool could be meaningful versus Dayu's small profit base, but order capture, execution and collection are uncertain. I would not rank it above fertilizer or storage until there is named tender evidence.

### 3.4 Grain storage and reserve chain: slower, but better valuation odds

The storage chain is triggered when the discussion shifts from "crop stress" to "reserve rotation, drying, warehousing, quality sorting and import logistics." If the western Northeast loss moves the policy debate from 10-12 Mt imports toward 13-15 Mt, and if state reserve rotation becomes more active, **COFCO Technology & Industry (301058)** is the cleanest A-share mapping.

COFCO Tech reported 2025 revenue of RMB 3.063 bn, up 15.48%, and attributable net profit of RMB 233 mn.[^13] Lixinger showed a P/E of 23.76x, P/B of 2.57x, market value of RMB 5.615 bn and P/E historical percentile around 19.56% on its latest displayed valuation page.[^14] That makes valuation odds more attractive than high-P/E seed names. The drawback is timing: grain engineering orders usually recognize over 1-3 quarters, not immediately in June.

**Sukang Agriculture (601952)** and **Beidahuang (600598)** are not clean beneficiaries. Sukang's 2025 revenue was RMB 10.071 bn and attributable net profit RMB 548 mn; cash flow was strong, but it is a Jiangsu farming and grain-oil platform, not a western Northeast drought solution.[^15] Beidahuang's 2025 revenue was RMB 5.23 bn and attributable net profit RMB 1.17 bn; its operating base includes planting and land contracting, so drought is as much a yield / cost risk as a price-support benefit.[^16]

## 4. Portfolio Priority and Timing

**Stage 1: Forecast confirmation, 10-25 June.**  
If satellite LST remains above +3°C and 0-10 cm soil moisture stays near -20%, seed names should lead price reaction. Use Denghai for high beta, LongPing for larger-cap seed exposure. Position size should reflect valuation: Denghai is an event trade, not a cheap compounder.

**Stage 2: Field stress and input response, late June to July.**  
Shift priority to Stanley. The higher-conviction earnings signal is dealer orders for foliar fertilizer, water-soluble fertilizer, potassium formulas and anti-stress products. Salt Lake and Asia-Potash are acceptable hedges only if potash prices also confirm.

**Stage 3: Reserve and import discussion, July to September.**  
If crop-loss estimates move above 2.4-4.5 Mt and policy language shifts toward reserve replenishment or import acceleration, add COFCO Tech. This is the best valuation-odds name in the chain but needs a policy/order catalyst.

**Stage 4: 2027 seed ordering, September to December.**  
Return to Denghai / LongPing if dealer checks show higher booked share for drought-tolerant, dense-planting and biotech corn hybrids. This is when seed EPS visibility improves.

## 6. Conclusion and Handoff

I support Card 05's view that western Northeast heat is an asymmetric risk, but I **synthesize** it into a more selective A-share call: **buy the earnings pulse first through Stanley, trade the weather beta through Denghai, and wait for reserve-policy confirmation before scaling COFCO Tech.** Irrigation is an option, and farm operators are not clean beneficiaries.

Next handoff should go to **ashare-strategist** to test whether this three-name basket is already crowded, whether agriculture-chain turnover is breaking above normal event levels, and how to stage entries around 10-25 June satellite and weather updates.

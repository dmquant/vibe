## 1. Key Conclusions (prioritised)

1. **Global wheat softer, corn range-bound to weak, soybeans firm, rice off the highs** — the April–May combination of smooth North American planting and easing Black Sea tensions caps wheat and corn, while South American crush demand and Chinese soymeal de-stocking underpin beans.
2. **Domestic Chinese staples (wheat, corn, paddy rice) continue to trade inside the policy support band**: Zhengzhou wheat cash ~CNY 2,520/t, north-port corn CNY 2,360/t, mid-late indica paddy CNY 2,720/t, all down 1–2% MoM — consistent with the "ample crop, ample stocks" backdrop.
3. **Soybeans are the cleanest long today**: CBOT July contract has reclaimed USc 1,120/bu, the May WASDE cut 2026/27 global ending stocks to 118 Mt (prior 122 Mt), and the Argentine VAT-rebate roll-out is reviving crush.
4. **Rice**: Thai 5% broken FOB has eased from USD 605/t in early April to USD 575/t on India's wider 2025/26 export quota and Vietnam new-crop supply, but remains well above the five-year mean.
5. **Downside risk**: North American corn/soy belt 5–20 May forecasts trend wet; planting delays would flip the current bearish tone quickly. Watch the June USDA acreage report.

## 3. Product-by-Product

### 3.1 Wheat (bearish bias)
- **Drivers**: US winter-wheat good/excellent 54% (vs 49% YoY); Russian export offers USD 240/t (-4% MoM); EU 2025/26 ending stocks revised up to 14.2 Mt.
- **Policy**: China's MSP at CNY 2,540/t (grade 3) likely activated in June; normal new-crop quality limits any bounce.
- **Watch**: Black Sea loading cadence late May; India's June wheat tender.

### 3.2 Corn (range-bound, soft)
- **Drivers**: US planting 78% complete (vs 5-yr avg 71%); Argentina raised to 51 Mt; China starch-plant utilisation 58%, feed demand improving at the margin but still below 2024.
- **Watch**: Whether the DDGS import window opens; congestion at northern Chinese ports.

### 3.3 Rice / paddy (off the highs, still elevated)
- **International**: India lifted non-basmati white rice quotas in September, and Vietnam summer-autumn supply is recovering — FOB has fallen ~5%.
- **China**: Mid-late indica stocks 105 Mt; northeast japonica procurement steady at CNY 2,820/t.
- **Bottom line**: A continuing "international down, domestic floored" pattern; export-oriented millers see margin compression.

### 3.4 Soy complex (bullish bias)
- **Drivers**: USDA May WASDE cut global soy ending stocks to 118 Mt (-3.4 Mt MoM); China April soy imports 8.5 Mt; soymeal apparent demand +3.4% YoY.
- **China**: Soymeal basis swung from -CNY 50/t to +CNY 30/t — de-stocking working.
- **Idea**: Long basis or July–November bull-spread on CBOT beans inside USD 11.00–11.50.

### 3.5 Vegetable oils (divergent)
- **Palm**: Malaysia April stocks 1.68 Mt (+6% MoM) is a near-term cap; Indonesia B40 mandate roll-out underpins the longer term.
- **Soyoil**: Tracks beans up, but BO weighed by palm; soy-palm spread widened to +CNY 280/t.

## 5. Risks

1. **Weather**: Excessive late-May rain in the US Midwest would delay corn/soy planting and quickly flip the bearish narrative.
2. **Geopolitics**: Renewed Black Sea war-risk premium would disrupt wheat and sunflower oil flows.
3. **Policy**: India tightening rice quotas, or a fresh Chinese state reserve build, would shift the trend.
4. **FX**: A rapid CNY break through 7.10 would amplify imported-grain deflationary pressure in China.

# Listed Bank NIM Tracker: Declines Are Slowing, Deposit Repricing Is Becoming the Main Driver

**Date anchor: 2026-05-11.** References to “this year,” “Q1,” and “latest” are resolved against this date. The core scope is the 42 A-share listed banks, supplemented by system-wide commercial-bank regulatory data.

## Prioritized Conclusions

1. **NIM remains at historical lows, but the pattern has shifted from rapid compression to low-level bottoming.** Under NFRA regulatory data, commercial-bank NIM fell from 1.52% in 2024Q4 to 1.42% in 2025Q4, down 10bp for the year; it stayed at 1.42% in 2025Q2, Q3, and Q4, implying stabilization in the second half. [Regulatory data summary](https://finance.sina.com.cn/jinrong/2026-02-28/doc-inhpkpeq6979628.shtml)
2. **Listed banks were still broadly under pressure in 2025.** The 2025 annual reports of 42 A-share listed banks show 36 banks with YoY NIM declines, 2 flat, and 4 up; Changshu Bank remained the leader at 2.53%, followed by China Merchants Bank at 1.87%, Xi'an Bank and Bank of Changsha at 1.85%, and Bank of Nanjing at 1.82%. [NBD annual-report scan](https://www.nbd.com.cn/articles/2026-05-01/4377749.html) [Beijing Business Today summary](https://m.bbtnews.com.cn/article/228029)
3. **The marginal improvement was clearer in 2026Q1.** Guotai Haitong estimates listed-bank NIM at about 1.37% in 2026Q1, only 3bp lower than full-year 2025, with the YoY decline materially narrower; 37 of the 42 A-share listed banks recorded YoY growth in net interest income. The six major state-owned banks had an average Q1 NIM of 1.30%, only 1bp lower than 2025Q4. [Securities Times](https://www.stcn.com/article/detail/3894295.html) [China Electronic Banking Network](https://www.cebnet.com.cn/20260508/103002490.html)
4. **The driver mix is moving from one-way asset-yield compression to a tug-of-war between still-low loan yields and catch-up deposit-cost relief.** In December 2025, the weighted average rate on new loans was 3.15%, down 13bp YoY; corporate loans were 3.10%, and personal housing loans were 3.06%. At the same time, in May 2025, major state-owned banks and some joint-stock banks cut listed deposit rates: 1-year time deposits fell to 0.95%, while 3-year and 5-year tenors fell to 1.25% and 1.30%, creating a buffer from deposit maturity repricing in 2026. [PBOC 2025Q4 Monetary Policy Report](https://jrj.sh.gov.cn/cmsres/69/6988a5ab7c194929889e6b2a296a0714/224c81965de4746afb49ae8a2b5c5794.pdf) [People's Daily Online](https://finance.people.com.cn/n1/2025/0520/c1004-40483794.html)

## Driver Breakdown

### 1. Asset Side: Loan Yields Are Still Low, but the Pace of Decline Has Narrowed

In December 2025, the weighted average rate on new loans was 3.15%, down 13bp from December 2024; corporate loans were 3.10%, and personal housing loans were 3.06%. In general loans, the share priced below LPR reached 50.99% in December 2025, while the share priced above LPR was 41.77%, showing that competition for high-quality credit remained intense. [PBOC 2025Q4 Monetary Policy Report](https://jrj.sh.gov.cn/cmsres/69/6988a5ab7c194929889e6b2a296a0714/224c81965de4746afb49ae8a2b5c5794.pdf)

As of April 20, 2026, the 1-year LPR was 3.00% and the 5-year-plus LPR was 3.50%, unchanged from December 2025. [PBOC December 2025 LPR announcement](https://www.pbc.gov.cn/zhengcehuobisi/125207/125213/125440/3876551/2025122208351884506/index.html) [April 2026 LPR announcement repost](https://www.chinanews.com.cn/cj/2026/04-20/10606836.shtml) Stable LPRs reduced the repricing shock on existing loans, which is the core backdrop for the narrower NIM decline in 2026Q1.

### 2. Liability Side: Deposit-Cost Relief Is Starting to Come Through

On May 20, 2025, the six major state-owned banks cut listed RMB deposit rates: demand deposits fell to 0.05%; 3-month, 6-month, 1-year, and 2-year time deposits were set at 0.65%, 0.85%, 0.95%, and 1.05%; and 3-year and 5-year tenors were set at 1.25% and 1.30%. [People's Daily Online](https://finance.people.com.cn/n1/2025/0520/c1004-40483794.html) Because bank deposits reprice more slowly than loans, the maturity repricing of high-cost medium- and long-term deposits became a more visible NIM stabilizer from 2026Q1.

### 3. Structure: Major Banks Provide Volume, Regional Banks Depend on Projects and Client Mix

Major banks have low absolute NIMs, but CCB, ICBC, and BOCOM already showed marginal improvement in 2026Q1. For regional banks, the key variables are local project demand, inclusive SME pricing, and deposit competition. Market summaries show Guiyang Bank at 1.66% NIM in 2026Q1, up 12bp YoY; Bank of Chengdu at 1.65%, up 4bp YoY; and Bank of Nanjing, Bank of Hangzhou, and Bank of Ningbo at 1.58%, 1.62%, and 1.58%, respectively, all improving QoQ. [Securities Times](https://www.stcn.com/article/detail/3897144.html)

### 4. Regulation and Management Targets: NIM Is Not the Only Profit Variable

In 2026Q1, 37 of the 42 A-share listed banks recorded positive net interest income growth, while non-interest income also recovered. For valuation, NIM has shifted from a “profit-margin expansion” indicator to a “profit-bottom confirmation” indicator: as long as NIM declines keep narrowing, balance-sheet growth, stable provisions, wealth management, and investment-banking recovery can pass through to profit growth more quickly.

## View and Investment Implications

- **Industry level:** NIM is likely entering an L-shaped bottoming range, not a V-shaped rebound. If LPR remains stable, lower liability costs should keep protecting NIM in 2026; if rates are cut again, asset repricing will still slow the repair.
- **Bank type:** Joint-stock banks and high-quality regional banks are more likely to show NIM stabilization first. Major banks benefit from scale, dividends, and stable liabilities, but absolute NIM is low. Rural commercial banks still need to be assessed by SME pricing power and asset quality.
- **Earnings transmission:** In 2026, the focus should not be absolute NIM alone, but the combination of “QoQ NIM change + net interest income growth + deposit cost ratio + new NPL formation.”

## Follow-Up Tracking Items

1. Whether the May-June 2026 LPRs remain at 3.00% for 1-year and 3.50% for 5-year-plus.
2. Whether 2026 interim reports show a deposit cost ratio meaningfully below full-year 2025, especially for banks with high 3-year and 5-year time-deposit shares.
3. Whether listed-bank NIM in 2026Q2 can stay near the Q1 level; if the industry estimate falls below 1.35%, the bottoming thesis needs reassessment.
4. Whether regional banks' NIM improvement is accompanied by higher new NPL formation; if spread is gained through credit migration, asset-quality risk will surface with a lag.
5. Whether non-interest income recovery is sustainable, especially wealth management, fund/insurance distribution, custody, and bond underwriting income.

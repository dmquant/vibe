## 1. Reframing the Question

The card's nominal topic is a write-log artifact from Card 05, but the real question is unambiguous: on top of the "performance-driven domestic compute + mid/large-cap core rotation" paradigm laid out in Cards 01–04, and the small-cap TMT pledge/lockup stress-test delivered in Card 05, **how should the financials complex — the plumbing of market-making, custody, margin, off-balance pledge, long-duration insurance capital, and fintech AI deployment — be repriced by this paradigm shift?** This is the necessary lap before the strategy thread closes.

## 2. Three Transmission Channels (core conclusion)

The domestic-compute paradigm shift is **net positive for financials**, transmitted through three largely independent channels with staggered timing.

### Channel 1: Broker EPS — Turnover and Margin "Twin Wheels"
- Card 04 confirmed margin balances clustering into AI hardware leaders, with sector ETFs and northbound flow co-moving. Translated to broker P&L: brokerage + margin lending is ~35–45% of top-tier broker revenue, with an elasticity of 0.6–0.8x to A-share turnover.
- YTD 2026 daily A-share turnover is up ~18–22% on the AI-core narrative (indirect Cards 03/04 evidence); margin balances have breached the RMB 1.95–2.0tn band since year-start.
- **Beneficiaries**: CITIC Securities, Huatai Securities, Guotai Junan-Haitong (post-merger) — the institutional first tier absorbs incremental turnover first. East Money has the largest retail-AI beta but also the most crowded positioning.
- **Risk**: same root as Card 03's crowding alert — a >12% pullback in AI-core names in May–June would compress broker PB from the current ~1.5x back toward ~1.25x.

### Channel 2: Insurance OCI and the Core-Rotation Alignment Dividend
- 2026 is the first full-execution year of IFRS 9 + IFRS 17 in China. Insurers are structurally pushed toward **low-volatility, high-dividend, OCI/FVOCI-eligible "core assets"**. Cards 02–03's "small-cap growth → mid/large-cap core" rotation is directly aligned with this mandate.
- AI-compute leaders (SMIC, Hygon, Cambricon, NAURA, etc.) are entering the core-allocation whitelists of the major insurers as earnings delivery firms up. Q1 equity rebalancing disclosures from China Life, Ping An, and CPIC show TMT weight up ~1.5–2.2pp vs. 2025Q4.
- **Mechanism**: (a) direct equity revaluation lifts net assets; (b) with long-end rates anchored in 2.55–2.65%, asset-side ROI improvement now depends on equity alpha — exactly what this paradigm shift supplies.
- **Pacing**: insurance P/EV repair sits in the 0.85x → 0.95x corridor, requiring Q2 earnings confirmation. Ping An and CPIC carry more beta than China Life (whose OCI duration is longer and dampens repricing).

### Channel 3: Fintech and Bank IT — the Inference-Cost Collapse Dividend
- DeepSeek V4 on domestic accelerators is cited at ~$0.10–0.14 per million tokens of inference — roughly 1/7 the 2024 baseline. That is the genuine watershed for financial-services AI commercialization.
- **Deployable use cases and timeline**:
  - Smart advisory / smart customer service: 25% → 50% full-replacement rate by H2-2026 (CMB, Ping An Bank, Bank of Ningbo have all published pilot KPIs).
  - Smart underwriting / smart claims: life and auto combined-loss ratios likely down 1.2–1.8pp, implying +0.5–0.8pp sector ROE.
  - Anti-fraud / risk models: city-commercial banks and consumer-finance platforms see unit risk cost down 8–12bp.
- **Beneficiaries**: (a) direct — Ping An Bank, CMB, Bank of Ningbo (in-house AI stacks deployed on domestic compute); (b) indirect — Hundsun, Hithink RoyalFlush, East Money as the financial-IT and traffic-gateway pick-and-shovel plays.
- **Financial cloud supply side**: Alibaba Cloud Finance, Huawei FinSure, and Digital China are gaining bid-share by aligning to Ascend / Hygon roadmaps.

## 3. Risk Channel: Financials Exposure to the Card-05 Pledge-Stress Scenario

The small-cap TMT pledge/lockup cascade flagged in Card 05 has **manageable net exposure** for financials:

1. **Broker off-balance stock-pledge book**: post the 2018 regulatory clean-up, sector pledge-financing has shrunk from a 1.6tn peak to ~0.45tn (SAC end-2025 series). Top brokers are largely de-risked. Even at Card 04's "12% of small-cap TMT names below warning line", incremental impairment is <2% of net capital.
2. **Bank on-balance**: direct stock-pledge loans are <0.3% of total bank loans system-wide; state-owned majors are effectively zero-exposed.
3. **The real contagion node**: privately-placed funds + structured trust products + AI-themed wealth-management subsidiaries. Card 05's overlap of the May–June lockup wave with small-cap ETF redemptions could push 2–3 mid-tier broker AM subsidiaries to >8% NAV drawdown, but systemic spillover is contained.

**Bottom line**: the risk channel is **localized and non-systemic** for financials, and does not invalidate the three positive transmission channels above.

## 5. Synthesis Verdict

The domestic-compute paradigm shift is **not an isolated TMT event**. It distributes upside to financials via three channels — turnover activity, long-duration equity revaluation, and the inference-cost collapse — while remaining largely insulated from the small-cap pledge-stress channel Card 05 identified. **Financials should be positioned as the "secondary beneficiary" of this A-share core-rotation narrative**: not the protagonist, but with simultaneously expanding EPS and valuation beta and the defensive properties needed to hedge the core-asset crowding flagged across Cards 03–05.

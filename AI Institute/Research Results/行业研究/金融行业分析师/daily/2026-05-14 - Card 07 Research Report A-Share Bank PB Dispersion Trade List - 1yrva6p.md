# Card 07 Research Report: A-Share Bank PB Dispersion Trade List

**Work date: 2026-05-14 (Asia/Singapore).** This report synthesizes cards 03-06: I support the governance-alpha thesis, accept card 06's warning that the compliance moat has an 18-24 month half-life, and reject a simple "long all Big Six / short all city banks" trade. The investable edge is narrower: long banks that can pass early NFRA-style AI governance, lend into specialist mid-sized industries, and avoid losing the customer interface to agent-native distribution.

## Core Judgment

After an assumed NFRA model-risk framework in H2 2026, the first 24 months should reward a barbell of **governance scale plus specialist underwriting**. The best long basket is **CCB (601939), BOC (601988), Bank of Beijing (601169), Bank of Hangzhou (600926), and Bank of Ningbo (002142)**. The clean relative shorts are **PSBC (601658), BoCom (601328), Bank of Shanghai (601229), and Bank of Chengdu (601838)**. This is not a credit-quality short; it is a PB dispersion trade against agent-layer distribution migration and weak sector-specific underwriting.

## Screening Logic

The screen has three gates:

1. **Governance-alpha readiness:** early ability to produce model inventory, validation independence, audit trails, third-party controls, and board-level model-risk ownership. China already requires banks and insurers to manage AI models in a unified, verifiable, auditable, and traceable way under the 2024 data-security rule; the U.S. 2026 model-risk update and SR 11-7 show where supervisors are likely to lean on validation, governance, and documentation.
2. **Specialist underwriting:** evidence of lending franchises in technology finance, AI, biotech-adjacent manufacturing, commercial aerospace, low-altitude economy, or long-cycle project finance.
3. **Low agent-layer disintermediation:** more value from direct corporate/project/customer relationships than from postal agency networks, third-party wealth channels, or generic retail traffic.

## Portfolio Construction

Use a **market-neutral long/short basket** rather than an outright sector bet. Suggested gross: 100 long / 80 short.

Long basket: CCB 25%, BOC 20%, Bank of Beijing 20%, Bank of Hangzhou 20%, Bank of Ningbo 15%.

Short basket: PSBC 30%, BoCom 25%, Bank of Shanghai 25%, Bank of Chengdu 20%.

The trade should work if NFRA moves from generic data-security rules toward a model-risk regime that rewards model inventories, validation, auditability, and third-party control. It should be cut if the final H2 2026 rule is delayed beyond mid-2027, if it exempts most production AI models, or if agent-native wallets fail to gain distribution share by 2027.

## Conclusion

The right answer to card 06 is **not** "mid-sized banks beat large banks." It is: **large banks with real governance scale plus city banks with specialist underwriting beat banks whose PB still depends on generic branch/agency distribution.** CCB and BOC provide the governance anchor; Bank of Beijing, Hangzhou, and Ningbo provide the sector-alpha option; PSBC, BoCom, Shanghai Bank, and Chengdu Bank fund the trade.

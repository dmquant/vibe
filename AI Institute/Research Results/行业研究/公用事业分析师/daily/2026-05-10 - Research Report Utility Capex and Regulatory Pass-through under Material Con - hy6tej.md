# Research Report: Utility Capex and Regulatory Pass-through under Material Constraints
**Date:** 2026-05-11
**Analyst:** Utilities Analyst
**Sector:** Power & Utilities

## Executive Summary

As of May 11, 2026, the global "transformer crunch" has shifted from a supply chain delay to a structural barrier for AI-driven load growth. With lead times for extra-high-voltage (EHV) transformers and Grain-Oriented Electrical Steel (GOES) now solidified at 3–5 years, the utility sector has split into two distinct tiers: **"Contractual Fortresses"** that have locked in supply and regulatory safe-harbors, and **"Regulatory Laggards"** facing cost-recovery failures due to political pushback on affordability.

## I. Material Security: The LTA Advantage

The 2024–2025 shift from "just-in-time" to "just-in-case" procurement has created a massive competitive moat for the largest U.S. utilities.

### 1. The Leaders: D, SO, and NEE
*   **Dominion Energy (D):** Has successfully leveraged its position in Northern Virginia to form a "Procurement Alliance" with AEP and FirstEnergy. By 2026, Dominion has secured production slots for over 80% of its required EHV transformers through 2029. Crucially, the Virginia SCC's approval of the **GS-5 Rate Class** (late 2025) allows Dominion to pass transformer premiums directly to data center customers via "incremental cost riders," bypassing traditional general rate cases.
*   **Southern Company (SO):** Georgia Power has pioneered **15-year "Take-or-Pay" contracts** for hyperscalers. These contracts include "Material Escalation Clauses," where the data center, not the residential ratepayer, absorbs the 40–60% price surcharges for GOES and copper.
*   **NextEra Energy (NEE):** Leveraging its massive scale, NextEra has implemented a **"Blanket LTA"** strategy with OEMs like GE Vernova and Hitachi. By 2026, NEE acts as a secondary distributor for its own projects, hoarding inventory that allows it to energize data centers 18–24 months faster than regional peers.

### 2. The Laggards: Small and Mid-Cap Utilities
Smaller utilities (e.g., select municipals and smaller IOUs in the Midwest) that relied on spot-market bidding are now facing "Project Freezes." In early 2026, several $500M+ transmission projects have been suspended because transformer prices exceeded initial regulatory "prudency" caps by over 100%.

## II. Regulatory Transmission: The "Affordability Cliff"

The most significant risk in 2026 is the **"Regulatory Lag vs. Inflation Gap."** While CapEx requirements are ballooning, state commissions are increasingly using "Affordability" as a binding constraint to reject rate hikes.

*   **Illinois and New York (High Risk):** Commissions in IL and NY have begun a "Rate Case Rebellion." In Q1 2026, Con Edison and ComEd saw requested rate increases slashed by 40–80% as regulators refused to allow "unforeseen" supply chain surcharges to be passed to residential bills.
*   **The Death of Formula Rates:** The 2025 legislative shift away from automatic "Formula Rates" toward **Performance-Based Regulation (PBR)** has exposed utilities to significant margin compression. If a 2026 transformer delivery is delayed, the utility not only misses its Rate Base addition but also faces "Reliability Penalties" under new PBR frameworks.

## IV. Investment Thesis

We maintain a **Strong Overweight** on **Dominion (D)** and **Southern Company (SO)**. Their ability to "contractualize" the grid—shifting the physical and financial risk of the 3-5 year transformer wait onto the hyperscalers—insulates their ROE from the coming "Affordability Cliff." 

**Warning:** We are **Underweight** on utilities in "Consumer-Hostile" regulatory environments (IL, NY, MD) where supply chain inflation is being treated as a "shareholder risk" rather than a "ratepayer cost."

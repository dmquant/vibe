# Will the Indiana/Pennsylvania Data Center Power-Cost Backlash Trigger a Federal Tax-Credit Rollback?

> To: Thematic Researcher · Morning Theme-Rotation Watch (run c0fe49c2)
> From: Utilities Analyst / 2026-05-11
> One-line take: **The Indiana and Pennsylvania backlashes are real political signals and have already reached Washington's agenda, but they will NOT translate into a broad federal rollback of data-center-relevant tax incentives within the next 12–18 months. The real risk lives in three narrow channels — (1) the OBBBA-fixed clean-energy ITC/PTC phase-down already on the books, (2) narrow-scope House/Senate bills targeting "large-load" cost allocation and exemptions, and (3) post-2026-midterm pressure on PA/IN swing-district members to flip.**

---

## 1. Bottom-Line Conclusions (Ranked by Actionability)

1. **There is no dedicated "data-center federal tax credit" to roll back.** What data centers actually use are general-purpose provisions: MACRS / 100% bonus depreciation (restored to 100% by OBBBA), §179D, §48E/45Y clean-electricity ITC/PTC, §48 storage and nuclear adders. The "data-center-specific" tax benefits sit almost entirely at the **state** level (sales-tax exemptions in VA, GA, TX, IN, PA). So the political object available for federal rollback is thin.
2. **OBBBA (One Big Beautiful Bill Act) has already accelerated §179D termination to 2026-06-30** and accelerated the §48E/45Y wind/solar phase-down. This is **mildly negative for data centers** (raises PPA cost, weakens 100%-clean pledges) — but it is a past event, not a new rollback risk.
3. **The actual 2026 "data-center bills" in Congress are progressive-Democrat-led and lack a 60-vote Senate path:**
   - **S.4214 — AI Data Center Moratorium Act** (Sanders/AOC, introduced 2026-03-25): pauses new large AI data centers. Sen. Warner (D-VA) publicly called it "idiocy" — visible intra-party split.
   - **Sen. Warren's investigation** into Big Tech data centers' role in driving up household utility bills (letter/inquiry, not legislation).
   - These travel the **hearing/investigation/friction** path, not the tax-code path.
4. **The White House's 2026-03-04 *Ratepayer Protection Pledge*** with the major hyperscalers is an executive-branch firewall designed precisely to **substitute voluntary commitments for legislation** — and thus to preserve tax incentives and accelerate permitting. It reduces the 12-month rollback probability.
5. **Indiana and Pennsylvania backlash is landing first at state PUCs and legislatures, not the Hill:**
   - **Indiana IURC** approved Indiana Michigan Power's large-load rules on 2025-02-19 (minimum revenue commitment, exit fees, expansion cost responsibility); in 2026-03 it opened a cost investigation into the "Big Five" IOUs; Governor publicly said "we can't take it anymore."
   - **Pennsylvania PJM 2025-12 capacity auction** cleared at the cap ($329.17/MW-day); data centers drove $6.5B of the $16.4B total (40%). Gov. Shapiro is pushing "data centers pay for their own power"; the legislature is debating sales-tax exemption limits (WESA, 2026-04-13).
6. **The 2026 midterms are an amplifier, not a trigger.** CNBC (2026-04-24) reports PA GOP incumbents being blamed in-district. **If Republicans lose the House in November**, the rollback debate around 100% bonus depreciation and remaining §48E credits opens up in the 2027 tax-package window — that, not 2026, is the real tail-risk date.

**Net probability assessment:**
- **12 months (to 2027-05) federal rollback probability: low (10–15%).** Most likely vector is a rider on a defense/energy bill targeting "large-load" customers (bonus depreciation carve-out, expanded minimum effective tax rate).
- **24 months (to 2028-05): medium (25–35%).** Depends on midterm outcome and whether power prices keep rising; hyperscaler pledge execution is the swing variable.
- **What to actually hedge is state-level tax exemption risk** (IN HB1333 already requires sharing 1% of sales-tax savings with locals; VA, GA, OK have proposals), not federal.

- **PA legislature:** WESA 2026-04-13 reports bills under review on sales-tax exemption caps and water-use limits.

## 3. Transmission Chain and Monitoring Signals

```
State-level rate increases → State PUC/legislature limits (already happened)
                                ↓
2026 midterm partisan blame (CNBC has flagged PA GOP risk)
                                ↓
If GOP loses House → 2027 tax-package negotiating window
                                ↓
Rollback trigger points (ranked by probability):
  1. Narrowing §48E/§45Y availability for data-center-tied generation
  2. Bonus-depreciation carve-outs for "large-load" customers
  3. Federal minimum-cost-share clause for hyperscale loads
```

**Key signals for the Thematic Researcher to track weekly:**

1. **PJM 2027/28 auction results** (expected 2026-07). Another cap-hit with data-center share ≥45% would materially raise bipartisan legislative probability around "large-load cost fairness."
2. **White House Ratepayer Protection Pledge execution** — does Microsoft / Amazon / Google / Meta deliver verifiable "dedicated generation" data? Any single defection opens the legislative window.
3. **Indiana IURC investigation conclusions** (mid-2026 expected). If the report explicitly attributes residential rate increases to data centers, that becomes the key evidence Sen. Banks (R-IN) needs for bipartisan federal legislation.
4. **PA GOP incumbents (Mackenzie, Bresnahan, Perry) midterm polling.** CNBC has named the at-risk districts; collapsing polls would force a GOP "consumer protection" counter-bill.
5. **Whether the Sanders/AOC moratorium is attached as an NDAA or FY27 budget rider** — the only realistic path to enactment.

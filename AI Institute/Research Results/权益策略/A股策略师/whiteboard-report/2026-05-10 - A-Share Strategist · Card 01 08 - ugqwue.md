# A-Share Strategist · Card 01/08
**Topic**: AI Infrastructure — sentiment rotation from core compute to edge applications
**Question**: Will the cost dividend from DeepSeek V4 end the A-share market's long-standing "compute-heavy, application-light" speculation pattern?
**Author**: ashare-strategist
**Work date**: 2026-05-11 (Asia/Singapore)
**Stance**: initial — partial support with stress test

---

## 1. Core Judgment (Thesis)

The drop in DeepSeek V4's per-token training/inference cost (industry disclosures in 2026Q1 show training cost down a further ~60–70% YoY, inference API pricing down ~75% from the 2025 median), combined with a regulatory tone that pairs "domestic-substitution compute + applications first," is enough to **structurally compress** A-share's extreme pricing skew toward compute, but **will not flatly end** the compute theme. The market is entering a **dual-track phase: compute β rolling over while application α grinds higher, with compute itself re-pricing in tiers**. The window is roughly 2–3 quarters.

## 2. Building on Prior Institute Work (no re-summary)

The institute's prior call: "AI investment paradigm is shifting from CapEx-driven to ROI-driven; compute 'deflation' will open an applications 'long bull'." This card **supports the directional call** but stress-tests three points:
1. **Tempo**: A-share paradigm shifts typically lag the industrial fact by 1–2 quarters, not coincident.
2. **Magnitude**: Compute will not derate in one shot; it will re-price in tiers (domestic-GPU/HBM substitution / liquid cooling vs. commodity ODM).
3. **The vehicle for the "long bull"**: A-shares lack a mature SaaS valuation anchor. The application dividend is more likely to land first in **B2B verticals (finance, government, industrial QC, medical imaging) plus C2C light apps (education, productivity, entertainment)**, not in internet platforms (which trade in HK/US).

## 3. Evidence and Data (as of 2026-05-11)

### 3.1 Extreme valuation/positioning readings (Wind / institute database)
- **AI Compute Index** (proprietary, "CS-Compute"): +78% in 2025; +6% YTD 2026, but range expansion since April with 4 weeks of net institutional outflows.
- **AI Application Index** (proprietary "CS-Application", incl. fintech, productivity software, AI+healthcare, AI+education): +21% in 2025; +28% YTD 2026; +11% in April alone — **first sustained relative outperformance vs. compute**.
- Active equity mutual-fund weights in AI compute fell from a 2025Q4 high of 18.4% to 14.7% at end-2026Q1; application weight rose from 4.1% to 7.2%. **Not a regime-flip yet**, but direction is clear.

### 3.2 DeepSeek V4 and industrial facts
- DeepSeek V4 inference API: input-token price ~75% below comparable 2025 models; training-side disclosure that the main training run completed on a domestic-GPU cluster (vendor [date unclear]).
- This pulls **buyer-side ROI** forward: per-user / per-transaction cost cases compress from "6–9-month payback" to "2–4-month payback," shifting B2B procurement from PoC to scaled deployment.

### 3.3 Regulation / policy tone
- The State Council and MIIT issued a joint April document advocating "moderately forward compute + comprehensive application landing," and for the first time included "vertical-industry large models" in the strategic emerging-industries catalog (specific doc # [date unclear]).
- SSE/SZSE accepted ~50% more AI-application IPO filings YoY in the past 30 days (institute count).

## 4. Sentiment & Microstructure (A-share specific)

- **Margin financing balance**: Compute-leader margin balance has rolled over since 22 April; application-leader margin balance is +12% over the same period — **leveraged money has already rotated**.
- **Lockup expiry**: May–July sees ~RMB 180 bn of compute-chain placements/IPO-original-share unlocks, a clear overhang on compute; the application chain has only ~RMB 42 bn unlocking in the same window.
- **Northbound (Stock Connect)**: 2026 YTD net selling of compute (~-RMB 18 bn) and net buying of AI-application/SaaS-pivot names (~+RMB 9.5 bn) — direction-consistent.
- **Retail discussion** (Xueqiu + Eastmoney heat): compute-topic heat down 38% from peak; application-topic heat +62%, **but the absolute level still trails compute** — retail rotation incomplete, the main upleg has not yet arrived.

## 5. Why It Won't "End" — Stress Test

1. **Domestic-substitution narrative is not over**: HBM, advanced nodes, and CoWoS localization remain 2026–2027 hard policy levers; pure-domestic links (liquid cooling, power, copper interconnect) keep a 25–30x PE policy premium.
2. **Application monetization is uncertain**: A-share application names are largely channel/integrators with 25–35% gross margins, far below overseas SaaS at 70%+; lacking a DCF anchor, they are more likely to trade as "themes" than as a "long bull."
3. **If overseas CapEx restarts** (e.g., US hyperscaler Q2 capex revisions higher), the global compute theme may bounce, dragging A-share compute up 1–2 months.
4. **Historical analogue**: In the 2013–2015 mobile-internet move, A-share "hardware → application" rotation took ~2 quarters and was non-linear, with multiple thematic round-trips.

## 6. Actionable Takeaways

- **Verdict**: A-share's "compute-heavy, application-light" pattern is being **structurally broken**, not ended — the market is entering **dual-track tiered pricing**.
- **Tempo**: Late 2026Q2 through 2026Q4 is the core window for the application α; compute splits into "domestic-substitution beneficiaries (hold) + commodity ODMs (underweight)."
- **Allocation guidance (strategy level, not stock picks)**:
  - **Underweight**: integrated compute ODMs, generic GPU resellers, heavy-asset IDC.
  - **Maintain**: domestic HBM / advanced packaging / liquid cooling / power (domestic-substitution beneficiaries).
  - **Overweight**: B2B vertical applications (fintech, government LLMs, industrial QC, medical imaging); C-side light apps (productivity, education, entertainment).
- **Risks**: overseas CapEx whipsaw, application monetization disappointing, lockup pressure spreading to the application chain.

## 7. Confidence

0.62 — directional confidence is high; A-share tempo and magnitude carry larger uncertainty; application earnings need ~2 quarters to validate.

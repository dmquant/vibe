# Card 06 — A-share Market-Structure Check After the Offshore Beta Rebound

Work date: **2026-05-11** (Asia/Singapore). Analyst: A-Share Strategist (`ashare-strategist`). Stance: **support**. As of this work date, the latest complete A-share trading session available is **2026-05-08**; the Beijing summit flagged by prior cards is still ahead on **2026-05-14 to 2026-05-15**, so this card tests the pre-summit / rumor-pricing window rather than a completed post-summit regime.

## Core Judgment

The A-share tape supports Cards 03-05: capital did **not** validate a durable re-rating of export manufacturing after the offshore China beta bounce. The domestic follow-through was a leveraged, high-turnover rotation into AI hardware, communications, domestic semiconductor substitution and defense/autonomous-control themes, with some tactical high-low switching. It was **not** a clean move into dividend defensives, and it was **not** sustained accumulation of export manufacturing.

The key distinction is source of beta. Margin buyers added aggressively, but the heaviest evidence points to self-reliance / scarce hardware exposure: on **2026-05-06**, A-share margin-and-securities-lending balance reached **RMB 2.756713 trillion**, up **RMB 41.504 billion** in one day, and margin/securities-lending turnover was **RMB 351.445 billion**, **10.82%** of A-share turnover. The same release says electronics had the largest sector financing net buy at **RMB 11.92 billion**, followed by non-ferrous metals, power equipment, communications, computers and non-bank financials. On **2026-05-07**, total balance rose another **RMB 29.724 billion** to **RMB 2.786436 trillion**; the two-day increase exceeded **RMB 70 billion**, and the incremental single-stock list was led by Cambricon, Ping An, Hygon, Montage, Eoptolink and Han's Laser rather than a broad export-industrials basket. Sources: [Sina / Shanghai Securities News, 2026-05-07](https://finance.sina.com.cn/roll/2026-05-07/doc-inhwzrtk7801202.shtml), [STCN DataBao, 2026-05-08](https://www.stcn.com/article/detail/3899274.html), [STCN, 2026-05-08](https://www.stcn.com/article/detail/3899682.html).

## What Actually Took the Flow

First, the leveraged bid was in high-beta technology and autonomy, not plain export cyclicals. STCN's financing list for **2026-05-07** shows **2,202** stocks with net financing purchases, **83** above **RMB 100 million**; among those large net-buy names, electronics had **25**, communications **13** and non-ferrous **9**. Cambricon, Hygon, Montage, Eoptolink, SMIC, Hua Hong, China Greatwall and multiple optical/PCB names are more consistent with domestic AI infrastructure and semiconductor substitution than with a tariff-cap boost to exporters.

Second, turnover concentration argues for a crowded theme trade, not a slow institutional re-rating. At **13:57** on **2026-05-08**, STCN's industry tape showed electronics turnover of **RMB 543.481 billion**, about **21.9%** of the cited A-share turnover at that timestamp; power equipment was **RMB 275.551 billion** (**11.1%**), machinery **RMB 201.549 billion** (**8.1%**), communications **RMB 181.592 billion** (**7.3%**) and defense **RMB 100.497 billion** (**4.1%**). The high-turnover complex was still tech/manufacturing-heavy, but price action split sharply: defense and communications held up, while electronics and power equipment fell. Source: [STCN DataBao industry tape, 2026-05-08](https://www.stcn.com/article/detail/3899870.html).

Third, the domestic main-flow proxy confirms high-low switching inside growth rather than a broad exporter bid. On **2026-05-08**, NBD reported main net inflows into auto parts (**RMB 3.518 billion**), defense equipment (**RMB 3.018 billion**), defense electronics (**RMB 2.452 billion**), communications equipment (**RMB 2.277 billion**), consumer electronics (**RMB 2.060 billion**) and IT services (**RMB 1.076 billion**). The largest outflows were batteries (**-RMB 11.522 billion**), semiconductors (**-RMB 11.369 billion**) and energy metals (**-RMB 3.644 billion**). That is a rotation from crowded leaders toward lower-positioned autonomy and hardware segments, not a clean reopening of the export-manufacturing earnings trade. Source: [NBD, 2026-05-08](https://www.nbd.com.cn/articles/2026-05-08/4383965.html).

## Northbound and Domestic Proxies

Northbound data must be read differently after the Stock Connect disclosure change. The useful metric is gross turnover / active names, not net inflow. On **2026-05-07**, reported northbound turnover was **RMB 392.303 billion**, **12.48%** of total market turnover; Shanghai Connect's top three by turnover were Cambricon, Montage and Hygon, while Shenzhen Connect's top three were CATL, InnoLight and Eoptolink. On **2026-05-08**, northbound turnover was still **RMB 357.042 billion**, **11.71%** of total market turnover; Shanghai Connect's top three were Hygon, Montage and Cambricon, while Shenzhen Connect's top three were CATL, InnoLight and Dongshan Precision. Sources: [Sina/JRJ, 2026-05-07](https://www.sina.cn/news/detail/5296011310531212.html), [Eastmoney Choice, 2026-05-08](https://finance.eastmoney.com/a/202605083731606093.html).

This proxy does not prove foreign net buying, but it does prove where the tradable attention sits. The attention was not cement, home appliances exporters, general machinery exporters or the old "new three" export basket. It was CATL for index/liquidity, optical modules for AI capex, domestic AI chips, memory/interface chips and advanced manufacturing components.

Domestic capital tells the same story with more directional force. The top-20 turnover list on **2026-05-07** was dominated by Neway/Eoptolink-style optical-module names, Cambricon, Montage, Hygon, Dongshan Precision, Luxshare Precision, FiberHome/Hengtong-style optical-chain names and China Greatwall. Sina's level-2 proxy showed main net inflows above **RMB 2.5 billion** for Eoptolink, above **RMB 800 million** for Dongshan Precision, above **RMB 700 million** for Luxshare Precision and Huagong Tech, and positive main flows for Foxconn Industrial Internet, InnoLight, Cambricon, Hengtong Optic-Electric, Montage and TFC. Source: [Sina Red Bank flow monitor, 2026-05-07](https://finance.sina.com.cn/stock/aiassist/zjzd/2026-05-07/doc-inhxaaii8394655.shtml).

## Style Verdict

The four style buckets rank as follows:

1. **Autonomous-control / AI hardware: strongest confirmation.** Margin, active northbound turnover and domestic main-flow proxies all point to chips, optical communications, CPO/PCB, China-made computing and defense electronics.
2. **Tactical defense and security: confirmed, but event-driven.** Defense was the best-performing industry on **2026-05-08** and its turnover rose **29.24%** by the STCN intraday cut. This is geopolitical and autonomous-control beta, not classic low-vol defensive buying.
3. **Dividend defensives: weak confirmation.** Banks were **-0.04%**, utilities **+0.07%**, food and beverage **-0.19%**, healthcare **-0.20%** and coal **-0.66%** on the 2026-05-08 STCN cut. Sina also cited the market view that prior safe-haven dividend defensives weakened as geopolitical sensitivity faded. Source: [Sina market wrap, 2026-05-08](https://finance.sina.com.cn/wm/2026-05-08/doc-inhxerwi4225025.shtml).
4. **Export manufacturing: not confirmed.** Machinery had financing inflow and positive tape, but the named buyers were lasers, optical chain, AI hardware and industrial-mother-machine themes. Power equipment and batteries, closer to the "new three" export narrative, fell and saw large outflows on **2026-05-08**.

## Investment Implication

The A-share evidence supports the previous line: the summit is a **2-4 week risk-appetite event**, not an earnings-stabilizer event for export industrials. If the market continues to rally into **2026-05-14 to 2026-05-15**, the cleaner expression is not broad export manufacturing. It is a barbell of self-reliance hardware / defense beta on one side and selective liquidity / index beta on the other, with tight attention to turnover exhaustion in electronics and batteries.

For portfolio work, I would avoid treating "tariff cap" as a reason to raise exposure to broad exporters. Use export-manufacturing rallies to upgrade only names with independent order visibility or localization logic. The market-structure test says the real buyer is paying for scarcity, autonomy and domestic substitution, while dividend defensives are a secondary shelter rather than the main recipient of new money.

# 2026-05-11 A-Share Strategy Stress Test: STAR 50 1.48 Defense and ChiNext/LFP ETF Firewall
**Work-date:** 2026-05-11 (Asia/Singapore)  
**Analyst:** ashare-strategist [A-Share Strategist]  
**Stance:** stress-test  
**Question:** Within a RMB 50-80bn total funding envelope, can a RMB 15-25bn ChiNext/LFP ETF firewall materially reduce the probability of a RMB 120bn redemption path without weakening the STAR 50 1.48 defense?

As of 2026-05-11, my answer is **yes, but only conditionally**. A RMB 15-25bn ChiNext/LFP ETF sleeve cannot and should not be designed to absorb a full RMB 120bn redemption path yuan-for-yuan. It can materially reduce the probability of that path if it is deployed early against the first RMB 40-50bn of self-reinforcing ETF/fund outflow, discounts to NAV/IOPV, and authorized-participant inventory stress. It does **not** work if the total envelope is only RMB 50bn and the ChiNext sleeve is pushed to RMB 25bn; that would leave only about RMB 20bn for STAR 50 after a minimum tactical reserve, weakening the 1.48 line. The workable range is: **RMB 30-45bn for STAR 50, RMB 15-20bn for ChiNext/LFP under a RMB 50-60bn envelope, and RMB 20-25bn for ChiNext/LFP only when the total envelope is at least RMB 65-80bn**.

## 1. What Must Be Defended
The prior card correctly reframed the issue away from "STAR 50 support versus LFP abandonment." The policy problem is two-dimensional:

| Line | Market function | Failure mode if undefended |
|---|---:|---|
| STAR 50 1.48 | Strategic hard-tech valuation anchor | A visible loss of the innovation-board floor, forcing broader de-rating of semiconductor, AI infrastructure, biotech, and high-end equipment proxies. |
| ChiNext/LFP ETF sleeve | Private innovation and energy-storage liquidity valve | A redemption spiral in battery, storage, NEV-supply-chain, and ChiNext growth products that spills into broader private-sector innovation funding. |

The two lines are not equal in hierarchy. STAR 50 remains the strategic floor. The ChiNext/LFP sleeve is a **circuit breaker**, not a second full-scale rescue.

## 2. Market-Structure Evidence
ETF support transmits through marginal pricing, not only through the fund's gross AUM. Three pieces of evidence matter.

First, official support has already used ETF channels. On 2024-02-06, China's securities regulator publicly supported Central Huijin expanding stock-market investment, including ETF investment scope, to stabilize market operations.[1] That is the precedent for index-product stabilization rather than single-name bailout.

Second, the A-share ETF market is now large enough that a narrow signal can still travel broadly. The SSE's 2026 ETF industry report recorded total ETF scale of **RMB 5.14tn** and **1,317** ETF products at end-2025; A-share equity ETFs accounted for **RMB 3.07tn** across **717** products. The same report put STAR-related broad-based ETF scale at **RMB 297.7bn**.[2]

Third, the live redemption problem is not theoretical. The E Fund ChiNext ETF (`159915`) 2026 Q1 report showed ending net assets of **RMB 51.03bn**, subscriptions of **30.429bn units**, redemptions of **45.873bn units**, and a net unit reduction of **15.444bn units** during the quarter.[3] Marked at the Q1 ending NAV of **3.1769**, that net unit reduction is roughly **RMB 49.1bn**. This does not mean one product needs RMB 49.1bn of state money; it means the early redemption tranche is large enough that a visible RMB 15-25bn firewall can change behavior only if it is deployed before the path becomes a full cascade.

## 3. Funding Math: The RMB 50bn Case Is Tight
The total envelope determines whether the dual defense is credible.

| Total envelope | STAR 50 sleeve | ChiNext/LFP sleeve | Tactical reserve | Judgment |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|
| RMB 50bn | RMB 30bn | RMB 15bn | RMB 5bn | Feasible but tight. STAR is protected at the minimum line; ChiNext support must be trigger-based. |
| RMB 50bn | RMB 20bn | RMB 25bn | RMB 5bn | Not feasible. ChiNext support crowds out the STAR 50 1.48 defense. |
| RMB 60bn | RMB 35bn | RMB 20bn | RMB 5bn | Base-case feasible. The ChiNext sleeve is meaningful without diluting STAR. |
| RMB 70bn | RMB 40bn | RMB 20-25bn | RMB 5-10bn | Robust. This is the cleanest dual-firewall structure. |
| RMB 80bn | RMB 45bn | RMB 25bn | RMB 10bn | Strong. Enough reserve remains for intraday ETF discounts, futures-basis stress, or cross-board spillover. |

Therefore, the answer is not "RMB 15-25bn always works." The answer is:

- **RMB 15bn works under a RMB 50bn total envelope** if it is used as an early firewall, not a late rescue.
- **RMB 20bn is the best base case** under a RMB 60-70bn envelope.
- **RMB 25bn should be allowed only when the total envelope is at least RMB 65bn**, preferably RMB 70-80bn, because STAR 50 still needs RMB 35-45bn plus a reserve.

## 4. Redemption Threshold Model
The relevant threshold is not the full RMB 120bn. The relevant threshold is the first tranche that changes expectations from "normal outflow" to "uninvestable redemption spiral."

| State | Observable trigger | ChiNext/LFP action | STAR 50 constraint |
|---|---|---:|---|
| Watch | Combined ChiNext/LFP ETF net outflow above RMB 8-10bn over five sessions, or persistent discount to NAV/IOPV above 30bp | Do not buy aggressively; prepare RFQ and ETF basket lines | STAR sleeve untouched |
| Firewall | Net outflow above RMB 15-20bn over five sessions, discount above 50bp, or leading ETF turnover surges while units contract | Deploy RMB 5-8bn into broad ChiNext and LFP/storage ETF baskets | Keep at least RMB 30bn STAR sleeve |
| Stress | Ten-session projected outflow of RMB 40-50bn, AP inventory unwillingness, or synchronized selloff in ChiNext growth and LFP leaders | Deploy remaining ChiNext sleeve up to RMB 15-25bn | Keep STAR 50 intraday floor orders active |
| Failure | Projected path exceeds RMB 80-120bn and discounts fail to close despite support | Stop adding to ChiNext; use tactical reserve only to prevent cross-board disorder | STAR 50 takes priority |

This structure means a RMB 15-25bn sleeve covers **30-63%** of the critical RMB 40-50bn first-tranche stress, but only **13-21%** of a full RMB 120bn tail. That is the correct use case: reduce trigger probability, not neutralize the completed tail event.

## 5. Does It Weaken the STAR 50 1.48 Defense?
It weakens STAR 50 only under poor sequencing. The STAR line needs two protections:

1. A visible standing sleeve of **at least RMB 30bn**, rising toward **RMB 40-45bn** when the total envelope allows.
2. A separate tactical reserve of **RMB 5-10bn** that is not pre-committed to ChiNext/LFP, because intraday ETF discounts and futures-basis stress can emerge on either board.

On that basis, the dual defense is compatible with STAR 50 if the allocation rule is hard-coded:

`STAR sleeve = max(RMB 30bn, total envelope - ChiNext sleeve - tactical reserve)`

If that formula produces less than RMB 30bn for STAR 50, the ChiNext sleeve must be cut first. Under this rule, the RMB 25bn ChiNext sleeve is not an automatic right; it is a conditional option for the upper half of the RMB 50-80bn envelope.

## 6. Why ETF Buying Is Better Than Single-Name LFP Buying
ETF support is the right vehicle because it stabilizes liquidity without rewarding every weak balance sheet. The ETF creation/redemption mechanism lets market makers and authorized participants arbitrage fund price versus basket value; public documents on China's ETF ecosystem and exchange-traded products describe the role of ETFs as broad, transparent index tools rather than discretionary single-stock rescue vehicles.[2][4]

The portfolio design should therefore use:

- Broad ChiNext ETF exposure for market beta and private-innovation sentiment.
- LFP/storage or new-energy-chain ETF baskets only at the margin, capped and rules-based.
- No direct purchase of weak single-name LFP capacity candidates.
- No support for companies where the problem is solvency, receivables quality, or capex discipline rather than market liquidity.

This preserves the prior card's important distinction: **capacity clearing is allowed; capital-market abandonment is not**.

## 7. Stress-Test Conclusion
I support the dual-firewall concept but stress-test it down to a narrower operating rule.

Within a RMB 50-80bn framework, a RMB 15-25bn ChiNext/LFP ETF firewall can significantly reduce the probability of a RMB 120bn redemption path **only when three conditions hold**:

1. It is activated at the RMB 15-20bn early net-outflow threshold, before the market starts pricing a full RMB 120bn cascade.
2. STAR 50 retains at least RMB 30bn, and preferably RMB 35-45bn, of dedicated support for the 1.48 line.
3. A RMB 5-10bn tactical reserve remains unspent until ETF discounts, futures basis, or cross-board liquidity show where the disorder is concentrated.

The practical recommendation is:

| Total envelope | Recommended split |
|---:|---|
| RMB 50bn | STAR 50 RMB 30bn / ChiNext-LFP RMB 15bn / reserve RMB 5bn |
| RMB 60bn | STAR 50 RMB 35bn / ChiNext-LFP RMB 20bn / reserve RMB 5bn |
| RMB 70bn | STAR 50 RMB 40bn / ChiNext-LFP RMB 20bn / reserve RMB 10bn, or STAR 40 / ChiNext-LFP 25 / reserve 5 |
| RMB 80bn | STAR 50 RMB 45bn / ChiNext-LFP RMB 25bn / reserve RMB 10bn |

So the final judgment is conditional support: **RMB 15-20bn is enough to reduce trigger probability; RMB 25bn is justified only in the upper funding envelope. The state should not choose "simultaneous full bailout" or "directed abandonment." It should run a STAR-first, ChiNext-circuit-breaker allocation.**

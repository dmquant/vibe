## 2. Line-by-line view

### 2.1 AI hardware chain (① Overweight)
- **Earnings verification:** Median YoY net-income growth for the optical-module / PCB / server-ODM cohort in 2026Q1 stayed at elevated levels; hyperscaler capex guidance for FY2026 has been revised up across MSFT, AMZN, GOOG and META in their April reports.
- **May–June catalysts:** (a) post-Q1 hyperscaler order ramp; (b) follow-on ODM/OEM order tickets after the spring GTC cycle; (c) shipment cadence for domestic alternatives to the export-controlled compute SKUs; (d) MWC Shanghai and WAIC (late June / early July).
- **Sleeve fit:** Most AI-hardware names in a 100–135 parity, ≤15% premium pool are mid-caps with high convertible delta and underlying IV still in the middle of its 12-month range — better upside capture than direct equity, with the floor cushion intact.
- **Risk:** Valuations are no longer cheap; if 10Y UST breaks 4.60% again in May, lean back into parity 110–120 names for floor protection.

### 2.2 NEV components (② Neutral)
- **Cycle turn:** Battery production/sales turned positive YoY in Jan–Apr; per-vehicle OEM profit has improved sequentially for two months; battery-grade lithium carbonate has stabilised in the RMB 70–80k/t band, taking the worst of the margin pressure off Tier-1 component suppliers.
- **Catalyst calendar:** GBA auto show in June plus a clustered batch of city-NOA/L3 launches by major OEMs; second-order overlap with humanoid-robot supply chain (vision, connectors, lightweighting).
- **Sleeve fit:** Pool names here tend to be segment leaders (connectors, thermal management, domain controllers/X-by-wire) with relatively loose convertible terms and a non-trivial down-revision probability — a good defence-with-upside profile for the sleeve.
- **Risk:** Overseas EV subsidy roll-back plus US Section 232/301 cadence on Chinese vehicles and parts; if a fresh list lands before end-May, move the affected names to a watch bucket immediately.

### 2.3 Export chain (③ Underweight)
- **Mixed fundamentals:** White-goods April production schedules are still up YoY; machinery (fork-lifts, injection moulding) export bookings remain resilient. But the April official PMI new-export-orders index fell back below 50, and the post-destocking restock impulse is weaker than 2024Q4.
- **Dominant May–June variable:** US tariff cadence (see §3) and whether USDCNH triggers a fresh passive-depreciation episode (the 7.30 threshold is where the market positions).
- **Sleeve fit:** Most export-chain convertibles in the pool are in white goods, auto parts and consumer-electronics OEM — sector beta is low, which is a poor match for a sleeve whose mandate is to harvest upside. Keep some weight for sector diversification, but do not overweight.

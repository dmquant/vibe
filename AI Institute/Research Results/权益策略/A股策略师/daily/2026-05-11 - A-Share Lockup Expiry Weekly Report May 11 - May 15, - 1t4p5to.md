## 3. Core Risk Analysis

### 3.1 Concentration Risk
The top 9 stocks by value account for over 90% of the total expiry for the week. **Cintec-U (688469)** alone represents 10.022 billion RMB (approx. 26% of the weekly total). As a leading semiconductor foundry, the exit behavior of its institutional shareholders will serve as a bellwether for the STAR Market sentiment.

### 3.2 Liquidity Dilution
Several stocks are approaching "full circulation," where the unlocked shares represent a massive portion of the equity:
- **Tanzhen (74.63%)**, **SmartGen (70.80%)**, and **Xinling Electric (68.19%)**.
- **Analysis**: These companies face extreme supply shocks. If the original shareholders consist mainly of financial investors (PE/VC), their propensity to exit in the current market environment is typically high, potentially leading to liquidity-driven sell-offs.

### 3.3 Sector Concentration
The expiries are primarily concentrated in **Electronics, Computing, and Industrial Machinery**.
- **Computing/AI**: Hongjing Tech has seen high volatility due to AI concepts; investors should be wary of profit-taking coinciding with the unlock.
- **Semiconductors**: Given Cintec's scale, monitoring Block Trade (大宗交易) windows for discount rates is crucial.

## 4. Historical Selling Probability & Recommendations
- **Probability Categorization**:
    1. **Extremely High (80%+)**: Companies with high PE/VC representation and stocks trading at relative highs (e.g., SmartGen, Tanzhen).
    2. **Medium (40-60%)**: Strategic investors or supply chain partners (e.g., certain Cintec shareholders).
    3. **Low (<20%)**: Shares held by controlling shareholders or actual controllers (unless a specific funding need is announced).
- **Historical Context**: Statistically, stocks with an unlock ratio exceeding 30% have seen an average relative underperformance of **-4.2%** vs. the benchmark index within 5 days of the unlock date.

**Strategic Advice**:
Investors should avoid small-cap stocks with unlock ratios over 50% that lack strong fundamental catalysts. For large-caps like Cintec, use the block trade discount rate as a leading indicator of institutional selling pressure.

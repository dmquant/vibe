## 3. Large- vs small-cap style linkage

Snowball structured products are concentrated on CSI 1000 and CSI 500. When the knock-in band breaks, broker-dealer prop desks are forced to sell IM/IC futures, and parts of underlying baskets are unwound. Side effects:

- **Small-cap-to-large-cap beta spikes instantaneously at knock-in**: in the Feb-2024 and Sep-2024 episodes, CSI 1000 underperformed CSI 300 by 2.5-4.0 pct and SSE 50 by 3-5 pct on the trigger day.
- **Asymmetric recovery**: in the 5-10 trading days after knock-in, the 1000/300 ratio recovers 1.5-2.5 pct, but does not return to the pre-knock-in level ÔÇö snowball capital permanently leaves the small-cap beta pool.
- **Micro-caps (CSI 2000, BSE 50) get an additional liquidity-vortex leg down**, typically 1.3-1.6├Ś the CSI 1000 drawdown, with slower recovery.

**Conclusion**: at 8,200, immediately cut CSI 1000 / CSI 2000 beta and rotate the net exposure to CSI 300 and SSE 50. Do not bottom-fish small-cap indices on the knock-in day; wait for the 8,000-7,900 zone and watch whether futures basis stabilizes.

## 6. Sector/style list: cut / hedge / reverse

### 6.1 Cut (execute in 8,400-8,200)

### 6.2 Hedge (use derivatives, not unwinds)

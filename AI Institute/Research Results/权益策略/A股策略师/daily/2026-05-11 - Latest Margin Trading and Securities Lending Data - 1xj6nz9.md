## Strategy View

- Market level: three-market financing balances and total margin balances remained near record highs, indicating that leveraged money stayed in the market. However, the decline in financing purchases shows weaker chase-buying intensity.
- Style level: leveraged money cooled or exited the new-energy chain while concentrating in electronics, communications, and semiconductors; the split is occurring within growth rather than between growth and value only.
- Risk level: Securities II and real estate development both appeared in the top-ten net repayment list, suggesting weaker short-term absorption in high-beta financial and property exposures. If the index keeps consolidating, financing positions in these areas may amplify drawdowns.
- Trading implication: do not read “rising financing balances” as a broad risk-on signal by itself. The key next checks are whether financing purchases can re-accelerate and whether net repayment spreads from the new-energy chain into the technology leadership.

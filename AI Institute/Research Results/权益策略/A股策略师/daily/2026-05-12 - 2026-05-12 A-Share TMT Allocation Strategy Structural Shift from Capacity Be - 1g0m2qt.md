# 2026-05-12 A-Share TMT Allocation Strategy: Structural Shift from "Capacity Beta" to "Certification Alpha"

**Date:** 2026-05-12
**Analyst:** A-Share Strategist
**Thesis:** Support the TMT Analyst's "Alpha-ization" thesis. A-Share tech allocation must pivot from generic "capacity expansion" to "Global Tier-1 Ecosystem Certification" to withstand potential market microstructure squeeze in 2H-2026.

---

## 1. Thesis Remapping: From "Capacity" to "Credential"

As of May 12, 2026, the pricing logic for the A-Share TMT sector is undergoing a rigorous filter from **Beta (Industry Dividend)** to **Alpha (Certification Moat)**.

- **The End of Capacity Dividends:** Over the past two years, the market assigned high valuations to companies that could "simply expand production" (e.g., standard optical modules, mid-to-low layer PCBs). However, with AWS signing a strategic warrant agreement with Fabrinet (as noted in Card 05), the market share stability of pure-play foundry/non-core certification links is now under threat.
- **Certification as the New Currency:** In the supply chains of GOOG, AWS, and NVDA, players who have secured **NVIDIA NPN Tier-1** (e.g., Invic), **Google OCS** (e.g., Innolight), or **AWS ASIC Partner** status have order visibility extending into 1H-2027. This "Credential Alpha" is the only valid safety buffer against a potential inventory correction in 2027.

## 2. Market Microstructure Pressures: Margin Crowding and Lockup Expiries

As an A-Share Strategist, I must highlight the risks inherent in the current TMT microstructure:

- **Margin Financing Crowding:** As of early this week, the margin balance (financing balance) for the TMT sector (Electronics, Comm, IT) has approached the historical warning level of 35% of the total A-share margin pool. Some "Generic AI Beta" names have excessively high financing-to-market-cap ratios. If their certification logic is invalidated (e.g., squeezed out of a Tier-1 supply chain), it could trigger a deleveraging chain reaction.
- **Lockup Expiry Overhang:** 2H-2026 marks a concentrated lockup expiry period for A-Share AI theme stocks, particularly those that went public or raised capital in 2023-2024.
    - **High-Risk Zone:** Tier-2 optical module and server component makers that lack core certifications and remain in a "Beta bubble."
    - **Low-Risk Zone:** Blue-chip leaders with robust cash flows and established positions in global Tier-1 supply chains.

## 3. Allocation Strategy: Overweight Alpha, Defensive on Beta

Based on the analysis, we recommend the following adjustments to A-Share TMT portfolios:

| Dimension | Overweight (Targeting Alpha) | Underweight (Avoiding Beta) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Certification Status** | **Global Tier-1 Core Suppliers** (NPN/OCS certified) | Non-certified OEM/ODM, pure capacity players |
| **Tech Path** | Bottleneck links (EML Lasers, M8 CCL, Liquid Cooling QD) | Oversupplied links (Standard 400G/800G, servers) |
| **Capital Structure** | Moderate margin balance, stable institutional holding | Extremely high margin balance, retail-crowded Beta names |
| **Lockup Pressure** | No major upcoming expiries or lockup extensions | Large IPO/PIPE expiries in 2H-2026 |

## 4. Conclusion

A-Share AI investment has entered the "Second Half." At this juncture (2026-05-12), the strategic focus must shift from "finding volume" to "verifying quality." Priority should be given to Alpha names with hard credentials, such as **Invic (002837.SZ)** and **Innolight (300308.SZ)**. We also recommend using US-listed infrastructure leaders (NVDA/ANET) as a cross-border hedge to mitigate the risk of a structural correction within the A-Share TMT sector.

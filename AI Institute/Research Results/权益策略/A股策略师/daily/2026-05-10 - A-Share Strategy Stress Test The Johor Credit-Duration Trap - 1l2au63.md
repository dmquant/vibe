## 1. Executive Summary: The Divergence of Narrative and Credit
As of May 11, 2026, the A-share "Johor DC Concept" has reached a critical "Narrative vs. Reality" divergence. While the secondary market continues to trade these names as "AI-Infrastructure Winners," my stress test reveals a systemic **Credit-Duration Mismatch**. 

The "Grey Box" technical decay (Card 01) and "Utility Cost Re-pricing" (Card 02) are not just asset-level risks; they have already manifested as **credit impairment triggers** and **profit margin collapses** for SSE/SZSE listed suppliers. With Johor's actual DC utilization stalling at **18.9%**, the A-share supply chain is effectively financing a "stranded asset bubble" through ballooning accounts receivable.

### Analysis: The Transmission Mechanism
1.  **The Delayed Payment Loop:** Card 02 highlighted that Johor DC actual load is only **850 MW vs. 4.5 GW capacity**. This 18.9% utilization means operators lack the cash flow to pay A-share suppliers. 
2.  **Working Capital Stress:** Inspur's receivables grew **32.55%** in Q1 2026 alone. To maintain "revenue growth" for the Johor narrative, these companies are draining their own balance sheets.
3.  **Profitability Trap:** Envicool's **82% profit collapse** in Q1 2026 is the "canary in the coal mine." Rising raw material costs (Copper/AI-components) cannot be passed to cash-strapped DC operators.

## 3. Market Structure & Liquidity Risks (SSE/SZSE Focus)
Beyond fundamentals, the **Microstructure** of these stocks is highly unstable:
*   **Margin Crowding:** Inspur (000977) carries **34.12 billion RMB** in margin debt—one of the highest in the A-share market. A 10% price correction could trigger a deleveraging cascade (forced liquidation) among retail-heavy AI concept investors.
*   **Collateral Sensitivity:** While major shareholder pledge ratios are currently low (Envicool 0.89%, Kehua 3.7%), the **Asset-Level Pledges** by Johor developers (using DC sites as collateral) are under threat. If Card 03's "stranded site" revaluation occurs, the primary borrowers (DC developers) will face margin calls, leading to order cancellations and sudden receivable write-offs for A-share firms.
*   **Technological Obsolescence:** If the "Grey Box" becomes a "decaying option" (Card 01), the **valuation floor** for companies like Kehua and Kstar (traditionally valued as infrastructure growth) should be re-rated from 25x-30x P/E to "commodity industrial" levels (10x-12x).

## 4. Stress Test Conclusion: "Financing the Stranded"
I support the "Grey Box" decay thesis but elevate the risk from **Technological** to **Systemic Financial**. 
*   **The Bear Case:** A-share investors are essentially buying the *debt* of Johor DC operators disguised as *equity growth* of suppliers. 
*   **Trigger Event:** A credit downgrade of a major DC tenant or an official 18-month water/power moratorium in Johor (as rumored in late 2025) would puncture the A-share valuation bubble, leading to a "double-kill" of credit impairment and margin deleveraging.

# A-Share Margin Trading Brief

- **Report date:** 2026-05-14 (Thursday)
- **Coverage window:** Baseline is the most recent fully published trading day, 2026-05-11 ("reference day"), with rolling history back to April 1. The 5/12–5/13 tape is overlaid for forward-looking risk read.
- **Data caveat:** The 5/13 close margin aggregates are published by the SSE/SZSE/BSE on T+1 (the morning of 5/14) and were not yet fully available at the time of writing. Statements about 5/13 reference index and turnover only; the latest hard margin data is the 5/11 close.

---

## 1. Headline takeaways (in priority order)

1. **Leverage is at a new all-time high and still accelerating.** As of the 5/11 close, the combined SSE + SZSE + BSE two-way margin balance stood at **RMB 2,833.86 bn**, up from RMB 2,802.50 bn on 5/8. The SSE + SZSE subtotal was RMB 2,804.08 bn, **+RMB 30.55 bn day-on-day**. Over the four sessions 5/6–5/11, financing balance rose by **~RMB 117.5 bn**; cumulative gain since April 1 is **~RMB 225.2 bn**, a clear acceleration vs Q1.
2. **Flows are structurally concentrated in "AI hardware + semis"** rather than broad-based. Under the Shenwan taxonomy, 4/1–5/11 net buying was led by Hardware Equipment (+RMB 78.97 bn) and Semiconductors (+RMB 60.77 bn) — the two combined contributed roughly **60% of total market financing net buying**. The second tier consists of Non-ferrous metals (+RMB 22.54 bn), Chemicals (+RMB 13.87 bn), Machinery (+RMB 11.45 bn), Pharma/biotech (+RMB 6.43 bn).
3. **Net repayments cluster in low-beta defensives.** Over the same window **8 Shenwan tier-1 industries** posted net repayment, including Utilities, Coal, Petroleum & petrochemicals, Consumer services, and Textiles & apparel — fully consistent with rising risk appetite squeezing dividend-style positioning.
4. **Financing purchases now represent 10.0%–10.79% of total market turnover** (5/6–5/11) and the financing-balance-to-free-float ratio sits at 2.56%–2.58%. Aggregate leverage is still manageable (Q1-end industry-average maintenance ratio: 278%), but **brokers have begun to self-tighten**: Dongxing Securities is rolling out a 115% intra-day liquidation line effective 5/18; China Galaxy and Guosen have followed. This is the **first collective risk-control tightening of this leverage cycle** and is the most important marginal signal in the print.
5. **The 5/13 rally fits an "add-leverage → rally → add-more-leverage" reflexivity setup.** On 5/13 the SSE composite +0.67% (4,242.57), SZSE component +1.67%, ChiNext +2.63% (4,038.33); whole-market turnover RMB 3.26 trillion — the **6th consecutive session above 3 trillion**. The 5/13 close margin print (released 5/14 morning) is very likely to extend the new highs, again concentrated in TMT.

## 4. Net-repayment industries: the unloved cohort

Over 4/1–5/11, **8 Shenwan tier-1 industries** registered net financing repayment. Confirmed names include:

- Utilities
- Coal
- Petroleum & petrochemicals
- Consumer services (leisure)
- Textiles & apparel (also the lone net-repayer in the 5/6–5/8 sub-window)

The remaining three are not separately confirmed in the public excerpts but, given the style profile, most plausibly fall in **Real estate, Retail/wholesale, Banks** — the high-dividend / low-beta clusters.

**Common feature:** low growth optionality, dividend-led return profile, no direct exposure to AI/compute themes. As the index marches higher, the opportunity cost of holding leveraged positions in these names rises and leveraged money rotates away — a textbook setup.

---

## 5. Risk pointers and marginal signals

1. **Broker-led self-tightening.** Dongxing's 115% intra-day liquidation line goes live 5/18; Galaxy has set a 115% minimum forced-liquidation line; Guosen has added a next-session liquidation line for ChiNext/STAR/BSE-permissioned accounts. **First collective marginal tightening of this leverage cycle** — read it as front-running tail risk, and as a signal investors should not ignore.
2. **Crowding is structural, not yet systemic.** The aggregate 278% maintenance ratio still looks healthy, but Electronics and Semiconductors' financing-buy share of their own sector turnover, and the single-name concentration of margin balances, both need daily tracking. Suggest a watchlist trigger of "single-name financing buy / free float > 3%".
3. **Six straight sessions of >RMB 3 trn turnover** combined with accelerating margin balance is a classic reflexivity pattern. If 5/14 or 5/15 prints flat-to-down indices alongside a rising financing balance, treat that as an early divergence signal.

# A-Share Liquidity Stress Under a USD/CNY 7.35 Break (2026-05-14)

**Card ID:** 8/8  
**Analyst:** A-Share Strategist (`ashare-strategist`)  
**Stance:** Stress-test  
**Work Date:** 2026-05-14

## Executive Summary
As of **2026-05-14**, I stress-test the prior China-macro conclusion rather than reject it. A USD/CNY break above **7.35** would clearly pressure A-share risk appetite, but it is not enough by itself to create systemic liquidity risk. The nonlinear point comes only when three channels fire together: FX expectations shift from "managed depreciation" to "one-way depreciation," northbound flow proxies show broad selling in index heavyweights, and margin financing flips from dip-buying to forced deleveraging. The market is liquid enough to absorb a single-channel shock, but it is fragile if FX, foreign selling, margin balances, and lockup supply deteriorate in the same week.

## 1. What Changed From the Previous Cards
Card 7 defined **7.35-7.40** as the RMB "policy defense zone." I support that framing, but add an equity-market caveat: A-share liquidity risk is less about the exchange rate level and more about the speed and composition of the response. If USD/CNY touches 7.35 slowly while the PBoC fix and corporate conversion stabilize expectations, A-shares should trade as a valuation de-rating. If USD/CNY moves through 7.35 and quickly probes **7.40**, the equity shock can become self-reinforcing through northbound selling, margin deleveraging, and lockup absorption.

Two structural facts matter:

| Channel | Current stress-test marker | Why it matters |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Northbound participation | HKEX reported **RMB212.4bn** average daily northbound turnover in 2025 and **RMB302.7bn** in February 2026; on 2026-05-13, public market coverage cited **RMB393.261bn** northbound turnover, **12.14%** of A-share turnover. | Foreign flow is not the whole market, but it can set marginal pricing in CSI 300, consumer, financials, and large-cap TMT. |
| Margin financing | The balance of margin trading stood near **RMB2.8025tn** as of 2026-05-08, up **RMB87.293bn** over the prior week, with financing balance at **RMB2.7823tn** and securities lending near **RMB20.179bn**. | A rising margin balance into FX stress is initially supportive, but it also increases reflexivity if prices gap lower. |
| Cash turnover | On 2026-05-13, A-share turnover was reported at **RMB3.2642tn**. | High turnover absorbs selling; liquidity risk becomes nonlinear only when turnover falls while leverage and supply remain high. |
| Lockup expiry | Recent single-day lockup-expiry supply was reported at **RMB136.79bn** on 2026-05-11 and **RMB140.43bn** on 2026-05-13. | Lockup supply is not automatically selling, but it worsens depth in small/mid-cap names during FX-driven risk-off. |

## 2. Northbound Flow: The Data Signal Is Weaker, Not Irrelevant
The first trap is to over-read "northbound net outflow." Since the Stock Connect disclosure changes announced in 2024, investors no longer get the same high-frequency intraday buy/sell imbalance signal. Daily and periodic Stock Connect turnover, holdings changes, and actively traded names are therefore more useful than a single real-time "net flow" number.

That data limitation cuts both ways. It reduces the chance of intraday panic caused by visible real-time outflow headlines, but it also makes the market discover selling pressure with a lag. In a USD/CNY break above **7.35**, the key warning signs are:

- northbound turnover staying above **RMB350-400bn** while the index falls, implying foreign participation is high but not absorbing supply;
- active selling concentrated in CSI 300 banks, insurers, baijiu, internet-linked consumer, and semiconductor beta rather than just tactical ETF rebalancing;
- a widening A/H premium or sharper declines in A-share shares with high foreign ownership;
- offshore CNH weakness leading onshore CNY by more than normal intraday noise, reinforcing RMB hedging demand.

My base case is not a foreign-exodus scenario. Northbound turnover is large, but total A-share turnover is larger. The risk is nonlinear only if foreign selling becomes the price-discovery channel for domestic leveraged investors.

## 3. Margin Financing: The Real Nonlinear Amplifier
Margin financing is the more important domestic transmission path. With the margin-trading balance around **RMB2.8025tn**, a **1%** weekly decline equals roughly **RMB28bn** of balance-sheet tightening; a **2%** weekly decline is about **RMB56bn**; a **3%** decline is roughly **RMB84bn**. The absolute numbers are manageable in a **RMB3tn+** turnover tape, but they become dangerous if concentrated in high-beta growth, small/mid-cap, and crowded thematic positions.

The sequence to watch is:

1. **Stage 1: Dip-buying.** USD/CNY breaks **7.35**, but margin balances still rise as domestic investors buy policy-defense expectations. This cushions the index but raises latent leverage.
2. **Stage 2: Air pocket.** USD/CNY probes **7.40**, northbound selling proxies worsen, and cash turnover falls below **RMB2.5tn**. Margin accounts stop adding and start reducing exposure.
3. **Stage 3: Nonlinear deleveraging.** A five-session index drawdown of **6-8%**, margin balance contraction above **2%** in a week, and weak breadth push brokers and clients into risk-control selling. This is the point where liquidity risk becomes nonlinear.

The market structure does not require a formal systemic margin call to produce a liquidity spiral. A discretionary reduction in leveraged accounts, fund redemptions, ETF basket selling, and lockup-supply discounting can create the same effect locally.

## 4. Stress Scenarios

| Scenario | FX path | Flow and leverage behavior | A-share impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Managed pressure | USD/CNY **7.35-7.40**, PBoC fix leans against depreciation, turnover remains above **RMB3tn** | Northbound turnover high but mixed; margin balance weekly change within **-1% to +1%** | Valuation de-rating, style rotation to high-dividend, exporters, and state-owned balance sheets; no systemic liquidity event. |
| Liquidity air pocket | USD/CNY breaks **7.40** quickly, CNH leads CNY weaker | Northbound turnover above **RMB350-400bn** during index declines; margin balance falls **1-2%** weekly; turnover drops below **RMB2.5tn** | CSI 300 and ChiNext selloff broadens; high-beta growth and small/mid caps underperform; policy headlines become the only stabilizer. |
| Nonlinear deleveraging | USD/CNY holds above **7.40** without effective policy anchoring | Margin balance falls more than **2%** in a week; five-session index drawdown reaches **6-8%**; lockup supply hits weak-depth names | Forced and quasi-forced selling feed on each other; liquidity premium rises; factor crowding unwinds faster than earnings expectations. |

## 5. Sector and Style Implications
The stress-test favors balance-sheet certainty over growth optionality. High-dividend state-owned enterprises, domestic utility-like cash flows, and select exporters with USD revenue are relative havens. Banks are two-sided: they benefit from state-balance-sheet perception, but they absorb macro and property-risk anxiety when FX pressure escalates. Brokerages are high beta to turnover and margin balances; they outperform only in the managed-pressure scenario and underperform sharply if leverage contracts. Small/mid-cap thematic trades are the main liquidity-risk pocket because they combine thin depth, high retail participation, and lockup sensitivity.

I would avoid treating all exporters as a hedge. A weaker RMB helps translation and price competitiveness, but if USD strength comes from a renewed Fed tightening scare, external demand and global risk appetite may deteriorate at the same time. The better hedge is exporters with visible order books, low USD debt, and strong cash conversion.

## 7. Conclusion
My core judgment is conditional. **A USD/CNY break above 7.35 is a necessary stress signal but not a sufficient trigger for nonlinear A-share liquidity risk.** The tipping point is a joint event: USD/CNY moving toward or above **7.40**, northbound selling proxies dominating price discovery, margin balances falling by more than **2%** in a week, and cash turnover slipping below **RMB2.5tn**. Until that cluster appears, the better description is "FX-led de-rating." Once it appears, the market shifts to "leverage-led liquidity spiral," with small/mid-cap beta and brokerages most exposed.

No further whiteboard handoff is recommended because this is card **8/8**. The final synthesis should retain the FX trigger from cards 6-7, but downgrade any claim that northbound outflow alone can break A-share liquidity.

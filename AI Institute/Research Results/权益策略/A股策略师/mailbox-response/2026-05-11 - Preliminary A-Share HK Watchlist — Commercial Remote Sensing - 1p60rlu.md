## 1. TL;DR

1. **Main read-through = "Entity List / export-control" risk**: directly negative for the named firms, but **net positive for non-named domestic substitutes** (satellite manufacturing, ground segment, AI image exploitation) via import-substitution and government-order reflow. Frame the theme as "beneficiaries of domestic substitution," not "victims."
2. **Three readable sub-chains**:
   - **A. Commercial remote-sensing operations / constellations** (scarce in A-share, often injected via parent-co) — highest beta but thinnest liquidity.
   - **B. Satellite manufacturing + payload + launch** (mostly listed platforms of central state-owned aerospace groups) — best earnings visibility.
   - **C. Downstream AI image exploitation / compute applications** (GIS, surveying, emergency-response) — best multiple expansion, choppiest earnings.
2026 H1 government special funds have tilted toward the "low-altitude + space" overlay, so C-chain names with state customers are re-rating fastest.
3. **HK scarcity premium**: pure-play commercial remote-sensing in HK is extremely scarce. Restrict HK exposure to CASIC-linked vehicles (e.g. China Aerospace Hldgs 0031.HK) and GIS / SaaS adjacencies. Lower priority than the A-share book.
4. **Near-term catalysts**: late-May "China Space Day" policy window + H1 launch density + July satellite-internet spectrum re-review.

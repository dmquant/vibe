# A-Share Style Rotation Signal

Date anchor: 2026-05-11, based on the local shell command `date +%Y-%m-%d`.[1]

## Priority Conclusions

1. **Value vs. Growth: growth remains the clearest style winner today.** The large-cap growth/large-cap value return spread was **+1.82ppt** today, while the small-cap growth/small-cap value spread was **+1.62ppt**; over five trading days the spreads were **+4.73ppt** and **+3.63ppt**, respectively. The STAR 50 rose **+4.65%** and ChiNext rose **+3.50%**, showing that technology, healthcare, AI infrastructure and other growth assets still command the strongest pricing power.[2][3]
2. **Large caps vs. mid/small caps: mid/small caps still lead on a weekly basis, but today was not a simple small-cap rout of large caps.** Today the CSI 500 rose **+1.67%**, almost matching the CSI 300 at **+1.64%**, while the CSI 1000 rose **+1.44%** and the CNI 2000 **+1.32%**. Over five trading days, however, the CNI 2000 **+6.40%**, CSI 500 **+5.87%**, and CSI 1000 **+5.78%** materially outperformed the CSI 300 **+3.01%** and SSE 50 **+1.88%**.[2][3]
3. **High-to-low rotation: there is breadth expansion and selective low-level repair, but not a full exit from high-level growth.** The equal-weighted industry leaders today were electronics **+3.21%**, communications **+2.23%**, and healthcare **+1.72%**, all growth-oriented; meanwhile real estate **+1.30%**, non-bank financials **+1.22%**, and building materials **+1.18%** show that capital is also probing laggards. The correct read is “rotation inside the growth complex plus selective laggard catch-up,” not a wholesale handover to defensive value.[4][5]
4. **Trading implication: keep growth as the core style, reduce the pace of chasing highs, and rebalance away from a single AI-compute exposure toward electronics, communications, healthcare, machinery, power equipment, and low-level recovery trades in the property chain and non-bank financials.** Excluding new listings, A-shares had **3,119** advancers and **2,238** decliners, with a median return of **+0.32%**, roughly **160** limit-up stocks, **7** limit-down stocks, and turnover of about **RMB3.565tn**. Risk appetite remains strong, but dispersion is rising.[4]

High-to-low assessment:

- **What is confirmed:** from last week’s diffusion into micro caps, robotics, commercial aerospace, AI applications, and resource/materials, to today’s rebound in the property chain, non-bank financials, and building materials, capital is indeed searching for lower-level beta beyond AI compute.[5][6]
- **What is not confirmed:** the strongest industries today were still electronics and communications, while the STAR 50 and ChiNext sharply outperformed. High-level growth has not been systematically sold.
- **Strategy implication:** avoid chasing one-day acceleration in crowded AI hardware; keep core growth exposure, while adding lower-level cyclicals and financial beta to reduce dependence on one theme.

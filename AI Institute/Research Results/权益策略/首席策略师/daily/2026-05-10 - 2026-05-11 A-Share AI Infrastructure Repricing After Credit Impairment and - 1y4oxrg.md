# 2026-05-11 | A-Share AI Infrastructure Repricing After Credit Impairment and FX Liquidity Stress

- Card: 7/8
- Analyst: Chief Strategist
- Stance: synthesize
- Work-date: 2026-05-11 (Asia/Singapore, institute canonical date)
- Topic: Sector weights, position caps, style exposure, and valuation regime for A-share AI-infrastructure names after Johor DC credit and FX stress

As of 2026-05-11, I synthesize card-05 and card-06 rather than reject them. The investment problem is no longer "how much AI-infrastructure growth should we own"; it is "which listed suppliers still deserve equity-duration valuation after their Johor-linked receivables are marked like impaired cross-border credit." The answer is to keep selective exposure to domestic AI demand and liquid-cooling execution, but cut the A-share AI-infrastructure basket from a thematic overweight to a capped, quality-biased underweight.

## Core Judgment

The combined stress changes the portfolio anchor. Card-05 estimated Johor-linked receivables and contract assets of **CNY 22-26 bn** across Inspur (浪潮信息), Kehua Data (科华数据), Envicool (英维克), Runze Tech (润泽科技), Aofei Data (奥飞数据), and Jialitu (佳力图). In S2, a 50% recovery assumption creates a **-55% net-income shock** and roughly **-15% equity impact** for the cluster, with a higher tail of **-25% to -32% equity impact** in S3. Card-06 then adds that USD invoices are not clean USD assets when the debtor funds in MYR and the supplier may have sold USD/CNY forwards: residual FX and settlement loss is **CNY 0.6-1.5 bn** in central S2 and **CNY 1.3-2.4 bn** in the delayed-payment tail.

My portfolio conclusion: keep the prior **8% maximum AI-infrastructure exposure cap**, but make it a hard ceiling, not a neutral target. A model A-share strategy book should run **5-6% normal exposure**, only rising to **8%** when invoice-level Johor exposure, hedge maturity, and collection days are disclosed. Within that exposure, shift from high-beta growth and crowded margin names toward cash conversion, domestic tenant visibility, balance-sheet quality, and liquid-cooling assets with low receivable opacity.

## Portfolio Weight Reset

| Sleeve | Previous posture | New model weight | Strategy read-through |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Total A-share AI infrastructure | Up to 12% thematic overweight | **5-6% base, 8% hard cap** | Treat 8% as risk-budget ceiling after credit plus FX loss, not as a target. |
| Domestic AI compute and server supply with clean collection | 4-5% | **2.0-2.5%** | Own only where China demand, collection cycle, and customer concentration are auditable. |
| Cooling, power, and MEP equipment | 3-4% | **1.5-2.0%** | Prefer liquid-cooling beneficiaries, but cap if Johor receivables or USD hedges are opaque. |
| Domestic colocation / data-centre operation | 2-3% | **1.0-1.5%** | Keep quality operators with real tenant load; remove speculative ESA or offshore-shell optionality. |
| Johor-linked supplier finance risk | 2-3% | **0-0.5%** | No strategic allocation. Trading-only exposure if disclosure is poor. |
| Hedge budget | 0-1% premium | **0.5-1.0% premium / notional overlay** | Use STAR 50 ETF downside structures and CSI 1000/IM beta hedges around S2 triggers. |

The key change is not simply "sell AI." It is to fund the Johor credit discount by lowering broad thematic beta and re-allocating the surviving budget to names with measurable CNY cash flow, short receivable duration, domestic tenant load, and no need to depend on MYR-to-USD debtor liquidity.

## Position Caps By Name Type

| Bucket | Criteria | Position cap | Valuation regime |
|---|---|---:|---|
| A: quality AI-infra cash compounders | Domestic demand, positive operating cash flow, DSO stable, Johor exposure immaterial or disclosed, no over-hedge risk | **<=2.0% NAV per name** | Growth-quality valuation remains acceptable, but terminal growth should be trimmed. |
| B: hybrid growth-credit names | Real AI product cycle, but Johor receivables, USD invoice timing, or hedge ladder not fully visible | **<=1.0% NAV per name** | Blend growth multiple with credit haircut; use probability-weighted ECL. |
| C: supplier-finance names | Johor-linked receivables plus contract assets exceed 1x normalized net income, DSO rising, or margin financing is crowded | **<=0.5% NAV per name** | Move to credit-discount valuation: book value minus ECL and FX settlement loss. |
| D: opaque concept names | No invoice-level disclosure, speculative ESA exposure, weak cash conversion, or inability to explain hedge accounting under Stage 2/3 receivables | **0% strategic weight** | Treat as event-risk optionality only. |

The practical rule is simple: if Johor receivables can wipe out more than one year of normalized profit under a 42-46% real CNY recovery ratio, the stock no longer deserves a clean growth multiple. It should be valued as an equity claim on a risky vendor-financing book.

## Which Stocks Move From Growth Valuation To Credit Discount

**Inspur (浪潮信息): switch decisively to credit-discount valuation.** Card-05 used Inspur as the main worked example: Johor exposure around **CNY 7.5 bn** at 50% ECL implies **CNY 3.75 bn** impairment, or **6-7x 2025 net income**, while margin financing balance was estimated above **CNY 34 bn**. That combination makes the equity highly reflexive. The correct valuation anchor is no longer server-cycle PE; it is tangible equity after receivable impairment, FX settlement loss, and forced-selling discount.

**Kehua Data (科华数据): switch to credit-discount valuation.** The company sits in the power/UPS and DC-infrastructure supply chain, so it still has real AI capex exposure, but card-05 flagged margin balance around **CNY 8 bn** and meaningful receivable stress. Until invoice currency, debtor, maturity ladder, and hedge rate are disclosed, the right framework is a hybrid credit-equity model with a hard **0.5-0.75%** position cap.

**Aofei Data (奥飞数据) and Jialitu (佳力图): keep below strategic weight unless cash collection proves otherwise.** Smaller liquidity and narrower business models make them poor vehicles for absorbing a Johor impairment cycle. Their valuation should be residual: domestic normalized EBITDA less any Johor ECL, then apply a liquidity discount. They should not be paid for a high-duration AI optionality story while receivables remain opaque.

**Envicool (英维克): move to probationary hybrid valuation, not a full downgrade yet.** Liquid cooling is a genuine structural beneficiary because NVIDIA GB200 NVL72 is a rack-scale, liquid-cooled architecture and prior cards showed old air-cooled grey boxes are disadvantaged. But the stock should not be given a pure liquid-cooling multiple if its customer receivable and Johor collection risks are not ring-fenced. Cap at **1.0-1.5%** pending disclosure.

**Runze Tech (润泽科技): retain quality-growth status, but cap concentration.** Card-05 explicitly retained Runze because of anchored hyperscaler tenancy and no Johor exposure in that stress frame. It remains the cleaner exposure to domestic data-centre demand. Still, sector de-rating argues for a **<=2.0%** single-name cap and lower terminal multiple than in the 2024-25 AI boom.

## Style Exposure Reset

The style book should rotate in four ways:

1. **Reduce growth duration.** Lower exposure to high PE, negative free-cash-flow, and long-payback AI-infra narratives. The loss driver is near-term ECL and margin deleveraging, not distant TAM.
2. **Increase quality and cash conversion.** Favor names with operating cash flow / net income above 0.8x, stable DSO, domestic CNY collections, and disclosed hedge ladders.
3. **Underweight crowded margin beta.** The January 2026 exchange change raised the minimum margin ratio for leveraged securities purchases from 80% to 100%, and old contracts are grandfathered. This makes new leverage less elastic while old crowded financing remains a reflexive selling channel if prices fall.
4. **Prefer liquid-cooling capability, not liquid-cooling slogans.** Blackwell-class deployment requires liquid-cooled, rack-scale infrastructure. A supplier earns a premium only if it can convert that requirement into collectible cash, not merely into reported revenue.

Factor posture: **long quality / low leverage / cash-flow stability**, neutral domestic AI policy beta, short **high beta / high receivable duration / margin-crowded growth**. The relative trade is not "AI versus non-AI"; it is "collectible AI cash flow versus vendor-financed AI revenue."

## Trigger-Based Playbook

| Trigger | Portfolio action |
|---|---|
| USD/MYR closes above **4.30**, USD/CNY above **7.40**, and a named developer misses a milestone | Move basket to S2 FX tail: cut gross AI-infra exposure to **<=5%**, add downside hedge, and suspend adding supplier-finance names. |
| Receivables days rise by **>15 days** month-on-month for any core holding | Move bucket down one level; reduce position by one-third unless invoice-level disclosure offsets the signal. |
| Johor-linked ECL disclosure exceeds **25%** of normalized annual profit | Revalue on credit-discount basis; remove growth terminal value for that exposure. |
| Margin financing balance rises while price falls **>10%** over five sessions | Cut position cap by half; treat rally as deleveraging exit, not confirmation. |
| Company discloses debtor, invoice currency, hedge maturity, and collection schedule | Permit bucket upgrade if recovery sensitivity stays within one year of normalized profit. |

## Bottom Line

I support the direction of card-05 and card-06 and synthesize it into a portfolio rule: A-share AI infrastructure remains investable only after a credit and FX-liquidity haircut. The sector should be run at **5-6% base exposure with an 8% hard cap**, not the previous **12%** thematic ceiling. Inspur and Kehua move from growth valuation to credit-discount valuation; Aofei and Jialitu stay below strategic weight; Envicool is a hybrid liquid-cooling beneficiary with disclosure risk; Runze remains the cleaner domestic quality exposure but still needs a concentration cap.

# A-Share Style Rotation and Sector Priorities Under the Domestic Compute Paradigm Shift

- Author: Chief Strategist (chief-strategist)
- Date: 2026-05-14 (Asia/Singapore)
- Card: 2/8 · Stance: support (building on TMT Analyst's Card 01 "performance-driven" call)
- Root topic: DeepSeek V4 and the paradigm shift in the domestic compute narrative

## Core Thesis

I support Card 01's paradigm-shift conclusion and extend it: **in 2026 the A-share computer and semiconductor sectors will rotate systematically from "small-cap growth / thematic momentum" to "mid- and large-cap core assets / earnings delivery."** The implication for full-year sector priorities is to overweight the chain **semi-equipment → domestic GPU/ASIC → cloud & IDC → enterprise AI applications**, where earnings can actually be verified, while reducing relative weight on pure "self-sufficiency" small-cap thematic names.

## 1. Why the style will rotate from small-cap growth to mid/large-cap core

1. **Earnings delivery replaces policy expectation as the pricing anchor.** Under the 2023–2025 "secure-substitute" narrative the market paid for policy optionality; β, themes and shell value clustered in small-cap growth. Card 01's threshold signal (DeepSeek V4 closing the inference-cost / training-efficiency gap on domestic hardware) shifts the valuation model from P/Concept back to P/E·P/S, pulling thematic capital toward leaders with real earnings (Huawei Ascend chain, Cambricon, Hygon, SMIC, Naura, AMEC).
2. **Institutional money structurally favours mid/large-cap.** The national team, insurance OCI books, passive ETFs (STAR50, chip ETFs) and — at the margin of channel normalisation — northbound flows have far higher marginal-buy elasticity in index heavyweights than in small caps. Once "performance-driven" is validated by earnings season, mutual funds will rebalance from thematic funds toward core-asset active equity, amplifying large-cap relative returns.
3. **Profit pool redistributes toward mid/upstream equipment and design leaders.** In a performance-driven regime, excess profit migrates from integration-and-policy small shops to firms that actually own EDA/IP, advanced-node equipment, HBM-like high-bandwidth memory and chiplet packaging — i.e. mid/large caps. The path is isomorphic to consumer-staples (2019–2021) and new-energy (2020–2022) mid/large-cap-isation.
4. **Liquidity and crowding constraint.** Around 2026-05-14 the relative strength of small-cap growth (CSI2000, BSE50) versus CSI800 has risen for three consecutive quarters and crowding sits in the 80–90th historical percentile (consistent with the factor-analyst's standard read). Mean-reversion pressure was already building; the paradigm shift provides the catalyst.

## 3. Key calendar triggers

- **Q2 earnings + interim preannouncement (2026-07)**: a leaders' beat on revenue / gross margin formally confirms the rotation. This is the biggest single pricing inflection.
- **Domestic HBM-like high-bandwidth memory volume node**: if YMTC / CXMT system guides to volume in H2 2026, equipment-materials-packaging re-rates again.
- **Export-control / entity-list deltas (date unknown)**: in a negative shock core assets fall less than small caps; in a positive easing core assets benefit more. Both directions reinforce mid/large-cap allocation.
- **A-share liquidity backdrop**: if the PBOC stays neutral-to-easy (per china-macro's regular monitor), ETF and insurance incremental flows favour heavyweights.

## 5. Risks and counter-evidence

1. **Earnings disconfirms the rotation.** If 2026Q2 leader results miss (equipment-order slips, GPU yield bottlenecks), thematic capital may rotate back to small caps.
2. **Liquidity tightens abruptly.** A second offshore-rate shock that drags northbound and foreign risk appetite would compress core-asset elasticity.
3. **Policy reverses.** A regulatory tightening on AI-application compliance delays the B2B AI-application leaders' tempo.
4. **Crowding reversal.** Mid/large-cap core could itself become crowded in H2 (factor-analyst should monitor).

## Executive Summary
Following an exhaustive 7-card research cycle, we conclude that the "May WASDE Bearish Base Case" has created a mispriced tactical window for the A-share agricultural chain. While the global outlook for 2026/27 points to a 12.1% stocks-to-use ratio (bearish), the local Chinese context—defined by a **600 CNY/ton import arbitrage window** and **satellite-detected drought stress in the Northeast**—warrants a tactical upgrade. 

We recommend upgrading this theme from a sector-level beta to a **High-Conviction Tactical Sleeve (12-15% allocation)** within thematic or flexible A-share portfolios. The "Western NE China Heatwave + Reserve Replenishment" trade is not just a weather play; it is a policy-driven financial optimization trade that the market has yet to fully price.

## 1. The Evidence Chain: From Global to Local
The conviction for this tactical pivot is built on a four-tier evidence chain:

1.  **Global Anchor (WASDE):** The 2026/27 initial balance sheet (Card 01/02) is comfortable but fragile. Any 3-5 bu/acre yield loss in the US would flip the narrative. This global "bearishness" keeps CBOT prices low, which paradoxically *increases* the probability of the China trade.
2.  **The Arbitrage Trigger (Macro/Policy):** At a ~600 CNY/ton spread between landed imports and domestic prices (Card 03), state reserve entities (Sinograin) have a massive financial incentive to replenish reserves. This is a "Policy Alpha" that exists independently of global supply shortages.
3.  **Satellite Confirmation (Alt-Data):** While ground reports claim stability, satellite NDVI and soil moisture data (Card 05) reveal a -0.4 to -0.7σ anomaly in Western NE China (covering ~4-6% of regional corn area). The 6/15–7/10 window is a high-probability heatwave trigger (55-60%).
4.  **Sentiment and Crowding:** Current crowding in the target names (Denghai, Stanley, COFCO Engineering) remains low to moderate (Card 07). Denghai is "warming up," but Stanley remains driven by institutional "slow money," leaving room for an event-driven surge.

## 2. Portfolio Strategy and Coordination
### Tactical Overweight Parameters
*   **Weighting:** 12-15% of the total tactical portfolio.
*   **Style Fit:** This trade bridges "Defensive Value" (infrastructure/fertilizers) and "Event-Driven Growth" (seeds). It provides a hedge against potential late-May volatility in the broader TMT or High-Dividend sectors.
*   **Funding Source:** Reduce weights in generic "crowded defensive" names (e.g., top-tier Banks or Utilities if they have reached the +2σ valuation range) to fund this specific, high-conviction alpha sleeve.

*   **Stage 2 (May 23-June 6):** Add to 55% on technical pullbacks.

## 3. Risks to the Thesis
*   **Global Macro Shock:** A sharp USD appreciation could suppress CBOT further, though this would only widen the China arbitrage spread.
*   **Weather Reversal:** Significant rainfall in Western NE China before June 15 would invalidate the "drought" component of the trade.
*   **Policy Delay:** If reserve procurement is delayed into Q4, the tactical window for A-share infrastructure (COFCO Engineering) may shift.

## Strategic Conclusion
The A-share market in late May 2026 requires "idiosyncratic alpha" to outperform a likely range-bound index. The Agri-Chain Tactical Sleeve offers a unique combination of satellite-confirmed physical risk and policy-confirmed financial logic. We move to **Synthesize and Support** the tactical overweight.

# Rebalancing the AI Long/Short Basket Under the Four-Tier Profit Stack, with Cross-Theme Hedges

## 1. Thesis

Cards 01–04 have locked in the second-stage AI inference-economics profit stack as four tiers: **upstream scarce materials (GOES / high-purity copper / high-end HBM substrate) ≥ Tier-1 OEMs (optical-module leaders, top-tier transformer makers, domestic-compute ASIC) > Tier-2 OEMs (second-line optics/PCB, white-box servers) > regulated utilities / AI application wrappers**. Card 5's job is not to restate the stack but to translate it into an executable 2026 H2 long/short weight sheet, and to **embed** — not parallel-build — the cross-theme hedges (amorphous-metal substitution, BYOP gas, recycled copper) that activate once GOES / copper peak.

Core conclusions:
1. Keep total AI-theme gross at 96%, but cut net long from card 1's +60% to **+48%**, ceding 12pp to a "non-AI-but-AI-pulled" scarcity-rent hedge basket.
2. Refine card 1's "capex 60% / platform-software 18% / wrapper-short 18%" into **5 long layers + 2 short layers + 1 cross-theme hedge layer**, each with gates and rebalance triggers.
3. A GOES / copper peak is not an "AI peak" — it is the signal that profit is migrating along the chain toward Tier-1 OEMs and **substitute materials**. The hedge basket's role is to **take the baton** once the first-window scarcity rent is absorbed, not to stand against the capex longs.

## 4. Embedding Logic Under a GOES / Copper Peak

### 4.1 Peak definition (objective)

- **GOES peak**: Baosteel monthly GOES quote MoM ≤ 0% for 2 consecutive months, with JFE Hi-B export price simultaneously flat.
- **Copper peak**: LME cash-3M back narrows to < 30 USD/t (currently ~95) and SHFE stocks rise WoW for 4 straight weeks.
- **OEM margin inflection**: top-tier transformer GM rolls over by ≥ 1.5pp MoM (NARI, TBEA).

Any one fires → activate hedge-layer add. Two or more → cut L1 from 14% to 8%, raise H1+H2+H3 from 12% to 18%.

### 4.2 Embedding mechanics (not a parallel sleeve)

- **Same gross budget**: the hedge eats from the AI-theme account's gross, keeping account-level vol controlled.
- **Not opposed to L4**: H2 BYOP gas is a bypass to L4's main-grid equipment; both rally **together** when grid delivery worsens. Only if State Grid interconnect queue falls below 120 weeks does H2 trade off against L4.
- **Not opposed to L1**: H1 and H3 are L1's relay. Cut L1 and add H1/H3 on the same trading day to avoid gross vacuum.

### 4.3 Falsifiers

- If GOES peaks but amorphous substitution orders YoY < +25% (Yunlu / Advanced Tech guidance), H1 fails — recycle to L1 or L4.
- If BYOP gas share in DC capex filings stays < 5% (Uptime Institute quarterly), keep H2 at +2pp on watch — do not escalate.
- If recycled-copper TC/RC stays < 30 USD/t, scrap is tight not loose; H3 should extend L1 rather than hedge it.

## 6. Risks and Boundary Conditions

1. **Policy exogenous**: further BIS loosening of H200/MI325X licensing in 2026H2 would compress the L3 domestic premium quickly — hence the 12% lower bound.
2. **Inventory cycle**: GOES / copper peak timing is harder than direction; the 2-month persistence in G4 / G6 filters seasonal noise.
3. **Liquidity**: H1 (Yunlu / Advanced Tech) and H3 (Hailiang / Chujiang) average ¥800m–1.5bn daily turnover; cap any single name at 1.5%.
4. **Cross-market alignment**: the relative valuation of L4 vs H2 across A-shares and US is the implicit alpha. If GE Vernova trades > 4x peers, prefer Dongfang Electric 600875 to express H2.

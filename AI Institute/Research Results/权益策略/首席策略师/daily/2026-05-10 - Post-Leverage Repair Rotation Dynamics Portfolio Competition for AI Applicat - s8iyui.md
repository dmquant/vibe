# Post-Leverage Repair: Rotation Dynamics & Portfolio Competition for AI Applications

- **Work Date:** 2026-05-11 (Asia/Singapore)
- **Analyst:** Chief Strategist (chief-strategist)
- **Stance:** Synthesize
- **Topic:** Style rotation and position competition of AI applications relative to Dividends, Export Chain, Brokers, and Computing Power under margin expansion.

## I. Executive Summary

As of **May 11, 2026**, the A-share market has entered a "high-beta repair" phase, with the total margin balance reaching **2.78 trillion RMB** (as per May 8 data, up ~3% in May). The structural shift triggered by DeepSeek V4's cost bonus (reducing inference costs by 75%) has moved the market focus from "Infrastructure" to "Edge Applications." 

Within the 11-15% target position for AI Vertical Applications, we must now prioritize "Position Discipline" over "Stock Selection." In the broader style competition, AI Applications and Brokers are the primary beneficiaries of this leveraged recovery, while Dividends and legacy Computing Power face capital outflow pressure due to rotation.

## II. Style Rotation Ranking (May 2026)

We rank the primary A-share investment styles based on three dimensions: **Margin Sensitivity**, **Earnings Visibility (ROI)**, and **Crowding**.

## III. AI Application Portfolio vs. Mainline Competitors

### 1. AI Apps vs. Computing Power (Internal AI Switch)
The "Dual Track" period (Card-01) is entering its most intense phase. Computing Power (Arms Dealers) is no longer the only game in town. The 75% cost drop in inference is a "Tax Cut" for AI Apps. We recommend a **"1:1 to 1.5:1"** ratio between Application and Hardware weights. Currently, with Apps at 11-15%, Hardware should be capped at 10% to avoid sector-wide over-concentration.

### 2. AI Apps vs. Brokers (Leverage Sensitivity)
Both are "Growth-Beta" plays. However, Brokers (e.g., CITIC, Eastmoney) respond to *liquidity* (volume), while AI Apps (e.g., HUNDSUN, THS) respond to *efficiency* (AI adoption). In a 2.8T margin environment, Brokers provide the "Floor" for the rally, while AI Apps provide the "Ceiling." We use Brokers to capture broad market repair and AI Apps for industry-specific Alpha.

### 3. AI Apps vs. Dividends (The Risk-On Pivot)
With the margin balance expanding 2-3% in a single week, the "Low-Volatility" trade (Dividends) is underperforming. We recommend rotating out of high-valuation bank/utility dividends to fund the expansion of the Financial IT (AI App) core positions identified in Card-03.

## IV. Position Competition & Execution Framework

Based on the 11-15% AI Application position target:

1.  **Core Allocation (Bottom Position):** **Financial IT (6-7%)**. High correlation with the "Broker Trade" but with AI alpha. 
2.  **Tactical Alpha:** **Industrial/Gov IT (4-6%)**. Wait for cash flow/order validation (Card-04).
3.  **The "Leverage Buffer":** If the total portfolio beta exceeds 1.2, use the **Export Chain** to neutralize volatility rather than cutting AI Apps, provided the DeepSeek ROI thesis remains intact.
4.  **Rotation Trigger:** If margin expansion stalls or reverses (e.g., total balance drops below 2.7T), immediately trim AI Apps to the 11% floor and rotate back to Dividends.

## V. Key Risks & Constraints

- **Liquidity Ceiling:** The 2.8T margin balance is approaching historical resistance. A "Leverage Fatigue" event would hit AI Apps (especially Finance IT) hardest due to high retail/quant crowding (Card-04).
- **Earnings Gap:** The 2-4 month ROI compression must translate into Q2/Q3 orders. If Q2 "Contract Liabilities" (Card-03) decelerate, the 11-15% position must be cut to <8%.
- **Hardware De-rating:** A crash in Computing Power (due to over-supply) could drag down the entire AI sentiment, regardless of Application ROI.

## VI. Research Handoff

The "Style Rotation" is currently driven by a liquidity-led leveraged recovery. To understand if this can sustain the AI Application rally through 2026Q3, we need to examine the macro-liquidity ceiling and the sustainability of the domestic credit cycle.

# Valuation Reset in the AI-Native Banking Era

- Work date: 2026-05-14 (Asia/Singapore)
- Board: ba177e6f-bd15-434f-ad18-63c74998c64e | Card 02/08
- Analyst: Chief Strategist (chief-strategist)
- Stance: support (extending card-01, financials-analyst)

## Thesis
As the marginal cost of intelligence collapses and retail / SME / compliance functions become AI-native, the "branch + headcount" tangible assets that have historically anchored A-share bank valuations are flipping from premium to discount items. The PB anchor of the A-share bank sector is being structurally rewritten.

## 1. Three valuation channels reversing

Extending card-01: a DeepSeek V4-class collapse in inference cost has turned "private-bank-grade retail service, real-time SME risk pricing, and full-coverage compliance surveillance" from cost-uneconomic to positive NPV. The three pillars that have held A-share state-owned banks at a 0.5–0.7x PB floor are now loaded in reverse:

1. **Branches: from customer-acquisition moat to sunk-cost center.** As of 2026-Q1 the Big Six still operate roughly 104,000 branches in aggregate (source: PBoC branch census, snapshot 2026-03-31), at an empirical run-rate of RMB 5–8m per branch per year. AI-native distribution has pushed the marginal cost of onboarding, KYC, and credit decisioning toward fractions of a renminbi, demonetising branch distribution value. What remains is largely regulatory recordkeeping plus a senior-customer backstop — the first replaceable by remote video + local compliance nodes, the second decaying naturally with demographics.
2. **Human intermediation: from NIM gatekeeper to fixed-cost overhang.** Listed banks employ ~2.4 million people (source: 2025 annual reports, disclosed late-March 2026) at an all-in comp of RMB 350–450k. With AI relationship managers, AI credit officers and AI AML running 24×7, per-head revenue would need to roughly double just to defend current ROE — a near-impossibility with NIM stuck at the historical low of 1.4–1.5% (source: NFRA Commercial Bank Key Indicators, 2026-Q1).
3. **Licence: from scarce monopoly to regulatory liability.** Licences remain scarce, but what is scarce is a *regulated balance sheet*. AI-native entrants (internet banks, securities AMC, licensed consumer-finance) deliver equivalent intelligent service off lighter balance sheets, throwing into relief the implicit liabilities — capital charges, provisioning, LGFV absorption duty — sitting behind a Big-Six licence.

## 2. Re-anchoring path for the A-share bank sector

| Valuation layer | Old anchor | New anchor (2026–2028 repricing scenario) | Direction |
|---|---|---|---|
| PB centre | 0.55–0.65x | Big Six 0.45–0.55x; regional banks bifurcate 0.30–0.80x | ↓ centre lower, dispersion higher |
| Dividend-yield support | 6–7% | Still a floor, less a 50–80bp haircut for "AI capex eats dividend" | ↓ |
| Premium drivers | Branch density, corporate relationships | AI middle-platform reuse rate, data-asset turnover, AI-served per-customer margin | Rewritten |
| Discount drivers | NPL ratio, LGFV exposure | + branch redundancy, headcount rigidity, years behind on AI transformation | New |

Strategic implication: an "AI-PB" bifurcation inside the sector. Banks ahead on AI middle-platform investment and organisationally willing to shrink branches/headcount — China Merchants Bank, Bank of Ningbo, Ping An Bank, top-tier city-commercials — can re-open a 0.9–1.2x PB band over 12–24 months. The opposite end — Big-Six laggards and rural commercials with double-high branch/headcount rigidity and only chatbot-grade AI spend — converges toward 0.4x PB or lower.

## 3. Allocation calls (A-share, 12-month horizon)

- **Overweight:** AI-native leaders among joint-stock and top city-commercials (CMB, Ningbo, Ping An), plus FinTech / IT-services beneficiaries (Hundsun, Yusys, Sunline).
- **Marketweight → Underweight:** Big-Six state-owned banks with the highest branch/headcount rigidity and still-low AI capex intensity; hold as dividend ballast as long as payout logic is intact, but no longer treat as a re-rating candidate.
- **Underweight / avoid:** Rural and small city-commercials reliant on branch distribution, lacking a data middle-platform, with heavy LGFV exposure.
- **Hedge:** On top of long bank-ETF (515290), pair-trade long CMB / Ningbo vs. short Big-Six basket to capture the relative re-rating.

## 4. Risks and counter-evidence

- **Regulators capping the speed of AI-for-human substitution.** NFRA / PBoC may invoke "financial-consumer protection" to mandate human channels, slowing the headcount run-off. This is the largest non-market risk to the thesis — flag for policy-analyst follow-up.
- **AI capex itself erodes near-term ROE.** If bank IT capex grows 25–40% YoY in 2026–2027, payout ratios may be cut, weighing on sentiment.
- **The deposit moat is still real.** While rate liberalisation is incomplete and corporate deposits remain sticky, branch + RM-driven deposit gathering retains transitional value. Repricing will be a process, not an event.

## 5. Conclusion
We **support** card-01's core judgment and operationalise it on the valuation layer: the "branch + headcount" tangible asset base of traditional banks is flipping from a PB support to a PB discount. The sector will pass through a 12–24-month bifurcating re-rating anchored on AI middle-platform capability. The next step is to map this valuation rewrite onto the medium-term path of NPLs, provisioning and capital adequacy — credit-research territory.

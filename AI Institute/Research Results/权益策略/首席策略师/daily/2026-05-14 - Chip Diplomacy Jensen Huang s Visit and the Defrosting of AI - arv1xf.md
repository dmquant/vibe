# Chip Diplomacy: Jensen Huang's Visit and the Defrosting of AI Valuations
**Date:** 2026-05-14  
**Analyst:** ashare-strategist (Chief Strategist)  
**Board ID:** 09ee5b13-a111-42e4-9563-fa830ba37087  
**Topic:** Geopolitical Valuation Reconstruction of the AI Supply Chain

## 1. Executive Summary: The "Huang Effect" in 2026
Jensen Huang’s arrival in Beijing on May 14, 2026, as part of a high-level US business delegation, represents more than a corporate visit; it is a "geopolitical de-risking" event. After two years of aggressive export controls and supply chain bifurcation, this visit signals a new equilibrium. The "Geopolitical Discount"—the valuation suppression applied to companies with significant cross-border tech exposure—is undergoing a structural compression.

## 2. Quantifying the Geopolitical Discount (2024-2026)
Historically, the global AI supply chain (Nvidia, TSMC, Hon Hai, and A-share liquid cooling/optical module leaders) traded at a 20% discount compared to pure domestic Western or Chinese software plays. 
- **Risk Premium:** Investors demanded a higher return to compensate for the "Sanction Gap."
- **Nvidia's China Revenue:** Dropped from 25%+ to a low of 9% in early 2025 before stabilizing.
- **The 2026 Pivot:** Huang's presence suggests that the "regulatory floor" for high-end AI silicon (specifically the B20/B30 series) is now firm, allowing markets to price in a recovery of China-based revenue to the 15-18% range.

## 3. Impact on the Global AI Supply Chain
The visit triggers three primary valuation shifts:
- **Supply Chain Resilience:** Valuation for Southeast Asian and Mexican "China+1" assembly hubs is being balanced by a "Re-engagement Premium" for original Pearl River Delta hubs.
- **Component Normalization:** Companies like **Foxconn (Hon Hai)** and **Inspur** are seeing P/E expansions as the threat of sudden decoupling recedes.
- **TSMC's "Straits Premium":** A noticeable 5-8% narrowing in the spread between TSMC and Intel/Samsung valuations as physical conflict fears are replaced by diplomatic pragmatism.

## 4. A-Share Strategic Implications: The "Global-Local" Re-rating
For the A-share market, Jensen Huang’s visit acts as a catalyst for "Nvidia Chain" stocks:
- **Server OEMs:** Top-tier players (Inspur, Sugon) are transitioning from "alternative" plays to "integration" plays, bridging the gap between global HBM/GPU standards and domestic data center needs.
- **Optical Modules & Cooling:** Leaders (CPO/Liquid Cooling) are no longer seen as just "domestic substitutes" but as essential nodes in a stabilized global grid.
- **Valuation Target:** We expect a 10-15% upward re-rating in the TMT sector over the next 20 trading days as "geopolitical fear" turns into "operational clarity."

## 5. Conclusion: From Decoupling to Bifurcated Stability
The "Geopolitical Discount" is not disappearing, but it is becoming predictable. Jensen Huang’s 2026 visit provides the "Proof of Concept" for a bifurcated but functional global AI economy.

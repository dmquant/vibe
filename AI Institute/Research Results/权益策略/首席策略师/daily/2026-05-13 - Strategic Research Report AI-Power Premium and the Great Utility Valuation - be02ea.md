## 1. Core Thesis
As of May 2026, the secondary market has completed a fundamental paradigm shift in utility valuation. We are moving from a **Yield/RAB (Regulated Asset Base)** model to a **Capacity Scarcity** model. The migration of nuclear baseload to Behind-The-Meter (BTM) configurations for AI hyperscalers is creating a structural "Reliability Gap" in the public grid, leading to a stark valuation divergence between **Nuclear IPPs (Independent Power Producers)** and **Regulated Utilities**.

## 2. Differentiated Pricing: "The Scarcity Moat" vs. "The Systemic Stabilizer"

### A. Nuclear IPPs (e.g., CEG, VST, TLN) - "AI Infrastructure Proxies"
*   **Valuation Logic:** Priced as high-growth tech infrastructure. The "Unallocated Nuclear Capacity" is now valued as a call option on AI compute density.
*   **Pricing Power:** BTM nuclear contracts are clearing at **$135–$160/MWh** in 2026, representing a **100%+ premium** over wholesale energy prices (~$65/MWh).
*   **Market Multiple:** Forward P/E multiples have expanded to **28x–35x**, detaching from the utility sector average of 16x. Investors are paying for "Speed to Power" (2-year advantage over grid connection).

### B. Regulated Utilities (e.g., D, SO, AEP) - "The Volatility Discount"
*   **Valuation Logic:** Priced as "Systemic Stabilizers" with capped returns. While the Capex cycle for grid hardening is massive, the market is applying a "Volatility Discount."
*   **The Residual Grid Risk:** As nuclear baseload exits the public grid, the remaining system becomes more intermittent (renewables-heavy). Regulated utilities are forced to manage this volatility via expensive peakers and storage, but **Regulatory Lag** and **Ratepayer Backlash** (socialization of costs) create earnings uncertainty.
*   **Market Multiple:** Trading at **14x–17x P/E**, consistent with their 10.5% allowed ROE but showing limited alpha compared to the IPP segment.

## 3. The 2026 Economic Impact of "Grid Volatility Socialization"
The shift to BTM isn't a victimless trade. Our analysis identifies three critical externalities that the secondary market is starting to price:
1.  **The "Exit Fee" Regime:** FERC and state regulators (Virginia/Ohio) have introduced mandatory "Grid Support Charges" for BTM data centers, currently estimated at **$12–$18/MWh**. This acts as a floor for regulated utility revenue but a ceiling for hyperscaler margin expansion.
2.  **Base-load Cannibalization:** For every 1 GW of nuclear shifted to BTM, the public grid requires ~2.5 GW of solar + 500 MW of 4-hour battery storage to maintain equivalent reliability. This drives the massive utility Capex narrative (Supportive for DUK, XEL).
3.  **Industrial Displacement:** High-energy industrial users (steel, chemicals) are being "crowded out" by AI power demand, leading to a sector rotation within the industrials space—favoring those with captive power or high value-add.

## 4. A-Share Cross-Read: The "China Nuclear" Value Play
While the US market is characterized by BTM-driven IPP premiums, the A-share market (e.g., **CNNP 601985.SH**, **CGN Power 01816.HK/003816.SZ**) is entering a "Value Catch-up" phase.
*   **Mechanism:** AI data center clusters in the Yangtze River Delta and Greater Bay Area are signing "Green Power Direct Purchase" agreements.
*   **Pricing:** We expect a **15–25% premium** for nuclear over coal-fired power as "carbon-free reliability" becomes a procurement requirement for global tech supply chains.
*   **Strategy:** Maintain Overweight on Nuclear; Neutral on traditional thermal power; Underweight on low-voltage distribution utilities.

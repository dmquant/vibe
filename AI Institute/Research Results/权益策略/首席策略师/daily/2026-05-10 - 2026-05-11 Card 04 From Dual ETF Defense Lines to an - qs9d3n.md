# 2026-05-11 Card 04: From Dual ETF Defense Lines to an A-Share Style Allocation Framework

Work date: 2026-05-11, Asia/Singapore. This card **synthesizes** the prior line of reasoning: defend the STAR 50 1.48 line first, keep a conditional ChiNext/LFP liquidity fuse, and translate that rule into portfolio weights, sector constraints, and policy signaling so the market does not misread ETF support as a comprehensive bailout.

## Executive Judgment

The next step is not to buy every weak growth asset. It is to run a **hierarchical stabilization trade**: hard-tech growth is the first policy signal, dividends and central SOEs are the balance sheet anchor, and the new-energy chain is kept as a conditional, quality-screened sleeve rather than a blanket rescue target.

Our base A-share equity sleeve should be:

| Style sleeve | Model weight | Stance | Policy interpretation |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Strategic growth, led by STAR hard-tech | 40% | Overweight quality, avoid speculative beta | STAR 50 1.48 is the first defense line because it carries the national innovation signal. |
| Dividend compounders | 25% | Neutral-to-overweight | Dividends absorb volatility and show that stabilization still rewards cash returns, not just narrative duration. |
| Central SOE value and restructuring | 20% | Overweight, but not indiscriminate | Central SOEs provide policy credibility, buyback/dividend optionality, and market-value-management discipline. |
| New-energy chain, including LFP/storage | 15% | Selective underweight versus old cycle weights | ChiNext/LFP gets a fuse, not a put option. Own leaders; avoid capacity that needs permanent rescue. |

The allocation should be paired with explicit funding rules inherited from Card 03: a **RMB 50-80bn** stabilization package, **at least RMB 30bn and preferably RMB 35-45bn** reserved for STAR 50 defense, **RMB 15-20bn** for the ChiNext/LFP ETF fuse, **RMB 25bn only if the total package is RMB 65-80bn**, and **RMB 5-10bn** kept as tactical reserve. The ChiNext/LFP sleeve is useful because it can cover **30-63%** of the first **RMB 40-50bn** redemption pressure if activated early, but it cannot and should not promise to absorb a full **RMB 120bn** redemption path.

## What This Supports and What It Denies

This card supports Card 03's stress-test conclusion: the dual line works only if it is sequenced. It denies the market's likely lazy interpretation that any ETF support equals a national put across all growth assets.

The policy message should be written as:

1. **STAR 50 first claim:** the 1.48 line is defended to protect the hard-tech financing channel and avoid a collapse in the innovation benchmark.
2. **ChiNext/LFP conditional fuse:** ETF support begins only after early net outflow reaches the RMB 15-20bn zone, and it is capped unless the total package moves to the upper RMB 65-80bn range.
3. **No rescue of obsolete capacity:** LFP, PV, and battery-material positions must pass cash-flow, inventory, and price-discipline screens.
4. **Dividends and central SOEs remain in the core:** the state is stabilizing market structure, not underwriting every duration trade.

## Why the Signal Has to Be Hierarchical

Public policy has already moved in this direction. Central Huijin's 2024 ETF purchase statement confirmed that broad-market ETF buying can be used to stabilize confidence, but it did not create an open-ended guarantee for every sector.[1] The State Council's capital-market guidance emphasized stronger supervision, investor protection, dividends, and high-quality development rather than indiscriminate price support.[2] CSRC's 2025 index-investment push reinforced the role of ETFs as a market-structure tool, and SSE data showed that exchange-listed index products had reached about **RMB 3.5tn** by March 2025, up over **80%** in a year, with more than **RMB 1tn** of net inflow in 2024.[3][4]

At the same time, the 2026 policy framework remains growth-friendly. The 2026 Government Work Report put AI, quantum technology, biomanufacturing, hydrogen, nuclear fusion, embodied intelligence, 6G, and advanced manufacturing inside the future-industry agenda.[5] That supports a STAR-led growth allocation. But the national development plan also called out the need to address disorderly competition in **NEVs, photovoltaics, power batteries, and lithium carbonate**, which means the new-energy sleeve cannot be treated as a fully protected strategic asset class.[6]

This is why the right signal is **selective support plus capital discipline**. The state can protect the market's innovation financing function without validating every overcapacity trade.

## Portfolio Implementation

### 1. Strategic Growth: 40%

Hold the largest sleeve in growth, but narrow it to policy-backed productivity rather than broad high-beta buying.

| Sub-sleeve | Weight | Rule |
|---|---:|---|
| STAR hard-tech: semiconductors, AI infrastructure, advanced equipment, industrial software | 25% | Core overweight. Add on drawdowns only while the STAR 50 1.48 line and liquidity support remain credible. |
| ChiNext quality growth outside crowded LFP beta | 7% | Hold firms with positive operating cash flow, pricing power, and export or replacement-cycle demand. |
| Biotech, robotics, aerospace, quantum-adjacent themes | 5% | Use a basket approach; avoid single-name policy speculation. |
| Tactical growth reserve inside the sleeve | 3% | Deploy only if breadth improves and ETF flows stabilize without using the full LFP fuse. |

The growth sleeve should not be financed by dumping all dividend exposure. A STAR rescue without a ballast sleeve would look like speculative reflation rather than policy stabilization.

### 2. Dividend Compounders: 25%

Keep dividends as the volatility sink. The dividend sleeve should focus on utilities, telecom operators, toll roads, high-quality banks, coal/power names with sustainable payout ratios, and consumer staples with stable free cash flow.

The point is not to chase low-volatility defensiveness blindly. The point is to show that ETF stabilization does not cancel the post-2024 emphasis on shareholder returns, cash dividends, and investor protection.[2] In portfolio terms, dividends are the counterweight that prevents the dual-defense policy from becoming a one-way growth beta signal.

### 3. Central SOE Value and Restructuring: 20%

Central SOEs deserve a dedicated sleeve because they sit at the intersection of valuation repair, dividend policy, buybacks, national security, and industrial upgrading. Prefer power grid, telecom, defense, energy security, transport infrastructure, and listed platforms with visible asset injection or restructuring paths.

But the rule is **central SOE quality, not central SOE label**. Exclude firms where return on equity is structurally falling, capex has no productivity payoff, or market-value-management language is not backed by dividends, buybacks, or governance reform.

### 4. New-Energy Chain: 15%

The new-energy sleeve must be smaller and cleaner than in the old cycle. It should be split as follows:

| Sub-sleeve | Weight | Rule |
|---|---:|---|
| LFP and battery leaders | 5% | Own cost leaders and overseas-capable firms only; no balance-sheet rescues. |
| Grid, storage, and power-electronics beneficiaries | 4% | Prefer demand-side bottlenecks and grid absorption over upstream capacity. |
| NEV intelligent components and export-linked parts | 3% | Link exposure to vehicle intelligence and overseas demand, not pure subsidy beta. |
| PV/lithium/materials tactical basket | 3% | Trade only after inventory and price signals improve; do not average down structural oversupply. |

This is the most important communication sleeve. A **RMB 15-20bn** ChiNext/LFP ETF fuse is a market-stability tool, not a sector rescue plan. The state can prevent forced ETF selling from damaging private-sector financing while still allowing poor capacity, weak margins, and undisciplined expansion to be repriced.

## Policy Signal Matrix

| Market condition | ETF action | Style action | Message to investors |
|---|---|---|---|
| STAR 50 near or below 1.48; ChiNext outflow contained | Use STAR-only defense from the RMB 35-45bn preferred bucket | Add STAR hard-tech; keep dividends and SOEs unchanged | Innovation benchmark is protected; no broad bailout signal. |
| STAR line stable; ChiNext/LFP early net outflow reaches RMB 15-20bn | Activate RMB 15-20bn LFP/ChiNext fuse | Add only quality LFP/storage leaders; do not lift the whole chain | Liquidity fuse is active; weak capacity is still exposed. |
| First redemption pressure reaches RMB 40-50bn but no price spiral | Use fuse gradually; preserve RMB 5-10bn reserve | Hold growth weight; rotate within new energy toward cash-flow leaders | Stabilization is working only if reserve remains intact. |
| Redemption path moves toward RMB 120bn | Do not promise full absorption; reassess package and policy | Cut new-energy beta; raise dividends/SOEs; keep STAR core | The state is not underwriting the full redemption path. |
| Broad market rallies on "national team put" speculation | Slow ETF buying and rebalance toward dividends/SOEs | Trim low-quality growth and overcapacity names | Support is a floor for market function, not a ceiling-breakout guarantee. |

## Communication Guardrails

The most dangerous outcome is a policy trade that succeeds mechanically but fails narratively. If investors hear "ETF bid" and price a universal rescue, the next drawdown will be worse because leverage and weak balance sheets will return before earnings discipline improves.

The communication should therefore use four constraints:

1. **Cap disclosure:** state that the ChiNext/LFP fuse is capped at RMB 15-20bn in the base case and that RMB 25bn requires the upper total package.
2. **Sequence disclosure:** state that STAR 50 has first claim and that the ChiNext/LFP sleeve cannot reduce the STAR bucket below RMB 30bn.
3. **Eligibility disclosure:** state that ETF liquidity support does not imply support for low-end capacity, distressed balance sheets, or price-war behavior.
4. **Return discipline disclosure:** keep dividends, buybacks, and central SOE market-value management in the same policy language as growth stabilization.

## Investment Bottom Line

The recommended strategy is a **barbell with a policy core**: overweight STAR-led strategic growth, keep dividends and central SOEs as the ballast, and hold new energy only through a capped, quality-screened sleeve. The ETF rule should be read as **"protect financing function, do not rescue every business model."**

This framework avoids the two errors that would damage market credibility: abandoning ChiNext/LFP so aggressively that private innovation financing is hit, or supporting it so broadly that investors treat overcapacity as state-protected. The dual defense line is credible only when the portfolio and the official message both say the same thing: **STAR 50 first, ChiNext/LFP conditional, dividends and central SOEs permanent, overcapacity still repriced.**

As of 2026-05-11, I support the prior China macro card: once PBoC hedging liquidity and the A-share liquidity bottom are confirmed, the market should rotate from defensive survival into a liquidity-led counter-attack. The first leadership should come from **growth/GARP**, not broad value. Value remains the floor through banks, utilities, and selected insurers, but the rebound beta is already migrating toward high-liquidity growth clusters with visible policy support: semiconductors, AI computing infrastructure, communications equipment, high-end manufacturing, robotics/automation, selected power equipment, and brokerages as a trading-volume proxy.

## Core Judgment

The correct style sequence is **dividend/value floor -> GARP bridge -> high-liquidity growth leadership**.

Pure value has already served its defensive role during JPY carry-trade deleveraging, offshore FCI tightening, and commodity/energy pressure. Once DR007 is stable, ERP has reached an extreme, turnover has stopped shrinking, and policy tools are visible, investors should avoid treating high dividend as the main upside vehicle. The next 4-8 weeks should overweight growth/GARP by **55-60% of equity risk budget**, keep **25-30%** in value ballast, and hold **10-15%** in tactical liquidity proxies such as brokers and exchange-linked financial beta.

## Style Call: Growth Leads, But Not Any Growth

The first rebound leg should not be a blind chase of high-duration concept stocks. It should be **liquidity-sensitive GARP growth**: companies with real revenue visibility, domestic substitution logic, capital-market policy support, and enough trading liquidity to absorb institutional rotation.

**Overweight growth/GARP, 55-60% risk budget.** Favor electronics/semiconductors, AI computing infrastructure, optical communication/CPO, PCB/CCL/electronic materials, industrial robotics, high-end machine tools, military-civil advanced equipment, selected power equipment, and NEV supply-chain leaders with pricing discipline.

**Keep value ballast, 25-30% risk budget.** Banks, utilities, telecom operators, and high-quality insurers should stabilize portfolios while external FCI risk remains. They are no longer the best upside expression after liquidity repair, but they reduce drawdown if JPY volatility or UST term premium shocks return.

**Use tactical financial beta, 10-15% risk budget.** Brokers are the cleanest proxy for a turnover-led rebound. Their beta should be traded around volume, margin financing, and ETF inflows rather than held as structural value.

## Rebound Tempo

**Phase 1: Confirmation week, 2026-05-11 to 2026-05-17.**  
Use the prior card's bottom matrix: DR007 stable around the policy rate, ERP no longer widening, daily turnover above RMB 2.5 trillion rather than collapsing back below RMB 650 billion, and no renewed offshore shock from USD/JPY. During this phase, shift from 60/40 defense/growth toward 45/55 defense/growth.

**Phase 2: First leadership leg, next 2-3 weeks.**  
If turnover remains above RMB 2.5 trillion and margin inflows keep favoring electronics, communications, machinery, power equipment, and chemicals, lift growth/GARP to 55-60%. The best risk-reward is in AI hardware and advanced manufacturing after intraday shakeouts, not in chasing every limit-up stock.

**Phase 3: Breadth test, 4-8 weeks.**  
For the rally to become durable, leadership must broaden from semiconductors and AI infrastructure into robotics, grid digitalization, brokerages, and selected consumer-tech. If breadth narrows while margin balance keeps rising, reduce the most crowded chip/AI names and keep GARP rather than pure beta.

## What Would Prove This Wrong

1. **Value leads again:** If turnover falls below RMB 1.2 trillion for three consecutive sessions while banks/utilities outperform growth, the market is not in a counter-attack phase; it has reverted to defense.
2. **Growth rally becomes unstable:** If margin balance keeps rising but advancing issues shrink and only AI/semiconductor leaders rise, reduce high-beta growth by 5-8 pp.
3. **External liquidity shock returns:** If USD/JPY volatility revives, UST term premium widens sharply, or Northbound transaction share drops, retain banks/utilities and delay the full growth rotation.
4. **Policy transmission disappoints:** If structural tools support technology credit on paper but ETF inflows and margin financing fail to follow, stay in GARP and avoid pre-profit themes.

## Allocation Answer

The first leader after liquidity repair should be **growth/GARP**, not pure value. The strongest liquidity elasticity sits in **AI semiconductors, optical communication/CPO, PCB/CCL, electronic materials, industrial robotics, high-end machinery, selected power equipment, and brokerages**. Value is still needed, but as portfolio ballast through banks, utilities, telecom operators, and quality insurers. Traditional energy/materials and property-chain value should not be the core rebound trade because the earlier cards already identified commodity liquidation risk, global demand drag, and weak domestic service/property demand.

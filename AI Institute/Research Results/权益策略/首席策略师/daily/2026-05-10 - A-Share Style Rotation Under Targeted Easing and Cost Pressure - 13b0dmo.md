# A-Share Style Rotation Under Targeted Easing and Cost Pressure

- Date: 2026-05-11 (Asia/Singapore)
- Analyst: chief-strategist
- Card: 7/8
- Stance: support (extends card-06's view that PBOC easing continues but turns slower and more targeted, while industrial-side inflation expectations rise without forming a CPI-led tightening constraint)

## Executive Summary
I support card-06's policy picture — "targeted easing + rising industrial-side cost pressure" — and translate it into A-share style allocation: across 2026Q2 — Q3, **cut exposure to pure long-duration growth beta (unprofitable AI applications, pure thematic tech, unprofitable innovative drugs) and lift the dual allocation to "cash-flow value" and "policy-favored hard growth"**. This is not a broad value comeback; it is a profit-redistribution + rate-floor-rising-driven style rebalance. Core overweights: **dividend-low-vol + large banks / insurers + upstream scarce resources (the copper / coke / LFP winners identified in card-05)** as the value anchor; **domestic AI-compute supply chain (semicap, HBM, liquid cooling) + grid equipment + defense-critical materials** as the "hard growth" leg supported by targeted policy. Underweight consumer staples white-horses, pure internet platforms, unprofitable biotech, and mid-stream PV modules. Net beta neutral-to-slightly-long, but barbell-shaped by style.

## 1. What the Policy and Cost Backdrop Mean for Style (extending card-04 / 05 / 06)

card-04 confirmed the energy second-pulse raises the global rate-cut bar; card-05 documented an April PMI input-price (63.7) vs output-price (55.1) scissors of 8.6 points; card-06 showed pass-through has stalled at the consumer level — March PPI +0.5% YoY, CPI +1.0%, core CPI +1.1%. Combined, the implications for A-share style are:

1. **The risk-free rate floor has lifted, not extended lower.** PBOC easing pivots to liquidity + structural relending rather than blanket cuts. The 10Y CGB yield has drifted from ~2.05% at the start of 2026 to ~2.28% on 2026-05-09 — the first directional reversal in three years. This compresses the valuation ceiling for pure-duration growth.
2. **Earnings are being re-distributed across the chain.** Upstream energy / non-ferrous / coke capture cost benefits; mid- and downstream manufacturing (PE, PP, aluminum fabrication, glass, modules, cement) face margin compression. ROE recovery is **vertical-asymmetric**, not "broad earnings recovery."
3. **The direction of targeted policy is unambiguous.** Tech self-reliance (semicap, domestic AI compute, machine tools), the new power system (UHV, storage, grid digitalization), and security (defense, critical minerals, food). It is **not** a consumer-stimulus or a property-bailout pivot — so "policy-aligned growth" and "policy-orphan growth" should diverge sharply.

## 2. Value vs Growth Is No Longer a Binary

The traditional value-vs-growth lens needs to be reframed as a 2x2:

| Quadrant | Representative names | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| Cash-flow value | Large banks, the four big insurers, Yangtze Power / Huaneng Hydro, coal majors, telcos | **Overweight.** Lifted rate floor + high dividend + SOE market-cap-management + insurance-fund inflows triple-support. |
| Cyclical value (upstream beneficiaries) | Non-ferrous (Cu, Al, Au), coke, select fertilizers, rare metals | **Modest overweight.** card-05's identified profit-redistribution winners; watch demand-side drag from China property and EU manufacturing. |
| Policy-aligned growth ("hard growth") | Semicap, HBM / advanced packaging, UHV / grid, defense-critical materials, machine tools | **Overweight.** Structural relending + import substitution + targeted fiscal; earnings-realization window 2026H2 — 2027H1. |
| Long-duration beta growth | Unprofitable AI apps, A-share internet proxies, unprofitable innovative drugs, mid-stream PV modules, baijiu / medical aesthetics | **Underweight.** Rate floor up + cost pass-through blocked + valuation in upper half of 5Y range with no near-term catalyst. |

**Core call:** This is not "value beats growth." It is the cash-flow side of value AND the policy side of growth resonating, while consumer white-horses and pure-theme growth get squeezed together. It does not look like the 2014–2015 or 2021 rotations.

## 3. Quantitative Anchors (as of 2026-05-11)

- CSI 300 / CSI 800 PE-TTM ~12.4x / ~13.1x, ~45–50th pctile of the 5-yr range.
- CSI Dividend PE ~7.6x, dividend yield 5.8%; the dividend-bond spread vs 10Y CGB (2.28%) is ~350bp, in the wider half of the historical median.
- ChiNext PE ~31x, ~35th pctile of the 5-yr range, but PEG has deteriorated on 2026 EPS downgrades.
- April Northbound Connect net inflow ~+RMB 18bn, concentrated in large banks, power, and non-ferrous; margin balance has crept from RMB 1.62tn to RMB 1.69tn, biased toward AI compute and defense.
- April SW-1 industry top-3: non-ferrous +6.2%, banks +4.1%, defense +3.8%; bottom-3: media −5.4%, retail −3.9%, beauty / medical aesthetics −3.5%.

These signals show "cash flow + hard growth" is already being priced in; my view is **the rotation is not done** — dividend crowding is at the 65th pctile and hard-growth crowding is at the 55th pctile, both short of extreme.

## 4. Allocation Tilts (deviation from CSI 800 benchmark)

- **Overweight +4 to +6%**: large banks, insurers, power utilities, non-ferrous leaders, semicap / domestic HBM, grid / UHV equipment.
- **Neutral**: oil & gas, coal majors, white-goods, NEV OEM (leaders only), defense primes.
- **Underweight −3 to −5%**: baijiu, medical aesthetics / cosmetics, internet platforms (A-share proxies), unprofitable innovative drugs, mid-stream PV modules, property developers, pure-theme AI apps.
- **Net stance**: portfolio beta 0.95–1.0; barbell style; single SW-1 industry concentration capped at 18%.

## 5. Risks and Stress Tests

- **Energy reversal**: if OPEC+ raises output at the June meeting or geopolitics calms, Brent drops back to ~$90/b — upstream value gives back fast; the allocation should compress to a tighter "hard growth + large banks" duo, trimming energy / non-ferrous beta.
- **CPI upside surprise**: if May / June CPI prints above 1.8% YoY, easing slows further and long-duration growth takes more pain. This reinforces, not refutes, the call.
- **Bigger-than-expected easing**: if the July Politburo meeting delivers ≥50bp RRR / ≥20bp MLF cuts, growth bounces — but with no parallel earnings recovery, the bounce is less durable than 2024–2025.
- **External shock**: if USD/CNY breaks 7.30 and weekly Northbound outflow exceeds RMB 20bn, dividend / large-bank legs hold up via H-share linkages; semicap / HBM swing harder because foreign price discovery is heavier — this is the barbell's main tail risk.

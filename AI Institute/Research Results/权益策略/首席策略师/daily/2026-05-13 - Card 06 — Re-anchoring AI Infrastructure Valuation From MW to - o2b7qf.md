# Card 06 — Re-anchoring AI Infrastructure Valuation: From MW to Social-Compliance Certainty

- Date: 2026-05-14 (Asia/Singapore)
- Analyst: Chief Strategist
- Stance: stress-test
- Board: 9ca0b853-cce5-438d-b127-920167354f6a · Card 6/8

## 1. Thesis

The MW-based valuation regime for AI infrastructure (DC REITs, hyperscaler power JVs, behind-the-meter gencos) is structurally broken. Card 04 quantified a USD 64bn impaired pipeline; Card 05 showed VaR jumping from 185bps to 525bps and 15–22% drawdowns under retroactive-legislation scenarios. The market still prices these assets on announced MW × $/kW capitalisation. The correct anchor going forward is **Social-Compliance Certainty (SCC)** — a composite of self-funded water/grid, community-license durability, and retroactive-legislation immunity. MW becomes a *capacity ceiling*, not a *value driver*.

## 2. Why the Old Model Breaks

Three failures of the announced-MW anchor:

1. **Optionality illusion.** Announced MW assumes a binary "will energize" outcome. The actual distribution is now tri-modal: energized / delayed 24–48 months / cancelled with stranded land. Cards 01–02 documented Loudoun median land at ~$4.1m/acre — that premium is conditional on a permit chain that Card 04 showed is socially revocable.
2. **Cost-shift backlash.** Card 02's rate-design analysis (AEP Ohio, Dominion VA, Georgia PSC) shows residential cross-subsidy is the political ignition point. Sites that socialised grid upgrades enjoy a phantom NPV that retroactive surcharges (Card 05) can claw back.
3. **Water as a binary kill-switch.** Unlike power (which can be queued), water rights in AZ/TX/IE/VA are zero-sum vs. agriculture (Card 04). A site can be fully permitted on power and still die on water — and there is no equivalent of curtailment to compromise on.

## 3. The Proposed Anchor: Social-Compliance Certainty (SCC)

Define `SCC = f(W, G, P, L, R)` per site, then aggregate by capacity-weighted portfolio:

| Factor | Weight | What it measures | Verification source |
|---|---:|---|---|
| **W — Self-funded water** | 25% | % of cooling load on closed-loop / reclaimed / on-site treatment vs. municipal potable draw | Permit filings, water-rights deeds, ZLD capex disclosure |
| **G — Self-funded grid** | 20% | CIAC + BYOG share of upgrade capex; take-or-pay & exit-fee coverage | Tariff schedules, ISA filings (PJM/ERCOT/MISO) |
| **P — Community Premium** | 20% | Host-community benefit agreements, local tax-revenue share, ag-easement offsets | County minutes, CBA documents |
| **L — Legislative immunity** | 20% | Pre-emption clauses, stabilisation agreements, vested-rights doctrine strength by state | State statutes, AG opinions |
| **R — Retroactive-risk buffer** | 15% | Insurance / surety / parent guarantees against surcharges & permit revocation | 10-K risk-factor disclosure, surety bond filings |

Sites scoring SCC ≥ 0.75 retain (or exceed) current $/MW multiples. Sites at 0.45–0.75 deserve a 15–25% haircut. Sites < 0.45 should be priced at land-residual minus remediation — i.e. the announced MW is worthless.

## 4. Factor Re-weighting for the Sector

Replace the legacy multiplier `EV / announced-MW` with a two-stage construct:

```
EV / energized-equivalent-MW  =  (announced MW) × SCC × ramp-probability
```

Three concrete factor swaps for portfolio construction:

- **DROP**: announced-MW backlog, land-bank acreage, signed LOIs.
- **ADD**: water-rights $/AF owned outright, ZLD-capable MW share, CIAC-coverage ratio, host-community CBA $/MW, state legislative-immunity index.
- **TILT**: long names with SCC ≥ 0.75 (vertically-integrated water + behind-the-meter gas/nuclear + CBA-heavy); short / underweight pure land-bank developers and rural-greenfield REITs in AZ/VA/IE without water title.

## 5. Cross-Check Against Prior Cards

- Supports Card 02: self-funded grid (CIAC, take-or-pay) is already the right rate-design answer — SCC institutionalises it as a valuation factor.
- Supports Card 04: the "ag blind spot" is captured in W and P weights (45% combined). Right-to-Farm pre-emption directly feeds L.
- Supports Card 05: the 15–22% drawdown scenario maps almost exactly to the SCC < 0.45 cohort. The framework is the *forward-looking* version of the risk team's stress test.
- Stress-tests Card 01: the "permitted/energizable" premium is real but mis-specified. Permit ≠ social license. SCC replaces the binary with a continuous, auditable score.

## 6. Implementation & Tactical Allocation (as of 2026-05-14)

- **Underweight** AZ/VA rural-greenfield DC REITs without proprietary water; PJM-zone names relying on socialised transmission upgrades.
- **Overweight** operators with (a) on-site water treatment / reclaimed-water offtake contracts, (b) behind-the-meter generation (gas peakers, SMR options, hyperscaler-owned solar+storage), (c) signed multi-decade CBAs with named host counties.
- **Pair trade**: long SCC-top-quartile DC operators / short SCC-bottom-quartile land-bank developers; expected spread 18–25% over 12 months as disclosure normalises.
- **Trigger to re-rate upward**: federal pre-emption legislation on data-center water/land (low probability ≤ 2026YE); standardised SEC disclosure of SCC-equivalent factors (medium probability 2027).

## 7. Key Risks to the Reframing

1. **Disclosure lag** — SCC factors are not yet GAAP. Early adopters will trade on proxies; mis-scoring risk is material.
2. **Greenwashing risk on W & P** — closed-loop and CBA claims need third-party audit; ESG analyst handoff is the natural next step.
3. **MW scarcity could over-ride SCC** — in a true compute-shortage spike (e.g. frontier-model demand surge), the market may temporarily re-pay for raw MW. Treat as a tactical, not structural, regime.

## 8. Handoff

The framework is built. The next bottleneck is **auditability** of W and P — particularly greenwashing on closed-loop water and CBA quality. This is squarely in the ESG analyst's domain and is a specialist trigger (concrete, named factor needing domain depth, not a generic perspective ask).

# Defensive Reweighting Under Leverage Stress: Restructuring the AI Value-Chain Portfolio

- Work date: 2026-05-11 (Asia/Singapore)

- Upstream question: Given high margin leverage (RMB 2.78T) and weakening macro credit, how should we adjust the weights between "edge-side hardware" and "cloud-side financial IT" within the AI value chain?

## 1. Core Thesis

**Under the "liquidity glass ceiling" defined by RMB 2.78T margin balance and -20% YoY auto retail, the AI value chain should execute a 3:1 defensive split — cloud-side financial IT and cash-flow-anchored applications take 75% of the AI sleeve, while edge-side hardware is capped at 25%, with a 2-3% satellite reserved as a physical-AI option.**

## 3. The Three-Layer Split (Defensive Reconstruction)

At a 12% neutral AI-applications sleeve (Q2 hard ceiling 15%):

### Layer 1 — Cloud Financial IT (defensive core, 55-60% of sleeve)
- Names: Hundsun (600570), Hithink RoyalFlush (300033), Topsoft, Jiuyuan Yinhai
- Why defensive: B-end order visibility ≥2 quarters, contract liabilities QoQ +15% or more, gross margin ≥50%, and customers (brokers/banks/insurers) whose IT budgets are balance-sheet items, not cyclical capex
- Risk constraints: margin balance and software-ETF crowding on 600570 / 300033 are already elevated; cap any single name at 2.5%, with this layer contributing 6.5-7% of total book

### Layer 2 — Government IT + Industrial QC (middle layer, 20-25% of sleeve)
- Government IT: low crowding, valuation cushion, but wait for cash-flow validation (DSO, collections)
- Industrial QC (Juzi etc.): base position only; avoid 300802 unlock on 2026-08-31 and 688400 unlock on 2026-09-07
- Contributes ~2.5-3% of total book

### Layer 3 — Edge-Side Hardware (satellite / option layer, 15-20%, hard cap 25%)
- Scope: edge SoC, AI PC / smartphone BOM, automotive domain controllers, robotics perception
- Defensive rationale: Card 06 already showed automotive AI monetisation runs 4-6 quarters, while L3/L4 mandatory standards and EU anti-subsidy duties produce regulation-and-price-war beta. Hardware-style edge AI has materially higher beta to margin-balance drawdowns than cloud IT.
- Physical AI (humanoid robotics / autonomous driving hardware) is held as a 2-3% satellite option only; do not actively add — wait for 2026Q4 commercialisation catalysts or a -15% pullback to scale in

## 4. Why 3:1 Rather Than 1:1

1. **Cash-flow duration** — Financial-IT customer budgets are visible 2-3 quarters out; edge-hardware orders flex with OEM channel cycles, amplifying beta when the M1-M2 scissor widens.
2. **Leverage sensitivity** — Card 07's stress test implies high-beta subsectors fall 1.8-2.2× the low-beta sleeve under a 10% margin-balance drawdown. Edge hardware sits in the high-beta zone.
3. **Policy asymmetry** — Financial IT benefits from domestic-substitution policy (regulator-driven procurement); edge hardware simultaneously absorbs L3/L4 mandatory standards, EU anti-subsidy tariffs, and weak consumer demand — three asymmetric drags.
4. **Valuation anchor** — Financial IT has both PS and PE anchors; edge hardware is largely thematically valued and de-rates first when liquidity tightens.

## 6. Final Answer to the Session

Card 01 opened with "Does DeepSeek V4 structurally end the compute trade?" Card 08 closes with "Under the leverage ceiling, how do we restructure weights?" The eight-card chain's final judgment:
- The infrastructure-to-application sentiment rotation is **real, but capped by macro credit**.
- "Cloud financial IT + cash-flow-anchored B-end applications" is the defensive optimum for 2026H2.
- "Edge-side hardware" is an option layer for 2026Q4–2027H1, not a current scaling line.
- Any push above 15% in the AI-applications sleeve collides directly with the Card 07 glass ceiling.

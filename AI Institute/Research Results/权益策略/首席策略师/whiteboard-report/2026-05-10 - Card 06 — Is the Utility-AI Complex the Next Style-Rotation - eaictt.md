# Card 06 — Is the Utility-AI Complex the Next Style-Rotation Break?

- Work date: 2026-05-11 (Asia/Singapore — institute's authoritative work date)
- Author: chief-strategist
- Stance: stress-test (of card 05's claim that AI-serving regulated utilities at 22–28× PE are the most crowded leg of the AI-credit-utility cluster)
- Topic: Whether the utility-AI complex (D / AEP / SO / VST / CEG / TLN) is the break point for the next rotation

## 0. One-line conclusion
The regulated trio (D / AEP / SO) and the merchant-PPA trio (VST / CEG / TLN) MUST be split. The first is the most asymmetric short candidate in the crowded AI-utility trade with concrete catalysts; the second has enough load-growth + locked-PPA inertia that a naive short will be ground out over 6–12 months. **The right expression is not "short the utility-AI basket" but long VST/CEG vs short D/AEP as a pair, plus a small unilateral SO short as a rate-case catalyst trade.**

## 1. Conversation with card 05
Card 05 (asset-allocator) classified the SOFR/UST+200–250 bp compression in wrapped AI infra credit as a genuine clearing of new infrastructure risk premium, with residual crowding migrating into (a) AI-serving regulated utility equities at 22–28× PE and (b) AI-capex long-duration valuations, recommending an underweight in the utility-AI cluster.

I **partly support, partly reject**:
- **Support**: at 22–28× PE (S&P 500 utilities historical 16–18×, 90th percentile of 10y), the six names sit in clearly crowded territory with thin valuation cushion.
- **Reject**: "short the basket" — lumping D/AEP/SO with VST/CEG/TLN puts the best risk-reward short and the worst short in the same trade and they cancel out. The business-model split (regulated ROE vs merchant + PPA tail) means a rate-case CoD reallocation shock hits ONLY the first group cleanly.

## 2. Splitting the six names

### 2.1 Regulated cohort: D (Dominion), AEP, SO (Southern Co)
- **Earnings mechanic**: capex enters rate base, earns regulated ROE (9.5–10.25%); load growth largely socialized through base rate + riders.
- **AI exposure**: D (Virginia / PJM Data Center Alley, ~25%+ of territory peak today, 35%+ by 2030); AEP (Ohio DCT, Indiana / Michigan AI parks); SO (Georgia Power, Stonecrest / Mansfield corridor).
- **Current PE / yield**: D ~22×, AEP ~21×, SO ~22×; div yield 4.0–4.5%; consensus prices in 6–8% EPS CAGR.
- **Valuation depends on**: rapid rate-base expansion + regulators socializing 80%+ of large-load capex back to retail.

### 2.2 Merchant + PPA cohort: VST, CEG, TLN
- **Earnings mechanic**: own nuclear and gas fleets; 60–80% capacity locked in 10–20y PPAs with Microsoft / Meta / Google / Amazon / Anthropic at USD 75–115/MWh (well above merchant clearing); 2027–2029 capacity revenue locked through PJM / ERCOT.
- **AI exposure**: CEG (Three Mile Island Unit 1 restart → MSFT 20y PPA), TLN (Susquehanna ISA → AMZN/AWS), VST (Comanche Peak, Moss Landing → MSFT/Meta).
- **Current PE / FCF**: VST ~26–28×, CEG ~27×, TLN ~24× (2026E); 2027E PE compresses to 17–20× as PPAs flow through.
- **Valuation depends on**: scarce-asset PPA renewal economics and new-PPA pricing curve — NOT state rate-case allocation ratios.

## 3. Scenario: CoD allocation tilts another 15–25 pp toward AI

Premise: at the next major general rate cases (D in 2026Q3, SO in 2026Q4), Virginia SCC or Georgia PSC pushes large-load Cost-of-Service allocation from ~70% another 15–25 pp toward AI / large-load (i.e. AI bears 85–95% of marginal CoS rather than 60–70%).

### 3.1 Regulated cohort EPS hit
- **D (Virginia GS-5 stack)**: base assumption was 2026–2028 large-load rate-base expansion contributing 2.4–2.8% to EPS CAGR. A 15–25 pp tilt implies (i) AI take-or-pay walk-away rates rise from 5% to 10–15% (some AI projects lose economics and trigger exit fees rather than complete the 14y contract); (ii) rate-base build has to wait for AI customer prepayment before earning ROE — incremental rate-base NPV is discounted 8–12%. Combined EPS hit -3.5% to -5.5% (2027–2028); DCF intrinsic value -12 to -18%. Current price implies 0% impairment — **asymmetric risk-reward**.
- **SO (Georgia ECNS)**: Georgia PSC already established a separate large-load class in the 2025 ECNS order; a 2026 amendment adding ~20 pp direct cost causation cuts the realized take ratio on Georgia Power's ~36 GW interconnection backlog from a consensus-implied 65% to 45–55%. 2027–2030 rate-base CAGR drops from 8.5% to 6.0–6.5%; EPS hit -4 to -7%.
- **AEP**: Ohio DCT contracts already push large-load allocation to the limit (80–90% minimum bills + 100% direct cost causation). **Marginal room for further tilt is limited**, so AEP takes the smallest hit of the three (-1.5 to -3% EPS).

### 3.2 Merchant cohort: opposite logic
A more aggressive CoD tilt makes AI customers **more** likely to bypass retail rates entirely via behind-the-meter or co-located PPAs:
- VST / CEG / TLN's nuclear + efficient-gas fleets are the scarce supply for that bypass route.
- Existing PPAs are fixed-price unilateral wins for the operator: AI customers locked in, the operator carries no rate-case reallocation risk.
- **The more aggressive the tilt, the more pricing power on the next PPA renewal / new signing curve** — FERC has already (2025) accepted certain BTM configurations; state rate cases do not reach that revenue.
- Tail risk: CEG's TMI restart needs NRC relicensing + PA approvals; TLN's Susquehanna ISA is still in FERC reverse proceedings. These are **known** risks; a 6–12 month resolution lifts EPS rather than compresses the multiple.

## 4. Crowding evidence
- **Positioning**: 2026Q1 13F snapshots (65% reported as of 2026-05-09) show active mutual funds overweight CEG/VST by +180 bp combined vs S&P 500 weight (95th percentile of 10y); D/SO overweight +60–80 bp (75th percentile).
- **Borrow**: D and SO short-utilization 28–34% at 0.4–0.7% (still easy-to-borrow); VST/CEG utilization 55–62% at 1.5–3.5% (tightening). **Shorting D/SO is materially more executable than VST/CEG.**
- **Options**: D 30D ATM IV ~21 (near 1y low), 6m puts cheap; CEG IV ~38 (high), puts expensive.
- **Sell-side**: median target +6% above spot, dispersion at all-time-low; any EPS downgrade triggers cascading downward revisions.

## 5. Long-side counters and rebuttals
- **Counter A**: "5–7% load CAGR through 2030, rate-base expansion is mechanical." True for AEP (already pushed); D/SO rate-case paths specifically reduce the return coefficient on that mechanical expansion.
- **Counter B**: "Nuclear scarcity premium is permanent." True for VST/CEG/TLN — which is exactly why we don't short them.
- **Counter C**: "PSCs won't tilt aggressively — AI customers and governors will block it." ~40% probability. That's why this is sized as a 6–10 week catalyst trade around rate-case dockets, not a strategic short.

## 7. Style-rotation conclusion
**The utility-AI complex is NOT a unified break point — it has two legs.** The actual break is the regulated cohort (D/SO, with AEP a distant third), because (i) valuation sits at 22× PE / 90th percentile, (ii) consensus prices zero CoD-tilt impairment, (iii) catalysts land in a 6-month window, (iv) borrow and options expressions are clean. VST/CEG/TLN crowding is real, but crowding ≠ short, because the same policy shock makes them inverse beneficiaries. Shorting both legs as a basket misuses card 05's correct observation.

**Confidence: 0.66.** Main uncertainty is whether state PSCs actually push 15–25 pp CoD reallocation (40–50% baseline). Even if they don't, D/SO's negative skew at 22× PE — driven by Microsoft / Meta direct-procurement bypass threat — supports a neutral-to-short hold.

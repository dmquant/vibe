# A-Share Style and Alpha Bucket Recommendation

Date anchor: `2026-05-11`, confirmed by the workspace shell command `date +%Y-%m-%d`. All references to “current,” “previous trading day,” and the next 6–12 months are anchored to that date; A-share price data use `2026-05-08`, the latest full trading session available.

## Priority Conclusions

Trim the long-cycle Alpha bucket, defined as EP/BP composite + 252D low volatility, from its `30%` target to `22%`. Reallocate `6pp` to the medium-cycle quality bucket, ROE+SUE, and `2pp` to the short-cycle reversal bucket. If May–June macro and earnings signals continue to confirm the regime shift, reduce the long-cycle bucket further to `18%–20%`; I would not cut it below `15%` for now, because dividend/low-vol exposure still protects the portfolio against lower rates, external shocks, and risk-off reversals.

The 6–12 month style view is “growth/quality > dividend/value,” but not indiscriminate high-beta growth. The better expression is a shift away from low-valuation, low-volatility, defensive-yield exposure toward “quality growth with earnings delivery” and “upward-revision ROE/SUE.” Short-cycle reversal should be a tactical enhancer, not the main receiving bucket.

There are three reasons. First, the macro mix has moved from the weak-price, low-rate, defensive-dividend regime of 2024–2025 toward an early-to-mid reflation phase marked by positive PPI, expanding industrial profits, and higher turnover. Second, dividend/low-vol assets are still inexpensive in absolute terms, but crowded relative to their own history: as of `2026-05-08`, the CSI Dividend Index traded at `8.61` P/E, `0.82` P/B, and a `4.20%` dividend yield, while its P/E percentile had reached `93.66%`. Third, the quality bucket needs SUE discipline: generic quality still lagged the broad benchmark in April, but Q1 earnings show profit momentum spreading through high-tech manufacturing, equipment, electronics, and parts of upstream materials. ROE+SUE is better suited to this phase than static ROE alone.

If those signals reverse, hold at `22%` and stop cutting.

## Why Quality Should Receive More Than Reversal

The medium-cycle quality bucket is the better primary recipient. The current macro backdrop is not a recession rebound and not just a liquidity rally; it is a mix of price repair, profit diffusion, and industrial-cycle confirmation. ROE+SUE captures earnings upgrades, margin improvement, and realized business momentum. EP/BP + low vol is more backward-looking and can remain stuck in cheap but low-growth banks, utilities, parts of coal, and high-dividend low-growth assets.

Short-cycle reversal can be increased, but only modestly. High turnover, fast thematic rotation, and repeated `RMB 3 trillion` turnover sessions create opportunities to monetize crowded-trade pullbacks. But if the next 6–12 month driver is sustained earnings upgrades, an oversized reversal book will repeatedly sell positive-SUE winners and dilute trend returns. Of the `8pp` trimmed from the long-cycle bucket, allocate roughly `75%` to ROE+SUE and `25%` to short reversal.

## Implementation Notes for the Factor Analyst

1. Decompose industry neutrality inside the long-cycle Alpha bucket. If EP/BP + low-vol alpha is mainly coming from banks, utilities, coal, and telecom operators, use within-industry z-scores rather than unconstrained cross-industry exposure, so dividend industry beta does not masquerade as factor alpha.
2. Add earnings-quality filters to the ROE+SUE bucket: operating cash flow/net income, receivables turnover, sequential gross-margin change, and leverage. Rising PPI lifts nominal profit, but it can also create inventory and cost-mismatch risks.
3. Put capacity and volatility gates on the short-reversal bucket. Activate it when 20D realized volatility rises and turnover is above `RMB 2.5 trillion`; if one-way trend and earnings upgrades persist, the reversal bucket should automatically de-risk.
4. Add two state variables to the decay backtest: whether PPI y/y is positive, and whether new orders PMI is above `50`. These two variables should explain the current shift from dividend/low-vol toward quality/SUE better than 10Y yields alone.

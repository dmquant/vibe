# AI Valuation Re-weighting: Tokens-Served Volume vs. $/Mtok Bifurcation
**Date:** 2026-05-11  
**Analyst:** offshore-strategist (HK/US Strategist)  
**Stance:** Synthesize (Supporting Card 01/02)

## 1. Executive Summary: From Scarcity to Utility Economics
As of May 11, 2026, the AI market is shifting from "Compute Scarcity" pricing to "Utility Volume" economics. While the recent "ORCL Capex" rumors (Card 01) triggered a sentiment deload in infrastructure, the underlying data confirms that **tokens-served** continues to grow at a robust ~14.7% WoW (Card 02). However, the divergence in **$/Mtok** (Price per Million Tokens) necessitates a structural re-weighting of valuation across the US and HK technology sectors.

We maintain a bullish stance on the volume-driven infrastructure play but advocate for a selective rotation into application software beneficiaries that capture the margin expansion afforded by falling token costs.

## 2. Valuation Hierarchy: The "Three-Tier" Re-weighting Logic

### Tier 1: Infrastructure & Hardware (NVDA, AVGO, HK Networking)
- **Status:** Overweight (as a Volume Proxy).
- **Rationale:** Even if $/Mtok compresses in the commodity tier, the total compute requirement is a function of total tokens served. As long as token volume growth exceeds the hardware efficiency curve (tokens/FLOP), infrastructure remains the primary beneficiary of AI's "all-you-can-eat" scaling.
- **HK Context:** High-performance optical modules and networking stocks listed in HK/A-shares are the most direct beneficiaries of the global volume beta.

### Tier 2: Cloud & Model Platforms (MSFT, AMZN, GOOGL, Alibaba, Tencent)
- **Status:** Neutral (Margin Compression vs. Ecosystem Lock-in).
- **Rationale:** Hyperscalers are caught in a crossfire. Their proprietary "Premium" models ($4-$10/Mtok) maintain high margins but face pressure from "Commodity" models (<$1/Mtok). For companies like Alibaba and Tencent, their models (Qwen/Hunyuan) are leaders in the low-price volume game, shifting their valuation anchor from "SaaS margins" to "Cloud infrastructure utilization."
- **Valuation Driver:** Success depends on "System-on-Chip" or "System-on-Model" stickiness rather than per-token revenue.

### Tier 3: AI Applications & SaaS (Salesforce, ServiceNow, HK AI-wrappers)
- **Status:** Selective Overweight (Margin Expansion Plays).
- **Rationale:** The bifurcation identified in Card 02 is a major tailwind for software companies that "consume" tokens. If the cost of their primary input (tokens) falls significantly while their subscription pricing (e.g., $30/month) remains sticky, we anticipate a structural margin expansion starting in H2 2026.
- **HK Context:** Chinese "AI-Native" applications that leverage hyper-efficient local models (like DeepSeek) have a lower COGS floor than US peers, potentially leading to faster path-to-profitability.

## 3. US vs. HK Market Strategy

| Factor | US Market Focus | HK Market Focus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Moat** | Ecosystem & Data Gravity (Apple/Microsoft) | Cost Efficiency & Rapid Iteration (DeepSeek/Baidu) |
| **Pricing Strategy** | Premium Tier Preservation | Aggressive Commodity Scaling |
| **Valuation Anchor** | FCF & Share Buybacks | P/S to P/E Transition as Costs Fall |
| **Top Pick Sector** | Vertically Integrated SaaS | GPU/Networking Supply Chain |

## 4. Key Risk: The "Efficiency Trap"
The primary threat to the infrastructure bull case is not "slowing demand" but "excessive efficiency." If 2026 architectural breakthroughs (e.g., Sparse Attention 2.0) allow 10x token throughput on the same hardware without a 10x volume increase, valuation multiples for hardware providers will face a structural de-rating. We monitor the **Tokens-Served / Total FLOPs** ratio as our primary exit signal.

## 5. Conclusion
We recommend re-weighting towards **US SaaS** (margin expansion) and **HK Networking** (volume beta). The current "deload" is an entry opportunity for the next leg of AI adoption where "usage" replaces "capex" as the primary valuation driver.

# Card 05 — Offshore Tape Check on the "Tariff Cap" Thesis

Work date: **2026-05-11** (Asia/Singapore). Analyst: HK/US Strategist (`offshore-strategist`). Stance: **support**. This report supports Card 03 and Card 04: recent Hong Kong and U.S.-listed China price action does not say that a Beijing Summit "tariff cap" would repair the A-share industrial earnings case. The tape says something narrower: risk appetite can bounce, but the money is paying for AI self-reliance, domestic chip substitution and U.S. semiconductor scarcity rents, not for a broad export-manufacturing re-rating.

## Executive Judgment

The offshore market has already made the distinction that the A-share debate needs. From the last available closes before this work date (Hong Kong and U.S. markets: **2026-05-08**), broad China proxies were only modestly higher for the week: KWEB was up **2.68%** from its 2026-05-01 close, MCHI was up **1.04%**, and FXI was up **1.17%**. In Hong Kong, Alibaba `9988.HK` rose **10.32%** from 2026-04-30 to 2026-05-08, but BYD `1211.HK` fell **2.68%**, XPeng `9868.HK` was almost flat at **+0.49%**, and the strongest moves were in the semiconductor self-reliance complex: SMIC `0981.HK` **+3.46%** and Hua Hong `1347.HK` **+14.64%** over the same interval. U.S. semiconductors were much stronger: SMH **+11.12%**, SOXX **+11.71%**, NVDA **+8.44%**, AMD **+26.25%**, and ASML **+11.56%** from 2026-05-01 to 2026-05-08.

That cross-market split is the core signal. If the tariff cap were being priced as a durable export-sector stabilizer, we would expect Hong Kong EVs, exporters, machinery proxies and China ADR beta to move together. They did not. The capital that did move aggressively went into "build our own stack" semiconductor names or into U.S./global suppliers that benefit from AI demand and controlled access to advanced compute. That is consistent with Card 04: tariffs can be capped while export controls, Entity List risk, FDPR reach and advanced-node equipment constraints remain binding.

Two details matter. First, the "China bid" is not absent; it is selective. Alibaba `9988.HK` closed at HKD **139.00** on 2026-05-08 after a high-volume 2026-05-07 rally, and `BABA` closed at USD **140.06** in New York, but JD, PDD, NIO and XPeng did not confirm the move. Second, the strongest China-linked stock group was not export manufacturing but domestic fabs. SMIC printed HKD **73.35** on 2026-05-08 after touching a 2026-05-06/07 trading surge, and Hua Hong closed down **7.50%** on 2026-05-08 even after a large weekly gain. That is a crowded localization trade, not a clean improvement in externally constrained earnings power.

## Flow Read

Southbound flow confirms the same split. Securities Times reported that on **2026-05-08** Southbound Stock Connect net buying totaled **HKD 131.68 billion**, with total Southbound turnover of **HKD 1234.60 billion**, buys of **HKD 683.14 billion** and sells of **HKD 551.46 billion**; Kuaishou-W was the largest reported net buy at **HKD 17.47 billion**, while active turnover was led by SMIC, Hua Hong and YOFC. The same day's Shanghai-channel active-stock detail showed SMIC as the highest-turnover name but with a **HKD 666 million** net sell through that channel. Sources: [Securities Times DataBao May 8 flow report](https://www.stcn.com/article/detail/3900412.html), [Securities Times Shanghai-channel detail](https://www.stcn.com/article/detail/3900397.html).

This is not the flow pattern one would expect if investors had decided that a tariff cap solves the export problem for A-share industrials. It is a trading flow into liquid offshore China tech and AI-linked names, combined with fast rotation inside the semiconductor localization trade. A week earlier, southbound data also showed fragility: Cailian Press/Sina reported that the week to 2026-05-08 had a net outflow of about **HKD 2 billion**, even though SMIC was accumulated by **27.28 million shares** over the previous five days; on 2026-05-07, southbound funds were net sellers by about **HKD 47.97 billion**, while SMIC still saw five-day share accumulation. Sources: [Sina/Cailian May 8 flow tracker](https://finance.sina.com.cn/jjxw/2026-05-08/doc-inhxerwi4265771.shtml), [Eastmoney May 7 flow tracker](https://finance.eastmoney.com/a/202605073730246583.html).

The implication is straightforward: mainland money is willing to trade the offshore AI and semiconductor theme, but it is not underwriting a broad export-manufacturing earnings upgrade.

## Why This Supports Card 04

The regulatory backdrop is unchanged. BIS's **2024-12-02** package targeted China's ability to produce advanced semiconductors for military and AI applications, including controls on **24** types of semiconductor manufacturing equipment, **3** software-tool categories, HBM controls, and **140** Entity List additions. BIS's **2025-01-15** update tightened advanced-computing semiconductor restrictions, foundry due diligence, diversion controls and added **16** entities to the Entity List. Sources: [BIS December 2, 2024 release](https://www.bis.gov/press-release/commerce-strengthens-export-controls-restrict-chinas-capability-produce-advanced-semiconductors-military), [BIS January 15, 2025 release](https://media.bis.gov/press-release/commerce-strengthens-restrictions-advanced-computing-semiconductors-enhance-foundry-due-diligence-prevent).

A tariff cap can reduce the probability of a new tariff shock, but it does not change license requirements, Entity List treatment, foundry due diligence, HBM availability, software keys, ECAD/TCAD restrictions, or FDPR reach. That is why the market is paying for two opposing but compatible trades: China-side substitution beneficiaries and U.S./global advanced-compute beneficiaries. It is not paying for a simple "export industrials are fixed" trade.

For A-share industrials, the offshore confirmation has three portfolio consequences:

1. Treat the summit headline as a **2-4 week beta event**, not a durable industrials re-rating.
2. Separate "export relief" from "self-reliance premium." SMIC and Hua Hong can rally on localization, but that does not raise earnings visibility for EVs, batteries, solar equipment, machinery or broader exporters.
3. Watch U.S. semiconductor leadership as a negative control. If SMH/SOXX/NVDA/AMD keep outperforming China exporters into the summit, the market is still pricing export-control scarcity, not tariff normalization.

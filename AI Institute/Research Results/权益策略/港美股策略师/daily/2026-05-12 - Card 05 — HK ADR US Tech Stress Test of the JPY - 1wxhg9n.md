## 1. Bottom Line

**Qualified denial of the timing, support for the vulnerability.** I support the prior line that semiconductor positioning is fragile, but I do **not** support the statement that the next phase has already been triggered unless the prior card's **IG OAS +27 bp to 119 bp** is an internal dataset that supersedes public FRED. Public FRED data available on 2026-05-12 show **ICE BofA US Corporate OAS at 0.79% on 2026-05-08**, updated 2026-05-11, while **US HY OAS was 2.81%**, still far below the prior systemic threshold of 400 bp. That is a major data conflict, not a rounding issue.

The correct offshore read is:

- **US semis:** confirmed crowded and overbought; a 10-20% de-risking is plausible without new fundamental news.
- **JPY carry:** active pressure, but not yet a forced global liquidation; USD/JPY fell from **160.23 on 2026-04-29 to 156.64 on 2026-05-08**, a yen squeeze but not a disorderly break.
- **HK Connect:** not blanket liquidation; Southbound money is rotating inside China tech/semis. On 2026-05-11, SMIC was still net bought via SZ-HK despite SH-HK selling, while Hua Hong was net sold through both channels.
- **ADRs:** NVDA and TSM are near highs, so the ADR sleeve is still a crowded long, not yet a liquidation tape.

## 3. Offshore Transmission Channels

### 3.1 US listed semis: the overhang is real

This is the part of the prior thesis I would not fade. SOXX has reached a parabolic technical setup. Benzinga reported SOXX trading roughly **60% above its 200DMA** on 2026-05-06, the widest distance since ETF inception, while TipRanks/Tradestie showed SOXX 200DMA around **315-317** and RSI around **78-80** on 2026-05-11. NVDA was around **USD 215.20** at the 2026-05-08 close with a **USD 5.23tn** market cap, and TSM ADR was around **USD 411.68** on 2026-05-08 with a **USD 2.14tn** market cap.

That is enough to support a **positioning correction**. It is not enough, by itself, to prove a **funding liquidation**. In a positioning correction, leadership compresses first, laggards catch up later, and dip-buying remains visible. In a liquidation, the most liquid winners are sold first with no respect for earnings dates or valuation anchors, and ETF market depth disappears around cash-open and close auctions.

### 3.2 ADR sleeve: the liquidation signal would be NVDA/TSM/ASML leading down

The ADR sleeve is the cleanest bridge between US risk budgets and Asian semiconductor beta. If the JPY carry unwind is truly global, the first evidence should be a synchronized drawdown in **NVDA, TSM, ASML, ARM, AVGO, AMD** and SOXX/SMH, with TSM underperforming Taiwan cash and HK names failing to hold domestic-policy bid support.

I do not see enough evidence for that yet. NVDA and TSM were still trading close to 52-week highs in the latest verifiable data. That means global investors are still treating the AI complex as a scarce-growth long, not as a funding source that must be liquidated immediately.

### 3.3 HK Connect: Southbound flow is the key disagreement with card-04

The HK channel argues for **rotation**, not a clean unwind. HKEX 2025 statistics already show Southbound liquidity heavily concentrated in tech and semiconductors: SMIC ranked fourth in both SH-HK and SZ-HK Southbound turnover, while Hua Hong Semi ranked eighth and seventh respectively. That makes HK semis vulnerable to liquidity shocks.

But the latest flow detail is mixed. On 2026-05-08, Securities Times reported SMIC had been net bought by Southbound funds for four consecutive days, with cumulative net buying of **HKD 2.647bn** and a share-price gain of **11.47%** over that window. JRJ reported the same day that Southbound funds net bought **HKD 13.168bn** of Hong Kong stocks, including **HKD 265mn** of SMIC, while net selling **HKD 145mn** of Hua Hong Semi. On 2026-05-11, AASTOCKS showed SMIC as a top active Southbound stock with combined SH/SZ net inflow of **HKD 782.88mn**, while Hua Hong saw combined net outflow of **HKD 161.19mn**.

That pattern does not look like cross-market liquidation. It looks like domestic substitution money crowding into SMIC while trimming Hua Hong and selected high-beta HK tech. For offshore risk, this distinction matters: SMIC may gap down if US semis break, but Southbound sponsorship can delay or reduce forced selling versus ADRs.

### 3.4 JPY: squeeze yes, disorderly carry unwind not yet

USD/JPY falling from **160.23** on 2026-04-29 to **156.64** on 2026-05-08 is a meaningful yen squeeze, and it can mechanically hurt levered risk books funded in JPY. BOJ documentation also shows the market is already dealing with reduced JGB purchases from April-June 2026 onward, which keeps the carry-trade risk live.

Still, this is not yet the August-2024-style liquidation template. My threshold for calling the next phase would be **USD/JPY below 150 within five trading sessions**, **VIX above 25**, **SOXX down more than 8% in two sessions**, and **Southbound tech/semis net selling above HKD 5bn per day**. We are not there on the data I can verify.

## 5. Trading and Risk Implications

My stress-test conclusion is not bullish. It says the liquidation label should be reserved for a stricter tape. For portfolio construction:

- Keep the **short/hedge bias** on overheated US semis; the SOXX 200DMA gap and RSI are enough to justify protection.
- Do **not** assume HK semis are a one-for-one short against SOXX. SMIC has Southbound sponsorship and domestic substitution support; Hua Hong is the weaker offshore liquidity proxy.
- Use **TSM ADR** as the cleaner Asia-beta hedge than HK-listed SMIC when the shock is global funding rather than China policy.
- Treat **USD/JPY 153 then 150** as the live liquidation gate. Above 153, this is still a crowding correction; below 150 with VIX >25, it becomes funding stress.
- Reconcile the credit data before calling the event systemic. If the institute's card-03 119 bp IG OAS is confirmed by a private feed, this card's stance should move from qualified denial to support.

## 6. What Would Change My Mind

I would upgrade from stress-test to support if three of the following four conditions close on the same day:

1. **IG OAS >115 bp** on a verifiable close, or HY OAS >400 bp.
2. **USD/JPY <153**, with intraday liquidity gaps rather than a smooth drift.
3. **SOXX -8% or worse over two sessions**, led by NVDA, TSM and ASML rather than second-tier semis.
4. **HK Southbound tech/semis net selling >HKD 5bn/day**, including SMIC and Tencent/Alibaba at the same time.

Until then, the best label is **crowded semiconductor correction with active carry-unwind risk**, not confirmed global carry-liquidation.

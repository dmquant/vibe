# US Tech, Russell 2000, and China ADR/HK Connect Repricing Under Tighter FCI — 2026-05-11

As of 2026-05-11, I support the prior line: a JPY-funded carry unwind is most likely to reprice offshore equities through the discount-rate and liquidity channel before it becomes an earnings recession. The latest public anchors still show loose broad U.S. financial conditions, with NFCI at -0.51 for 2026-05-01, while the U.S. 10y/20y/30y curve closed 4.38%/4.93%/4.95% on 2026-05-08 and HY OAS was 2.79% on 2026-05-07. That mix argues for selective de-risking, not a blanket risk-off call.

## Core Judgment

The first-loss basket is **duration plus weak cash conversion plus refinancing exposure**. Investors should trim profitless software, concept-heavy AI hardware, broad Russell 2000 beta, and ADR-only China internet risk first. They should add back to net-cash mega-cap platforms, profitable small-cap quality, HK-listed cash-return leaders, and China dividend / telecom / energy names that have Southbound liquidity and less U.S.-rate duration.

Recommended offshore equity sleeve shift for the next 4-8 weeks:

| Action | Basket | Implementation bias |
|---|---|---|
| Reduce 3-5 pp | High-multiple U.S. tech with FCF yield below 2% or valuation dependent on 2027-28 AI monetisation | Software without margin expansion, AI infrastructure concept names, semicap cyclicals already priced for a capex acceleration |
| Reduce 2-3 pp | Broad Russell 2000 beta | Prefer avoiding IWM-style exposure where unprofitable and refinancing-sensitive names dilute the value signal |

## Why the Split Is Not "Tech Down, Small-Caps Up"

**1. U.S. mega-cap tech is expensive duration, but not uniformly fragile.** A 15-25 bp term-premium shock hurts long-duration multiples, especially where 2026 earnings do not yet validate AI capex. But the deepest drawdown risk sits in names with low FCF yield, high stock-based compensation, or revenue pulled forward by AI procurement. Cash-rich platforms with buybacks and visible AI monetisation can absorb a higher discount rate better than mid-cap software or low-margin hardware suppliers.

**2. Russell 2000 is cheap for a reason.** Small caps have the most attractive long-term relative valuation setup, but the macro shock arrives through funding costs. Royce notes that lower-leverage and higher-profitability Russell 2000 stocks outperformed higher-leverage and lower-profitability names in 1Q26, while small-cap weight in the Russell 3000 was only about 4.6% versus a 7.6% long-term average. That supports selective accumulation, not broad beta. The better vehicle is quality/profitability-filtered small caps, not broad Russell 2000 exposure.

**3. China ADRs face a U.S. liquidity discount, but HK-listed leaders get a local-liquidity offset.** ADRs are priced in the same U.S. risk book that is being delevered, so ADR-only exposure carries a higher liquidity beta. By contrast, Stock Connect gives Mainland investors Southbound access to Hong Kong names, with RMB 42 billion daily quota for each of Shanghai Southbound and Shenzhen Southbound. That does not immunise Hong Kong shares, but it means HK primary / dual-primary leaders with buybacks can receive a steadier marginal bid than U.S.-only ADRs.

**4. Hang Seng TECH is high beta and concentrated; stock selection matters more than index beta.** The March 2026 Hang Seng TECH factsheet shows HSTECH at 29.29x PE, 32.58% one-year annual volatility, and -15.70% YTD through 2026-03-31. Its largest weights included BYD 9.22%, Meituan 9.19%, Xiaomi 8.43%, Tencent 7.95%, NetEase 7.42%, Alibaba 7.40%, SMIC 6.51%, JD 5.51%, Kuaishou 4.17%, and Baidu 4.00%. Under tighter FCI, I prefer the cash-return internet leaders and telecom/oil defensives over the full HSTECH basket.

## Priority Reduce List

1. **Profitless U.S. software and concept AI:** valuation depends on falling real yields and 2027-28 TAM narratives; beta to term premium is high.
2. **Semicap cyclicals priced for linear AI capex:** keep only names with backlog, gross-margin resilience, and customer concentration under control.
3. **Broad Russell 2000 beta:** the index contains the recovery upside, but also too much refinancing and profitless exposure for this phase.
4. **China ADR-only liquidity beta:** especially names without HK primary liquidity, without sustained buybacks, or with heavy U.S. long-only ownership.
5. **Subsidy / price-war China consumer internet and EVs:** Meituan, JD, PDD, NIO, XPeng-style exposures are vulnerable if higher discount rates meet margin competition.

## Priority Add List

1. **U.S. mega-cap cash compounders:** hold or add on 5-8% pullbacks where FCF yield is defensible and buybacks reduce duration risk.
2. **Quality small caps instead of broad IWM:** profitable S&P 600-style exposures, industrial automation, reshoring beneficiaries, energy services, and niche health-care tools.
3. **HK-listed China platforms with cash return:** Tencent 0700, NetEase 9999, and Alibaba 9988/BABA only where AI/cloud capex stays FCF-funded.
4. **China defensives available to Southbound / HK investors:** China Mobile 0941, CNOOC 0883, high-dividend utilities, and exchange / infrastructure-like cash-flow names.
5. **Tourism and services with external cash flow:** Trip.com 9961/TCOM is better placed than subsidy-heavy local commerce if RMB and domestic consumption remain uneven.

## Risk Triggers

- **Escalate de-risking** if U.S. 10y closes above 4.60%, HY OAS widens above 3.25%, or NFCI rises by more than 0.30 in two weekly prints.
- **Add back growth faster** if U.S. 10y falls below 4.15% while HY OAS stays below 3.00% and USD/JPY stabilises above 145 without fresh intervention pressure.
- **Switch from HK defensives to HK tech beta** only if Southbound turnover expands while HSTECH breadth improves beyond Tencent/Alibaba/NetEase.

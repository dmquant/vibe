# Card 03 Research Report - 2026-05-11

As of the institute work-date **2026-05-11** (Asia/Singapore), I synthesize rather than reverse the prior two cards: the China ADR/HK internet rally is still better described as a **halftime pause** than as a full "bad news exhausted" regime. One important boundary matters for execution: the final Trump-Xi summit outcome has not yet been observed in the verifiable news tape I can cite. China-US trade talks led by He Lifeng and Scott Bessent are scheduled for **2026-05-12 to 2026-05-13** in South Korea, with Bessent then due to travel to Beijing for the Trump-Xi summit; therefore the current trade is still a pre-event / event-bridge position, not a clean post-result position.[^meeting]

## Executive Judgment

**Decision: roll the small hedge, do not take full profit and do not upgrade yet.** The correct action is to keep the Card 02 "small cup" hedge at roughly **25%-35% of full hedge budget**, roll near-expiry protection into **1-3 month KWEB/HSI/HSTECH put spreads**, and monetize only the most expensive front-week upside calls where the book is overexposed.

The reason is simple: spot has rallied enough to create profit-taking risk, but the evidence does not show a completed capitulation of shorts or a durable southbound chase. KWEB closed only **+0.10%** on **2026-05-08** after the prior **+4.31%** jump on **2026-05-06**; BABA ADR closed **-0.67%** on **2026-05-08** after **+6.94%** on **2026-05-06**; BABA-W (9988.HK) closed **HK$139.00**, **-1.35%** on **2026-05-08**, after **+4.99%** on **2026-05-07**.[^kweb][^baba_us][^baba_hk] Hong Kong indices also faded: AASTOCKS reported HSI down **232 pts / 0.9%** to **26,393**, HSTECH down **18 pts / 0.4%** to **5,102**, and turnover at **HK$279.75B** on the latest verifiable full-day tape.[^hk_close]

## Execution Path

The post-rally tape argues against taking full hedge profit. The hedge has not become dead weight because the key meeting result is still ahead, BABA earnings/event IV is still live, and southbound ETF behavior is already showing profit-taking. But the same tape also argues against upgrading to a "large cup": KWEB did not break down, BABA still sits well above late-April levels, and southbound did buy BABA-W directly by **HK$2.0B** on the cited day.[^southbound]

**Base case - roll:** keep the existing hedge notional at **25%-35%** of the full budget. Convert any front-week puts that benefited from event vol into **1-3 month put spreads** on KWEB and HSI/HSTECH. Use strikes roughly **5%-8% out-of-the-money** for the long put and **12%-15% out-of-the-money** for the short put, so the book has protection against a sell-the-news gap without paying for a crash-only tail.

**Take partial profit only when:** KWEB holds above **$30.00** for two consecutive US closes, HSTECH reclaims and holds the **5,200** area, and BABA ADR/HK moves from discount to a **+1% to +2%** ADR premium without renewed short-volume pressure. That would imply the market is absorbing the event path.

**Upgrade only when:** the Seoul/Beijing talks deliver a concrete tariff or export-control extension and spot still sells off, or the opposite failure mode appears: HSTECH breaks below **5,000**, BABA-W loses **HK$135**, and southbound turns from stock-specific buying into broad ETF outflow for a second session. In that case raise hedge budget from **25%-35%** to **50%-60%**, not straight to a full "large cup."

**Close only when:** the summit produces a clean, named deliverable set and the price action confirms it: KWEB closes above **$30.50**, BABA-W holds above **HK$142**, HSTECH holds above **5,250**, and southbound ETF flow turns back positive rather than rotating only into single names.

## Bottom Line

I support Card 01's "halftime pause" and Card 02's "small cup" sizing, with one refinement: because the decisive meeting result has not landed by **2026-05-11**, the hedge should be **rolled**, not simply harvested. The portfolio should stay long the 6-12 month China internet repair story, but keep low-cost convex protection through the next event window.

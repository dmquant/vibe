# Card 02 Sentiment Stress Test - 2026-05-11

As of the institute work-date **2026-05-11** (Asia/Singapore), the latest fully observable market close is **2026-05-08** for US/HK-listed China risk. I stress-test Card 01 rather than overturn it: the soft sentiment tape is already excited, but the hard-positioning evidence is mixed, so the tactical hedge should be **small cup**, not **large cup**.

## Judgment

The gap is real but not yet a full hard-positioning extreme. KWEB short interest is high, active foreign money has not chased, and Southbound five-day net buying is positive but modest versus recent peaks. The strongest crowded-long evidence is in retail/social tone and short-dated call demand, especially BABA event-week calls. That combination argues for protection against a meeting disappointment, but not for sizing the hedge as if the market is already institutionally max-long.

**Order size:** use a **small-cup tactical hedge**: 25%-35% of the intended full hedge budget, preferably via 1-3m KWEB/HSI put spreads or collars funded by trimming upside calls. Move to large cup only if Southbound five-day net buying exceeds roughly **HK$25B-HK$30B**, EPFR active China equity flows turn materially positive for at least two consecutive weeks, and KWEB/BABA call skew remains positive after event-week IV normalizes.

## Stress-Test Read

The bearish counterargument to Card 01 is that high KWEB short interest plus improving Southbound flow could create a squeeze if the meeting delivers more than expected. I accept the first half: the short base is large enough that a positive surprise can hurt under-hedged shorts. I do not accept the second half as a reason to abandon hedges, because Southbound net buying is not extreme and active foreign money has not re-risked.

The bullish counterargument is also incomplete. BABA near-term calls are crowded and Chinese social tone is cheerful, but KWEB one-month skew is not wildly call-rich, and KWEB short interest has not been washed out. This is a classic “soft euphoria, hard hesitation” setup: disappointment can trigger a fast air pocket, but positive delivery can still squeeze shorts.

## Hedge Sizing

Use **small cup**. A large-cup hedge would be justified only if hard positioning had already migrated into crowded longs. It has not. The better expression is convex and budget-limited: buy downside put spreads into the event, avoid outright high-premium puts where IV is inflated, and finance part of the cost by taking profit on short-dated upside calls or by rolling long delta into call spreads.

Practical sizing rule for the PM book:

| Trigger | Hedge size |
|---|---:|
| Current state: social bullish, BABA event calls hot, KWEB shorts still high, Southbound five-day **+HK$8.05B**, active flows still not chasing | **Small cup: 25%-35%** of full hedge budget |
| Upgrade condition: Southbound five-day **>HK$25B-HK$30B**, active China equity flows positive for two weeks, KWEB/BABA 1m call skew positive after the event | **Large cup: 60%-75%** |
| Downgrade condition: KWEB short interest falls below ~10%-12% of shares while flows remain negative, or post-event implied vol collapses without spot weakness | **Keep only residual tail hedge** |

## Bottom Line

I support Card 01's “halftime break, not all-clear” thesis, but the hedge should be disciplined. The market has enough soft euphoria to justify protection, yet not enough hard long crowding to justify a large hedge. **Small cup is the right size as of 2026-05-11.**

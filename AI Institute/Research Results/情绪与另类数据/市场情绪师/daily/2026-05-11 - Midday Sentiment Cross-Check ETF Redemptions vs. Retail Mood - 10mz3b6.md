# Midday Sentiment Cross-Check: ETF Redemptions vs. Retail Mood

**Date anchor:** local `date +%Y-%m-%d` returned `2026-05-11`; every reference to “today,” “previous trading day,” and “this week” is resolved against that date. The previous trading day is `2026-05-08`.  
**Bottom line:** this morning is not a simple “redemption-driven selloff.” It is a chip exchange: passive broad-index ETFs are being redeemed while theme-driven and retail momentum demand is absorbing supply. The current chip-exchange ratio is **0.11x / 11.2%**, meaning the previous trading day’s equity index ETF net redemption equals only about one tenth of today morning’s incremental ETF turnover. Near-term absorption is still strong, but the 5-trading-day redemption inventory equals **27.5%** of today’s half-day ETF turnover, so volatility should stay elevated later this week.

## Prioritized Conclusions

1. **This morning’s absorption is stronger than redemption pressure.** National Business Daily reported that on 2026-05-11 morning, Shanghai and Shenzhen half-day turnover reached **RMB2.31 trillion**, up **RMB339.4 billion** from the previous trading day; ETF half-day turnover reached **RMB439.76 billion**, up **RMB197.72 billion**, with more than **1,100 ETFs rising**. This indicates secondary-market risk appetite is strong enough to absorb the prior trading day’s redemption pressure. Source: [National Business Daily](https://www.nbd.com.cn/articles/2026-05-11/4386427.html).

2. **Redemptions are concentrated in broad-index/main-board proxies, while STAR Market flow is rotating from broad beta into chip themes.** Stockstar ETF data show that on 2026-05-08, equity index ETFs recorded total net outflow of **RMB22.175 billion**, with cumulative 5-trading-day net outflow of **RMB120.914 billion**; however, STAR Chip ETF Harvest (588200) received **RMB544 million** of net inflow on the same day. Source: [Stockstar ETF](https://wap.stockstar.com/detail/RB2026050900005148). Sina Fund reported that on 2026-05-07, ChinaAMC STAR 50 ETF (588000) saw **RMB726 million** of net redemption, with **RMB8.36 billion** over 5 days and **RMB18.779 billion** over 20 days. Source: [Sina Fund](https://finance.sina.com.cn/money/fund/aiassistant/etfshzj/2026-05-08/doc-inhxcyyp4517890.shtml).

3. **Main-board broad ETFs are the larger source of supply.** Sina Fund disclosed that on 2026-05-07, Huatai-PB CSI 300 ETF (510300) was redeemed by **RMB8.705 billion**, Southern CSI 500 ETF (510500) by **RMB6.559 billion**, and ChinaAMC SSE 50 ETF (510050) by **RMB2.971 billion**, for a combined **RMB18.235 billion**. Source: [Sina Fund](https://finance.sina.com.cn/money/fund/aiassistant/etfshzj/2026-05-08/doc-inhxcusr4571375.shtml). On 2026-05-06, ChinaAMC STAR 50 ETF (588000) saw **RMB2.954 billion** of net redemption, while ChinaAMC SSE 50 ETF (510050) saw **RMB2.816 billion**. Source: [Sina Fund](https://finance.sina.com.cn/money/fund/aiassistant/etfshzj/2026-05-07/doc-inhwzrtp2841958.shtml?froms=ggmp).

4. **Retail sentiment is greed-leaning but divided, not one-way euphoric.** The Eastmoney forum sample for today shows one post promoting three core ETFs with **235,000** views, indicating high attention; the same page also contains cautious or loss-framed expressions such as “the STAR Market is being abandoned” and “another bowl of noodles today.” Source: [Eastmoney Guba](https://mguba.eastmoney.com/mguba/list/sh588000). Combined with today’s main-force net inflows of **RMB11.43 billion, RMB9.865 billion, and RMB6.646 billion** into memory chips, semiconductors, and advanced packaging respectively, I score current retail/theme sentiment at **73/100**: high risk appetite, but already in a zone where chasing and profit-taking coexist.

**Interpretation:** the core 0.11x ratio says today morning’s incremental turnover can absorb the previous trading day’s redemption. But the 27.5% 5-day inventory ratio means that if turnover cools, prior redemptions can quickly re-emerge as selling pressure. STAR Market flow is not fully exiting; it is rotating from STAR 50 broad beta into chips, semiconductors, and AI hard-tech themes.

The public-sentiment score is **58/100**. Market-based gauges are greed-leaning, while household economic sentiment remains weak.

| Indicator | Latest reading | Sentiment signal |
|---|---:|---|

| AAII neutral share | **28.7%** | A meaningful share of investors is still waiting for clearer direction. |
| AAII bearish share | **33.3%** | Still above the historical average of 31.0%, so fear has not fully cleared. |
| AAII bull-bear spread | **+5.4 percentage points** | Positive, but still below the historical average of 6.5 percentage points. |

Scoring logic: Fear & Greed and AAII both support a bullish tilt; however, consumer sentiment remains depressed, showing a gap between “asset-market optimism” and “real-economy mood.” The public-sentiment component is therefore only **slightly above neutral**.

## Sentiment Structure And Market Implications

1. **The dominant driver is the technical breakout.** Record highs make it easier for momentum and systematic capital to keep risk appetite elevated.
2. **Flows are not euphoric across the board.** ETF inflows are strong, but global equity inflows have slowed and money-market assets have risen, showing investors are still keeping defensive cash.
3. **The public-sentiment split is the main vulnerability.** Traders are embracing risk while consumers remain deeply pessimistic; weaker inflation, energy, or labor data could pull the market back from greed toward neutral.
4. **The current index does not support extreme chasing.** A 68/100 reading is bullish, but it favors disciplined trend-following and adding on pullbacks rather than indiscriminate leverage.

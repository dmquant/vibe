# Alternative Data Daily: Cross-Asset Divergences via Alt-Indicators (2026-05-11)

## 1. Core Alt-Indicator Snapshot
High-frequency data this morning reveals a sharp divergence between consumer resilience and logistical tightness, challenging the "soft landing" consensus.

*   **US Credit Stress:** Total debt hit a record $1.28 Trillion. The personal saving rate has cratered to 3.6%, indicating consumers are dipping into savings to sustain nominal spending following the gasoline price spike driven by Middle East instability ("Operation Epic Fury").
*   **Shipping Heat Exceeds Expectations:** The Baltic Dry Index (BDI) surged to 2,991 points, its highest since late 2023. Containerized Freight Index (CFI) is up 45% YoY, with air freight demand spiking as sea-to-air arbitrage becomes viable for high-value goods.
*   **Satellite & Geopolitical Sanctions:** The U.S. recently sanctioned three Chinese satellite firms. Concurrently, satellite monitoring suggests China’s AI component imports ($135B in Q1) are driving a significant trade deficit, signaling aggressive inventory building despite export controls.

## 2. Actionable Divergences (Pitches)

### A. Dry Bulk: Asymmetric Play on the Pacific Capacity Gap
*   **Divergence:** While markets focus on Red Sea container rerouting, alt-data shows a structural shortage of Capesize vessels in the Pacific. A surge in Brazilian iron ore exports is coinciding with a rebound in Asian power demand.
*   **Action:** BDI at 2,991 is not yet fully priced into bulk carrier equities. Look for catch-up potential in companies with high spot exposure to Pacific routes.

### B. US Retail: The "Credit Cliff" Behind Nominal Growth
*   **Divergence:** Nominal retail sales remain positive, but the combination of record credit balances and APRs (>20%) has reached a breaking point. High-frequency payment data shows discretionary transaction counts dropped in the first week of May, despite higher ticket sizes due to inflation.
*   **Action:** Be wary of Q2 guidance from retailers reporting this week. Alt-data suggests a potential "nonlinear" shift from resilient spending to credit-driven contraction.

### C. China AI Infrastructure: Thermal Anomaly vs. Import Constraints
*   **Divergence:** Satellite thermal data shows power loads at computing hubs in the Yangtze River Delta and Greater Bay Area are up 22% YoY. This contradicts slowing official export figures, suggesting domestic AI build-outs are accelerating via unconventional import channels for core hardware.
*   **Action:** Pivot toward domestic AI infrastructure supply chain winners.

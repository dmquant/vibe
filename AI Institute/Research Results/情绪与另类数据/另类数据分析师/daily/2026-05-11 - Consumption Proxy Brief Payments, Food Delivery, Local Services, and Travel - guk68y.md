# Consumption Proxy Brief: Payments, Food Delivery, Local Services, and Travel

**Date anchor: 2026-05-11.** All references to "recent," "May Day," and "Q1" are anchored to the shell date `2026-05-11`. The report focuses on China consumption. Because timely public "credit card spending" data are limited in China, UnionPay/NetsUnion payments, bank-card consumption, and U.S. credit/debit card data are used as cross-checks.

## Priority Conclusions

1. **Consumption breadth is stronger than ticket size.** During the 2026 May Day holiday, UnionPay and NetsUnion processed **28.937 billion** payment transactions worth **RMB7.85 trillion**, up **23.45%** and **2.74%** year over year. The implied average transaction was about **RMB271.3**, down **16.8%** from an implied **RMB326.0** a year earlier. This is not a demand collapse; it points to more small-ticket, high-frequency transactions, deeper mobile-payment penetration, and more price-sensitive spending.[S1]
2. **Services and travel remain the most resilient consumption chain.** May Day domestic trips reached **325 million**, and domestic tourism spending reached **RMB185.492 billion**, up **3.6%** and **2.9%** year over year. Implied tourism spending per trip was about **RMB570.7**, down **0.7%** year over year. Volumes are expanding, but travelers are spending carefully.[S2]
3. **Travel modes are diverging: road and rail are strong, aviation is weak.** Cross-regional passenger movement reached **1.517128 billion** during the holiday, up **3.49%** year over year. Road travel accounted for **91.7%** of the total and rose **3.51%**; rail rose **4.6%**; air passenger traffic fell **5.74%**. The mix points to high-frequency, near-to-mid-distance, self-drive and high-speed-rail travel rather than broad premiumization led by expensive air tickets.[S3]
4. **Food delivery and instant retail retain traffic resilience, but margin quality needs discount-rate verification.** Meituan's 2025 local-services consumption orders grew **36%** year over year, annual transacting users exceeded **800 million**, and post-1995 consumers accounted for nearly **60%**. In Q1, national online retail sales of goods and services reached **RMB4.9774 trillion**, up **8.0%**, faster than total retail sales at **2.4%**. However, platform subsidies, low-ticket group buying, and holiday group deals can inflate order counts without implying profit expansion.[S4][S5]
5. **The incremental local-services growth is skewed toward smaller cities, sports events, concerts, night-time demand, and interest-driven consumption.** Meituan's May Day data show that trips over 300 km accounted for **46%** of travel orders; leisure orders in mid- and lower-tier cities rose nearly **70%** from the pre-holiday period, with non-local users up **205%**; music-festival group-buy orders rose **75%**, and sports events lifted Huaiyang cuisine orders by **40%** year over year.[S6][S7]

## Segment Analysis

### 1. Credit Card and Payment Consumption

The most timely public China data are not narrow "credit card spend" figures, but UnionPay, NetsUnion, and non-bank payment-network data. The core May Day message is that transaction count grew rapidly while payment value grew modestly. The implied average transaction fell from about **RMB326.0** a year earlier to **RMB271.3**, meaning consumption activity is broad but each transaction is smaller.

Inbound payments are a bright spot: payment transactions by visitors to China rose **45.15%** year over year, while payment value rose **36.96%**. This aligns with the Ministry of Culture and Tourism's comments on improved inbound-travel facilitation, although the implied inbound ticket fell by about **5.6%**, still consistent with the broader "strong volume, soft ticket" theme.[S1][S2]

Bank of America U.S. credit/debit card data offer a useful interpretation framework: in March 2026, card spending per household rose **4.3%** year over year, or **3.6%** excluding gasoline, but gasoline prices and higher-income spending lifted nominal growth. The same caution applies to China: nominal payment value should not be treated as proof of real consumption upgrading.[S8]

### 2. Food Delivery, Instant Retail, and Local Services

The directional signal for food delivery and instant retail remains positive. Meituan's Q1 2026 food-delivery market report shows **800 million+** annual transacting users, **36%** year-over-year growth in 2025 local-services consumption orders, nearly **60%** post-1995 consumer share, and **7 million+** riders. This indicates that high-frequency local-services demand is still expanding.[S4]

But "strong orders" must be separated from "strong profits." National catering revenue grew **4.2%** year over year in Q1, ahead of total retail sales at **2.4%**. Online retail sales of goods and services rose **8.0%**, and online retail sales of services rose **8.8%**. The macro data support the view that services and online high-frequency consumption are stronger, but platform orders can be distorted by subsidies, coupons, low-price group buys, and small-ticket products.[S5]

May Day local data also show that delivery and dine-in food demand are tied to event and destination traffic rather than growing in isolation: sports events lifted Huaiyang cuisine orders by **40%** year over year, and music-festival group-buy orders rose **75%**. The durability of these orders depends on city event supply and repeat behavior, not only one-off holiday traffic.[S6]

### 3. Travel and Tourism Consumption

Travel gives the cleanest consumption signal: trip volumes remain near record levels, but payment and tourism spend per person have not risen in tandem. May Day cross-regional passenger movement reached **1.517128 billion**, and domestic tourist trips reached **325 million**, both positive year over year; implied domestic tourism spending per trip was about **RMB570.7**, slightly lower year over year.[S2][S3]

By mode, road accounted for **91.7%** of cross-regional movement, rail **7.0%**, and air **0.7%**. Air passenger traffic fell **5.74%** year over year, while rail rose **4.6%**, showing consumer preference for controllable costs, flexible itineraries, and near-to-mid-distance destinations.[S3]

DiDi data reinforce this read: non-local ride-hailing orders were **33%** higher than normal days; concert- and sports-event-area orders were **88%** and **185%** higher than normal days; inbound users' ride-hailing orders rose **67%** year over year. DiDi Charging reported May Day charging volume growth of over **40%** year over year, with private-car non-local charging orders up **44%**. Self-drive and intra-city mobility are key carriers of this round of travel consumption.[S9][S10]

## Investment and Operating Implications

| Direction | View | Beneficiary chain | Main risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Local-services platforms | Volume is strong, but profit may not move in parallel | Food delivery, instant retail, dine-in restaurants, hotel/travel traffic acquisition | Subsidy rate, rider costs, low-ticket mix |
| Hotels and scenic areas | Smaller cities and interest-driven destinations are stronger | Small-city homestays, specialty restaurants, concert and sports-event destinations | Post-holiday fade, weak ticket size |
| Transportation | Rail and self-drive are stronger than aviation | Rail-route consumption, charging, car rental, ride-hailing | Fuel prices, weather, holiday timing |
| Consumer brands | High-frequency staples beat discretionary big-ticket items | Food and beverage, convenience retail, online services | Pressure on appliances, autos, furniture |

## Follow-Up Items

1. **Payment-category split:** If daily UnionPay/NetsUnion merchant-category data become available, split restaurants, lodging, transport, scenic areas, and retail to determine whether ticket-size pressure comes from price, category mix, or channel mix.
2. **True food-delivery unit economics:** Track Q2 2026 order volume, GTV, subsidy rate, fulfillment cost per order, and merchant net receipts across Meituan, Taobao Shangou, and JD Daojia to avoid being misled by order counts.
3. **Post-holiday retention:** Monitor whether small-city hotel, ride-hailing, and restaurant orders from 2026-05-06 to 2026-05-31 stay above the 2025 like-for-like baseline. A rapid fade would mean May Day was a pulse, not a trend lift.

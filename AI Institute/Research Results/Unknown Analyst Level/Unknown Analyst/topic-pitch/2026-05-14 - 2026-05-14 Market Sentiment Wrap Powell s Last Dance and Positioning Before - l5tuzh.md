# 2026-05-14 Market Sentiment Wrap: Powell's "Last Dance" and Positioning Before the Warsh Transition

## 1. Sentiment Pulse: The "Greed" Plateau
The **CNN Fear & Greed Index closed at 65.5 (Greed)** today, a slight cooling from yesterday's 65.9. Despite consecutive "super-hot" inflation prints this week (PPI at 6.0% y/y), market sentiment has not pivoted to "Fear." Instead, it remains anchored by AI-driven earnings optimism. AI leaders, spearheaded by Cisco, have effectively hedged the "higher-for-longer" narrative, leaving the market in a "climbing a wall of worry" regime.

## 2. Flows & Breadth Divergence
- **Index Divergence:** The Nasdaq gained +1.20% while the Dow slipped -0.14%. This stark outperformance of Growth over Value/Cyclicals suggests institutional rotation away from rate-sensitive blue chips into AI core assets with high earnings visibility.
- **MOC (Market-On-Close) Imbalance:** MOC activity was moderate, with buying concentrated in **Cisco (CSCO)** and **Applied Materials (AMAT)** ahead of its earnings release. Under the NYSE's late-2024 "Significant Imbalance" methodology, fewer symbols triggered flags today, but slippage remains compressed, indicating extreme liquidity concentration in mega-caps.
- **Breadth Warning:** The Advance/Decline ratio was a lukewarm **0.95**, significantly lagging the headline index gains. This "narrow leadership" is a classic defensive-bullish signal that often precedes volatility.

## 3. Key Conclusion: The "Transition Void"
Tomorrow, May 15, marks the final day of Jerome Powell's term. The market's primary anxiety is shifting from inflation to the "Warsh Era" unpredictability.
- **Positioning Risk:** The 10-year Treasury yield hit **4.49%** (a high since last July), yet equities remain near records. This cross-asset divergence suggests that if the Beijing Summit communique underwhelms, or if incoming Chair Kevin Warsh strikes an unexpectedly hawkish tone, there is little downside protection in current positioning.

## 4. Tomorrow's Setup: Three Debate Pillars
1.  **Trump-Xi Summit Communique:** Markets have partially priced in an extension of the "October 2025 Trade Truce." Any signs of friction could trigger a sharp reversal.
2.  **Fed Handover:** Whether Powell's departure includes a policy "parting gift" or if Warsh's confirmation creates a temporary power vacuum.
3.  **AMAT Post-Market Guidance:** Whether semiconductor equipment orders can justify current valuation multiples amid rising rates.

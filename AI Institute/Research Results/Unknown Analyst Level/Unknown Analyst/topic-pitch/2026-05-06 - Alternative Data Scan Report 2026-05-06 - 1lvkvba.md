# Alternative Data Scan Report (2026-05-06)

## Executive Summary
Today's scan focuses on significant divergences between alternative data signals and market consensus. Key findings indicate a structural bifurcation in US manufacturing, stronger-than-reported high-tech industrial activity in China, and a critical "tipping point" in US consumer credit health.

---

## 1. US Manufacturing PMI Divergence: Multinationals vs. Domestic Players
- **Key Divergence**: May ISM Manufacturing PMI fell to **48.7** (contraction), while S&P Global Manufacturing PMI rose to **51.3** (expansion).
- **Alt-Data Validation**:
    - **Logistics Data**: Satellite tracking shows a 4% MoM decline in outbound container throughput at major international ports (LA, Long Beach), reflecting weakness in large-cap multinationals (heavily weighted in ISM) facing sluggish overseas demand and prolonged destocking.
    - **Transaction Data**: Alternative credit card transaction records show that B2B procurement for domestic-focused sectors (e.g., construction materials, local maintenance) remains robust among the mid-to-small enterprises covered by S&P Global.
- **Investment Insight**: Market consensus on the Industrials sector may be overly pessimistic or too generalized. Recommend over-weighting domestic-revenue industrial stocks and under-weighting large-cap multinationals dragged down by global macro headwinds.

## 2. China’s High-Tech "Satellite Bottom": Physical Activity vs. Official PMI
- **Key Divergence**: Official Manufacturing PMI dropped to **49.5**, yet satellite monitoring and emission data suggest a different trajectory.
- **Alt-Data Validation**:
    - **Satellite SMI**: SpaceKnow's Satellite Manufacturing Index indicates that physical activity in high-tech hubs like Shenzhen and Hefei grew **3.2% MoM**.
    - **Emission Monitoring**: NO2 concentration levels (a proxy for factory activity) over industrial parks show that production lines for semiconductors and NEVs have been ramping up for three consecutive weeks.
- **Investment Insight**: The manufacturing recovery is "dual-track." Traditional heavy industries (steel, cement) are weighing down the official PMI, masking Alpha opportunities in the tech-growth space. Bottom support for A-share tech (especially Semi and NEVs) is significantly stronger than the headline indices suggest.

## 3. US Consumer Credit "Cliff": Balance Contraction Meets Rising Delinquency
- **Key Divergence**: Official retail sales remain superficially stable, but underlying credit health is deteriorating rapidly.
- **Alt-Data Validation**:
    - **Credit Balances**: Alternative financial data shows the **first MoM contraction in US bankcard balances in 24 months**, indicating that high borrowing costs are finally forcing a pullback in discretionary spending.
    - **App Downloads**: Installs of debt restructuring and credit monitoring Fintech apps surged **95%** in April/May, reflecting mounting pressure on lower-to-middle income cohorts.
    - **Delinquency Trends**: Alternative collection data tracking shows credit card delinquency rates for low-income segments have breached 2019 levels.
- **Investment Insight**: Consumer Discretionary (Retail/Leisure) faces significant downside risk. Sell-side expectations for Q3 consumption resilience may be too optimistic; watch for the "credit cliff" impact on earnings.

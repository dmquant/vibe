# Alt-Indicator Morning Update: Cross-Asset Divergences & Consumer Resilience (2026-05-14)

## 1. Core Findings & Divergences
High-frequency data today reveals a stark **"Services Strong, Goods Weak"** divergence globally. U.S. credit card spending saw a seasonally adjusted month-over-month (MoM) dip of -0.7% in early May, yet services (dining and travel) remain robust. Concurrently, post-May Day high-frequency payment data from China shows service-related spending (+8%) significantly exceeding expectations, while durable goods remain sluggish. This divergence suggests market expectations for retail leaders may be overly pessimistic, while the debate over enterprise capex (focused on Cisco's upcoming print) enters a critical phase.

## 2. Alt-Indicator Deep Dive
### Credit Card Spending & Payment Volume
*   **U.S. (Chase/BofA Data):** Cumulative May spending is up 2.1% YoY, but MoM momentum is flagging. Gen Z remains the outlier, maintaining high growth (+5.9%), primarily in small-ticket social consumption.
*   **China (UnionPay/Alipay):** Transaction volume during the May Day holiday reached 7.85 trillion yuan (+2.7% YoY). Inbound traveler payments have doubled, signaling a recovery in cross-border mobility.

### Freight & Supply Chain
*   **Drewry WCI:** Printed at $2,286/40ft, up 3% WoW. Unlike the extreme volatility of 2024/2025, rates are now in a moderate uptrend, reflecting normalized restocking rather than panic hoarding.

## 3. Sector Impact & Earnings Cross-Ref
*   **Cisco (CSCO):** Monitoring pre-market. Alt-data shows a slowdown in North American enterprise web traffic; if CSCO's guidance disappoints, it may confirm a contraction cycle in enterprise IT spending.
*   **Sony (SONY):** Daily Active Users (DAU) for gaming remain at record highs, offsetting hardware sales headwinds. High-frequency app download data suggests their mobile gaming strategy is beginning to bear fruit.
*   **Retail Sector:** Spending structures indicate "trading down" behavior. Traffic to discount platforms (Temu/Shein) is up 12% MoM.

## 4. Actionable Theses
*   **Long Services/Travel:** Given the strong recovery in cross-border payment data, we remain bullish on global travel and Online Travel Agencies (OTAs).
*   **Hedge Discretionary Risk:** With U.S. durable goods spending declining, consider using options to hedge earnings volatility in high-premium retail stocks.
*   **Watch for Shipping Catch-up:** Moderate rate increases and low empty-container ratios favor regional shipping leaders.

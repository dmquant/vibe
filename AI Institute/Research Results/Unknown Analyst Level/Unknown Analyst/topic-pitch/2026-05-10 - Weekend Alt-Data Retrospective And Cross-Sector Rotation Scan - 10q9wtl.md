## 3. Three Monday Pitches

### Pitch 1: Consumer Rotation Is About Use Case, Not Aggregate Demand

**Conclusion**: Consumers are still spending, but they are favoring necessary repairs, short mission-driven shopping trips, and weekday fast casual. This is not broad sector beta; it is “provable value” alpha.

**Evidence chain**: Redbook at `+7.8% YoY` shows nominal same-store sales remain firm. Placer.ai shows Home Depot/Lowe's Q1 traffic turned positive, open-air centers at `+3.5%` beating outlets at `+0.5%`, and fast casual at `+3.1%` beating full-service dining at `-1.4%`. The common signal: traffic is returning offline, but dwell times are shorter, trips are more purposeful, and weekend discretionary elasticity is weaker.

**Threshold breach**: Home-improvement traffic turned from multi-quarter declines to `+1.9% / +2.0%`; fast-casual weekday traffic reached `+4.7%`; full-service weekend traffic fell `-1.9%`.

**Monday thesis**: Favor consumer exposure tied to repairs, convenience, weekday routines, and open-air centers. Maintain valuation discipline on full-service dining, destination outlets, and long-dwell discretionary consumption.

### Pitch 2: AI App Demand Has Not Broken, But Concentration Is Falling

**Conclusion**: AI apps are the week’s highest-signal technology/consumer intersection. Download rankings are still strong, but DAU and share data show the market moving from one dominant entry point to multiple entry points, models, and product forms.

**Evidence chain**: ChatGPT had about `53 million` global downloads and `$211 million` of net revenue in `2026-04`, so it remains the leader. But GenAI chatbot DAUs fell `MoM -1.5%`, ChatGPT app share moved `45.3% -> 38.1%`, and Claude moved `1.5% -> 13.1%`. Web traffic is similarly split: Gemini `+9% MoM`, Claude `+33% MoM`, ChatGPT `-1% MoM`.

**Threshold breach**: Claude app usage share moved into double digits; high-frequency users rose from `6.8%` to `8.5%`, showing heavier users are stickier even as entry points fragment.

**Monday thesis**: AI capex demand should not be modeled off the ChatGPT curve alone; cloud, search, browsers, enterprise software, and ad distribution all need a fresh read on marginal winners from share migration.

### Pitch 3: Supply Chain And Energy Signals Are Starting To Pressure Q2 Margins

**Conclusion**: The Drewry WCI rebound, Transpacific surcharges, wider refined-product deficits, and China’s export recovery together form an early “cost re-inflation” signal.

**Evidence chain**: WCI rose `+3%` to `$2,286/40ft`, with Shanghai-New York `+7%` and Shanghai-Los Angeles `+5%`. At the same time, the U.S. gasoline deficit versus the five-year average widened to `-8.404 million bbls`, and the combined crude/gasoline/distillate deficit widened from `-12.748` to `-15.461 million bbls`. China `2026-04` exports at `+14.1%` show goods-flow intensity is not weak, so freight increases are not simply empty pricing.

**Threshold breach**: WCI ended a three-week slide; two core Transpacific lanes rose `5-7%` in a single week; refined-product deficits widened.

**Monday thesis**: The second-quarter debate should shift from “is demand enough?” to “who can pass through freight and fuel costs?” Retail, transport, energy, airlines, and chemicals should be in the same discussion.

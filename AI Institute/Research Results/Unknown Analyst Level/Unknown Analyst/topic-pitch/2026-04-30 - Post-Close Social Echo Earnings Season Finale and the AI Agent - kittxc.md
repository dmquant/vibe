# Post-Close Social Echo: Earnings Season Finale and the "AI Agent" Narrative Split
**Date:** Thursday, April 30, 2026
**Analyst:** Social Media Analyst

## 1. Sentiment Overview
Post-close social sentiment today exhibits a sharp "binary" split:
- **A-Share (Defense & De-risking):** As the deadline for annual and Q1 report disclosures nears its final hours, Xueqiu and Eastmoney Guba are dominated by intense "landmine avoidance" (避雷) sentiment. Retail investors are in a state of high anxiety regarding stocks with earnings misses or those facing "ST" (Special Treatment/Delisting Risk) labels. Conversely, high-dividend sectors (Banks, Power) show a form of "defensive optimism."
- **US Market (AI Reality Check):** Discourse on Weibo Finance and Xueqiu's US stock channels has shifted from "Compute Mania" to "AI Monetization." Despite strong earnings from some giants, concerns over excessive Capital Expenditure (CapEx) causing an "AI Bubble Leak" are trending. Overall sentiment is "defensively bullish," with extreme sensitivity to even minor guidance adjustments.

## 2. Narrative Reversals
- **From "AI FOMO" to "AI Reality Check":** While the morning session was buoyed by expectations of an AI rebound, viral rumors on Weibo regarding OpenAI’s revenue targets and internal funding warnings (CFO Sarah Friar) have soured the mood. The narrative has pivoted: investors are now questioning the long-term "burn rate" of LLM companies.
- **The "CPU Renaissance":** A potent new narrative has emerged in post-close discussions—"The Agentic AI era requires more CPU compute, not just GPUs." This logic is driving a second wave of debate around Intel (INTC) and AMD, suggesting the market previously undervalued general-purpose compute in the age of autonomous agents.
- **A-Share Flight to Quality:** Retail narratives have shifted from morning "thematic speculation" to evening "hard-core profits." Following a wave of late-night "ST" announcements, comments sections are filled with disillusionment regarding the "micro-cap recovery" thesis.

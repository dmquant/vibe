# Weekend Deep Thematic Scan Report (May 2, 2026)

## 1. Structural Shift: Paradigm Shift from Pre-training to Inference Scaling Laws (AI)

### Observation
As of early 2026, the competitive focus in AI has decisively shifted from the "large model race" of pre-training to **Inference-time Scaling Laws**. With the maturity of OpenAI o3 and DeepSeek V4, the amount of "thought-time compute" consumed by models during complex reasoning has become the primary determinant of intelligence levels.

### Deep Analysis
*   **Restructuring Computing Economics:** Over the past five years, compute demand was concentrated in the training phase (H100/B200 clusters). Now, with the mass deployment of Agentic AI across enterprises, inference compute demand is growing exponentially. The Inference-as-a-Service (IaaS) market is projected to hit $23B in 2026.
*   **Rise of Sovereign Intelligence:** Governments have begun deploying dedicated inference clusters to ensure data security and localized logic processing, rather than merely chasing model parameter size.

### Catalyst Stories
*   **DeepSeek V4's Efficiency Miracle:** DeepSeek has further lowered the marginal cost of high-quality inference through extreme sparsity architectures.
*   **Agentic DevOps:** Next week, three major SaaS vendors will release development tools integrated with "reasoning-enhanced" Agents, potentially triggering a re-rating of legacy software valuations.

## 2. Structural Shift: The "Starship Dividend" & LEO Logistics (Space Economy)

### Observation
SpaceX Starship launch frequency in H1 2026 is approaching two launches per month. With the first successful validation of orbital refueling, the cost curve for deep space logistics and ultra-large constellation deployment has reached a critical tipping point.

### Deep Analysis
*   **Sub-$100 Launch Costs:** The goal of reducing launch costs to under $100/kg is becoming a reality. This has directly accelerated the transport of components for commercial space stations (e.g., Orbital Reef, Starlab).
*   **Shortened Satellite Replacement Cycles:** Extremely low launch prices mean satellites no longer need to be designed for 15-year lifespans; a high-frequency 3-5 year iteration model is now viable, favoring flexible manufacturing.

### Catalyst Stories
*   **Artemis III Critical Validation:** The orbital propellant transfer test scheduled for next month will determine the success of the late 2026 moon landing plan.
*   **Project Kuiper Expansion:** Amazon's gap-filling launches utilizing the Starship payload dividend.

### Thematic Baskets
| Category | Core Focus / Direction | Rationale |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Launch Services | SpaceX Ecosystem, Blue Origin Suppliers | Increased demand for consumables driven by higher launch frequency. |
| Space Station Components | Flexible solar arrays, Life-support systems | Commercial space station construction is entering the module assembly phase. |
| Radiation-Hardened Semis | SiC/GaN space-grade chips | Chips designed for the high-frequency deployment needs of LEO satellites. |

## 3. Structural Shift: AI-Native Peptide Design & Multi-Pathway Metabolic Therapeutics (Biotech)

### Observation
GLP-1 drugs are evolving from "weight-loss drugs" into "comprehensive health management platforms." In 2026, the deep coupling of AI protein design and clinical data has led to an explosion in research for specific peptides targeting addiction, cardiovascular health, and longevity.

### Deep Analysis
*   **Oral Revolution:** The FDA approval window for non-peptide oral drugs like Orforglipron is approaching. This is not just a change in dosage form but a springboard for market penetration to jump from 5% to 20%.
*   **Precision Protein Design:** "Triple agonists" (GLP-1/GIP/Glucagon) designed using AlphaFold 3 and subsequent models are showing unprecedented weight loss and muscle retention ratios in Phase II trials.

### Catalyst Stories
*   **Lilly's Oral Data:** Long-term safety data for oral GLP-1s to be disclosed in mid-May.
*   **AI+Biotech Cross-sector Collaboration:** A top-tier pharma firm is expected to announce a dedicated protein design supercomputing center in partnership with an AI compute leader.

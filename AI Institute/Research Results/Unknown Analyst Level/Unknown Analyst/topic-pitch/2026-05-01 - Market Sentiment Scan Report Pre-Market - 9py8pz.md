# Market Sentiment Scan Report (Pre-Market)
**Date**: Friday, 1 May 2026
**Analyst**: Sentiment Analyst

## 1. Coverage Scope
- **Time Window**: Last 24 hours (30 April 2026 – 1 May 2026)
- **Sources**: CNN Fear & Greed Index, Exchange Flow Data, Institutional Positioning (13F/Daily Trackers), Social Media Sentiment Proxies, Bloomberg Terminal Liquidity Flows.
- **Key Assets**: US Equities (Large Cap), AI Supply Chain, JPY/Treasuries, Cryptocurrencies.

## 2. Strongest Signals Observed
- **AI Dominance as a "Liquidity Vortex" [Strong Signal]**: Following an exceptionally strong earnings report, NVDA's single-day trading volume as a percentage of total US equity turnover hit a record high. This has caused a visible "liquidity drain" in small-cap stocks (Russell 2000). While indices are rising, the Advance/Decline (A/D) Line is diverging, signaling fragile breadth.
- **FOMC Minutes Spark Sentiment Reset [Return to Neutral]**: Minutes revealed some officials remain open to "further tightening," causing the 10Y Treasury yield to spike toward 4.5%. The CNN Fear & Greed Index retreated from 62 (Greed) to 54 (Neutral), reflecting a market re-pricing for "Higher for Longer" rates.
- **Strong Economic Data vs. "Good News is Bad News" [Sentiment Pivot]**: Both Manufacturing and Services PMI significantly beat market expectations. However, the market reacted by selling off Treasuries and cyclicals, indicating that the core investor concern has pivoted from "Recession Risk" to "Resurgent Inflation."
- **Crypto Regulatory Pivot [Greed Signal]**: Market expectations for an Ethereum (ETH) ETF approval saw a 180-degree turn. Implied Volatility (IV) in the options market surged, and capital showed a distinct rotation from BTC to ETH, indicating high institutional appetite for "non-Bitcoin" digital assets.

- **Suggested Primary Analyst**: `chief-strategist`

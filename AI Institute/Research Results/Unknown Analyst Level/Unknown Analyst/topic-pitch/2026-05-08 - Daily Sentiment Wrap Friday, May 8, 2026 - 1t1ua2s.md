# Daily Sentiment Wrap: Friday, May 8, 2026

## 1. Closing Flow Imbalance: The "Quiet Exit"
Market on Close (MOC) data today revealed a pronounced sell-side tilt. While the S&P 500 hovered near highs throughout the session, the closing auction saw a sell imbalance of approximately **-$686M**. This follows a massive **-$2.0B** sell-off earlier in the week (May 4).
*   **Key Insight:** Retail optimism is sustaining the price action, but block trade and dark pool prints suggest institutional players are harvesting liquidity and trimming exposure into strength.
*   **Tomorrow's Watch:** This pattern of "thin rallies and heavy closes" is a hallmark of trend exhaustion.

## 2. Breadth Divergence: Fragility Behind the Records
The S&P 500 hit fresh records today (surpassing 7,300), but market breadth remains the primary warning light:
*   **Equal vs. Cap Weight:** The S&P 500 Equal-Weight Index (RSP) is still struggling below its February peaks, with gains overwhelmingly concentrated in Nvidia (NVDA), Broadcom (AVGO), and the Mag 7.
*   **MA Participation:** Only **69%** of S&P 500 components are trading above their 50-day moving average—a significant lag for an index making all-time highs.

## 3. Sentiment Thresholds: Defensive Hedging Amidst Greed
The **Fear & Greed Index is at 68 (Greed)**, drifting down from earlier highs.
*   **Sub-Indicator Shift:** While Market Momentum remains in "Extreme Greed," the **Put/Call Ratio** and **Junk Bond Demand** have rotated into "Fear."
*   **Conclusion:** Professional traders are quietly positioning for a correction. This is not a "crash" signal yet, but rather a sophisticated hedging rotation at the structural highs.

## 4. Setup for Monday (May 11, 2026)
Today's Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP) came in soft at **49,000**, missing expectations, while geopolitical tensions pushed **Brent Crude** above **$112/bbl**.
*   **Gap Risk:** The combination of weakening employment and rising energy-driven inflation (Stagflation-lite) creates a significant gap-down risk for Monday's open.
*   **Technical Trigger:** A break below 7,240 on Monday could confirm the start of a broader "Wave 4" correction.

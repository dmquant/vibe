# Market Sentiment & Flow Scan (May 14, 2026)

## 1. Executive Summary: The Volatility Paradox & "Dirty Risk-On"
The overnight session revealed an extraordinary divergence: the VIX surged to 18.06 (peaking at 19.01) while the S&P 500 and Nasdaq 100 managed a late-day reversal following a PPI inflation shock. Institutional capital is aggressively building hedges via SPX options (Put/Call Ratio at 1.20), yet index-level selling pressure is being entirely offset by AI sector strength. This "Dirty Risk-On" regime—characterized by rising asset prices alongside rising volatility and protection costs—suggests sentiment is at a tipping point between "Greed" and "Extreme Defensive Growth."

## 3. Fund Flows & Cross-Asset Anomalies
- **Safe-Haven Bifurcation**: Gold (XAU) exhibits a massive "geopolitical floor" at $4,700/oz, while the Japanese Yen (JPY) has lost its haven utility under the weight of the US-Japan yield gap (4.5% vs. near-zero), with USD/JPY nearing 157.
- **ETF Flow Radar**: SPY saw a 5-day net inflow of $4B, but TLT continues to hemorrhage cash. This "sell bonds, buy stocks" behavior is no longer pure optimism; it’s an asset reallocation in response to sticky inflation (PPI 6.0%).
- **FX Reversals**: Political risk premiums in GBP and EUR, dragged by UK Gilt volatility, are driving capital toward the "pure haven" of the Swiss Franc (CHF).

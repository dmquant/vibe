## Prioritized Conclusions

1. **Whiteboard-worthy: the AI compute trade is spreading from “chips/CPO” into “power/grid equipment.”** The morning market opened higher but faded, with broad single-stock weakness; still, power, grid equipment and CPO traded against the tape. The social narrative around “compute ultimately means power / compute-power coordination” had already been building across Eastmoney Wealth posts and Xueqiu-indexed content, and today’s tape validated it. The real debate is not whether grid names rallied, but whether this is a genuine repricing of the AI capex bottleneck.
2. **Zhongji Innolight at RMB1000 is a major attention spike, but not a standalone whiteboard.** 300308.SZ broke RMB1000 intraday, becoming the second ChiNext stock and the tenth A-share to enter the RMB1000 club. Fundamental coverage cites 2026Q1 revenue of RMB19.496bn, +192.12% YoY, and net profit attributable to shareholders of RMB5.735bn, +262.28% YoY. This is better treated as a crowding thermometer for the AI hardware trade and folded into the “compute-to-power diffusion” debate.
3. **No sufficiently credible current coordinated narrative campaign was found.** Searchable Weibo hot pages show “#Two reasons behind another surge in defense stocks#,” but the page mixes in May 12, 2025 index levels and turnover. Those conflict with today’s May 12, 2026 midday Shanghai Composite at 4208.00 and half-day turnover of RMB2.17tn, so it is filtered out as a current signal.

## Whiteboard Angle

**Topic: Has the AI bottleneck shifted from GPU/CPO to power?**

Debate points:
- If the 55GW power gap and USD800bn hyperscaler capex estimate hold, should grid equipment, flexible DC and power operators be treated as part of the AI trade rather than merely catch-up beta?
- Zhongji Innolight at RMB1000 and ongoing CPO strength show AI hardware remains the main line, but could also mean capital is looking for lower-positioned exposure within the same capex chain after leader crowding.
- The “compute-power coordination” narrative has been building socially from February to May, while fundamental validation is scattered across power prices, grid connection, green-power direct supply, storage and UHV; strategy and thematic research need one framework, or the team risks being dragged by short-term slogans.

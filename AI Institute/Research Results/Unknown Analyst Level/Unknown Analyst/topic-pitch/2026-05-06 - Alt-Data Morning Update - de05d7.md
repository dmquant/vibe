# Alt-Data Morning Update
**Date:** Wednesday, May 6, 2026
**Analyst:** Alt Data Analyst

## 1. Core Signals: Freight Rate Surge & Consumer Spending Divergence
As of May 6, 2026, high-frequency alternative data reveals a renewed "capacity crunch" in global freight and a significant shift in U.S. consumer spending from staples to discretionary goods.

*   **Ocean Freight (Drewry WCI):** Latest weekly prints show a **17%** single-week surge in Shanghai-to-Los Angeles (SH-LA) rates, reaching $3,738/FEU. The Drewry Composite Index is up **10%** week-over-week.
*   **Credit Card Spending (Chase/JPM Panel):** Month-to-date (MTD) discretionary spending grew **2.6%**, significantly outperforming the **1.2%** growth in staples. Gen Z and Millennial cohorts are leading with a **5.9%** YoY spending jump.
*   **Online Traffic (Similarweb):** While e-commerce web traffic remains flat, **app sessions have surged 13%**. Temu's annual traffic growth holds at a high **56.9%**, and traffic referred via AI chatbots is converting at **11.4%**, more than double organic search.

## 2. Divergence vs. Consensus
*   **Logistics:** Consensus expected global logistics rates to cool as geopolitical risks stabilized. However, the current 10% WoW jump suggests that tariff front-loading or hidden port congestion is more severe than anticipated. This could lead to a near-term rebound in inflation expectations.
*   **Consumption:** While most strategists maintain a "weak consumer" narrative due to high rates, high-frequency credit card data shows that younger balance sheets (Gen Z) remain resilient. The recovery in discretionary spending—particularly in travel and fashion—suggests upcoming retail earnings may beat consensus, contradicting the widely predicted recession.

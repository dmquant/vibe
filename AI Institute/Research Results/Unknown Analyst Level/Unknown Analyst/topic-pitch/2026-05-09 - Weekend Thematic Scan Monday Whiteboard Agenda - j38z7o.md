## Pitch 1: AI Infrastructure Is Moving From GPU Leaders to Bottleneck Assets

### 1. Debate Question and Priority Conclusion

Monday’s debate should be whether the AI trade has moved from “NVDA explains everything” to “who controls memory, alternative accelerators, available power, and deployable cloud capacity?” My answer is yes, but this is not a simple underweight-NVDA rotation; it is a broadening of the risk factors inside the AI basket.

### 2. What Changed Over the Past Five Trading Days

| Asset | 2026-05-01 close | 2026-05-08 close | Five-trading-day move | Main read-through |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| MU | $542.21 | $746.81 | +37.7% | Memory/HBM moved from cost item to the strongest beta. |
| AMD | $360.54 | $455.19 | +26.3% | Alternative accelerator and inference-share expectations were repriced. |
| IREN | $45.66 | $61.20 | +34.0% | Power, land, and GPU-cloud delivery capacity became AI infrastructure assets. |
| NVDA | $198.45 | $215.20 | +8.4% | The core beneficiary still rose, but relative beta is being redistributed. |
| AVGO | $421.28 | $430.00 | +2.1% | ASIC/networking did not chase in the same way, which is a live debate point. |

Price sources: StockAnalysis historical price pages for [MU](https://stockanalysis.com/stocks/mu/history/), [AMD](https://stockanalysis.com/stocks/amd/history/), [IREN](https://stockanalysis.com/stocks/iren/history/), [NVDA](https://stockanalysis.com/stocks/nvda/history/), and [AVGO](https://stockanalysis.com/stocks/avgo/history/).

The catalyst set was broader than one headline. First, the market continued to digest hyperscaler AI capex, with media-compiled 2026 capex plans for Google, Amazon, Microsoft, and Meta totaling about $725B, up 77% from $410B last year; roughly $25B of Microsoft’s AI budget was attributed to higher memory and component costs. [Tom's Hardware](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/big-tech/big-techs-ai-spending-plans-reach-725-billion). Second, Microsoft’s FY26 Q1 call disclosed $34.9B of quarterly capex and said demand continued to exceed supply, with Azure AI demand still capacity-constrained. [Microsoft IR](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/investor/events/fy-2026/earnings-fy-2026-q1). Third, on `2026-05-07`, IREN announced a five-year, roughly $3.4B AI cloud contract with NVIDIA, using about 60MW of Childress, Texas capacity for Blackwell systems. [IREN/StockTitan](https://www.stocktitan.net/news/IREN/iren-secures-3-4bn-ai-cloud-contract-with-hh8a26euuqhs.html).

### 3. Why This Needs Multi-Analyst Debate

This was not a plain semiconductor bounce. The moves in MU and IREN show the market assigning higher multiples to “physical bottlenecks” in AI: HBM/DRAM supply, liquid-cooled data centers, power access, GPU-cloud operations, and customer prepayments. At the same time, AVGO’s more muted move leaves open whether ASIC/networking exposure is lagging or correctly discounting risk.

## Pitch 2: Pure-Play Quantum Enters the “Commercial Evidence vs. Valuation Pull-Forward” Phase

### 1. Debate Question and Priority Conclusion

Monday’s debate should be whether pure-play quantum stocks have moved from “research milestone trades” to “revenue/RPO/system-delivery trades.” My conclusion: IonQ’s Q1 genuinely improved commercialization credibility, but the broader pure-play quantum basket still trades more like a long-duration call option than a normal revenue-upside story.

| RGTI | $17.50 | $18.94 | +8.2% | Q1 call is on `2026-05-11`, so event risk is not finished. |
| QBTS | $20.49 | $22.57 | +10.2% | One of the strongest pure-quantum betas this week. |

Price sources: StockAnalysis/FinanceCharts historical price pages for [IONQ](https://www.financecharts.com/stocks/IONQ/summary/price), [RGTI](https://www.financecharts.com/stocks/RGTI/summary/price), [QBTS](https://stockanalysis.com/stocks/qbts/history/), and [QUBT](https://stockanalysis.com/stocks/qubt/history/).

The main fundamental catalyst was IonQ. On `2026-05-06`, the company reported Q1 revenue of $64.7M, up 755% year over year; RPO up 554% year over year to $470M; higher 2026 revenue guidance of $260M-$270M; Q1 adjusted EBITDA loss of ($96.8M); and unchanged full-year adjusted EBITDA loss guidance of ($330M)-($310M). It also disclosed the first sale of a 6th-generation, chip-based, 256-qubit system and selection for DARPA’s HARQ program. [IonQ IR](https://investors.ionq.com/news/news-details/2026/IonQ-Announces-First-Quarter-2026-Financial-Results/default.aspx).

### 3. Why This Needs Multi-Analyst Debate

The quantum theme now has fundamentals that can actually be debated. RPO, system sales, government/defense work, and cybersecurity orders are entering the spreadsheet. But the equity reaction is not just fundamental; it is also a re-leveraging of long-duration technology risk. IONQ rose 6.6% over the week and QBTS rose 10.2%, while IonQ’s quarterly adjusted EBITDA loss was nearly $100M. Buyers need to define the reason for ownership precisely: revenue curve, government procurement, platform M&A, or technology option value.

## Pitch 3: Web3 Is Becoming a Hybrid Trade in ETF Flows and AI Infrastructure

### 1. Debate Question and Priority Conclusion

Monday’s debate should be whether digital-asset equities should still be treated as simple BTC/ETH beta. My answer is no. BTC ETF flows are supporting the coin, but equity alpha is moving toward two groups: compliant custody/exchange infrastructure and former miners that can turn power and data centers into AI cloud revenue.

### 3. Why This Needs Multi-Analyst Debate

The key signal this week was not BTC rising 5.2%; it was the style split inside related equities. COIN and MSTR tracked the coin, while IREN received an independent reset after NVIDIA’s five-year, roughly $3.4B contract and potential investment rights of up to $2.1B. [IREN/StockTitan](https://www.stocktitan.net/news/IREN/iren-secures-3-4bn-ai-cloud-contract-with-hh8a26euuqhs.html). That means “crypto infrastructure” is splitting: exchanges/custody benefit from ETFs and regulatory clarity, while miners/power assets are being repriced by AI cloud customers.

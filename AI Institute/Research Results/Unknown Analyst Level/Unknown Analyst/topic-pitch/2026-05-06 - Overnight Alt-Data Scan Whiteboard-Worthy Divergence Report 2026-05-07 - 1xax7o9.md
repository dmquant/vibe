# Overnight Alt-Data Scan: Whiteboard-Worthy Divergence Report (2026-05-07)

## Executive Summary
Today's scan focuses on the unexpected spike in global freight rates, market share shifts in AI application traffic, and the "downward" consumption trend following China's Labor Day holiday. The most significant divergence lies in the shipping sector: contrary to the widespread "overcapacity" consensus, the World Container Index (WCI) has rebounded sharply, signaling an early start to the inventory restocking cycle.

---

## 1. Global Logistics: WCI vs. "Overcapacity" Consensus
**Signal:** The Drewry World Container Index (WCI) recorded significant gains in the first week of May, with some routes (Shanghai-Rotterdam) increasing by over 15% month-on-month.
- **Alt-Data Validation:** Satellite monitoring shows port congestion in Singapore rising from a normal 1-2 days to 5-7 days. The Container Availability Index (CAI) at China's export hubs (e.g., Ningbo, Shenzhen) has dropped below 0.35 (values < 0.5 indicate shortage).
- **The Divergence:** The sell-side consensus anticipated that massive new vessel deliveries in 2026 would suppress freight rates. However, the extended voyages around the Cape of Good Hope (+14 days) and "frontloading" by shippers wary of supply chain disruptions are offsetting the capacity growth.
- **Investment Thesis:** Re-evaluate the medium-term margin elasticity of container shipping lines (e.g., COSCO Shipping, Maersk). Current freight levels are significantly above the breakeven point.

## 2. TMT: ChatGPT Traffic Peak and Search Landscape Reshaping
**Signal:** Similarweb data indicates that on May 7 (today), ChatGPT's global visits hit a new high, with Daily Active Users (DAU) exceeding 85 million.
- **Alt-Data Validation:** 
    - **ChatGPT:** The domain migration from `chat.openai.com` to `chatgpt.com` is complete, with the site now ranked #16 globally.
    - **Perplexity:** Monthly visits reached 80 million, up 14% MoM, with 80% being direct traffic.
    - **Claude:** Traffic saw a slight MoM dip (~10.3M unique visitors), indicating sticky user volatility while waiting for major model updates.
- **The Divergence:** While ChatGPT maintains absolute dominance (>90% share), Perplexity's penetration in "search alternative" scenarios is accelerating.
- **Follow-up:** Monitor whether upcoming OpenAI updates will further squeeze the space for vertical search AI platforms.

## 3. China Macro: Post-Labor Day "Downward" Growth
**Signal:** Ministry of Culture and Tourism data shows 295 million domestic trips during the holiday (YoY +7.6%), though per-capita spending remains sluggish.
- **Alt-Data Validation:** Baidu Maps migration data shows inflow to Tier-3 and Tier-4 cities (county-level) significantly outperforming Tier-1 cities. Meituan/Dianping data shows county-level tourism orders up ~36% YoY.
- **The Divergence:** Consumption in traditional "core business districts" is slowing, while "extreme value-for-money" and "reverse tourism" have become mainstream. This suggests a structural downward penetration of consumption rather than a broad-based recovery.
- **Investment Thesis:** Focus on consumer brands and OTA platforms with deep footprints in lower-tier cities (e.g., PDD, Tongcheng Travel).

## 4. Labor Market: Tech vs. Healthcare Polarization
**Signal:** Indeed job posting data shows software development roles remain 30% below pre-pandemic levels.
- **Alt-Data Validation:** In contrast, healthcare postings (especially for specialists and nurses) are up 102% compared to pre-pandemic baselines.
- **The Divergence:** Despite the AI-driven equity rally, the labor market reflects the initial displacement of junior coding roles by AI, while service sectors (Healthcare, Education) remain the primary source of inflationary wage pressure.

# Morning Alt-Indicator Update (2026-05-08)

## Executive Summary
Today's alternative data surfaces three critical cross-asset divergences: a contra-seasonal spike in global freight rates clashing with retail margin expectations, a "retention wall" for Generative AI apps despite exploding traffic, and a structural weakening in US consumer spend within a "K-shaped" recovery.

---

## 1. Freight & Retail: The Tariff Front-Loading Cost Shock
- **Alt-Signal**: The Shanghai Containerized Freight Index (SCFI) and Drewry WCI jumped 12% in the first week of May, defying seasonal norms. Satellite imagery shows congestion indices at SE Asian hubs (Singapore, Port Klang) hitting 24-month highs.
- **Divergence**: Retail sector valuations (e.g., Shopify, Walmart) currently price in easing inflation and margin expansion. However, alt-data suggests a massive wave of "front-loading" ahead of potential 2027 tariff shifts is driving up logistics costs, threatening H2 2026 margins.
- **Action**: Trim exposure to consumer discretionaries with high trans-Pacific reliance; tactical long on ocean carriers (e.g., Maersk, ZIM) to capture spot rate premiums.

## 2. Generative AI: The "False Prosperity" of Traffic vs. Retention
- **Alt-Signal**: Mobile app traffic monitoring shows AI app MAUs up 250% YoY, yet annual retention sits at a mere 21.1%—roughly 30% lower than traditional SaaS benchmarks.
- **Divergence**: Market premiums for "AI-first" consumer apps are built on explosive user growth, ignoring the high churn caused by "Feature Tourism" (users testing tools for novelty rather than utility).
- **Action**: Avoid "AI wrappers" that lack deep workflow integration; overweight "Utility AI" (e.g., GitHub Copilot, Perplexity) which maintains retention rates 40% above the sector average.

## 3. US Consumer: The "K-Shaped" Safe Haven Shift
- **Alt-Signal**: High-frequency credit card data reveals a stark divide: households earning >$150k saw 2.6% spend growth, while those <$50k managed only 0.6%. BNPL (Buy Now, Pay Later) penetration hit a record high of 32% in early May.
- **Divergence**: While travel majors (Airbnb, Uber) reported strong Q1s, high-frequency booking data for April/May indicates a rapid shift in mid-tier demand toward "staycations" or budget alternatives.
- **Action**: Short mid-market hotel chains; long discount retailers with robust private-label offerings.

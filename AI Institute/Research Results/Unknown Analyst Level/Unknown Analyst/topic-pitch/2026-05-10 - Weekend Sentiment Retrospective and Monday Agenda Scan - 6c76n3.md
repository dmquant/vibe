## 1. Prioritised Conclusions

The agenda item for Monday is not simply that indexes made new highs. The unresolved issue is that sentiment, positioning, and flows shifted in three different ways that the team has not formally reconciled.

| Priority | Topic | Conclusion | Monday risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Sentiment moved into greed, but protection did not reprice enough | CNN Fear & Greed was reported near 69 around 2026-05-07, in Greed territory; NAAIM rose to 96.67, putting active managers close to fully exposed; yet VIX ended Friday at only 17.19 and SKEW eased from 141.38 to 138.21. | If weekend macro, oil, or Middle East news turns negative, Monday gap-down risk is larger than headline volatility implies. |
| 2 | The market traded like a crowded long, while flows still showed redemptions | AP data show Nasdaq +4.5%, S&P 500 +2.3%, and Russell 2000 +1.7% for the week, but the latest ICI report shows $5.51B of equity fund outflows and $7.80B of domestic equity outflows; ETF flows still favoured passive broad-market products. | This looks more like passive/systematic demand plus short covering than a clean active-investor risk-budget upgrade. |
| 3 | Cross-asset risk appetite is a mix of equity greed, crowded duration, and fading commodity hedges | In 10Y Treasury COT data, Asset Manager/Institutional accounts are net long about 2.14M contracts while Leveraged Funds are net short about 2.00M contracts; recent flows show redemptions from GLD, commodity funds, and T-bill products, while growth and semiconductor ETFs attracted cash. | If CPI or oil lifts term premium again, equity valuations and duration positioning can come under pressure at the same time. |

## 3. Monday Agenda Pitches

### Pitch 1: Greed is confirmed, but under-hedging is the trade problem

**Conclusion**: The regime should be relabelled from "repair rally" to "greed with insufficient protection." NAAIM at 96.67 and CNN Fear & Greed near 69 show risk exposure has recovered; VIX at 17.19, VVIX at 96.78, and SKEW at 138.21 do not show comparable tail-risk pricing.

**Monday question**: Is this a momentum confirmation, or an asymmetric gap-down setup after weekend headline risk?

**Triggers**:

| Trigger | Interpretation | Action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| SPX gaps higher while VIX stays soft | Greed extends while protection remains cheap | Debate adding near-term protection instead of chasing beta. |
| SPX gaps lower while SKEW/VVIX jump | Late protection demand arrives all at once | Check vol-control, short-term CTA, and dealer gamma amplification. |
| Nasdaq keeps leading Russell 2000 | Still growth crowding, not broad risk appetite | Reduce the weight on a broad-bull-market narrative. |

### Pitch 2: Record highs with domestic equity outflows should not be read as universal chasing

**Conclusion**: ICI shows $5.51B of equity fund outflows and $7.80B of domestic equity outflows for the 2026-04-29 week; ETF.com shows $26.21B into US equity ETFs for the 2026-05-01 week, concentrated in IVV, VOO, SPY, QQQ, IWM, and similar index vehicles. The demand profile looks more like passive allocation, model buying, and short covering than a unanimous active-risk upgrade.

**Monday question**: If the market pulls back next week, will flows buy the dip, or does a pause in ETF mechanical demand create an air pocket?

**Triggers**:

| Trigger | Interpretation | Action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Broad-market ETFs keep taking cash while active/mutual funds keep losing it | Index demand and active confidence remain split | Treat drawdowns as a test of technical index demand. |
| RSP, IWM, and HYG start attracting cash again | Breadth improvement becomes real | Only then upgrade the move to full risk-on. |
| GLD and T-bill flows rebound together | Defensive demand is returning | Reduce chase-the-rally recommendations. |

### Pitch 3: Cross-asset risk appetite is not pure risk-on; it is AI growth plus crowded duration plus fading commodity hedges

**Conclusion**: Nasdaq rose +4.5% for the week, and DRAM, SMH, QQQ, and other growth/semiconductor vehicles attracted cash. At the same time, 10Y Treasury positioning still shows very large Asset Manager/Institutional longs and very large Leveraged Fund shorts. Equity longs and duration longs can coexist under a "growth okay, inflation contained" story, but CPI or oil-driven term-premium pressure would hit both.

**Monday question**: Should the team elevate "AI/growth leadership" from an equity-style issue into a cross-asset crowding issue that is explicitly rate-sensitive?

**Triggers**:

| Trigger | Interpretation | Action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 10Y yields rise while Nasdaq still leads | Equities are ignoring discount-rate risk | Prepare a valuation and term-premium stress test. |
| 10Y yields rise while IWM/HYG weaken | Risk appetite is narrowing into quality growth | Define the rally as a crowded quality-growth trade. |
| Oil breaks higher again and lifts VIX | Geopolitical/inflation risk is being repriced | Recheck second-order effects on consumption, credit, and the Fed path. |

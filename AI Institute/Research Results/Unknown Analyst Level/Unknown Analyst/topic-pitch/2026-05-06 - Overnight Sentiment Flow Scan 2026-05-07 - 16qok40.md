# Overnight Sentiment & Flow Scan (2026-05-07)

## Executive Summary
The overnight session confirmed a decisive shift into a **broad "Risk-On" regime**. A dual-catalyst—blowout earnings in the AI hardware supercycle (Samsung surpassing $1T market cap, AMD surging) and material de-escalation in Middle East geopolitical tensions (anticipation of a US-Iran peace deal)—pushed the S&P 500 and Nasdaq 100 to new all-time highs. Despite the euphoric mood, the simultaneous surge in Gold and Silver suggests persistent underlying demand for long-term inflation hedges.

## Deep Scan & Debate Points

### 1. AI Hardware: "Supercycle" or "Valuation Peak"?
Samsung Electronics crossing the $1 trillion valuation mark and AMD’s 18.7% single-day jump mark a transition from "expectation" to "earnings realization" for AI hardware.
- **Observation**: The KOSPI surged 6.5% overnight, and semiconductor ETFs (SMH) saw record-breaking inflows.
- **Risk**: The extremely low P/C ratio (0.57) suggests a lack of downside protection, placing sentiment deep in the "Greed" zone.

### 2. Geopolitical Premium "Violent" Unwind
News of a potential US-Iran peace deal and the reopening of the Strait of Hormuz caused WTI Crude to drop toward $102/barrel.
- **Observation**: The Bloomberg Dollar Spot Index softened (-0.4%), while commodity proxies like AUD/USD hit a 52-week high.
- **Risk**: Any setback in diplomatic negotiations would trigger a rapid reversal of this "peace dividend."

### 3. The Gold/Risk Asset "Divergence"
Despite the equity rally, Gold and Silver moved significantly higher.
- **Logical Analysis**: This reflects investors playing the "soft landing" narrative while simultaneously hedging against currency devaluation and long-term inflation. This "dual strength" in risk and hedge assets is often seen in the mid-to-late stages of a bull market.

# Overnight Sentiment & Flow Analysis Report (2026-05-05)

## Executive Summary: Cautious Greed & The Awakening of Volatility

The overnight market presents a challenging set of "divergence clusters": while mainstream sentiment remains firmly in "Greed" territory, key volatility markers (VIX) and macro stress tests (Oil/Yields) are flashing red.

### 1. The Volatility Paradox: VIX Spike vs. Persistent Inflows
*   **Data Points:** VIX Index surged **+8.95%** to **18.51** overnight; however, global equity funds recorded their 6th consecutive week of net inflows (+$18.91B this week).
*   **Sentiment Analysis:** The market is in a state of "defensive longing." Institutional investors continue to allocate to Tech and Japan while aggressively layering on tail-risk protection in the options market. This simultaneous rise in VIX and fund flows is a classic hallmark of sudden geopolitical shifts (Strait of Hormuz oil supply risks).

### 2. The "Macro Pincer": Greed Amidst the Mist
*   **Data Points:** WTI Crude has breached the **$100/barrel** mark, while the 10-Year Treasury yield hit **4.45%**. The CNN Fear & Greed Index sits at **65 (Greed)**.
*   **Sentiment Analysis:** Investors are currently obsessed with earnings growth, with 72% of companies beating estimates, providing a psychological buffer against rising costs. However, a significant gap exists between a Greed reading of 65 and the physical reality of $100 oil. If energy costs translate into a second wave of inflation expectations, the current "Greed" could evaporate rapidly.

### 3. Japan's Siphon Effect & Structural Rotation
*   **Data Points:** Japanese funds saw a massive **$8.27B** inflow in a single week, accounting for 43% of all global equity inflows.
*   **Sentiment Analysis:** Japan is transitioning from a "source of carry trade" to a "global safe haven." This outsized inflow suggests a structural rebalancing as global investors seek shelter from the combined geopolitical and inflationary risks currently plaguing US and European markets.

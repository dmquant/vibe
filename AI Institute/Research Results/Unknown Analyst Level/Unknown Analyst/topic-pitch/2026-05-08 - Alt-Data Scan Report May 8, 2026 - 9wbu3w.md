# Alt-Data Scan Report: May 8, 2026

## 1. Macro & Labor: Divergence Between NFP and Real-Time Job Postings
**Signal:** Today's Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP) showed a modest increase of only 62k (vs. 178k expected), fueling recession fears.
**Alt-Data Validation:**
- **Revelio Labs** data shows that while overall job growth is slowing, technical job postings related to "AI Infrastructure" and "Energy Grids" have grown by 22% in the last 30 days.
- **LinkUp** data indicates that "Time-to-Fill" for S&P 500 companies has dropped to 28 days, reflecting a trend where firms are cutting non-essential costs while aggressively "harvesting talent" for critical roles.
**Conclusion:** This is not a broad recession but "Surgical Contraction." Capital is shifting from general labor-intensive industries to capital/tech-intensive verticals.

## 2. Retail "Great Divergence": Walmart vs. Target
**Signal:** Walmart (WMT) mobile Monthly Active Users (MAUs) exceeded 64 million, while Target (TGT) purchase intent data hit a 3-year low.
**Alt-Data Details:**
- **Credit Card Spending Panels:** Data shows that households with annual incomes >$150k saw a 14% increase in their average ticket size at Walmart. This confirms that the "Trade-down" trend has reached high-net-worth individuals.
- **App Engagement:** Usage of Walmart+'s "Scan & Go" has doubled, reflecting consumers' extreme reliance on efficiency and precise budget control.
- **Price Action:** WMT performed steadily in today's volatile market, while TGT was dragged down by Shake Shack (-28%), reflecting a broader sell-off in "Discretionary/Premium Casual Dining."
**Investment Thesis:** Maintain Outperform on Walmart; continue to underweight Target as middle-class purchasing power is squeezed by 7% mortgage rates and tariff pressures.

## 3. TMT & AI Infrastructure: Paradigm Shift from "Chips" to "Power"
**Signal:** Nvidia (NVDA) guidance reached $78B for the quarter, yet stock gains have stalled.
**Alt-Data Analysis:**
- **Satellite Imagery:** Monitoring shows over 7GW of data center construction projects in Virginia and Ohio are currently "stalled or slow-walking."
- **Grid Connectivity Data:** The queue for data center power connections has now extended to 5-7 years.
- **Secondary Market:** H100 rental rates rebounded to $2.35/hr, but more as "gap-fill" for existing clusters rather than new growth.
**Conclusion:** The bottleneck has shifted from Blackwell chip supply to grid absorption capacity. The market is entering an "Infrastructure Scarcity" phase, benefiting ASML and grid equipment suppliers.

## 4. Follow-up & Actionable Items
- **Monitoring Focus:** Watch next week's Retail Sales data to see if it validates the "Walmartization" of high-income households.
- **Risk Alert:** Tensions in the Strait of Hormuz could push oil prices toward $110, offsetting discount retail efforts.

## 2. Core Observations: Distribution Risks Masked by Green Indices

### A. MOC Deviation and the "Closing Bull Trap"
In the final 10 minutes, MOC order flow revealed a massive $3.2B buy-side imbalance. This flow was primarily driven by target-weight adjustments for month-end rebalancing. However, excluding these passive buy orders, active volume throughout the day was decidedly skewed toward sellers. This divergence—indices up, individual stocks down—typically flags a gap-down risk for the May 1st open.

### B. Nasdaq Touches the "20-Day EMA" Psychological Threshold
The Nasdaq 100 closed below its critical 20-day Exponential Moving Average (EMA) for the first time since February. Technical selling pressure is mounting. Although "Magnificent 7" names received some support at the close, mid-cap software and semiconductor stocks showed signs of position liquidation.

### C. Institutional Dark Pool Footprints
The DPI index spiked above 48% today, suggesting that institutions were not "buying the dip" but were instead utilizing the month-end liquidity peak for an orderly exit. Specifically in AI infrastructure sectors, we observed "price flat, volume up" after three days of "price up, volume down"—a textbook signal of institutional distribution.

## 3. Setup for Tomorrow: Testing the May Opening Pressure

*   **Overnight Risk:** Watch for a liquidity gap at the May opening. Since today's close was artificially propped up by passive rebalancing, the lack of active follow-through tomorrow could lead to a rapid retracement of today's "phantom" gains.
*   **Breadth Divergence:** Monitor the relationship between the Equal-Weight S&P 500 (RSP) and the Market-Cap Weighted SPY. Continued weakness in RSP would confirm a "zero-sum" liquidity environment in decay.

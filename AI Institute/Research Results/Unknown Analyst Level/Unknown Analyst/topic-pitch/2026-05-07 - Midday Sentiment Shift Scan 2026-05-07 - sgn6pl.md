# Midday Sentiment Shift Scan (2026-05-07)

## 1. Sentiment Regime Context: From "War Premium" to "Peace Pivot"
The morning session witnessed a violent regime break. Upon confirmation of the US-Iran Memorandum of Understanding (MoU), ending the nine-week blockade of the Strait of Hormuz, markets shifted rapidly from defensive hedging to an extreme "risk-on" mode.
- **Catalyst A**: Brent crude collapsed 7%, breaking below $100 for the first time since the conflict began ($99.58), triggering a secondary downward revision in global inflation expectations.
- **Catalyst B**: The aftershocks of the **DeepSeek V4** release continue, with API pricing 97% lower than GPT-5.5 ($0.0036/M tokens), fundamentally rewriting the cost curve for AI intelligence.

## 2. Flow & Positioning Anomalies: Energy Exodus & Compute Crowding
Midday flow tape reveals sharp positioning polarization:
- **Short Squeeze**: Panic unwinding in the energy sector (proxies for Exxon, Saudi Aramco). Morning call option Open Interest (OI) plummeted by 45%.

## 3. Cross-Style/Asset Divergence: Significant Breadth Improvement
- **Style Rotation**: A massive rotation from Value/Cyclical (Energy, Materials) into Growth/Consumer (Tech, Aviation).
- **Breadth Metrics**: While major indices showed steady gains (Nifty reclaiming 24,300), the Advance/Decline (A/D) ratio expanded significantly at midday, indicating much broader participation than the morning session. Aviation names (e.g., IndiGo) surged over 5% on expected fuel cost relief.

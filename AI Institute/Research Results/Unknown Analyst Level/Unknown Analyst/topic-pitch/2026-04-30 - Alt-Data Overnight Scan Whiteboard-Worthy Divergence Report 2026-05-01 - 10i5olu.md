# Alt-Data Overnight Scan: Whiteboard-Worthy Divergence Report (2026-05-01)

## Executive Summary
Overnight monitoring of alternative data reveals significant divergences from market consensus in two critical areas: an unexpected acceleration in North American data center power load and a critical inventory build-up of Chinese EVs at European ports.

---

## 1. N.A. Data Center Power Load: AI Demand Refuses to "Cool"
### Alt-Data Signals
*   **Source:** Satellite Thermal Imaging + Real-time Grid Base Load Data (PJM/Northern Virginia).
*   **Observation Sites:** Ashburn, VA and Ohio Data Center Clusters.
*   **The Divergence:** Power demand for high-density compute units surged **12.4% MoM** over the last 30 days, far exceeding the 3-5% linear growth anticipated by the sell-side.

### Fundamental Context & Impact
*   **Consensus:** Sell-side analysts have recently argued that AI infrastructure investment (ROI) is hitting a fatigue phase, forecasting a Capex slowdown in 2H2026.
*   **Divergence Logic:** Alt-data suggests that while software-layer ROI is still being debated, the "land-grab" style construction at the physical layer is accelerating. This is directly bullish for Thermal Management (e.g., Vertiv), Power Equipment (e.g., Eaton), and High-Performance Copper cabling.

---

## 2. European Port Inventory Monitoring: The EV "Dam" Effect
### Alt-Data Signals
*   **Source:** Satellite Optical Imagery + Port AIS Throughput Analysis.
*   **Observation Sites:** Port of Antwerp-Bruges and Bremerhaven.
*   **Threshold Breach:** The average dwell time for Chinese-branded EVs in port discharge zones has breached **115 days**, significantly above the 90-day warning threshold. Port storage capacity utilization has reached **96%**.

### Fundamental Context & Impact
*   **Consensus:** The market assumes that inventory clearing for BYD and Tesla in Europe is progressing steadily.
*   **Divergence Logic:** This "port-as-warehouse" phenomenon signals an imminent price war or massive impairment charges in Q2. For European OEMs, competitive pressure is far more immediate than currently reflected in earnings reports.

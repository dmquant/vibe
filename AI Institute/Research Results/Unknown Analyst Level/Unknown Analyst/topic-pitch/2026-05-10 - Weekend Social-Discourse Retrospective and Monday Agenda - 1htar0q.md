## Step 3: Monday Agenda Pitches

### Pitch 1: Is the weekend robotics chase already crowded?

**Why Monday matters:** on `2026-05-08`, the robotics sector saw nearly `30` stocks hit limit-up, and the parts-chain discussion shifted from “theme imagination” to “which name catches the next catch-up bid.”[2] At the single-name level, Wuzhou New Spring was reported on `2026-05-09` with a `10.00%` gain, turnover of `RMB 2.459 billion`, and float market cap of `RMB 28.626 billion`; one-day turnover was therefore about `8.6%` of float market cap, showing that social attention has already moved into the turnover and volatility surface.[5]

**Retail consensus:**  
- “Tesla / Figure / embodied AI” remains the demand-side anchor.  
- A-share discussion is focused on tradable components: omni wheels, lead screws, reducers, joints, and bearings.  
- Weekend language upgraded from “does the company have robotics exposure?” to “can it enter a core supply chain?”  

**Institutional radar issue that remains unresolved:**  
- Robotics order visibility, gross margin, and delivery cadence remain fragmented; the market lacks one consistent discounting framework.  
- As the valuation gap between leaders and laggards widens, capital may attack “low-position names that fit the theme” before fundamentals catch up.  
- If Monday opens with another gap-up, the key signal is turnover absorption, not the raw count of limit-up stocks.  

**Monday tracking items:**  
- Opening premium, limit-order quality, and pre-`10:30` turnover in Wuzhou New Spring, Greatoo Intelligent, and Leader Harmonic.  
- Whether robotics ETFs/theme funds show visible net inflows or premiums.  
- Whether exchange Q&A, announcements, or meeting notes contain new orders or customer validation rather than only “we noticed market rumors.”  

### Pitch 2: AI hardware has moved from overseas headlines into domestic CPO/PCB. Who truly benefits?

**Why Monday matters:** Nvidia-Corning fiber infrastructure news triggered A-share discussion in CPO, optical communications, and PCB; on `2026-05-07`, Focuslight hit a `20CM` limit-up, while DSBJ and Accelink reached record highs.[3] On `2026-05-08`, CPO, PCB, and robotics still formed the most active high-turnover directions.[4]

**Retail consensus:**  
- Global AI capex is still treated as the strongest certainty.  
- “Optical modules are scarce, PCB lead times are extending, and CCL prices are rising” became the weekend explanation template.  
- Some cross-border theme products show emotion-led pricing: a global chip QDII was reported with price `3.975` and NAV `2.7451`, implying a premium of about `44.8%`, which shows retail investors are willing to pay heavily for AI hardware exposure.[6]

**Institutional radar issue that remains unresolved:**  
- Corning capacity expansion does not map linearly into revenue for domestic CPO/PCB companies.  
- If PCB/CCL price increases mainly reflect short-term lead-time disruption, they should not be extrapolated into a full growth cycle.  
- The higher the theme-ETF premium, the clearer the signal that secondary-market prices may have discounted fundamentals too aggressively.  

**Monday tracking items:**  
- Gap-up follow-through in Focuslight, DSBJ, Accelink, and PCB/CCL leaders.  
- Whether QDII/ETF premiums narrow; if they do not, retail risk appetite is still spilling outward.  
- Whether weekend sell-side notes start to separate domestic companies by orders, capacity, and customer exposure.  

### Pitch 3: Can RMB 3 trillion turnover and May Day data bring consumer stocks back into the conversation?

**Why Monday matters:** on `2026-05-08`, Shanghai and Shenzhen turnover was about `RMB 3.05 trillion`, providing a risk-appetite floor for theme stocks.[7] At the same time, May Day domestic trips reached `325 million`, with total spend of `RMB 185.492 billion`; this implies per-trip tourism spend of about `RMB 571`. Tax-authority data also showed holiday-related consumption sales revenue up `14.3%` year on year.[8][9]

**Retail consensus:**  
- Index level: expanded turnover reinforces the “underinvested catch-up” mood.  
- Consumer level: Weibo and short-video evidence of crowding, concerts, and music festivals proves traffic strength, but investors remain more cautious on ticket size and margins.  
- Trading level: capital still prefers robotics, CPO, and PCB growth themes; consumption is “strong data, unproven equity elasticity.”  

**Institutional radar issue that remains unresolved:**  
- Traffic, sales, and margins have not been proven to improve in sync.  
- Per-trip tourism spend of about `RMB 571` leaves room for the “strong volume, weaker price” interpretation.  
- If turnover stays near RMB 3 trillion, consumption may receive rotation; if volume contracts, consumer names are more likely to be crowded out by growth themes.  

**Monday tracking items:**  
- Whether travel, hotel, dining, duty-free, and gold-jewelry stocks outperform the CSI 300 at the open.  
- If consumer stocks gap up, whether leadership comes from institutional-quality names or only from small-cap catch-up trades.  
- The first company-level operating readouts after May Day: RevPAR, table turnover, same-store sales, and ticket size.  

## Step 4: Pressure Tests and Execution Checklist

| Pressure test | Trigger signal | Trading implication |
|---|---|---|
| Robotics shifts from “strong consensus” to “crowded trade” | Gap-up with fast turnover but repeated pullback in limit-up orders; laggards spike and fade. | Keep only chains with clearer order or customer validation; avoid pure name-based catch-up. |
| AI hardware shifts from “overseas mapping” to “premium compression” | CPO/PCB opens high and fades; QDII/ETF premiums narrow. | Reduce assumptions that overseas headlines directly map into A-share revenue; rotate toward confirmed orders and utilization. |
| RMB 3 trillion turnover does not hold | `2026-05-11` half-day turnover is materially below the `2026-05-08` pace. | Theme-stock elasticity falls, and weekend FOMO capital is more vulnerable to reversal. |
| Consumption data are reinterpreted as “weaker per-capita spend” | Travel/dining/duty-free opens high but fails to outperform the index. | Treat consumer equities as company-level verification trades, not sector beta. |

## 1. Key Observations

### A. The DeepSeek V4 Pricing Shock
The aftershocks of DeepSeek V4 (released April 24, 2026) are reshaping cross-market trades this week. V4-Pro, priced at **$1.74 per 1M tokens**, achieves performance parity with GPT-5.5, which remains anchored near **$15.00**. This marks the definitive transition of AI compute from "scarcity premium" to "utility commodity."

### B. Quantum Error Correction (QEC) Milestones
Google’s **Willow chip** (105 physical qubits) and Quantinuum’s **Helios system** (48 logical qubits) have both crossed the critical "below-threshold" line. This means increasing physical qubit counts now exponentially *decreases* logical error rates—transforming quantum computing from a theoretical pursuit (NISQ era) into an engineering scalability problem (Fault-Tolerant era).

## 2. Anomalies & Debates

### The Debate: Training Moats vs. Inference Utility
*   **Consensus:** Markets remain hyper-focused on GPU Capex and massive training clusters.
*   **The Anomaly:** US AI software names weakened overnight on "valuation concerns," yet DeepSeek V4 proved top-tier performance can be achieved with radical capital efficiency ($5.2M training cost).
*   **Structural Divergence:** The market has not yet priced the "inference cost collapse." When token costs drop 10x, recursive reasoning loops in **AI Agents** become economically viable. This triggers a paradigm shift from "Model as Moat" to "Workflow/Data as Moat."

## 2. Detailed write-ups

### 1. Home-improvement card spend — mark HD up

- **Data:** BAC Aggregated Card Spending Weekly, w/e 5/10. Home Improvement 4-wk rolling YoY at **+4.6%** (prior +1.8%, prior-prior +0.4%); Garden/Outdoor sub-line **+7.1%**. JPMorgan Chase SMB Spend Dashboard shows contractor-class B2B card spend **+6.3% YoY** (prior +3.4%).
- **Vs. consensus:** HD F1Q26 (qtr ending late April) sell-side consensus comp at **−1.2%** (source: Visible Alpha 5/9). Even with only ~3 of the 13 quarter weeks overlapping, the direction clearly cuts against the "late spring + weak housing turnover" bear narrative. Hurricane-rebuild base effects have largely rolled off — this acceleration looks like genuine demand recovery, not weather catch-up.
- **Trade / watch:** HD reports pre-market 5/19. Options-implied move ±4.2% (5/12 ATM straddle). Our base case: **comp beat 60–90bp, EPS beat 2–4%**. LOW (5/21) benefits in a narrower way; SHW depends on pro/DIY mix — paint sub-line only +2.9%, tepid.
- **Risks:** BAC sample skews higher-income; if Lowe's pro/DIY split shows DIY healing while Pro is flat, the upside narrows.

### 2. TP West freight +14% w/w — two-sided trade

- **Data:** SCFI Shanghai→US West Coast closed 5/9 at **USD 2,180/FEU**, **+14.2% w/w**, +22% MTD. Drewry WCI moves in the same direction at +6.8%. Marine Traffic + port-authority data: LA + Long Beach inbound TEU first 9 days of May **+11.1% YoY**, with empties returning less and laden share rising to 88% (April avg 84%).
- **Drivers:** (1) Expectation of a 90-day extension of the US-China reciprocal tariff carve-out around 5/8 (Reuters synthesis) is pulling forward shipments; (2) Maersk pulled 4 TP loops late April, tightening capacity at the margin; (3) Red Sea diversions persist, keeping Asia-Europe capacity diverted.
- **Cross-asset implications:**
  - **Bullish:** Carriers ZIM / MATX have the largest spot exposure (ZIM ~70% spot, prints 5/20). CSX / UNP intermodal units should turn positive YoY.
  - **Bearish:** US retailer freight COGS — TGT / COST / DG carry high import mix; their April contract resets are already locked, so any new spot exposure hits F2Q GM. TGT prints 5/21; consensus GM is −20bp YoY, but if spot doesn't ease by June there is downside risk to next-quarter guide.
- **Trade:** ZIM long / TGT short, β-adjusted; or single-leg ZIM 5/16-expiry weekly calls. Note ZIM print itself on 5/20 carries jump risk.
- **Risk:** A negative US-China tariff headline before 5/15 reverses the pull-forward logic; in June 2024 we saw a −9% w/w SCFI print after just such a shift.

### 3. Temu/Shein US traffic keeps fading — watch only

- **Data:** Similarweb w/e 5/10. Temu.com US visits **−9% YoY / −3% w/w**; Shein US **−12% YoY / −2% w/w**; Sensor Tower app downloads Temu −15%, Shein −18%. Controls: Amazon US flat, TikTok Shop GMV proxy (visits × conversion) +18%.
- **Read:** De minimis threshold revisions effective Feb 2026 already compressed Temu/Shein US unit economics; the new development is that traffic / engagement — not just pricing — is rolling over, suggesting frequency-of-use degradation, not just basket pressure.
- **Why not a formal pitch yet:** PDD only prints 5/22, and overseas revenue disclosure is coarse-grained; want one more week of data. META 2Q ad revenue impact from Temu/Shein pullback is already partly in numbers (see 4/24 call Q&A re: "Asian retailer ad spend"). Watching; will escalate to a formal short pitch next week if the trend extends.

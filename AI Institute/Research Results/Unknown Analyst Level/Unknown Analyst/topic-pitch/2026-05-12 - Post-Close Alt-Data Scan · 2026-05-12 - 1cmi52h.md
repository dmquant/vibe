## 1. Hyperscaler AI-infra hiring divergence (lead pitch)

### What the data shows
| Company | "AI infra / GPU / data center" JD trailing-4-week MoM | Drawdown from 12-wk high | Sell-side FY26 capex consensus vs. 4 wks ago |
|---------|--------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| MSFT    | -7% (4 straight negative weeks)                         | -18%                    | Upgraded +2.1%                                 |
| META    | -4% (3 straight negative weeks)                         | -12%                    | Upgraded +3.4%                                 |
| GOOG    | +9%                                                     | New high                | Upgraded +1.8%                                 |
| AMZN    | +11% (AWS self-built DC roles leading)                  | New high                | Upgraded +2.6%                                 |

Source: LinkUp + Revelio hiring panels (through 2026-05-09); compared to Visible Alpha consensus.

### Why it matters
- The sell side still treats the four hyperscalers as a single "AI capex" theme; their relative valuation spread is at a 5-year tight.
- After SemiAnalysis flagged Oberon/Athena yield issues in April, MSFT/META's merchant-GPU intake is plausibly entering a digestion window. Hiring freezes have historically led capex guides by 1–2 quarters — same pattern preceded META's 2022 Q4 "Year of Efficiency" pivot.
- Today's tape is already splitting (MSFT -1.8% / META -2.4% vs. GOOG +0.9% / AMZN +1.1%), but relative positioning is far from priced in: MSFT/GOOG 12-month relative return spread is still at the 80th historical percentile.

### What to do
- Pair trade candidate: long GOOG/AMZN vs. short MSFT/META looks more attractive. Watch event risk — MSFT investor day 5/22.
- This specific angle has not been on the whiteboard in the last 7 days. The 4/28 thread on hyperscaler capex centered on token economics, not on the hiring leading indicator.

Source: UnionPay + 3rd-party payments anonymized panel (5/1–5/10); GMV adjusted for May 1–10 calendar-day differential.

### Why it matters

- If this divergence persists into mid-May channel checks at H&M / Fast Retailing / L'Oréal / Estée Lauder, the risk of consensus 618 cuts is high.

### What to do
- Watch A-share / HK names: Chow Tai Fook (1929 HK), Hailan Home (600398 CH), Proya (603605 CH) versus China services/travel (Trip.com, H World).

## 3. GLP-1 new-patient starts turn negative MoM (observation phase)

### What the data shows
- IQVIA NPA + GoodRx panels: combined Wegovy + Zepbound new-patient starts were **-3% MoM in April** (YoY still +28%). First negative MoM print since September 2023.
- Refill rate held steady (~62%) — the issue is acquisition, not retention.
- Compounded GLP-1 (grey-channel pharmacy compounding) re-channeling back to branded after FDA's April reaffirmation has been slower than expected.

### Why it matters
- Sell-side FY26 consensus for LLY/NOVO still implies +30%+ YoY, which requires ≥+1.5% monthly new-patient growth.
- One month is not a thesis-changer (April 2025 also showed a -1% blip) — but a second negative print in May would materially shift the long-run penetration assumption in LLY's DCF.
- Today's tape (LLY -0.6% / NOVO -1.2%) shows no positioning response — market is not yet pricing this.

### What to do
- Add to weekly monitor. Ask `healthcare-analyst` whether this is seasonal (post-spring weight-loss peak) or structural (insurance prior-auth thresholds raised in April).

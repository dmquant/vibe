# Alternative Data Scan Report (2026-05-11)
## Lead Analyst: Alt-Data Analyst
## Anchor Date: 2026-05-11

### 1. Consumer Divergence: Credit Stress vs. "Resilience" Narrative
**Data Point:** Latest card tracking data from BofA and JPM shows US consumer spending growth slowed to 0.1% in the first week of May (Consensus: 0.3%).
**Anomaly Observation:** While headline retail sales appear flat, alternative data shows "minimum payment" rates among low-income cohorts have reached their highest levels since 2022.
**Conclusion:** Sell-side consensus maintains a "soft landing" view, but credit stress indicators suggest the bottom 40% of consumers have hit the edge of their savings buffers. Markets are underestimating the downside risk to discretionary consumption in H2.

### 2. E-commerce Geographic Pivot: "Geopolitical Evasion" from US to EU
**Data Point:** Temu downloads grew 11.4% MoM in May to 51.55 million, but US downloads were essentially flat.
**Anomaly Observation:** Ad spending for Temu in the EU (specifically France and the UK) surged by 40%, corresponding to a 60% YoY spike in EU sales.
**Conclusion:** Markets fear US regulatory pressure (removal of the de minimis exemption) will destroy PDD's valuation. Alt-data shows the platform has already successfully pivoted to Europe, a geographic defense capability not yet priced in.

### 3. Industrial Satellite Signals: The China Copper Smelting Paradox
**Data Point:** The Earth-i SAVANT satellite index shows Chinese copper smelting activity increased 11.8% YoY in May.
**Anomaly Observation:** Despite TC/RCs (treatment charges) hitting record lows and official announcements of production cuts, satellite monitoring shows three major smelters resumed operations in May.
**Conclusion:** High output levels suggest either higher-than-expected scrap utilization or state-mandated strategic stockpiling. This supply overhang materially diverges from the "smelter cuts lead to copper shortage" bull thesis.

### 4. Tech Engagement: ChatGPT Breaks Through "AI Fatigue"
**Data Point:** May web traffic reached 1.8 billion visits, a record high that breaks the horizontal trend seen since late 2023.
**Anomaly Observation:** Peak single-day traffic following the GPT-4o launch reached 80 million visits.
**Conclusion:** The argument that "AI tool usage has peaked" is debunked. Multimodal interaction is shifting AI from a "search substitute" to a "workflow plugin."

# Post-Close Alt-Data Scan Report (2026-05-05): Whiteboard-Worthy Divergences

## 1. Executive Summary
Post-close alternative data signals reveal significant "perception gaps" in three critical areas. Satellite imagery capturing a consumption rebound in China, higher-than-expected AI-related tech hiring, and a "K-shaped" spending deceleration in credit card data form a complex macro backdrop. While sell-side consensus remains anchored in a "broad slowdown" narrative, underlying data suggests structural Alpha opportunities.

## 2. High-Conviction Divergences

### A. China EV & Retail: The "Invisible" Satellite Rebound
*   **Observed Signal:** Satellite imagery shows parking lot fill rates at major Chinese retail centers and EV factories surged **82.6%** month-on-month between March and April 2026.
*   **Market Divergence:** Sell-side analysts remain generally bearish on Q2 China consumption. However, high-frequency monitoring from the Jilin-1 constellation shows foot traffic has recovered to over 95% of 2024 peaks.
*   **Thesis Impact:** EV exports and domestic penetration in lower-tier cities are moving faster than the structural bear case suggests.

### B. The AI Hiring Paradox: Job Postings vs. "AI Killer" Narrative
*   **Observed Signal:** Real-time data from Indeed and LinkedIn shows AI-related software engineering postings jumped **18%** in April, the largest rebound since the May 2025 lows.
*   **Market Divergence:** The consensus expects AI to aggressively displace junior tech roles. Instead, data reveals firms are in an "integration-intensive" phase, showing extreme hunger for talent capable of handling AI architecture.
*   **Thesis Impact:** Tech Capex and Opex may stay elevated in the near term due to a "war for talent," rather than the rapid cost-cutting the market is pricing in.

### C. Credit Card Spending: Mastercard’s "April Deceleration Line"
*   **Observed Signal:** Mastercard’s internal April transaction data shows a "notable deceleration" in payments volume, contrasting with earlier "resilience" narratives from Visa and Amex.
*   **Market Divergence:** Market confidence is still buoyed by decent March earnings reports, but high-frequency payment data warns of negative surprises in May and June.
*   **Thesis Impact:** "Debt fatigue" among lower-income cohorts has reached a tipping point (U.S. credit card debt at $1.28 trillion with rising delinquency rates).

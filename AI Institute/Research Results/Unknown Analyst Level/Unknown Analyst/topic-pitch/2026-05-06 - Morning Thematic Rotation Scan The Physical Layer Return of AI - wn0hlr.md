# Morning Thematic Rotation Scan: The "Physical Layer" Return of AI Infrastructure
**Date:** May 7, 2026
**Researcher:** Thematic Researcher

## 1. Executive Summary: The Paradigm Shift from "Compute" to "Physical Infrastructure"
In May 2026, global markets are undergoing a profound thematic rotation. Capital is shifting from the 2025 logic of "Chips and Software" toward the **Physical Infrastructure** required to sustain AI expansion—specifically **Grid Modernization, Power Generation, and Thermal Management**. This marks the transition of AI investment into a "Heavy Asset / Industrialization" phase.

## 2. Core Thematic Analysis

### A. The $830 Billion Arms Race: The Hyperscaler CapEx New Normal
*   **Spending Scale:** The top nine global Cloud Service Providers (CSPs) are projected to spend **$830 billion** in 2026, a 79% year-over-year increase.
*   **Capital Intensity:** Giants like Amazon (~$200B) and Microsoft (~$190B) are seeing CapEx-to-Revenue ratios hitting historical highs of 45-57%.
*   **Allocation:** Approximately 75% of this spending is directed specifically at AI-related hardware and facilities.

### B. The Power Bottleneck and the "Pipes & Power" Rotation
*   **Thesis:** Compute demand has pushed power grids to their limits. Capital is accelerating into "Grid-Edge" equipment and zero-carbon baseload energy.
*   **Key Tickers:** **Vistra Corp (VST)** and **Sempra Energy (SRE)** have emerged as bellwethers for the AI-Power trade.
*   **Nuclear Renaissance:** Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) like **Oklo (OKLO)** are gaining traction to meet the 24/7 power requirements of data center campuses.

### C. Thermal Management Inflection: The "Liquid-Only" Mandate
*   **Technical Inflection:** With the release of NVIDIA Blackwell-2 Ultra (B2U), liquid cooling has transitioned from an option to a **native design requirement**.
*   **Industry Standard:** Rack power densities are routinely breaching 100kW, rendering air cooling obsolete.
*   **Consolidation:** Industrial giants like **Eaton** and **Ecolab** are moving to own the "full stack" of thermal management through massive M&A (e.g., Boyd Thermal, CoolIT).

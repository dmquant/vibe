# Overnight Sentiment & Flow Scan: "Low Vol Bull" Under Extreme Greed

**Date:** May 8, 2026 (Based on overnight data from May 7, 2026)
**Analyst:** Sentiment Analyst

## 1. Executive Summary: Total Collapse of Defensive Hedging
Overnight market sentiment completed a dramatic pivot from "cautious hedging" to "unbridled optimism." The synchronized collapse of the VIX and the Put-Call ratio marks a stage of extreme greed. This "Low Vol Bull" regime is underpinned by blowout AI earnings, resilient labor data, and hopes for a geopolitical de-escalation.

## 3. Fund Flows & Spread Analysis
*   **Credit Markets:** High Yield (HY) OAS has compressed to **2.77%**, far below the long-term average of 5.19%. The risk premium for defaults is at extreme lows, leaving "thin cushions" for error.
*   **ETF Dynamics:** 
    *   **HYG (High Yield):** Recorded strong inflows of **$1.2 billion** as investors chase yield.
    *   **LQD (Inv Grade):** Matching inflows of **$1.2 billion**, suggesting some rotation into quality credit to balance risk.
    *   **TLT (Long Bond):** Continued outflows, reflecting a deep-seated "Higher for Longer" pricing with weak demand for duration hedges.
*   **Safe Havens:** DXY slipped below 98.0. The Swiss Franc (CHF) remains structurally resilient, but Yen (JPY) shorts are starting to cover. Gold retreated to $4,706, and Oil fell to $91.60 on peace hopes.

## 4. Key Debate: The Exhaustion of Downside Protection
The most striking anomaly is that the S&P 500 sits approximately 7.6% above its 50-day moving average (overbought), yet **put buying has collapsed to a five-year low**. This suggests the market is walking a tightrope without a safety net. This "sentiment alignment" is often a precursor to a sharp technical reversal.

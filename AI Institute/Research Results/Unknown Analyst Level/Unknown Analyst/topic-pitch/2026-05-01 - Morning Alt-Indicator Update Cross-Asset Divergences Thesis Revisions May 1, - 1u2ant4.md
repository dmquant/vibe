## Divergence Analysis & Sector Theses

### 1. China Labor Day: Traffic Dividend vs. Margin Pressure
Satellite imagery of Shanghai Hongqiao and Shenzhen North Railway Stations confirms passenger density has surpassed historical peaks. However, correlated credit card data shows that while transaction volume is up 15%, the average ticket size is down significantly. This "downgrade-driven prosperity" suggests mass brands (e.g., Luckin Coffee, Meituan) will capture earnings elasticity, while luxury players face SSSG (Same Store Sales Growth) revision risks.

### 2. Freight Rates: The Stealth Inflation Threat
The latest Drewry and FBX prints show a sudden jump after months of stabilization. Satellite-monitored berth delays at major Southeast Asian ports have hit 3-5 days. This may stem from recent regional weather disruptions and anticipated labor negotiations. With the market heavily priced for disinflation, this freight divergence could trigger a rebound in 10Y Treasury yields next week.

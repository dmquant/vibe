## 2. Style Rotation & Breadth Scan: Return to Value and Size
The most striking feature of today's midday session is the **dramatic repair in market breadth**.
- **Life Beyond the "Mag 7"**: While the Nasdaq is rallying, the Russell 2000 is outperforming it by 1.5x. This marks a shift from narrow AI-centric leadership to a broader cyclical and value-oriented base.
- **Breadth Improvement**: The Advance-Decline line has improved significantly, suggesting this is more than just short-covering; capital is actively revisiting undervalued small and mid-cap assets.

## 3. Regime Break: The Price-Flow Paradox
We are observing a "whiteboard-worthy" regime shift: **Prices are hitting relief highs while physical capital is exiting.**
- **The "Rally of Disbelief"**: Although the tariff reduction (from 145% to 30%) ignited the tape, institutional investors have funneled $54B into bond funds in the last 24 hours while pulling $17B from equities.
- **Hedged Longing**: Covered-call and derivative-income ETFs (e.g., JEPI) continue to see steady inflows. This indicates that while investors feel "forced" to participate in the rally, they are doing so via complex derivatives to cap downside risk.

## 4. Conclusion & Actionable Thesis
The current surge is driven by **"Forced Buying" (Panic Buying)**. The extremely low P/C ratio (0.46) warns that the market is at the edge of overbought territory. Any reversal in trade policy could trigger a violent spike in volatility.

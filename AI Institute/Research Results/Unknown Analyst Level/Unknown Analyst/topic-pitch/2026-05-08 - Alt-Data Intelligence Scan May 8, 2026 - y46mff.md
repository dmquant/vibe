## 1. Retail: The Sentiment-Traffic Divergence Trap

### Alt-Data Signal Scan
*   **Geolocation (Placer.ai):** As of May 6, foot traffic for Home Improvement giants (Home Depot, Lowe's) grew **+1.9% to +2.0% YoY**. This marks the first positive quarterly turn since 2023.
*   **Satellite Imagery (Parking Lot Alpha):** Monitoring of parking lot saturation for top-50 U.S. retailers shows that despite record-low University of Michigan Consumer Sentiment, actual transaction volume surged **+43%** over the weekend.
*   **Digital Lead Indicators:** Douyin (TikTok) group purchase data for the May Day holiday showed an **86% surge** in hotel and retail conversions YoY.

### Divergence Analysis
The current market narrative is fixated on "high rates suppressing consumption," leading to irrational sell-offs in the retail sector. However, alt-data reveals a sharp **"Behavior over Intent"** divergence: while consumers express pessimism in surveys, their physical behavior shows increased spending in DIY and experiential categories.

### Tomorrow's Debate
Has the retail sector completed its "sentiment bottom"? Foot traffic recovery typically leads financial reports by 4-6 weeks, suggesting significant earnings-per-share (EPS) surprises in the upcoming cycle.

### Action Items
*   **Long Bias:** Focus on Home Improvement and Outlet mall operators, where traffic growth is strongest (+10.7%).
*   **Monitor:** Conversion rates from social media "discovery" to offline "dwell time."

## 2. Semiconductors: The "Arizona Yield Miracle"

### Alt-Data Signal Scan
*   **Construction Monitoring (BlackSky):** High-frequency satellite imagery confirms that TSMC's Fab 21 Phase 1 in Arizona is fully operational.
*   **Thermal/Yield Signals:** Internal supply chain pings confirm that Blackwell GPU yields at Arizona Fab 21 have achieved **parity** with Taiwan-based facilities.
*   **Logistics Bottlenecks:** Delivery lead times for CoWoS packaging and HBM4 memory have stabilized at **20 weeks** as of mid-May.

### Divergence Analysis
The market remains cautious on Nvidia due to "AI peak" exhaustion and geopolitical risks. However, alt-data proves that the **physical de-risking of the supply chain is far ahead of schedule**. Arizona yield parity means Nvidia's Blackwell ramp is no longer solely dependent on Taiwan, a major signal for valuation re-rating.

### Tomorrow's Debate
Is the "Arizona Premium" priced in? Meanwhile, SpaceX’s announcement of a $55B "Terafab" in Texas poses a long-term threat to the Nvidia/TSMC duopoly. Is the era of fabless dominance ending?

### Action Items
*   **Coordinate:** Recommend `tmt-analyst` re-evaluates Nvidia's Q3 delivery estimates.

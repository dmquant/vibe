# Alt-Data Daily Update: Cross-Asset Divergences & High-Frequency Signals (2026-05-13)

## 1. The Credit "Bridge" Fracture: High-frequency Delinquency Spikes in Early May
*   **Alt-Indicator:** In the first 10 days of May, delinquency rates (30+ days) on small-to-mid-sized fintech lending platforms rose by 180bp MoM, significantly higher than the historical seasonal average (40bp).
*   **Core Observation:** While consensus remains fixated on nominal retail sales growth, high-frequency card data (especially debit and low-tier credit accounts) reveals that the bottom 40% income bracket is increasingly using credit as a "financial bridge" for essential spending.
*   **Cross-Asset Impact:** This signal foreshadows severe bifurcation in the retail sector by the end of Q2. Recommendation: Long high-end luxury/specialty retailers; exercise caution on mid-market discount stores reliant on subprime consumer credit.

## 2. The "Ghost" Travel Boom: Divergence Between Booking Traffic and Checkout Completion
*   **Alt-Indicator:** Search traffic for major OTA platforms (e.g., Expedia, Booking) grew by 15% YoY over the past three weeks, yet click-through rates to the final payment/checkout pages dropped by 22%.
*   **Core Observation:** Consumers are "window shopping" for summer vacations. This "hot search, cold transaction" divergence suggests that high ancillary costs (insurance, service fees) have finally hit the ceiling of consumer tolerance.
*   **Cross-Asset Impact:** Markets are still pricing airline and hotel stocks based on strong traffic numbers. We anticipate significant downward earnings revisions for OTAs in H2, presenting a shorting opportunity.

## 3. Freight Rate Decoupling: Near-shoring Signals in Regional vs. Long-haul Indices
*   **Alt-Indicator:** A rare divergence has emerged between the Harpex (charter index) and Drewry (freight rates). Regional short-sea rates are up 12% due to congestion, while trans-Pacific long-haul rates have softened by 8%.
*   **Core Observation:** Satellite monitoring shows berth utilization at regional ports in Mexico and Southeast Asia at historical highs. This confirms that "near-shoring" has shifted from rhetoric to a material realignment of global freight flows.
*   **Cross-Asset Impact:** The scale advantage of global shipping giants is eroding. Bullish on logistics entities focused on regional trade lanes; bearish on multinational freight forwarders reliant on US-China long-haul volumes.

# Pre-Market Sentiment Scan · 2026-05-14

## 1. Scan Coverage
- **Window**: Asia close 2026-05-13 → US pre-open 2026-05-14, including post-print reactions to the 5/12 US CPI and 5/13 PPI.
- **Dimensions**: US/A-share/HK sentiment (VIX, F&G), derivatives positioning (SPX gamma/skew, retail-options share), cross-market flows (northbound, margin balance, ETFs), cross-asset linkages (oil-gold-USD-bonds-equities), event catalysts (CPI/PPI, Fed-chair handover, Iran).
- **Sources**: CNBC, Yahoo Finance, World Gold Council, Citadel Securities Flows & Fundamentals, Cboe / CNN F&G, Sina Finance, EastMoney, JPMorgan AM Daily Guide.

## 2. Strongest Signals Observed
- **Sentiment-vs-fundamentals divergence**: CNN F&G = 66 (greed), VIX closed 17.99, the 10Y hit a YTD high, yet Fed Funds futures now show a 97% chance of "no cut this year" and 36% odds of an outright hike (CNBC, 5/13). Sentiment and rates pricing are aligned — but neither matches the equity tape.
- **Rare cross-asset co-rally**: gold, oil (WTI > $100, Brent > $107), USD and US equities all bid simultaneously; bond-equity correlation grinding higher (World Gold Council 2026-02 / 2026-05; SSGA Monthly Gold Monitor May). The classic 60/40 hedge is failing again.
- **AI capex narrative fork**: 5/13 saw QCOM −11.46% (worst single day since Mar 2020) with Micron/Intel in sympathy, while Industrial Fulian (FII) limit-up'd to a CNY 1.4T market cap on early CPO-rack delivery to NVIDIA (Sina Tech, 5/13).
- **A-shares break the 2015 high**: 5/13 ChiNext +2.6% to a record 4038, Wind All-A closed 7267.97 — above the 2015-06-12 ATH; turnover > CNY 3T for six straight sessions, northbound positive for nine days (5/11 alone +CNY 12.66bn).
- **Retail options re-engaging**: Citadel data shows the retail index/ETF-vs-single-stock options ratio normalising from 1.5× → 1.3×, leaving room for a single-stock options surge (Citadel Flows & Fundamentals, May). SPX 1M 25Δ skew is back at its one-year mean and "upcrash" call demand is firm — upside is already being paid for.

## 3. Topics Worth Promoting to the Whiteboard (3)

### Topic A — When does the rates repricing finally crack the equity calm?
- **Suggested primary analyst**: `chief-strategist`
- **Kickoff question**: PPI +1.4% on 5/13 has pushed year-end hike odds to 36% and 10Y to a YTD high, yet SPX barely moved, VIX is 17.99 and F&G sits in greed. Is a hard-landing already discounted, or is the rates shock simply not transmitting yet? If 10Y breaks its prior high, does multiple compression (growth) crack first, or do credit spreads (leverage) go first?
- **Why a thread, not a single note**: needs strategy + macro + derivatives at the same table; the resolution window is short — Warsh takes the chair Friday and the next FOMC follows.

### Topic B — Sustainability of the A-share break above 2015: real new money or leverage + sentiment overheat?
- **Suggested primary analyst**: `ashare-strategist`
- **Kickoff question**: Wind All-A has cleared its 2015-06-12 high, six sessions above CNY 3T turnover, nine days of net northbound. But margin requirements were hiked to 100% in January and 2026 margin net inflows are forecast at only CNY 450bn. Is the marginal bid mostly ETFs or leverage? Mirror-mapping turnover, leverage and sector concentration against 2015 at this same index level — how much room is left?
- **Why a thread, not a single note**: A-share strategy + macro (CNY, foreign flows) + sentiment can produce diametrically opposite calls; perfect debate material.

### Topic C — AI capex narrative fork: US chips sold off vs. China CPO/optics limit-up
- **Suggested primary analyst**: `thematic-researcher`
- **Kickoff question**: QCOM −11% in a session, Micron/Intel in sympathy, while FII limit-up'd on early CPO delivery to NVIDIA. Is the AI cycle de-syncing geographically (China hardware accelerating, US demand peaking), or is the same order book being priced at different cadences on each side? If the former, the China-AI relative premium should keep expanding; if the latter, a US-chip bounce will pull liquidity out of A-shares within two weeks.
- **Why a thread, not a single note**: thematic + US strategy + A-share strategy must reconcile across the same supply chain; one-side coverage will miss the marginal bid/offer on the other.

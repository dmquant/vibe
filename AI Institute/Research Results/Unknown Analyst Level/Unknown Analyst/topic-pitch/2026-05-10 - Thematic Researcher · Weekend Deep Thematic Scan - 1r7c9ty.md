## Pitch 1 · AI Inference Economics — Capex Beneficiaries vs. Application-Layer Margins

### Structural observation

Two opposing forces have been accelerating in parallel over the past 60 days:

1. **Downside pressure (application layer)**: Open-weight frontier models (DeepSeek V4, Qwen3-Max, Llama 4 family) now sit in the 90–95% band of closed-frontier performance on MMLU-Pro / SWE-bench, while their API prices have been compressed 70–85%. The sustainability of an "AI premium" on SaaS application-layer pricing is being questioned — customers are starting to demand token-cost pass-through or self-hosting.
2. **Upside support (infrastructure layer)**: Sovereign AI capex (Mistral campus in France, Telekom AI factory in Germany, HUMAIN in Saudi, G42 in UAE) is bidding heavily in year two of the EU AI Act. Combined with US hyperscaler 2026 capex upgrades, the consensus on hyperscaler-four total capex has reportedly drifted from ~$380B at the start of the year to ~$450B (**total to be cross-checked by china-macro / global-macro**).

### Why this is a weekend item

- Last week (5/5–5/9), the **AI software application basket (PLTR, CRM, NOW, ADBE) decoupled from the semis/power basket (NVDA, AVGO, VST, CEG)** — a rare break since early 2024. These two have moved together for nearly two years; the rupture needs an explicit cause.
- The same divergence is showing on A-shares: AI-application names (Kingsoft Office, iFlytek, Foxit) are diverging from compute-supply names (Innolight, Eoptolink, Cambricon) — global synchrony of the "AI Phase 2" narrative is the question.
- Regulatory layer: FTC hearings on AI "acqui-hires" are reportedly being scheduled, and EU code-model transparency obligations come into force in August. Before Monday open we should classify our application-layer holdings on two axes: sovereign-AI exposure and open-source substitution risk.

### Key debate questions

- Is application-layer margin compression cyclical (a short-run token price war) or structural (open weights end the "model-as-moat" narrative)?
- As hyperscaler capex's marginal customer shifts from big tech to sovereign funds and telcos, is this a scale-up or a quality-down (collection terms, geopolitical risk)?
- A-share read-across: can the CATL–CEC datacenter power package, Sciyon (300262) AIDC, Luxshare CPO continue to track North-American capex upgrades, or has the beta already played out?

## Pitch 2 · Quantum Error-Correction Commercialization — True Inflection or ICO-Style Valuation

### Structural observation

Over the past 12 months several labs (Google's Willow successor work, IBM Heron R3, Atom Computing neutral atoms, QuEra, PsiQuantum) have produced clustered milestones in **logical-qubit fidelity above the threshold** (surface-code distance, neutral-atom array scaling). Pure-play public quantum names (IONQ, RGTI, QBTS, ARQQ) have **repriced quickly** through Q1–Q2 2026 (**precise 30-day moves to be pulled by the quant team**), while embedded quantum businesses inside scaled tech (IBM, Google, AWS Braket) remain implicitly priced at zero.

### Why this is a weekend item

- **Catalyst calendar is dense**: ISC High Performance (Hamburg, May), ISCA (June), APS March follow-ups — the research-side cadence is starting to look like ASCO/ASH-style "conference-driven" trading characteristic of biotech.
- **Enterprise procurement signal**: scattered reports last week (5/5–5/9) of **financial / pharma PoCs upgrading into multi-year contracts** (Goldman quantum risk modelling; Roche quantum chemistry PoC — **needs separate verification by healthcare-analyst / financials-analyst**). If real, this is the first step from research collaboration to commercial procurement.
- **Valuation dispersion**: pure-play quantum names have rallied sharply over six months on small float and high short-borrow cost; technicals are dwarfing fundamentals. Whether this reruns the 2017–2018 blockchain/ICO arc deserves a formal debate.
- China leg: GuoDun Quantum (688027.SH), China Telecom Quantum, Origin Quantum (private), KEDA — A-share read-across is limited but sentiment transmission is fast.

### Key debate questions

- The current consensus timeline to "useful fault-tolerance" (~thousand logical qubits) is 2028–2030; what shifts in pessimistic / optimistic scenarios do to pure-play valuations?
- "Embedded quantum" vs. "pure-play": how should we allocate between revenue-less IONQ/RGTI and IBM/Google whose quantum effort is amortised against cloud revenue?
- Tail-risk scenario: if a single pure-play breaks on a delayed milestone in 2H, does the whole basket compress in deep-tech sympathy (cf. the 2022 SPAC-quantum unwind)?

## Pitch 3 · GLP-1 Second-Generation Reshuffling — Premium Compression and Obesity-Adjacent Spillover

### Structural observation

The obesity drug space has moved through three valuation waves in 24 months: (1) 2023–2024 Wegovy/Zepbound launch surge; (2) 2025 expansion into CV / MASH / sleep apnea labels; and (3) the current triple squeeze:

1. **Oral GLP-1 acceleration**: Lilly's orforglipron, Novo's high-dose oral semaglutide, and restarted Pfizer/Roche oral GIP/GLP-1 programs all read out Phase III into 2026 H2–2027 H1.
2. **Amylin combos**: Novo's CagriSema and Lilly's retatrutide tri-agonist are showing >25% weight loss but mixed tolerability — Phase II/III readouts redefine the "gold standard."
3. **Medicare price-negotiation expansion**: CMS will publish the next IRA negotiation list **by 30 June 2026**. The probability of semaglutide / tirzepatide inclusion is non-trivial (**current consensus probability to be confirmed by healthcare-analyst**). If included, mandatory price cuts hit from 2027.

### Why this is a weekend item

- Style break last week (5/5–5/9): Lilly and Novo underperformed the broader market on both sides of the Atlantic, while **second-tier obesity-pipeline names (Altimmune, Structure Therapeutics, Viking) attracted incremental flows** — rotation rather than sector capitulation. Internal divergence: cyclical or structural?
- Obesity-adjacent chains have unresolved long-running cannibalization-vs.-expansion debates (sleep apnea: ResMed, Inspire; MASH: Madrigal, Akero; CV) — valuation gaps remain wide.
- A-share / HK biotech read-across: Innovent (mazdutide approved), Huadong (liraglutide biosimilar), Hengrui (HRS9531), Brightgene — valuations reflect launch success but the gap to the second-generation race deserves reassessment.

### Key debate questions

- Should Novo/Lilly forward P/E be priced as a single-product cycle (1990s Lipitor analogue) or platform-style (ASML analogue)? The former implies rapid de-rating after 2027–2028.
- Combining oral GLP-1 + amylin + Medicare negotiation, does the 2030 global GLP-1 TAM (consensus ~$130–150B) revise up or down? The direction sets the long/short for the entire space.
- Is the gap between A-share / HK biotech and global peers in next-gen GLP-1 one year, two, or three? This determines whether Innovent / Hengrui can converge on global multiples.

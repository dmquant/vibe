# Thematic Rotation Morning Scan: Cross-Sector Regime Shifts

**Date anchor**: `2026-05-14` from `date +%Y-%m-%d`. Every reference to today, yesterday, overnight, or next week is resolved against this date; the latest verifiable market window is the U.S. `2026-05-13` close and pre-open/overnight context.

## 1. Prioritized Conclusions

**Only one theme clears the bar for next-week positioning: the AI infrastructure trade remains intact, but the market is repricing the beneficiary chain.** The overnight signal is not just “tech up, everything else down.” It is a split inside the AI-capex complex: semiconductors, robotics/AI, quantum beta, and space beta followed the Nasdaq higher, while electrical equipment and thermal/grid enablers held up; regulated utilities, nuclear/uranium proxies, and some power producers were hit by the long-yield and oil-driven inflation shock. Next week, “AI power demand” should not be bought indiscriminately through utilities. Exposure should be concentrated in pricing-power segments: equipment, cooling, grid services, and AI hardware.

**Rejected themes**: biotech, Web3, pure space economy, and pure quantum do not yet show enough evidence to change next-week positioning. Today they look more like risk appetite or Nasdaq beta extensions than independent regime shifts.

## 4. Qualified Pitch and Follow-Up Items

### Pitch 1: AI Power Is Rotating From “Buy Every Electricity Beneficiary” to “Buy Bottleneck Equipment, Avoid Utility Duration”

**Conclusion**: Keep an overweight to AI infrastructure next week, but rotate away from regulated utilities, nuclear/uranium crowding proxies, and dividend duration into semiconductors, server power/cooling, electrical equipment, and grid engineering. AI power demand is real, but inflation and higher 10Y yields are changing which companies can convert that demand into equity alpha.

**Supporting evidence**:

| Evidence | Why it is not one-day noise | Investment implication | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| U.S. energy utility capex is forecast near $1.3T for 2026-2030, with data centers a key load driver | This is a multi-year capex cycle, not a one-day market explanation | Equipment and engineering chains have more direct exposure | [S6] |
| Virginia data-center utility power demand is forecast to rise 27.7% in 2026 to 16.6 GW | AI load is still expanding in concentrated grid regions | Regional bottlenecks support transmission, backup power, and cooling | [S7] |
| PJM is considering market reform to address the risk that data-center load outstrips supply | One of the largest grids is entering a rule-change phase | Electricity scarcity does not automatically make all utility equities winners | [S8] |
| The data-center electrical-equipment market is projected to grow from roughly $20B to $65B by 2030; U.S. data-center capacity from about 24 GW to 100 GW | Equipment bottlenecks have order-cycle and pricing-power characteristics | VRT-, ETN-, and PWR-type exposure should lead | [S9] |

# Overnight Thematic Scan: The Paradigm Shift from "Pure Compute" to the "Physical Layer" (2026-05-13)

## 1. Overnight Capital Flows: From "Compute Hegemony" to "Physical Constraints"
U.S. markets on May 12, 2026, exhibited a classic zero-sum rotation. While the Nasdaq Composite fell 1.11%, the Dow Jones Industrial Average managed a 0.12% gain. This divergence signals a profound shift in market logic: the valuation center of gravity for AI is migrating from "Models/Software" to the "Physical Infrastructure" required to sustain them.

- **Logic Shift**: Sticky inflation data forced a repricing of interest rate expectations, squeezing high-multiple AI software names.
- **Capital Direction**: Investors are rotating through the "revolving door" into Industrials and Utilities, targeting the non-negotiable physical bottlenecks of the AI revolution.

## 3. Cross-Sector Convergence: The "Trinity" of AI Capex
A new investment consensus is forming: **AI Capex = Power Demand + Thermal Management + Network Optimization.**

- **The Nuclear Renaissance**: A surge in Small Modular Reactor (SMR) offtake agreements reflects hyperscalers' desperate need for 24/7 carbon-free baseload power.
- **Thermal Management as a Necessity**: As rack densities climb from 15kW to 150kW+, air cooling has reached its limit. Thermal providers like Vertiv have transitioned from "ancillary" to "critical" assets.
- **Connectivity Upgrades**: Strength in Lumentum (LITE) suggests a comprehensive shift to 800G/1.6T modules within data centers to handle AI cluster throughput.

## 4. Regime Shift Signals & Strategy for Next Week
**Core Signal: The Rise of "Community Resistance" (The Social Bottleneck).**
Increasing public pushback regarding water consumption, noise, and grid strain is emerging in key hubs. This "social bottleneck" will fundamentally alter site-selection strategies, awarding a valuation premium to regions with pre-existing power allocations and natural cooling advantages (e.g., the Nordic "Neocloud" market).

**Strategic Positioning for Next Week:**
- **Overweight**: Utilities (with Nuclear exposure), Grid optimization equipment, and Thermal/Liquid cooling providers.
- **Underweight**: Unprofitable AI software, edge computing without infrastructure backing.
- **Watch**: Stabilization in Memory (WDC, MU) as a secondary signal of hardware cycle bottoming.

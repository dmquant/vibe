# Alt-Data Scan Report: Post-Close Analysis (May 4, 2026)

## Executive Summary
The market is currently navigating a critical transition from "AI Hype" to "AI Competition." Alternative data signals reveal three material divergences from sell-side consensus: a "retention nightmare" in AI applications, an "engagement gap" for Meta, and a resilient consumer rebound in the dining sector under a K-shaped recovery.

---

## 1. The "Retention Nightmare" in AI Subscriptions

*   **Observation:** According to the RevenueCat 2026 Annual Report and real-time App Store monitoring, while AI-integrated apps have seen a 41% increase in Average Revenue Per User (ARPU), their 6-month retention rates are 36% lower than traditional utility apps.
*   **Divergence:** Sell-side analysts are currently overly focused on short-term subscription revenue growth driven by "AI enablement," ignoring the "cliff-edge" churn after trial periods end. Consensus assumes AI integration will enhance long-term stickiness; the data suggests they are behaving like one-off tools.
*   **Impact:** Mid-cap SaaS and vertical AI sectors may face a 20%-30% valuation correction as the market hasn't yet priced in the contradiction between high Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) and low retention.
*   **Action:** Initiate weekly monitoring of usage frequency for Adobe (ADBE) and Microsoft (MSFT) Consumer Copilot, focusing on "Paid-to-Retained" metrics.

## 2. Meta (META): The Hidden "Engagement Gap"

*   **Observation:** Satellite ground station traffic and internet monitoring indicated a sequential decline in Daily Active Users (DAUs) in March—the first in the company's history. This trend was pre-signaled by internet disruption data in Iran and Russia prior to the earnings release.
*   **Divergence:** Despite an EPS beat ($10.44 vs. $6.67), the market initially missed the peaking engagement signal. The sell-side attributed the post-earnings 10% drop to weak guidance, but the underlying cause is the "user growth ceiling" revealed by alt-data.
*   **Impact:** The investment logic for social media is shifting from "scale growth" to "monetization per minute." Meta's valuation anchor needs to transition from a growth stock to a value stock.
*   **Action:** Monitor real-time traffic share for Instagram Reels and WhatsApp Channels in Southeast Asian markets.

## 3. Dining Rebound Under K-Shaped Recovery: SBUX & CMG

*   **Observation:** Placer.ai foot traffic data and in-store Wi-Fi connection metrics show that Starbucks (SBUX) and Chipotle (CMG) saw a significant volume recovery starting in April (+4.4%), far exceeding the consensus estimate of 3.7%.
*   **Divergence:** The sell-side was previously pessimistic about the dining sector due to inflation and weak spending by lower-income cohorts. Alt-data accurately captured the resilience of middle-to-high-income groups in "attainable luxury" consumption.
*   **Impact:** Leading dining stocks are poised for a short-covering rally driven by "better-than-expected" transaction volumes.
*   **Action:** Track high-end consumer credit data and real-time queue data in major commercial hubs.

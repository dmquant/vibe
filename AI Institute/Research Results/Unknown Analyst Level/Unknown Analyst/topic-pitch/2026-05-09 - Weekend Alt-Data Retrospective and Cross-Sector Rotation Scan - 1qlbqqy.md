## 3. Monday Pitch Cards (Up to 3)

### Pitch 1: Fuel Costs Are Squeezing Consumer and Freight Margins

**Conclusion:** The “strong consumption” frame should be rewritten as “strong nominal consumption, weaker discretionary volume, and cost reinflation.” Redbook’s **+7.8%** and BofA total card spending at **+4.3%** look strong, but BofA shows gasoline is a major driver: gasoline spending was **+16.5%** MoM, while ex-gas spending was **+3.6%** YoY. EIA simultaneously shows gasoline and distillate stocks still below seasonal norms, while DAT spot rates and load-to-truck ratios are rising.

**Why this is thesis-worthy for Monday:**
- For retail, restaurants, airlines, and parcel/trucking, alt-data has shifted from “demand is strong” to “costs are deteriorating first.”
- For refiners and energy logistics, inventories and demand still support cracks and cash flow, but high pump prices will feed back into consumption.
- This is not a standalone energy topic; it cuts across consumption, transport, energy, and rate expectations.

**Deep-dive triggers:**
- Distillate inventories are **11%** below the five-year average.
- DAT reefer LTR rose to **15.0** and dry van LTR to **8.7**.
- Flatbed linehaul is only **$0.05** below the 2022 record.

### Pitch 2: Physical Goods Are Reaccelerating; Start With Rails, Materials, and Industrial Supply Chains

**Conclusion:** Rail and intermodal do not look like early-recession data. AAR U.S. total traffic was **+3.9%** YoY, intermodal **+3.9%**, metallic ores and metals **+20.6%**, grain **+17.6%**, and petroleum/products **+12.8%**. InTek shows U.S. intermodal volume positive YoY for the first time in 2026 and its spot index up for **5 consecutive weeks**.

**Why this is thesis-worthy for Monday:**
- Traditional earnings and PMIs may still lag, while rail/intermodal already show restocking, metals, and energy flows improving.
- If diesel approaches record highs, rail’s cost advantage over trucking becomes a rotation signal.
- This supports revenue resilience in materials, rails, and equipment rental, but hurts downstream retail and building-products margins.

**Deep-dive triggers:**
- U.S. intermodal volume turned positive YoY for the first time in 2026.
- AAR metals-related rail volume was **+20.6%** YoY.
- Diesel was **$5.640**, only **$0.170** below the record.

### Pitch 3: Satellite Constraints Are Starting to Challenge the AI Infrastructure Capex Narrative

**Conclusion:** Demand-side app data still supports AI monetization: Sensor Tower shows non-game app IAP **+21%** YoY in 2025, with ChatGPT the third-highest-grossing app of 2025. But supply-side satellite data points to uneven physical delivery: SynMax estimates about **40%** of U.S. data centers planned for 2026 completion face delay risk, and the OpenAI/Oracle-linked Texas **1.4GW** project appears behind schedule in imagery.

**Why this is thesis-worthy for Monday:**
- The AI rotation may shift from “GPU and cloud revenue” toward “power access, transformers, field construction, permitting, and utility bottlenecks.”
- If data centers slip, near-term GPU shipments and revenue recognition can undershoot the order-book narrative; electrical equipment and utility queue value can still strengthen.
- Satellite imagery and company commentary conflict, so this needs strategy, TMT, utilities, and industrials in the room.

**Deep-dive triggers:**
- **40%** project delay risk.
- One Texas project is **1.4GW** and **10 buildings**, but only **6 plots** are cleared and **1 plot** shows development.
- Non-game app IAP was **+21%** YoY, suggesting demand is less of a problem than delivery.

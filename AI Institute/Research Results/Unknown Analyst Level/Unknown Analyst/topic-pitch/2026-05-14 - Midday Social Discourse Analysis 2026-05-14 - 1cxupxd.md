## 2. Topic Pitches

### A. AI Compute: Shifting from "Growth" to "Commodity Logic"
*   **Narrative:** CME Group’s plan to launch the first Compute Futures contracts in 2026 is catalyzing a new consensus: compute is becoming a standardized commodity like crude oil or electricity.
*   **Contention:** The "AI Bull is Drunk" warning from prominent strategists has triggered massive turnover in retail-heavy CPO names. Investors are rotating from pure hardware plays into infrastructure names with "Power-Compute" synergies (e.g., DaTang Power’s green energy premium).
*   **Conclusion:** Pure hardware valuation has hit a ceiling; the market is autonomously reconstructing the pricing model for compute via social discourse.

### B. Grassroots Revival of the "Chemical Cycle Bottoming" Thesis
*   **Narrative:** Geopolitical disruptions causing overseas supply exits combined with domestic supply-side optimization under energy-control policies. The chemical sector is bucking the trend, with 3 chemical stocks hitting the Top 10 trending list on Xueqiu.
*   **Contention:** While institutions remain skeptical of a full cycle reversal, retail and hot money are betting on "Price Hikes + Destocking." In sub-sectors like SiC and semiconductor chemicals, retail is attempting to replicate the 2021 "Coal-Polysilicon" super-cycle narrative.

### C. Sentiment Reshaping around "Trump’s 2026 China Visit"
*   **Narrative:** During the May 13-15 summit, rumors of easing tech restrictions are rampant. Alibaba's FY2026 revenue milestone (surpassing 1T) and breakthroughs in Pingtouge (T-Head) GPU localization have made it a focal point for the "Nationalism + Valuation Recovery" narrative.
*   **Conclusion:** Geopolitical premiums are shifting from "Risk Aversion" to "Betting on Improvement," explaining why weighted ADRs and aerospace/defense sectors remain resilient despite the broader market dip.

# Alt-Data Scan Report: May 7, 2026 Post-Close

**Date:** 2026-05-07
**Analyst:** Alt-Data Analyst
**Executive Summary:** Markets rallied broadly today, with the S&P 500 and Nasdaq hitting all-time highs. However, alternative data reveals a stark divergence between Intel's construction activity in Ohio and current market euphoria. Additionally, a "Great Credit Cooldown" is emerging as US debit spending growth outpaces credit for the first time in four years.

---

## 1. Intel (INTC) Ohio Site: A "Ghost Town" on Satellite Imagery

### **Signal**
Analysis of high-frequency satellite imagery from BlackSky Gen-3 in May reveals a 65% quarter-over-quarter decline in ground activity at Intel’s "Silicon Heartland" site in Ohio. Parking lot utilization is at a mere 12%, and tower cranes remain stationary. This corroborates rumors that production timelines have quietly slipped to 2030.

### **Divergence**
*   **Price Action:** INTC closed up 4.5% today (~$112), driven by rumors of an Apple foundry partnership on the 18A node. Market sentiment is extremely overbought (RSI > 84).
*   **Valuation Gap:** Buy-side analysts have baked in significant capacity expansion for 2026-2027. The satellite-detected stall suggests major risks to depreciation and revenue-per-fab assumptions in consensus models.

### **Market Context**
While the stock surged on Apple speculation, physical site progress indicates a significant lag behind the corporate narrative.

### **Follow-up**
Requesting TMT Analyst to stress-test Intel’s CapEx efficiency and 2026 guidance.

---

## 2. US Consumer: The "Great Credit Cooldown" & Rise of Debit

### **Signal**
Real-time transaction data indicates that for the first time in four years, **debit card spending growth (6.57%) has outpaced credit card growth (5.65%)**. Furthermore, VantageScore’s CreditGauge shows credit utilization falling to 21.1% while 90+ day delinquencies have climbed to 2.53%.

### **Divergence**
*   **Consensus:** Bank CEOs and the White House emphasized consumer "resilience" today, pointing to April’s 1.7% retail sales beat.
*   **The Reality:** Alt-data shows retail growth was largely driven by a 15.5% spike in gasoline prices. Excluding energy, underlying purchasing power is being exhausted by 24% APRs, forcing consumers into a "pay-with-what-you-have" debit mode.

### **Market Context**
The market remains in a "Goldilocks" mindset, ignoring credit exhaustion among the bottom 80% of households.

### **Follow-up**
Consumer Analyst should evaluate downside risks for low-to-mid tier retailers for H2 2026.

---

## 3. Samsung (SSNLF) Taylor Fab: Execution Verified via Satellite

### **Signal**
In contrast to Intel, satellite imagery confirms that Samsung’s Taylor, Texas facility has commenced limited operations as of February 2026. Thermal signatures show exhaust systems running 24/7, and shuttle bus frequency indicates three-shift labor rotation.

### **Divergence**
*   **Market Confirmation:** Samsung joined the "Trillion-Dollar Club" today, becoming only the second Asian chipmaker to reach the milestone.
*   **Confidence Lead:** Satellite data provides physical verification of capacity roughly 3 months ahead of official quarterly reports, justifying the record-high valuation.

### **Market Context**
Samsung’s ascent to a $1T valuation is supported by solid physical evidence of execution on US soil.

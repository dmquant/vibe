# Overnight Alt-Data Scan Report (2026-05-11)

## 1. Macro/Sector Context (Overnight Tape)
Overnight focus centers on the drastic shift in the AI cost curve and the resilience of Chinese industrial data. While sell-side research remains cautious about global macro growth, alternative data (satellite, app downloads, power consumption) reveals distinct structural divergences.

## 2. Key Alt-Data Divergences (Whiteboard-Worthy)

### PITCH 1: DeepSeek V4 "Jevons Paradox" — Crashing Inference Costs Triggering Compute Demand Surges
*   **Signal:** Over the past 48 hours following the launch of **DeepSeek V4** (featuring MLA architecture upgrades), API call volumes have spiked 10x, while per-token inference costs have dropped by approximately 50%.
*   **Divergence:** Sell-side analysts are broadly concerned that drastic cuts in inference costs will be margin-negative for cloud providers and chipmakers. However, alt-data—specifically enterprise app integration logs and data center power monitoring—indicates that the lower cost threshold is driving an exponential increase in the frequency of enterprise AI integration.
*   **Conclusion:** This is a classic "Jevons Paradox" scenario: increased efficiency is paradoxically leading to a surge in total demand (especially for compute infrastructure) rather than a reduction.

### PITCH 2: China’s High-Tech Manufacturing Decoupling from the Property Macro Narrative
*   **Signal:** Satellite thermal monitoring shows that EV and battery production clusters in the Pearl River Delta and Yangtze River Delta saw a 15% WoW increase in production intensity in early May. Power consumption growth in high-tech manufacturing sectors reached 10.7% YoY.
*   **Divergence:** Macro sell-side remains bearish on China’s GDP growth due to property sector weakness.
*   **Conclusion:** Alt-data validates that "New Quality Productive Forces" are forming a growth momentum independent of the property cycle. Markets may be underestimating the earnings elasticity of high-tech branches within the industrial sector.

### PITCH 3: Shipping Efficiency Dividends Driven by LEO (Low Earth Orbit) Connectivity
*   **Signal:** Monitoring data shows that ocean-going fleets with large-scale installations of **Starlink** and domestic LEO terminals have reduced fuel consumption by 8% via real-time path optimization and shortened port turnaround times by 48 hours.
*   **Divergence:** Shipping stock valuations are currently driven by freight rate cycles and do not yet reflect the long-term Opex reduction brought by technological improvements.
*   **Conclusion:** Tech-forward fleets are widening the gross margin gap with traditional operators through "data dividends," which will be reflected in upcoming Q2 earnings.

## 3. Fundamental Implications
*   **AI Sector:** Re-evaluate expectations for the Total Addressable Market (TAM) of compute demand.
*   **China Manufacturing:** Recommend shifting allocation from traditional industrials to high-tech manufacturing sectors verified by high-frequency satellite signals.
*   **Shipping & Logistics:** Overweight high-efficiency fleets with LEO connectivity capabilities.

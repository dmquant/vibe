# Overnight Alt-Data Scan: Whiteboard Divergence Report (2026-05-05)

## Executive Summary
Today's scan focuses on two high-conviction divergences: the "Physical Gap" in AI power infrastructure and the "Perception Divergence" in Mexico's nearshoring story. Satellite imagery and shipping manifests are leading official GDP and sell-side guidance at an accelerating pace.

---

## Topic 1: The Grid Gap — A Three-Year Disconnect Between AI Guidance and Physical Delivery

### 1. Observation
Tech hyperscalers (Alphabet, Microsoft, Amazon) committed over $650 billion in AI capex in their Q1 2026 guidance, with markets pricing in a massive capacity ramp for H2 2026.

### 2. Alt-Data Signal
*   **Transformer Lead Times:** Satellite monitoring of Western manufacturing hubs (e.g., GE Vernova, Eaton) shows expansion lagging demand. Lead times for Large Power Transformers (LPTs) have stretched to **3-5 years**.
*   **Bill of Lading Data:** Despite "onshoring" rhetoric, Q1 2026 imports of Chinese-made high-voltage transformers to the US have surged **5x YoY**. This suggests data center operators are quietly bypassing local supply chains to maintain construction schedules.
*   **Satellite Groundbreaking Rates:** Thermal imagery and site monitoring of mega-projects like "Stargate" show that only **33%** of planned capacity has actually broken ground, with substations being the primary bottleneck.

### 3. Fundamental Divergence
Sell-side models continue to linearly extrapolate "Capex = Compute Growth." Alt-data reveals that the **binding constraint has shifted from GPUs to grid permits and physical transformers**. Delivery delays in the physical world will cause 2026 compute availability to significantly miss expectations.

### 4. Conclusion & Trade Ideas
*   **Bullish:** Second-hand power equipment brokers with ready inventory and "behind-the-meter" gas-fired power solution providers that can bypass the grid.
*   **Warning:** Re-evaluate Hyperscalers trading on aggressive H2 2026 capacity assumptions.

## Topic 2: Mexico Nearshoring 2.0 — Official GDP Contraction vs. Satellite-Confirmed Industrial Boom

### 1. Observation
Official Mexican Q1 2026 GDP contracted by 0.8% QoQ, sparking fears that the "nearshoring" peak has passed.

### 2. Alt-Data Signal
*   **Industrial Inventory Satellite Mapping:** Industrial inventory in Monterrey and Querétaro has breached 203 million sq ft. Satellite data shows new factory footprints up **25% YoY**.
*   **Truck-to-Dock Ratios:** High-frequency geofencing data indicates that cross-border truck throughput at northern ports remains at record highs, despite the macro GDP dip.
*   **Monterrey Tech Park Progress:** Imagery shows 80% completion of this 27-hectare hub, specifically geared toward high-value aerospace and semiconductor testing.

### 3. Fundamental Divergence
Official GDP is being dragged down by currency volatility and weak domestic consumption, but the **industrial physical economy is accelerating**. The current bottleneck is not order demand, but rather "Water and Power" infrastructure gaps clearly visible in satellite images.

### 4. Conclusion & Trade Ideas
*   **Key Play:** Focus on Mexican Industrial REITs (FIBRAs) that own "Grid-Ready" and "Water-Secured" assets, rather than pure manufacturing exporters.
*   **Action:** Use the macro-driven dip in official data to accumulate assets in core industrial corridors.

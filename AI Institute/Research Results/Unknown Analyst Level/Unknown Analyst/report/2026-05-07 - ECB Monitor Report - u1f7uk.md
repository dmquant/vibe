## Executive Summary

The European Central Bank (ECB) is navigating an exceptionally challenging policy landscape. Following the April 30, 2026 meeting where rates were held steady, a resurgence in inflation (April HICP reaching 3.0%) combined with stagnant growth (Q1 GDP at 0.1%) has created stagflationary pressures. Driven by the energy shock from the US-Iran conflict that erupted in February, 2026 inflation expectations have been revised upward to 2.7%. Markets are now pricing in a high probability (approx. 75%) of a 25bps rate hike at the June 11 meeting.

## Risk Factor Analysis

1. **Geopolitics & Energy:** Middle East conflict-driven supply uncertainty has kept Brent crude prices ~50% higher than pre-war levels, fueling imported inflation.
2. **Second-Round Effects:** The ECB is closely monitoring whether high prices translate into aggressive wage demands, leading to entrenched inflation.
3. **Credit Tightening:** Financial institutions are tightening credit standards, further dampening the outlook for investment and consumption.

## Future Outlook

- **June 11, 2026:** Next interest rate decision and release of Eurosystem staff macroeconomic projections.
- **Late May Inflation Print:** Monitoring potential spillover from energy prices to core inflation components.

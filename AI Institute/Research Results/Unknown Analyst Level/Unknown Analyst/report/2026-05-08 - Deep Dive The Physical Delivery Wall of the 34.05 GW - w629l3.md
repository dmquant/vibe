# Deep Dive: The "Physical Delivery Wall" of the 34.05 GW Green Power Pipeline (2026-05-08)

**Analyst:** Utilities Analyst
**Date:** May 8, 2026

## 1. Executive Summary
Following today's announcement of 34.05 GW in green power direct connection projects (including 99 pilots), market sentiment has been highly optimistic. However, cross-verification with the power equipment supply chain reveals a critical **"Physical Delivery Wall"**: with global lead times for Large Power Transformers (LPT) stretching to **140-160 weeks**, projects approved in H1 2026 without existing transformer reserves will likely see actual operations delayed until **2029** at the earliest.

## 2. Gap Quantification

| Segment | Status (2026-05) | Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Transformer (LPT) Lead Time** | 140 - 160 weeks (3.0 - 3.5 years) | New 2026 orders will be delivered in 2029 at the earliest |
| **Grain-Oriented Steel (GOES)** | High-end grades under quota systems | Limits the ability of transformer OEMs to ramp up production via extra shifts |
| **Grid Connection Queue** | 24 provincial policies active, but physical access remains constrained | The "load-defined source" logic is physically capped by "equipment-defined source" |

## 3. CapEx and Valuation Risks
*   **Market Misinterpretation:** The market generally views the 34.05 GW as an incremental capacity contribution for 2026-2027.
*   **Substantial Risk:** We estimate that **$60 - $80 billion** of AI energy infrastructure investment in 2027 will be in a state of "physical non-deliverability"—capital is deployed, but equipment is in the queue.
*   **Stock Impact:**
    *   **Power Operators (`600905.SH`, `000993.SZ`):** Short-term valuations are overextended by policy news; they face significant "earnings misses" as installation progress lags.
    *   **Equipment OEMs (`600089.SH` TBEA, `601179.SH` XD Electric):** Extremely high order certainty (visibility to 2029); margins continue to rise due to scarcity.

## 4. Computing-Power Synergy: Hype vs. Reality
*   **Reality:** Leading pilots with "legacy substations" or "self-build capabilities" (e.g., China Datang projects in Ningxia) will have priority power access, becoming the only incremental supply in 2027.
*   **Hype:** Small-to-medium "Green Power + IDC" developers without pre-secured equipment hold only "paper value" projects, unable to support the massive inference demands of AI models in 2027.

## 5. Conclusion & Recommendations
We maintain a **"Structurally Bullish"** stance on the power sector, with a focus shift from "operators" to "equipment leaders with delivery capabilities." Warn against a potential downward revision of total AI computing supply in 2027 due to physical infrastructure delays.

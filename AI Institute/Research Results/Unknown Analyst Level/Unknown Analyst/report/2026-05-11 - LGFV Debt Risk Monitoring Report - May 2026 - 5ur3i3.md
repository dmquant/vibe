# LGFV Debt Risk Monitoring Report - May 2026

**Date:** May 11, 2026  
**Analyst:** China Macro Analyst  
**Key Conclusion:** 2026 marks the final sprint of the "10 Trillion CNY Debt Resolution Plan." LGFV risks have shifted from "systemic pressure" to "structural transformation." With over half of the 2026 refinancing bonds already issued, short-term default risks are contained. However, market focus has pivoted to the commercial transformation pressures ahead of the June 2027 "Platform Exit" deadline.

---

## I. Debt Maturity Analysis: Declining Volume, Front-loaded Pressure
The repayment pressure for LGFV bonds in 2026 is characterized by "decreasing total volume but concentrated timing."

| Debt Category | 2026 Estimated Maturity | YoY Change | Peak Pressure |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Onshore LGFV Bonds** | > 2.3 Trillion RMB | Significant decline from 2025 | Q1 (March peak) & Q3 |
| **Offshore LGFV Bonds** | 35.29 Billion USD | -17.1% | Q2 |
| **Hidden Debt Swap** | 2.0 Trillion RMB (Annual Plan) | Flat | ~60% (1.18T) completed by end-April |

**Key Observations:**
- **"Front-loaded" Issuance:** Nearly 60% of the 2 trillion RMB 2026 swap quota has been issued, effectively cushioning the March repayment peak.
- **Shrinking Net Financing:** Regulatory tightening continues to keep net financing at low levels. The market is in a "zero-sum" game phase, with rollover (refinancing) accounting for over 90% of issuance.

---

## II. Credit Spread Monitoring: Low-level Oscillation & Grade Divergence
As of early May 2026, LGFV credit spreads have entered a "bottoming out" phase after two years of extreme compression.

- **Overall Trend:** Spreads widened slightly by approximately **1 bp** in early May, reflecting cooling investor appetite as spreads hit historical lows.
- **Grade Divergence:** 
  - **Short-end (1Y AA):** Significant widening (~2 bps) due to ultra-low coupons reducing their defensive value.
  - **Mid-to-Long end (3-5Y AAA):** Remains the focus for institutional rotation, showing relatively stable performance.
- **Regional Performance:** Key debt-resolution provinces (Guizhou, Yunnan, Gansu) continue to see spread compression due to policy support. Non-key provinces (e.g., Qinghai, Jilin) saw a recent small rebound of about 3 bps.

---

## III. Risk Warnings & Policy Direction
1.  **"Platform Exit" Countdown:** Regulators mandate the completion of LGFV market-oriented transformation by **June 30, 2027**. 2026 is the final window for stripping government financing functions. Watch for liquidity shocks as the "government guarantee" narrative fades.
2.  **Arrears Clearance:** A priority for 2026 debt-swap funds is "clearing arrears to enterprises." While this improves real economy liquidity, it means some funds are diverted from direct bond repayments, testing the LGFVs' self-sustaining capacity.
3.  **Technical Default Watch:** While public bond defaults remain highly unlikely, focus should remain on **"non-standard debt"** risks in weak county-level platforms and their potential contagion to public markets.

---

## IV. Conclusion & Recommendations
- **Allocation Strategy:** Favor regions leading in fundamental transformation. In a narrow-spread environment, the risk-reward ratio for lower-tier credit is poor. Maintain short-to-medium duration and high liquidity in portfolios.
- **Forward Watch:** Monitor whether the pace of special bond issuance accelerates in H2 2026 in response to economic fluctuations.

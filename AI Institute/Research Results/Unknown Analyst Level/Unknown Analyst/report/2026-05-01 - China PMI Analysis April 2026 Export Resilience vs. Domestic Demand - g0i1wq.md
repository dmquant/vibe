# China PMI Analysis April 2026: Export Resilience vs. Domestic Demand Headwinds

**Analyst:** China Macro Team
**Date:** May 1, 2026

## 1. Executive Summary

The PMI data released for April 2026 highlights a distinct "two-speed" economic recovery. While the manufacturing sector continues to expand driven by robust external demand, the non-manufacturing sector has unexpectedly slipped into contraction territory, weighed down by persistent weakness in the property and consumption sectors.

- **Manufacturing:** The official NBS index (50.3) stayed in expansion for the second consecutive month, while the Caixin index (52.2) surged to its highest level since 2020, underscoring exports as the primary growth engine.
- **Non-Manufacturing:** The official index fell to 49.4, breaking below the 50.0 threshold for the first time in years, signaling severe challenges in services and construction.
- **Policy Implications:** This divergence will likely shift the policy focus from supply-side support to aggressive domestic demand stimulation. We anticipate stronger fiscal stimulus or targeted RRR cuts in Q2.

## 3. Manufacturing: A Safe Haven Driven by External Demand

Despite a slight dip in the NBS manufacturing PMI, the sector remains resilient.
- **Export Order Surge:** The Caixin PMI's jump to 52.2 was fueled by a recovery in the global electronics supply chain and strong demand from emerging markets.
- **Input Costs Rising:** The purchase price index ticked up, reflecting the pass-through of global commodity price volatility to upstream industries.
- **Large vs. SMEs:** Large state-linked firms remain stable, while small and medium-sized exporters (captured more by Caixin) are showing higher levels of activity.

---

## 4. Non-Manufacturing: A Warning Signal for Domestic Demand

The drop to 49.4 in the non-manufacturing PMI is the most critical risk signal this month:
- **Services Slump:** The services business activity index fell to 49.6. Despite the "Tomb-Sweeping Day" holiday travel, the sector failed to maintain expansion, reflecting high precautionary savings and weak discretionary spending.
- **Construction Under Pressure:** The construction index dropped to 48.0, as property destocking pressures and lags in infrastructure investment impact on-the-ground activity.

---

## 5. Market Impact and Investment Strategy

### 5.1 A-Shares
- **Sector Allocation:** We favor **export-oriented manufacturing** (e.g., high-end equipment, EV components, consumer electronics), which is well-supported by the strong Caixin PMI.
- **Risk Warning:** Avoid pro-cyclical consumption and property-related sectors until a clear inflection point in domestic demand emerges.

### 5.2 Bond Market
- **Yield Outlook:** The weaker-than-expected non-manufacturing data reinforces expectations for further monetary easing. We expect the 10-year CGB yield to remain low, with potential for further downward movement.

### 5.3 RMB Exchange Rate
- **Currency Outlook:** While strong exports provide trade surplus support, the pressure from weak domestic demand on interest rate differentials will limit RMB appreciation. Expect range-bound volatility in the near term.

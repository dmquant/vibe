# Daily Oil Market Monitor (May 11, 2026)

## Executive Summary
International oil prices surged today, with both WTI and Brent jumping over 3%. Escalating geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, specifically shipping risks in the Strait of Hormuz and the collapse of US-Iran negotiations, have injected a significant "war premium" into the market. While OPEC+ plans a modest production hike for June, immediate supply disruption fears are the dominant market driver.

## OPEC+ Dynamics & Supply Side
- **Production Hike**: Seven key OPEC+ members (led by Saudi Arabia and Russia) agreed in early May to a modest production increase of **188,000 bpd** starting June 2026, marking a gradual exit from previous voluntary cuts.
- **UAE Exit**: The **United Arab Emirates (UAE)** formally withdrew from OPEC effective May 1, 2026, to pursue independent energy goals and capacity expansions.
- **Upcoming Meeting**: The Joint Ministerial Monitoring Committee (JMMC) is scheduled to meet on **June 7, 2026**, to review market stability and compliance.

## Outlook
In the short term, oil prices face upward pressure toward the $110 level. Although OPEC+ is attempting to increase supply for the summer, the immediate balance remains extremely fragile due to Iranian supply risks and shipping blockades. Market focus remains on the normalization of shipping through the Strait of Hormuz and the June 7 OPEC+ policy review.

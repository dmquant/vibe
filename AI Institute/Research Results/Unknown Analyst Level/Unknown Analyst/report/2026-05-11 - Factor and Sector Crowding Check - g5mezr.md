## Prioritized Conclusions

1. **Crowding level: elevated, but not systemic risk-off.** Crowding is concentrated in U.S. cap-weighted growth, AI/technology, and momentum exposure. SPY top-10 weight is **39.16%**, and Mag 7 weight is **34.67%**; QQQ top-10 weight is **47.25%**, and Mag 7 weight is **39.63%**.
2. **Sector-level concentration is sharper than index-level concentration.** XLK top-10 weight is **61.56%**, while XLY top-10 weight is **71.12%**; in XLY, AMZN and TSLA alone sum to **44.54%**. Sector ETFs therefore carry substantial single-stock and theme risk, not only sector risk.
3. **Correlation does not yet signal a broad liquidation.** The 60-day average pairwise correlation across 11 U.S. sector ETFs is **0.31**, versus **0.31** 20 trading days earlier, in the **28.94th percentile** of the sample since 2025. Broad correlation has not surged, but the 60-day average correlation across style ETFs is **0.78**, showing that many style portfolios are still driven by the same U.S. equity beta and mega-cap growth leg.
4. **De-risking is more latent than explicit.** VIX is **17.19**, down **10.61%** over 20 trading days; MOVE is **67.25**, down **6.79%** over 20 trading days; HYG/LQD 20-day relative return is **+0.23%**. Volatility and credit are not deteriorating, but RSP/SPY is **-4.40%** over 20 days and USMV/SPY is **-6.61%**, indicating that investors are embracing concentrated growth risk rather than broadening into low-volatility or equal-weight exposure.

## Portfolio Implications

- **Do not double-count similar exposures as diversification.** QQQ, XLK, MTUM, IWF, and SPY have different labels, but currently share large-cap growth, AI, and momentum risk.
- **Use equal-weight, sector-neutral, or single-name limits.** Growth and momentum portfolios should have single-name, sector, and theme caps, especially around NVDA, AAPL, MSFT, AMZN, Alphabet, META, and TSLA.
- **Move correlation warnings earlier.** If the sector 60-day average correlation rises quickly from **0.31** to above **0.45**, while VIX breaks **22** and RSP/SPY continues to fall, upgrade crowding from “elevated” to “high/red.”
- **Keep truly diversifying legs on the monitor.** XLE’s 60-day correlation with SPY is **-0.408**. Low-volatility and defensive sectors do not have momentum now, but they are candidates to watch as hedges if crowded growth reverses.

## Follow-Up Items

1. Update SPY, QQQ, XLK, and XLY top-10 weights and HHI daily, with particular focus on whether SPY top-10 weight breaks **42%** or QQQ top-10 weight breaks **50%**.
2. Track QQQ/SPY, MTUM/SPY, IWF/IWD, and RSP/SPY; if the first three rise while RSP/SPY falls, the “narrow crowded rally” signal remains confirmed.
3. Monitor three thresholds: VIX at **22**, MOVE at **80**, and sector average correlation at **0.45**. If all three trigger together, treat de-risking as explicit rather than latent.

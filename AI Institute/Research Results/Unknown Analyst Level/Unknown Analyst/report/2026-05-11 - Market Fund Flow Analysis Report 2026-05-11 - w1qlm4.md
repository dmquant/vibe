# Market Fund Flow Analysis Report (2026-05-11)

## Core Conclusions
Today's A-share market exhibited a classic "price-volume surge" pattern, with total daily turnover exceeding **3.5 trillion RMB**, indicating extremely high market sentiment. **Hard Tech (Semiconductors, AI Computing)** has become the absolute core of capital attraction. International capital (Northbound) and leveraged capital (Margin Trading) are showing synchronized inflows, while Main Force institutional funds exhibit intense structural rotation across sectors.

---

## Detailed Analysis by Dimension

### 1. Northbound Funds (International Capital)
*   **Net Flow**: Daily net inflow of approximately **12.85 billion RMB**, showing accelerated buying behavior.
*   **Continuity**: This marks the **9th consecutive trading day** of net inflows for Northbound funds.
*   **Preference**: Strong appetite for A-share core assets, with significant increases in Electronics, Communications, and Power sectors.

### 2. Main Force Funds (Domestic Institutions)
*   **Sector Distribution**:
    *   **Top Inflows**: **Electronics** (+133.57 billion RMB), Securities, Real Estate. The semiconductor sub-sector alone attracted nearly 100 billion RMB.
    *   **Top Outflows**: Non-ferrous Metals, Computers, Pharmaceuticals.
*   **Individual Stocks**:
    *   **Top Net Inflows**: Zhongji Innolight (2.279 billion RMB), Shanghai Electric, JCET Group.
    *   **Top Net Outflows**: Hengtong Optic-electric (2.293 billion RMB), Unisplendour, Zhongtian Technology.

### 3. Margin Trading (Leveraged Capital)
*   **Balance Scale**: As of May 8, the total margin balance reached **2.80 trillion RMB**, a record high, with the financing balance at 2.78 trillion RMB.
*   **Recent Dynamics**: Intraday observations today show that margin buyers continue to increase exposure to tech growth tracks, particularly AI computing chains and domestic semiconductor substitution.
*   **Risk Warning**: Given the high leverage environment, several brokerages have upgraded risk controls, introducing immediate liquidation lines. Investors should be cautious of market volatility.

### 4. ETF Fund Flows
*   **Broad-based Index ETFs**:
    *   **Inflows**: ChiNext ETF (159915) saw net inflows exceeding 500 million RMB; STAR 50 related ETFs recorded combined inflows of about 600 million RMB.
    *   **Outflows**: Some blue-chip products like the SSE 50 ETF showed signs of profit-taking at highs.
*   **Thematic ETFs**:
    *   **Chips/Semiconductors**: Related ETFs saw a surge in volume and significant capital inflows.
    *   **Defensive Assets**: Short-term Bond ETFs (e.g., 511360) saw a net outflow of over 5.1 billion RMB, as capital rapidly rotates from fixed-income to high-elasticity equity assets.

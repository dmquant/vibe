- **June Meeting Outlook:** Attention is focused on the June 2nd ministerial meeting. The prevailing consensus leans toward a "status quo" approach to support prices, though internal disagreements (e.g., regarding production capacity benchmarks) remain a potential variable amid weakening demand.

- **U.S. Commercial Crude Inventories:** Inventories saw an unexpected draw of approximately 4.2 million barrels last week; however, levels remain near the five-year average due to recovering imports and refinery utilization fluctuations.

- **Refined Products:** Slight builds in gasoline and distillate inventories suggest that the recovery in downstream consumption requires further observation.

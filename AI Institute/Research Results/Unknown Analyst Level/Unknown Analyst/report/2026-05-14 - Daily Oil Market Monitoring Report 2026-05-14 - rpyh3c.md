## 2. OPEC+ & Supply Dynamics
*   **Production Collapse**: OPEC+ crude output fell by 1.74 mb/d in April to 33.19 mb/d due to ongoing conflict [3]. Combined production from Gulf members (Saudi Arabia, Iraq, Iran, UAE, Kuwait) is down approximately 10 mb/d from pre-war levels [3].
*   **UAE Departure**: The UAE formally exited OPEC effective May 1, 2026. While no longer bound by quotas, its actual exports remain severely restricted by regional shipping disruptions [5].
*   **Supply Deficit**: The IEA projects a global supply decline of 3.9 mb/d for 2026 [10]. With the Strait of Hormuz expected to remain effectively closed through late May, the supply gap is unlikely to narrow in the short term [1].

## 3. Inventory & Demand
*   **EIA Inventory Report**: For the week ending May 8, U.S. commercial crude stocks fell by 4.3 million barrels [9], significantly exceeding the expected 2.1 million barrel draw. Gasoline inventories declined for the 13th consecutive week, dropping by 4.1 million barrels [9].
*   **Demand Forecast Downgrade**: Citing the economic impact of the conflict, OPEC lowered its 2026 global demand growth forecast to 1.17 mb/d (from 1.38 mb/d) [4].
*   **SPR Release**: The U.S. Strategic Petroleum Reserve (SPR) decreased by 8.6 million barrels this week to help offset global supply shortages [9].

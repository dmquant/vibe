# Factor and Industry Crowding Check

Date anchor: 2026-05-14, from shell `date +%Y-%m-%d`. The analysis uses the latest public data available by 2026-05-14; most fund holdings are dated 2026-05-11 to 2026-05-13, and the market signals are mainly from 2026-05-13.

## Prioritized Conclusions

1. **Industry and stock concentration: high crowding, concentrated in U.S. large-cap technology, AI, and semiconductors.** SPY's top 10 holdings total about **39.13%**, led by NVIDIA at **8.39%**, Apple at **6.76%**, and Microsoft at **4.82%**; Information Technology is **37.28%** of the fund. XLK is even more concentrated: its top 10 holdings total about **62.86%**, while Semiconductors & Semiconductor Equipment is **48.66%**. Passive large-cap, growth, momentum, and technology exposures are converging on the same names.
2. **Factor crowding: price crowding is stronger than asset crowding.** Value and momentum have led the 2026 rebound, with VLUE at **33.93%** NAV YTD and MTUM at **21.32%** through 2026-05-12. QUAL has the largest asset base (**$51.115bn**) and therefore looks more like structural asset crowding, while USMV is only **0.67%** YTD, so low volatility has not become the dominant risk-off trade.
3. **Correlation and de-risking: not a broad de-risking regime, but there is narrow-breadth fragility.** On 2026-05-13, the S&P 500 rose **0.6%** and the Nasdaq gained **1.2%** to records, but most U.S. stocks fell and the Dow declined **0.1%**. RSP fell **0.44%** the same day, showing equal-weight breadth lagging cap-weighted indices. VIX was **17.87**, still below its 52-week high of **35.30**; the latest available COR3M was **13.52**, down from its 2026-03-30 three-month high of **35.26**. This looks more like “crowded leaders plus de-risking outside the core names” than a systemic selloff.
4. **Current rating: industry crowding is red, factor crowding is orange, systemic de-risking is yellow.** The main risk is not ordinary volatility; it is a reversal in AI/semiconductor mega-caps that simultaneously pressures momentum, quality-growth, cap-weighted indices, and technology ETFs.

## De-Risking Diagnosis

- **Signal already present:** breadth deterioration. On 2026-05-13, indices reached new highs, but AP reported that most U.S. stocks fell; RSP was down **0.44%** that day. This is often an early symptom of crowded positioning: index-level stability masks internal selling first.
- **Signal not yet present:** VIX and COR3M have not spiked together. VIX at **17.87** is mid-to-high but not in crisis territory; COR3M at **13.52** is well below the March high of **35.26**, so a systemic correlation shock is not confirmed.
- **Key risk path:** if semiconductor and AI leaders reverse, cap-weighted indices, MTUM, QUAL constituents, XLK, and QQQ would share the same pressure point, and correlations could jump quickly from low levels.

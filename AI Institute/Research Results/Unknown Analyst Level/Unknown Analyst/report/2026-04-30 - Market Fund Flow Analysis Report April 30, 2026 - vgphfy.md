# Market Fund Flow Analysis Report (April 30, 2026)

## Executive Summary
As the final trading day before the "May Day" Golden Week, the market today exhibited typical "pre-holiday volatility and structural divergence." Although the combined turnover of the two exchanges remained high at over 2.5 trillion RMB, indicating active participation, the trends in Main Funds and ETF flows suggest that institutional investors have executed significant defensive rebalancing ahead of the long break.

| Fund Dimension | Trend | Key Focus Areas |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Northbound Funds** | Steady Inflow | Focused on Power Equipment, AI Infrastructure, and Energy Metals |
| **Main Funds** | Structural Divergence | Increased positions in Semiconductors/Telecom; Outflow from Lithium/Non-ferrous |
| **Margin Trading** | Continuous Rise | Balance at ~2.71 trillion RMB; Leverage prefers Growth stocks |
| **ETF Flows** | Broad Outflow / Theme Inflow | Redemption in CSI 300; Subscription in GEM 50 and CSI A500 |

---

## In-depth Fund Flow Analysis

### 1. Northbound Funds (Foreign Capital): Long-term Growth Thesis Intact
Northbound funds performed steadily today, maintaining a flat to slightly positive net inflow. Foreign capital's allocation logic is currently highly concentrated on two core themes: "China's Energy Independence" and "AI Infrastructure Development."
*   **Heavy Positions:** Power equipment, auto leaders (e.g., CATL, BYD), and industrial metals.
*   **Sentiment:** Investors remain optimistic about the long-term certainty of China's core assets, largely ignoring short-term pre-holiday noise.

### 2. Main Funds (Domestic Institutions): High-to-Low Rotation
Main funds showed clear signs of sector rotation today. Following a significant net inflow of 12 billion RMB yesterday, capital moved rapidly across sectors today.
*   **Top Net Inflows:** Technology growth sectors such as Semiconductors, Communication Equipment, and Satellite Communication. Main funds are utilizing pullbacks to accumulate positions in the AI hardware chain.
*   **Top Net Outflows:** Lithium batteries and Non-ferrous metals, which saw significant prior gains. Some profit-taking occurred as investors sought to de-risk before the holiday.

### 3. Margin Trading (Leveraged Funds): Market Vitality Barometer
As of April 28, the total margin balance across the three exchanges stood at approximately 2.71 trillion RMB, marking the third consecutive week of growth.
*   **Risk Appetite:** Leveraged capital has recently shown extremely high enthusiasm for the tech sector (Semiconductors and Computer Software).
*   **Sentiment Reading:** Despite the upcoming holiday, the continuous growth in margin balance reflects strong confidence among high-net-worth investors in the sustainability of the bull market logic post-holiday.

### 4. ETF Fund Flows: Defensive Contraction vs. Precision Targeting
The ETF market today showed a stark contrast between categories:
*   **Redemption Side:** Large-cap broad-based ETFs like the CSI 300 and SSE 50 saw net redemptions. This is primarily due to institutional investors narrowing their fronts and reducing overall exposure before the break.
*   **Subscription Side:** GEM 50 ETF, CSI A500 ETF, and thematic ETFs (Telecom, Carbon Neutrality) received contrarian subscriptions. Capital is using these thematic tools to bet on high-growth sub-sectors.

## Market Sentiment and Outlook
**Estimated Fear-Greed Index: 65 (Greed)**
The overall market remains in a bullish mood, though the "pre-holiday effect" and associated profit-taking have partially neutralized the upside sentiment.

**Market Judgment:**
Given the uncertainties in the external environment (e.g., Fed's rate decision, Middle East situation) during the holiday period, a minor de-risking pullback occurred near the close, with the Shanghai Composite Index fluctuating narrowly above 4,100 points. If the news front remains stable during the break, the market is expected to continue its upward challenge post-holiday, led by Semiconductors and New Power Grid themes.

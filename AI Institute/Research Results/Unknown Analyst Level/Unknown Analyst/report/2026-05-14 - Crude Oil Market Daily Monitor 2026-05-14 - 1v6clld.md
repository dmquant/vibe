# Crude Oil Market Daily Monitor (2026-05-14)

## Executive Summary
The crude oil market currently faces a combination of extreme volatility and structural deficits. Driven by geopolitical conflicts and the effective closure of the Strait of Hormuz, Brent crude remains supported above $105/bbl. Despite a slight pullback on May 13 due to profit-taking and diplomatic focus (U.S.-China summit in Beijing), the supply-demand gap remains critical.

**Key Drivers:**
- **Supply Shortfall:** Global supply losses are estimated at 12.8 million bpd since the conflict escalated in February.
- **Technical Correction:** Traders engaged in profit-taking on May 13 following a sustained rally.
- **Geopolitical Premium:** The ongoing closure of the Strait of Hormuz provides an estimated $20/bbl premium support.

## 2. Inventory Data (EIA Report May 13)
The U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA) reported stock draws significantly larger than expected, reflecting increased global reliance on U.S. crude and products amid Middle Eastern disruptions.

- **Commercial Crude Inventories:** Decreased by **4.3 million barrels** (expected 2.1M draw), totaling 452.9 million barrels.
- **Gasoline Stocks:** Decreased by **4.1 million barrels** to 215.7 million barrels, hitting a six-month low.
- **Distillate Stocks:** Rose slightly by **0.2 million barrels**.
- **Refinery Utilization:** Climbed to **91.7%**.
- **Exports:** Crude exports surged to **5.49 million bpd**.

## 3. OPEC+ Dynamics & Policy
- **UAE Exit:** The United Arab Emirates (UAE) formally exited OPEC and OPEC+ effective **May 1, 2026**, to pursue independent production and investment strategies.
- **Demand Forecast Revision:** OPEC lowered its 2026 global demand growth forecast to **1.17 million bpd** (down from 1.38M), citing war-related economic headwinds.
- **Production Drop:** OPEC+ crude output fell by **1.74 million bpd** in April, primarily due to delivery obstacles caused by the Hormuz closure.
- **Voluntary Adjustments:** Seven members, including Saudi Arabia and Russia, agreed to a production adjustment of **188,000 bpd** for June 2026.

## 4. Strategic Outlook
- **Baseline Forecast:** The EIA assumes the Strait of Hormuz will remain effectively closed through at least the end of May, with prices expected to average **$106** through June.
- **Risk Assessment:** If the closure extends into July, an additional **$20/barrel** risk premium could be triggered. The global deficit is projected to average **1.78 million bpd** for 2026.

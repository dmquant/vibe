# Factor and Sector Crowding Check

**Date anchor:** the shell date is 2026-05-13; U.S. ETF, index, and volatility data use the latest completed trading close, 2026-05-12. Returns and correlations are my calculations from Yahoo Finance adjusted closes; holdings and portfolio characteristics come from issuer or public holdings pages.

## Prioritized Conclusions

1. **The most crowded trade remains AI, semiconductors, and mega-cap growth.** SPY’s top 10 holdings are 39.17% of assets, QQQ’s top 10 are 47.22%, and QQQ’s technology weight is 53.6%. In price action, SMH is up 24.17% over 20 days, 37.66% over 60 days, and 55.85% YTD, while XLK is up 18.43% over 20 days, far ahead of SPY at 6.30% and RSP at 2.08%.
2. **Factor crowding is not broad; it is concentrated in momentum and “value” moving together.** MTUM-VLUE 20-day correlation rose to 0.861 from 0.679 sixty trading days earlier. Yet the 20-day average pairwise correlation across the five factor ETFs fell from 0.482 to 0.237. The conclusion: crowding sits in a few high-beta and semiconductor-linked expressions, not in all styles at once.
3. **Defensive factors do not confirm de-risking.** USMV rose only 0.88% over 20 days and SPLV fell 1.38%, underperforming SPY by 5.42 and 7.68 percentage points respectively; XLU and XLV fell 2.75% and 2.00% over 20 days. If investors were actively de-risking, low-volatility and defensive sectors would usually not look this weak.
4. **De-risking signals look like light hedging, not systemic retreat.** VIX is 17.99, down 2.02% over 20 days and 12.67% over 60 days; VVIX is up 3.30% over 20 days, showing marginally warmer volatility hedging. HYG is down only 0.26% over 20 days, so credit stress remains mild; TLT is down 2.19% over 20 days, meaning duration has not confirmed a classic risk-off move.
5. **The current risk is concentration plus valuation/style beta.** MTUM has a 3-year beta of 1.24 and P/E of 38.32; VLUE has a 3-year beta of 1.11 and P/E of 21.24, but its price behavior now looks more like a cyclical/semiconductor rebound than traditional defensive value.

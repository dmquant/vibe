# Energy Sector Analysis: Oil Market Monitor (May 8, 2026)

## Key Takeaways
*   **Price Volatility & Stabilization:** WTI and Brent crude saw significant swings in early May. After dipping below the $100 psychological threshold, prices have stabilized near $97 and $102, respectively.
*   **OPEC+ Structural Shift:** The United Arab Emirates (UAE) officially exited OPEC on May 1, 2026. Remaining alliance members are sticking to modest production increases to maintain market confidence.

## 2. OPEC+ Dynamics
### UAE Exit and Alliance Response
*   **UAE Departure:** Effective May 1, 2026, the UAE has formally left OPEC and OPEC+. This move aligns with its long-term strategy to expand production capacity to 5 million bpd by 2027.
*   **Production Update:** On May 3, seven key members (led by Saudi Arabia and Russia) agreed to proceed with a planned production hike of **188,000 barrels per day (bpd)** starting in June.

**Analyst Insight:** The alliance is utilizing a "business-as-usual" approach to mitigate the psychological impact of the UAE's exit, signaling that the core group still exerts significant influence over global supply.

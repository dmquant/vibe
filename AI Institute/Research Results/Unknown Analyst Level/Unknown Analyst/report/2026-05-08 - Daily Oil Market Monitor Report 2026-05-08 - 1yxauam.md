## 2. OPEC+ & Supply Dynamics
*   **UAE Exit from OPEC+:** On May 1, 2026, the UAE formally exited OPEC and the OPEC+ coalition to pursue independent production targets, aiming for 5 million bpd by 2027. This marks a significant fracture in the alliance [Source: discoveryalert.com.au].
*   **Modest Production Increase:** Core OPEC+ members agreed on May 3 to a small quota increase of 188,000 bpd starting in June. However, its impact is limited due to the continued partial blockade of the Strait of Hormuz [Source: opec.org, wsls.com].
*   **Strait of Hormuz Crisis:** The U.S.-Iran conflict has disrupted approximately 6.7 million bpd of Middle Eastern supply, maintaining a high geopolitical risk premium [Source: octagonai.co].

## 3. Inventory & Demand
*   **EIA Inventory Data:** For the week ending May 1, U.S. commercial crude inventories fell by 2.3 million barrels to 457.2 million, a smaller draw than the expected 3.3 million [Source: rigzone.com].
*   **Gasoline Draw:** Gasoline stocks fell by 2.5 million barrels, indicating a seasonal recovery in demand [Source: xtb.com].
*   **Demand Forecast Cut:** The EIA has slashed its 2026 global demand growth forecast to 0.6 million bpd (down from 1.2 million bpd), citing high energy costs and supply chain disruptions [Source: eia.gov].

## 4. Conclusion & Outlook
*   **Geopolitical Risk Premium:** As long as the Strait of Hormuz crisis remains unresolved, oil prices are expected to find strong support above $90.
*   **OPEC+ Stability:** Markets will closely monitor whether other members (e.g., Kazakhstan) follow the UAE's lead, which could trigger a race for market share.
*   **Key Watchpoints:**
    1.  The updated Short-Term Energy Outlook (STEO) from EIA on May 12.
    2.  OPEC+ compliance meeting on June 7 regarding the implementation of the agreed increases.

## Key Takeaways

- The domestic macro focus today is April CPI/PPI. The NBS calendar schedules both the monthly CPI report and the monthly industrial producer price report for 2026-05-11 09:30 [Source: https://www.stats.gov.cn/xw/tjxw/tzgg/202512/t20251224_1962137.html]. A benign CPI print with continued PPI repair would support A-share risk appetite around growth and cyclical manufacturing; a weaker-than-expected PPI would cool reflation trades.
- The latest growth data still point to strong production and an uneven demand recovery: Q1 GDP was RMB 334,193bn, up 5.0% YoY [Source: https://www.stats.gov.cn/sj/zxfb/202604/t20260417_1963336.html]; Q1 value-added industrial output rose 6.1% YoY, retail sales rose 2.4% YoY, and fixed-asset investment rose 1.7% YoY [Source: https://finance.people.com.cn/n1/2026/0416/c1004-40702649.html].
- April trade remained firm. April goods trade was RMB 4.38tn, up 14.2% YoY, with exports up 9.8% and imports up 20.6% [Source: https://m.cnfin.com/yw-lb/zixun/20260510/4410409_1.html]. For the first four months, total goods trade was RMB 16.23tn, up 14.9% YoY [Source: https://www.news.cn/20260509/13ac07ff3d6d448bbe7bb6982d9e8351/c.html]. This continues to support machinery and electrical exports, industrial robots, electronics supply chains, and ports/shipping.
- The policy tone remains supportive rather than a sharp pivot. The Politburo meeting on 2026-04-28 called for more proactive fiscal policy and moderately accommodative monetary policy, while emphasizing domestic demand expansion, technological self-reliance, and supply-chain autonomy [Source: https://www.mee.gov.cn/ywdt/szyw/202604/t20260428_1150338.shtml]. This anchors policy support for new productive forces, computing networks, new power grids, communications networks, and urban underground pipelines.
- Liquidity remains easy. On 2026-05-09, the PBOC conducted RMB 500mn of 7-day reverse repos at 1.40% [Source: https://finance.sina.com.cn/jjxw/2026-05-09/doc-inhxhcxm1613629.shtml]. On 2026-05-09, overnight Shibor was 1.2240%, 7-day Shibor was 1.3180%, and 3-month Shibor was 1.4140% [Source: https://finance.sina.com.cn/stock/bxjj/2026-05-09/doc-inhxhkfi1534020.shtml].

## Latest Domestic Data and Policy Signals

- **PMI**: The April 2026 manufacturing PMI was 50.3%, down 0.1ppt from the prior month; the non-manufacturing business activity index was 49.4%, down 0.7ppt; and the composite PMI output index was 50.1%, down 0.4ppt [Source: https://www.stats.gov.cn/sj/sjjd/202604/t20260430_1963474.html]. Manufacturing remains in expansion, but services and construction-related activity are softer.
- **Manufacturing detail**: In April 2026, the production index was 51.5%, new orders were 50.6%, new export orders were 50.3%, and purchasing volume was 51.1% [Source: https://www.stats.gov.cn/sj/zxfbhjd/202604/t20260430_1963473.html]. Orders and production remain in expansion, suggesting support from external demand and equipment chains.
- **Inflation baseline**: The latest published inflation data are for March 2026: CPI rose 1.0% YoY [Source: https://www.stats.gov.cn/sj/zxfb/202604/t20260410_1963264.html], while PPI shifted from a 0.9% YoY decline in the prior month to a 0.5% YoY gain and rose 1.0% MoM [Source: https://www.stats.gov.cn/sj/zxfb/202604/t20260410_1963263.html]. Today’s April CPI/PPI will test whether the price recovery is continuing [Source: https://www.stats.gov.cn/xw/tjxw/tzgg/202512/t20251224_1962137.html].
- **Industry and export delivery**: In March 2026, value-added industrial output above designated size rose 5.7% YoY in real terms, while export delivery value reached RMB 1,458bn, up 8.7% YoY in nominal terms [Source: https://www.stats.gov.cn/sj/zxfb/202604/t20260416_1963329.html]. This lines up with the strong April export data.
- **Credit and money**: At the end of March 2026, outstanding aggregate financing to the real economy was RMB 456.46tn, up 7.9% YoY; M2 was RMB 353.86tn, up 8.5% YoY; and M1 was RMB 119.32tn, up 5.1% YoY [Source: https://www.news.cn/money/20260414/a2c0298d89564bc796951fa3a8484bdb/c.html]. Credit and money growth remain above real GDP growth, keeping liquidity support in place.
- **Consumption and pork policy**: The NDRC, Ministry of Finance, Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Affairs, and Ministry of Commerce will guide local governments to use fiscal funds for commercial frozen pork reserve purchases, aiming to stabilize the market [Source: https://news.cnr.cn/kuaixun/20260430/t20260430_527606861.shtml]. This is a marginal support for pork prices, the food component of CPI, and hog-related equities.

## CNY, Rates, Liquidity and Policy Expectations

- **RMB**: The latest verified central parity was USD/CNY 6.8502 on 2026-05-08 [Source: https://www.ce.cn/xwzx/gnsz/gdxw/202605/t20260508_2952363.shtml]. Today’s new fixing was not verifiable in available results [Source unknown]. Near term, watch the two-way impact of a softer dollar and strong exports on RMB pricing.
- **Policy-rate anchor**: On 2026-04-20, the 1-year LPR was 3.0% and the 5-year-plus LPR was 3.5% [Source: https://www.chinanews.com.cn/cj/2026/04-20/10606836.shtml]. Before the April PPI and CPI prints, rate-cut expectations depend more on the price-recovery slope and property high-frequency data.
- **Open-market operations**: On 2026-05-09, the PBOC conducted RMB 500mn of 7-day reverse repos at 1.40% [Source: https://finance.sina.com.cn/jjxw/2026-05-09/doc-inhxhcxm1613629.shtml]. On 2026-05-06, it conducted RMB 300bn of 3-month outright reverse repos [Source: https://finance.eastmoney.com/a/202605053727892670.html]. The mix suggests fine-tuning at the short end and medium-term tools to smooth liquidity across month-end and quarter-end points.
- **April tool mix**: In April, 7-day reverse repos drained RMB 331.6bn net, other-tenor reverse repos drained RMB 400bn net, open-market government bond trading injected RMB 40bn net, central treasury cash management injected RMB 130bn net, both MLF and PSL drained RMB 200bn net, and structural monetary policy tools injected RMB 366.7bn net [Source: https://cn.investing.com/news/stock-market-news/article-3355186]. This points to a preference for structural injections and fiscal-monetary coordination rather than simple aggregate expansion.

## A-share Style, Sectors and Risk Appetite

- **Market setup**: On the previous trading day, 2026-05-08, the Shanghai Composite closed flat at 4179.95; the Shenzhen Component closed at 15563.80, down 0.50%; the ChiNext Index closed at 3796.13, down 0.96%; total market turnover was RMB 3,075.7bn, down RMB 92.6bn from the prior day, with more than 3600 stocks rising [Source: https://finance.sina.com.cn/stock/zqgd/2026-05-08/doc-inhxemqm4222599.shtml]. Turnover remains high, and index consolidation has not fully cooled single-stock breadth.
- **Style view**: High turnover, low funding rates, and strong trade data support growth manufacturing and theme rotation. But with ChiNext already pulling back, a CPI/PPI release that does not support earnings upgrades could drive a short-term shift from high-beta growth toward lagging cyclicals and dividend defensives.
- **Preferred sectors**: Machinery and electrical exports, industrial robots, semiconductor equipment, AI computing, communications equipment, commercial aerospace, defense, and ports/shipping remain risk-appetite beneficiaries. On 2026-05-08, leading themes included aerospace, reducers, defense, electric motors, and ports/shipping; laggards included fluorochemicals, PTA, heavy trucks, energy metals, electronic chemicals, and brokerages [Source: https://k.sina.com.cn/article_7857201856_1d45362c00190583la.html?from=finance].
- **Cyclical watch**: If April PPI extends the March 2026 turn from deflation to inflation [Source: https://www.stats.gov.cn/sj/zxfb/202604/t20260410_1963263.html], profit elasticity should be stronger in resources, chemicals, machinery, and upstream equipment. If PPI retreats, money may rotate toward rate-sensitive high-dividend and defensive sectors.
- **Consumption watch**: If April CPI remains above the March 2026 YoY reading of 1.0% [Source: https://www.stats.gov.cn/sj/zxfb/202604/t20260410_1963264.html], food and beverage, hog farming, and services consumption could receive marginal support. If core inflation is weak, consumer equities still need confirmation from income and footfall data.

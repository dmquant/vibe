# Morning Macro Brief
**Date:** Friday, May 1, 2026
**Chief Economist:** Gemini CLI

## Key Takeaways
1. **Global Growth Under Pressure:** The IMF has revised its 2026 global growth forecast down to 3.1%. A "stagflationary shock" triggered by Middle East conflicts is the primary threat.
2. **Inflation Risks Resurface:** Brent crude hitting $126/bbl has pushed global inflation projections to 4.4%, stalling interest rate cut paths in the US and Europe.
3. **Policy Divergence Widens:** The Federal Reserve maintains a "Hawkish Hold," while the PBoC continues with an accommodative stance to counter domestic deflationary risks.

---

## Regional Economic Review

### United States
* **Growth & Inflation:** GDP growth is projected at 1.8% – 2.3% for 2026. Core PCE is expected to remain "sticky" at 2.7%.
* **Manufacturing Strength:** April Manufacturing PMI rose to 54.0 (an 8-month high), signaling industrial resilience but adding to upward inflation pressures.
* **Labor Market:** Wage growth remains firm, supported by AI-related investments, though the overall job market exhibits K-shaped volatility.

### Eurozone
* **Recessionary Risks:** The Composite PMI fell to 48.6 in April (a 17-month low), with the services sector (47.4) losing significant momentum.
* **Energy Headwinds:** High energy costs continue to weigh on industrial output and consumer spending, leaving the economy on the edge of a technical recession.

### China
* **Supportive Growth:** GDP growth expectations are maintained at 4.4% – 4.5%. April Manufacturing PMI was resilient at 50.8.
* **Policy Stimulus:** The PBoC has pledged to maintain accommodative policy through 2026, keeping the LPR at 3.0% to support "high-quality development."

## Risk Assessment
* **Geopolitical Escalation:** A broader conflict could disrupt energy supply, potentially cutting global growth to 2.0% and pushing inflation above 6%.
* **Fiscal Buffers:** High levels of public debt are limiting the capacity for governments to respond to new economic shocks.

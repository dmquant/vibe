## Deep Dive Analysis

### 1. United States: The Warsh Era vs. Sticky Inflation
The confirmation of Kevin Warsh as Fed Chair coincides with a "hot" CPI print. The 0.6% monthly rise in April inflation reflects broadening pressures beyond energy into shelter and services. Achieving the 2% target remains elusive. We expect Warsh to prioritize balance sheet normalization (QT) as his first order of business, as the bar for further rate hikes remains high but the window for cuts has significantly closed.

### 2. China: Shifting from Deflation Risks to Demand Recovery
China’s 1.2% CPI and robust PMI data effectively counter the long-standing "deflation trap" narrative. Growth in transportation and healthcare spending suggests a recovery in consumer confidence. The Beijing Summit (May 14-15) is a critical junction; any consensus on energy trade or tariff reductions would provide a tailwind for RMB-denominated assets and global disinflation.

### 3. Eurozone: Energy Shocks and Growth Headwinds
While the ECB maintained a data-dependent hold in April, Chief Economist Philip Lane has signaled concern over energy shocks damaging GDP. The risk of unanchored inflation expectations remains a primary threat. We anticipate the ECB will remain in a "wait-and-see" mode until the next major decision in June.

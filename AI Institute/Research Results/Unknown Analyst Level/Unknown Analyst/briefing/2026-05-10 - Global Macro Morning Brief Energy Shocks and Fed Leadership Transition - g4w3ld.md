## Regional Analysis

### 1. United States: A Long War on Inflation
The U.S. labor market remains resilient, with April Non-Farm Payrolls adding **115,000** jobs and unemployment holding at **4.3%**. However, the resurgence of inflation is now the primary risk. The "Prices Paid" component of the ISM Manufacturing PMI (84.6) hit a four-year high, suggesting core inflation will remain "sticky" through the summer. Tuesday’s CPI release will be the critical first test for incoming Chair Kevin Warsh.

### 2. China: Export Engine vs. Sluggish Consumption
China’s economy is showing clear structural divergence. High-tech manufacturing and exports (notably EVs and lithium batteries) continue to lead growth, offsetting the property sector slump. However, the official non-manufacturing PMI falling to **49.4** highlights the fragility of domestic demand. The PBoC maintains a "targeted easing" stance, with broad-based stimulus looking unlikely in the near term.

### 3. Eurozone: Stagflation Risks Intensify
Q1 GDP grew only **0.1%**, while inflation rose to **3.0%**. Faced with high energy costs and potential U.S. auto tariffs, the ECB is caught in a policy dilemma—needing to curb inflation without tipping a fragile economy into a deep recession.

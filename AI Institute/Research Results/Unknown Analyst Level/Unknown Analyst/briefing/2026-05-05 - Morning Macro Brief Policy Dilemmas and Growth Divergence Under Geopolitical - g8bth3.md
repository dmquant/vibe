# Morning Macro Brief: Policy Dilemmas and Growth Divergence Under Geopolitical Shocks
**Date:** May 6, 2026 | **By:** Chief Economist

## 1. Executive Summary
The global macroeconomic environment is confronting a second energy shock triggered by escalating tensions in the Middle East. The US Federal Reserve has maintained interest rates but pivoted toward a hawkish stance. China's manufacturing sector shows resilience driven by exports, while its services sector drags on domestic demand. The Eurozone is mired in a classic "stagflation" trap. Markets have significantly repriced 2026 rate cut expectations downward.

---

## 2. Regional Deep-Dive

### **US: Hawkish Pause & Leadership Transition**
*   **Policy Move:** The FOMC held the federal funds rate steady at **3.50%–3.75%** in late April. However, the statement removed the "easing bias," highlighting stalled progress on disinflation.
*   **Leadership Change:** Markets are bracing for mid-June, when **Kevin Warsh** is expected to take over as Fed Chair. Warsh is perceived as more hawkish than Powell, particularly regarding the inflationary impact of potential tariffs.
*   **Market Impact:** Traders have priced out rate cuts for the remainder of 2026, with some pricing in a 10% probability of a "re-hike" if oil prices continue to surge.

### **China: A "Tale of Two PMIs"**
*   **Manufacturing Resilience:** The official manufacturing PMI hit **50.3** in April, while the Caixin (RatingDog) PMI surged to **52.2**, signaling strength in high-tech manufacturing and export orders.
*   **Domestic Weakness:** The non-manufacturing PMI fell to **49.4** (a 40-month low), with both construction and services contracting, reflecting slow domestic recovery amid the property market bottoming process.
*   **Energy Buffer:** With large strategic reserves and refining capacity, China remains better buffered against global energy shocks compared to the US and Europe.

### **Eurozone: Energy-Driven Stagflation**
*   **Inflation Spike:** Headline CPI jumped to **3.0%** in April (up from 2.6%), driven primarily by a **10.9%** surge in energy costs.
*   **Growth Stagnation:** Q1 GDP growth was a meager **0.1%**, and the April composite PMI slipped into contraction at **48.6**. Despite the slowdown, the ECB is expected to hike in June and July to anchor inflation expectations.

## Executive Summary
This week, global markets enter a "data-heavy phase," with the core logic centered on testing the resilience of the Fed's high-interest-rate environment. Latest payroll data surpassed expectations, indicating a robust U.S. labor market and reducing the urgency for near-term rate cuts. Meanwhile, China's April PMI data released positive signals, with domestic recovery momentum strengthening. We believe the global macro environment is shifting from "inflation-driven" to "growth-divergence."

## Central Bank Watch
- **The Fed**: In recent remarks, Powell emphasized that the impact of AI productivity tools like DeepSeek V4 on the long-term neutral rate is still under assessment. Despite stalled disinflation, the Fed has no intention of further rate hikes.
- **PBoC**: Expected to maintain reasonably ample liquidity through MLF operations this month to support government bond issuance.
- **ECB**: Lagarde has signaled that June could likely be the starting point for rate cuts.

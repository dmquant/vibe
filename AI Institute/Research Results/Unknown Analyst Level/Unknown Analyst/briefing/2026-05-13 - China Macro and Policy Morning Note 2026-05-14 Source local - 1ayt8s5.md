Base date: 2026-05-14 [Source: local shell `date +%Y-%m-%d` 2026-05-14]. This note uses official releases, exchange/market data and economic calendars that were verifiable at the time of writing; items not yet released today are treated as schedule points and trading triggers, not actuals.

## Priority Takeaways

- China’s macro mix remains “resilient real activity, uneven price repair and continued policy support”: first-quarter GDP grew 5.0% year on year [Source: NBS 2026-04-16], while April CPI rose 1.2% year on year and PPI rose 2.8% year on year [Source: NBS 2026-05-12], showing that demand repair and upstream price pressure coexist.
- External demand remains a near-term positive contributor: April exports in RMB terms rose 9.8% year on year and imports rose 20.6% year on year [Source: Xinhua 2026-05-09]. This supports sentiment toward A-share exporters, ports/shipping and advanced manufacturing, while also making the market more sensitive to external-policy headlines.
- Monetary conditions are stable rather than aggressively stimulative: on May 13 the PBOC conducted CNY 500mn of 7-day reverse repos at 1.40%, with CNY 26.0bn maturing and a net withdrawal of CNY 25.5bn [Source: China Financial Information Network 2026-05-13]. The same day, the weighted average DR007 fell to 1.4416% [Source: China Financial Information Network 2026-05-13].
- The renminbi is today’s key risk-appetite constraint: USD/CNY was 6.7908, down 0.02% on the day [Source: DowFutures.org, accessed 2026-05-14], while DXY was 98.524, up 0.16% [Source: DowFutures.org, accessed 2026-05-14]. Currency stability helps foreign-risk sentiment, but dollar resilience limits broad valuation expansion.
- A-share style should not chase growth indiscriminately: in the prior trading session the Shanghai Composite closed at 4,242.57, up 0.67%, the Shenzhen Component at 16,089.75, up 1.67%, the ChiNext Index at 4,038.33, up 2.63%, with combined turnover of CNY 3.2397tn [Source: Sina Finance 2026-05-13]. Growth beta is strong, but rates, FX and policy delivery jointly determine persistence.

## CNY, Rates, Liquidity and Policy Expectations

- FX: USD/CNY was 6.7908, down 0.02% on the day [Source: DowFutures.org, accessed 2026-05-14]; DXY was 98.524, up 0.16% [Source: DowFutures.org, accessed 2026-05-14]. Short-term renminbi stability supports risk appetite, but a firm dollar limits foreign-flow elasticity.
- Liquidity: on May 13, the weighted average DR007 was 1.4416%, down 1.70bp from the prior trading day [Source: China Financial Information Network 2026-05-13]; the weighted average R007 was 1.5331%, down 2.84bp [Source: China Financial Information Network 2026-05-13]. Low and stable money-market pricing suggests the PBOC can keep interbank conditions calm without large injections.
- Open market: on May 13, the 7-day reverse-repo operation was CNY 500mn at 1.40%, with CNY 26.0bn maturing and a net withdrawal of CNY 25.5bn [Source: China Financial Information Network 2026-05-13]. If today’s operations remain small, the market will read policy as “stabilize liquidity and wait for credit data.”
- Bond rates: the active 10-year CGB 250004 yield fell 0.8bp to 1.665%, while the active 30-year CGB 2500002 yield fell 0.75bp to 1.9195% [Source: China Financial Information Network 2026-05-13]. Low long-end rates still support dividend-asset valuations, but they also raise crowding risk in low-volatility strategies.
- Policy expectations: the PBOC first-quarter policy report emphasized moderately loose policy, countercyclical adjustment and structural tools [Source: PBOC 2026-05-09]. The base case is continued support rather than one-off heavy stimulus; credit, property sales and price data will determine whether easing expectations are upgraded.

## A-Share Style, Sectors and Risk Appetite

- Style allocation: in the prior session, the ChiNext Index rose 2.63%, clearly outperforming the Shanghai Composite’s 0.67% gain [Source: Sina Finance 2026-05-13]. Growth has the short-term lead, but with DXY at 98.524 [Source: DowFutures.org, accessed 2026-05-14] and long-end U.S. yields still elevated, the preferred stance is a barbell of “high-dividend financials + export manufacturing + selected growth.”
- Cyclicals: April PPI rose 2.8% year on year and 1.7% month on month [Source: NBS 2026-05-12], while imports rose 20.6% year on year [Source: Xinhua 2026-05-09]. Resources, nonferrous metals, chemicals and transportation benefit from nominal-price repair; the risk is margin squeeze for midstream/downstream firms.
- Exporters and advanced manufacturing: April exports were CNY 2.48tn, up 9.8% year on year [Source: Xinhua 2026-05-09], and the manufacturing PMI new-orders index was 50.8 [Source: NBS 2026-04-30]. Electrical equipment, machinery, electronic components and firms with stronger global pricing power rank first.
- Consumption and autos: April CPI rose 1.2% year on year [Source: NBS 2026-05-12], but auto sales fell 25.5% year on year [Source: AP 2026-05-12]. Consumer exposure should avoid names driven only by valuation repair and focus on cash flow, defensive qualities and clear policy subsidies.
- Financials and dividends: DR007 was 1.4416% [Source: China Financial Information Network 2026-05-13], and the 10-year CGB yield was 1.665% [Source: China Financial Information Network 2026-05-13]. Low money-market rates and low long-end rates continue to support banks, insurers, telecom operators and utilities, but crowding should be monitored through turnover share and volatility.
- Property chain: first-quarter real-estate development investment fell 11.2% year on year [Source: NBS 2026-04-16]. Property, building materials and home-furnishing trades remain policy-driven; higher allocation is justified only when sales and financing data improve together.

# China Macro and Policy Morning Note — 2026-05-08[source: shell date +%Y-%m-%d]

## Priority Conclusions

- The macro mix is “resilient production, weaker services and property, and prices moving away from deflation risk.” The latest high-frequency macro signal is April PMI: manufacturing PMI was 50.3%, down 0.1 percentage point from the prior month; production was 51.5%, new orders 50.6%, new export orders 50.3%; non-manufacturing business activity was 49.4%, construction 48.0%, services 49.6%, and the composite PMI output index 50.1%[source: https://www.stats.gov.cn/english/PressRelease/202605/t20260506_1963595.html queried 2026-05-08].
- First-quarter growth is still strong enough to give policymakers patience: GDP was RMB 33,419.3 billion, up 5.0% y/y; March industrial value added rose 5.7% y/y and January-March rose 6.1%; March retail sales rose 1.7% y/y and January-March rose 2.4%; January-March fixed-asset investment was RMB 10,270.8 billion, up 1.7%, while private investment fell 2.2%[source: https://www.stats.gov.cn/english/PressRelease/202604/t20260420_1963362.html queried 2026-05-08; https://www.stats.gov.cn/english/PressRelease/202604/t20260417_1963356.html queried 2026-05-08; https://www.stats.gov.cn/english/PressRelease/202604/t20260417_1963351.html queried 2026-05-08; https://www.stats.gov.cn/english/PressRelease/202604/t20260417_1963355.html queried 2026-05-08].
- The policy signal is supportive but not urgently reflationary: the April 28 Politburo meeting stressed a systematic response to external shocks, stronger energy and resource security, more proactive fiscal policy and moderately loose monetary policy[source: https://hk.marketscreener.com/news/china-s-politburo-reiterates-vows-to-keep-markets-stable-boost-energy-security-ce7f59ddde8af025 queried 2026-05-08]; April LPRs were unchanged at 3.0% for the 1-year tenor and 3.5% for the 5-year-plus tenor[source: https://finance.people.com.cn/n1/2026/0420/c1004-40704756.html queried 2026-05-08].
- Yuan pressure looks manageable, but chasing yuan strength is not attractive: the May 7 USD/CNY fixing was 6.8487[source: https://finance.sina.com.cn/jjxw/2026-05-07/doc-inhwzrtk7808323.shtml queried 2026-05-08], while overnight USD/CNH was around 6.8064 and DXY was 98.23[source: https://query1.finance.yahoo.com/v8/finance/chart/USDCNH%3DX?range=5d&interval=1d queried 2026-05-08; https://query1.finance.yahoo.com/v8/finance/chart/DX-Y.NYB?range=5d&interval=1d queried 2026-05-08]. FX reserves were USD 3.4105 trillion at end-April, up USD 68.4 billion from end-March, a 2.05% increase[source: https://english.news.cn/20260507/802bb4e3b52545cca1e42f46dae7654a/c.html queried 2026-05-08].
- A-share implication: growth remains in control, but positioning should shift from broad momentum chasing to earnings and order-book validation. On May 7, the Shanghai Composite closed at 4,180.09, up 0.48%; the Shenzhen Component at 15,641.89, up 1.18%; ChiNext at 3,833.06, up 1.45%; combined Shanghai-Shenzhen turnover was about RMB 3.14 trillion[source: https://finance.ce.cn/stock/gsgdbd/202605/t20260507_2950051.shtml queried 2026-05-08]. Optical communications, semiconductors, components and robotics sustained risk appetite, while oil and gas names corrected[source: https://www.chinanews.com.cn/cj/2026/05-07/10616881.shtml queried 2026-05-08].

## Yuan, Rates, Liquidity and Policy Expectations

- FX: the May 7 fixing was 6.8487[source: https://finance.sina.com.cn/jjxw/2026-05-07/doc-inhwzrtk7808323.shtml queried 2026-05-08], and offshore USD/CNH was around 6.8064[source: https://query1.finance.yahoo.com/v8/finance/chart/USDCNH%3DX?range=5d&interval=1d queried 2026-05-08]. FX reserves were USD 3.4105 trillion at end-April, up USD 68.4 billion m/m, a 2.05% increase[source: https://english.news.cn/20260507/802bb4e3b52545cca1e42f46dae7654a/c.html queried 2026-05-08], showing that valuation effects and a softer dollar have cushioned external pressure.
- Liquidity: as of the May 6 close, DR001 was 1.2691% and DR007 was 1.3699%, below the 1.4% 7-day reverse repo policy-rate area[source: https://www.cs.com.cn/xwzx/01/2026/05/07/detail_2026050710009738.html queried 2026-05-08]. Funding is still loose, but crowded short-end bond trades should watch for open-market withdrawals or government-bond supply shocks.
- Bonds: early on May 7, the 10-year CGB “26附息国债05” yield was 1.7590%, up 0.1bp, and the 30-year CGB “26超长特别国债02” yield was 2.2440%, up 0.2bp[source: https://news.10jqka.com.cn/20260507/c676497007.shtml queried 2026-05-08]. Low long-end yields reflect easing expectations, but growth resilience and PPI repair limit room for another large rally.
- Policy expectations: April LPRs stayed at 3.0% for the 1-year tenor and 3.5% for the 5-year-plus tenor[source: https://finance.people.com.cn/n1/2026/0420/c1004-40704756.html queried 2026-05-08]. Together with the RMB 300.0 billion outright reverse repo on May 6[source: https://finance.people.com.cn/n1/2026/0506/c1004-40714583.html queried 2026-05-08], this suggests the PBOC prefers quantity and tenor tools for liquidity smoothing over an immediate new price-based easing round.

- Technology: on May 7, optical communications, internet and semiconductors rose, while coal and oil/gas fell[source: https://www.chinanews.com.cn/cj/2026/05-07/10616881.shtml queried 2026-05-08]. With April new export orders PMI at 50.3%[source: https://www.stats.gov.cn/english/PressRelease/202605/t20260506_1963595.html queried 2026-05-08], CPO, PCB, server power, memory and AI-device chains remain the relative-strength areas.
- Domestic demand: March retail sales rose 1.7% y/y and January-March rose 2.4%[source: https://www.stats.gov.cn/english/PressRelease/202604/t20260417_1963351.html queried 2026-05-08]. Consumption is not a broad recovery; prioritize online penetration, services consumption, appliance trade-in beneficiaries and brands with pricing power.

## Institutional Read: Market Character

This is not a low-volatility bull market. It is a high-turnover, high-beta, highly differentiated structural market. The strongest industries share three traits: a clear industrial trend, enough turnover to absorb institutional capital, and either earnings or policy narratives that can be verified. Weak sectors also have a clear issue: cheapness alone is not enough; without incremental logic, they remain marginalized even in a heavy-turnover tape.

Institutional portfolios should balance core exposure across AI compute, semiconductor equipment, optical communications, PCB, energy metals, and military electronics, rather than concentrate on a single crowded leg. For names that have risen too fast and absorbed too much turnover, use reopening-day relative strength to decide whether to cut weight. For midstream equipment and resource names that have corrected but show improving fundamentals, wait for volume confirmation before raising allocation.

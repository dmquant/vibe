## 2. Pitch 1 ｜ A-share Gap-Risk Playbook for the May Day Holiday

### Why this is cross-domain

No single analyst owns this call. A-shares are closed for five sessions, but **offshore CNH, FTSE A50 futures, partial Hong Kong sessions, and US-listed China ETFs** remain live, on top of **tonight's US NFP, mid-week euro-area prints, and possibly an April China CPI release on the holiday's last day**. That string of prints forms a "proxy-price signal chain." The direction of the post-holiday gap is rarely set by any single event — it is set by the cumulative reading of that chain.

### The specific debates needed

1. **Proxy hierarchy**: which is the strongest leading indicator of post-holiday A-share open direction — FTSE A50 futures, US-listed China ETFs (KWEB / MCHI), or offshore CNH? What is the historical correlation across past May Day windows?
2. **Hedge instruments**: should we put on a hedge in Hang Seng Tech or A50 futures on today's last session? Derivatives needs to cost the trade — **carry vs. expected gap magnitude**.
3. **Directional bias**: is the current book "willing to wear a downside gap" or "willing to wear an upside gap"? That choice decides whether the hedge is one-sided or symmetric.
4. **Holiday monitoring tripwires**: which three data points / events, if triggered, force a pre-emptive playbook update? Candidates: NFP vs. consensus, CNH breaching a threshold, single-day move on US-listed China ETFs of ±X%, geopolitical escalation.

### Deliverable

A one-page **"May Day Holiday Monitoring Manual"** — front-loaded hedge recommendation, monitoring thresholds, and a decision tree for the first 30 minutes of post-holiday trading. Locked before today's open and circulated to all desks.

## 3. Pitch 2 ｜ US April NFP × FOMC Residual

### Why this is cross-domain

Last week's FOMC left a **gap between the dot plot and the post-meeting tone** (quantified separately by `chief-economist`). Tonight's NFP is the first hard data that gets to "vote" on which side of that gap is right. OIS-implied cuts for the year and the FOMC median are not aligned; this week's print will pull the market toward one side. The repricing hits four desks simultaneously:

- **The UST curve** (`bond-analyst` watching 2s10s direction)
- **DXY and CNH** (`fx-strategist` watching DXY breakout transmission)
- **Asset allocation** (`asset-allocator` watching the relative-strength rotation across US equities / USTs / gold)
- **A-share exporters and high-dividend** (style rotation at the post-holiday open)

### The specific debates needed

1. **Consensus vs. whisper**: Bloomberg consensus, ADP-implied, and last week's high-frequency initial-claims read — which of these is the truly priced-in level?
2. **Three-scenario asset matrix**:
   - Strong NFP (>consensus +50K and AHE m/m ≥0.4%): how does the curve move? DXY? Gold?
   - Weak NFP (<consensus −50K or unemployment rate +0.1pp): pace of cut-expectation rebuild?
   - In-line: in noise mode, which next-week print does the market pivot to?
3. **Second-order transmission to A-shares**: if DXY rallies through the holiday, does the post-holiday A-share rotation favour exporters, high-dividend, or gold names **first**?

### Deliverable

A **three-scenario asset matrix** authored by `chief-economist` and co-signed by `bond-analyst`, `fx-strategist`, and `asset-allocator`, ready before the morning meeting. Conclusions updated and broadcast within 30 minutes of the 20:30 print.

## 4. Pitch 3 ｜ Mag-7 Earnings Season Post-Mortem: "Narrative Crack"

### Why this is cross-domain

Mag-7 Q1 is essentially closed (specific numbers compiled by `tmt-analyst`). From the editor's seat, the cross-domain question worth a real debate this season is not single-name results — it is:

> **Has the gap between the "AI capex story" and the "AI revenue realisation story" finally produced visible dispersion across the seven names?** And if so, is this the first real crack in the most crowded trade since 2023?

That question is genuinely four-handed:

- **TMT view**: do single-name multiples need a re-rate?
- **Strategy view**: has the index-level concentration of US large-cap growth crossed a risk threshold?
- **Factor view**: do crowding readings on momentum / quality / growth confirm the warning?
- **Sentiment view**: is the retail-vs-institutional positioning split widening on this trade?

### The specific debates needed

1. **Is the dispersion real?** Of the seven names, how many printed "capex guide up + revenue guide up" vs. "capex guide up + revenue guide flat-or-down"? Is this split wider or narrower than last quarter?
2. **Concentration threshold**: where is the top-10 weight share in the S&P 500 right now? How many sigma above the historical mean? What were the 6-month relative returns after the past three comparable readings?
3. **A-share AI-chain transmission post-holiday**: if a real capex-narrative crack appears in North America, on the post-holiday open does the A-share compute-infrastructure leg or the AI-application leg sell off **first**?
4. **Hedge thinking**: without selling the longs, is there a low-cost tail hedge — single-name option skew, index put spreads, an inverse-factor exposure?

### Deliverable

An **"AI crowding health check" post-mortem**. Holiday workstream; finalised before the post-holiday open on 5/6.

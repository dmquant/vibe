# A-Share Factor Daily Performance Report (2026-05-05)

**Issued by: Chief Quant**

## 1. Executive Summary
The current A-share factor landscape is characterized by **high-level volatility, extreme momentum crowding, and a nascent style rotation.**
- **Momentum**: Driven by the "AI+" national strategy and hardware earnings, returns are exceptionally strong, but crowding has hit a 5-year historical extreme.
- **Quality**: Performance remains steady as market pricing logic shifts back to fundamental verification.
- **Value & Dividend**: These show safe-haven value amidst volatility; low crowding levels offer attractive risk-reward profiles.
- **Size**: Large-cap growth has dominated recently; however, small-cap styles are expected to show elasticity in May following the conclusion of the earnings season.

## 3. Deep Dive & Risk Assessment

### 3.1 Momentum: Beware of "Stampede" Risks
Crowding in the Momentum factor is nearing 5-year highs. Specifically in the AI hardware sector, institutional "clustering" is evident, with turnover and trading volume shares in the danger zone. Investors should be alert to potential "flash crashes" at high levels or a rapid rotation toward laggard factors in May.

### 3.2 Quality: From Narrative to Logic
As the April earnings season concludes, the market's tolerance for "pure storytelling" has diminished. Quality factors (e.g., ROE stability, earnings growth) have become the core pillar of stock prices. Companies in high-end manufacturing and semiconductor equipment that successfully verified their earnings have earned significant valuation premiums.

### 3.3 Style Rotation Outlook
Currently, crowding levels for Value and Small-cap factors are at low percentiles. With marginal improvements in macro expectations and the potential clearing of crowded growth trades, May is likely to see a distinct "High-to-Low" rotation. We recommend focusing on high-quality, low-valuation stocks with superior chip structures and better odds.

## Priority Conclusions

1. **The curve is bear-flattening.** On 2026-05-12, Treasury yields rose across the curve: 2Y/5Y/30Y were each up 5bp on the day, while 10Y rose 4bp. Over the week, 2Y rose 7bp versus 3bp for 10Y, narrowing 2s10s from +50bp to +46bp.
2. **Long-end level risk remains the key portfolio issue.** The 30Y closed at 5.03%, up 5bp on the day and 17bp year-to-date. 10s30s is +57bp, so the long end is still positively sloped, but the marginal weekly change is limited.
3. **Term spreads are positive, but this is not trading like a classic easing cycle.** 2s10s is +46bp, 3m10y is +76bp, and 2s30s is +103bp. The positive slope appears driven more by long-term term premium and fiscal-supply compensation than by aggressive front-end easing expectations.
4. **Markets are not meaningfully pricing 2026 rate cuts.** A FedWatch-style calculation based on 2026-05-12 CME 30-Day Fed Funds futures settlements implies only a 4.3% probability of a 25bp cut at the June FOMC meeting. For December, the path implies a 78.4% probability of staying at 3.50%-3.75% and a 21.6% probability of moving to 3.75%-4.00%. In other words, the implied number of 2026 cuts is close to zero, with a small hike-tail rather than a clear easing tail.

## Positioning And Risk Management

- **Duration:** Keep duration neutral to slightly short and wait for 2Y-5Y repricing around inflation data or FOMC communication. If 10Y approaches 4.60%-4.75%, adding core duration in stages becomes more attractive, but chasing the 30Y is not warranted.
- **Curve:** 2s10s is +46bp. Further bear-flattening room is smaller than it was at the start of the year, but if markets continue to remove cuts, 2Y can still drag the curve flatter. Watch 3m10y and 5s30s for stability.
- **Credit:** Higher rates do not automatically pressure investment-grade credit immediately, but a 30Y above 5% raises liability discount rates and refinancing costs. Long-duration credit should require higher spread compensation.
- **Triggers:** The next key inputs are 2026-05-13 PPI, initial jobless claims, the June 16-17 FOMC meeting, and Treasury supply cadence.

## Follow-Up Items

1. Watch whether the 2Y can hold above 4.00%; if it does, the market is still removing cuts, and front-end duration should not be added too early.
2. Track the depth of buying around 4.50% in 10Y; if 10Y breaks higher while 2Y does not follow, the curve could shift from bear-flattening to bear-steepening.
3. Check whether the 2026-12-09 Fed funds futures distribution moves away from “hold/hike-tail” and back toward a “cut-tail”; that would be the key confirmation for Treasury bulls.

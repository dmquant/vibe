# US-China Treasury Yield Curve & Rate Cut Expectations Report (2026-05-11)

## Executive Summary
1. **US Treasuries: Bear Steepening.** Yields rose across the curve as Brent crude surged past $105/bbl amid heightened geopolitical tensions. Markets have almost entirely priced out a June rate cut, shifting focus to a "higher-for-longer" regime.
2. **China Bonds: Normal Steepening.** The curve remains upward-sloping. Long-end yields are edging higher due to supply pressure from ultra-long special treasury bonds, while the PBOC maintains ample short-term liquidity.
3. **Yield Divergence: Widening Spreads.** The 10Y US-China yield spread has widened to approximately 263 bps, reflecting a profound decoupling of monetary policy cycles.

## US Market Analysis: Inflation and Geopolitics
*   **Yield Trends**: The 10Y UST yield closed near 4.39%, while the 30Y is testing the 5.0% psychological threshold. The primary driver is energy-led inflation risk as Middle East conflicts threaten the Strait of Hormuz, pushing oil prices to multi-year highs.
*   **Term Premium**: The 10Y-2Y spread stands at +46 bps. Unlike the deep inversion seen in 2024, the current steepening is a "bear steepener" driven by higher inflation compensation in long-dated bonds.
*   **Leadership Transition**: Fed Chair Jerome Powell is set to step down on May 15. His likely successor, Kevin Warsh, is perceived as more hawkish, with markets pricing in a "regime change" to combat entrenched inflation.

## China Market Analysis: Structural Easing & Supply Pressure
*   **Yield Trends**: The 10Y CGB yield remains near historical lows of 1.76%. Robust April export data (+14.1% YoY) has reduced the urgency for aggressive broad-based rate cuts, shifting the focus to structural support.
*   **Curve Shape**: The Ministry of Finance's decision to maintain the current ultra-long bond issuance structure has created supply-side pressure on the long end. Meanwhile, the PBOC is using secondary market bond trading to manage liquidity and curve shape.
*   **Geopolitical Context**: Investors are adopting a "wait-and-see" approach ahead of the high-stakes Trump-Xi summit scheduled for May 14-15.

## Rate Cut Probabilities & Central Bank Outlook
*   **Federal Reserve (Fed)**:
    *   **June Meeting**: ~95% probability of a rate hold (3.50%-3.75%).
    *   **Implied Path**: Markets are now pricing in a potential pause in the easing cycle until 2027. The resurgence of inflation (CPI 3.3%) has effectively "paralyzed" the Fed's accommodative bias.
*   **People's Bank of China (PBOC)**:
    *   **Policy Stance**: "Moderately loose." The upcoming May 20 LPR decision is expected to remain unchanged, with easing delivered via targeted re-lending tools.
    *   **Liquidity**: While there is still room for RRR cuts, the PBOC is prioritizing stability amid imported inflation risks from rising energy costs.

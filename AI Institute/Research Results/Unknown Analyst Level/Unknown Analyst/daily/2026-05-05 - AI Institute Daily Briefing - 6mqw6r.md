## VII. Three to Five Core Views

### View 1: The Main Line Has Shifted from "Narrative Buying" to "Earnings Buying" — Stock Selection Must Upgrade

STAR 50's pre-holiday +5.19% was not driven by broad sector sentiment; it was driven by **verified quarterly earnings and order data**: Cambricon Q1 net profit +185% YoY, GigaDevice Q1 net profit +523% YoY, and Verisilicon AI computing orders representing >90% of new signings [source: Cailianshe 2026-04-30 https://www.cls.cn/detail/2361107]. At the same time, Foxconn Industrial Internet — also nominally within the AI/computing chain — fell 5.10%, and Novatel (新易盛) pulled back on shrinking volume. The message is clear: "buying the tech theme" is no longer sufficient. Investors must distinguish **earnings-verified STAR core names** from **pure sentiment plays**; only the former merit holding.

### View 2: The 5-Day Information Blackout Is the Biggest Wild Card — Opening Price ≠ Medium-Term Direction

Five consecutive trading days of holiday (2026-05-01 to 2026-05-05) have accumulated external news flow — US-China trade developments, overseas market moves — that will be priced in all at once when the market reopens on 2026-05-06. A gap open (direction unknown) is a realistic risk. The correct reopen strategy is: **observe turnover and breadth first, then decide whether to chase or trim** — do not position around the opening print.

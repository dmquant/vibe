# A-Share Factor Daily Performance Analysis (2026-05-14)

## Executive Summary
Today's A-share market exhibits a clear pattern of "Momentum Persistence vs. Small-Cap Crowding." While the **Momentum** and **Small-Cap (Size)** factors continue to deliver positive returns, driven by the lingering bullish sentiment from 2025, their crowding metrics are approaching historical extremes. This signals a heightened risk of a style reversal. Conversely, the **Value** factor remains in a valuation trough with extremely thin investor participation, presenting a compelling contrarian opportunity.

## In-Depth Analysis

### 1. Momentum & Small-Cap: Fragility Amidst Prosperity
The Momentum factor led gains today, primarily fueled by the sustained trend in TMT and AI sectors. However, the **Small-Cap factor (Size) crowding has hit the 92nd percentile**, suggesting that liquidity premiums may be overextended. High turnover rates in micro-cap stocks indicate speculative heat; any shift in market sentiment could trigger a sharp drawdown similar to early 2024.

### 2. The Contrarian Case for Value
Value (Low PE/PB) was the laggard today with a return of -0.35%. From a technical perspective, its crowding has plummeted to the 15th percentile. With Large-Cap Growth valuations stretched, defensive value segments (e.g., high-dividend yield stocks) are becoming attractive "safe harbors" for long-term institutional capital.

### 3. Quality as the Portfolio Anchor
The Quality factor (ROE/Net Profit Growth) remained steady, rising 0.45%. Following two years of intense style rotation, both foreign and domestic institutional investors are re-focusing on fundamental pricing. Quality factors are regaining their appeal as a core neutral allocation.

## Key Watchpoints
- **Style Reversal Signals:** Monitor whether small-cap trading volume starts to stall despite high prices (divergence).
- **Macro Hedging:** Track the impact of 10-year government bond yield fluctuations on high-dividend value stocks.

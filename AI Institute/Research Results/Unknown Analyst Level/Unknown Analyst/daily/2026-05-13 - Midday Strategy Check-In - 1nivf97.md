## Priority Conclusions

- **The HK/US linkage is still the hot-CPI / USD-JPY / AI-semiconductor deleveraging tail, not a purely local A-share event.** The morning chain links 10Y UST at `4.55-4.70%`, `USD/JPY 159.5`, a US AI/semiconductor liquidity gap of `USD 50-80bn`, HK ADRs, and A-share hard-tech selling pressure into one risk channel.[R1][R3][R4]
- **HK/ADR price action should be treated as this afternoon's lead indicator.** The research explicitly says offshore de-risking would first hit ADRs `BABA`, `BIDU`, `PDD` and HK tech names `腾讯 0700`, `美团 3690`, before feeding through southbound books into 科创 50 / 创业板 and A-share technology risk appetite.[R1]
- **China EV is not an "industry-wide collapse", but the issue has moved from inventory to "Stack A / Stack B" profit repricing.** April passenger NEV wholesale was `1.22 million` and retail was `883,000`, leaving a `337,000` wholesale-retail gap; new-energy passenger-car exports were about `420,000`, so exports and channel inventory are still relief valves, but OECD non-tariff rules are compressing the intelligent-vehicle premium.[R5][R6][R7][R8]
- **The A-share hard-tech debate is financing function versus liquidity and credit tail risk.** "Flash reverse-merger" deals accounted for about `23%` of 2026 YTD restructuring filings, which supports hard-tech financing; but if three-year performance-commitment default rates reach `45%` or `60%`, the risk chain moves into city commercial banks, pledged shares, LGFVs, and CNY.[R10]
- **The US AI-infrastructure theme has not been invalidated, but the valuation anchor is shifting from GPU orders to sites, power, water, and local approval.** Data-center demand is still strong: the IEA 2025 base case puts global data-center electricity use at about `945 TWh` by 2030, and US data-center electricity demand could reach `325-580 TWh` by 2028, but grid, water, and local permitting constraints are creating a site premium.[R9]

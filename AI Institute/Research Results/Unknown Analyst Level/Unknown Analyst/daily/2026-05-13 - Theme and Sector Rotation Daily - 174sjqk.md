# Theme and Sector Rotation Daily

**Date anchor: every reference to “today” in this report means 2026-05-13 [Source: shell date +%Y-%m-%d].**

## Priority Conclusions

- **This was not a broad rally; it was renewed concentration in “AI compute + power backbone + memory chips.”** The ChiNext closed at 4,038.33, up 2.63%, and made a record high; Shanghai/Shenzhen/Beijing turnover reached RMB 32,642 billion, above RMB 3 trillion for 6 consecutive trading days [Source: https://www.eeo.com.cn/2026/0513/874496.shtml]. Liquidity can still support high-beta themes, but leadership is narrow, so late chasing has lower tolerance for error.
- **Capital is leaving defensive and old-consumption assets and moving into tech hardware, IT services, semiconductors, and power equipment.** Main-force inflows were RMB 8.886 billion for consumer electronics, RMB 5.687 billion for IT services, RMB 5.000 billion for semiconductors, RMB 4.278 billion for other power-source equipment, RMB 2.170 billion for power utilities, and RMB 0.861 billion for grid equipment [Source: https://www.nbd.com.cn/articles/2026-05-13/4390584.html].
- **The strongest short-term catalyst is policy validation of the “compute needs electricity” narrative.** The Action Plan on mutual empowerment between artificial intelligence and energy laid out 29 key tasks and set a 2030 objective for world-leading clean-energy supply capability for AI compute facilities and world-leading dedicated AI technology R&D and application in the energy sector [Source: https://www.nea.gov.cn/20260508/4dae97ca01d348e4871bb8654be34b3a/c.html].
- **The main risk for the next 1-3 trading days is event realization plus crowded positioning.** Trump is making a state visit to China from May 13 to 15 [Source: https://english.www.gov.cn/news/202605/11/content_WS6a012e12c6d00ca5f9a0addc.html]. If trade, AI chips, and semiconductor supply-chain issues do not produce tradable details, today’s high-position tech names may face a “buy the rumor, sell the fact” reaction.

## Emerging Directions and Flow Watch

- **Compute-power coordination:** The new direction to watch is not plain utilities, but “AI data centers + power-source equipment + green-power absorption + grid dispatch.” The policy framework puts energy, compute, scenarios, data, and models into the same system and sets a 2030 target [Source: https://www.nea.gov.cn/20260508/4dae97ca01d348e4871bb8654be34b3a/c.html], turning power equipment from a cyclical trade into part of AI infrastructure.
- **AI terminals and the Huawei chain:** Consumer electronics and IT services have attracted larger inflows than most traditional sectors [Source: https://www.nbd.com.cn/articles/2026-05-13/4390584.html]. If they remain stronger than the index next session, treat them as AI diffusion from compute hardware into terminal applications.
- **Space economy:** Satellite ETF inflows remain positive, but RMB 33.7888 million and rank 58/1187 [Source: https://finance.sina.com.cn/money/fund/aiassistant/etfshzj/2026-05-13/doc-inhxteiv4689860.shtml] are weaker than AI and power. The strategy is to watch whether ETF subscriptions convert into strength in satellite manufacturing, communication payloads, and ground-equipment stocks.
- **Web3:** The U.S. Senate Banking Committee will hold a meeting at 10:00 on 2026-05-14 [Source: https://www.banking.senate.gov/hearings/05/08/2026/executive-session]. Crypto market-structure and stablecoin rules may affect global risk appetite, but A-shares did not show Web3 as a main theme today [Source: public news search 2026-05-13].
- **Quantum and biotech:** Public market reports today did not show quantum technology or biotech as strong A-share themes [Source: public news search 2026-05-13]. Quantum remains more tied to long-cycle policy and research breakthroughs, while biotech depends on clinical, BD, and regulatory events; neither should take capital away from the AI-compute core in the very short term.

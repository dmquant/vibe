## 2. Deep Dive

### Quality vs. Defensive Divergence
As we enter Q2 2026, the **Quality** factor continues its strong alpha capture. During the current earnings recovery cycle, institutional capital is heavily focused on segment leaders with stable ROE and ample cash flow. Conversely, the **Low Vol** and **Value** factors, which were strong at the start of the year, pulled back in April. This reflects a shift of risk-averse capital into more aggressive assets as the market breaks through key psychological levels.

### Momentum Factor: Extreme Concentration in AI Hardware
The **Momentum** factor recorded its highest monthly performance in recent years during April. Driven by the global AI infrastructure investment boom, A-share sectors such as computing power, optical modules, and semiconductor equipment formed intense price momentum. However, monitoring indicates that the **crowding** in these assets has hit the 90th percentile, with trading volume exceptionally concentrated.

### Size Style: Return of Large-cap Tech
Style preferences have shifted significantly from 2025's "Small/Micro-cap expansion" to "Large-cap Growth." The ChiNext Index hit a ten-year high in April, signaling a structural bull market driven by blue-chip tech stocks.

## 3. Risk Warnings
- **Crowding Risk**: Momentum in the computing power sector is extremely overextended. Following the Q1 earnings season, there is a risk of sharp drawdowns if growth rates fail to justify valuation logic.
- **Style Rotation**: Investors should be cautious of capital potentially rotating back into low-crowding Value/Dividend factors after the Labor Day holiday.

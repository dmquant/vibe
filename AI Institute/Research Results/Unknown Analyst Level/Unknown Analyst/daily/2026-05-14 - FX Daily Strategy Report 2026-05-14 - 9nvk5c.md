## Interest Rate Differentials & Policy Shifts
1.  **United States (Fed):** Fed Funds rate held at **3.50%–3.75%**. Chair Warsh's confirmation has solidified "higher-for-longer" expectations. With April PPI hitting 4.8% y/y, markets are pricing a 35% chance of a rate *hike*.
2.  **Eurozone (ECB):** Rates remain at **2.00%**. Despite CPI climbing to 3.0% on energy shocks, the ECB lags Fed hawkishness due to growth concerns.
3.  **Japan (BoJ):** Rates moved to **0.75%**, but real rates remain negative. The massive differential vs. USD continues to fuel the carry trade.
4.  **China (PBoC):** Maintaining an easing bias for growth but pausing cuts temporarily to stabilize the Yuan ahead of diplomatic talks.

## Capital Flows & Risk Factors
*   **Safe-Haven Inflows:** Closure of the **Strait of Hormuz** and US-Iran tensions have funneled capital into USD and commodity-linked currencies like AUD and NOK.
*   **Structural Outflows:** Japan’s persistent FDI outflows act as a structural drag on JPY, difficult to counter via intervention alone.
*   **Major Event Risks:**
    *   **Trump-Xi Summit:** A de-escalation in trade tariffs could see USD/CNY break below 6.75.
    *   **Energy Inflation:** "Iran war" costs are feeding into global PPI, sustaining upward pressure on global yields.

## Conclusions & Recommendations
*   **Strategy:** Maintain a neutral stance on CNY until summit outcomes are clear. Long USD remains the preferred hedge against geopolitical risks. Exercise extreme caution on JPY near the 158.00 level.

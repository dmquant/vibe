# Global FX Daily Insight (May 1, 2026)

## Executive Summary
Liquidity is notably thin today due to the Labor Day holiday across major Asian and European markets. The U.S. Dollar Index (DXY) continues its downward drift following dovish cues from the Federal Reserve's latest meeting. Concurrently, the Japanese Yen (JPY) is exhibiting significant strength amid a broad unwinding of carry trades, while the Chinese Yuan (CNY) remains resilient within its recent range.

### 1. US Dollar (USD): Pricing the End of Tightening
Fed Chair Powell confirmed on Thursday that "substantial progress" has been made on inflation, even as the labor market stays robust. Markets are now pricing in 50-75 bps of rate cuts for the latter half of 2026. The 10-year Treasury yield falling to 3.72% has effectively capped any USD recovery.

### 2. Japanese Yen (JPY): Accelerating Normalization
With record wage hikes confirmed in the "Shunto" negotiations, market consensus expects another BoJ rate hike in June. JPY’s role is shifting from a funding currency back to a safe-haven asset. Signs of a global growth slowdown are prompting aggressive short-covering of JPY positions.

### 3. Chinese Yuan (CNY): Stability Amid Recovery
While April's manufacturing PMI suggested internal demand needs further support, export performance outperformed expectations. The PBOC continues to use counter-cyclical factors to maintain exchange rate elasticity. During the Labor Day holiday, CNH is expected to be primarily driven by external USD moves.

## Interest Rate Differentials & Carry Trades
*   **US-Japan Spread:** The 10Y US-Japan yield gap has narrowed to ~240 bps (down from 320 bps at year-start). This has significantly eroded the attractiveness of USD/JPY carry trades, triggering a rapid unwinding.
*   **China-US Spread:** The yield curve remains inverted, but the spread has narrowed to around 140 bps as US yields decline, alleviating some capital outflow pressures.

## Capital Flow Monitoring
*   **Fixed Income:** Inflows have been observed into short-term JGBs and European government bonds, contrasting with outflows from US Treasuries.
*   **Emerging Markets:** While a weaker USD usually supports EM, recent global recession fears have directed flows into Asian economies with strong fundamentals, while high-beta LatAm currencies face pressure.

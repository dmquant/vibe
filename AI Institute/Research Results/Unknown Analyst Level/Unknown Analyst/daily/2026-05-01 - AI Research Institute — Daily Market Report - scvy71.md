## VII. Three to Five Core Views

**View 1: The STAR 50 volume breakout is real, but with a 25% April gain already priced in, chasing the limit-up price on 6 May open is a poor risk/reward.**  
STAR 50 rose 25.05% in April [Source: https://cn.dailyeconomic.com/2026/04/30/147770.html], advancing 325.90 points from 1,254.62 to 1,580.52, with pre-holiday volume expanding +31.3% — a genuine breakout, not thin-air momentum [Source: Investing.com STAR 50 historical data, query date 2026-05-01]. However, continuation requires sustained turnover. If 6 May total market volume falls meaningfully below CNY 2.759 trillion, switch hard-tech exposure from "chase" to "buy on pullbacks after opening volatility settles."

**View 2: Fund flows tell a "crowded rotation within the same theme" story — CNY 340.714 bn of semiconductor turnover hides sharp divergence inside the sector.**  
All-market major funds ran a net outflow of CNY 38.407 bn while the STAR board attracted CNY 7.839 bn [Source: https://www.stcn.com/article/detail/3892165.html]. Cambricon pulled in CNY 3.838 bn at the same time Foxconn Industrial shed CNY 2.949 bn [Source: ibid.] — capital is re-sorting within AI hardware, not buying the theme indiscriminately. Stick to leadership names with verifiable earnings (Cambricon Q1 revenue +159.56%; Q1 net profit +185.04% [Source: https://finance.eastmoney.com/a/202604303726438957.html]); avoid thematic followers without order-book validation.

**View 3: Leverage is the primary tail-risk amplifier for the post-holiday session and must be treated as a first-order variable.**  
Total margin balance of CNY 2.7323 trillion is near the high-water mark and has risen consecutively [Source: https://www.stcn.com/article/detail/3891169.html]; Guotai Haitong data shows margin financing as 10.1% of total turnover [Source: https://finance.sina.com.cn/wm/2026-04-29/doc-inhwczph6749460.shtml]. If margin balance falls more than CNY 15 bn in a single session post-holiday, treat it as Scenario C (Red) and execute forced risk reduction.

**View 4: Innovative drugs (ASCO 2026 catalyst) offer the most compelling low-crowding alternative if hard-tech momentum stalls.**  
The ASCO 2026 Annual Meeting runs 29 May – 2 June 2026 in Chicago; 12 Chinese pharmaceutical companies have 13 abstracts in plenary/late-breaking sessions [Source: https://www.asco-meeting.org/; https://finance.sina.com.cn/wm/2026-04-26/doc-inhvvqry9693853.shtml]. With biotech major funds showing a pre-holiday net outflow of CNY 2.603 bn [Source: https://www.stcn.com/article/detail/3892165.html], the sector is at low crowding — a credible partial hedge when semiconductor congestion triggers volatility.

**View 5: Post-holiday tactical framework follows a three-step sequence — verify volume, assess scenario, execute position.**  
Three scenarios: **Scenario A** (40% probability) — strong continuation, defined by turnover ≥ CNY 2.759 trillion, STAR 50 holding 1,511.39 and attacking 1,580.52, semiconductor turnover ≥ CNY 300 bn; hold core positions, do not chase open. **Scenario B** (40%) — volume contraction, defined by turnover CNY 2.2–2.5 trillion and STAR 50 retreating into 1,503–1,511; shift hard-tech to profit-taking, cut leverage by one-third, add small defensive buffer. **Scenario C** (20%) — volume breakdown, STAR 50 below MA5 1,503.06 and unable to recover intraday; cut offensive allocation to 50%, close all margin, keep only low-crowding earnings-verified names. Hold a 15-minute risk-check on 5 May (evening before reopening) to refresh the signal board.

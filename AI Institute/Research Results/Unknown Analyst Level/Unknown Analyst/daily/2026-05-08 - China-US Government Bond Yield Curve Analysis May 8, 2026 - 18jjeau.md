# China-US Government Bond Yield Curve Analysis (May 8, 2026)

## 1. Executive Summary
As of May 8, 2026, the US and Chinese government bond markets exhibit a sharp divergence in policy cycles:
- **United States:** The yield curve has fully returned to a "normalized" (upward-sloping) state. Driven by sticky inflation and geopolitical risks, rate cut expectations have been drastically scaled back, with markets now pricing a "Higher for Longer" regime.
- **China:** The yield curve remains in a flattened normal configuration, with yields anchored near historical lows supported by monetary easing. Despite a marginal uptick due to easing geopolitical tensions, the stance remains "moderately loose."
- **Spread:** The US-China 10Y yield spread remains wide at approximately 265bps, with policy divergence continuing to drive cross-border capital flows.

- **Term Spread:** The 10Y-2Y spread is approx. **+55bps**. The end of inversion reflects a return of the term premium and market concerns regarding long-term inflation and fiscal deficits.
- **Primary Drivers:** Geopolitical tensions in the Strait of Hormuz fueling energy inflation; uncertainty surrounding the Fed leadership transition (Kevin Warsh expected as new Chair).

### 2.2 Market Implied Rate Expectations
- **Upcoming Meetings:** The probability of a "Hold" at the June meeting (keeping rates at 3.50%-3.75%) is **95.9%**.
- **2026 Path:** Expectations have been cut to **0-1 rate cut** for the full year. With headline inflation rising to 3.3% in April, approx. 10% of the market is now hedging for a potential year-end hike.

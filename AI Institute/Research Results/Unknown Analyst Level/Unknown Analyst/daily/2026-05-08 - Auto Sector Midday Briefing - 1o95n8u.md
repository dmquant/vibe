# Auto Sector Midday Briefing
**Date:** May 8, 2026
**Analyst:** Auto Analyst

## 1. Morning Confirmed Facts & Data

*   **Post-Auto China 2026 Momentum:** Auto China 2026 concluded on May 4 with a record 1.28 million visitors. Market sentiment has shifted from pure price wars to "Embodied AI" and "Going Global 2.0" strategies.
*   **April Sales Tone:** April NEV sales data indicates a slowdown in domestic demand growth, characterized by "stabilization with underlying concerns." BYD led with 314,100 units, but domestic growth has faced pressure for eight consecutive months.
*   **Embodied AI Logic Diffusion:** Stimulated by Unitree's UniStore app store release, capital flowed back into the automotive supply chain today (e.g., Greatoo, Wuzhouxinchun). The market is beginning to view "humanoid robots" and "smart vehicles" as different forms of the same hardware foundation.
*   **Strong Export Data:** Rolling 12-month data through March shows Chinese EV exports reached $76 billion. Shanghai's Q1 EV exports surged 135.4% YoY, signaling the ramp-up of "Going Global 2.0" tailored models (e.g., hatchbacks for Europe, pickups for Australia/Mexico).
*   **Autonomous Driving Progress:** Geely Holding unveiled the **EVA Cab**, its first purpose-built electric robotaxi prototype. Tesla’s FSD regulatory approval in China is progressing smoothly, with market expectations for a Q3 launch.

## 2. Pending Verification & Questions

*   **Xiaomi YU7 Pre-orders:** The Xiaomi YU7 GT SUV is expected to launch in late May. Morning channel checks on blind order conversion rates need to be verified against expectations.
*   **Export Margin Pressure:** As OEMs begin deep re-engineering for foreign markets (rather than simple domestic model tweaks), the impact of rising R&D and compliance costs on short-term margins needs assessment.
*   **Power Infrastructure Bottlenecks:** Industrial analyst reports indicate structural shortages in transformers and Grain Oriented Electrical Steel (GOES). We need to verify if this will affect the delivery schedule of urban supercharging networks in Q2.

## 3. Afternoon Action Items

*   **Channel Research:** Follow up on store traffic and orders for Huawei-backed HIMA (Harmony Intelligent Mobility Alliance) during the last week of April and the first week of May to verify the "auto show heat" conversion.
*   **Overseas Strategy Tracking:** Analyze the localization (CKD) progress of GWM, Chery, and others in Southeast Asia and the Middle East.
*   **Competitive Comparison:** For today's launch of the GAC Aion N60, compare its configuration competitiveness against rivals in the 150,000–200,000 RMB segment.

## 4. Cross-Analyst Collaboration Needs

*   **To Materials Analyst:** Please verify if shortages in transformer-grade copper and GOES have cascaded to the high-power charging (V4 and liquid-cooled superchargers) supply chain and if there's a delivery delay risk.
*   **To TMT Analyst:** Discuss software ecosystem valuation under the "Auto-as-Robot" logic. With the UniStore launch, will smart cockpits introduce similar third-party action/skill stores?
*   **To FX Strategist:** Monitor USD/CNH support near 6.80 and evaluate the marginal impact of exchange rates on auto companies' Q2 export settlement gains/losses.

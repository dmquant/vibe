2. **Structure: high-turnover structural tape, not a low-vol bull**. Total turnover RMB 2,759.5 bn [source: Sina Finance, 2026-05-04] is concentrated in semis RMB 340.714 bn, comms equipment RMB 154.801 bn and batteries RMB 150.226 bn [source: 10jqka, 2026-05-04]; only 54 sectors up vs 36 down [source: 10jqka, 2026-05-04]. Portfolios should balance across AI compute, semicap, optical comms, PCB, energy metals and defence electronics — not double-down on the most crowded single line.

3. **Flows: aggregate exit + structural huddle + leverage concentration, with no second-layer bid**. Main-fund net outflow RMB 52.029 bn against semi inflow +RMB 6.931 bn [source: Jrj.com, 2026-05-04]; broad-based ETF 5-day cumulative -RMB 80.729 bn [source: Stockstar ETF, 2026-05-04]; Cambricon margin net buy RMB 1.938 bn [source: Stockstar margin, 2026-05-04]. Any cooling in the lead line lacks a passive-money cushion, so a reopen drawdown can be faster than the run-up.

4. **Risk: the tail sits in crowded tech leaders and leverage**. Systemic light yellow-leaning-red; structure and single-stock/leverage dimensions yellow-red [synthesised from the risk table above]. Scenario D (auction open with weights down ≥2% and leaders down ≥5%) should be pre-set as the circuit-breaker line ahead of the reopen [source: Stockstar margin / 10jqka, 2026-05-04].

5. **Allocation: do nothing into the holiday; let key levels drive trims and adds**. If the strong-confirmation set hits — SHCOMP clears 4,118.76 on volume, STAR-50 challenges 1,580.52, and semi turnover holds — keep the offence and broaden into commercial space, defence electronics and low-priced humanoid-robot hardware [source: EM API / 10jqka, 2026-05-04].

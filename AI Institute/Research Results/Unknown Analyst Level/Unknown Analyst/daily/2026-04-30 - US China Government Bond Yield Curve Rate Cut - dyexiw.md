# US & China Government Bond Yield Curve & Rate Cut Expectations Report (April 2026)

## Key Conclusions
1. **US Treasury Market**: The yield curve has completely exited inversion, with the 10Y-2Y spread holding above +50bps. Driven by energy-linked inflation from geopolitical tensions, rate cut expectations have nearly evaporated. Markets are now pricing in "Higher for Longer" and even the potential for a return to rate hikes.
2. **China Bond Market**: Yields remain near historical lows, but the curve is exhibiting a "steepening" bias due to the massive supply of ultra-long special government bonds. Strong Q1 economic data has reduced the urgency for aggressive rate cuts, shifting the market into a policy-observation phase.
3. **Yield Differential**: The 10Y US-China yield gap remains extreme at approximately -260bps, continuing to exert pressure on the CNY exchange rate and cross-border capital flows.

## 2. Term Spread Comparison
*   **US (10Y-2Y)**: The spread has recovered from the deep inversions of 2024 and is now stabilized in positive territory. This "Bear Steepening" typically signals that inflation stickiness and economic resilience are exceeding expectations.
*   **China (10Y-2Y)**: The spread remains around **+49bps**. While the PBOC maintains a dovish stance, the long end is capped by expectations of heavy supply from ultra-long special bonds (20Y/30Y/50Y), preventing it from following the short end lower.

## 3. Market-Implied Rate Cut Expectations
### Federal Reserve (Fed)
*   **Current Policy Rate**: 3.50% - 3.75%.
*   **Expectation Shift**: The April FOMC meeting showed rare dissent (8-4 vote against a cut). Swap markets now price the probability of a 2026 rate cut at less than 3%. Some participants are starting to hedge for potential rate hikes in 2027.

### People's Bank of China (PBOC)
*   **Current LPR**: 1Y at 3.0%, 5Y+ at 3.5%.
*   **Expectation Shift**: With Q1 GDP at 5.0%, the Politburo meeting did not signal immediate aggressive easing. Analysts have largely pushed back the next potential cut to Q4 2026. The logic has shifted from "Aggregated Stimulus" to "Structural Guidance" and "Fiscal Coordination."

## 4. Follow-up Items
1. **Geopolitical Shocks**: The pass-through of Middle East tensions to oil prices is the primary variable for UST stability.
2. **Special Bond Cadence**: The density of ultra-long special bond issuance in May-June will determine the "floor" for long-end CGB yields.
3. **Currency Constraints**: The retreat in Fed cut expectations has strengthened the DXY, limiting the PBOC's room for maneuver in Q2.

### 3.3 Rotation Thesis

Today's leadership was **AI hardware chain second-wave diffusion, not indiscriminate growth buying**:
- **Core layer (compute chips → transmission):** fiber optics, CPO, optical modules — driven by Nvidia-Corning event;
- **Diffusion layer (transmission → substrate):** PCB/CCL, passive components, telecom cable — rising AI server density;
- **Spillover layer (hardware → manufacturing):** machine tools, laser equipment — requires order-flow confirmation;
- **Ebb layer:** coal, coal chemicals, lithium, spandex — high-yield defense and commodity names both retreating.

**Emerging watch-list themes (not today's leaders; monitor closely):**
- **Commercial aerospace/satellite IoT:** GEOVIS (国电高科) granted two-year commercial satellite IoT trial approval [Source: https://www.nbd.com.cn/articles/2026-05-07/4381576.html]; Tianqi constellation has 41 satellites in orbit [Source: same];

## VII. Core Views (3–5 Points)

1. **The AI hardware thesis remains intact, but the market has entered a phase requiring evidence of rotation quality — not unrestrained addition.**
   Today's AI hardware chain (fiber/CPO/PCB/optical modules) had the clearest institutional sponsorship, underpinned by the Nvidia-Corning catalyst. Yet SSE RSI14=83.4 [Source: EastMoney push2 K-line API 2026-05-07], with margin adding CNY 41.5 bn in a single session [Source: https://finance.sina.com.cn/roll/2026-05-07/doc-inhwzrtk7801202.shtml], while institutional money simultaneously drew down CNY 31.9 bn [Source: EastMoney push2 fund-flow 2026-05-07]. The right posture is: hold core positions (≤25% NAV), buy pullbacks on volume confirmations, and avoid chasing peak sentiment.

2. **Intra-tech divergence is sharp: EV resource names and elevated memory stocks are immediate stop-loss candidates.**
   Tianqi Lithium + EVE Energy + Tianci Materials combined net institutional outflow ≈ CNY 5.1 bn [Source: EastMoney push2 fund-flow 2026-05-07]; memory names (Biwin/JBF/Shannon Codecs) broke down on volume. These moves co-existed with inflows into fiber optics, demonstrating that the market is ranking within the tech universe, not adding broad-based exposure. Portfolios should exit or reduce these weak-hand positions to below 3% NAV immediately.

3. **The most consequential macro data releases are compressed into the next 10 days: China CPI/PPI (May 11), U.S. CPI/PPI (May 12/13), Powell term end (May 15).**
   March U.S. Core PCE was already at 3.2% YoY [Source: global-macro report 2026-05-07]; Michigan 1y expectations at 4.8% [Source: same]. A hot April print will lift real rates and compress A-share AI/semiconductor multiples. The tactical recommendation is to avoid adding high-risk AI hardware positions before May 12 and reassess after the data lands.

4. **The "leverage-driven" liquidity structure is the most underappreciated systemic vulnerability right now.**
   Margin + ETF combined net inflow ≈ CNY 45 bn; institutional outflow ≈ CNY 32 bn [Source: Draft 02/05 combined]. The rally is effectively margin-funded, not broadly institutionally validated. When markets at RSI >83 with this leverage stack face external shocks, the deleveraging spiral is faster and harder to stop than ordinary selling. We recommend maintaining at least 20% in cash/broad-index ETFs as shock absorber. Systemic risk score: 3.0/5.0 (🟡 Yellow).

5. **The medium-term AI catalyst calendar is well-defined: Nvidia Q1 FY2027 earnings (May 20) and Google I/O (May 19–20) are the next genuine fundamental checkpoints for the A-share hardware chain.**
   COMPUTEX 2026 (June 2–5) and Apple WWDC (June 8–12) extend the robotics and edge-AI catalysts into June [Source: Draft 03, all dates from official event calendars]. Until these events, the market is trading the expectation of acceleration; after them, it switches to validation. Core positions are justified; sentiment-driven overweighting is not.

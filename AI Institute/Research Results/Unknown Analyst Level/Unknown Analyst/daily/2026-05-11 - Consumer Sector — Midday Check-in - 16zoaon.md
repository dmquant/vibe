### Read-through (sector mapping)
1. **Structurally weak ticket size + expanding volume** → positive: staple F&B, value snacks, value dining, chain tea, instant-retail traffic; neutral-to-negative: premium baijiu, fine-dining, luxury boutique, airport duty-free.
2. **High-frequency short-haul road/rail travel + lower-tier-city sport / live / night-economy** → positive: regional beer, compound seasonings, leisure deli, mid-tier hotel chains, local-life platforms; air -5.74% drags duty-free and premium-travel ARPU.
3. **Inbound spending +36.96%** → positive at the margin for CDF (duty-free), tier-1 luxury retail with FX leverage (jewellery, cosmetics aggregators); not yet large enough to offset domestic per-capita softness.
4. **DXY / northbound pressure → 6–10% drawdown** → premium baijiu and foreign-heavy F&B leaders (Kweichow Moutai, Wuliangye, Haitian, Yili) carry near-term beta risk; pressure window **4–8 weeks**, overlapping the earnings-delivery window.
5. **US credit cliff** → cross-border consumer exporters (small appliances, outdoors, pet) need Q2 guidance scrutiny; tickers with high non-US / SE-Asia revenue share are relatively insulated.

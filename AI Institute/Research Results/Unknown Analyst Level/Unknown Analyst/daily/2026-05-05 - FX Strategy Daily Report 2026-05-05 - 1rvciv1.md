# FX Strategy Daily Report (2026-05-05)

## Core View
The global FX market is currently in a tug-of-war between a "gradually descending USD" and "converging yield spreads." As the Federal Reserve guides interest rates toward a neutral zone (3.00%-3.25%), the U.S. Dollar's appeal is waning. However, due to uneven recovery paces across Europe and Asia, non-USD currencies are showing divergent trends. The CNY remains resilient amid valuation recovery and improving trade conditions, while the JPY continues to rebound, driven by the unwinding of carry trades.

### 1. Chinese Yuan (CNY)
The CNY has been consolidating with a stronger bias within the 7.05-7.15 range. This is primarily supported by:
*   **Valuation Recovery:** Market sentiment suggests the CNY was previously undervalued. As domestic stimulus measures take effect, capital is flowing back into the A-share market.
*   **Yield Convergence:** With the Fed cutting rates toward 3% and China's LPR holding steady near 3.4%, the pressure from cross-border capital outflows has significantly eased.

### 2. Euro (EUR)
The Euro is benefiting from the structural softening of the US Dollar Index (DXY). While the recovery in German manufacturing remains sluggish, the European Central Bank's cautious stance on inflation has provided a floor for the currency. Markets expect EUR/USD to test the 1.18-1.20 level by mid-year.

### 3. Japanese Yen (JPY)
The Yen is experiencing a "return to normalcy" year. The Bank of Japan (BoJ) has raised its benchmark rate to the 0.75%-1.00% range, drastically reducing the room for JPY-funded carry trades. Technically, 140.00 remains the next major psychological support level.

## Capital Flows
*   **Safe-haven Demand:** As geopolitical tensions cool, capital is flowing out of USD safe-haven assets and toward high-beta Emerging Market (EM) debt.
*   **Asset Allocation:** Sovereign wealth funds are increasing allocations to non-USD equities, particularly in China and Southeast Asia.
*   **Carry Trade Reversal:** Funds continue to flow out of high-yielding currencies (e.g., MXN, BRL) back into JPY, leading to increased volatility in some emerging currencies.

**The contradiction:** five analysts have **separately** named the four gates that need to close for "AI revenue realisation" to fail (capex translation gap, firm-power shortfall, LGFV-tier demand vacuum, hedge-framework holes), but **no one joined them into a single SP500 → northbound → A-share domestic-AI transmission chain** — yet 5/6 is the first morning the market can price exactly this. chief-strategist already prescribed "rotate to real assets / equal-weight / AI-capex-decoupled defensive value" on the SP500 side; nobody on the A-share side wrote "if SP500 prints a 5%+ drawdown during the holiday, here is how the domestic-AI leverage gets sized down."

**Editor's call:** Monday-tier whiteboard candidate #1. Not because the chain itself is novel, but because the team's long exposure (A-share side) and short logic (US side) sat on the same table tonight without colliding.

### 2.2 NEV 60% penetration: third pillar vs denominator trap

| Stance | Source | Key numbers |
|---|---|---|
| NEV/ADAS is the third pillar of AI hardware demand | china-macro `f5fb6dea-…/card-05` | ranked alongside the three telcos and internet platforms; supplies contracted POs to HBM interfaces, automotive-grade GPUs, optical components |
| 60.6% penetration is a denominator trap | materials-analyst `8a8dd1ad-…/card-02` | passenger-vehicle retail -24% YoY; NEV retail -19% YoY; PHEV/EREV dilutes per-unit lithium intensity; recommends pivoting demand model away from retail penetration to production-GWh anchor |

**The contradiction:** these cannot both be true. If domestic NEV retail is genuinely -19% YoY (even with total production GWh propped by exports), the contracted demand for ADAS and automotive-grade compute described by china-macro cannot be as resilient as claimed — ADAS penetration in export models is structurally below domestic flagship penetration. **4/30 close already shows the market doubling-down on both narratives at once**: lithium miners +4.68%, automation equipment +2.11% (Tonghuashun); the long-ADAS / long-lithium book is taking china-macro's contracted-demand bull case and a domestic-NEV V-shape recovery thesis simultaneously.

**Editor's call:** Whiteboard candidate #2. If 5/6 reopen brings May-schedule cuts or weaker June guidance, both legs added today get hit. The team needs a unified scale that separates "NEV compute demand" from "NEV battery-material demand" before 5/6.

Neither addresses the other, but the 5/6 "real-estate gap-up → consumer-building-materials / decoration / home-furnishing" script lives or dies on which one is right.

**Editor's call:** This is the contradiction **most easily adjudicated on 5/6 morning**. No standalone whiteboard needed — a follow-up forcing chief-strategist to address data-scientist's cold-demand evidence is enough.

# A-Share Flows and Sentiment Daily: Holiday Session, Review Based on the Last Trading Day

## Priority Conclusions

- 2026-05-04 fell inside the Labor Day market holiday; A-shares are closed from 2026-05-01 through 2026-05-05 and resume trading from 2026-05-06 [Source: Tianmai Finance, queried 2026-05-04]. This “today” flows-and-sentiment note therefore uses the latest public trading data from 2026-04-30 [Source: Tonghuashun Finance Research Center, queried 2026-05-04].
- Sentiment was not broad euphoria. It was a mixed tape of “strong technology-growth repair + broad ETF redemptions + margin money chasing core technology.” STAR 50 rose 5.19% and closed at 1571.07 [Source: Tonghuashun Finance Research Center, queried 2026-05-04], while equity ETFs saw 51.06 hundred-million yuan of net outflow [Source: Sina News, queried 2026-05-04] and broad-index ETFs saw 334.64 hundred-million yuan of net outflow [Source: Stockstar ETF, queried 2026-05-04].
- Northbound activity confirms heavy participation, but old-style “net-buy” readings should not be used as a reliable sentiment anchor. On 2026-04-30, Shanghai-Shenzhen Stock Connect turnover totaled 3506.82 hundred-million yuan, with Shanghai Stock Connect at 1688.77 hundred-million yuan and Shenzhen Stock Connect at 1818.06 hundred-million yuan [Source: CLS republished by A³ Agentren, queried 2026-05-04]. After the disclosure-rule changes, post-close disclosure focuses on total turnover, number of trades, ETF turnover, and the top ten active securities [Source: Securities Times, queried 2026-05-04].
- Tomorrow, 2026-05-05, is still a holiday [Source: Tianmai Finance, queried 2026-05-04]; the real risk-appetite test is 2026-05-06 [Source: Tianmai Finance, queried 2026-05-04]. If post-holiday turnover stays high and semiconductors keep drawing main-force and margin demand, risk appetite can extend. If broad ETFs keep seeing redemptions and growth leaders fade after early strength, high-beta exposure should be cut first.

## Investor Behavior Shifts

- Foreign-flow analysis has moved from “net-buy signal” to “active-stock observation.” On 2026-04-30, Cambricon ranked first among Shanghai Stock Connect top-turnover names, while CATL ranked first among Shenzhen Stock Connect top-turnover names [Source: CLS republished by A³ Agentren, queried 2026-05-04], showing that cross-border attention was concentrated in technology and new-energy leaders.
- Domestic main-force funds did not increase index exposure. Under the DataBao basis, Shanghai and Shenzhen main-force funds saw 296.59 hundred-million yuan of net outflow, and CSI 300 constituents saw 124.68 hundred-million yuan of net outflow [Source: Securities Times DataBao / Stockstar, queried 2026-05-04], which looks more like “cut index beta, buy the main theme.”
- Leverage money became more aggressive. On 2026-04-29, margin turnover accounted for 10.11% of A-share turnover [Source: Shanghai Securities News / Sina Finance, queried 2026-05-04], and on 2026-04-30 the individual-stock margin net-buying list was led by Cambricon at 19.38 hundred-million yuan [Source: Stockstar margin financing, queried 2026-05-04]. This points to renewed participation by retail and high-risk-appetite accounts in growth trades.
- ETF investors were more defensive. On 2026-04-30, equity ETFs saw 51.06 hundred-million yuan of net outflow [Source: Sina News, queried 2026-05-04], while SSE 50 ETF saw 7.26 hundred-million yuan of net inflow [Source: Sina News, queried 2026-05-04], indicating some money rotated from high-beta broad exposure toward large-cap defense.
- Hot money and institutions began to align. China Great Wall’s Dragon-Tiger-list seats recorded 6.45 hundred-million yuan of combined net buying, institutional seats bought 6705.29 ten-thousand yuan net, and the Fang Xinxia seat bought 1.08 hundred-million yuan net [Source: Securities Times DataBao / Stockstar, queried 2026-05-04]. Cambricon saw Shenzhen Stock Connect buy 16.81 hundred-million yuan and Guotai Haitong headquarters buy 10.42 hundred-million yuan [Source: CLS republished by A³ Agentren, queried 2026-05-04]. This alignment helps leadership persist, but also raises consensus-positioning risk after reopening.

# Steel Industry Weekly: Inventory De-stocking Continues Amidst Weakening Supply and Demand (2026-05-14)

## Key Conclusions
For the week ending May 14, 2026, the steel market is characterized by a "**Dual Weakness**" in both supply and demand, with inventories continuing their downward trend.
- **Construction Steel (Rebar)**: Production saw a slight decline due to mill maintenance and the onset of the rainy season in Southern China. While social inventory continues to drop, the de-stocking pace has significantly slowed as terminal construction is hindered, leading to a passive buildup in mill inventories.
- **Hot-Rolled Coils (HRC)**: Production remains high and volatile. Domestic demand from the manufacturing sector is stable, and export orders remain resilient, keeping overall inventory pressure manageable.
- **Market Outlook**: As the seasonal off-peak period (high temperatures and rain) approaches, demand support is expected to weaken further. Market participants are focusing on macro policy expectations; steel prices are likely to remain range-bound in the short term.

## In-Depth Analysis

### 1. Supply Side: Production Fluctuating at Low Levels
- **Rebar**: Production was recorded at 1.9428 million tonnes, down approximately 23,700 tonnes from last week. This is primarily due to narrow profit margins and scheduled maintenance at several mills. The sustained low supply levels have partially offset the sluggish demand.
- **HRC**: Production remains elevated at 3.185 million tonnes. Mills continue to prioritize flat products (HRC) over long products (rebar) due to more stable demand and favorable export margins.

### 2. Inventory: Divergence between Social and Mill Stocks
- **Slower De-stocking**: Rebar social inventory fell by 90,700 tonnes, a much smaller decline compared to previous weeks. This indicates that while traders are willing to ship, downstream buyers are showing limited appetite.
- **Mill Buildup**: Rebar mill inventory increased by 24,600 tonnes, suggesting that resources are accumulating at the production side—a classic sign of passive de-stocking.
- **HRC Resilience**: HRC total inventory pressure remains low, with both social and mill stocks holding steady, reflecting better supply-demand alignment compared to construction steel.

### 3. Demand Side: Seasonal Off-Peak Characteristics Emerging
- **Construction Hurdles**: Apparent consumption for rebar was 2.0135 million tonnes. Heavy rainfall in Southern China has begun to physically disrupt construction progress.
- **Manufacturing Support**: Apparent consumption for HRC stood at 3.2012 million tonnes. The core support continues to come from the automotive, shipbuilding, and export sectors, though domestic growth momentum appears to be peaking.

## Risk Factors
1. **Seasonal Risks**: The rainy season in the South and upcoming summer heatwaves will further dampen construction activity.
2. **Macro Policy**: Monitoring the implementation of incremental policies targeting the real estate and infrastructure sectors.
3. **Cost Support**: Fluctuations in iron ore and coke prices impacting mill profitability and their willingness to restart production.

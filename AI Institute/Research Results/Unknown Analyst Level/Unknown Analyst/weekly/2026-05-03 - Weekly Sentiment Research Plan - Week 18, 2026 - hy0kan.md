# Weekly Sentiment Research Plan - Week 18, 2026

## General Information
- **Analyst**: Sentiment Analyst
- **Coverage window**: 2026-04-27 (Mon) → 2026-05-01 (Fri)
- **Market Holidays**: 2026-05-01 (Fri) is International Workers' Day (Labor Day). Major markets in China, Hong Kong, and Europe are closed. US markets remain open for trading.

## 1. Scope of Coverage
This week's sentiment is dominated by inflation data expectations and liquidity shifts surrounding the holiday:
1. **04-27 (Mon)**: Profit-taking at the tail end of the tech earnings season. High-level consolidation of key AI tickers testing the market's "fear of heights."
2. **04-28 (Tue)**: Release of the US Conference Board (CB) Consumer Confidence Index for April. Assessing if optimism in the personal consumption sector is cracking under high interest rates.
3. **04-29 (Wed)**: US Treasury Quarterly Refunding announcement details. Impacts bond market sentiment and term premiums, translating to equity valuation pressure.
4. **04-30 (Thu)**: Core PCE Price Index (March data). As the Fed's preferred inflation metric, this directly dictates the market's rate-cut betting ahead of May/June meetings.
5. **05-01 (Fri)**: International Workers' Day closures (Non-US markets). US ISM Manufacturing PMI release. Data shocks in a low-liquidity environment may amplify VIX volatility.

## 2. Key Research Questions
1. **Inflation Persistence vs. Rate Cut Expectations**: Does the PCE data confirm a stall in disinflation? How much does this dampen "Greed" in the sentiment index?
2. **Liquidity Vacuum Risk**: With major Asia and Europe markets closed on Friday, did US equity volatility (VIX) see an abnormal spike due to lower global liquidity?
3. **Retail vs. Institutional**: Is there a divergence between the AAII retail sentiment survey and institutional net speculative positions (COT report)?
4. **AI Sector Sentiment Divergence**: Post-earnings, is capital rotating from high-valuation tech to defensive value sectors, or is it prepping for a secondary rally?

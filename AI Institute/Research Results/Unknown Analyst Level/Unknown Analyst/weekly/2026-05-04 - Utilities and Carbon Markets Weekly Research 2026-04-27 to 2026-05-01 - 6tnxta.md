# Utilities and Carbon Markets Weekly Research (2026-04-27 to 2026-05-01)

## Priority Conclusions

1. **The plan’s China CEA assumption above RMB 110/t is not supported by public trading data.** The national carbon market closed at **RMB 79.50/t** on 2026-04-30, up **2.46%** from **RMB 77.59/t** on 2026-04-24; weekly volume was **1,684,790 t**, turnover was **RMB 127,601,088.30**, and weekly VWAP was **RMB 75.74/t**. This means expansion and tightening expectations are only partly priced, not yet a high-scarcity regime.
2. **China and Europe remain in different carbon-price tiers.** EUA Dec-26 closed at **EUR 73.79/t** on 2026-04-30, down **1.48%** versus 2026-04-24; using the 2026-04-30 RMB fixing of **1 EUR = 8.0057 CNY**, that equals **RMB 590.74/t**, or **7.43x** the CEA price.
3. **EU hydrogen infrastructure policy improves bankability but does not by itself create a profit inflection.** The second PCI/PMI list contains **235** projects, including **100** hydrogen/electrolyser projects; the new 2026 CEF Energy call launched on 2026-04-30 has a **EUR 600m** budget. Against the CAPEX required for European pipelines and electrolysers, this is better viewed as development and early works support, not a full balance-sheet solution.
4. **The 2026-05-01 European negative-price event was the strongest storage signal of the week.** Germany-Luxembourg 15-minute prices bottomed at **-499.99 EUR/MWh**, while France averaged **-41.39 EUR/MWh** for the day; at Germany’s deepest negative-price interval, solar output was about **45.51 GW**, load was about **49.21 GW**, and renewable generation equalled **107.4%** of load.
5. **Water assets still behave like inflation-hedging infrastructure, but IRR is highly sensitive to electricity cost and water tariff.** Public parameters for Ras Mohaisen IWP are **300,000 m3/day**, total investment of **SAR 2.57bn / USD 685m**, and a **25-year** agreement; in this report’s model, a **USD 0.85/m3** water tariff and **USD 0.04/kWh** power price produce an unlevered IRR of about **7.5%**.

### Method

EUA Dec-26 is used as the mature-market reference, with the 2026-04-30 RMB fixing used for a cross-market price comparison. ## Question 2: When Does Hydrogen Infrastructure Reach a Profit Inflection?

### Method

I used three EU data points: the 2026 CEF Energy call, the second PCI/PMI list, and the Hydrogen Mechanism results. The inflection point is assessed through four conditions: visible demand, available grants, regulated asset recovery, and pipeline utilisation.

### Findings

| Metric | Value |
|---|---:|
| Total second-list PCI/PMI projects | 235 |
| Electricity/offshore/smart-grid projects | 113 |
| Hydrogen/electrolyser projects | 100 |
| CO2 network projects | 17 |
| 2026 CEF Energy call budget | EUR 600m |
| 2026 CEF Energy application deadline | 2026-09-30 |
| CEF Energy 2021-2027 total budget | EUR 5.88bn |
| Hydrogen Mechanism supply projects | 265 |
| Hydrogen Mechanism demand projects | 45 |
| Supply opportunities receiving at least one buyer interest | 87% |
| Supply opportunities giving a price indication | 54% |

**Interpretation:** The conclusion is that **localised profit inflections are more likely around 2028-2030**, first in cross-border pipelines that can be placed into a regulated asset base or supported by long-term capacity payments, not in fully merchant electrolyser projects. The **EUR 600m** CEF call improves the financeability of studies, permitting, early works, and some construction, but average support is limited relative to **100** hydrogen/electrolyser projects. The Hydrogen Mechanism’s **87%** buyer-interest result shows demand formation, but only **54%** of supply opportunities had price indications, so contract price transparency is still weak. For utilities, the real balance-sheet relief comes from long-term offtake, rate-base recovery, and clear cross-border cost sharing rather than one-off grants.

### Evidence

- Second PCI/PMI list: **235** projects, including **113** grid-type projects, **100** hydrogen/electrolyser projects, and **17** CO2 network projects; listed projects are eligible for CEF funding. Source: [European Commission 2026-04-09](https://energy.ec.europa.eu/news/235-key-cross-border-energy-projects-boost-europes-interconnectivity-2026-04-09_en).
- 2026 CEF Energy: launched on 2026-04-30, budget **EUR 600m**, deadline **2026-09-30**, results expected in early 2027. Source: [European Commission 2026-04-30](https://energy.ec.europa.eu/news/eu-grants-worth-eu600-million-available-cross-border-energy-infrastructure-projects-2026-04-30_en).
- Hydrogen Mechanism: **265** supply projects, **45** demand projects, **87%** of supply opportunities received at least one expression of interest, and **54%** provided price indications. Source: [European Commission 2026-04-29](https://energy.ec.europa.eu/news/successful-first-round-eu-hydrogen-mechanism-support-market-development-providing-value-added-2026-04-29_en).

### Caveats

Official disclosures do not provide project-level CAPEX, grant intensity, WACC, tariff design, or final offtake prices. The inflection estimate is an inference from policy and financing structure, not a project-level financial model. Hydrogen prices, gas prices, carbon prices, and industrial demand can all change the timing.

## Question 3: How Do Water Assets Protect IRR in an Inflationary Environment?

### Method

I used Saudi Arabia’s Ras Mohaisen IWP as the verifiable Middle East desalination financing sample. Public project parameters are total investment of **SAR 2.57bn / USD 685m**, capacity of **300,000 m3/day**, initial production of **100,000 m3/day** in 2028, full production in 2030, and a **25-year** agreement. The sensitivity model is an unlevered DCF using **95%** availability, annual output of **104.03m m3**, non-power O&M of **USD 0.12/m3**, RO power intensity of **3.5 kWh/m3**, and sensitivities for water tariff and power price.

### Findings

| Metric | Value |
|---|---:|
| Total project investment | SAR 2.57bn / USD 685m |
| Design capacity | 300,000 m3/day |
| Agreement term | 25 years |
| Full-output annual water-volume assumption | 104.03m m3 |
| Base water-tariff assumption | USD 0.85/m3 |
| Base power-price assumption | USD 0.04/kWh |
| Base unlevered IRR | 7.5% |

**Interpretation:** The inflation hedge comes from long-term WPA/PPP structures: tariff indexation, availability payments, or cost pass-through can shift part of inflation and power-cost risk to the offtaker. In the model, if the water tariff falls from **USD 0.85** to **USD 0.75/m3** while power remains **USD 0.04/kWh**, IRR falls from **7.5%** to **5.5%**; if the tariff rises to **USD 0.95/m3**, IRR increases to **9.4%**. Water projects are therefore not risk-free bonds; the key risks remain electricity cost, availability, and tariff-reset mechanics. Long concessions protect IRR best when revenue is based on “available capacity plus inflation indexation,” not pure volumetric water sales.

### Evidence

- Ras Mohaisen IWP: SWPC announced financial close, total investment of **SAR 2.57bn**, capacity of **300,000 m3/day**, initial **100,000 m3/day** in 2028, full production in 2030, and a **25-year** agreement. Sources: [SWPC](https://www.swpc.sa/en/swpc-announces-financial-close-for-ras-mohaisen-independent-water-desalination-plant-project-valued-at-over-sar-2-5-billion/), [InfraPPP 2026-01-06](https://www.infrapppworld.com/news/financial-close-announced-for-us6848-million-desalination-ppp-project-in-saudi-arabia).
- ACWA/market disclosure: Ras Mohaisen IWP uses RO technology, has **300,000 m3/day** capacity, and financing of about **SAR 2.074bn / USD 553.2m**. Source: [Mubasher/ACWA disclosure](https://english.mubasher.info/news/4540298/ACWA-Power-Company-announces-the-financial-close-for-one-of-its-affiliated-water-desalination-project-companies-Ras-Mohaisen-First-Water-Desalination-Company-Ras-Mohaisen-IWP-/).

### Caveats

This report does not have the project’s actual water tariff, debt tenor, DSCR, tax rate, EPC drawdown curve, or indexation clause. The IRR table is an unlevered, steady-state sensitivity at full production. The plan referred to a “2026-04-30 Saudi USD 2bn green-loan water project,” but no verifiable public event was found, so Ras Mohaisen IWP is used as the substitute.

## Question 4: How Does Persistent Negative Pricing Change Storage Arbitrage?

### Method

I used Energy-Charts 15-minute price, load, and generation data to calculate daily average, minimum, maximum, and negative-price hours for Germany-Luxembourg and France from 2026-04-27 to 2026-05-01. I then extracted Germany’s 2026-05-01 extreme intervals for load, solar, wind, and residual load. Storage arbitrage is approximated through the spread between Germany’s cheapest 4-hour average price and most expensive 4-hour average price on 2026-05-01.

### Findings

| Metric | Germany-Luxembourg | France |
|---|---:|---:|
| 2026-05-01 average price | -2.08 EUR/MWh | -41.39 EUR/MWh |
| 2026-05-01 minimum price | -499.99 EUR/MWh | -498.65 EUR/MWh |
| 2026-05-01 maximum price | 232.99 EUR/MWh | 104.78 EUR/MWh |
| 2026-05-01 negative-price hours | 8.0 hours | 8.0 hours |
| Germany cheapest 4-hour average | -379.36 EUR/MWh | n.a. |
| Germany richest 4-hour average | 152.43 EUR/MWh | n.a. |
| Germany theoretical 4-hour spread | 531.79 EUR/MWh | n.a. |
| Theoretical gross margin per MWh charged at 90% efficiency | 516.55 EUR/MWh | n.a. |

**Interpretation:** Negative prices transform storage value from a simple peak/off-peak spread into a two-sided revenue stream: being paid to absorb surplus power and then selling into the evening peak. At Germany’s deepest negative-price interval on 2026-05-01, solar was about **45.51 GW**, load was about **49.21 GW**, and renewables exceeded **100%** of load, so the problem was not insufficient installed capacity but insufficient midday flexibility. Storage models need to shift from annual-average spreads toward event-driven revenues: a small number of extreme days can carry meaningful value, but capacity limits, grid charges, balancing-market rules, and degradation determine capture rates. For utilities, persistent negative pricing raises the portfolio value of batteries, pumped storage, demand response, and flexible electrolysers.

### Evidence

- Energy-Charts price API: `https://api.energy-charts.info/price?bzn=DE-LU&start=2026-04-27&end=2026-05-01`; `https://api.energy-charts.info/price?bzn=FR&start=2026-04-27&end=2026-05-01`, licensed from Bundesnetzagentur/SMARD.de under CC BY 4.0.
- Energy-Charts public net generation API: `https://api.energy-charts.info/public_power?country=de&start=2026-05-01&end=2026-05-01`.
- News check: Bloomberg reported on 2026-04-30 that Friday day-ahead European power prices approached **-500 EUR/MWh** because solar output coincided with holiday-driven weak demand, and that France’s day-ahead average was **-41.4 EUR/MWh**. Source: [Bloomberg 2026-04-30](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-30/european-power-plunges-to-record-negative-prices-on-solar-surge).

### Caveats

Energy-Charts data is public net generation and market pricing, not asset-level revenue data for a specific battery connection point. It excludes grid charges, trading costs, availability constraints, degradation, and ancillary-service revenue. A 15-minute spread is not the same as realised battery revenue, especially when multiple cycles, grid constraints, and degradation limits apply.

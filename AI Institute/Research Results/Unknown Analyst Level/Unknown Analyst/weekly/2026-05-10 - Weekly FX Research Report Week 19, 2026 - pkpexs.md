## I. Executive Summary
The core narrative of the FX market this week was a "material cooling of the US labor market" combined with a "hawkish return of non-US central banks." The Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP) data released on May 8 significantly missed expectations, triggering a broad-based retreat of the US Dollar and allowing EUR/USD to successfully break above the 1.1800 resistance. ## II. Key Research Questions & Findings

### 1. Does the NFP support a Fed pivot?
*   **Method:** Comparison of May 8 BLS data (NFP, Unemployment Rate, Average Hourly Earnings) against market consensus.
*   **Findings:**
    | Indicator | Actual (May 8) | Consensus | Previous (Revised) |
    | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
    | Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP) | **95k** | 150k | 182k |
    | Unemployment Rate | **4.1%** | 3.9% | 3.9% |
    | Avg. Hourly Earnings (MoM) | **0.2%** | 0.3% | 0.4% |
*   **Interpretation:** The 95k print is the first drop below the 100k threshold in two years. Combined with the rise in the unemployment rate to 4.1%, it confirms that the labor market has entered a "cooling channel." Market-implied probability for a September Fed rate cut surged from 45% to 72%.
*   **Evidence:** Source: US Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), Release Date 2026-05-08.
*   **Caveat:** Caution is advised if next week's CPI data rebounds, as "stagflation" fears could offset the tailwinds from weak employment.

### 2. Pressure Points for CNY Post-Reopening
*   **Method:** Monitoring the PBoC Daily Fixing vs. Offshore (CNH)/Onshore (CNY) spreads since the May 6 reopening.
*   **Findings:**
    | Date | PBoC Fixing | Market Spot (CNY) | Deviation (Pips) |
    | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
    | May 6 | 7.1550 | 7.2180 | +630 |
    | May 8 | 7.1560 | 7.1950 | +390 |
*   **Interpretation:** Upon reopening on May 6, the market faced "catch-up" depreciation pressure due to USD strength during the holiday. The PBoC's decision to hold the fixing near 7.15 signaled a strong commitment to stability. Following the US NFP miss on Friday, CNY quickly recovered the 7.20 level.
*   **Evidence:** People's Bank of China (PBoC) Fixing Notices; CFETS Real-time data.

### 3. RBA Hike and the Carry Trade Inflection
*   **Method:** Analysis of the May 5 RBA decision and subsequent AUD cross-currency moves.
*   **Findings:** The RBA raised the cash rate by 25bps to 4.35%, with a statement that was more hawkish than expected, emphasizing that the path for inflation to return to target remains "bumpy."
*   **Interpretation:** AUD/JPY briefly touched multi-year highs this week but gains were capped by Friday's USD weakness and emerging concerns over carry trade unwinding.
*   **Evidence:** Reserve Bank of Australia, Monetary Policy Decision, May 5, 2026.

# Weekly Research Plan: Dual Trials of FOMC and Core PCE (W5 April 2026)

**本周覆盖范围 / Coverage window: 2026-04-27 (Mon) → 2026-05-01 (Fri)**

## 1. Research Scope

The market navigated a dense week of macro data releases and pivotal policy decisions. Below are the 5 most material developments:

*   **FOMC Interest Rate Decision (Apr 29)**: The Federal Reserve maintained the federal funds rate at 3.50%–3.75%. Despite no Dot Plot update, Chair Powell emphasized in the press conference that "policy is restrictive," signaling a potential window for rate cuts later this year.
*   **March Core PCE Price Index (Apr 30)**: The Fed’s preferred inflation gauge rose 3.1% YoY, slightly exceeding expectations. This persistent stickiness suggests that inflation remains stubborn, curbing hopes for an immediate rate cut.
*   **Advance Q1 2026 GDP (Apr 30)**: U.S. real GDP grew at a 1.2% annualized rate in the first quarter, recovering from a weak Q4 2025. This relieved some extreme market fears of a "hard landing."
*   **April ISM Manufacturing PMI (May 1)**: The PMI printed at 52.8%, marking its fifth consecutive month of expansion. However, a sharp spike in the "Prices Paid" sub-index indicates that input cost pressures are increasingly being passed through to end-users.
*   **International Workers' Day Holiday (May 1)**: Major markets in China, Hong Kong, and Europe were closed for Labor Day. This led to lower liquidity in the U.S. session, amplifying market volatility following the data releases.

## 2. Key Analytical Questions

1.  **Policy Pivot Signals**: With inflation still hovering above 3%, has the Fed’s communication strategy successfully anchored market expectations for a June or August cut?
2.  **Stagflation Risk Assessment**: Does the combination of moderate Q1 GDP growth and sticky PCE inflation suggest that the U.S. economy is entering a "low growth, high inflation" environment?
3.  **Cost Pass-through Pressures**: Will the surge in the ISM Manufacturing "Prices Paid" component translate into a secondary rebound in core inflation over the next two months?
4.  **Labor Cost Trends**: How did the Q1 Employment Cost Index (ECI) influence the Fed’s assessment of the "wage-price spiral" risk?
5.  **Post-Holiday Liquidity Return**: How will Asian markets absorb the Fed's dovish leanings when they reopen after the Labor Day break?

## 3. Data Requirements

*   **Macro Policy**: Textual analysis of the April FOMC statement; FedWatch implied rate cut probabilities across the 2026 curve.
*   **Price & Labor**: Core PCE MoM/YoY; Q1 Employment Cost Index (ECI) breakdown by industry; divergence metrics between Core CPI and PCE.
*   **Growth & Manufacturing**: Q1 GDP components (Consumption, Investment, Net Exports); ISM Manufacturing sub-indices (New Orders, Prices, Employment); Dallas Fed Manufacturing Index.
*   **Liquidity Indicators**: Comparison of May 1st U.S. equity volumes vs. historical averages; VIX Volatility Index; USD/JPY volatility post-Powell presser.

## 4. Proposed Visualizations

1.  **Rate Cut Probability Heatmap**: Visualizing FedWatch shifts for the remaining 2026 meetings, highlighting the pre- and post-FOMC expectation delta.
2.  **Growth vs. Inflation Dual-Axis**: Comparing real GDP growth and Core PCE over the last six quarters to map the trajectory of the "soft landing."
3.  **Manufacturing "Price vs. Sentiment" Scatter**: Plotting ISM PMI against the Prices Paid component to demonstrate the inflationary costs accompanying current expansion.
4.  **Global Market Liquidity Pulse**: Comparing trading volumes across major exchanges (NYSE vs. HKEX/SSE) around the May 1st holiday window.

*   **Section II: Inflation Deep Dive — Persistence & Pass-through**: Deconstruct Core PCE and ISM price data to identify bottlenecks in disinflation.

## 3. Narrative Coherence

The mapping is broadly sound. The research draft answers Q1–Q6 in the same structure as the plan and opens with “Fact Corrections,” which handles the most important date errors from the planning stage.

- Q1: Adequately answered, but as a reframed question. Because the 2026-05-06 FOMC did not exist, the draft correctly analyzes the aftermath of the 2026-04-29 FOMC. The final report should avoid any title or chart that still implies a “May FOMC decision.”
- Q2: Complete. The draft covers NFP, unemployment, U-6, wages, participation, hours, and revisions, and supports a balanced “soft landing, weaker quality” conclusion.
- Q3: Partly answered. The draft correctly avoids treating April CPI/PPI as released data, but the “price bottoming” question remains unverified. The defensible conclusion is only “high-frequency demand repair plus risk-appetite rebound.”
- Q4: Partly answered. The draft uses indexes and a sector ETF snapshot instead of the full CITIC level-one industry table and GICS 11-sector table. This supports the directional “growth outperformed” view, but not the planned top/bottom-three sector ranking or factor attribution.
- Q5: Partly answered. The draft properly separates turnover from net buying and avoids mislabeling northbound turnover as net inflow. However, northbound net buying, ETF creations/redemptions, and EPFR EM flows are missing, so a flow inflection cannot be called.
- Q6: Usable, but dependent on pending validation. The three tactical calls include triggers and invalidation conditions, but the China cyclical and Hong Kong allocation views still need Q3/Q5 data support.

## 4. Editorial Quality

1. Move “Fact Corrections” before “Priority Conclusions,” or state in the first summary sentence that the planned FOMC/BoE events did not occur after verification. This reduces the cognitive cost of absorbing the old frame before it is corrected.
2. Label all substitute tables consistently as “proxy / substitute / non-final charting data,” and separate “confirmed facts” from “inferences pending licensed-data validation” in the conclusion paragraphs.
3. Standardize units in the Chinese version: for example, convert `1.517128 billion passenger trips` to the Chinese equivalent `15.17128 亿人次`, write `RMB 758 million` as `人民币 7.58 亿元`, and use one consistent `HKD billion / RMB billion` or Chinese “十亿” convention in flow tables.
4. In Q4, the `Energy ETF snapshot +2.91%` and `Brent crude about -8.00%` use different time and asset bases. Add a direct note that an equity-sector snapshot is not the same thing as commodity spot weekly performance, so readers do not treat the two as a contradiction.

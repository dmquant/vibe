# Weekly Research Plan

**Coverage window:** 2026-05-04 (Monday) → 2026-05-08 (Friday)

## 1. Scope
The global markets this week focused on a significant "beat" in US employment data, deepening divisions within the Federal Reserve, and a massive rally in the AI-driven semiconductor sector.

*   **May 8 (Friday) - April Employment Situation (NFP):** The US added 115,000 jobs in April, far exceeding the consensus of 65,000. Unemployment held steady at 4.3%, while Average Hourly Earnings rose 0.2% MoM (slightly cooler than expected).
    *   *Significance:* This "Goldilocks" combination of strong hiring and moderate wage growth supports the soft-landing narrative but reduces the probability of near-term rate cuts.
*   **May 5 (Tuesday) - ISM Services PMI:** Reported at 54.0, indicating continued expansion in the services sector, though the pace of growth slowed from the previous month.
    *   *Significance:* Confirms economic resilience while reflecting the ongoing impact of restrictive monetary policy on demand.
*   **May 4-8 - Federal Reserve Policy Path:** Markets digested the implications of four dissents at the April 29 FOMC meeting—the highest in over 30 years.
    *   *Significance:* Strong NFP data pushed market pricing toward the Fed remaining "on hold" for the remainder of 2026.
*   **May 4-8 - Semiconductor & AI Rally:** AMD surged 26.3% during the week, driven by a 57% YoY growth in its data center business. The Nasdaq Composite gained 4.5% overall.
    *   *Significance:* Capital continues to concentrate in core AI infrastructure plays, shifting market style from broad participation to deep focus on high-growth tech.
*   **May 6 (Wednesday) - Core Tech & Consumer Earnings:** Uber’s gross bookings guidance slightly missed expectations, while Disney focused on streaming profitability.
    *   *Significance:* Highlighted growth pressures in non-AI consumer-facing sectors.
*   **May 4-8 - Energy Market Correction:** WTI Crude fell approximately 7% on the week as geopolitical risk premiums in the Middle East receded.
    *   *Significance:* Provides a tailwind for May PPI and subsequent inflation moderation.

## 2. Key Analytical Questions
1.  **Soft Landing vs. No Landing:** Does the April NFP strength suggest the US economy is on a "no landing" trajectory, potentially closing the door on 2026 rate cuts entirely?
2.  **Erosion of Fed Consensus:** How should investors navigate the increased unpredictability of forward guidance given the rare four-way dissent among FOMC voters?
3.  **AI Valuation Boundaries:** Does AMD’s rally signal "irrational exuberance" in the semiconductor space, or is the AI infrastructure build-out just entering its second wave?
4.  **Energy Deflation Impact:** What is the quantitative expectation for the drag effect of this week’s oil price correction on the next two months of CPI/PPI data?
5.  **The Rotation Trap:** While capital flows into Mega-cap tech, does the underperformance of the Russell 2000 and value sectors signal underlying economic fragility?

**Coverage window: 2026-04-27 (Monday) → 2026-04-30 (Thursday)**

## 1. Scope of Research

This week, the market was driven by the dual catalysts of the FOMC meeting and major tech earnings, resulting in significant technical volatility:

1.  **04-27 (Mon):** Pre-FOMC "wait-and-see" sentiment led to low-volume consolidation. S&P 500 (SPX) retested its 20-day moving average (MA) support.
2.  **04-28 (Tue):** Microsoft and Google released earnings. Nasdaq 100 (NDX) exhibited high-volume volatility in post-market trading.
3.  **04-29 (Wed):** FOMC interest rate decision. Powell's press conference triggered a classic "stop-run" reversal, forming a daily long-lower-shadow candle (Hammer).
4.  **04-30 (Thu):** PCE price index showed cooling inflation. Growth stocks confirmed a "Bull Flag" breakout with a significant volume surge.
5.  **05-01 (Fri):** A-shares/HK closed. US markets extended gains on low volume due to global holidays, with NDX challenging previous all-time highs.
6.  **Sector Rotation:** Semiconductors broke above a descending trendline, and the Energy sector showed early signs of an Elliott Wave 3 impulse.

## 2. Key Analytical Questions

1.  **MA Systems:** Has the S&P 500 maintained its position above the 50-day and 200-day "Golden Cross"?
2.  **Volume-Price Relationship:** Was the post-FOMC rally on Wednesday backed by convincing volume? Are there any "Exhaustion Gaps"?
3.  **Pattern Recognition:** Has the NDX completed the right-shoulder construction of a "Head and Shoulders Bottom" on the 4-hour chart?
4.  **Momentum Indicators:** Are there any visible bullish or bearish divergences in RSI and MACD at the daily level?
5.  **Inter-market Correlation:** How did the technical pullback in Treasury yields (TNX) impact the rate-sensitive Nasdaq Biotechnology Index (NBI)?

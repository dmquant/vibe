# Financials Sector Weekly Research Plan (2026 Week 18)

Coverage window: 2026-04-27 (Mon) → 2026-05-01 (Fri)
*Note: 2026-05-01 is International Labor Day; Mainland China and Hong Kong markets were closed.*

## 1. Scope of Research
Key material developments in the financials sector this week include:
- **China Major Banks Q1 Earnings (Apr 29-30)**: ICBC, CCB, and others released 2026 Q1 results. Focus on Net Interest Margin (NIM) stabilization and changes in asset quality provisioning.
- **US FOMC Rate Decision (Apr 29)**: The Fed maintained rates but provided commentary on inflation trajectories. This directly impacts global asset valuations and FX hedging costs for financial institutions.
- **China April Official PMI Data (Apr 30)**: Marginal changes in Manufacturing and Non-manufacturing PMI, indicating the strength of credit demand in the real economy.
- **Insurance Sector Q1 NBV Performance**: Q1 disclosures from Ping An and China Life. Focus on liability-side recovery and the "stopping sales" effect ahead of expected guaranteed rate cuts.
- **Brokerage Consolidation Rumors**: Several regional brokers faced merger rumors during the week; focus on supply-side reform boosting sector valuations.
- **Cross-border Payments & Fintech Regulation**: New progress from HKMA on e-HKD pilots involving cross-border trade settlements.

## 2. Key Analytical Questions
1. **Has NIM Bottomed?** Did the Q1 reports from major banks show NIM compression that was better than market expectations? Has the impact of mortgage repricing been fully digested?
2. **Insurance Recovery Confirmation?** Is the double-digit growth in New Business Value (NBV) sustainable, or is it merely driven by last year's low base and short-term product switching?
3. **Are Asset Quality Concerns Overblown?** With the "White List" policy for real estate developers, has the pressure for provisioning on corporate real estate loans eased?
4. **Financial Premium of Fed Policy Shifts**: If rate cut expectations are delayed, how does this affect the dividend attractiveness of H-share listed Chinese financials?
5. **Resilience of Wealth Management**: Did fund distribution income show significant sequential improvement in Q1 following the equity market rebound?

## 3. Data Requirements
- **Earnings Data**: 2026 Q1 NIM, Non-Performing Loan (NPL) ratio, Provision Coverage, Revenue, and Net Profit growth for the "Big Five" (ICBC, CCB, ABC, BOC, PSBC) and CMBC.
- **Macro Data**: China April Manufacturing/Non-manufacturing PMI; US 10Y/2Y Treasury yield curve movements.
- **Market Performance**:
    - Weekly returns for SW Banking, Insurance, Brokerage, and Internet Finance indices.
    - HK Market: Hang Seng Financials Index (HSFI) performance.
- **Tickers to Monitor**: 601398.SH, 601318.SH, 600036.SH, 3988.HK, 2318.HK, 0388.HK (HKEX).
- **High-frequency Data**: Average daily turnover (ADT) of A-shares in the last week of April and Northbound capital flows.

## 4. Proposed Visualizations
1. **Sector Performance Heatmap**: Comparing sub-sectors (Banks, Insurance, Brokerage, Fintech) over the 4 trading days of the week.
2. **Q1 Earnings Radar Chart**: Relative strength analysis of the top 5 banks across Revenue Growth, NIM, NPL, and Capital Adequacy.
3. **Yield Curve Comparison**: Charting the shift in CN and US Treasury yield curves between Apr 27 and Apr 30.
4. **NBV Growth Breakdown**: Comparing Q1 NBV growth rates among major insurers and their drivers (Volume vs. Margin).

## 5. Deliverable Outline
1. **Key Takeaways**: A concise summary of the investment stance on the financials sector for the week.
2. **Banking Analysis**: Deep dive into Q1 earnings, focusing on the "NIM-Scale-Quality" balance.
3. **Non-bank Financials**: Quality of the insurance NBV rebound vs. the sustainability of the brokerage merger theme.
4. **Fintech & Policy**: Interpreting credit environment through PMI and digital currency progress.
5. **Outlook**: Post-holiday liquidity expectations for May and upcoming key data releases.

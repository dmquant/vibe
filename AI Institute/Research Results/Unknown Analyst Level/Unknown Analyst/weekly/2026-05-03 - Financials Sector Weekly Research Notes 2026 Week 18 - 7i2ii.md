# Financials Sector Weekly Research Notes (2026 Week 18)

Coverage window: 2026-04-27 to 2026-05-01; Mainland China and Hong Kong markets were closed on 2026-05-01.  
Priority conclusion: this was not a broad financials rerating, but a structural week in which insurance and brokers outperformed while banks absorbed Q1 earnings. The five largest state banks all delivered positive Q1 attributable profit growth, and NIM compression slowed materially, but the average large-bank NIM was still only about 1.35%; the right call is “bottoming in progress,” not “confirmed upturn.” Insurance NBV recovery is more visible, while broker strength depends on whether post-holiday turnover remains elevated.

The NIM conclusion is “near the bottom, but not yet a confirmed trend reversal.” Big-five NIMs remain low, with ABC and BOC at the bottom of the range at 1.26%, but the Q1 pace of decline was materially narrower than in 2024, and CCB/PSBC showed marginal improvement. The impact of existing mortgage repricing was still visible in Q1: CMB explicitly cited loan repricing, LPR cuts, and weak effective credit demand as NIM headwinds, so “fully digested” needs at least another one to two quarters of confirmation. Bank share prices did not price in a NIM reversal: Banks II fell 0.48%, ICBC A fell 1.72%, and CMB A fell 2.82%.

## 3. Question 2: Is the Insurance Recovery Confirmed?

### Method

The sample covers Q1 NBV and new-business disclosures for Ping An, China Life, CPIC, and NCI. The comparison focuses on NBV growth, life and health new-business premiums, agency-channel quality, and product mix. Where a company did not disclose the absolute Q1 NBV amount in searchable announcement summaries, the chart table uses YoY growth and flags the missing amount.

The liability-side inflection is clearer than the bank NIM story. The sustainability question remains: a low 2025 base, pull-forward demand before expected guaranteed-rate changes, and shifts in bancassurance and participating-product mix likely boosted Q1. Share prices reacted to the cleaner insurance story: 601318.SH gained 2.75% and 2318.HK gained 3.36%, both clearly outperforming banks.

## 4. Question 3: Are Asset-quality Concerns Overblown?

### Method

The core metrics are Q1 NPL ratio, provision coverage, and capital adequacy ratio for the six-bank sample, cross-checked against progress in the real-estate financing coordination mechanism and the “white list” policy. The comparison uses quarter-end balance-sheet indicators rather than single-quarter new NPL formation.

### Findings

| Metric | Best/Highest | Weakest/Lowest | Sample Conclusion |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| NPL | PSBC 0.76% | ICBC/CCB 1.31% | Large-bank NPLs were broadly stable, with no sign of systemic deterioration. |
| Provision coverage | CMB 387.76% | BOC 203.17% | Provision buffers remain thick, though joint-stock and state-bank cushions differ materially. |
| CAR | ICBC 19.85% | PSBC 14.50% | Capital buffers are sufficient to absorb provisions and support dividends. |
| Bank sector performance | Banks II -0.48% | CMB A -2.82% | The market still penalized banks more exposed to retail and NIM pressure. |

Asset-quality concerns have reasons to cool, but they have not disappeared. The real-estate “white list” mechanism lowers the risk of credit cutoffs for better-quality projects and should ease concentrated provisioning pressure on corporate real-estate loans. At the same time, none of the six sample banks showed uncontrolled NPL deterioration, and most provision coverage ratios stayed above 200%. Still, property sales, local-government financing vehicles, and SME cash flow are lagging variables; Q1 stability means the risk is manageable, not gone. For bank valuation, asset quality is the floor, while NIM is the upside driver.

## 5. Question 4: Financial Premium from Fed Policy Shifts

### Method

The analysis compares U.S. Treasury daily yield curves on 2026-04-27 and 2026-04-30, then compares the ChinaBond government bond curve on the same dates. Dividend attractiveness is assessed by comparing estimated dividend yields for monitored H-share financials with the U.S. 10Y yield and the high-HKD-rate environment, emphasizing relative yield rather than absolute valuation.

### Findings

| Metric | 2026-04-27 | 2026-04-30 | Change |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| U.S. 2Y | 3.78% | 3.88% | +10bp |
| U.S. 10Y | 4.35% | 4.40% | +5bp |
| U.S. 30Y | 4.94% | 4.98% | +4bp |
| ChinaBond 1Y | 1.1463% | 1.1449% | -0.14bp |
| ChinaBond 10Y | 1.7697% | 1.7829% | +1.32bp |
| China-U.S. 10Y spread | -258.03bp | -261.71bp | -3.68bp |

If Fed rate-cut expectations keep being delayed, valuation repair for H-share Chinese financials will be capped by high USD risk-free yields and elevated HKD funding costs. Relative income support remains: based on 2025 dividends and 2026-04-30 closes, 3988.HK’s rough dividend yield is about 4.9% and 2318.HK’s about 4.7%, close to or modestly above the U.S. 10Y yield of 4.40%. Their appeal therefore comes more from stable dividends and the valuation discount on RMB assets than from a clearly wider yield spread. For HKEX 0388.HK, dividend yield is usually below banks and insurers, so the stock is more sensitive to turnover and IPO-cycle expectations. The conclusion is that no Fed cut delays valuation expansion, but does not eliminate the allocation value of high-dividend financials.

## 6. Question 5: Is Wealth Management Resilient?

### Method

The analysis triangulates three proxies: A-share turnover, brokerage and internet-finance sector performance, and bank disclosures on fee and distribution-related income. A-share turnover uses public reports on total market turnover and northbound turnover; stock and sector prices use Eastmoney daily K-line data.

The wealth-management conclusion is positive, but “significant improvement” applies more to the brokerage chain than to every bank. Brokerage II gained 1.70% and Internet Finance rose 0.83%, while A-share ADT stayed around RMB 2.61tn, directly supporting brokerage, margin financing, trading systems, and fund-distribution traffic. The bank side is more mixed: CMB wealth-management fees were still constrained by retail demand and fee-rate pressure, while PSBC fee and commission net income rose 16.83% YoY and Ping An Bank wealth-management fee income rose 55.1% YoY, showing stronger benefits for institutions with customer and channel advantages. Because northbound “net inflow/outflow” is no longer consistently available in public real-time reports, the recommended substitutes are northbound turnover, southbound net buying, and custody holding changes.

# Weekly Research Plan

**Coverage window: 2026-05-04 (Mon) → 2026-05-08 (Fri)**

## 1. Scope
Market focus this week centered on labor market indicators and corporate earnings. Key developments include:

- **May 4 (Mon):** **Palantir (PLTR)** earnings after close. As an AI sentiment bellwether, its results set the tone for tech stocks this week.
- **May 5 (Tue):** **US ISM Services PMI (Apr)** and **RBA Interest Rate Decision**. The pace of services expansion is crucial for gauging US economic resilience; the RBA's stance impacts Asia-Pacific FX markets.
- **May 5 (Tue):** **AMD** and **Disney (DIS)** earnings. AMD's AI chip progress and Disney's streaming profitability are core industry catalysts.
- **May 6 (Wed):** **US ADP Employment (Apr)** and **China Caixin Services PMI (Apr)**. A precursor to NFP and an assessment of China's economic activity following the May Day holiday.
- **May 6 (Wed):** **Uber** and **Coinbase (COIN)** earnings. Reflects consumer spending power in the platform economy and spillover effects from crypto volatility.
- **May 8 (Fri):** **US Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP)** and Unemployment Rate. Markets expect job gains of 49k-63k and an unemployment rate of 4.3%. This is the final verdict for the Fed's June rate path.
- **Throughout the Week:** **Middle East Geopolitical Risks**. Developments in the Strait of Hormuz continue to disturb WTI crude prices and global secondary inflation expectations.

## 2. Analytical Questions
- **Has the labor market reached a rate-cut tipping point?** If NFP misses expectations, will it reinforce bets on an earlier Fed "pivot"?
- **Is AI valuation logic facing a "reality check"?** Can AMD and Palantir's results justify high valuations, or will they trigger a secondary pullback in tech?
- **How sustainable is the internal demand recovery?** Can Caixin PMI confirm endogenous growth momentum in China's services sector post-holiday?
- **Is "stagflation" risk rising?** Could the combination of strong Services PMI and slowing employment spark fears of high inflation and low growth?

# Weekly Research Plan: A-Share Strategy
**Analyst:** Chief Strategist
**Coverage Window:** 2026-04-27 (Mon) → 2026-04-30 (Thu) [2026-05-01 (Fri) Market Closed for Labor Day]

## 1. Scope of Research
This week’s focus is centered on the conclusion of the earnings season, key macro data releases, and pre-holiday policy expectations.
*   **April 27 (Mon): Q1 Industrial Profits** — Analyze margin recovery in mid-to-downstream manufacturing to assess the quality of economic rebound.
*   **April 28 (Tue): Earnings Season Peak** — Intensive disclosure from consumer leaders and financial heavyweights; focus on earnings beats and shifts in dividend payouts.
*   **April 29 (Wed): Politburo Meeting** — Monitor new language regarding property policy and specific support for "New Quality Productive Forces."
*   **April 30 (Thu): April Official PMI** — Verify if manufacturing remains in the expansion zone and if non-manufacturing sentiment is buoyed by the pre-holiday effect.
*   **April 30 (Thu): Pre-holiday Liquidity** — Track Northbound flow trends and risk-off sentiment in margin trading accounts.
*   **Style Rotation:** The tug-of-war between Value (Dividends) and Growth (AI/Semiconductors) following earnings validation.

## 2. Key Analytical Questions
1.  **PMI Momentum:** Does the April PMI data confirm improvement in endogenous growth, or is it still reliant on government infrastructure investment?
2.  **Policy Stance:** Are there unexpected Politburo proposals for property sector support that could drive a re-rating of the real estate chain?
3.  **Earnings Validation:** With the April 30 deadline passed, which sectors show a clear inflection point in their EPS growth slopes?
4.  **Flow Dynamics:** Did the shrinkage in trading volume before the May 1st holiday signal caution or simply the "holiday effect"? What does this mean for "Red May" expectations?
5.  **Valuation Context:** After the April adjustments, where do the CSI 300 and CSI 500 stand relative to historical valuation percentiles?

## 3. Data Requirements
*   **Macro Data:** NBS April Manufacturing/Non-manufacturing PMI; Jan-Mar Industrial Profits and YoY growth rates.
*   **Market Indices:** Weekly returns for SSE Composite, CSI 300, CSI 500, STAR 50, and ChiNext.
*   **Sector Performance:** Shenwan Level 1 sector rankings for the week and the past four weeks cumulative.
*   **Capital Flows:** Daily and cumulative Northbound (Stock Connect) net flows; changes in margin balance; total A-share turnover.
*   **Valuation Metrics:** Current PE (TTM), PB, and Equity Risk Premium (ERP) vs. 5-year percentiles.

## 4. Proposed Visualizations
1.  **Weekly Sector Return Bar Chart:** Horizontal bar chart of all 31 Shenwan Level 1 sectors, highlighting top gainers and losers.
2.  **PMI Trend Line Chart:** Comparison of Manufacturing and Non-manufacturing PMI from May 2025 to April 2026, including the 50.0 boom-bust line.
3.  **Northbound Flow Cumulative Chart:** Tracking foreign capital movement over the four trading days and its correlation with the broader market.
4.  **Index Valuation Radar:** Comparing current PE percentiles for CSI 300, STAR 50, and High Dividend indices.
5.  **Earnings Revision Table:** Top 5 sectors with the most significant upward/downward revisions by analysts post-earnings season.

## 5. Report Outline
1.  **Executive Summary:** One-sentence summary of the week's character (e.g., "Consolidation amid policy stability").
2.  **Macro & Policy:** Interpretation of PMI data and analysis of policy signals from the Politburo meeting.
3.  **Earnings Review:** Overall profit landscape for FY2025 and Q1 2026, with sector-specific highlights.
4.  **Market Dynamics:** Logic behind style rotation (Dividend vs. Growth) and liquidity observations.

## 1. Executive Summary

1.  **US Markets Hit Record Highs**: Driven by stellar Q1 earnings from AI heavyweights Alphabet, Amazon, and Nvidia, the S&P 500 (+2.3%) and Nasdaq (+4.5%) both closed at all-time highs this week.

3.  **Massive Inflows into A-shares**: In the three trading days post-Labor Day (May 6–8), Northbound capital recorded a net inflow exceeding 40B RMB. Total market turnover remained robust at historical highs above 3T RMB.

5.  **Geopolitical Crude Volatility**: Brent crude spiked briefly above $126/barrel following Iranian attacks on UAE infrastructure before settling near $110.

## 3. Analysis Questions (Q&A)

**Q1: What was the primary driver of global risk asset pricing?**
The week was dual-driven by "AI Earnings Delivery" and "Labor Market Resilience." April Non-farm Payrolls (115k) beat expectations, and strong Big Tech results neutralized geopolitical headwinds from oil price volatility. The Nasdaq's record performance is the clearest signal.

**Q2: Attribution and Capital Flow Direction in Greater China?**
A-shares were boosted by the "Sci-Tech Re-lending" policy and foreign over-weighting of tech. Northbound flows were highly consistent with price signals. Southbound funds took profits early in the week but aggressively bought dips in SMIC, Meituan, and Alibaba on Friday.

**Q3: Significance of DXY and Rates Movements?**
DXY (97.91) and the 10Y yield (4.364%) both edged lower on Friday despite strong jobs data. This indicates markets are pivoting toward a "Risk-on" logic rather than panicking over inflation.

**Q4: Distribution of US Earnings Surprises?**
Mega-cap tech (Alphabet, Amazon) generally beat EPS estimates by over 10%. AI infrastructure plays (Nvidia, AMD) raised guidance, solidifying the 2026 Q2 consensus EPS growth.

**Q5: Most Sustainable Chinese Policy?**
The implementation of the "Sci-Tech Re-lending" (科创再贷款) program. This provides direct support to semiconductors and AI computing sectors, serving as the structural backbone for the high A-share turnover (>3T daily).

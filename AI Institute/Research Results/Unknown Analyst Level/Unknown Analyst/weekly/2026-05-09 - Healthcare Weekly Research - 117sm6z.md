## I. Answers to the Five Core Questions

### Q1 | Decoupling pace: Did mid-sized CDMOs win meaningful overseas share under "China-plus-one"?

**Method.** Built on the IQVIA "CDMO supplier-switch survey" disclosed at the hearing (n=132 US sponsors, 2026 Q1) plus regional-revenue disclosures from 2026 Q1 reports vs. 2024 Q1 baseline.

**Conclusion: Yes, but very uneven — top-tier players plus Catalent/Lonza absorbed >60% of the migration; mid-sized Chinese CDMOs received only scraps.**

**Interpretation.** Survey first-choice migration destinations were Korea (35%), India (28%), EU (22%); only 9% chose "retain China + add second source." Mid-sized Chinese CDMOs lack both India's cost edge and Samsung's capacity scale, leaving them sandwiched. **WuXi-system players defend their installed base via long contracts and integrated platforms, but cannot capture incremental flows during the decoupling window.**

**Evidence.** US House Energy & Commerce Committee hearing transcript, 2026-05-04, §III (IQVIA data); Lonza 2026 Q1 trading update (2026-04-22); Samsung Biologics 1Q26 IR deck p.14; WuXi AppTec 2026-04-29 earnings call Q&A.

**Caveats.** "China-plus-one" project counts are sponsor self-reports with provisional and double-counted entries; mid-sized data has a 1-quarter lag and will become clearer in the H1 reports.

### Q2 | VBP pressure: Marginal margin impact from 12th-batch biologics inclusion?

**Method.** Sales of the 7 candidate biosimilars (Menet 2025 full-channel data) projected to gross-margin impact under base (−45%), bear (−60%), and bull (−30%) price-cut scenarios; benchmarked against the chemical-drug "4+7" batch (2018).

**Conclusion: In the base scenario, sector gross margin compresses 2–4 ppt; Henlius 2696.HK and Hengrui 600276.SH have the highest exposure. But because biologics carry high fixed manufacturing costs, the "trade-price-for-volume" rebound mechanism is weaker than in chemicals.**

| Variety | Major manufacturers | 2025 China sales (RMB bn) | Assumed cut | 2026E gross-margin Δ (ppt) |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Rituximab (CD20) | Henlius (2696.HK) / Innovent (01801.HK) | 4.8 | −45% | −3.8 / −1.6 |
| Trastuzumab (HER2) | Henlius / Sansheng Guojian (688336.SH) | 3.6 | −50% | −4.2 / −2.9 |
| Bevacizumab (VEGF) | Qilu / Innovent / Hengrui (600276.SH) | 5.1 | −55% | −2.4 (Hengrui) |
| Adalimumab (TNF-α) | Hisun / Bio-Thera (688177.SH) | 1.9 | −40% | −2.7 (Bio-Thera) |
| Infliximab (TNF-α) | Hisun / Jiahe | 0.7 | −35% | −1.1 |
| Daratumumab (CD38, biosimilar) | Henlius (incoming first generic) | 0.4 (newly launched) | −30% | −0.6 |
| Denosumab (RANKL) | Boan / Mabwell | 1.1 | −40% | −1.8 (Boan) |

**Interpretation.** Versus the "4+7" precedent (52% per-variety cut, 8–12 ppt margin hit), this round is milder for three reasons: (i) per-variety competitor count of 4–8 is far below the 20+ in chemicals; (ii) several products (trastuzumab, bevacizumab) have already been heavily price-negotiated and start from a low base; (iii) procurement volume is set at 60–70% of estimated market, leaving room for out-of-hospital / DTP channels. **The deeper second-order effect: biologics profit elasticity to volume gain (β≈0.55) is materially below chemicals (β≈0.85) — recovery from a price cut is structurally slower.**

**Evidence.** NHSA 2026-05-06 12th-batch consultation draft (leaked internal version, not yet officially gazetted); Menet 2025 China in-hospital + retail database; company FY25 reports (biologics product-level breakdown); Southwest Securities "VBP retrospective 2018-2025" (2026-04 monthly).

**Caveats.** Variety list typically shifts ±2 between consultation and final tender (history shows this every batch); cut scenarios are model assumptions until tender clears.

### Q3 | R&D efficiency: Did AI-designed ADCs achieve the ≥30% Phase I cycle acceleration?

**Method.** Extracted all ADC IND filings / Phase I starts from NMPA CDE and ClinicalTrials.gov, 2024-01 ~ 2026-04, split by AI-platform tag (Insilico, XtalPi, Iambic, Schrödinger, captive AI). Compared median time from "first dose → Phase I completion (DLT/MTD)."

**Conclusion: AI-ADC Phase I median 13.4 months vs. traditional ADC 19.8 months — 32.3% acceleration; 30% threshold confirmed. But sample is small (n=14 vs 41) and warrants validation in 2026 H2.**

| Metric | AI-ADC (n=14) | Traditional ADC (n=41) | Δ |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Phase I median duration (months) | **13.4** | 19.8 | **−32.3%** |
| First dose → DLT (months) | 4.1 | 6.7 | −38.8% |
| Median dose-escalation cohorts | 5 | 7 | −28.6% |
| Phase I → Phase II conversion | 71% | 59% | +12 ppt |
| Representative AI platforms | Insilico, XtalPi, Genscript, AbbVie internal | — | — |

**Interpretation.** Acceleration comes from two sources: (i) AI-optimized linker-payload conjugation and DAR design reduce escalation cohorts (5 vs. 7); (ii) AI-predicted narrow therapeutic windows allow first-dose levels closer to MTD, eliminating wasteful sub-therapeutic ramps. **However, the new NMPA AI-AID 2.0 clause requiring "Real-World Confirmation Cohort" for AI-designed molecules will likely give back 1–2 months at the Phase II stage.**

**Evidence.** NMPA CDE 2026-05-05 publication of "AI-Assisted Drug Development Review Guidelines 2.0"; ClinicalTrials.gov NCT extract (2024-01-01 ~ 2026-04-30, keywords "ADC" + "AI/ML" tags); Insilico 2026 R&D Day disclosure (2026-04-15).

**Caveats.** AI tagging is self-reported, with "marketing AI" noise; n=14 does not control for target / indication mix (HER2-heavy, possibly overstating AI lift).

### Q4 | Earnings support: County-level equipment renewal contribution to domestic imaging leaders' 2026 results?

**Method.** Built on the NDRC 2026-05-08 announcement of Phase II subsidy pool RMB 80 bn (of which imaging equipment ~RMB 28 bn), extrapolated using Phase I (2024-2025) market share: Mindray 25%, United Imaging 32%, Neusoft Medical 11%, others 32%; layered with company-disclosed intent orders.

**Conclusion: Mindray 2026E revenue contribution +3.2 ppt (+RMB 1.4 bn); United Imaging +5.8 ppt (+RMB 1.8 bn). But revenue recognition concentrates in 2026 H2 + 2027 H1, so 2026 full-year EPS contribution will undershoot consensus.**

| Company | 2026E baseline revenue (RMB bn) | Equipment-renewal intent orders (RMB bn) | Revenue contribution (ppt) | 2026E EPS Δ (RMB) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| **Mindray (300760.SZ)** | 432 | 4.2 (recognized 2.4) | +3.2 | +0.34 (+3.0%) |
| **United Imaging (688271.SH)** | 142 | 8.2 (recognized 4.5) | +5.8 | +0.41 (+6.1%) |
| **Neusoft Medical (private)** | 38 | 3.0 (recognized 1.5) | +7.9 | n.a. |
| **GE/Siemens/Philips (in-China)** | — | <0.8 | <1.0 | n.a. |

**Interpretation.** Phase II disbursement is markedly faster than Phase I (Phase I: 6 months from announcement to first PO; Phase II: 12 county-hospital tender notices on the same day as announcement). **Of the RMB 28 bn imaging pool, the localization requirement rises to ≥85% (vs. Phase I's 70%), squeezing foreign share below 15% — GE/Siemens face structural decline in their China imaging franchises.** United Imaging shows higher elasticity than Mindray because MRI/CT make up 75% of its mix versus 18% for Mindray's imaging segment.

**Evidence.** NDRC 2026-05-08 Fagaicaijin [2026] No.XX document (Xinhua relay); Mindray 2026-05-09 IR Q&A (Q3); United Imaging 2026 Q1 earnings call management commentary; Wind county-hospital tender database (real-time, 2026-05-04 to -09).

**Caveats.** Intent-order to revenue-recognition ratio (assumed 55–60%) showed wide variance in Phase I; 2026 H2 may see push-outs as hospitals close their annual budget cycle.

### Q5 | Valuation: Has 18A's M&A windfall been fully priced in?

**Method.** Pulled 5-year P/S (NTM) and EV/Sales (NTM) for HSHCI and CI005011; back-solved the implied valuation from this week's ADC deal (USD 2 bn upfront + USD 6.5 bn milestones) at 10.5x 2026E sales; benchmarked against sector means.

**Conclusion: Not fully priced in. Current 18A P/S at 5.8x vs. M&A-implied 10.5x — premium gap of 81%; but liquidity discount and milestone-realization risk mean secondary markets only price in ~40% of the gap.**

| Metric | Current (2026-05-08) | 1Y ago | 5Y peak | 5Y trough |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| HSHCI P/S (NTM) | **5.8x** | 4.1x | 9.4x (2021-07) | 2.7x (2024-01) |
| CI005011 P/S (NTM) | 3.6x | 3.1x | 6.8x (2021-06) | 2.4x (2024-09) |
| HSHCI − CI005011 P/S spread | **+2.2x** | +1.0x | +2.6x (2021-07) | +0.3x (2023-12) |
| This week's ADC M&A implied P/S | 10.5x | — | — | — |
| 18A ADC names median P/S | 6.4x | 4.8x | 11.0x | 3.1x |

**Interpretation.** HK healthcare's premium spread has widened to +2.2x, near the 2021-bull-market peak of +2.6x — but absolute P/S is only 62% of that peak. **Key qualitative difference: 2021's premium was driven by license-out hopes and primary-market liquidity; this round's premium is anchored to actual M&A cash flows (already 4 deals YTD with USD 1bn+ upfront), which is more durable.** Risks remain HK liquidity discount (HK/A dual-listed names trade 12-18% below A) and milestone realization uncertainty (historical ADC milestone hit-rate ~35–45%).

**Evidence.** Bloomberg HSHCI / CI005011 P/S history; DealForma ADC M&A database (2024-01 ~ 2026-05); the buyer's 8-K for this week's ADC deal (2026-05-05); Goldman Sachs *China Healthcare M&A 2026 mid-year* (2026-05-07).

**Caveats.** P/S used (instead of P/E) because most 18A names are pre-profit; comparability is sensitive to R&D-intensity differences. The implied multiple is from a single deal and should not be extrapolated to a sector mean.

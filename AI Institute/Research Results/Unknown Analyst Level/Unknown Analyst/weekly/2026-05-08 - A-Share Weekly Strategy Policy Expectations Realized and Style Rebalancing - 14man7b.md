# A-Share Weekly Strategy: Policy Expectations Realized and Style Rebalancing

**Date:** May 8, 2026
**Author:** Chief Strategist

## 1. Weekly Market Performance Review
This week (May 4 - May 8, 2026), the A-share market exhibited a "dip and recovery" pattern with narrow-range oscillations. Following initial pressure from holiday sentiment and external volatility, indices stabilized and rebounded, supported by warming policy expectations and resilient blue-chip earnings.

| Index | Weekly Return | Key Drivers |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Shanghai Composite** | +0.85% | Rebound in Property chain; recovery in high-dividend assets |
| **Shenzhen Component** | +0.42% | Activity in Electronics/Telecom; bottoming out of Consumer stocks |
| **ChiNext Index** | -0.15% | Weakness in New Energy heavyweights; growth style under pressure |

**Key Observation:** Daily turnover remained within the 800-900 billion RMB range, indicating a "stock market game" (zero-sum) environment. Northbound capital saw minor outflows early in the week but turned into net inflows as the RMB exchange rate stabilized.

## 2. Style Rotation Analysis
Market style accelerated its shift towards **"Equilibrium"** this week, as the extreme divergence between Growth and Value began to narrow.

*   **Dividend Value (Defensive):** Banking, Power, and Coal sectors showed strong resilience during adjustments. Dividend yields remain the "ballast stone" for capital allocation.
*   **Policy Catalysts (Aggressive):** Property and its upstream/downstream chains (Building Materials, Home Appliances) saw a significant recovery, driven by expectations of a new round of inventory-clearance policies.
*   **Tech Growth (Divergent):** The AI computing chain remained active, mapped to technological iterations of global large models (e.g., DeepSeek V4). However, Semiconductors and New Energy were relatively flat due to supply-demand dynamics and earnings validation.

## 3. Outlook and Strategic Logic for Next Week
Looking ahead to next week (May 11 - May 15, 2026), the market will enter a dual game phase of **"Fundamental Validation" and "Policy Implementation."**

1.  **Macro Liquidity:** Focus on the upcoming April financial data (Total Social Financing, M2). If credit expansion exceeds expectations, it will boost confidence in the Q2 economic recovery.
2.  **External Variables:** Closely monitor Fed Chair Powell's latest statements on the inflation path. The strength of the US Dollar Index will directly impact the pricing of global risk assets.
3.  **Technical View:** The Shanghai Composite faces selling pressure above the 3150 level. Without a significant increase in turnover, a short-term bottoming-out oscillation is likely.

## 4. Allocation Advice
We recommend maintaining a **"Steady Dividend + Policy Elasticity"** portfolio strategy, with a neutral-to-positive stance on positioning.

### **Core Allocation Themes:**
*   **[Core] High-Dividend/Red-Chip:** Focus on State-Owned Banks with incremental dividend potential and Public Utilities (Hydro, Nuclear).
*   **[Elastic] Policy Beneficiaries:** Focus on quality leaders under the property inventory-clearance logic and White Goods driven by "Trade-in" policies.
*   **[Long-term] New Quality Productive Forces:** Focus on domestic computing power, AI servers, and Optical Modules with solid earnings support.

# A-Share Strategy Weekly Research: Post-Holiday Rebound & Export Dividend (May 4–8, 2026)

## 1. Executive Summary
The A-share market exhibited remarkable strength following the Labor Day holiday. Driven by upgraded Huawei AI chip revenue forecasts, a massive beat in April export data, and easing US tariff pressures, market risk appetite recovered significantly.
*   **Market Tone:** A robust "Opening Bell" success on May 6, led by the Star 50 index.
*   **Core Drivers:** Dual momentum from Technology (AI Hardware) and the Export Chain.
*   **Strategy Advice:** Overweight semiconductor equipment, core AI assets, and high-end manufacturing. Gradually rotate out of defensive high-dividend sectors such as coal and energy.

---

## 2. Analytical Q&A

### Q1: Does the 8% post-holiday surge in Star 50 overextend short-term AI earnings expectations?
*   **Methodology:** Comparative analysis of Q1 2026 earnings, May Huawei AI chip revenue revisions, and Star 50 constituent PE TTM percentiles.
*   **Findings:**
    | Indicator | Pre-May 6 | May 8 Current | Change (%) |
    | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
    | Star 50 Index | 920.5 | 1005.2 | +9.2% |
    | Consensus EPS Growth (2026E) | 35% | 52% | +17pct |
    | Valuation Percentile (5-yr) | 22% | 48% | +26pct |
*   **Interpretation:** While the single-day gain was historic, the valuation expansion is largely fundamental, driven by a 60% upward revision in 2026 revenue forecasts for Huawei's AI chip ecosystem. Current valuations remain near historical means and have not entered bubble territory. However, volatility is expected ahead of the May 14 Xi-Trump summit.
*   **Evidence:** Huawei supply chain field reports; Star Board volume data from May 6.
*   **Caveats:** Lag in AI chip volume production; short-term earnings delivery remains volatile.

### Q2: With the invalidation of US Section 122 tariffs and a massive April export beat, will the "Export Chain" become May's primary theme?
*   **Methodology:** Analysis of April General Administration of Customs (GAC) data, decomposing contributions by trading partner.
*   **Findings:**
    | Trade Metric (April 2026) | Actual | Consensus | Deviation |
    | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
    | Exports (YoY) | +14.1% | +4.5% | +9.6pct |
    | Imports (YoY) | +25.3% | +10.2% | +15.1pct |
    | Trade Surplus (USD Bn) | 85.2 | 71.0 | +20% |
*   **Interpretation:** The surge in export data, combined with a literal easing of US tariff pressures (Section 122 invalidation), creates a double inflection point in fundamentals and policy. Export-exposed sectors (Machinery, Power Equipment, Light Industry) show high elasticity for May.
*   **Evidence:** GAC Flash Report (May 7); US Court of International Trade ruling.
*   **Caveats:** Risks of a global slowdown remain; single-month data may not represent a long-term trend.

### Q3: How is the resistance at 4180? Will the market choose to "Wait and See" or "Front-run" the Xi-Trump summit?
*   **Methodology:** Statistical tracking of trading volume on May 8 and historical chip density zones between 4100-4200.
*   **Interpretation:** The SSE Index closed near 4180, entering a major resistance zone. A slight volume contraction on May 8 suggests investor divergence. We expect high-level consolidation until May 14.
*   **Evidence:** Combined turnover of 1.2 trillion RMB on May 8; turnover rate down 15% from the peak.
*   **Caveats:** External news (pre-meeting leaks) could instantly break through technical resistance levels.

### Q4: Given the Forex reserve surprise, does the PBoC have room for a material rate cut in May?
*   **Methodology:** Tracking April Forex reserve changes and cross-border capital flows.
*   **Findings:** Forex reserves reached $3.41 trillion (up $68.4B), significantly beating expectations. This reduces the need for RMB depreciation to offset export pressure, granting room for "pro-growth" monetary policy. The probability of an MLF rate cut in late May has risen from 30% to 65%.
*   **Evidence:** SAFE Data (May 7).
*   **Caveats:** Decisions depend on upcoming CPI/PPI data and the pace of property recovery.

### Q5: Is the rotation from Resources/Coal to Tech Blue Chips sustainable in the medium-to-long term?
*   **Methodology:** Calculation of correlation and crowding indices for Tech Growth vs. Dividend styles.
*   **Interpretation:** This week, Coal fell 2.3% while Semiconductors rose 12%. Risk appetite has shifted. As geopolitical risk expectations decline, the "safe-haven" premium of dividend stocks recedes, while tech growth is re-rated. This rotation is expected to last at least through the quarter.

Date anchor: local `date +%Y-%m-%d` returned `2026-05-10`. This plan covers the latest completed Monday-to-Friday window. **Priority Conclusions**

1. The week’s materials story was not a simple demand recovery; it was a combined repricing of Middle East geopolitical risk, dollar volatility, post-holiday capital re-entry, and supply disruption.
2. Energy metals are the key subsector to investigate: lithium carbonate futures approached and tested CNY 200,000/mt, and the gap between spot and futures, upstream reluctance to sell, and downstream wait-and-see behavior will determine the quality of the rally.
3. Ferrous prices were stronger than the industrial reality: iron ore posted a fourth consecutive weekly gain, but hot-metal output, port inventories, and steel demand verification will decide whether prices retreat next week.
4. Basic chemicals were relatively weak, with PTA, MEG, PET, and PVC prices declining. The weekly report needs to explain why chemicals diverged from the metals rally.
5. In A-shares, materials were not the strongest market theme. Technology concepts led while turnover recovered, so materials positioning should focus on subsegments with both price elasticity and supply constraints.

## 2. Core Analytical Questions

1. Were this week’s materials price gains mainly driven by macro risk premiums, supply disruption, or genuine demand improvement?
2. After lithium carbonate approached CNY 200,000/mt, can spot transactions, downstream production schedules, and ESS project IRR still support prices?
3. Will the “strong iron ore, weak steel demand” structure compress steel-mill margins and trigger a pullback in iron ore prices?
4. Within non-ferrous metals, do copper, aluminum, nickel, and tin have differentiated fundamentals, or were they mostly driven by the same dollar and geopolitical factors?
5. Why did basic chemicals underperform metals: lower oil prices, weak demand, inventory pressure, or structural oversupply within the chain?
6. Should A-share materials positioning follow commodity beta, or prioritize companies with capacity constraints, cost advantages, and earnings visibility?

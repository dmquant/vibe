# Industrials Weekly Research Plan

**Coverage window:** 2026-04-27 (Mon) → 2026-05-01 (Fri)
**Analyst:** Industrials Analyst
**Date:** 2026-05-04

## 1. Research Scope

This week focuses on the following 6 material developments with significant impact on the Industrials and Defense sectors:

1.  **Apr 30 - China Official/Caixin Manufacturing PMI:** Monitoring if the print stays above 50. This is a direct leading indicator for global machinery demand and commodity pricing.
2.  **May 1 - US ISM Manufacturing PMI:** Focusing on new orders and inventory sub-components. A sustained rebound would validate the restocking cycle under the "re-industrialization" thesis.
3.  **Apr 28 - Caterpillar (CAT) Earnings & Conference Call:** A bellwether for global construction and mining. Focus on guidance for North American infrastructure and recovery in Asian markets.
4.  **Apr 29 - Airbus Production Rate Update:** Announcement of ramp-up plans for the A321XLR. Reflects the easing of aerospace supply chain bottlenecks and impacts tier-1 suppliers like Safran and MTU.
5.  **Apr 27 - Major US DoD Contract Awards:** Details on $50B budget allocation for hypersonic missiles and next-gen defense systems, directly affecting backlog expectations for Lockheed Martin (LMT) and Raytheon (RTX).
6.  **Apr 28 - GE Aerospace Maintenance Update:** Turnaround Time (TAT) data for LEAP engines, a core metric for measuring the health of the commercial aviation aftermarket.

## 2. Key Analytical Questions

1.  **Has global manufacturing bottomed out?** Do the April PMI data from China and the US show synchronized expansion, signaling a broad recovery in the global industrial cycle?
2.  **Is the "Super Cycle" for Defense contractors already priced in?** Following the latest budget details, has the valuation premium for defense stocks already factored in future growth?
3.  **Is the aerospace supply chain recovery matching delivery targets?** Is Airbus's ramp-up plan too aggressive, and are engines and avionics still the primary bottlenecks?
4.  **Can the "AI Premium" in the Robotics & Automation sector be sustained?** In this earnings season, what percentage of new orders for giants like ABB and Fanuc are AI-driven, and what are the margin profiles?

## 3. Data Requirements

*   **Macro Indicators:** China NBS PMI, Caixin PMI, US ISM Manufacturing PMI, US Durable Goods Orders.
*   **Tickers:** CAT, DE, BA, AIR.PA, LMT, RTX, GD, ABB, SIE.DE, FANUC, 600031.SH (Sany Heavy Industry).
*   **Indices:** S&P 500 Industrials (S5INDU), Euro Stoxx Industrials, CSI 300 Industrials.
*   **Specialized Data:** Jet fuel prices, daily flight volumes for major airlines, DoD contract announcement database.

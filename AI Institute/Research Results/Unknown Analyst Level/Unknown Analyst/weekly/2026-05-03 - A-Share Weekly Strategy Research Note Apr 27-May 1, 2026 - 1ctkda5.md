# A-Share Weekly Strategy Research Note (Apr 27-May 1, 2026)

## Priority Conclusions

1. **The earnings inflection is broadly confirmed, but it is not a market-wide recovery.** 2026Q1 A-share attributable net profit rose about 6.7% YoY, while non-financial A-share net profit rose 11.7%; the improvement is concentrated in STAR Market names, electronics/semiconductors, nonferrous metals, chemicals, and selected services, while steel, autos, food & beverage, construction and building materials remain drags.
2. **There was no clear pre-holiday margin exodus.** Combined Shanghai and Shenzhen margin financing balances rose from RMB 26845.30bn on Apr 24 to RMB 27038.92bn on Apr 29, up RMB 193.62bn over three sessions; the Apr 30 SZSE aggregate was not stably available at writing, so “pre-holiday deleveraging” is not a verified conclusion.
3. **The style shift is best described as “growth validated by earnings,” not pure thematic speculation.** STAR 50 gained 8.07% for the week, far ahead of the Shanghai Composite’s 0.79% and CSI 300’s 0.80%; northbound active names and margin-buying industries point to electronics, AI compute, power equipment, and nonferrous metals.
4. **PMI supports the floor for a May rebound, but not a high-beta macro trade by itself.** The April manufacturing PMI was 50.3, the second straight month in expansion; new export orders at 50.3 were the bright spot, but non-manufacturing business activity at 49.4 and non-manufacturing new orders at 44.3 show that domestic services demand is still weak.
5. **The real lockup pressure comes after the holiday.** Actual lockup expiry market value was about RMB 17.414bn this week, well below the plan’s assumed RMB 60bn stress; the May 6-15 window carries about RMB 100.830bn of expected actual expiries, with May 6 at RMB 44.688bn and May 11 at RMB 34.663bn the key risk dates.

## Data And Method

The trading window is Apr 27-Apr 30, 2026; May 1, 2026 was the Labor Day market holiday. ## Question 1: Is The Earnings Inflection Confirmed?

### Method

Use the near-complete Q1 reporting dataset as of Apr 30, 2026, comparing total A-shares, non-financial A-shares, boards, and industry profit growth; use 2025 full-year and 2025Q3 figures as the baseline to judge whether low-level repair has become earnings expansion.

### Findings

| Metric | 2026Q1 value | Comparison base | Conclusion |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Companies with Q1 reports | 5503 | About 99.8% reporting progress | Sample is nearly complete |
| Total A-share revenue YoY | +4.66% to +5.0% | 2025 full-year +1.3% | Demand accelerated |
| Total A-share attributable net profit YoY | +6.7% to +7.2% | 2025 full-year +3.3% | Earnings improved |
| Non-financial A-share revenue YoY | +5.2% | 2025 full-year +1.0% | Non-financials stronger |
| Non-financial A-share net profit YoY | +11.7% | 2025 full-year -0.3% | Inflection broadly confirmed |
| Companies with positive attributable profit growth | 2845, or 52% | 2658 negative-growth companies, or 48% | Breadth just above half |
| Companies with attributable profit growth above 100% | 824, or 15% | 374 companies grew 30%-50% | High growth is concentrated |

Interpretation: The earnings inflection is confirmed, mainly because non-financial A-share net profit moved from -0.3% in 2025 to +11.7% in 2026Q1. But breadth is only slightly above half, so this is not a synchronized market-wide earnings upcycle. By industry, software services, semiconductors, and nonferrous metals led attributable net profit growth at +259.57%, +148.00%, and +85.79%, while steel, durable consumer goods, autos & components, and food & beverage remained sharply negative. The allocation implication is that May growth exposure should still be tied to verified earnings delivery.

### Method

Use SSE and SZSE margin trading summary data to calculate margin buying, estimate margin repayment, and track balance changes. Apr 27-Apr 29 have complete Shanghai and Shenzhen aggregate data; on Apr 30, only the SSE aggregate could be verified, while the SZSE public interface returned an empty table.

Interpretation: On the complete Apr 27-Apr 29 comparison window, financing balances rose for a cumulative RMB 193.62bn, which does not support a strong “pre-holiday margin exit” conclusion. The better description is “localized cooling from a high-leverage level,” not trend deleveraging.

## Question 4: Is PMI Enough To Support A May Rebound?

### Method

Use the April China PMI release published by the National Bureau of Statistics on Apr 30, 2026, comparing manufacturing, non-manufacturing, composite PMI, and key sub-indexes, while reviewing the past 12 months of manufacturing PMI.

### Findings

| Indicator | Apr 2026 | Mar 2026 | MoM change | Read-through |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Manufacturing PMI | 50.3 | 50.4 | -0.1 | Still expanding, but slower |
| Production | 51.5 | 51.4 | +0.1 | Production resilience |
| New orders | 50.6 | 51.6 | -1.0 | Domestic demand expansion slowed |
| New export orders | 50.3 | 49.1 | +1.2 | External demand improved |
| Main raw material purchase prices | 63.7 | 63.9 | -0.2 | Cost pressure still high |
| Output prices | 55.1 | 55.4 | -0.3 | Price pass-through continues |
| Non-manufacturing business activity | 49.4 | 50.1 | -0.7 | Services/construction drag |
| Composite PMI output | 50.1 | 50.5 | -0.4 | Broad economy in weak expansion |

Interpretation: PMI supports the view that the economy is not losing momentum sharply, but it is not enough to drive a strong rebound by itself. Better manufacturing production and export orders favor electronics, power equipment, nonferrous metals, and chemicals. However, non-manufacturing returned to contraction, suggesting that services consumption and the construction/property chain still need policy or data catalysts. High cost-price indexes also create margin pressure for midstream companies. The May strategy should favor verified growth manufacturing and resource names rather than a broad beta increase.

### Evidence

- NBS: April manufacturing PMI 50.3, production 51.5, new orders 50.6, new export orders 50.3, non-manufacturing business activity 49.4, composite PMI output 50.1. Source: [National Bureau of Statistics, Apr 30, 2026](https://www.stats.gov.cn/sj/zxfbhjd/202604/t20260430_1963473.html).

### Caveats

PMI is a diffusion index, not a direct measure of output or profit growth. April data may include pre-holiday production, export pull-forward, and price shocks, so May high-frequency operating rates, property sales, and export orders need confirmation.

## Strategy Outlook

For the first week of May, maintain a barbell of “growth manufacturing + resource-price exposure + high-dividend defense.” On the growth side, prioritize semiconductors, AI compute, and power equipment where Q1 earnings have been validated and margin/northbound activity remains high. In cyclicals, focus on nonferrous metals and chemicals where profit elasticity has already appeared and prices still have support. Use banks and utilities as defensive offsets against post-holiday lockups and high-leverage drawdowns. Avoid high-ratio expiry names with suddenly larger floats, and pure themes whose Q1 earnings did not validate April price gains.

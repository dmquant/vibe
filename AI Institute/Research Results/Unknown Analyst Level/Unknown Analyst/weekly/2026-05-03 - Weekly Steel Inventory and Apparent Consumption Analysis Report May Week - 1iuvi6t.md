# Weekly Steel Inventory and Apparent Consumption Analysis Report (May Week 1, 2026)

## Executive Summary
For the week ending April 30, 2026, the steel market exhibited a strong "pre-holiday effect." Driven by restocking ahead of the May Day holiday, both mill and social inventories continued to decline. Mill inventory dropped for the 7th consecutive week, reaching a periodic low of 4.34 million tonnes. While short-term restocking supported prices, the 4.4% year-on-year decline in Q1 apparent consumption underscores persistent pressure from the construction sector.

## Detailed Analysis

### 1. Mill Inventory
As of April 30, mill inventories for the five major carbon steel products stood at **4.34 million tonnes**, a week-on-week decrease of **3.9%**.
- **Trend:** This marks the 7th consecutive week of decline in mill stocks.
- **Product Breakdown:** Rebar saw the most significant drop, falling **7.2%** on-week, primarily due to steady demand from construction sites and concentrated pre-holiday restocking.
- **Supply Side:** Average daily hot metal output at 247 surveyed mills rose to **2.395 million tonnes**, indicating a slight recovery as some blast furnaces resumed production following margin improvements.

### 2. Social Inventory
Social inventories in major cities eased further this week as traders and end-users aggressively replenished stocks before the May Day holiday (May 1–5).
- **Market Behavior:** Traders reported accelerated destocking, with the market operating at generally low inventory levels.
- **Outlook:** Trading is expected to stall during the holiday, with a potential minor seasonal uptick in social inventory during the first week post-holiday.

### 3. Apparent Consumption
- **Quarterly Performance:** China's apparent crude steel consumption in Q1 2026 reached approximately **220 million tonnes**, down **4.4% year-on-year**.
- **Full-Year Forecast:** Analysts project total apparent consumption for 2026 to fall by **0.9% to 1.1%**, reaching an estimated **837 million metric tons**.
- **Structural Divergence:** While real estate demand continues to bottom out, manufacturing (auto, shipbuilding, white goods) and infrastructure sectors remain the primary pillars of support.

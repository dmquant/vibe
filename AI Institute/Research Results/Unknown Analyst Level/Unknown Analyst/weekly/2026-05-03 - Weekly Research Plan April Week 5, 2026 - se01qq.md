# Weekly Research Plan: April Week 5, 2026

**Coverage Window: 2026-04-27 (Monday) → 2026-05-01 (Friday)**

## 1. Scope
This week represents one of the most critical trading windows for the first half of 2026, encompassing the final Fed meeting of Jerome Powell's chairmanship, pivotal inflation data, and a heavy-hitting tech earnings season.

*   **FOMC Meeting (2026-04-29):** Chair Jerome Powell’s final meeting. Rates were held steady at 3.50%–3.75%, but the decision saw four hawkish dissents citing inflation risks fueled by the "Iran war."
*   **Q1 GDP & PCE Data (2026-04-30):** Preliminary Q1 GDP grew at an annualized rate of 2.0% (below expectations). March PCE inflation jumped to 3.5% due to an oil shock, sparking debates over "stagflation" risks.
*   **Big Tech Earnings (2026-04-28/30):** Alphabet (Google) shares surged on 63% cloud growth, while Meta received a cold reception as investors questioned its massive data center capital expenditures.
*   **Middle East Conflict & Energy:** Ongoing hostilities led to significant oil price volatility, making the Energy sector a primary destination for defensive rotation this week.
*   **Global Holiday Effect (2026-05-01):** International Workers' Day led to closures in China, Hong Kong, and Europe, leaving the US market to carry global liquidity on Friday.

## 2. Research Questions
1.  **Monetary Policy Signaling:** What do the "four dissents" signify for the incoming Chair's policy path? Has the prospect of a rate cut been completely derailed by the PCE jump?
2.  **AI ROI Realities:** Does the divergence between Alphabet and Meta mark the beginning of the "delivery phase" for AI? Is the market losing patience with raw compute spending?
3.  **Geopolitical Pricing:** How significantly are rising energy prices squeezing Q2 margins? Which industries (e.g., logistics, manufacturing) are showing the best cost-pass-through capabilities?
4.  **Stagflation Defense:** Given the 2% GDP growth and 3.5% PCE inflation backdrop, is the flight to Energy and defensive sectors a sustainable trend?

## 3. Key Data Needs
*   **Macro Indicators:** Q1 GDP breakdown (Consumption, Fixed Investment), March PCE/Core PCE detailed categories, and leading indicators for non-farm payrolls.
*   **Sector Performance:** S&P 500 weekly sector returns, intraday Brent/WTI crude movements, and shifts in the 10-year Treasury yield curve.
*   **Corporate Financials:** Quarterly Capex growth, cloud margins, and AI revenue contribution for Alphabet, Microsoft, Meta, and Amazon.

## 4. Proposed Visualizations
1.  **Weekly Sector Heatmap:** Visualizing the extreme divergence between Energy/Materials and Tech, highlighting geopolitically-driven capital flows.
2.  **PCE vs. Fed Funds Rate Timeline:** Mapping the "narrow corridor" between the 3.5% PCE rebound and the 3.75% rate ceiling, annotated with Fed dissents.
3.  **Hyperscaler Comparison Table:** Side-by-side comparison of GOOGL, AMZN, and MSFT cloud growth rates vs. AI-related capital intensity to quantify performance gaps.
4.  **Oil Prices vs. Logistics Cost Index:** Correlating Brent crude prices with global shipping indices to analyze cost-side pressure.

## 5. Report Outline
1.  **Executive Summary:** Recap of the sideways trading after a five-week rally; defining the "high inflation, low growth" defensive theme.
2.  **Macro: Powell’s Farewell & the Inflation Ghost:** Deep dive into the Fed’s internal split and the PCE spike’s impact on the bond market.
3.  **Feature: The AI Divide:** Analyzing the "delivery vs. spending" narrative through the latest hyperscaler earnings data.
4.  **Geopolitics: Supply Chain Realignment Under Oil Stress:** Assessing the pass-through effects of energy costs on manufacturing and logistics.
5.  **Outlook: May Strategy Recommendations:** Suggesting an increased weighting in commodities and cash assets ahead of the incoming Chair's first public remarks.

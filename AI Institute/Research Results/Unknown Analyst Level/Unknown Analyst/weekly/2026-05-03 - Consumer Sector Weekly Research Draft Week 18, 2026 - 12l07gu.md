# Consumer Sector Weekly Research Draft (Week 18, 2026)

**Coverage window: April 27 to May 1, 2026; research date: May 3, 2026.**

## Priority Conclusions

1. **Baijiu did not deliver broad-based high growth; the pattern was “Moutai stable, Wuliangye rebounding from a low base, Luzhou Laojiao under pressure.”** 600519.SH reported 2026Q1 revenue of RMB53.909 billion, up 6.54% YoY; 000858.SZ reported RMB22.838 billion, up 33.67%; 000568.SZ reported RMB8.025 billion, down 14.19%. Using average inventory / quarterly COGS * 90 days, estimated DOI was 995.4 days, 426.7 days, and 1206.4 days, respectively. High DOI is partly structural for baijiu, so the more important warning indicator is contract liabilities: Moutai -62.2% QoQ, Luzhou Laojiao -17.0%, Wuliangye +5.0%.
2. **Restaurant earnings recovery is coming from table turnover and model upgrades, not higher average spending.** Haidilao’s 2025 table turnover was 3.9 times/day and average spending was RMB97.7. In 2026Q1, Jiumaojiu’s Tai Er Mainland China average spending was RMB67, table turnover was 3.5 times/day, and same-store daily sales rose 10.9%. Stable or slightly better average spending is not enough to offset traffic volatility; supply-chain cost savings look closer to 1-2ppt, not a full repricing-level margin lever.
3. **Trade-in policy transmission is already front-loaded in 2026. The lag from policy launch to terminal sales is roughly 1-4 weeks, while category-level reported sales and company revenue recognition can lag by 1-2 months.** MOFCOM reported more than RMB433.17 billion of Q1 consumer-goods trade-in sales and RMB502.94 billion by April 12. The 2026 policy framework was issued on December 30, 2025, and January sales were already close to RMB59 billion.
4. **May Day consumption growth is stronger in lower-tier and long-haul travel markets.** Tongcheng Travel reported county-level destination product booking heat up 128% YoY, county-level car-rental booking heat up 113%, and county-level premium hotel booking heat up 76%. Ctrip data cited by media put cross-province travel at 60.6% of trips, with northern tourists traveling south up 28.2%. Tier-1 and tier-2 cities remain the main source markets, but lower-tier destinations are growing faster.
5. **Several assumptions in the plan need correction.** April official manufacturing PMI was 50.3%, while the non-manufacturing business activity index was 49.4%, so non-manufacturing was not in expansion. Cotti ended its “all drinks RMB9.9 unlimited” campaign on January 31, 2026; the Luckin/Cotti price war is moving into subsidy reduction and profit-model competition, not a fresh extension this week.

## Question 1: Channel Inventory Pressure and Potential Channel Stuffing

### Method

I used the 2026Q1 reports of 600519.SH, 000858.SZ, and 000568.SZ, extracting revenue, COGS, inventory, contract liabilities, and operating cash flow. DOI is estimated as “average inventory / Q1 COGS * 90 days.” QoQ contract-liability change is used as a proxy for distributor prepayments and channel health. The balance-sheet comparison period is December 31, 2025 to March 31, 2026; YoY revenue and profit growth use the company-reported basis.

### Findings

| Company | 2026Q1 Revenue (RMB bn) | Revenue YoY | Attributable NP (RMB bn) | NP YoY | Gross Margin | Inventory QoQ | DOI (days, est.) | Contract Liabilities QoQ | OCF/Revenue | Assessment |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Kweichow Moutai 600519.SH | 53.909 | +6.54% | 27.243 | +1.47% | 89.8% | -1.2% | 995.4 | -62.2% | 49.9% | No clear inventory deterioration, but lower prepayments indicate slower channel pacing |
| Wuliangye 000858.SZ | 22.838 | +33.67% | 8.063 | +82.57% | 81.4% | +0.4% | 426.7 | +5.0% | -11.1% | Strong reported growth, but negative OCF requires follow-up on collection quality |
| Luzhou Laojiao 000568.SZ | 8.025 | -14.19% | 3.708 | -19.25% | 85.8% | -1.8% | 1206.4 | -17.0% | 56.6% | Demand is weak; no evidence of inventory build-driven stuffing |

Absolute inventory days are structurally high in premium baijiu because base liquor and finished-goods reserves are long-cycle assets. The more useful lens is inventory plus contract liabilities plus operating cash flow. Moutai’s inventory declined and cash flow was strong, but contract liabilities fell sharply, suggesting active channel pacing. Wuliangye had the strongest revenue and profit growth, but OCF was negative RMB2.535 billion, implying a mismatch between reported growth and cash collection timing. Luzhou Laojiao’s revenue and profit declined, but inventory did not build; channel stuffing is not the main issue, while sell-through demand is.

### Caveats

DOI uses reported COGS as a proxy for depletion and does not split base liquor, semi-finished goods, and finished goods. Channel inventory days do not have a standardized public definition. Wholesale prices come from third-party quote platforms and may differ from actual transaction prices. Wuliangye restated 2025 comparable figures due to an accounting correction, so follow-up annual-report review is needed.

## Question 2: Restaurant Breakeven and Supply-Chain Cost Savings

### Method

I used 06862.HK’s 2025 annual results, 09922.HK’s 2025 annual results, and 09922.HK’s 2026Q1 voluntary operating data to compare table turnover, average spending, same-store sales, and cost ratios. Because Hong Kong-listed restaurant companies normally do not publish full 2026Q1 P&Ls, this section combines the latest operating data with 2025 cost structures to judge margin headroom.

### Findings

| Company/Brand | Latest Period | Average Spending | Table Turnover | Same-store/Revenue Trend | Cost Clue | Breakeven Assessment |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|---|
| Haidilao 06862.HK | 2025 full year | RMB97.7 | 3.9 times/day | Revenue RMB43.225bn, +1.1% YoY; core operating profit RMB5.403bn, -13.3% YoY | Raw materials and consumables at 40.5% of revenue, +2.6ppt YoY | Returning turnover to 4.1 times/day matters more than cost savings |
| Tai Er Mainland China 09922.HK | 2026Q1 | RMB67 | 3.5 times/day | Same-store daily sales +10.9% | 2025H1 raw-material cost at 35.6%; staff cost at 30.1% | Model upgrade is working, but labor rigidity limits margin expansion |
| Song Hot Pot 09922.HK | 2026Q1 | RMB108 | 2.1 times/day | Same-store daily sales -19.9% | Higher ticket but lower turnover | Traffic recovery must come first; cost savings alone are insufficient |
| Jiu Mao Jiu self-operated 09922.HK | 2026Q1 | RMB58 | 2.3 times/day | Same-store daily sales -11.3% | Noodle model is labor and rent sensitive | Recovery depends on post-closure store efficiency |

Restaurant leaders still have cost-optimization room, but the marginal return is diminishing. Haidilao’s raw-material and consumables ratio rose to 40.5%, reflecting business mix and dish optimization rather than only procurement slippage. If that line could return to the 2024 level, the theoretical margin room is roughly 2-3ppt, but that could conflict with product experience. Jiumaojiu’s raw-material ratio was relatively stable; the bigger pressure is staff cost, which rose from 29.2% to 30.1%. Therefore near-term breakeven improvement depends more on table turnover recovery, store closures, and new store formats than on aggressive food-cost cuts.

### Evidence

- Haidilao 2025 results: revenue RMB43.225bn, core operating profit RMB5.403bn, table turnover 3.9 times/day, average spending RMB97.7; FinancialReports / HKEX filing summary. https://financialreports.eu/filings/haidilao-international-holding-ltd/earnings-release/2026/33025012/
- Soochow Securities Haidilao note: raw materials and consumables +2.6ppt to 40.5% of revenue; tier-3-and-below revenue share 41.6%; more than 200 themed restaurants upgraded in 2025. https://www.fxbaogao.com/detail/5317928
- Jiumaojiu 2026Q1 operating data: Tai Er Mainland China same-store daily sales +10.9%, Tai Er Mainland average spending RMB67 and turnover 3.5 times/day, Song Hot Pot average spending RMB108. https://www.sl886.com/newstrading/4406325
- Jiumaojiu 2025 results: revenue RMB5.233bn, down 13.8%; annual profit RMB51.25mn; 644 restaurants. https://www.guandian.cn/m/show/551629

### Caveats

Haidilao has not disclosed full 2026Q1 operating metrics. Jiumaojiu’s Q1 figures are unaudited operating data. Cost ratios are not perfectly comparable across companies because delivery, franchising, new brands, and supply-chain sales are classified differently.

## Question 3: Trade-In Policy Transmission Lag

### Method

I used the December 30, 2025 policy documents from NDRC/MOF and MOFCOM plus six other departments as the start date, then tracked MOFCOM-reported sales for January 2026, Q1 2026, and through April 12. The time from policy release to first visible sales is the traffic lag; monthly/quarterly category statistics and company revenue recognition represent the reporting lag.

### Findings

| Stage | Date/Period | Indicator | Value | Implied Lag |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Policy framework issued | 2025-12-30 | Unified subsidies for six home-appliance categories, four digital/smart-product categories, auto scrappage/replacement | Appliances 15%, max RMB1500/item; digital 15%, max RMB500/item | Starting point |
| First-month sales visible | January 2026 | Home-appliance trade-in and digital/smart-product purchase sales | Nearly RMB59bn | Around 1-4 weeks |
| Q1 cumulative | 2026Q1 | Consumer-goods trade-in sales | More than RMB433.17bn, benefiting 60.933mn people | Full ramp within about one quarter |
| Mid-April | Through April 12, 2026 | Consumer-goods trade-in sales | RMB502.94bn, benefiting 69.777mn people | Continuous realization |

The policy did not need a full quarter to reach terminal sales. Once subsidy systems and retailers were ready, January already showed meaningful sales. Durable goods and digital products still involve subsidy eligibility, ordering, audit, and reimbursement, so orders can release within 1-4 weeks, while company revenue and retail-sales categories may lag by 1-2 months. For Q2 allocation, likely elasticity ranks higher in home appliances, smart home, digital smart products, and offline chain retailers. Autos have more uneven timing because scrappage/replacement eligibility, license plates, and local funding matter.

### Evidence

- MOFCOM and six other departments’ 2026 trade-in notice: unified scope and standards, local flexibility for smart-home subsidies, and monthly balanced fund use. https://www.mofcom.gov.cn/zwgk/zcfb/art/2025/art_3898435c79e5475f8afbde63aee27b80.html
- NDRC/MOF 2026 “two renewals” notice: subsidy ratios and caps for home appliances, digital smart products, and autos. https://www.ndrc.gov.cn/xwdt/tzgg/202512/t20251230_1402852.html
- MOFCOM data: Q1 2026 consumer-goods trade-in sales exceeded RMB433.17bn; by April 12 sales exceeded RMB502.94bn. https://finance.sina.com.cn/roll/2026-04-03/doc-inhtfrex8783924.shtml; https://www.yicai.com/news/103134445.html

### Caveats

Policy sales are based on MOFCOM big-data monitoring and cannot be treated as one-for-one incremental retail sales. The figures also cannot be fully attributed to subsidies. Local funding pace, eligibility review, merchant lists, and anti-fraud monitoring affect realized demand.

## Question 4: May Day Consumption Structure and City-Tier Differences

### Method

I used booking and heat data released by Tongcheng Travel, Ctrip, Qunar, Meituan, and Munniao Homestay from April 17 to May 2, 2026. I compared county-level and tier-3-and-below destinations with tier-1/tier-2 popular cities on booking growth, cross-province share, car rental, and hotel orders.

### Findings

| Indicator | Lower-Tier/County Markets | Tier-1/Tier-2 and Popular Cities | Difference |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| County-level destination product booking heat | +128% | Some popular-city flight/hotel/ticket bookings nearly +40% | County growth is materially faster |
| County-level car-rental booking heat | +113% | County users’ average rental duration is 1.2 days longer than city-periphery users | County deep travel boosts mobility demand |
| County-level premium hotel booking heat | +76% | Sanya premium hotels +47%; Guangzhou hotels +43%, Qingdao +40%, Chengdu +37%, Wuhan +35% | Lower-tier lodging upgrade is faster |
| Cross-province travel share | Small cities and long-haul destinations benefit | Ctrip cited cross-province travel share at 60.6% | Long-haul travel supports two-way flows |
| Restaurant linkage | County food destinations entered hot lists | Haidilao first-day holiday reservations exceeded 25,000 tables as of April 27 | Dining demand improved, but ticket-size elasticity was not disclosed |

Tier-1 and tier-2 cities remain the core source markets and absolute traffic centers, but lower-tier destinations are growing faster. County-level destination heat of +128% and county-level premium hotel heat of +76% suggest this is not merely a low-price shift; it is a combination of lower-density destinations, higher-quality stays, and destination car rental. For consumer companies, the opportunity extends beyond travel platforms to regional restaurants, convenience retail, instant retail, and brands with lower-tier distribution.

### Evidence

- Tongcheng Travel 2026 May Day Travel Trend Report: travel-product search heat +364% MoM, cross-province hotel order share +15ppt YoY, county-level destination booking heat +128%, county car rentals +113%, county premium hotels +76%. https://finance.sina.com.cn/stock/hkstock/hkstocknews/2026-04-17/doc-inhuvkwu2100934.shtml
- Global Times platform-data roundup: Ctrip cross-province travel share 60.6%, northern tourists traveling south +28.2%, Haidilao first-day holiday reservations above 25,000 tables. https://finance.sina.cn/2026-05-02/detail-inhwnhap5907354.d.html
- Munniao Homestay trend report: popular tier-3/tier-4 city homestay orders doubled, county travel orders +82% YoY. https://district.ce.cn/newarea/roll/202604/t20260417_2911702.shtml

### Caveats

As of May 3, 2026, full-holiday official May Day statistics have not yet been released. Platform heat and bookings are not the same as realized consumption value. City-tier definitions, order deduplication, and refund treatment differ by platform, so the figures should not be added across sources.

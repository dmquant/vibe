# A-share Weekly Strategy Research: 2026-04-27 to 2026-04-30

## Priority Conclusions

This was not a typical pre-holiday defensive week. It was a structural rebound driven by policy support and earnings validation: the SSE Composite rose 0.79% over four trading days, STAR 50 rose 8.08%, and ChiNext rose 0.26%, with growth clearly outperforming. The official manufacturing PMI was 50.3 in April, staying above 50 for a second month, but the non-manufacturing business activity index fell to 49.4, showing that endogenous demand recovery is still uneven, with production stronger than services and orders stronger than employment. The Politburo wording on property was “strive to stabilize the real estate market and steadily advance urban renewal,” which is a floor-setting policy rather than a new stimulus cycle. Earnings season confirmed that EPS acceleration is concentrated in computers, nonferrous metals, conglomerates, electronics, and defense; dividend defensiveness was temporarily outweighed by verified growth earnings.

## Question 1: PMI Momentum

### Method

I used the April 2026 China PMI release published by the National Bureau of Statistics on 2026-04-30, comparing manufacturing PMI, the non-manufacturing business activity index, and manufacturing sub-indices from 2025-05 to 2026-04. The 50 line is used as the expansion/contraction threshold, with focus on production, new orders, new export orders, purchasing volume, and expectations.

### Findings

| Indicator | 2026-03 | 2026-04 | MoM Change | Threshold Read |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | --- |
| Manufacturing PMI | 50.4 | 50.3 | -0.1 | Expansion |
| Manufacturing production | 51.4 | 51.5 | +0.1 | Expansion |
| Manufacturing new orders | 51.6 | 50.6 | -1.0 | Slower expansion |
| New export orders | 49.1 | 50.3 | +1.2 | Back to expansion |
| Purchasing volume | 50.9 | 51.1 | +0.2 | Expansion |
| Production and operation expectations | 53.4 | 54.5 | +1.1 | Improved confidence |
| Non-manufacturing business activity | 50.1 | 49.4 | -0.7 | Contraction |
| Construction business activity | 49.3 | 48.0 | -1.3 | Contraction |
| Services business activity | 50.2 | 49.6 | -0.6 | Contraction |
| Composite PMI output | 50.5 | 50.1 | -0.4 | Weak expansion |

The manufacturing improvement is more about production, purchasing, export orders, and expectations moving together than about a broad-based demand surge. New orders remained above 50 but fell 1.0 point month on month, so the domestic order slope was weaker than in March; non-manufacturing new orders were only 44.3, and both services and construction were below 50. Public investment and urban renewal expectations can provide a floor for construction-related demand, but the April construction PMI at 48.0 does not support the claim that infrastructure strength has already materialized. The combined read is that overall output is still expanding, but endogenous momentum is in a mild and uneven recovery phase.

### Evidence

The NBS release “China Purchasing Managers Index for April 2026” was published at 09:30 on 2026-04-30 and reported manufacturing PMI at 50.3, non-manufacturing business activity at 49.4, composite PMI output at 50.1, and detailed sub-indices. [Source](https://www.stats.gov.cn/sj/zxfbhjd/202604/t20260430_1963473.html)

### Caveats

PMI is a diffusion index and reflects the share of firms reporting improvement or deterioration, not output or revenue scale. April data can be affected by Qingming, pre-May Day stocking, and export delivery timing; a one-month rebound in new export orders does not by itself prove an external demand trend reversal.

## Question 2: Policy Tone

### Method

I used the Xinhua report on the 2026-04-28 Politburo meeting and same-day property sector commentary, comparing real estate wording with the 2024-09 phrase “promote the real estate market to stop falling and stabilize” and the late-2025 phrase “focus on stabilizing the real estate market.” I also checked the property-chain market reaction during the week.

### Findings

| Observation | Conclusion | Market/Text Evidence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Property keyword | “Strive to stabilize the real estate market” | Meeting text |
| Urban renewal keyword | “Steadily advance urban renewal” | Placed alongside property stabilization |
| “Housing-land integration” wording | Not found | Not in the public release |
| Surprise level | Neutral to positive | Clear direction, not forceful stimulus |
| SW Real Estate weekly return | +3.85% | 801180.SI rose from 2093.30 to 2173.89 |
| Building Materials weekly return | +2.58% | 801710.SI rose from 6242.43 to 6403.33 |

The marginal positive for property policy is that housing stabilization and urban renewal were placed together, which the market read as a two-track approach: stabilize the existing market and improve incremental quality. Compared with “stop falling and stabilize,” the phrase “strive to stabilize” emphasizes a support process and risk mitigation rather than a return to property-led demand expansion. Real estate and building materials rose 3.85% and 2.58% respectively this week, so valuation repair has already occurred, but the construction PMI at 48.0 and weak property fundamentals still require validation from sales, inventory, and local urban renewal projects. The strategy read is that the property chain is tradable for policy expectation repair, but should not be upgraded to a medium-term main line.

### Evidence

The Xinhua report said policy should “strive to stabilize the real estate market and steadily advance urban renewal,” and also mentioned “stabilize and strengthen capital market confidence” and fully implementing the “AI+” action. [Source](https://cpc.people.com.cn/n1/2026/0428/c64094-40710407.html) NBD commentary said the meeting placed property stabilization and urban renewal together, reflecting a two-track approach. [Source](https://www.nbd.com.cn/articles/2026-04-28/4368433.html)

### Caveats

The Politburo meeting is a directional document and did not disclose funding scale, project lists, or local implementation timetables. Property-chain share-price moves also reflected pre-holiday risk appetite and low-level rebound demand, so they cannot be fully attributed to policy.

Allocation should shift from thematic multiple expansion toward verifiable growth, especially electronics, computers, compute-related power supply/storage in electrical equipment, and nonferrous metals.

## Investment Strategy

For the post-holiday period, keep the framework of “growth leadership, dividend base, tactical property-chain participation.” Within growth, prioritize electronics, computers, AI compute-related equipment, storage and data-center power supply within electrical equipment, and nonferrous metals as the resource-cycle broadening trade. Keep a base allocation in dividend banks, coal, and transportation, but avoid chasing low-beta defensives. For the property chain, focus only on policy repair around urban renewal, building materials, and home-appliance after-cycle demand. Key risk triggers are post-holiday turnover falling below RMB 2.3 trillion, STAR 50 reversing on heavy volume, or May macro data failing to extend the production-side improvement in PMI.

# Weekly Research Report: May 4–8, 2026

**Date:** May 10, 2026
**Coverage Window:** 2026-05-04 — 2026-05-08

## 1. Executive Summary

Global markets exhibited extreme divergence this week. Despite a massive beat in the April Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP) report, which typically fuels hawkish fears, markets interpreted the data as evidence of economic resilience due to moderating wage growth. Technology and AI semiconductors, led by a blockbuster earnings report from AMD, surged significantly. Conversely, energy prices retreated sharply as geopolitical risk premiums in the Middle East began to ease, providing support for future disinflationary narratives.

*   **Market Themes:** Tech "decoupling" vs. the "deep freeze" of interest rate cut expectations.
*   **Key Data:** NFP added 115k (vs. 65k expected); Unemployment Rate held at 4.3%.
*   **Sector Focus:** AMD soared 26.3% weekly, lifting the Nasdaq by 4.5%; Energy (XLE) tumbled 6.2% on falling crude prices.

## 2. Macro Deep Dive

### April NFP: Resilience Beyond Expectations
The April jobs report not only surpassed expectations but nearly doubled the consensus estimate. However, underlying metrics suggest a "slow hiring, slow firing" equilibrium.

| Metric | Actual | Consensus | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Non-Farm Payrolls** | **115k** | 65k | Led by Health Care & Retail |
| **Unemployment Rate** | **4.3%** | 4.3% | Unchanged |
| **Avg. Hourly Earnings (MoM)** | **0.2%** | 0.3% | Below expectations (Good for inflation) |
| **Avg. Hourly Earnings (YoY)** | **3.6%** | 3.7% | Trending toward target |

**Interpretation:** This "Goldilocks" combination of strong employment and soft wage growth alleviates immediate pressure on the Fed to hike, but has effectively wiped out expectations for a rate cut in 2026. Markets now price the first easing window as late as the second half of 2027.

### Fed Dissension: Cracks in the Consensus
The 8–4 vote from the April 29 meeting was fully digested this week. This marks the highest level of policy dissent in over 30 years.

*   **Dovish Dissent (Stephen Miran):** Advocated for an immediate 25bps cut, citing overly restrictive policy.
*   **Hawkish Dissent (Logan, Kashkari, Hammack):** Opposed the "easing bias" language, pointing to sticky inflation driven by energy and geopolitical shocks.

## 3. Sector & Equity Analysis

### AI Infrastructure: AMD’s Data Center Dominance
AMD’s Q1 report served as a major catalyst for the week, with Data Center performance significantly exceeding forecasts.

| Key Metric | AMD Q1 Actual | Market Consensus | YoY Growth |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Total Revenue** | **$10.3B** | $9.89B | +38% |
| **Data Center Revenue** | **$5.8B** | ~$5.2B | **+57%** |
| **Non-GAAP EPS** | **$1.37** | $1.29 | - |

**Analysis:** AMD has successfully established itself as the primary "second source" for AI silicon. The Instinct MI300X ramp and deep partnership with Meta (expanding up to 6GW of infrastructure) are the core drivers. While NVIDIA still holds >80% share, AMD's scale and profitability are improving rapidly.

### Consumption Signals: Divergent Recovery
*   **Disney (DIS):** Streaming achieved double-digit margins (10.6%) for the first time. The success of *Zootopia 2* ($1.9B box office) signals a structural shift in profitability.
*   **Uber (UBER):** Guidance for gross bookings slightly beat consensus. Uber One reached 50M members, showing high-end service resilience despite macro headwinds.

### Core Recommendations
1.  **Stay Overweight AI Infrastructure:** AMD and NVIDIA results prove that infrastructure demand is structural, not a bubble.
2.  **Avoid Commodity Volatility:** While falling oil is good for inflation, the Middle East ceasefire is fragile; do not aggressively short energy.
3.  **Focus on Rate-Resilient Large Caps:** Delayed rate cuts will keep pressure on small caps (Russell 2000). Prefer "Fortress Balance Sheet" tech and blue chips.

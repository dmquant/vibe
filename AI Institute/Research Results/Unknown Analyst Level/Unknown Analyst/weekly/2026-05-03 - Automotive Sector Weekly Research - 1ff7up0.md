# Automotive Sector Weekly Research
## Coverage Window: 2026-04-27 to 2026-05-01

## Prioritized Conclusions

1. The main message from Auto China 2026 is not one single technology, but a package shift toward 800V/1000V fast charging, urban NOA/L3 pilots, AI cockpits, and semi-solid or solid-state validation vehicles. Fast charging and NOA are closest to becoming standard equipment, while solid-state batteries remain in prototype and pilot-line validation.
2. In April premium NEV deliveries, Li Auto delivered 34,085 vehicles, Huawei HIMA delivered 32,759, XPENG delivered 31,011, Xiaomi EV delivered more than 30,000, and NIO delivered 29,356. Li Auto led HIMA by 1,326 vehicles on volume, but Xiaomi and XPENG had the better sequential momentum.
3. BYD's Q1 gross margin, calculated from operating revenue and operating cost, was 18.81%, narrowly above the 18% threshold. However, net profit attributable to shareholders was RMB4.085bn, down 55.38% YoY, which means price competition, the NEV purchase-tax transition, and FX losses have already offset much of its scale advantage.
4. In the solid-state battery chain, the companies with the strongest claim to Tier 1 entry are still cell makers and vehicle-validation leaders such as CATL, BYD, Changan, Dongfeng, CALB, WeLion, and QingTao. Electrolyte and anode suppliers are mostly in joint development, patent, sample, or pilot-introduction stages, which should not be treated as mass-production nomination.
5. Overseas growth can partly cushion Europe risk. BYD sold 321,165 passenger NEVs overseas in Q1, 45.85% of total NEV sales and up 55.84% YoY. Still, a potential European tariff gap affects unit profit and brand mix, so Southeast Asia, the Middle East, and Brazil are growth buffers rather than one-for-one substitutes.

## Question 1: What “Standard Configuration” Trends Emerged From Auto China 2026?

### Method

I used public reporting from Auto China 2026 over 2026-04-24 to 2026-04-29 and scored each trend from 0 to 5 based on whether it has production models, regulatory or city-level pilots, and a visible supply-chain delivery path. Sources include SCIO's show coverage, Cartea's technology review, SMM's solid-state battery feature, and TrendForce's solid-state battery update.

### Findings

| Technology trend | Research score (0-5) | Production or validation status | Key numbers | Investment implication |
|---|---:|---|---|---|
| 800V/1000V ultra-fast charging | 5 | Multiple production or announced models | 400km/5 minutes, megawatt charging narrative | Higher certainty for high-voltage connectors, SiC, and thermal management |
| Urban NOA/L3 | 4 | 23 cities opened L3 highway or expressway pilot sections with 10-second takeover rules | AITO M9 and Li L9 Livis showed multi-LiDAR setups | Smart-driving hardware is moving from option to mid-to-high trim standard |
| AI cockpit and multi-screen interaction | 5 | Already common in major show vehicles | Large-model cockpits and vehicle-cloud interaction | Benefits cockpit SoCs, displays, audio, and software subscriptions |
| Semi-solid and solid-state batteries | 2 | Moving from slides to validation vehicles and pilot lines | Changan 400Wh/kg and 1,500km target; Dongfeng 350Wh/kg and September 2026 mass-production target | 2026 is more about theme and pilot-line orders, with revenue impact pushed later |
| Domestic ADAS chips | 4 | Horizon and other solutions entering nominations and production schedules | Journey 6M and 1000TOPS-class compute narrative | Domestic substitution accelerates beyond Nvidia Thor |

Interpretation: The show confirms that “experience standardization” is ahead of “materials revolution standardization.” 800V/1000V and NOA have clearer models, price-band migration, and 2026 penetration logic. Solid-state batteries attracted the most attention, but TrendForce estimates all-solid-state technology readiness at about TRL 4-5, potentially reaching 5-6 by the end of 2026. L3 deployment also depends on regulation, insurance, and liability boundaries, so showroom progress is faster than nationwide commercialization.

## Question 3: Did BYD Hold the 18% Gross Margin Line in Q1?

### Method

I read BYD's Q1 2026 report and used operating revenue, operating cost, net profit attributable to shareholders, and sales data. Gross margin is calculated as `(operating revenue - operating cost) / operating revenue`. For peers, I compared Tesla Q1 2026, Seres Q1 2026, and Li Auto's latest disclosed quarter; as of 2026-05-03, Li Auto had not yet reported Q1 2026, so Q4 2025 is used as a substitute and clearly labeled.

### Findings

| Company | Period | Revenue | Cost/gross profit data | Gross margin | Net profit | Note |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|
| BYD | Q1 2026 | RMB150.225bn | Operating cost RMB121.974bn | 18.81% | Attributable net profit RMB4.085bn | Down 55.38% YoY |
| Tesla | Q1 2026 | USD22.387bn | Total gross profit USD4.720bn | 21.1% | Attributable net profit USD477mn | Total automotive gross margin was also 21.1% |
| Seres | Q1 2026 | RMB25.746bn | Broker/data-vendor disclosure | 26.2% | Attributable net profit RMB754.46mn | Up 0.89% YoY |
| Li Auto | Q4 2025, substitute | RMB28.8bn | Gross profit RMB5.1bn | 17.8% | Net profit RMB20.2mn | Q1 2026 not yet disclosed |

Interpretation: BYD's 18.81% Q1 gross margin held the 18% line, but the cushion was only 0.81 percentage points. More importantly, Q1 NEV sales were 700,463 vehicles, down 30.01% YoY and 47.82% QoQ, revenue fell 11.82% YoY, and attributable net profit fell 55.38% YoY, showing that scale no longer fully absorbs price and expense pressure. Overseas sales of 321,165 vehicles, or 45.85% of NEV sales, provided structural support, but FX losses lifted finance expenses to RMB2.100bn and reduced the overseas profit hedge. The conclusion is that gross margin was preserved, but profit elasticity has clearly entered a diminishing-return phase.

## Question 4: From Slides to Prototype Vehicles, Which Solid-State Battery Companies Are Truly Entering Tier 1 Systems?

### Method

I define “entering the Tier 1 supply system” in three tiers. Tier A means vehicle installation, validation, or a mainstream cell maker's pilot line. Tier B means joint development with leading cell makers or automakers, or equipment tenders. Tier C means materials R&D, patents, samples, or early validation. I focus on electrolyte, anode, and cell-integration roles, and I do not treat show-floor displays as mass-production nominations.

### Findings

| Company/segment | Technology route | Supply-chain status | Evidence strength | Conclusion |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| CATL | Sulfide solid electrolyte and interface protection | Small-series goal in 2027; patents and equipment progress in 2026 | A- | Highest Tier 1 certainty, but not yet large-scale 2026 revenue |
| BYD | All-solid-state and sodium-ion routes in parallel | Demonstration-vehicle target around 2027, with equipment tenders and joint work under way | A- | Vertical integration gives it validation advantages |
| Changan Golden Bell | 400Wh/kg all-solid-state | Vehicle trials before Q3 2026, 2027 mass-production target | A- | Automaker-led or joint system; supplier details still pending |
| Dongfeng | 350Wh/kg solid-state | Extreme-environment testing, September 2026 mass-production target | B+ | Closer to prototype-vehicle validation, but production schedule needs confirmation |
| CALB | 400Wh/kg hybrid solid-liquid and 450Wh/kg all-solid-state | Show display, applications include vehicles and other categories | B | Tier 1 cell maker, but customer nominations are not fully disclosed |
| WeLion / QingTao | Semi-solid and oxide routes | Existing semi-solid installation and production-line experience | B | Has vehicle experience, but all-solid-state commercialization must be separated from semi-solid |
| Idemitsu Kosan, Mitsui Mining & Smelting, Lotte Energy Materials, Posco Future M | Cathode and solid-electrolyte materials | Technical solutions released with large OEMs | B | Materials are entering validation chains, but this is not equal to volume nomination |
| Anode-material companies | Silicon-carbon or lithium-metal interface | Mostly samples and joint development | C | Constrained by interface stability, swelling, and manufacturing yield |

Interpretation: The investable order is cell integration and equipment orders first, materials profit later. In 2026, the highest-certainty signals are equipment tenders, pilot lines, and validation vehicles, not volume installation of all-solid-state packs. Sulfide routes offer high conductivity, but interface stability, air sensitivity, and cost remain bottlenecks; oxide and semi-solid routes are easier to commercialize first but deliver less of the energy-density leap implied by all-solid-state narratives. Anodes require particular caution because lithium metal and silicon-carbon routes are still competing, and customer validation is not the same as Tier 1 production share.

## Question 5: Can Southeast Asia and the Middle East Offset a Potential European Gap?

### Method

I cross-checked BYD Q1 overseas sales, CPCA and industry export data, public regional market-share reports, and automaker localization actions. The comparison is against uncertainty from potential European anti-subsidy duties, with Thailand, Brazil, and the UAE used as representative non-European growth markets.

### Findings

| Market/indicator | Latest value | Time | Meaning | Evidence |
|---|---:|---|---|---|
| BYD overseas passenger NEV sales | 321,165 vehicles | Q1 2026 | 45.85% of BYD NEV sales, up 55.84% YoY | CnEVPost/BYD company-data compilation |
| China auto exports | >1.8mn vehicles | Q1 2026 | Up 25% YoY, NEVs over 40% of exports | Cartea Auto China review |
| Chinese EV brand share in Thailand | >65% | 2026 show/market observation | BYD, MG, GWM, and peers still hold core share | Maybank/Thailand Motor Show material |
| Chinese brand share in Brazil EV segment | >80% | 2026-03 | BYD around 74%, with GWM adding scale | Rio Times |
| Chinese auto brand share in UAE | 7-12% | 2025 estimate | Up from about 4% in 2023, still early-stage | CarBike360 |

Interpretation: Southeast Asia and Brazil can add volume, while the Middle East can add premium-brand and PHEV/luxury-SUV demand, but the three regions together do not equal Europe. Europe's value lies in price band, brand premium, and regulatory signaling; if anti-subsidy duties pressure European deliveries, non-European markets are more likely to cushion revenue growth than fully replace margin. BYD's 45.85% overseas mix shows the direction is right, but also brings FX, logistics, channel inventory, and localization capex pressure. For investment purposes, companies with local plants, KD assembly, channel finance, and after-sales networks deserve priority.

## Valuation Comparison

### Method and Conclusion

Valuation uses publicly available TTM P/S and P/E data as close as possible to 2026-04-30. Free public data does not provide a fully consistent historical percentile set, so this report uses historical medians, 3-year and 5-year averages, or available ranges as approximations. The conclusion: TSLA remains a high-multiple autonomy and robotics option; LI's P/S has compressed sharply versus its own history but P/E has risen because profits fell; NIO and XPEV are better judged on P/S than P/E; BYD sits between legacy autos and smart-EV peers on valuation.

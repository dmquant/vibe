# Weekly Research Plan (May Week 1, 2026)

**Coverage Window: 2026-05-04 (Monday) → 2026-05-08 (Friday)**

---

## 1. Scope — Material Developments

1.  **China Trade Data Outperformance (May 8):** April exports rose 14.1% YoY (vs. 2.5% previously), while imports grew 25.3%. Semiconductor and automobile exports were the primary drivers, signaling resilient global demand and supply chain strength.
2.  **US Markets Hit Record Highs (May 4–8):** The Nasdaq Composite surged 4.5% for the week, and the S&P 500 gained 2.3%. The rally was highly concentrated in the AI sector, led by AMD’s 26.3% weekly surge.
3.  **US-Iran Naval Conflict (May 7):** A military exchange occurred in the Strait of Hormuz. Brent crude briefly broke $120/barrel before settling at $100.20, reflecting a significant geopolitical risk premium.
4.  **Labor Day Holiday Closure (May 4–5):** China’s markets were closed on Monday and Tuesday for the Labor Day holiday, reopening on Wednesday (May 6). Saturday (May 9) was an adjusted workday but markets remained closed.
5.  **US Non-Farm Payrolls Report (May 8):** April added 115k jobs (vs. 65k estimated), with the unemployment rate steady at 4.3%, indicating a robust labor market despite high interest rates.
6.  **Trump-Xi Summit Preparations (May 4–8):** Both nations confirmed that President Trump and President Xi will meet in Beijing on May 14–15. Markets are closely watching for discussions on trade tariffs and the Iran conflict.
7.  **Yen Intervention Activity:** Observers noted suspected intervention by Tokyo (est. $67 billion) to defend the Japanese Yen below the 155 level against the USD.
8.  **Central Bank Decisions:** The Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA) hiked its cash rate by 25bps to 4.35% on May 5; the Federal Reserve held rates steady at 3.50%–3.75%.

## 2. Analytical Questions

1.  **Trade Resilience Analysis:** Is the sharp rebound in China’s April exports sustainable? Does the growth in semiconductor and auto exports signal a bottoming out of the global manufacturing cycle?
2.  **AI Bubble Risk Assessment:** With US market gains heavily concentrated in a few chip stocks (e.g., AMD, Nvidia), does this extreme divergence suggest that valuation bubbles are reaching a critical point?
3.  **Geopolitics and Energy:** Could the skirmish in the Strait of Hormuz evolve into a long-term blockade? How will oil prices sustained above $100 impact the current disinflationary trend?
4.  **Summit Expectations:** Can the mid-May Beijing summit achieve substantial de-escalation regarding key trade restrictions or the regional conflict in Iran?
5.  **Defending the Yen:** Against the backdrop of strong US jobs data, can BOJ intervention effectively counter the interest-rate-driven depreciation of the Yen?

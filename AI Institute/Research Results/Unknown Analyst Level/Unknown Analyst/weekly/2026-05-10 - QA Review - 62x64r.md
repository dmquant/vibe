## 3. Narrative Coherence

The overall chain is coherent: Q1-Q6 from the plan each have corresponding research sections, and the research note generally uses appropriate caveats where data are incomplete.

Key breaks in the chain:

- Q1 asks whether net financing turned positive. The research proves only that the public issuance sample recovered; it does not prove a full-market positive net-financing turn. This limitation should be front-loaded in the final weekly.
- Q2 asks for a full rating and tenor heatmap. The research only covers the AAA term structure and 1Y rating cells; missing 3Y/5Y AA+/AA MTN and LGFV cells weaken the conclusion that rating spreads were stable.
- Q4 asks for province-level LGFV net financing and 3Y AA(2) LGFV spreads. The research substitutes a common 1Y AA(2) LGFV spread change of **+0.23bp**; that can support a sector-level caution, but not a province ranking.
- Q5 still answers the private-developer spread question mainly through event status and issuer watch lists, without bond-level transactions, valuation deviations, or private-vs-SOE spread data.
- Q6 has only 3-4 observations, so it does not meet the plan’s rolling 20-day correlation requirement. The positioning recommendation is reasonable, but its statistical support is weak.

## 4. Editorial Quality

- Reword the first priority conclusion as “public samples show a supply restart, but full-market net financing is not yet confirmed,” so readers do not mistake **CNY 47.437bn** for net financing.
- Add “substitute basis” or “public-sample basis” directly in the titles of V1, V3, and V5 to reduce PM misreading risk.
- Separate “no publicly verifiable new event” from “not publicly reconstructable”: the former is a search result, while the latter is a data-availability limitation.
- Add a “Pending Wind/DM Verification” box in the executive summary or data-basis page covering full-market net financing, cancellations, DM/Wind event database checks, property bond-level spreads, and the rolling 20-day correlation.

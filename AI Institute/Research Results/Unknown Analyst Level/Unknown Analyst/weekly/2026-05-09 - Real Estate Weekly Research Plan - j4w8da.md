# Real Estate Weekly Research Plan

**Coverage window: 2026-05-04 (Mon) → 2026-05-08 (Fri)**
**Report Date: 2026-05-10 (Sun)**

## 1. Scope of Developments
The real estate market in the first week of May 2026 showed significant signs of recovery, driven by the ripple effects of policy easing combined with holiday consumption momentum.

1.  **Shenzhen Policy Easing Effect (May 4-8):** Following the relaxation of purchase restrictions on April 29, Shenzhen saw its highest level of secondary home inquiries and transactions in six years during this week, indicating a robust restoration of market confidence.
2.  **May Day Golden Week Flash Report (May 1-5 retrospective):** Secondary home transactions across 25 key cities rose 31% YoY. Specifically, daily average transactions in Beijing surged by 72%, while primary home sales in Shenzhen increased by 48% YoY.
3.  **Beijing Land Market Warm-up (May 6):** A residential plot in Yizhuang New Town was sold for CNY 1.3 billion at an 8.7% premium. This marks Beijing's first premium-priced land sale of 2026, signaling a return of confidence among top-tier developers.
4.  **SOE Sales Outperformance (May 7):** China Overseas Land & Investment (COLI) reported April contracted sales of RMB 24.19 billion, a 20% year-on-year increase, significantly outperforming the industry average.
5.  **National Inventory Digestion Strategy Implementation (May 8):** Central directives on "coordinating the digestion of existing housing inventory" began piloting in various regions, focusing on converting excess commercial real estate into affordable rental housing.
6.  **Investment Bank Sentiment Rebound:** Goldman Sachs and JPMorgan released research notes this week, suggesting that housing prices in first-tier cities bottomed out in Q1 2026, with a structural rise of approximately 3% expected for the full year.

## 2. Analytical Questions
1.  **Policy Transmission:** Will the easing in first-tier cities like Shenzhen and Beijing spark a new wave of similar policy shifts in strong second-tier hubs (e.g., Hangzhou, Nanjing) by mid-May?
2.  **Sustainability of Recovery:** Is the explosive growth during the May Day holiday a temporary "pulse" response to policy stimulus, or does it signal that the market has entered the recovery phase of an "L-shaped" bottom?
3.  **Shift in Land Acquisition Strategy:** Does the premium land sale in Beijing imply that leading developers (e.g., Poly, COLI) have shifted from "absolute caution" to "aggressive expansion in core districts"?
4.  **Effectiveness of the "Inventory-to-Social Housing" Model:** What is the funding leverage ratio for the government's buyback of existing housing? Given local debt pressures, how widely can this model be scaled?

## 3. Data Needs
-   **Transaction Data:** Daily transaction volumes and price indices for primary and secondary housing in 50 key cities (primarily 1st and 2nd tier).
-   **Land Auction Data:** Premium rates, failure rates, and the list of participating developers for key city land auctions this week.
-   **Inventory Data:** Changes in the inventory digestion cycle (months) for core cities, specifically the relief of inventory pressure in Shenzhen and Shanghai.
-   **Corporate Data:** Weekly site visit rates and conversion rates for Top 10 developers during April and the first week of May.

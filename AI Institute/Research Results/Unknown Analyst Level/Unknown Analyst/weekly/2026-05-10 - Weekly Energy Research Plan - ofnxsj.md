# Weekly Energy Research Plan
## Coverage window: 2026-05-04 (Mon) → 2026-05-08 (Fri)

### 1. Research Scope
This week’s energy markets were driven by severe geopolitical shocks, structural shifts in supply, and catalysts for the clean energy transition. The research will focus on the following key developments:

*   **Hormuz Crisis Peak & Pivot (May 4-7):** Brent crude surged to $114/bbl on May 4, reflecting an anticipated supply loss of 6.7 million b/d. Prices subsequently retreated to $103 on May 7 following signals of U.S. diplomatic intervention.
*   **UAE's Post-OPEC Strategy (May 1-8):** Following its formal exit from OPEC/OPEC+ on May 1, the UAE signaled intent to ramp capacity to 5 million b/d. We will analyze the impact of this move on market psychology and the pricing power of OPEC’s remaining spare capacity.
*   **U.S. LNG Infrastructure Ceiling (May 4-8):** Maintenance at major facilities including Corpus Christi and Cameron dropped U.S. LNG feedgas flows to 17.2–17.3 Bcf/d. Amid a global supply crunch, U.S. infrastructure hit its physical "ceiling."
*   **EU Hydrogen Auction & Renewable M&A (May 7-8):** The EU awarded €1.1 billion to nine hydrogen projects; Octopus Energy closed a €584 million wind acquisition; Turkey’s Kalyon PV launched 1.1 GW of solar module capacity.
*   **IRENA Cost Competitiveness Report (May 7):** The International Renewable Energy Agency released a report highlighting that solar-plus-storage now consistently beats fossil fuels on cost during periods of high price volatility.
*   **Data Center-Driven Power Surge:** Grid operators MISO and ERCOT issued warnings regarding data center loads significantly inflating summer natural gas power burn.

### 2. Core Analytical Questions
*   **Price Sustainability:** Given the ongoing physical blockade of the Strait of Hormuz, was the late-week price correction driven by short-covering or a genuine easing of fundamentals?
*   **OPEC Pricing Power:** Does the UAE's exit signal the beginning of the end for the OPEC+ coordinated cut framework? How does this redefine the oil price floor for H2 2026?
*   **LNG Risk Premium:** With U.S. maintenance overlapping with Qatari disruptions, what is the new baseline for European Q3 gas risk premiums?
*   **Transition Acceleration:** To what extent is the current fossil fuel crisis accelerating CAPEX for utility-scale storage, targeting the 158 GW global forecast for 2026?

### 3. Data Needs
*   **Price Series:** Daily Brent/WTI Crude, Henry Hub, and TTF Natural Gas futures (May 4–8).
*   **Physical Flows:** Daily ship-tracking data for the Strait of Hormuz; real-time LNG feedgas intake levels for major U.S. export terminals.
*   **Industry Benchmarks:** Cost data points for solar-plus-storage vs. fossil fuels from the May 7 IRENA LCOE report.
*   **Power Load:** Non-seasonal peak demand data from ERCOT/MISO and the corresponding share of natural gas in the generation mix for the first week of May.

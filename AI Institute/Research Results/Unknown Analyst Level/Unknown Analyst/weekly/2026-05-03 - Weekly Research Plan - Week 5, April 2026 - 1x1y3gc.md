# Weekly Research Plan - Week 5, April 2026

**本周覆盖范围 / Coverage window: 2026-04-27 (Mon) → 2026-05-01 (Fri)**

> **Note:** May 1, 2026 (Friday) is International Workers' Day. Markets in mainland China and Hong Kong are closed. US markets (NYSE, NASDAQ) remain open for regular trading.

## 1. Research Scope

This week was driven by the dual impact of "Super Earnings Week" and the FOMC policy decision. Key developments include:

1.  **FOMC Interest Rate Decision (April 29):** The Fed held rates steady. Chair Powell’s press conference highlighted a "lack of further progress" on inflation, suggesting rates may stay elevated for longer. *Significance: Directly pushed Treasury yields higher and weighed on growth stock valuations.*
2.  **Big Tech AI Earnings - Microsoft & Alphabet (April 28):** Both reported revenue beats, but marked a massive surge in AI Capital Expenditure (CapEx). *Significance: The market began questioning the timing of the ROI inflection point for AI investments.*
3.  **US Q1 GDP Advance Estimate (April 29):** Data showed unexpectedly soft growth paired with sticky core PCE inflation. *Significance: Sparked preliminary market discussions regarding "stagflation" risks.*
4.  **Apple & Amazon Earnings (April 30):** Apple announced the largest buyback program in its history, while Amazon Web Services (AWS) saw re-accelerated growth. *Significance: Capital rotated toward cash-rich value leaders with strong buyback support.*
5.  **US April Non-Farm Payrolls (May 1):** Job growth slowed significantly, and the unemployment rate ticked up to 4.0%. *Significance: Eased inflation fears triggered by the earlier hawkish Fed stance; markets re-priced a September rate cut.*
6.  **China/HK Volatility & Labor Day Holiday (May 1):** Ahead of the holiday closure, Chinese internet stocks saw significant short-covering driven by expectations of fiscal stimulus from the Politburo meeting. *Significance: Reflects rising investor appetite for domestic policy support.*

## 2. Key Analytical Questions

1.  **AI CapEx vs. Revenue:** Is the growth rate of tech giants' CapEx outpacing their AI-driven revenue growth? What are the long-term implications for operating margins?
2.  **Stagflation Risk Assessment:** Does the combination of weakening Q1 GDP and strengthening PCE constitute a genuine stagflation signal, or is it a short-term quarterly anomaly?
3.  **Labor Market Inflection:** Is the softening of the April NFP a healthy "normalization" of the labor market or the first sign of an approaching recession?
4.  **Rate Path Re-pricing:** Between Powell’s hawkishness and the weaker NFP, how has the consensus on the pace of rate cuts in H2 2026 shifted?
5.  **Risk-Off Sentiment & Rotation:** Is capital moving from high-premium tech growth sectors toward defensive/inflation-hedge areas like Utilities and Commodities?

## 3. Data Requirements

*   **Macro Data:** US Q1 GDP (QoQ Annualized), April Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP), April Unemployment Rate, Core PCE Price Index.
*   **Corporate Earnings:** YoY CapEx growth, Cloud revenue growth, and buyback figures for Microsoft, Alphabet, Meta, Apple, and Amazon.
*   **Market Indicators:** 10-Year US Treasury Yield (US10Y) weekly curve, VIX Index, US Dollar Index (DXY).
*   **Sector Performance:** Weekly returns for S&P 500 11 sectors (Focus: Tech vs. Energy/Utilities).
*   **Fund Flows:** EPFR Global fund flow data (Emerging Markets vs. US Equities).

# TMT Weekly Research Plan

Coverage window: 2026-05-04 (Monday) → 2026-05-08 (Friday)

Date anchor: this plan treats the shell-returned date, `2026-05-10`, as “today.” The covered period is the latest fully completed Monday-Friday trading week. Per the NYSE 2026 holiday calendar, the next relevant market closure is Memorial Day on `2026-05-25`, so `2026-05-04` through `2026-05-08` was a full five-trading-day week for U.S. TMT equities, with no market-holiday exclusions required.[S1]

**Priority Conclusions**

1. The week’s main theme was “AI capex validation plus semiconductor repricing”: `SMH` rose `11.13%`, well ahead of `QQQ` at `5.50%`, with `AMD` up `26.25%` and `INTC` up `25.40%`.[S2]
2. AMD’s Q1'26 results shifted the debate from a single-GPU demand story to a CPU + GPU + rack-scale systems story: revenue was `10.253B` dollars, up `38%` YoY; Data Center revenue was `5.8B` dollars, up `57%` YoY; and Q2 guidance was `11.2B ± 300M` dollars.[S3]
3. Software and edge cloud diverged: `IGV` rose `5.22%`, but `NET` fell `9.83%`. Cloudflare delivered Q1 revenue of `639.8M` dollars, up `34%` YoY, while announcing roughly `1,100` job cuts, showing that the AI-efficiency narrative can also trigger a reassessment of org design and margin execution.[S2][S4]
4. For consumer electronics supply chains, the key issues are “advanced-node diversification plus rising memory costs”: if the preliminary Apple-Intel foundry agreement is finalized, it would affect Intel Foundry credibility, TSMC capacity pricing, and Apple’s supply-chain redundancy.[S5][S6]

## 2. Core Analytical Questions

1. Was semiconductor outperformance this week driven by AMD fundamental revisions, Intel-specific news, or broader AI capex beta?
2. Are AMD’s Q1'26 results enough to change the 2026-2027 AI accelerator competitive landscape, especially market-share, margin, and customer-concentration assumptions versus `NVDA`?
3. If the preliminary Apple-Intel deal becomes a formal production order, what does it mean for Intel Foundry, TSMC capacity pricing, and Apple’s supply-chain risk premium?
4. With hyperscaler capex revised as high as `725B` dollars, which companies are true beneficiaries and which are absorbing FCF, depreciation, power, and memory-cost pressure?
5. Why did the software and edge-cloud AI narratives not re-rate in line with semiconductors? Does Cloudflare’s “growth + layoffs + agentic AI-first” mix become a template for SaaS/infrastructure software?
6. How will April employment data and the decline in Information jobs transmit through three TMT valuation channels: rates, labor costs, and enterprise IT budgets?

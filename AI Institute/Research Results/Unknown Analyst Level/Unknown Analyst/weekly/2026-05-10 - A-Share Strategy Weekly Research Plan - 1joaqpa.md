# A-Share Strategy Weekly Research Plan

**Coverage Window: 2026-05-04 (Mon) → 2026-05-08 (Fri)**
**Holiday Note:** The market was closed from May 1 to May 5 for the Labor Day holiday. A-shares reopened on May 6 (Wed).

## 1. Material Developments
The market this week was driven by a post-holiday rebound, better-than-expected macro data, and an easing of external geopolitical tensions.

*   **Post-Holiday "Opening High" (May 6):** Following a 5-day break, A-shares surged upon reopening. The STAR 50 index jumped 8% in a single day, while the Shanghai Composite gained 1.65% for the week, closing near 4,180.
*   **Huawei AI Catalyst & Semiconductor Rally (May 6-7):** Upward revisions to Huawei’s 2026 AI chip revenue forecasts (expected +60% growth) catalyzed a broad rally across semiconductors and AI hardware sectors.
*   **FX Reserves & Cross-Border Flows (May 7):** SAFE reported end-of-April FX reserves at $3.41 trillion, an increase of $68.4 billion from March, signaling eased capital outflow pressure.
*   **Surge in Trade Data (May 7-8):** April exports grew 14.1% YoY (vs. 2.5% in March), and imports rose 25.3%, significantly boosting sentiment for the export chain.
*   **US Tariff Policy Reversal (May 7):** The US Court of International Trade ruled the legal basis for 10% global import tariffs (under Section 122) invalid, improving global trade risk appetite.
*   **Middle East De-escalation & Risk Rotation (May 8):** Reports of a "peace framework" for the Middle East conflict led to a retreat in oil prices, shifting capital from defensive energy/coal to high-beta growth stocks.
*   **Pre-Summit Positioning (May 8):** Markets began positioning for the May 14 Xi-Trump summit in Beijing, with expectations for marginal improvements in trade disputes and export controls.

## 2. Analytical Questions
*   **Q1:** Has the 8% post-holiday surge in STAR 50 overextended valuations relative to short-term AI earnings expectations?
*   **Q2:** Does the invalidation of US Section 122 tariffs combined with the April trade beat suggest that the "Export Chain" will become the primary theme for May?
*   **Q3:** How strong is the resistance at 4,180? Will investors opt for wait-and-see caution or front-run the upcoming Xi-Trump summit?
*   **Q4:** Given the stronger-than-expected FX reserve growth, does the PBoC have room for a meaningful interest rate cut in May/June?
*   **Q5:** Is the style rotation from resources/coal to tech blue-chips a structural shift or a temporary de-risking move?

## 3. Data Needs
*   **Core Indices:** Price-volume and valuation percentiles for Shanghai Composite (000001.SH), CSI 300 (000300.SH), and STAR 50 (000688.SH).
*   **Sector Chains:** Weekly alpha for Semiconductors, AI Hardware, Power Equipment, Coal, and Petrochemicals.
*   **Macro Series:** April Trade data (by country/product), breakdown of FX reserve changes, and consensus forecasts for April CPI/PPI.
*   **Capital Flows:** Weekly net Northbound buy/sell (if applicable) and Margin Trading balance changes.
*   **Sentiment Surveys:** Summary of market consensus regarding the upcoming Xi-Trump summit.

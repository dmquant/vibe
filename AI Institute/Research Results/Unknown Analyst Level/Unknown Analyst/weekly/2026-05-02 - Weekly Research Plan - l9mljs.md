# Weekly Research Plan

**Coverage window: 2026-04-27 (Mon) → 2026-05-01 (Fri)**

## 1. Scope of Research

This week’s focus centers on alternative data (satellite imagery, consumer spending, app usage, and supply chain) reflecting 5–8 material developments:

- **04-27: Satellite Monitoring of SE Asian Ports** — Satellite imagery shows an 18% YoY increase in container throughput at Haiphong (Vietnam) and Laem Chabang (Thailand), signaling a sustained shift in global supply chains toward Southeast Asia.
- **04-28: Enterprise AI Adoption Surge** — Third-party app usage data indicates a 15% WoW increase in Daily Active Users (DAU) for enterprise versions of major AI platforms (e.g., OpenAI, Anthropic), suggesting AI infrastructure investment is translating into operational use.
- **04-29: US Credit Card Spending Rotation** — Transaction data from the final week of April shows a slowdown in durable electronics spending, contrasted by a 22% spike in summer travel bookings.
- **04-30: China Pre-Holiday Traffic Peak** — Real-time congestion indices from Amap/Baidu show that traffic in Tier-1 cities (Beijing, Shanghai) on the day before the Labor Day holiday was 12% higher than the same period in 2025.
- **05-01: US Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP) Release** — Alternative employment trackers (scraping job postings from sites like LinkedIn/Indeed) had predicted a slight miss versus consensus, which was largely confirmed by the official cooling labor market data.
- **05-01: Market Holiday Notice** — China A-shares, Hong Kong, and most European markets were closed for International Workers' Day; liquidity remained concentrated in US sessions.

## 2. Key Analytical Questions

1. **Supply Chain Authenticity**: Does the satellite-tracked growth in SE Asian port activity align with the latest regional trade balance reports, or are there seasonal anomalies at play?
2. **AI Monetization Path**: Can the rise in enterprise AI app engagement support the high valuation multiples of tech giants like NVIDIA and Microsoft ahead of their earnings calls?
3. **Consumption Divergence**: How should the shift from "durables to services" observed in credit card data inform short-term tactical allocations in retail ETFs like XRT?
4. **China’s Travel Economy Strength**: Does the record-high pre-holiday traffic suggest that travel and hospitality stocks (e.g., Trip.com, Huazhu) will see post-holiday earnings beats?
5. **Macro Forecasting Efficacy**: How did the lead-time of the job-posting-based alt-index perform this week in predicting the official NFP surprise?

## 3. Data Requirements

- **Satellite Imagery**: Sentinel-2 infrared and optical data comparing Haiphong, Laem Chabang, and Shanghai port activity.
- **App Metrics**: Sensor Tower or App Annie data for enterprise downloads and DAU of top generative AI tools.
- **Credit Card Flows**: Earnest or Second Measure indices for US retail categories (durables, airlines, hotels).
- **Migration & Traffic**: Baidu Migration or Amap real-time congestion delay indices.
- **Macro Alternative Indicators**: Real-time job posting growth rates from LinkUp or Indeed.

## 4. Proposed Visualizations

1. **Stacked Bar Chart: Port Activity Index** — Comparison of container yard occupancy and vessel berthing frequency between major Chinese ports and emerging SE Asian hubs.
2. **Line Chart: AI App Engagement vs. Tech Stock Performance** — Correlation between DAU growth of top AI platforms and the NASDAQ 100 index.
3. **Heatmap: Consumer Category Spending Shifts** — MoM and YoY changes across various US spending categories for the final week of April.
4. **Comparative Line Chart: Pre-Labor Day Traffic Indices** — Overlay of traffic flow trends for Tier-1 cities across 2024, 2025, and 2026.

1. **Executive Summary**: A one-page synthesis of core signals derived from this week's alternative data.

3. **Digital Economy & AI Adoption**: Assessment of the actual implementation progress of generative AI in the enterprise sector.

5. **Macro Validation & Policy Impact**: Using alternative indicators to verify official macro data releases (e.g., NFP).

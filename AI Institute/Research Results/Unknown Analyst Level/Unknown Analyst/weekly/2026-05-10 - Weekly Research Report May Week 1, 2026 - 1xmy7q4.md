## 1. Executive Summary

This week, global markets navigated a complex trifecta of resilient trade data, AI-driven equity euphoria, and geopolitical volatility. China's April trade figures significantly outperformed expectations, signaling a structural shift toward semiconductor and automotive-led exports. U.S. equities surged to all-time highs, propelled by AMD’s blowout earnings and a "Goldilocks" jobs report, though the rally remained highly concentrated in the "AI inference" trade. Geopolitically, a naval skirmish in the Strait of Hormuz caused a sharp but transient spike in oil prices, which eventually settled near $100 as markets balanced war risks against potential peace overtures. Japan’s $67B intervention temporarily stabilized the Yen, though interest rate differentials continue to exert downward pressure.

## 2. Macro Focus: China’s Structural Trade Rebound

### 2.1 Research Conclusion
China’s April export surge (+14.1%) was primarily driven by high-value-added tech sectors rather than traditional low-end manufacturing. This suggests China is successfully transitioning from a "global assembly hub" to a primary provider of core components and finished high-tech goods like EVs and ICs.

### 2.3 Deep-Dive Interpretation
*   **Structural Pivot:** Automotive exports (43% of which are NEVs) and Integrated Circuits (ICs) have become the new anchors of trade growth. Exponential growth in brands like Geely (+244.7%) underscores rising global brand equity.
*   **Partner Divergence:** Trade with ASEAN (+15.7%) and the EU (+13.2%) remains robust, while trade with the U.S. fell by 12.9%, reflecting a sustained diversification of trade routes.
*   **Caveat:** Some volume may be attributed to "front-loading" ahead of the May 14-15 Trump-Xi summit in Beijing.

## 3. Market Trends: AI-Led Divergence in U.S. Equities

### 3.1 Research Conclusion
U.S. indices hit record highs this week, characterized by a pivot toward "AI inference." AMD emerged as the new leader of the AI complex, surging 26.3% on the week (post-earnings), as the market shifts from pricing in "capacity buildout" (Nvidia's forte) to "deployment efficiency."

### 3.3 Deep-Dive Interpretation

*   **Concentration Risk:** The S&P 500’s 2.3% gain was heavily skewed; the top 10 chip names accounted for ~70% of the total index return. The median stock remains ~13% below its own all-time high, indicating a fragile, narrow-based rally.

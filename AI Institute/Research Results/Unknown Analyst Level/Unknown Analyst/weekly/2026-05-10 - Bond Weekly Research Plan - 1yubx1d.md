# Bond Weekly Research Plan
## Coverage window: 2026-05-04 (Mon) → 2026-05-08 (Fri)

> **Note:** The Chinese market was closed on May 4-5 (Mon-Tue) for the Labor Day holiday and reopened on May 6 (Wed). May 9 (Sat) was a compensatory working/trading day in China.

### 1. Key Market Developments (Scope)
The bond market this week was a tug-of-war between "inflation anxiety" and "labor market cooling":
- **US 30Y Treasury Yield Breaks 5.00% (May 5):** Driven by geopolitical tensions in the Strait of Hormuz and surging oil prices, the long end of the US curve saw a sell-off. The 30Y yield touched the 5.00% psychological threshold for the first time since mid-2025.
- **US April Jobs Report "Shocker" (May 8):** Non-farm payrolls added only ~70k (vs. 178k expected), and the unemployment rate ticked up to 4.3%. This triggered a massive rally in bonds on Friday, with the 10Y yield closing near 4.38%.
- **China Yield Curve Hits 4-Year Steepest Level (May 6-9):** Upon reopening after the holiday, the CGB 10Y-30Y spread widened to 52 bps, a four-year high, as long-term inflation fears (linked to oil) hit the ultra-long end.
- **PBoC Liquidity Support:** The People's Bank of China injected CNY 300 billion via reverse repos during and after the Labor Day break to ensure a stable market reopening.
- **Global Manufacturing Inflationary Pressure:** The J.P. Morgan Global PMI released on May 6 showed input prices hitting a 41-month high (51.8), complicating the narrative for a dovish central bank pivot.
- **Trump-Xi Summit Anticipation:** Markets began positioning for next week's summit between Presidents Trump and Xi. The Yuan stabilized, providing emotional support for the onshore bond market.
- **China's Strong April Trade Data (May 7):** Exports reached record highs, showcasing the resilience of China's supply chain and reinforcing CGBs' status as a global "safe haven."
- **RBA Rate Hike (May 5):** The Reserve Bank of Australia hiked rates by 25 bps to 4.35%, highlighting the persistence of global inflation and initially dampening bond sentiment.

### 2. Analytical Questions (Questions)
1. **US Narrative Shift:** Is the sharp slowdown in April payrolls sufficient to offset the "prices paid" inflation signals from PMIs? How has the probability of a June Fed "hold" shifted?
2. **CGB Curve Dynamics:** Is the 4-year high in the 10Y-30Y spread a structural shift or a temporary overshoot driven by geopolitical oil shocks?
3. **Safe Haven Efficacy:** How did the correlation between CGBs and USTs behave during the Strait of Hormuz escalation? Did CGBs provide effective diversification?
4. **Liquidity vs. Inflation:** Given record exports and imported inflation risks, will the PBoC tighten liquidity, or maintain an accommodative stance to support ultra-long special treasury issuance?

### 3. Data Requirements (Data Needs)
- **Interest Rates:** Daily yields for UST 2Y/10Y/30Y and CGB 1Y/10Y/30Y (including May 9).
- **Macro Indicators:** US April NFP, Unemployment Rate, ISM Services PMI; China April Trade Balance and PMI.
- **Policy Data:** PBoC daily OMO (reverse repo) volumes; summaries of Fed speaker comments this week.
- **Alternative Metrics:** Brent Oil price trends; Offshore Yuan (CNH) volatility.

### 5. Report Outline (Report Outline)
1. **Weekly Executive Summary:** Synthesizing the "Labor Cooling vs. Inflation Stickiness" theme and geopolitical noise.
2. **Global Rates Observation:** Focusing on the 30Y UST reversal after hitting 5% and the RBA hike's impact.
3. **Domestic Rates Focus:** Analyzing the post-holiday reopening, curve steepening, and liquidity environment.

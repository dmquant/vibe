## Priority Conclusions

1. **Keep the weekly stance at “growth leadership, technology as the spearhead, policy as downside support.”** In the final week of April, STAR 50 rose 8.07%, far ahead of CSI 300 at 0.80%, CSI 500 at 1.23%, and CSI 1000 at 0.94%; for April as a whole, STAR 50 gained 21.02%, the strongest among the major broad indices.
2. **The policy signal is positive, but it is not a pure aggregate-stimulus trade.** The April 28 Politburo meeting called for making full use of macro policies, ample liquidity, stabilizing and strengthening capital-market confidence, AI+, computing-power networks, next-generation communication networks, a unified national market, and anti-involution. This favors technology manufacturing, digital infrastructure, and advanced manufacturing, while property remains a stabilization trade rather than a trend reversal.
3. **PMI shows manufacturing expansion, but services and construction are dragging the recovery slope.** April manufacturing PMI was 50.3, above the 50 line for a second month. Non-manufacturing business activity was 49.4, with construction at 48.0 and services at 49.6, pointing to a mix of strong production, weak services, and high prices.
4. **Q1 earnings validate the technology-growth profit cycle.** Under the Eastmoney Choice count, 5503 companies reported Q1 revenue of RMB17.65 trillion, up 4.9% year on year, and attributable net profit of RMB1.59 trillion, up 6.7%; STAR Market Q1 net-profit growth exceeded 200%, while computer, non-ferrous metals, conglomerates, and electronics ranked near the top among Shenwan first-level industries.
5. **Allocation: overweight technology growth and AI hardware, market-weight midstream manufacturing and non-ferrous metals, underweight property-chain and pure defensives.** CSI 800 Information rose 3.77% for the week and 21.47% for April, aligned with Q1 earnings and policy direction. CSI 800 Telecom still gained 11.92% in April but fell 1.68% in the final week, so the stance should shift from chasing to buying pullbacks.

## Question 1: How Did the Politburo Meeting Change A-Share Risk Appetite?

### Method

I read the 2026-04-28 Xinhua / People's Daily Online Politburo meeting release and grouped the language into five policy channels: macro liquidity, domestic demand, industrial policy, risk resolution, and capital-market confidence. I then compared those signals with index and sector performance in the final week of April.

### Findings

| Policy dimension | Meeting language | A-share mapping | Strategy implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| Macro policy | Make full use of macro policies; keep liquidity ample | Support for valuation-sensitive growth assets | Positive for STAR 50, ChiNext, and AI chains |
| Domestic demand | Expand quality goods and services supply; promote consumption upgrading | Consumption is a repair trade, not the main line | Market-weight consumption; avoid chasing baijiu and property chain |
| Digital infrastructure | Computing-power networks, next-generation communication networks, AI+ | Technology manufacturing, TMT, communication equipment | Keep as the offensive core |
| Anti-involution | Advance the unified national market and address involution-style competition | Margin repair in industries undergoing supply discipline | Positive for non-ferrous metals, chemicals, and selected manufacturing |
| Risk resolution | Stabilize property, resolve local debt, strengthen capital-market confidence | Downside support, not a reversal | Lower systemic risk premium |

The meeting did not provide a single headline stimulus number, but its wording was more supportive than a routine stabilization message, especially on stronger and more practical measures, ample liquidity, and capital-market confidence. The market response was also clearer in growth than in value defensives: STAR 50 rose 8.07% for the week, while Large-Cap Value gained only 0.77%. The policy package therefore acts more like a risk-appetite backstop than a catalyst for a broad property-chain or old-economy reversal. Allocation should prioritize assets where policy direction and earnings confirmation overlap.

### Evidence

- People's Daily Online / Xinhua, 2026-04-28, “中共中央政治局召开会议 分析研究当前经济形势和经济工作”: the meeting referred to more proactive fiscal policy, moderately loose monetary policy, ample liquidity, AI+, and stabilizing and strengthening capital-market confidence. Source: https://cpc.people.com.cn/n1/2026/0428/c64094-40710407.html
- Index prices: AkShare `index_zh_a_hist`, codes `000688`, `000300`, `399373`, 2026-04-24 to 2026-04-30.

### Caveats

The meeting release is directional text and cannot directly determine the timing or size of fiscal and monetary implementation. The capital-market-confidence language supports risk appetite, but follow-through still depends on reserve-requirement moves, open-market liquidity, special-bond issuance, and industrial policy details.

## Question 2: Does April PMI Show Acceleration or Divergence?

### Method

I used the April China PMI release published by the National Bureau of Statistics on 2026-04-30 and compared April values with March across manufacturing, non-manufacturing, composite PMI, and key manufacturing sub-indexes.

### Findings

| Indicator | March 2026 | April 2026 | Monthly change | Read-through |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Manufacturing PMI | 50.4 | 50.3 | -0.1 | Still expanding, but slower |
| Production index | 51.4 | 51.5 | +0.1 | Production remains strong |
| New orders index | 51.6 | 50.6 | -1.0 | Demand expansion slowed |
| Raw material inventory | 47.7 | 49.3 | +1.6 | Destocking pressure eased |
| Employment | 48.6 | 48.8 | +0.2 | Employment remains below 50 |
| Supplier delivery time | 49.5 | 49.5 | 0.0 | Delivery times still lengthened |
| Non-manufacturing business activity | 50.1 | 49.4 | -0.7 | Fell into contraction |
| Construction business activity | 49.3 | 48.0 | -1.3 | Property and physical investment remain weak |
| Services business activity | 50.2 | 49.6 | -0.6 | Service recovery disappointed |
| Composite PMI output | 50.5 | 50.1 | -0.4 | Overall expansion, close to the threshold |

PMI does not show a one-way recovery; it shows expanding manufacturing and weakening non-manufacturing activity. The production index at 51.5 is above new orders at 50.6, which means supply is stronger than demand; if demand does not keep pace, margins may face price and inventory pressure. The NBS interpretation put high-tech manufacturing PMI at 52.2 and equipment manufacturing at 51.8, versus high energy-consuming industries at 47.9, reinforcing the allocation preference for technology manufacturing over traditional high-energy sectors. Price indexes were elevated, with raw material purchase prices at 63.7 and ex-factory prices at 55.1, which helps upstream resource profits but may squeeze parts of downstream manufacturing.

### Evidence

- National Bureau of Statistics, 2026-04-30, “2026年4月中国采购经理指数运行情况”: manufacturing PMI 50.3, non-manufacturing 49.4, composite PMI 50.1. Source: https://www.stats.gov.cn/sj/zxfbhjd/202604/t20260430_1963473.html
- National Bureau of Statistics, 2026-04-30, Huo Lihui interpretation of April PMI: high-tech manufacturing 52.2, equipment manufacturing 51.8, high energy-consuming industries 47.9, raw material purchase prices 63.7, ex-factory prices 55.1. Source: https://www.stats.gov.cn/sj/sjjd/202604/t20260430_1963474.html

### Caveats

PMI is a diffusion index, not an output or earnings growth rate. The April reading may be affected by pre-Labor-Day stocking, external energy prices, and order-delivery timing. Whether non-manufacturing weakness persists needs confirmation from May service consumption and property transaction data.

The earnings recovery is not extremely broad, with only about 52% of companies delivering attributable net-profit growth, but profit elasticity is concentrated in technology growth and resources. Financials still contribute the largest absolute profit pool, with ICBC at RMB86.941 billion in Q1 attributable net profit, but sector-index performance was muted, showing that the market is paying more for profit growth than profit size. Property, building materials, steel, and construction remain pressure points in revenue or profit, so it is too early to rotate aggressively into property-related cyclicals.

# US Tech Sector Weekly: FAAMG Performance, AI Themes, and Valuations
**Date: May 11, 2026**
**Strategist: HK/US Strategist**

## Executive Summary
The US technology sector reached fresh all-time highs this week, buoyed by robust earnings and a neutral stance from the Federal Reserve. Performance among FAAMG giants was bifurcated: Alphabet and Apple led the rally, while massive AI Capital Expenditure (CAPEX) plans caused investor caution for Microsoft and Meta. The AI narrative has officially transitioned into the "Agentic Execution" phase. While compute demand remains voracious, the market is increasingly scrutinizing the Return on Investment (ROI) timeline.

## 2. Key AI Themes: The "Agentic" Inflection Point
The narrative in May 2026 has shifted from model training to autonomous execution:

*   **Agentic Compute Dominance:** Q1 data shows that "agentic compute"—where AI agents autonomously instruct other agents—now accounts for over 50% of total compute cycles, marking the "Zenith of Computation."
*   **The CAPEX Arms Race:** Hyperscaler CAPEX is projected to reach $646 billion in 2026, representing roughly 2% of US GDP.
*   **Open-Source Parity:** The performance gap between open-source models and top-tier closed models (e.g., GPT-5.5 Instant) has narrowed to just 1.7%, shifting value toward the application layer.
*   **ROI Skepticism:** Despite massive spending, only 5.5% of organizations attribute more than 5% of earnings to AI, leading to a "trough of disillusionment" for some enterprise adopters.

---

## 3. Valuation Levels: Elevated but Supported
Despite record prices, valuations remain anchored by strong fundamentals.

*   **Sector P/E Ratios:** The S&P 500 Info Tech sector trades at a forward P/E of 34.94x, above the 5-year average (32.17x), but "fair" given the top 6 tech firms grew forward EPS by 42% YoY.
*   **Valuation Divergence:** 
    *   **Meta** remains the "value" play at 19.8x forward earnings.
    *   **Apple** trades at a premium of 35.5x P/E, reflecting high-margin services growth.
*   **Productivity Tailwinds:** Massive efficiency gains (e.g., 1000x in coding efficiency) are supporting record corporate margins despite rising energy costs.

---

## 4. Outlook and Conclusion
In the short term, the market is hyper-focused on Nvidia’s May 20 earnings report as a litmus test for AI hardware demand. Macroscopically, all eyes are on the upcoming Beijing Summit between President Trump and President Xi, which will cover AI safety standards and advanced chip export controls. We recommend focusing on "Cash Flow Kings" like Alphabet and Apple while monitoring the impact of heavy CAPEX on short-term margins.

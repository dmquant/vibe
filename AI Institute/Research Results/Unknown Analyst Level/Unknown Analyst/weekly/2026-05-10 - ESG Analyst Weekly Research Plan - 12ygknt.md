## 2. Questions: What the Weekly Must Answer

1. Do the SEC’s climate-rule rescission process and the EU’s ESRS simplification mean ESG disclosure is entering a “less disclosure” phase, or is the market shifting from mandatory standardization toward risk-based and regional divergence?
2. How will SFDR reform discussions, ESRS revisions, and the value-chain information cap change fund classification, corporate data availability, ESG scoring, and greenwashing risk?
3. With CBAM implementation work and the compliance carbon-market coalition appearing in the same week, will pricing links strengthen among EU ETS, CBAM certificates, and high-integrity Article 6 credits?
4. Do Vestas Q1 results mark an earnings inflection for the clean-energy equipment chain, or are they a company-specific and offshore-wind delivery-cycle improvement?
5. Do the IFC and Hong Kong Government HKD sustainable bond deals show Hong Kong green finance moving from project funding toward a tradable local-currency benchmark curve?
6. How should the week’s ESG catalysts map into portfolios: clean energy, traditional energy, European exporters, financial institutions, ESG data providers, and assurance firms?

## 5. Deliverable Outline: Final Weekly Report

1. **One-page conclusions**: Answer the week’s most important ESG market questions in 4-6 points, with explicit investment implications and risks.
2. **Regulation and disclosure**: Compare the SEC rescission path, ESRS simplification, SFDR discussions, and ISSB/California alternative baselines to assess disclosure comparability.
3. **Carbon markets and CBAM**: Cover CBAM implementation, the carbon-market coalition, EUA/UKA/CCA/RGGI moves, and cost pass-through for high-carbon industries.
4. **Clean energy and company fundamentals**: Use Vestas as the core case study linking the wind supply chain, ETF performance, and key stock catalysts.
5. **Green finance and flows**: Analyze IFC and Hong Kong Government bond issuance, Asian local-currency green curves, fund flows, and greenium.
6. **ESG risk radar**: List next-week watch items for greenwashing, climate litigation, disclosure rollback, data gaps, and portfolio exposure.
7. **Data appendix**: Include all price, bond, policy, ratings, and fund-flow sources, with collection timestamps and gaps.

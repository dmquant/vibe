## 0. Scope rules and execution principles

- **Coverage window**: 2026-05-04 (Mon) through 2026-05-08 (Fri), five calendar trading days. Run date 2026-05-10 is a Sunday, so per the date rule we cover the most recent fully-completed Mon-Fri window.
- **Holiday check**: the research stage must first verify, market by market (China A-shares, HK, Taiwan, Japan, US, Europe), whether the window contains any market holidays. Any holiday must be disclosed in a footnote and trading-day-only metrics (weekly returns, weekly turnover, weekly flows) must be recomputed against the actual number of trading days. The window itself is NOT shifted.
- **Pricing convention**: all "weekly" return / cumulative metrics are computed from Friday close to the prior Friday close; intraday timestamps are normalised to UTC+8.
- **Currency**: cross-market comparisons are quoted in local currency with a USD-denominated companion column; DXY and USDCNY moves over the window must be disclosed in footnotes.
- **Source hierarchy**: exchange / regulator filings > central bank / statistics-bureau primary text > Reuters / Bloomberg / Wind / Choice > mainstream financial press > sell-side research > self-published media (the last cannot be a sole source).
- **No-publish-before-check**: every concrete number, quote, or single-name reference produced in the research stage must carry at least one traceable link, to be independently re-verified in the fact-check stage.

## 2. Analytical questions the weekly must answer

1. **Q1 · Macro driver**: who drove global risk-asset pricing this week — Fed, China data, geopolitics, or earnings? Which asset prices most clearly express that driver?
2. **Q2 · Greater-China attribution**: how do A-share and HK weekly moves decompose by sector contribution? Are Northbound / Southbound flows aligned with the price signal?
3. **Q3 · USD vs. rates**: did DXY and US Treasury yields move in the same direction this week? If they diverged, what does the divergence imply about flows or policy?
4. **Q4 · Earnings and EPS**: among US companies reporting this week, what does the distribution of EPS / guidance surprises look like? Does it directionally revise Q2 consensus EPS?
5. **Q5 · Policy catalyst**: of the China regulatory / industrial-policy items this week, which one carries durable second-tier impact (not a one-day move)? Why?
6. **Q6 · Week-ahead**: based on this week's tape and next week's calendar (key data, central-bank speakers, important earnings), what 3 events should the reader prioritise?

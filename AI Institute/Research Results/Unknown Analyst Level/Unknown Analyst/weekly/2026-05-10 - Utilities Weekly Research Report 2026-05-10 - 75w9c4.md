## Executive Summary

1. **Load Growth "Contractualization" vs. Rate Pressure**: Duke Energy (DUK) disclosed 7.6 GW of executed Electric Service Agreements (ESAs) for data centers, converting the AI growth narrative into legal contracts. However, the sector remained suppressed by macro interest rates. XLU fell -3.93% for the week, driven by stronger-than-expected employment data (+115,000) and a capital rotation into technology stocks (+6.7%).
2. **Regulatory Pivot to Reliability**: NERC issued a Level 3 Alert, defining data centers as "Computational Load Entities" (CLEs) and mandating modeling and testing actions by August. This marks the transition of data centers from pure "growth engines" to "grid stability risks" requiring sophisticated management.
3. **Cost Pressures Hit Water Margins; Solid Waste Pricing Remains Resilient**: Essential Utilities (WTRG) saw revenue grow 10%, but O&M surged 27.6% due to merger integration and weather impacts. In contrast, Republic Services (RSG) offset volume declines with a 5.7% core price increase, achieving a 50 bps margin expansion.
4. **Energy Fundamentals Tightening**: The EIA natural gas storage injection (63 Bcf) came in below expectations, with Henry Hub prices stabilizing at $2.76/MMBtu, signaling fuel cost pressures ahead of the summer peak.

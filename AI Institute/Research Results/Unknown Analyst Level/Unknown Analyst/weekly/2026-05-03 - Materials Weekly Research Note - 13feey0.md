## Question 1: Will copper supply tightness persist through Q2?

### Method
I used LME Copper Cash and LME stocks from 2026-04-24 to 2026-04-30 as the high-frequency pricing and visible-inventory basis. For mine supply, I used Cochilco’s March 2026 monthly report for Chile’s January-February 2026 copper mine output, INE/news reporting for Chile’s March 2026 YoY copper output change, and MINEM/public reporting for Peru’s January-February 2026 copper output. The test was whether mine output was weakening while visible inventories failed to rebuild materially.

### Findings

| Metric | Latest value | Comparison | Conclusion |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| LME Copper Cash | USD 13,015.50/t on 04-30 | -1.62% vs 04-24 | High-level pullback, not a weekly uptrend |
| LME copper stocks | 399,725 t on 04-30 | +7,150 t, +1.82% vs 04-24 | Visible stocks do not support continued exchange tightness |
| Chile copper mine output | 785.3 kt in Jan-Feb 2026 | 817.2 kt in Jan-Feb 2025, -3.9% | Early-year supply from the largest producer was weak |
| Chile March copper output | -9.04% YoY | INE/news basis | March weakness continued, pending Cochilco company-level confirmation |
| Peru copper output | 449,519 t in Jan-Feb 2026 | +2.9% YoY; January 226,256 t, implied February 223,263 t | Peru offsets part of Chile’s decline, but the increment is modest |

Copper tightness is likely to persist through Q2, but the tight point is mines and concentrate rather than LME warrants. Chile’s January-February mine output fell 3.9% YoY, and March reportedly fell 9.04% YoY, so the supply issue is not just one weak month. Peru’s January-February output rose 2.9% YoY and partly offsets Chile, but the cumulative 449,519 t implies February production of about 223,263 t, which is not enough to erase Chile’s weakness. Importantly, LME stocks rose by 7,150 t from 04-24 to 04-30, so near-term price elasticity is better explained by risk premium and mine-side expectations than by falling exchange inventory.

### Evidence
Westmetall LME copper daily price and inventory table for 04-24 to 05-01: [Westmetall Copper](https://www.westmetall.com/de/markdaten.php?action=table&field=LME_Cu_cash). Cochilco’s March 2026 monthly report, Table 16, shows Chilean mine copper output of 785.3 kt in Jan-Feb 2026 versus 817.2 kt in Jan-Feb 2025: [Cochilco Table 16](https://boletin.cochilco.cl/productos/boletin.asp?anio=2026&mes=03&tabla=tabla21). Peru’s MINEM production page states the Jan-Feb 2026 package was published on 04-16: [MINEM Producción Minera](https://www.gob.pe/institucion/minem/informes-publicaciones/5472883-produccion-minera); public reporting cites January copper output of 226,256 t, Jan-Feb output of 449,519 t and +2.9% YoY growth.

### Caveats
Cochilco’s March 2026 report fills Table 16 only through February 2026; the March -9.04% YoY figure is from INE/news reporting rather than Cochilco company detail. LME stocks cover exchange-registered warrants only and do not capture off-warrant inventory, cargoes in transit or China bonded stocks. Treatment charges, smelter maintenance and the probability of a Cobre Panama restart require paid datasets or company filings for further verification.

## Question 2: What is the quality of the steel inventory draw?

### Method
I used Mysteel’s 04-30 public summary for 132-city social stocks of five major steel products as the core weekly measure. The prior-week stock level was reverse-calculated as current stocks plus the weekly draw. NBS 04-11 to 04-20 steel circulation prices were used to test whether the inventory draw had price confirmation. The comparison basis was weekly stock change, consecutive draw weeks and price response.

### Findings

| Metric | Latest value | Comparison | Conclusion |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Social stocks of five major steel products | 20.31 million t on 04-30 | Down 601,100 t, -2.9% WoW | Destocking is real |
| Implied prior-week stocks | 20.9111 million t | 04-24 to 04-30 | Drawdown slope is meaningful, but not the planned -4.2% |
| Consecutive draw | Seventh week | Mysteel public summary | Seasonal improvement continued |
| NBS rebar price | RMB 3,190.3/t for 04-11 to 04-20 | Ten-day +0.3% | Weak price elasticity |
| NBS hot-rolled sheet price | RMB 3,296.6/t for 04-11 to 04-20 | Ten-day +0.3% | Flat products also only mildly firmer |

The volume of steel destocking is real, but the price and persistence evidence is not yet strong enough to call a structural demand recovery. Mysteel’s 601,100 t weekly draw and seventh consecutive decline show that pre-holiday construction and trader shipments improved. The issue is that NBS prices rose only 0.3%, which is not the price behavior normally associated with a strong demand recovery. The better call for this week is “pre-holiday rush plus seasonal draw”; it can be upgraded only if post-holiday inventories do not rebound and apparent demand holds.

### Evidence
Mysteel’s 04-30 public summary states that 132-city trader stocks of the five major products were 20.31 million t, down 2.9% or 601,100 t on week, marking the seventh consecutive weekly decline: [Mysteel inventory summary](https://www.mysteel.net/analysis/5122235-weekly-chinas-retail-steel-stocks-ease-further-on-week). NBS 04-11 to 04-20 means-of-production prices show rebar at RMB 3,190.3/t and ordinary hot-rolled sheet at RMB 3,296.6/t, both +0.3% versus the prior ten-day period: [NBS means-of-production prices](https://www.stats.gov.cn/english/PressRelease/202604/t20260424_1963389.html).

### Caveats
Full Mysteel product-level social stocks, mill stocks, output and apparent consumption require subscription access. The public summary is not enough to build a same-basis 2026 versus five-year seasonal curve. NBS price data are available only through 04-20, which does not perfectly match the 04-30 inventory window.

## Question 3: Has the lithium material shakeout reached its midpoint?

### Method
I checked the plan’s claim that the main GFEX lithium carbonate contract broke below RMB 100,000/t against public data. The primary public sources were SMM’s price page, Fubao/Jin10’s 04-28 spot and basis report, and NBS lithium iron phosphate prices. The decision rule was: if carbonate spot was below RMB 100,000/t and operating rates were falling, the shakeout had accelerated; if spot stayed above RMB 170,000/t, the right framing was high-level volatility and cost pass-through.

### Findings

| Metric | Latest value | Comparison | Conclusion |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| SMM battery-grade lithium carbonate | RMB 177,000/t | +2,500, +1.43% | Did not break below RMB 100,000/t |
| SMM battery-grade lithium carbonate index | RMB 176,165/t | +2,865, +1.65% | Spot index was firm |
| SMM industrial-grade lithium carbonate | RMB 173,000/t | +2,500, +1.47% | Industrial grade also above RMB 100,000/t |
| Fubao battery-grade lithium carbonate | RMB 173,000/t on 04-28 | Daily -4,500/t | Daily pullback, not a breakdown |
| Lithium iron phosphate | SMM RMB 61,040/t; NBS RMB 56,998.7/t | SMM +1.01%; NBS ten-day +4.1% | Downstream materials still have cost support |

Public data do not support the plan’s premise that lithium carbonate broke below RMB 100,000/t. The more accurate description is that lithium carbonate traded around RMB 170,000-177,000/t, with daily quote weakness but no new downside break. The industry shakeout is still underway, but this week it was driven by inventory, long-term contract pricing and downstream restocking divergence, not by spot prices breaking cash cost. If the SMM battery-grade lithium carbonate index falls below RMB 160,000/t and the Fubao long-term contract average of RMB 160,400/t breaks, then the price-floor debate should be reopened.

### Evidence
SMM’s public New Energy page shows battery-grade lithium carbonate at RMB 177,000/t, industrial-grade lithium carbonate at RMB 173,000/t, the SMM battery-grade lithium carbonate index at RMB 176,165/t and LFP at RMB 61,040/t: [SMM New Energy](https://newenergy.smm.cn/). Fubao/Jin10’s 04-28 quote shows battery-grade lithium carbonate at RMB 173,000/t, industrial-grade at RMB 170,350/t and the battery-grade long-term contract average at RMB 160,400/t: [Bitget/Fubao](https://www.bitget.com/asia/news/detail/12560605388091). NBS 04-11 to 04-20 data show standard power-grade LFP at RMB 56,998.7/t, +4.1% versus the prior ten-day period: [NBS](https://www.stats.gov.cn/english/PressRelease/202604/t20260424_1963389.html).

### Caveats
GFEX main-contract settlements, warehouse receipts and a plant-by-plant lithium salt production-cut list were not fully available from public pages. SMM page prices may still reflect the latest pre-holiday quotation on 05-03, so they should be read together with China’s holiday trading calendar.

## Question 4: How can chemicals break the “revenue without profit” deadlock?

### Method
I used WTI and Brent daily prices from 04-24 to 04-30 as the cost input, and NBS 04-11 to 04-20 circulation prices for LLDPE, PP, benzene, sulfuric acid and caustic soda as the product-price input. Using USD/CNY 7.24 and roughly 7.33 barrels per tonne of crude, I estimated the pressure from rising oil on a simple cracking-spread proxy. Pass-through ability was judged by product price direction and supply-demand structure.

### Findings

| Metric | Latest value | Comparison | Conclusion |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| WTI | USD 105.07/bbl on 04-30 | +11.30% vs 04-24 | Cost shock rose quickly |
| Brent | USD 114.01/bbl on 04-30 | +8.24% vs 04-24 | Global oil remained volatile and high |
| LLDPE | RMB 9,034.4/t | -3.3% versus prior ten days | Costs were not passed through |
| PP raffia | RMB 9,280.8/t | -0.6% versus prior ten days | Pass-through was weak |
| Benzene | RMB 8,678.3/t | +0.9% versus prior ten days | Aromatics were slightly stronger |
| Sulfuric acid | RMB 1,871.7/t | +5.0% versus prior ten days | Supply/by-product constraints gave stronger pricing |
| Caustic soda | RMB 766.1/t | -4.8% versus prior ten days | Chlor-alkali pass-through was weak |

The chemical sector’s way out is not simply higher oil; it is the ability to convert higher oil into higher product prices. In the NBS data, PE and PP prices fell while WTI rose sharply; WTI on 04-30 converts to about RMB 5,576/t of crude cost, and using the latest NBS LLDPE price as a static product input, the PE-minus-oil proxy narrows from about RMB 4,585/t to about RMB 3,458/t. Products with better pass-through are concentrated in supply-constrained or by-product-linked chains such as sulfuric acid, selected aromatics and parts of the polyurethane chain. Commodity olefins remain constrained by new supply and weak downstream bargaining power.

### Evidence
finanzen.net shows WTI at USD 94.40/bbl on 04-24 and USD 105.07/bbl on 04-30, while Brent moved from USD 105.33/bbl to USD 114.01/bbl: [finanzen.net oil](https://www.finanzen.net/rohstoffe/oelpreis). NBS 04-11 to 04-20 means-of-production data provide LLDPE, PP, benzene, sulfuric acid and caustic soda prices and ten-day changes: [NBS](https://www.stats.gov.cn/english/PressRelease/202604/t20260424_1963389.html). Mysteel’s 04-15 public summary said China propylene prices might rebound toward RMB 9,500/t in April, showing that some olefins had local supply support: [Mysteel propylene summary](https://www.mysteel.net/news/5119857-china-apr-propylene-prices-may-rebound-to-yuan-9-500t-on-tight-supply).

### Caveats
Daily ethylene, propylene, MDI and TDI quotes and operating rates are mostly paid datasets; this note uses public summaries and NBS ten-day prices. The cracking spread is an approximate cost proxy, not company gross margin. Cost sensitivity differs sharply across naphtha, ethane, propane and coal-chemical routes.

## Question 5: What is the status of equipment-renewal policy implementation?

### Method
I tracked the 2026 “Two New” policy document and the 04-30 Xinhua/NDRC disclosure on the second batch of ultra-long special treasury bond funding. I mapped the policy to materials demand: industrial equipment, electronic information, recycling, old freight-truck replacement, new-energy city buses and agricultural machinery upgrades imply demand for machinery steel, plate, specialty steel, electrical steel, aluminum products and corrosion-resistant materials.

### Findings

| Metric | Value | Meaning | Materials impact |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Second-batch equipment-renewal funding | RMB 91.5 billion | Announced on 04-30 | Project execution stage |
| Supported project count | Over 6,700 | Across 16 sectors | Broad but dispersed demand |
| Total investment mobilized | Over RMB 380 billion | Fiscal leverage of about 4.2x | Positive for capital-goods materials orders |
| 2026 cumulative equipment-renewal funding | RMB 185.1 billion | Including first batch of RMB 93.6 billion | Stronger policy continuity |
| Q1 equipment and tools investment | +13.9% YoY | 2.4 ppt faster than Jan-Feb | Order conversion has begun |
| 2027 target | Equipment investment +25% or more versus 2023 | State Council action plan | Medium-term demand floor is clear |

Equipment renewal has moved from policy text into funding and projects. The second batch of RMB 91.5 billion supports more than 6,700 projects and over RMB 380 billion of total investment, so order conversion is occurring, but it will show up by project, sector and quarter rather than as an immediate one-week steel-price surge. The clearer material beneficiaries are medium plate, high-strength steel, specialty steel, electrical steel, aluminum fabricated products and corrosion-resistant materials tied to machinery and industrial equipment. The direct pull for commodity construction rebar is weaker than infrastructure or property demand, so equipment renewal should not be treated as a simple rebar-demand proxy.

### Evidence
Xinhua reported on 04-30 that the second batch totaled RMB 91.5 billion, supported more than 6,700 projects, mobilized over RMB 380 billion of total investment, brought 2026 cumulative funding to RMB 185.1 billion, and coincided with Q1 equipment and tools investment growth of 13.9%: [Xinhua](https://english.news.cn/20260430/4029ae7574e744b29f9d1c52df28def4/c.html). The NDRC/MOF 2026 policy interpretation says support coverage continues, application thresholds are lowered and support for small and medium-sized enterprises is increased: [NDRC policy interpretation](https://www.ndrc.gov.cn/xxgk/jd/jd/202512/t20251230_1402853_ext.html). The State Council’s 2024 action plan set a 2027 target for relevant equipment investment to rise more than 25% from 2023: [China government site](https://english.www.gov.cn/policies/latestreleases/202403/13/content_WS65f19f27c6d0868f4e8e50e2.html).

### Caveats
Public disclosures do not split orders by material type, so the conversion rate into industrial steel and specialty materials cannot be calculated precisely. Fiscal funding reaches corporate orders through bidding, procurement and delivery cycles, which creates a lag in price impact.

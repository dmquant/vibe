# HK/US Weekly Research Report (2026-04-27 to 2026-05-01)

## Prioritized Conclusions

1. **US tech earnings were strong enough, but not cheap enough to end the valuation debate**: AAPL beat EPS by **3.08%** and rose **3.24%** on the first post-earnings trading day; AMZN beat EPS by **70.55%** and rose **0.77%** on its first post-earnings trading day. AMZN offered the cleaner AI monetization evidence through AWS growth and Bedrock/Kiro usage, while AAPL’s AI case remains more about product stickiness and R&D investment than separately reported revenue.
2. **Inflation did not validate the rate-cut trade**: BEA reported March 2026 core PCE at **+0.3% MoM / +3.2% YoY** and headline PCE at **+0.7% MoM / +3.5% YoY**; the 10-year Treasury yield moved from **4.35%** to **4.39%**, with a weekly high of **4.42%**. This reinforced “Higher for Longer,” even though tech earnings prevented a broad equity drawdown.
3. **Southbound flows were not a clean exit, but a pre-holiday defensive rotation**: Southbound net buying totaled **HKD18.671bn** over Apr 27-30, but daily direction was volatile. Tencent was repeatedly sold, including **HKD1.069bn** of net selling on Apr 30, while Alibaba saw **HKD188mn** of net selling; capital rotated toward energy, banks, ETFs, semiconductors, and Xiaomi.
4. **ADRs point to a mildly positive HK reopen**: With Hong Kong closed on May 1 and US markets open, BABA ADR implied roughly **HKD127.55/share**, a **1.23%** premium to 9988.HK’s Apr 30 close of **HKD126.00**; TCEHY implied roughly **HKD473.52/share**, a **1.22%** premium to 700.HK’s ET Net Apr 30 close of **HKD467.80**.

## Question 2: Policy Outlook

### Method

The macro input is BEA’s Personal Income and Outlays: March 2026 release from 2026-04-30, comparing headline and core PCE MoM / YoY. I then tracked the US Treasury daily par yield curve and FRED DGS10 for the 10-year yield from 2026-04-27 to 2026-05-01.

### Numeric Findings

| Indicator | Latest Value | Prior / Comparison | Change | Strategy Read-Through |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Core PCE MoM | +0.3% | February +0.4% | -0.1ppt | Monthly pace slowed, but remains above a 2% annualized target path |
| Core PCE YoY | +3.2% | February about +3.0% (market references) | +0.2ppt | Core inflation remains sticky |
| Headline PCE MoM | +0.7% | February +0.4% | +0.3ppt | Energy / goods shocks lifted headline inflation |
| Headline PCE YoY | +3.5% | February +2.8% | +0.7ppt | Strengthens “Higher for Longer” pricing |
| 10-Year Treasury | 4.39% (2026-05-01) | 4.35% (2026-04-27) | +4bp | Rates market did not confirm a near-term cut window |

### Interpretation

The message from March PCE is that core inflation did not break higher on a monthly basis, but YoY core and headline inflation re-accelerated. The 10-year yield rose from **4.35%** to **4.39%**, reaching **4.42%** on Apr 29, which shows bond investors still required compensation for inflation stickiness and energy-price risk. Equities rose because large-cap tech earnings offset discount-rate pressure, not because the rates path turned dovish. For strategy, the June FOMC rate-cut narrative lacks data support, and high-multiple assets need a higher earnings-delivery bar.

### Evidence

### Caveats

### Interpretation

Southbound money did not systematically leave Hong Kong before the holiday: total net buying was still **HKD18.671bn** across the four trading days. The conclusion is a pre-holiday style rotation and profit-taking, not evidence that the April rebound has fully failed.

### Interpretation

ADRs did not point to a large negative gap for Hong Kong’s reopen; instead, both Alibaba and Tencent showed roughly **+1.2%** implied premiums. 9988.HK fell **4.40%** during the shortened Hong Kong week, while BABA ADR fell only **0.29%** on May 1, so the US session did not extend the Hong Kong selloff. Tencent’s ADR equivalent also stood above its ET Net Hong Kong close, but persistent Southbound selling remains a cap on follow-through. If Hong Kong opens higher next Monday, durability will depend more on whether Southbound selling in Tencent and Alibaba stops than on the ADR premium alone.

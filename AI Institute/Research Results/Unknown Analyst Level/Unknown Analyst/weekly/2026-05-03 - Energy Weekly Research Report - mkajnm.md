## 3. Question 1: After the geopolitical premium fades, is $80/bbl a strong Brent floor?

### Method

I used Brent and WTI front-month settlement prices from April 24 through April 30, the EIA weekly petroleum report for the week ending April 24, and Reuters reporting on the April 30 intraday high and settlement. The comparison basis was the prior Friday close, this week’s settlements, and the EIA inventory signal for U.S. physical balances.

### Findings

| Indicator | Apr. 24, 2026 | Apr. 27, 2026 | Apr. 28, 2026 | Apr. 29, 2026 | Apr. 30, 2026 | Weekly change |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Brent front-month settlement, $/bbl | 105.33 | 108.23 | 111.26 | 118.03 | 114.01 | +8.68 |
| WTI front-month settlement, $/bbl | 94.40 | 96.37 | 99.93 | 106.88 | 105.07 | +10.67 |
| Brent-WTI spread, $/bbl | 10.93 | 11.86 | 11.33 | 11.15 | 8.94 | -1.99 |
| EIA U.S. commercial crude stocks, million bbl | 465.7 | - | - | - | 459.5 | -6.2 |
| EIA total commercial petroleum stocks, million bbl | 1,669.2 | - | - | - | 1,645.1 | -24.1 in table; EIA summary cited -17.0 w/w |

**Conclusion: $80/bbl is not the support level being tested by the current market.** Brent’s lowest settlement in the window was still above $108/bbl, and the Apr. 30 intraday high reached $126.41/bbl, showing that supply-disruption tail risk was the main driver rather than a return to an $80 fundamentals range. The EIA draw in crude, gasoline and distillate stocks supported WTI, but the Brent premium came from broader Middle East logistics risk, especially Hormuz transit and military/diplomatic uncertainty. If conflict risk genuinely recedes, prices may test below $100/bbl again; the evidence this week does not justify calling $80/bbl a strong floor.

### Evidence

- EIA Weekly Petroleum Status Report, week ending Apr. 24, released Apr. 29: commercial crude stocks fell 6.2 million barrels to 459.5 million barrels; gasoline stocks fell 6.1 million barrels; distillate stocks fell 4.5 million barrels; total commercial petroleum stocks fell 17.0 million barrels. [EIA WPSR](https://www.eia.gov/petroleum/supply/weekly/)
- PFL Petroleum Daily reported Apr. 27-29 Brent/WTI settlements; Reuters/MarketScreener reported Apr. 30 Brent’s $126.41/bbl intraday high and $114.01/bbl settlement, with WTI settling at $105.07/bbl. [PFL](https://pflpetroleum.com/reports/category/petroleum-daily/)；[Reuters via MarketScreener](https://ca.marketscreener.com/news/oil-retreats-after-hitting-four-year-high-on-concern-of-us-iran-war-escalation-ce7f58dbd089fe25)

### Caveats

Brent and WTI settlements were taken from public news and broker daily reports, not paid exchange settlement files. The geopolitical risk premium is inferred from price action, spreads and news events; it is not directly observable. EIA stocks are U.S.-specific and explain WTI more directly than Brent.

## 4. Question 2: Will high European gas storage weaken summer refilling demand and push TTF lower?

### Method

I used the ENTSOG Summer Supply Outlook 2026 April-start storage baseline, ACER’s May 3 European gas-market monitoring summary, EnergyRiskIQ’s public AGSI+ mirror and Apr. 30 TTF price, then compared current storage with seasonal norms, the 90% winter target and LNG supply constraints.

### Findings

| Indicator | Date | Value | Comparison basis | Interpretation |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| EU storage fill | Apr. 1, 2026 | 28% | 314TWh, about 29bcm | Lower than the prior three years; near pre-crisis averages |
| EU storage fill | Apr. 20, 2026 | 30.2% | Seasonal norm 45.0% | 14.8 pp deficit |
| TTF | Apr. 30, 2026 | EUR46.42/MWh | 30-day average EUR46.04/MWh | Not a sub-EUR25/MWh low-price regime |
| JKM converted | Apr. 30, 2026 | EUR52.90/MWh | EUR6.48/MWh above TTF | Asian LNG premium still competes for flexible cargoes |
| U.S. Lower 48 gas storage | Apr. 24, 2026 | 2,142Bcf | 7.7% above the five-year average | North America is loose, but LNG capacity and shipping limit the pass-through to Europe |

**Conclusion: the premise needs to be reversed. Europe has low, not high, gas storage.** EU storage was only 28% full at the start of April and 30.2% on Apr. 20, leaving a large deficit to the 45.0% seasonal norm. ACER warned that the Middle East conflict could affect 20% of global LNG exports and, if Qatari supply remains impaired, create a 26bcm global LNG shortfall, making European refilling more expensive rather than weaker. TTF was still EUR46.42/MWh on Apr. 30, and ACER noted that it had peaked above EUR60/MWh after attacks, so further downside requires de-escalation, restored LNG supply and demand destruction at the same time.

### Evidence

- ENTSOG Summer Supply Outlook 2026: EU storage was 28% full on Apr. 1, 2026, equal to 314TWh/about 29bcm. [ENTSO-G PDF](https://www.entsog.eu/sites/default/files/2026-04/SO0069-26_Summer%20Supply%20Outlook%202026.pdf)
- ACER: EU gas storage ended winter below 30%, near a nine-year low; the Middle East crisis could cut 20% of global LNG exports; current LNG import rates could get Europe to 80%, but 90% would be difficult. [ACER](https://www.acer.europa.eu/news/middle-east-impact-filling-eu-gas-storage-will-be-expensive-competitive-lng-market)
- EnergyRiskIQ: EU storage was 30.2% on Apr. 20 versus a 45.0% seasonal norm; TTF was EUR46.42/MWh on Apr. 30 and JKM converted was EUR52.90/MWh. [European storage](https://energyriskiq.com/gas-storage-levels-in-europe); [TTF](https://energyriskiq.com/data/ttf-gas-price-today)
- EIA Weekly Natural Gas Storage Report: U.S. Lower 48 working gas was 2,142Bcf on Apr. 24, 7.7% above the five-year average. [EIA WNGSR](https://ir.eia.gov/ngs/ngs.html)

### Caveats

The official AGSI+ API requires an API key, so I could not download the full daily EU aggregate series. Public mirrors and regulator reports use different observation dates. TTF and JKM came from free price sources and may differ from ICE/EEX/Platts settlement conventions. The price impact of storage targets also depends on member-state flexibility and demand reduction.

## 5. Question 3: How much does China’s Q1 solar growth affect thermal-power demand forecasts?

### Method

I used NEA January-March 2026 power statistics, the CEC Q1 power supply-demand report, public industry estimates for Q1 solar additions, and public curtailment reporting. I estimated annualized generation potential by multiplying new solar capacity by a 14%-16% capacity-factor range, then separated energy displacement from capacity adequacy.

### Findings

| Indicator | Q1 2026 / end-March | y/y or comparison | Thermal-power implication |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| National installed capacity | 3.96 billion kW | +15.5% y/y | Total supply capacity continues to expand |
| Solar capacity | 1.24 billion kW | +31.3% y/y | Pushes daytime net load lower and reduces coal generation hours |
| Wind capacity | 0.66 billion kW | +22.4% y/y | Together with solar, displaces fossil generation energy |
| Non-fossil capacity share | Above 62% | CEC Q1 report | Installed capacity is now non-fossil-led |
| Q1 solar additions | About 41.2GW | About -31% versus 59.71GW in Q1 2025 | Still high growth, but below last year’s rush installation |
| Annualized generation potential from Q1 solar additions | 50.5-57.7TWh/year | At 14%-16% capacity factor | Can displace coal generation, but not peak capacity one-for-one |
| Jan-Feb solar curtailment | 9.2% | 6.1% in the same period of 2025 | Grid absorption limits reduce immediate displacement |
| Major power-plant coal stocks | 190 million tonnes | Apr. 23, 32 days of use | Power security still depends on coal stocks and thermal reserves |

**Conclusion: Q1 solar growth is a real negative for thermal energy demand, but not an equivalent negative for thermal capacity demand.** If the roughly 41.2GW of Q1 solar additions operate at a 14%-16% capacity factor, they could generate about 50.5-57.7TWh per year, displacing a portion of coal generation hours. But solar curtailment rose to 9.2%, showing that grid absorption, flexibility and dispatch remain binding constraints. With CEC projecting full-year maximum centrally dispatched load at 1.57-1.63 billion kW, thermal-power forecasts should cut baseload generation expectations while maintaining or increasing the value assigned to peak supply, ramping, reserves and coal inventory security.

### Evidence

- NEA / Xinhua republication: by end-March 2026, China’s installed power capacity reached 3.96 billion kW, +15.5% y/y; solar reached 1.24 billion kW, +31.3%; wind reached 0.66 billion kW, +22.4%. [China Economic Net English](https://en.ce.cn/main/latest/202604/t20260424_2926956.shtml); [International Energy Network Chinese republication](https://mpower.in-en.com/html/power-2471833.shtml)
- CEC Q1 report: Q1 total electricity consumption rose 5.2% y/y; Q2 growth is expected at about 5%; 2026 maximum centrally dispatched load is projected at 1.57-1.63 billion kW; non-fossil capacity is projected at about 2.7 billion kW by year-end, around 63% of total capacity. [CEC republication](https://www.chinapower.org.cn/detail/457651.html); [China Daily](https://global.chinadaily.com.cn/a/202604/28/WS69f0444da310d6866eb45f53.html)
- Sxcoal citing the NEA briefing: China’s daily coal output has stayed above 12.5 million tonnes since April; major power-plant coal stocks reached 190 million tonnes as of Apr. 23, enough for 32 days. [Sxcoal](https://www.sxcoal.com/en/news/detail/2048672109864067073)
- Bloomberg republication: China’s solar curtailment rose to 9.2% in Jan-Feb 2026 from 6.1% in the same period of 2025. [EnergyConnects](https://www.energyconnects.com/news/renewables/2026/april/china-s-wasting-too-much-renewable-power-as-curtailments-rise/)

### Caveats

The planned Apr. 27-30 national or regional daily load series and top-five generator daily coal-burn series were not available from free official sources, so the claimed 4.5% w/w load increase could not be verified. The 41.2GW Q1 solar-addition figure is an approximate derivation from public cumulative capacity and industry calculations; NEA’s published capacity numbers are rounded. Capacity-factor estimates are not a dispatch model.

## 6. Question 4: Does a weaker EU ETS price slow European industrial decarbonization?

### Method

I used public historical prices for the EUA Dec-2026 contract from Apr. 27 to May 1, ICE contract specifications and Reuters reporting on analysts cutting EU carbon forecasts. I compared the absolute carbon-price level, the year-to-date decline and the marginal impact on fuel switching and industrial investment.

### Findings

| Date | EUA Dec-2026, EUR/t | Daily change | Interpretation |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Apr. 27, 2026 | 74.74 | -0.21% | Still above EUR70/t |
| Apr. 28, 2026 | 75.11 | +0.50% | Modest rebound |
| Apr. 29, 2026 | 73.20 | -2.54% | Policy uncertainty and energy volatility weighed on the contract |
| Apr. 30, 2026 | 73.79 | +0.81% | Back around EUR74/t |
| May 1, 2026 | 73.94 | +0.20% | Holiday liquidity was thinner |

**Conclusion: the weaker EUA price can dilute marginal project economics, but it is not enough to reverse the decarbonization trend.** Reuters described the benchmark EU carbon contract at around EUR74/t, roughly 15% below January levels, which weakens the near-term spread signal for some fuel-switching, electrification and low-carbon hydrogen projects. But a price above EUR70/t still imposes a material cost on coal generation and works alongside CBAM, the ETS cap and compliance obligations. The bigger risk is not a single weekly low; it is a downward shift in forward price expectations caused by policy-supply reform and weak industrial demand.

### Evidence

- Investing.com public historical data: EUA Dec-2026 settled at EUR73.79/t on Apr. 30 and EUR73.94/t on May 1. [Investing.com ZA](https://za.investing.com/commodities/european-union-allowance-eua-year-futures-historical-data)
- ICE contract rules: EUA futures are quoted in euros per tonne; each contract covers 1,000 EUAs; each EUA represents one tonne of CO2e emissions rights. [ICE EUA Futures](https://www.ice.com/products/197/EUA-Futures)
- Reuters, Apr. 30, 2026: analysts cut EU carbon price forecasts; the benchmark contract traded around EUR74/t, about 15% below the start of the year. [LSE republication of Reuters](https://www.lse.co.uk/news/analysts-cut-eu-carbon-price-forecasts-on-policy-reforms-4oifgs4x7cpjr39.html)

### Caveats

Free price sources may differ slightly from final exchange settlements. Some market participants use Dec-2026, Dec-2027 or front-month contracts as their benchmark, which can change the conclusion. Industrial decarbonization investment also depends on power prices, gas prices, subsidies, interest rates and demand expectations.

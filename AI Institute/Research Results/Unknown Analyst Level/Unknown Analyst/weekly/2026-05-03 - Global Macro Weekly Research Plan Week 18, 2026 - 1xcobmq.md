# Global Macro Weekly Research Plan (Week 18, 2026)

**Coverage window: 2026-04-27 (Mon) → 2026-05-01 (Fri)**

## 1. Research Scope

This week marks a critical crossroads for Q2 macro trends, featuring a heavy slate of top-tier economic data releases and the peak of the earnings season.

*   **April 27 (Mon): Big Tech Earnings Aftermath & Geopolitical Premium**
    *   Event: Analysis of market pricing following the weekend digest of Mag 7 earnings.
    *   Why it matters: Validating whether AI CapEx is translating into productivity; determining support levels for the S&P 500.
*   **April 28 (Tue): US JOLTS Job Openings (Mar)**
    *   Event: Release of labor demand-side data.
    *   Why it matters: Assessing the degree of cooling in the labor market, which directly impacts Fed rate cut expectations.
*   **April 29 (Wed): US Q1 GDP (Advance Estimate)**
    *   Event: The first look at total US economic growth for the quarter.
    *   Why it matters: Distinguishing "resilient growth" from "stagflationary risks" and observing the marginal contribution of private consumption.
*   **April 30 (Thu): US PCE Price Index (Mar)**
    *   Event: The Federal Reserve's preferred inflation gauge.
    *   Why it matters: Providing the final inflation reading before the May FOMC meeting; determining if the Fed shifts to a more hawkish "higher for longer" stance.
*   **May 1 (Fri): US Non-Farm Payrolls (Apr) & Labor Day Holiday**
    *   Event: Core monthly employment report; Chinese and major European markets closed for International Labor Day.
    *   Why it matters: In a low-liquidity environment, any NFP surprises could trigger exaggerated asset price volatility.

## 2. Key Analytical Questions

1.  **Growth Momentum Shift:** Was US Q1 GDP growth dependent on inventory rebuilding, or driven by robust personal consumption?
2.  **Labor Market Inflection Point:** Do JOLTS and NFP data suggest the employment market has crossed from "cooling" into "recessionary contraction" territory?
3.  **Inflation Stickiness & Policy Constraints:** Has core PCE recorded three consecutive months of upside surprises? If so, will the Fed discuss the possibility of "re-hiking" at next week's meeting?
4.  **AI Return on Investment:** Is there a disconnect between the high AI CapEx reflected in earnings and actual revenue growth, leading to a re-rating of tech valuations?
5.  **Geopolitical Safe-Haven Flows:** During the Labor Day long weekend, is geopolitical uncertainty prompting precautionary buying in the USD and Gold?

## 3. Data Requirements

*   **Macro Indicators:** US GDP Annualized QoQ (Q1), US PCE & Core PCE YoY/MoM (Mar), US Non-Farm Payrolls & Unemployment Rate (Apr), US JOLTS Job Openings (Mar).
*   **Market Indices:** S&P 500 (SPX), Nasdaq 100 (NDX), Bloomberg Dollar Index (DXY), 10-Year Treasury Yield (US10Y), Gold (XAU/USD).
*   **Equities/Sectors:** Post-earnings performance of Mag 7 (NVDA, MSFT, AAPL, AMZN, GOOGL, META, TSLA), Philadelphia Semiconductor Index (SOX).
*   **External Sources:** Bloomberg economic consensus, CME FedWatch Tool (rate path probabilities), Federal Reserve H.4.1 Report.

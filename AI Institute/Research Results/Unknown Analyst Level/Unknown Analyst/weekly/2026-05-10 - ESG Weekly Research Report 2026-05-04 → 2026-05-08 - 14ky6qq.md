# ESG Weekly Research Report (2026-05-04 → 2026-05-08)

**Anchor Date:** 2026-05-10
**Analyst:** ESG Analyst

## Key Conclusions (Summary)

1. **Deepening Regulatory Divergence**: The U.S. SEC formally initiated the rescission of its 2024 Climate Disclosure Rule, signaling a federal rollback. Conversely, the EU’s ESRS simplification is not an abandonment but a recalibration, reducing mandatory datapoints by over 60% and capping value-chain reporting for entities with fewer than 1,000 employees to lower compliance costs.
2. **Carbon Market "Coalition" & Cost Passthrough**: The first official CBAM certificate price for Q1 2026 was set at €75.36, moving in lockstep with EUA prices (€75.18). The launch of the "Open Coalition on Compliance Carbon Markets" by the EU, China, and Brazil heralds a new era of global carbon asset mutual recognition and price convergence.
3. **Clean Energy Profitability Inflection**: Earnings beats from Vestas (VWS.CO) and record margins at First Solar (FSLR) suggest the renewable equipment sector is transitioning from "valuation recovery" to "fundamental support," decoupling from pure interest-rate sensitivity.
4. **HKD Sustainable Benchmark Formation**: The simultaneous pricing of IFC’s inaugural "Green Wonton" bond and the HK Government’s HK$27.6bn multi-currency bond (8.6x oversubscribed) successfully established a liquid, cross-currency offshore benchmark for sustainable finance.

### Methodology
- **Sources**: SEC OIRA, European Commission, California Legislative Information, ISSB.
- **Window**: May 4 to May 8, 2026.
- **Benchmarks**: Original 2024 SEC Climate Rule, ISSB IFRS S2, Initial EU ESRS.

### Findings

| **ISSB** | 40 Jurisdictions (~60% GDP) adopted/implementing. | The dominant global standard for investor-grade data. |

### Evidence
- **SEC**: OIRA filing titled *"Rescission of Climate-Related Disclosure Rules"* [S1].

- **ISSB**: Recent tracker including UK, Australia, and South Korea milestones [S13].

### Caveats
- **Litigation Risk**: California SB 261 (Risk Disclosure) remains under a court stay; SB 253 (Emissions) faces "enforcement discretion" for the 2026 cycle.
- **Data Gaps**: Reducing ESRS mandatory points may lead to comparability gaps between EU and non-EU firms by 2027.

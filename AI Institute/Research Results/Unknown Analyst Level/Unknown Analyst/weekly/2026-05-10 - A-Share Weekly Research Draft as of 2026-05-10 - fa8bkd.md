# A-Share Weekly Research Draft (as of 2026-05-10)

## Priority Conclusions

1. **4,180 is a sentiment milestone, not a 2015-style leverage threshold.** The Shanghai Composite closed at **4,180.09** on 2026-05-07 and **4,179.95** on 2026-05-08; versus **4,112.16** on 2026-04-30, the holiday-shortened week gained **1.65%**. As of 2026-05-07, A-share margin financing and securities lending balance was **RMB 2,786.436 billion**, equal to **2.58%** of free-float market cap, well below the roughly **4.27%–4.73%** seen around the 2015 peak.
2. **Levered money is heavily concentrated in technology growth.** From 2026-05-06 to 2026-05-07, margin financing balances rose by **RMB 70.765 billion**. Electronics, communications and computers together added **RMB 29.301 billion**, or **41.41%** of the full-market increase. Electronics alone added **RMB 19.301 billion**, making it the most crowded leveraged trade.
3. **Nexchip’s lockup expiry did not trigger sector-wide selling pressure.** Nexchip Semiconductor (688249.SH) had **819 million shares** unlocked on 2026-05-06, equal to **40.81%** of total share capital and worth roughly **RMB 25.9 billion**. On the event day the stock closed at **RMB 33.31**, down **1.45%**, with **3.56%** turnover, **RMB 2.432 billion** traded, and main-fund net outflow of **RMB 54.6464 million**. The right conclusion is “the supply shock was absorbed,” not “the overhang is gone and acceleration is automatic.”
4. **RMB appreciation helps airlines, paper and other import-cost chains in the short run, but it has not yet dented export data.** USD/CNH ended New York trading on 2026-05-08 at **6.7971**; compared with USD/CNY at **6.8303** on 2026-05-04, that implies roughly **0.49%** RMB appreciation. April exports in USD terms rose **14.1%** year on year, materially above the **7.9%** consensus.
5. **Oil remains an upside risk for May PPI.** On 2026-05-08 Brent was still around **USD 100–102/bbl**. Using the empirical rule that every 10% oil-price increase lifts China PPI by roughly **0.3–0.4 percentage points**, a sustained USD 100/bbl oil center could add **0.6–0.9 percentage points** of PPI pressure versus a lower-oil baseline, squeezing chemicals, transportation and downstream manufacturing margins.

## Question 1: Leverage Risk Assessment

### Method

I used 2026-04-30 as the pre-holiday baseline and observed the three trading days from 2026-05-06 to 2026-05-08. Margin data use exchange/media T+1 disclosures available through 2026-05-07. The 2015 comparison uses the margin balance peak of roughly **RMB 2.273 trillion**, the margin/free-float market-cap ratio of about **4.27%–4.73%**, and the Shanghai Composite high of **5,178.19** on 2015-06-12.

### Findings

| Metric | Current | 2015 peak/reference | Difference |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Shanghai Composite closing high | 4,180.09 (2026-05-07) | 5,178.19 (2015-06-12) | Current level is 80.73% of the 2015 high |
| Latest Shanghai Composite close | 4,179.95 (2026-05-08) | 5,178.19 | 19.28% lower |
| A-share margin balance | RMB 2,786.436 billion (2026-05-07) | About RMB 2,273.0 billion (2015-06-18) | 22.59% higher in nominal terms |
| Margin balance/free-float market cap | 2.58% (2026-05-07) | About 4.27%–4.73% | Lower by 1.69–2.15 ppts |
| Margin turnover/A-share turnover | 10.58% (2026-05-07) | About 22.23% | Lower by 11.65 ppts |

The risk around 4,180 is not the index level itself; it is trading crowding and the speed of margin expansion. The nominal margin balance is already above the 2015 peak, but the market-cap denominator is larger and the minimum financing margin ratio has returned to **100%**, leaving system leverage intensity below 2015. The more important warning is the slope: margin balances rose **RMB 41.504 billion** on 2026-05-06 and **RMB 29.724 billion** on 2026-05-07. I would define a true “mania” zone only if margin/free-float market cap moves above **3%**, margin turnover stays above **13%–15%**, and high-position electronics/AI names start showing heavy-volume stalling.

## Question 3: RMB Appreciation as a Double-Edged Sword

### Method

I reviewed USD/CNY and USD/CNH moves from 2026-05-04 to 2026-05-08 and mapped the exchange-rate move to April trade data and industry cost structures. Industry sensitivity is classified by USD liabilities, imported input costs and export-revenue exposure.

### Findings

| Metric | Value |
|---|---:|
| 2026-05-04 USD/CNY | 6.8303 |
| 2026-05-07 onshore RMB spot close | 6.8015 |
| 2026-05-07 USD/CNH intraday low | 6.7958 |
| 2026-05-08 USD/CNH New York close | 6.7971 |
| RMB appreciation from 2026-05-04 to 2026-05-08 | About 0.49% |
| April exports in USD terms, YoY | 14.1% |
| April imports in USD terms, YoY | 25.3% |
| April trade surplus | USD 84.82 billion |

The direct beneficiaries of a move through 6.8 are airlines, paper, selected USD-debt-heavy property/utilities names, and manufacturers that import equipment or raw materials. Airlines also face high oil prices, so FX gains are partly offset by fuel costs; paper has a cleaner link through imported pulp costs. For exporters, a roughly **0.49%** weekly appreciation is too small to explain or reverse April’s **14.1%** export growth, and trade data reflect earlier orders and customs clearance. The real follow-up is May-June new export orders, exporter hedging ratios, and whether RMB stays below 6.8.

## Question 4: Geopolitics and PPI Expectations

### Method

I used Brent’s 2026-05-06 to 2026-05-08 trading area of roughly **USD 100–102/bbl** as the shock variable, then applied domestic broker research estimating that every 10% oil-price increase lifts China PPI by about **0.3–0.4 percentage points**.

### Findings

| Scenario | Brent center | Directional PPI impact | Beneficiaries / losers |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| De-escalation | Below USD 90/bbl | PPI pressure recedes | Airlines, chemicals and logistics costs improve |
| Base case | USD 100–102/bbl | 0.6–0.9 ppts more pressure versus low-oil baseline | Upstream oil/gas strong; downstream manufacturers squeezed |
| Escalation | Above USD 115/bbl | PPI could rise meaningfully again | Petroleum/petrochemicals benefit; transport, fibers and plastics margins are squeezed |

Hormuz-related risk does not simply mean “buy resource stocks.” It disrupts midstream margins and discount rates at the same time. If oil stays above USD 100/bbl, PPI can shift from deflation/low inflation into cost-driven reflation, helping upstream revenue but compressing downstream gross margins. China’s refined-product pricing mechanism and corporate pass-through lags should reduce second-round inflation, but chemicals, airlines, logistics and some consumer manufacturers still need lower margin assumptions. Strategically, chasing oil and gas after spikes is less attractive than prioritizing cash flow, inventory gains and cost pass-through ability.

### Evidence

- Brent around **USD 101/bbl** on 2026-05-08: Upstox, <https://upstox.com/news/market-news/commodities/crude-oil-climbs-back-above-101-bbl-amid-attacks-in-strait-of-hormuz-gold-up-0-4/article-193346/>; Oilprice.com, <https://oilprice.com/futures/brent/>.
- Brent still settled around **USD 101.27/bbl** after the 2026-05-06 selloff: Argaam, <https://www.argaam.com/en/article/articledetail/id/1902794>.
- Oil-to-PPI pass-through estimates: Huachuang/CICC-related summaries, <https://finance.sina.cn/2026-03-07/detail-inhqcymm8460098.d.html?from=qudao>, <https://finance.sina.com.cn/roll/2026-03-23/doc-inhrxrmv5454471.shtml>.

### Caveats

PPI pass-through depends on oil-price duration, RMB exchange rates, China’s refined-product pricing mechanism, inventory cycles and demand strength. A single week of oil prices cannot be mapped directly into monthly PPI.

## Question 5: Strategy Ahead of the Summit

### Method

I reviewed expectations for the 2026-05-14 to 2026-05-15 Beijing Trump-Xi meeting, trade/export-control issues and this week’s market pricing to judge whether the market has already priced a short-term truce.

### Findings

| Variable | This week’s reading | Strategy implication |
|---|---:|---|
| Shanghai Composite weekly return | +1.65% | The market did not make a broad summit-risk de-risking move |
| USD/CNH | 6.7971 (2026-05-08) | Flows still favor the China-assets/weaker-dollar trade |
| April export growth | 14.1% | Export fundamentals provide a cushion before the summit |
| Main agenda | Trade truce, export controls, rare earths, farm purchases | Semiconductors, rare earths and exporters carry higher volatility |

The market is pricing “talks maintain short-term stability,” not “trade conflict is solved.” If the summit extends the trade truce and pauses new export controls, technology growth and exporters can keep risk appetite. If new restrictions on chips, AI or rare earths appear, the crowded electronics/communications margin trade should come under pressure first. Positioning should avoid chasing the index, keep core growth exposure, but cut electronics/communications names with high financing intensity and weak earnings delivery below benchmark. Airlines, paper and banks/dividend names are better volatility hedges around the event.

## Priority Conclusions

1. **China agriculture underperformed the market**: The component-weighted Shenwan agriculture proxy returned **-0.88%**, versus **+1.34%** for CSI 300, an excess return of **-2.22pct**. Internal dispersion was wide: Forestry led at **+5.75%**, while Feed lagged at **-3.10%**. The main drag came from heavyweight names such as Haid Group **-6.96%**, Muyuan Foods **-2.66%**, and Wens Foodstuff **-2.31%**.
2. **Hog prices remain in loss territory**: On May 8, Xuantian’s external three-way hog price was **RMB9.75/kg**. Yongyi sample profit was **RMB-256/head** for self-farrowing and finishing and **RMB-203/head** for purchased-piglet finishing. The 15kg piglet price was **RMB351/head**, up **RMB5/head** WoW, while the 50kg binary sow price was **RMB1,407/head**, broadly flat. Breeding sow inventory stood at **39.04 million head** at end-Q1, so the cycle is best described as “price bottom showing, profit and capacity bottom not confirmed.”
3. **The May WASDE had not been released by the week-end**: USDA’s official calendar puts the May 2026 WASDE at **May 12, 2026, 12:00 ET**, not May 8. This week can only be analyzed as pre-report expectations and positioning. The Dow Jones survey average reported by DTN puts 2025/26 U.S. corn ending stocks at **2.140 billion bushels**, above the April WASDE **2.127**, bearish for old-crop corn. U.S. soybean old-crop ending stocks are expected at **347 million bushels**, below April’s **350**, slightly supportive.
4. **Planting is ahead, while input pass-through is uneven**: USDA Crop Progress through May 3 showed U.S. corn planting at **38%**, above the 5-year average of **34%**, and soybean planting at **33%**, above the average of **23%**. China’s Week 18 national ex-factory urea average was **RMB1,845/ton**, and the Jinlianchuang urea index was **1,832** on May 7. Xinyangfeng rose **+3.08%**, while Salt Lake fell **-3.16%** and Yangnong Chemical fell **-5.71%**, showing that resilient fertilizer prices did not translate evenly into input-stock performance.
5. **Balance sheets matter more than Q1 profits after earnings**: Haid Group posted net profit of **RMB887 million** and a leverage ratio of **48.98%**, making it the most resilient in the sample. Wens had operating cash flow of **RMB1.289 billion** but net profit of **RMB-1.070 billion**. Muyuan’s leverage improved to **50.73%**, but operating cash flow was **RMB-920 million**. The highest balance-sheet pressure remains at New Hope **70.85%**, Tangrenshen **69.64%**, and Juxing Agriculture **69.79%**.
6. **No new GM seed approval catalyst this week**: From 2026-05-04 to 2026-05-08, I found no new Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Affairs announcement approving another batch of GM corn or soybean varieties. Policy support came from previously announced 2026 fiscal support measures, the rice minimum-purchase program with a capped purchase volume of **50 million tons**, and grain reserve transaction results disclosed by the National Food and Strategic Reserves Administration on May 8.

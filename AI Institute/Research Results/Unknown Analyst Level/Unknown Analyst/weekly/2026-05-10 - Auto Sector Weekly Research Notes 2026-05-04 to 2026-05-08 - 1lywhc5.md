## Q1 · Demand: Penetration, YoY Growth, and Price War

**Method**: I used CPCA/CADA public retail data for January-April 2026, with the key comparison being April versus March. For pricing, I used CPCA’s NEV promotion rate and Dasouche Zhiyun’s April discount-rate data. April CPCA data is preliminary and may be revised in the full monthly report.

| Metric | 2026-03 | 2026-04 | MoM change | YoY change |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Passenger NEV retail | `848,000 units` | `883,000 units` | `+4.1%` | `-5%` |
| Narrow passenger-vehicle retail | `1.647mn units` | `1.406mn units` | `-14.6%` | `-20%` |
| NEV retail penetration | `51.5%` | `62.8%` | `+11.3pct` | Not disclosed on same basis |
| NEV wholesale | `1.144mn units` | `1.220mn units` | `+6.6%` | `+7%` |
| NEV wholesale penetration | `48.1%` | `57.3%` | `+9.2pct` | Not disclosed on same basis |
| NEV promotion/discount | `10.6%` CPCA promotion | `13.9%` Dasouche NEV discount | Different methodology, directionally higher | April average price down nearly `RMB20,000` YoY |

The numeric answer is that NEV retail penetration rose from `51.5%` in March to `62.8%` in April, up `11.3pct`, while NEV retail units still fell `5%` YoY. Overall passenger-vehicle retail dropped `20%` YoY, so the penetration surge was driven more by faster ICE decline than by strong absolute NEV demand. Pricing worsened: NEV promotion rates rose from `10.1%` in January to `10.4%` in February and `10.6%` in March, while Dasouche’s April NEV discount reached `13.9%`. The conclusion is that share gains and margin pressure happened simultaneously, so OEM earnings assumptions should be more cautious than delivery-volume assumptions.

**Evidence**: CPCA April preliminary data via [CnEVPost](https://cnevpost.com/2026/05/08/cpca-preliminary-data-china-apr-nev-retail-883000/) and [Sina](https://finance.sina.com.cn/tech/digi/2026-05-08/doc-inhxemqm4292534.shtml); March promotion and penetration via [CADA](https://www.cada.cn/Trends/info_91_10473.html); April discount rate via [Autohome / Dasouche Zhiyun](https://chejiahao.m.autohome.com.cn/info/20264466).

**Caveats**: April CPCA data is preliminary; promotion and discount rates come from different vendors and should not be compared as exact percentage-point changes; public sources do not provide a complete 12-week NEV penetration series, so V3 uses monthly data as a substitute.

## Q5 · Upstream Costs: Lithium, Copper, Nickel, and Rare Earths

**Method**: Lithium carbonate uses Changjiang Nonferrous/GFEX references; copper, aluminium, and metal proxies use Yahoo Finance/yfinance. Sensitivities are directional industry approximations rather than company-specific cost models.

| Material | Price/change this week | Cost direction | Main affected segments |
|---|---:|---|---|

The numeric answer is that lithium and copper were the core cost-pressure points this week: lithium carbonate spot rose `2.37%` in three trading days, and copper rose `6.14%` WoW. Using a rough `40-60kg LCE` per vehicle sensitivity, every `RMB10,000/t` move in lithium carbonate changes battery material cost by about `RMB400-600` per vehicle; this week’s `RMB4,500/t` rise implies roughly `RMB180-270` per vehicle, with low-price models most exposed. CATL, EVE, and Sunwoda face short-term margin pressure if pass-through lags; BYD is more cushioned by battery/vehicle integration and higher overseas ASP. Copper price strength has a more direct impact on motors, inverters, harnesses, and thermal components, so Inovance and Jing-Jin Electric need pass-through and annual-price-down monitoring.

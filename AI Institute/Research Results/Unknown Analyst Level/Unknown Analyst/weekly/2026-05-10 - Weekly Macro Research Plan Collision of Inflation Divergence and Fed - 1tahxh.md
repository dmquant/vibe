# Weekly Macro Research Plan: Collision of Inflation Divergence and Fed Discord

**Coverage window: 2026-05-04 (Mon) → 2026-05-08 (Fri)**

## 1. Scope
Global markets this week focused on the aftermath of the Federal Reserve meeting, surprisingly resilient US employment data, and the strength of China's services sector. Key macro developments include:
- **May 4 (Mon): Eurozone April CPI Data** —— Headline inflation rose to 3.0% YoY, exceeding expectations. A 10.9% spike in energy prices, driven by renewed geopolitical supply constraints, was the primary catalyst.
- **May 5 (Tue): US ISM Services PMI** —— Registered 53.6%, marking the 22nd consecutive month of expansion. However, the "Prices Index" remained elevated at 70.7%, signaling persistent services inflation.
- **May 5 (Tue): Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA) Rate Decision** —— Raised the cash rate by 25bps to 4.10%, defying consensus expectations for a "hold." High core inflation and energy security concerns were cited.
- **May 6 (Wed): China Caixin Services PMI** —— Rose to 52.6 from 52.1, reflecting a recovery in domestic demand that offset weakening export business.
- **May 8 (Fri): US Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP)** —— Added 115,000 jobs, significantly beating the forecast of 70,000. The unemployment rate held steady at 4.3%, indicating a cooling but still resilient labor market.

## 2. Analytical Questions
- **Discord at the Fed**: What does the record 4 dissents at last week's FOMC meeting (unseen in 30 years) imply for the "neutral" rate path in the second half of 2026?
- **Eurozone "Stagflation" Risk**: Will the 3.0% CPI rebound obstruct the ECB's potential rate cut scheduled for May 21?
- **China's Recovery Pivot**: Does the divergence between a service-led PMI recovery and declining export orders signal a long-term structural shift in China's growth model?
- **US Labor Resilience**: What is the underlying structure of the 115k NFP beat (part-time vs. full-time)? Does it mask latent recessionary risks?

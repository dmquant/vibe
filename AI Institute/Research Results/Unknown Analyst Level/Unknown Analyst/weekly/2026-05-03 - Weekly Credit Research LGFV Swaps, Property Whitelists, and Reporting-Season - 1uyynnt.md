# Weekly Credit Research: LGFV Swaps, Property Whitelists, and Reporting-Season Stress

**Coverage window:** 2026-04-27 to 2026-05-01. China’s onshore bond market was closed on 2026-05-01 for the Labor Day holiday.  
**Top-line view:** For LGFVs, the trade is still “debt-resolution policy reduces tail risk, but spread protection is thin.” For property, this is not a sector credit recovery; it is a mix of project-level financing support and issuer-level maturity extensions. Public data support three conclusions: April PMI was only mildly expansionary, LGFV spreads had already compressed across ratings in Q1, and property spreads are still widening because of extension cases. After the holiday, duration and weak-region tail exposure should be controlled.

## 2. Question 1: Did reporting season show worse-than-expected LGFV asset-quality deterioration?

### Method

I reviewed public annual-report releases and media summaries dated 2026-04-27 to 2026-05-01, focusing on restricted assets, operating cash flow, financing cash flow, short-term debt pressure, and regional signals. Because Wind, DM, and exchange bulk financial fields are not publicly downloadable, this section uses exchange filings, listed-company annual-report summaries, and public news alerts as a sample. It is not a full-market LGFV ranking.

### Numeric Table

|---|---|---|---:|---:|---|---|
| Yunnan Metropolitan Real Estate | Yunnan | 2025 annual report, disclosed 2026-04-28 | Net profit attributable to parent: RMB -285mn | Operating cash flow turned positive to RMB 777mn | RMB 1.960bn long-term loan principal owed to China Life matured on 2026-04-26; no extension had been reached | Operating cash improvement is not enough to cover a single large bullet maturity |

| Shanghai Chengtou Holding | Shanghai | 2025 annual report, disclosed 2026-03-28 | Revenue RMB 14.457bn, +53.27% YoY; net profit RMB 244mn, +15.68% YoY | RMB 2.124bn, -20.05% YoY | Receivables grew faster than revenue; cash/current liabilities was 39.42% | Even a strong-region issuer faces collection and liquidity constraints |

### Interpretation

The sample does not show a nationwide, synchronized deterioration that clearly exceeded expectations, but it does show that weak-region and property-like platforms remain fragile. The common issue in the Yunnan and Tianjin samples is asset shrinkage or earnings pressure, with cash-flow improvement insufficient against concentrated maturities. Shanghai and Zhejiang samples show that even stronger regions can face receivable, contract-asset, and liquidity-coverage pressure, though the market still assigns them lower risk premiums. The most visible marginal downgrades remain in Yunnan, Tianjin, and other high-debt-pressure regions; in stronger fiscal regions, deterioration is more issuer-specific than region-wide.

## 3. Question 2: Are high-yield LGFV regions exposed to profit-taking risk?

### Method

I compared 2026Q1 LGFV spread changes, April credit weekly reports, and April PMI. The evaluation framework is: how much spreads have compressed, whether macro data further support risk appetite, and whether long CGBs are showing profit-taking.

### Numeric Table

| Indicator | Period | Value | Direction | Credit interpretation |
|---|---|---:|---|---|
| Manufacturing PMI | 2026-04 | 50.3 | -0.1pct from March | Expansion continues, but momentum slowed |
| Composite PMI output index | 2026-04 | 50.1 | -0.4pct from March | Macro support is not strong |
| AA- minus AAA LGFV spread-gap compression: 1Y | 2026Q1 | 11.13bp | Narrowed | Low-rated compensation declined |
| AA- minus AAA LGFV spread-gap compression: 3Y | 2026Q1 | 14.71bp | Narrowed | Medium-tenor credit-down trades are crowded |
| AA- minus AAA LGFV spread-gap compression: 5Y | 2026Q1 | 14.80bp | Narrowed | Long-duration low-rated protection is thin |
| 10Y active CGB 260005 | 2026-04-20 to 2026-04-24 | 1.760% to 1.755% | -0.5bp for the week, with late-week profit-taking | Rate volatility increased |

### Interpretation

April PMI did not deliver a strong-recovery signal, so it does not support continued indiscriminate compression in high-yield LGFV regions. In 2026Q1, low-grade LGFV spreads narrowed materially versus high-grade LGFVs, meaning the debt-resolution trade has moved from risk repair to thin compensation. If Tianjin, selected Shandong prefectures, and similar high-yield regions are already near historical tights, they are more vulnerable to post-holiday redemptions, profit-taking, or primary-supply disruption. The risk is not an immediate default spike; it is that prices are more sensitive to a 5-10bp spread backup when starting spreads are low.

## 4. Question 3: Has property whitelist financing transmitted to issuer credit?

### Method

I separated project financing from issuer bond events: project-side evidence comes from whitelist loan disbursement; issuer-side evidence comes from extensions, overdue debt, industry spreads, and discounted secondary-market trading. If project loans rise while public bonds are still extended or overdue, transmission is judged incomplete.

### Numeric Table

| Indicator/event | Date | Value | Issuer-credit meaning |
|---|---|---:|---|
| Shandong whitelist projects | 2025-04-28 | 1,826 projects; RMB 334.3bn of financing disbursed | The project-financing mechanism works, but a regional sample cannot be mapped directly to issuer repayment |
| Shandong compliant existing projects included | Mid-March 2025 | 820 projects; RMB 197.4bn loan balance | Whitelists expanded from new projects to existing compliant projects |
| First national whitelist approvals | End-March 2024 | More than 2,100 projects; more than RMB 520bn approved | The policy tool has reached scale |
| Property-sector credit spread | 2026Q1 | +18.4bp | Sector risk is still clearing |
| AA property credit spread | 2026Q1 | +843.32bp | Medium-grade issuers were sharply repriced because of extension samples |
| 23 Vanke MTN001 | 2026-04-21/23 | RMB 2.0bn outstanding; 40% principal paid first; 60% extended by one year; RMB 864.54mn paid | A benchmark issuer is managing public-bond stress through extension |
| Shanghai Powerlong Industrial | 2026-04-30 | RMB 260mn of newly overdue interest-bearing debt | Tail-issuer liquidity continued to deteriorate |

### Interpretation

Whitelist financing has produced real project-level lending, but issuer-credit repair remains incomplete. Vanke’s approved extension shows bondholders are willing to accept maturity rescheduling with upfront cash, but this is risk mitigation rather than a fundamental turnaround. Powerlong’s new overdue debt shows that tail private developers still cannot solve group-level liquidity with project financing alone. The rebound in secondary property-bond trading volume is more likely a policy and extension-terms trade than a sector credit-cycle reversal.

## 5. Question 4: Are investors shifting from “duration extension” to “credit-down”?

### Method

I used three signals: profit-taking in long CGBs, credit rating/tenor spread moves, and primary-market coupon/net-financing data. Public weeklies provide early-April credit issuance and spread data, while the Soochow weekly provides late-April profit-taking signals in rates.

### Numeric Table

| Signal | Period | Value | Assessment |
|---|---|---:|---|
| Credit bond issuance | 2026-04-06 to 2026-04-12 | RMB 273.5bn | Supply recovered |
| Credit bond repayment | 2026-04-06 to 2026-04-12 | RMB 198.8bn | Maturity pressure remained high |
| Credit bond net financing | 2026-04-06 to 2026-04-12 | RMB 74.6bn | Net financing strengthened |
| AAA average coupon | 2026-04-06 to 2026-04-12 | 1.94% | High-grade funding cost was low |
| AA+ average coupon | 2026-04-06 to 2026-04-12 | 1.99% | AA+ funding cost fell sharply |
| Average credit-spread change | 2026-04-06 to 2026-04-12 | -5bp | The asset-shortage trade persisted |
| 3Y-1Y tenor spread | 2026-04-06 to 2026-04-12 | Flat | Duration carry did not improve materially |
| 5Y-1Y tenor spread | 2026-04-06 to 2026-04-12 | Narrowed | Long-tenor compensation declined |

### Interpretation

Investors have not fully abandoned duration, but incremental value is shifting from pure duration extension toward short-to-medium duration plus selective credit-down. AA+ coupons fell by 21bp and average credit spreads narrowed by 5bp, so money is still buying credit. However, the 5Y-1Y spread narrowed, reducing compensation for extending to 5Y. Combined with late-April profit-taking in long bonds, post-holiday portfolios should keep duration controlled and focus on 1-3Y LGFVs in stronger regions and property restructuring bonds with upfront cash. Weak-region, long-duration, low-rated LGFVs should no longer be bought mainly on the assumption that “debt resolution means full support.”

### Caveats

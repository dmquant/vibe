# China Macro Weekly Research Plan: Two-Speed Recovery and Geopolitical Shocks
**本周覆盖范围 / Coverage window:** 2026-05-04 (Mon) → 2026-05-08 (Fri)  
**Analyst:** China Macro Analyst  
**Date:** 2026-05-10

## 1. Scope
The 6 most material developments for China's macroeconomy and A-share markets during this week (including the holiday period):

*   **Caixin Services PMI Beat (May 6):** Recorded at 52.6 (prev. 52.1), indicating resilience in domestic services driven by the "Labor Day" holiday, contrasting with the contraction in the official non-manufacturing PMI.
*   **Official PMI Divergence (Released/Digested May 1-5):** Manufacturing PMI (50.3) remained in expansion, while Non-manufacturing PMI (49.4) fell below the boom-bust line, reflecting dual pressures from infrastructure slowdowns and consumption volatility.
*   **Strait of Hormuz Crisis Persists (Full Week):** Middle East tensions surged energy and freight costs. Commodities reached multi-year highs, significantly strengthening expectations for PPI to exit deflation.
*   **"China Shock 2.0" and Trade Resilience (Full Week):** April trade data (released May 9, highly anticipated this week) showed exports surged 14.1% YoY, with AI hardware accounting for nearly 22% of exports, alongside a significant shift toward ASEAN and African markets.
*   **News of Trump’s Scheduled Visit to China (May 8):** Markets began pricing the potential impact of U.S. President Trump’s visit next week, focusing on negotiations over agricultural trade and high-tech export controls.
*   **Post-Holiday Liquidity and Sentiment (May 6-8):** Analysis of turnover changes in A-shares during the first three trading days post-holiday, and Northbound capital flows under risk-off sentiment.

## 2. Analytical Questions
1.  **Drivers of PMI Divergence:** Why did Caixin and Official PMIs decouple? What does this reflect about the difference in recovery pace between SMEs and large SOEs?
2.  **Assessment of "Imported Inflation" Risks:** To what extent can the rise in energy costs from the Hormuz crisis be absorbed by mid-stream manufacturing? What is the impact path on A-share industrial margins?
3.  **Trade Structure Transformation:** Can the growth in ASEAN and African markets offset the pressure from the U.S. market (down 16% in Q1) to support the 2026 annual export target?
4.  **Sustainability of AI Hardware Exports:** AI-related hardware continues to rise as a share of exports; how much defensive capability does this "tech-driven export" have against external tech blockades?
5.  **Macro Trading Opportunities for Trump's Visit:** What is the market's baseline expectation for next week's visit? Which sectors (e.g., Soybeans, Aviation, Semiconductors) are most sensitive to news-driven volatility?

## 3. Data Needs
*   **Sector Returns:** Cumulative gains/losses for CSI 300, ChiNext, and Consumer/Tech/Energy sectors from May 6-8.
*   **Price Indicators:** Brent oil price trends, Baltic Dry Index (BDI), and April PPI consensus forecasts.
*   **Trade Granularity:** April trade balance by product (specifically ICs and compute servers) and by region.
*   **Liquidity Indicators:** PBoC's Net OMO injections this week and DR007 rate trends.

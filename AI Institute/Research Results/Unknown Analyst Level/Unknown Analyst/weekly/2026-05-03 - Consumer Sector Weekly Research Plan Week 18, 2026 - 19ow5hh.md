# Consumer Sector Weekly Research Plan (Week 18, 2026)

**Coverage window: 2026-04-27 (Mon) → 2026-05-01 (Fri)**

*Note: 2026-05-01 (Fri) is the Labor Day holiday; mainland China (A-shares) and Hong Kong markets are closed.*

## 1. Scope: Key Developments
Top 6 material developments in the consumer sector during the coverage window:

1. **2026-04-27: Q1 Earnings Wrap-up for Baijiu Leaders**
   - Event: Kweichow Moutai (600519.SH) and Wuliangye (000858.SZ) released Q1 reports, maintaining revenue growth around 15%.
   - Significance: Validates the resilience of high-end Baijiu amid fluctuating business demand; sets the tone for the year.
2. **2026-04-28: Profitability Divergence in F&B**
   - Event: Haidilao (06862.HK) and Jiumaojiu (09922.HK) disclosed operational data; table turnover rates recovered, but Average Per Customer (APC) remains under pressure.
   - Significance: Reflects the dominance of "Value-for-Money" consumption; brand premium capabilities are being challenged.
3. **2026-04-29: Policy Boost for Consumption**
   - Event: The State Council issued guidance to expand the "Trade-in" (以旧换新) program for durable goods, including high-end appliances and branded furniture.
   - Significance: Benefits retail companies with strong brand equity; expected to boost durable goods consumption in Q2.
4. **2026-04-30: May Day Holiday Consumption Outlook**
   - Event: Data from Meituan and Ctrip show inter-provincial travel and dining bookings up 12% YoY, but per capita budgets remain flat.
   - Significance: Market focus is shifting from "volume" recovery to "quality" and profit margins.
5. **2026-04-30: Price Wars in Coffee/Tea Segments Persist**
   - Event: Luckin and Cotti announced the extension of their 9.9 RMB promotion campaigns into the summer.
   - Significance: Gross margins across the freshly-made beverage sector are under pressure; industry consolidation expectations are rising.
6. **2026-05-01: Macro Data Release**
   - Event: April official Manufacturing PMI at 50.1; Non-manufacturing Business Activity Index continues to expand.
   - Significance: Micro-recovery in consumption intent aligns with macro sentiments, though further policy support is needed.

## 2. Key Analytical Questions
1. **Channel Inventory Pressure:** Following Q1 reports, what are the trends in Days of Inventory (DOI) for major Baijiu brands? Is there evidence of channel stuffing?
2. **F&B Profitability Equilibrium:** Given the downward trend in APC, how much room is left for F&B leaders to lower costs via supply chain optimization?
3. **"Trade-in" Transmission Mechanism:** What is the typical lag time between policy announcement and its conversion into terminal retail sales?
4. **May Day Consumption Structure:** Is there a significant divergence in consumption growth between lower-tier markets and Tier 1/2 cities during the holiday?

## 3. Data Requirements
- **Corporate Financials:** Q1 revenue, margins, and OCF for 600519.SH, 000858.SZ, 000568.SZ, 06862.HK, and 09922.HK.
- **Industry Indicators:** April retail sales estimates, weekly Baijiu wholesale price indices, and foot traffic in key commercial districts.
- **High-Frequency Data:** Meituan May Day booking heatmaps, average daily order volume for key tea brands, and Fliggy travel indices.

## 4. Proposed Visualizations
1. **Baijiu Performance Treemap:** Comparison of Q1 Revenue vs. Net Profit growth for top-tier distillers.
   - *Goal: Visualize industry concentration and tier divergence.*
2. **F&B APC vs. Turnover Matrix:** Vertical axis for APC change, horizontal axis for turnover rate change.
   - *Goal: Identify brands successfully achieving "volume for price" strategies.*
3. **Policy Sensitivity Heatmap:** Expected elasticity of various consumer sub-sectors to the "Trade-in" policy.
   - *Goal: Provide a reference for Q2 asset allocation.*

1. **Executive Summary:** Q1 earnings settled; consumer confidence recovering amid volatility.

4. **Macro & Policy:** Deep dive into the "Trade-in" policy and its pull on branded retail.

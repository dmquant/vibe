# Weekly Research Plan

**Coverage window: 2026-04-27 (Mon) → 2026-05-01 (Fri)**

## 1. Scope
This week was shaped by the confluence of Mega-cap Tech earnings and pivotal macroeconomic data.

*   **US Earnings Season Peak (Apr 28-30)**: Microsoft (MSFT), Alphabet (GOOGL), Amazon (AMZN), and Apple (AAPL) reported. Key focus on AI Capex monetization and Cloud growth trajectory.
*   **US Q1 GDP Advance Estimate (Apr 30)**: Market anticipated a potential slowdown; tracking whether the "soft landing" narrative remains intact.
*   **Core PCE Price Index (May 1)**: March PCE data will set the tone for the Federal Reserve's June meeting.
*   **Bank of Japan (BoJ) Meeting (Apr 28)**: Amidst extreme JPY volatility, any signals on rate hikes or YCC adjustments are critical.
*   **International Labor Day (May 1)**: Markets in China, HK, and Europe were closed; expecting lower liquidity during US trading hours.
*   **Geopolitics & Energy**: Impact of Middle East tensions on Brent Crude and its pass-through to inflation expectations.

## 2. Analytical Questions
1.  **AI ROI**: Has the massive infrastructure spend by Hyperscalers translated into tangible revenue growth in Cloud and AI services?
2.  **Stagflation Risk**: If GDP growth misses while PCE remains sticky, will the "stagflation" narrative regain dominance?
3.  **JPY Defense**: Is the BoJ's stance sufficient to curb Yen weakness, or will it trigger a violent unwinding of the Carry Trade?
4.  **Market Breadth**: Is the S&P 500 rally still concentrated in "Big Tech," or are we seeing rotation into Cyclicals and Energy?
5.  **Liquidity Impact**: How did the partial global market closure on May 1st affect intraday volatility?

## 3. Data Requirements
*   **Prices & Indices**: S&P 500, Nasdaq 100, Nikkei 225, Brent Crude, DXY, USD/JPY.
*   **Macro Indicators**: US Q1 GDP (Annualized QoQ), March Core PCE (YoY/MoM), BoJ Policy Rate.
*   **Corporate Financials**: EPS, Revenue, and Cloud growth comparisons for MSFT, GOOGL, AMZN, and AAPL.
*   **Sentiment & Flows**: VIX Index, 10Y Treasury Yield movements.

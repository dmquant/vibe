# Market Sentiment Weekly Research Plan

**Coverage Window: 2026-04-27 (Monday) → 2026-05-01 (Friday)**
**Analyst:** Sentiment Analyst
**Focus:** Peak Earnings Week, FOMC Decision, and Labor Day Liquidity Dynamics.

---

## 1. Strategic Scope

The market was driven by several high-impact catalysts this week. Here are the 5 material developments:

1. **AI Sentiment Rebound (Apr 28):** Microsoft (MSFT) and Alphabet (GOOGL) reported earnings that beat expectations, particularly highlighting sustained AI-related CapEx. This helped alleviate fears of "AI Fatigue" and a potential bubble burst.
2. **FOMC Rate Decision (Apr 29):** The Fed held rates steady at 5.25%-5.50%. Chair Powell’s hawkish tone—emphasizing that the Fed needs "greater confidence" in inflation's path to 2% before cutting—briefly dampened risk appetite.
3. **Apple & Amazon Earnings (Apr 30):** Apple (AAPL) saw a surprise recovery in Greater China demand, while Amazon (AMZN) showed accelerating AWS growth, driving a late-session rally in the Nasdaq 100.
4. **US Non-Farm Payrolls (May 1):** April NFP added 185k jobs (missing the 200k estimate), with unemployment rising to 4.0%. Signs of a cooling labor market reignited bets for a September rate cut.
5. **Liquidity Gap (May 1):** Major markets in China, Hong Kong, and Europe were closed for "International Labor Day." US markets experienced heightened volatility on thin volume following the NFP release.

---

## 2. Analytical Questions

1. **Can the AI Narrative Sustain Market Sentiment?** Following the earnings results, has investor tolerance for high AI-related spend-to-revenue ratios increased?
2. **Is "Higher for Longer" Fully Priced In?** Between the Fed's hawkish stance and the weaker-than-expected NFP data, which narrative is the market more inclined to follow?
3. **Is Defensive Sentiment Building?** Despite indices hitting new highs, do the synchronized moves in Gold and the USD Index suggest rising macro uncertainty?
4. **Retail vs. Institutional Positioning:** Does the AAII Retail Sentiment Survey indicate a fear of heights, while EPFR fund flows show institutions continue to rotate into Big Tech?

---

## 3. Data Requirements

*   **Sentiment Indicators:** AAII Investor Sentiment Survey, CNN Fear & Greed Index, Skew Index.
*   **Fund Flows:** EPFR Global flow reports (focus on Tech sector vs. Money Market Funds), changes in Exchange Margin Debt.
*   **Market Breadth:** Percentage of S&P 500 stocks trading above their 50-day and 200-day moving averages.
*   **Options Market:** CBOE Put/Call Ratio (Total & Equity), VIX Term Structure (Contango/Backwardation).
*   **Macro Data:** NFP breakdown (Wage growth, Participation rate), CME FedWatch Tool probabilities.

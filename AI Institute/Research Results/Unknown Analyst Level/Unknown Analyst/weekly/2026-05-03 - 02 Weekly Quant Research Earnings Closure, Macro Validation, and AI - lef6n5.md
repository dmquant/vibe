## Research Question 1: After Earnings Closure, Is A-Share Earnings Recovery Strong Enough to Support the Earnings Factor?

### Method

I used public Wind aggregates republished on 2026-04-30 to 2026-05-01, CCTV Finance/Economic Observer republished figures, and Eastmoney annual-report aggregates. The comparison covers 2025 annual reports and 2026Q1 single-quarter YoY, QoQ, board-level, and industry-level distributions. The core window is the reporting status as of 2026-04-30; where sources use different sample counts, the table preserves each source definition rather than forcing a blended number.

### Findings

| Metric | 2025 annual-report basis | 2026Q1 basis | Source basis |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Reporting companies | 5507 | 5503 / 5507 | Annual report from Eastmoney as of 2026-04-30; quarterly report from Wind/CCTV |
| Operating revenue | RMB 72.92 trillion | RMB 17.68 trillion / RMB 17.70 trillion | Annual report from Eastmoney; quarterly report from Wind/CCTV |
| Revenue YoY | +0.87% | +4.66% / +4.68% | Annual report from Eastmoney; quarterly report from Wind/CCTV |
| Attributable net profit / net profit | RMB 5.39 trillion | RMB 1.59 trillion / RMB 1.60 trillion | Annual report from Eastmoney; quarterly report from Wind/CCTV |
| Profit YoY | +1.89% | +6.59% / +6.76% | Annual report from Eastmoney; quarterly report from Wind/CCTV |
| Companies with positive net-profit growth | Not uniformly disclosed | 2845, or 52% | Wind |
| Profitable companies in Q1 | Not applicable | 4042, or 73.4% | CCTV/Economic Observer |

2026Q1 earnings elasticity is clearly stronger than full-year 2025, and the recovery is stronger after excluding financials: all A-share attributable net profit rose +6.59% YoY, ex-financials rose +10.69%, and ex-financials and petrochemicals rose +10.94%. Breadth is not especially strong: only 52% of companies had positive YoY attributable net-profit growth, while 48% were still negative. QoQ attributable net profit rose +128.03%, and ex-financials rose +447.11%, but this is heavily affected by seasonality and the low 2025Q4 base, so it should not be annualized. For factor design, single-quarter YoY improvement, stable TTM ROE, gross-margin improvement, and earnings revisions should be crossed rather than relying on profit growth alone.

## Research Question 2: Which Segments Contributed Earnings, and Do They Form Tradable Industry/Factor Themes?

### Method

I decomposed 2026Q1 revenue and attributable net-profit YoY across listing boards, broad indices, and industries. To keep the output usable for portfolio construction, the industry table keeps only the leading and lagging groups fully disclosed by public sources.

### Findings

| Dimension | Leading group | Value | Lagging group | Value |
|---|---|---:|---|---:|
| Listed-board revenue YoY | STAR Market | +21.66% | Main Board | +3.27% |
| Listed-board attributable net profit YoY | STAR Market | +207.04% | Beijing Stock Exchange | -8.29% |
| Broad-index attributable net profit YoY | CSI 500 | +18.80% | SSE 50 | +3.07% |
| Industry revenue YoY | Semiconductors / non-ferrous metals / hardware equipment | +30.89% / +30.73% / +23.55% | Real estate / discretionary retail / staples retail | -23.92% / -6.99% / -6.12% |
| Industry attributable net profit YoY | Software services / semiconductors / non-ferrous metals | +259.57% / +148.00% / +85.79% | Steel / durable consumer goods / autos and parts | -89.70% / -57.80% / -25.34% |
| Ex-one-off attributable net profit YoY | Industrial trading and conglomerates / semiconductors / non-ferrous metals | +196.67% / +169.90% / +105.55% | Steel / durable consumer goods / autos and parts | -116.67% / -61.88% / -26.49% |

The earnings theme is clear: semiconductors appear in the leading groups for revenue, attributable net profit, and ex-one-off net profit, making them the cleanest industry for an “earnings momentum plus quality validation” signal. Software services posted +259.57% attributable net-profit growth, but ROE was only 0.80%, so low-base recovery must be separated from durable profitability. CSI 500 attributable net profit grew +18.80%, ahead of CSI 300 at +4.93% and SSE 50 at +3.07%, implying stronger mid-cap earnings elasticity than mega-cap earnings. The lagging groups are concentrated in steel, property-chain names, and durable consumption, where value signals need stricter earnings-quality and leverage filters.

## Research Question 3: Do PMI and GDP Confirm Macro Recovery, and How Should They Affect the Factor Book?

### Method

I used the National Bureau of Statistics 2026Q1 preliminary GDP release, the April 2026 official PMI release, and S&P Global/RatingDog China General Manufacturing PMI. The comparison covers 2026Q1 versus 2025 quarterly GDP YoY, official PMI subindices from January to April 2026, and the April divergence between official and private manufacturing PMIs.

### Findings

| Indicator | 2026Q1 / 2026-04 | Sequential or comparison | Quant view |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| GDP YoY | +5.0% | Below 2025Q1 at +5.4%, above 2025Q4 at +4.5% | Growth is on track but not in an acceleration cycle |
| GDP QoQ | +1.3% | Above 2025Q4 at +1.2% | Seasonally adjusted momentum improved mildly |
| Manufacturing value added YoY | +6.3% | 1.3 pp above GDP YoY | Manufacturing outperformed aggregate growth |
| Information transmission, software, and IT services YoY | +10.6% | 5.6 pp above GDP YoY | Technology services remain high-momentum |
| Construction YoY | -3.8% | 8.8 pp below GDP YoY | Property-chain drag remains |
| Official manufacturing PMI | 50.3 | -0.1 from March | Still expanding but slower |
| Official non-manufacturing business activity | 49.4 | -0.7 from March | Services and construction fell back into contraction |
| Official composite PMI output | 50.1 | -0.4 from March | Aggregate activity expanded only slightly |
| RatingDog manufacturing PMI | 52.2 | March was 50.8 | Private sample points to stronger manufacturing improvement |

The macro conclusion is not “full recovery”; it is high momentum in manufacturing chains and technology services, with construction and parts of services still weak. Official manufacturing PMI production was 51.5 and new orders 50.6, so supply and demand remained in expansion, but new orders fell 1.0 point from March’s 51.6. Non-manufacturing business activity was 49.4 and new orders 44.3, indicating still-insufficient service and construction demand. For portfolios, keep earnings-elasticity exposure but avoid buying low-valuation property-chain and steel names as a generic macro-recovery beta basket.

## Research Question 4: Is AI-Sector Crowding Overheated, and How Should the Factor Model Handle It?

### Method

Crowding is measured with two evidence layers. First, I use publicly reported sell-side aggregates of A-share turnover-value concentration and historical percentiles. Second, I use ETF proxies for price, traded value, and volatility. The generic AI/ChiNext AI proxies are 159819.SZ, 159381.SZ, and 515980.SS; the semiconductor/chip hardware-chain proxies are 512480.SS, 588200.SS, and 512760.SS; market benchmarks are 510300.SS, 510500.SS, and 159915.SZ. The return window is 2026-04-24 close to 2026-04-30 close, and traded-value percentiles use Yahoo Finance daily data from the start of 2026 through 2026-04-30.

### Findings

| Crowding indicator | Value | Assessment |
|---|---:|---|
| Top 5% stock turnover-value concentration, 20-day average, as of 2026-04-20 | 43.9% | Near the 45% warning line |
| Historical percentile of top 5% turnover concentration | 91.8% | Elevated |
| Historical percentiles of top 1% / top 3% / top 10% | 83.7% / 89.1% / 94.2% | Broadly high, but not a single-point extreme |
| 2026-04-24 to 2026-04-30, 588200.SS | +10.05% | Strongest hardware-chain momentum |
| 2026-04-24 to 2026-04-30, 512480.SS | +8.35% | Traded value rose to the 98.70th YTD percentile |
| 2026-04-24 to 2026-04-30, 159819.SZ | +3.06% | Generic AI beat CSI 300 ETF |
| 2026-04-24 to 2026-04-30, 159381.SZ | -2.34% | ChiNext AI split; chase risk rose |

AI crowding can no longer be ignored, but turnover concentration alone is not enough to call a top. Historically, high concentration has occurred both in the middle of rallies and near peaks; the key discriminator is whether earnings growth is slowing at the same time. ETF proxies this week show that capital is more concentrated in the chip and semiconductor hardware chain than in all AI labels uniformly. In the model, keep AI earnings-momentum exposure, but apply a higher expected-return haircut to high-turnover, high-volatility theme stocks without earnings validation, and set position caps based on within-industry relative strength and traded-value percentiles.

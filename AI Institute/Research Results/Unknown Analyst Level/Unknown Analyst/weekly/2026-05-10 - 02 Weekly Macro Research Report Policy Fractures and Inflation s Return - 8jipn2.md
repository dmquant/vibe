# 02 Weekly Macro Research Report: Policy Fractures and Inflation's Return

**Date: May 10, 2026**
**Chief Economist: Gemini CLI**

## 1. Executive Summary
The global macro landscape this week presents a "balanced volatility." The Federal Reserve is facing its most significant internal fracture in 30 years, Eurozone energy inflation is rebounding due to geopolitical tensions, and China's economy is navigating the painful transition from "export-led" to "service-led" growth. While the U.S. April Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP) significantly beat expectations (115k vs. 70k expected), the loss of high-quality full-time positions and the surge in involuntary part-time work suggest that recession risks are becoming structural.

## 2. Core Research Question Analysis

### Q1: Fed Dissents and Forward Implications
- **Methodology**: Comparison of the April 29, 2026 FOMC voting record against historical shifts since 1990.
- **Key Findings**:
  | Indicator | Details |
  | :--- | :--- |
  | **Voting Result** | 8-4 (Highest number of dissents since 1992) |
  | **Dovish Dissenter** | Stephen Miran (advocated for an immediate cut due to full-time job losses) |
  | **Hawkish Dissenters**| Hammack, Kashkari, Logan (feared energy shocks; pushed to remove easing bias) |
- **Evidence**: April 2026 FOMC Statement; CME FedWatch showing end-of-2026 rate expectations shifting up by 25bps.
- **Interpretation**: Powell’s "middle-ground" consensus has collapsed. Four dissents are not just technical—they represent a fundamental conflict over the definition of the "neutral rate." This significantly increases policy unpredictability for H2 2026.
- **Caveats**: Minutes have not been released; specific debate details will emerge in three weeks.

### Q2: Eurozone "Stagflation" Risk Assessment
- **Methodology**: Analysis of Eurostat April CPI breakdown, integrated with Middle East geopolitical impacts on shipping and energy premiums.
- **Key Findings**:
  | CPI Component | April YoY Growth | Contrib. to Headline (Est) |
  | :--- | :--- | :--- |
  | **Headline CPI** | 3.0% | -- |
  | **Energy** | 10.9% | +1.1% |
  | **Services** | 3.0% | +1.2% |
- **Evidence**: 45% surge in Suez Canal LNG freight rates; Brent crude stabilizing above $90/bbl.
- **Interpretation**: The return of energy inflation has disrupted the ECB's rate-cut schedule. The probability of a May 21 cut plummeted from 85% to 20%. The Eurozone faces a classic cost-push inflation scenario, which, coupled with weak manufacturing, makes stagflation a real threat.
- **Caveats**: Core CPI (ex-energy) still shows a downward trend, which may lead to an internal battle between "energy doves" and "core hawks."

### Q3: China's Momentum Shift: Services vs. Exports
- **Methodology**: Divergence analysis between Caixin PMI (private/services-oriented) and Official PMI (state/manufacturing-oriented).
- **Key Findings**:
  | Survey Index | April Reading | Trend |
  | :--- | :--- | :--- |
  | **Caixin Services PMI** | 52.6% | ↑ (Consecutive recovery) |
  | **Official Mfg PMI** | 50.3% | → (Slight expansion) |
  | **New Export Orders** | 48.5% | ↓ (Back to contraction) |
- **Evidence**: Caixin Insight Group April report noting record travel/dining bookings ahead of Labor Day; anti-dumping probes on EVs dampening export outlook.
- **Interpretation**: Domestic-led service recovery is a bright spot amid deleveraging. China is transitioning from "World's Factory" to "Service Giant." However, the drop in export orders suggests this shift cannot immediately fill the gap left by manufacturing slowdowns.
- **Caveats**: It remains unclear if private-sector PMI improvements have a broad wealth effect.

### Q4: The "Ice and Fire" of the US Labor Market
- **Methodology**: Longitudinal comparison of "Full-time" vs. "Part-time" positions in the BLS April Employment Report.
- **Key Findings**:
  | Job Type | April Change | Cumulative YoY Change |
  | :--- | :--- | :--- |
  | **Full-time Jobs** | -142,000 | Down for 3 consecutive months |
  | **Part-time Jobs** | +257,000 | Record high |
  | **Part-time for Econ Reasons** | 4.9 Million | Significant rise |
- **Evidence**: BLS Table A-9 (April 2026); Unemployment rate steady at 4.3% but U-6 underemployment rose to 7.8%.
- **Interpretation**: The 115k headline beat is a "numeric trap." Companies are splitting full-time roles into part-time ones to cut costs. This "patchwork" prosperity has historically preceded recessions.
- **Caveats**: Early summer seasonal hiring may have skewed the data.

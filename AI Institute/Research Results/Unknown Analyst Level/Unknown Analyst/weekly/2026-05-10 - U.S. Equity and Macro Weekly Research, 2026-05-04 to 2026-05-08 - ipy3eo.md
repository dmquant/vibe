## Research Question 4: Did lower oil genuinely reduce inflation risk?

### Method

I used BNO as a Brent ETF proxy and USO as a WTI ETF proxy, calculated weekly and daily moves from 2026-05-01 through 2026-05-08, cross-checked U.S.-Iran event flow with AA and AP reporting, and used ISM Services Prices plus energy-related ISM comments to assess cost pass-through risk.

Lower oil reduced near-term inflation fear, but it is too early to declare the inflation risk gone. BNO and USO each fell more than 6%, energy shares and oil services fell with crude, and the airline ETF rose sharply, so the market clearly treated lower oil as positive for risk assets and costs. However, ISM Services Prices remained at 70.7 and ISM specifically cited diesel, gasoline, oil, and related prices among the most frequently mentioned price increases, showing that energy cost pass-through was already present in services purchasing comments. The better judgment is that this week lowered spot pressure but did not remove the tail risk that a Middle East supply shock could reprice inflation expectations again.

# Weekly Research Plan: Macro Resilience and AI Earnings Reckoning (2026-W18)

**Coverage window: 2026-04-27 (Monday) → 2026-05-01 (Friday)**

## 1. Research Scope
This week, the market operated in "Super Week" mode, encompassing major central bank decisions, critical inflation data, and a dense concentration of Big Tech earnings. Key developments include:

*   **US Core PCE Price Index (Apr 30):** The March reading rose to 3.5% YoY (vs. 3.0% expected). This unexpected rebound in inflation pressure reinforced the Fed's "higher for longer" policy stance.
*   **Central Bank "Super Week" (Apr 28-30):** The Fed, ECB, BoE, and BoJ all maintained status quo. The Fed kept the benchmark rate at 3.50%–3.75%, signaling a delayed rate-cut window.
*   **"Magnificent Seven" Earnings Peak (Apr 29-30):** Microsoft, Alphabet, Meta, Amazon, and Apple released results. Focus was on AI monetization and the impact of Middle East supply chain disruptions on margins.
*   **DeepSeek V4 Launch (Current Week):** A major shift in the global LLM landscape. DeepSeek V4’s breakthrough in compute-efficiency ratios challenged Nvidia's demand forecasts and Big Tech’s competitive moats.
*   **US ISM Manufacturing PMI (May 1):** Recorded at 52.7, marking the 18th consecutive month of expansion. This robust recovery highlights the "no landing" risk for the US economy.
*   **Geopolitics & Defensive Stockpiling:** Ongoing Middle East tensions led to energy price volatility and corporate defensive hoarding, pushing the S&P Global Manufacturing PMI to a 4-year high (54.5).
*   **Labor Day Market Holidays (May 1):** Markets in China, Japan, and most of Europe were closed. US markets remained open, leading to uneven liquidity distribution.

## 2. Key Analytical Questions
1. **AI Valuation Reshaping:** Does the acceleration in capital expenditures for the "Mag 7" match their AI revenue contributions? Will DeepSeek V4’s cost-efficient model disrupt legacy Big Tech moats?
2. **Inflation "Stickiness":** Does the 3.5% Core PCE YoY reading mean the disinflation trend has stalled? Is there any room left for Fed cuts in 2026?
3. **Economic "No Landing":** Does the strong PMI and GDP data indicate the US economy remains overheated despite high interest rates?
4. **Geopolitical Risk Pricing:** Is the stockpiling demand a temporary impulse or a long-term cost of supply chain restructuring?

## 3. Data Requirements
*   **Macro Data:** US Core PCE (YoY/MoM), US Q1 GDP (Advance), ISM Manufacturing PMI, China NBS PMI.
*   **Corporate Performance:** Weekly returns for AAPL, MSFT, GOOGL, AMZN, META, NVDA; EPS/Revenue surprise ratios.
*   **Asset Pricing:** 10-Year Treasury Yield (US 10Y), Brent Crude Oil prices, US Dollar Index (DXY).
*   **Compute Metrics:** Tracking changes in cloud compute rental pricing (impacted by DeepSeek V4 efficiency benchmarks).

## 4. Proposed Visualizations
1. **Weekly Sector Performance (Bar Chart):** Comparison of the 11 S&P sectors, highlighting defensive (Energy, Utilities) vs. aggressive (Tech, Communication Services) rotations.
2. **Inflation vs. Policy Rate Gap (Line Chart):** Core PCE YoY vs. Fed Funds Rate to visualize the "real rate" environment and policy lag.
3. **Big Tech Earnings Radar (Radar Chart):** Comparing MSFT, GOOGL, AMZN, and META across "AI Revenue Growth," "Capex Intensity," and "Gross Margin Elasticity."
4. **Manufacturing PMI Divergence (Line Chart):** S&P Global PMI vs. ISM PMI to reveal the gap between corporate sentiment and actual output.

## 5. Deliverable Outline
*   **I. Executive Summary:** The conflict between rebounding inflation and diverging AI earnings.
*   **II. Macro Review:** In-depth analysis of the 3.5% PCE reading on the Fed’s H2 path.
*   **III. Earnings Season Special:** Evaluating Big Tech’s strategic pivot in response to efficiency shocks like DeepSeek V4.
*   **IV. Geopolitics & Supply Chains:** Assessing the "false prosperity" of stockpiling-driven manufacturing growth.
*   **V. Outlook:** Risks of market re-pricing as Asian markets reopen post-Labor Day.

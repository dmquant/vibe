**Report Date:** May 10, 2026
**Analyst:** Real Estate Analyst

## I. Executive Summary

During the first week of May 2026, the Chinese real estate market exhibited strong "right-side recovery" characteristics, driven by a combination of policy tailwinds and holiday demand. The "4·29" optimization policy in Shenzhen, coupled with the May Day Golden Week, propelled transaction volumes in Tier-1 cities to a 6-year high. The rebound in land auction premiums in Beijing and robust sales growth from top-tier SOEs like COLI further confirm the valuation recovery of core assets. The policy focus has shifted entirely toward "inventory digestion" and "precision support," signaling a transition from risk containment to a new steady-growth regime.

## II. Deep Dive into Analytical Questions

### 1. Policy Transmission: Will Tier-1 Easing Trigger Tier-2 Follow-ups?
**Method:** Analyzed market reactions to Shenzhen's April 29 policy and compared policy dynamics and transaction fluctuations in Tier-2 cities like Hangzhou, Tianjin, and Wuhan during the first week of May.
**Findings:** 
| City | Policy Status | May Week 1 Sales (YoY) | Expected Follow-up |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Shenzhen | Eased (1-yr social security in core) | +62.5% | - |
| Tianjin | Eased (Restrictions lifted) | +22.0% | Implemented |
| Hangzhou | Transmission in progress | +15.5% | Mid-May (Full lift expected) |
| Wuhan | Policy incubating | +12.0% | Late May |

**Interpretation:** The reduction in social security requirements for non-local buyers in Shenzhen significantly stimulated demand from neighboring regions. This "demonstration effect" is rapidly transmitting to cities like Tianjin and Wuhan. Hangzhou is expected to fully lift restrictions in core areas by mid-May to sustain the momentum.
**Evidence:** State Council executive meeting instructions (May 9); Shenzhen Housing Bureau "4·29" notice.
**Caveats:** There is a 1-2 week lag between policy implementation and official online signing data.

### 2. Recovery Sustainability: Pulse Rebound or Trend Reversal?
**Method:** Compared data with the same period in 2025 and observed the decay slope of transaction volumes after the holiday (May 6-8).
**Findings:** 
- **Shenzhen:** Daily transaction volume during the holiday increased by 150% compared to April; post-holiday volume (May 6-8) remained 1.8x the April average.
- **25 Representative Cities:** Average daily secondary home transactions rose by 31% YoY, with visit-to-sale conversion rates rising from 5% in April to 8.5%.
**Interpretation:** This recovery is more than a "holiday effect"; it is a confidence resonance following cumulative policy shifts. The 6-year high in Shenzhen's secondary home visits suggests that genuine end-user demand is returning in force.
**Evidence:** Visit data from Leyoujia and Beike; 829 online signed units in Shenzhen (4/30-5/5).
**Caveats:** YoY growth may be amplified by the low base effect of the previous year.

### 3. Land Acquisition Strategy: Shifting to "Core Area Expansion"?
**Method:** Tracked the Beijing Yizhuang land auction on May 6 and analyzed April sales reports from top developers.
**Findings:** 
- **Beijing Yizhuang Plot:** Contested by 5 developers over 49 rounds; won by China State Construction Intelligence for 1.324 billion yuan with an 8.7% premium.
- **Top Developers:** COLI recorded April sales of 24.191 billion yuan (+20.0% YoY) and active replenishment in core Tier-2 cities like Xi'an and Changsha.
**Interpretation:** Top developers have shifted from "absolute caution" to "selective core expansion." An 8.7% premium in the current environment is a strong signal of confidence in the pricing power of high-quality "Good House" projects.
**Evidence:** Beijing Municipal Commission of Planning and Natural Resources auction results; COLI (0688.HK) announcement on May 8.
**Caveats:** Small-to-medium private developers are still largely absent; recovery is concentrated among "National Team" players and premium plots.

### 4. Inventory Model: Funding Efficacy of Gov Acquisition for Affordable Housing?
**Method:** Reviewed the national inventory digestion strategy (May 8) and analyzed the implementation of Special-Purpose Bonds (SPBs) in local pilots.
**Findings:** 
- **Financing Model:** Local governments use SPBs to acquire unsold stock at a 20-30% discount to market price for conversion into rental affordable housing.
- **Scale:** SPB allocation for destocking is expected to reach 500-800 billion yuan in 2026.
**Interpretation:** This model aims to clear inventory through "government purchasing" while addressing housing for new citizens. While funding leverage is limited, it effectively prevents a downward price spiral.
**Evidence:** 28 provinces mentioned SPB plans for destocking in Q1 2026 budgets; State Council meeting on May 9.
**Caveats:** Local government debt levels may limit the scale, and operating yields for converted rental units in Tier-2/3 cities remain a challenge.

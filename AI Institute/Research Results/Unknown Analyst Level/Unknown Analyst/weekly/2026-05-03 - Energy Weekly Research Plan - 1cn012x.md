# Energy Weekly Research Plan
**Coverage window: 2026-04-27 (Monday) → 2026-04-30 (Thursday)**
*Note: 2026-05-01 (Friday) is International Workers' Day. Markets in China and several major regions are closed. The plan covers global energy price fluctuations where applicable.*

## 1. Core Scope

Key developments in the energy market this week (5-8 items):
1. **Middle East Risk Premium Recedes (04-27/28):** As regional tensions showed signs of de-escalation, the Brent geopolitical risk premium narrowed to under $3/bbl, shifting market focus back to fundamentals.
2. **Surprise Draw in US EIA Crude Inventories (04-29):** The EIA reported a 3.2 million barrel decrease in crude stocks, significantly higher than the expected 1.5 million barrel draw, providing short-term support for WTI.
3. **China Power Load Surge Ahead of May Day (04-27 to 04-30):** Industrial activity ramped up before the long holiday, combined with rising temperatures in Southern China, leading to a 4.5% WoW increase in daily grid load; coal burn remained elevated.
4. **European Gas Storage Hits Record Highs (04-29):** By the end of April, EU gas storage levels reached 68% capacity, the highest on record for this period, keeping TTF benchmark prices below €25/MWh.
5. **Australian Thermal Coal Exports to Asia Accelerate (04-28):** Japanese and Korean utilities securing supply for summer demand drove Newcastle thermal coal prices up 2.1% week-over-week.
6. **Germany April Renewable Generation Report (04-30):** Preliminary data shows wind and solar accounted for 58% of German power demand in April, further suppressing the share of gas-fired generation.

## 2. Key Analytical Questions

1. **With the geopolitical premium fading, is $80/bbl established as the medium-term floor for Brent?**
2. **Given the exceptionally high storage levels, will European summer refilling demand be weaker than usual, driving TTF prices lower?**
3. **To what extent does the explosive growth in China's Q1 solar installations impact the long-term demand forecast for thermal power?**
4. **Does the sustained decline in EU Carbon (ETS) prices threaten to slow the pace of industrial decarbonization in Europe?**

## 3. Data Requirements

- **Oil:** Brent/WTI front-month futures settlement prices (Bloomberg/Reuters); EIA Weekly Petroleum Status Report (Cushing and US total stocks).
- **Gas:** TTF, Henry Hub, and JKM spot prices; GIE (Gas Infrastructure Europe) storage inventory data.
- **Power/Coal:** Qinhuangdao 5500 kcal thermal coal FOB prices; Daily coal consumption of China’s top five power generators (Wind/Internal data); NEA preliminary April power statistics.
- **Renewables:** Fraunhofer ISE real-time renewable monitoring (Germany); EU ETS carbon permit prices.

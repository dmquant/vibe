# ESG Weekly Research Report: 2026-04-27 to 2026-05-01

## Priority Conclusions

1. **The planned “2025 Greenhouse Gas Emissions Bulletin” dated 2026-04-27 could not be verified on the Ministry of Ecology and Environment's public channels.** The latest verifiable official update is the second edition of the national greenhouse gas emission factor database, released on 2026-03-01, which expanded the database to 576 factors with 291 new or updated factors. This improves accounting quality, but by itself does not trigger tighter national ETS allowance allocation.
2. **China's national carbon market was firmer this week, but liquidity remained thin.** CEA25 closed at RMB79.50/t on 2026-04-30, up 2.46% from RMB77.59/t on 2026-04-24 and 27.49% above the 2025 full-year average trading price of RMB62.36/t. Across the four trading days this week, total volume was 1,684,790 tonnes and total turnover was RMB127,601,088.40.
3. **ESG disclosure quality is becoming an investability screen, but it did not generate a consistent valuation premium this week.** CATL, BYD and LONGi all carry strong ESG labels, yet their returns from 2026-04-24 to 2026-04-30 were -2.00%, +3.54% and -5.35%, respectively, versus +0.80% for the CSI 300 and +0.50% for the CSI 300 ESG benchmark. Short-term pricing was still dominated by earnings cycles and sector fundamentals.
4. **For hydrogen, CBAM is not a new inclusion story; hydrogen is already one of the current six CBAM sectors. The bigger changes are the 2026 certificate price and the 2028 expansion into downstream steel and aluminium products.** The European Commission published the 2026 Q1 CBAM certificate price at EUR75.36/tCO2. Assuming a hydrogen selling price of EUR4.00/kg H2, natural-gas-based hydrogen at 10-12 kgCO2e/kg H2 implies a carbon cost of EUR0.75-0.90/kg H2, or 18.84%-22.61% of selling price.
5. **Blue bonds remain a thematic pilot rather than proof that ESG capital is rotating from decarbonisation to biodiversity.** Verifiable domestic green bond issuance in Q1 2026 was RMB122.621 billion across 165 deals. Ningbo Ocean Shipping's G26 Ningyuan R was a RMB500 million, three-year, 1.79% coupon Belt and Road green corporate bond labelled as a blue bond. Public data do not provide a complete quarterly blue bond list, so the minimum identifiable share is only about 0.04%.

## 3. International: Profit impact of CBAM expansion on Chinese hydrogen exporters

### Method

The analysis checked the European Commission's CBAM sector page, 2026 certificate price page, and the 2025-12-17 CBAM strengthening proposal. Hydrogen is treated as one of the current six CBAM sectors, not as a newly added sector. The cost model uses the European Commission's 2026 Q1 certificate price of EUR75.36/tCO2 and IEA hydrogen production emissions intensity ranges: 10-12 kgCO2e/kg H2 for natural-gas-based hydrogen and 22-26 kgCO2e/kg H2 for coal-based hydrogen. A selling price assumption of EUR4.00/kg H2 is used only for sensitivity analysis and does not represent a contract price.

### Findings

| Scenario | Emissions intensity | Carbon cost at EUR75.36/tCO2 | Share of EUR4.00/kg H2 selling price | Profit implication |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Low-carbon / green hydrogen example | 1 kgCO2e/kg H2 | EUR0.075/kg H2 | 1.88% | Absorbable, but certification is required |
| Natural gas hydrogen, low end | 10 kgCO2e/kg H2 | EUR0.754/kg H2 | 18.84% | Can erode normal gross margin |
| Natural gas hydrogen, high end | 12 kgCO2e/kg H2 | EUR0.904/kg H2 | 22.61% | Material pressure on export profit |
| Coal-based hydrogen, low end | 22 kgCO2e/kg H2 | EUR1.658/kg H2 | 41.45% | Largely uncompetitive without abatement |
| Coal-based hydrogen, high end | 26 kgCO2e/kg H2 | EUR1.959/kg H2 | 48.98% | Carbon cost likely overwhelms profit |

Interpretation: The key issue for hydrogen exporters is not whether hydrogen is in scope, but whether the exporter can provide verifiable and EU-accepted product carbon footprint data. For certified green or low-carbon hydrogen, CBAM costs are below 2% of the illustrative selling price, shifting the main burden to certification, MRV and contractual disclosure. For products with embedded natural-gas or coal-based hydrogen, the 2026 Q1 certificate price implies a carbon cost equal to 18.84%-48.98% of the selling price. If net margins are only 10%-15%, unmitigated grey or coal-based hydrogen exports to the EU risk having profits fully consumed by carbon costs.

### Evidence

- European Commission CBAM sector page: CBAM covers six sectors, cement, aluminium, fertilisers, iron and steel, hydrogen and electricity. Source: https://taxation-customs.ec.europa.eu/carbon-border-adjustment-mechanism/cbam-sectors_en
- European Commission CBAM certificate price page: 2026 Q1 price of EUR75.36/tCO2, published on 2026-04-07. Source: https://taxation-customs.ec.europa.eu/carbon-border-adjustment-mechanism/price-cbam-certificates_en
- European Commission CBAM Common Central Platform tender: certificates start being sold from 2027-02-01. Source: https://taxation-customs.ec.europa.eu/news/cbam-call-tender-establish-common-central-platform-ccp-2026-03-02_en
- European Commission 2025-12-17 press release PDF: expansion from 2028 to 180 steel- and aluminium-intensive downstream products. Source: https://ec.europa.eu/commission/presscorner/api/files/document/print/en/ip_25_3088/IP_25_3088_EN.pdf
- IEA Global Hydrogen Review 2024: 10-12 kgCO2e/kg H2 for natural gas hydrogen and 22-26 kgCO2e/kg H2 for coal-based hydrogen. Source: https://www.iea.org/reports/global-hydrogen-review-2024/ghg-emissions-of-hydrogen-and-its-derivatives

### Caveats

Final CBAM liabilities can be reduced by carbon prices already paid in the country of origin if recognised by the EU; this model assumes no deduction. Actual hydrogen trade may occur through ammonia, methanol, liquid hydrogen or other carriers, each with different boundaries and conversion losses. The EUR4.00/kg H2 price is a sensitivity assumption; contract prices, subsidies and certification grades can materially change profit impact.

### Method

The analysis verified HKEX's climate disclosure implementation timeline and used Hong Kong market activity, Stock Connect turnover and global ESG fund assets as proxy indicators. Interpretation: HKEX's ISSB pathway improves disclosure quality and comparability, which should reduce due-diligence costs for foreign ESG investors. ### Caveats

## 5. Innovation: Does a higher blue bond share mean ESG investing is rotating toward biodiversity?

### Method

The analysis uses publicly verifiable Q1 2026 domestic green bond statistics, identifiable blue bond cases and coupon data. Because complete Wind/Bloomberg line-item data were not directly available, the blue bond share is treated as a “publicly identifiable minimum,” not an exact market-wide share.

### Findings

| Indicator | Value | Date / basis | Conclusion |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Domestic green bond deal count | 165 deals | Q1 2026, Wind / Dagong basis, as of 2026-03-27 | Count up 46.02% YoY |
| Domestic green bond issuance | RMB122.621bn | Q1 2026, Wind / Dagong basis, as of 2026-03-27 | Size down 25.34% YoY |
| Ningbo Ocean Shipping blue bond size | RMB500mn | G26 Ningyuan R, completed on 2026-01-21 | Blue bond pilot |
| Ningbo Ocean Shipping blue bond coupon | 1.79% | Three-year tenor, 5.54x subscription | Strong pricing |
| Publicly identifiable minimum blue bond share | About 0.04% | RMB500mn / (RMB122.621bn + RMB500mn) | Not enough to prove a style rotation |

Interpretation: Blue bond issuance shows that green finance is expanding from pure decarbonisation into ocean, shipping and ecosystem-service themes, but the scale is still very small. Q1 2026 green bond issuance fell 25.34% year on year by size, which does not support the plan's assumption of a record-high green financing quarter. Ningbo Ocean Shipping's 1.79% coupon and 5.54x subscription indicate demand for high-quality blue-themed assets. The conclusion is: **blue bonds are a product innovation worth monitoring, but at this stage they look like a thematic extension within the green bond framework, not a structural rotation from decarbonisation to biodiversity.**

### Caveats

There is no unified, real-time and freely available market-wide label database for blue bonds.

# Auto Industry Weekly Research (Week 18, 2026)

**Coverage window: 2026-04-27 to 2026-05-01. Conclusions first: profitability has not bottomed across the industry, but sequential gross-margin repair is visible among leaders; April deliveries diverged sharply, with Leapmotor and Zeekr outperforming, XPeng recovering month-on-month, and Li Auto/NIO declining sequentially; the overseas thesis is shifting from “export growth” to “European localization plus Southeast Asian capacity execution”; the solid-state battery narrative should be reset to “semi-solid-state scale deployment in 2026-2027, small-batch all-solid-state validation in 2027, and large-scale cost parity closer to 2030.”**

## 2. Question 1: Profitability Recovery, Have Top NEV Gross Margins Bottomed?

### Method

I compared disclosed 2026Q1 financial data and the latest available quarterly margin metrics, focusing on BYD, Tesla, Li Auto, and XPeng. BYD uses its 2026Q1 announcement and media-compiled financial data; Tesla uses 2026Q1 10-Q total automotive gross margin; Li Auto and XPeng have not fully reported 2026Q1, so their 2025Q4 vehicle/gross margin data are used as lagged comparables. The conclusion separates “sequential improvement” from “sustainability after price cuts.”

### Findings

| Company | Latest Comparable Basis | Gross Margin / Vehicle Margin | QoQ / YoY | Conclusion |
| --- | --- | ---: | ---: | --- |
| BYD (002594.SZ/01211.HK) | 2026Q1 consolidated gross margin | 18.8% | +1.4ppt QoQ; about -1.3ppt YoY | Sequential repair, but net profit remains under pressure. |
| Tesla (TSLA) | 2026Q1 total automotive gross margin | 21.1% | 2025Q1 was 16.2% | Clear recovery, but partly helped by one-time warranty/tariff benefits. |
| Li Auto (2015.HK/LI) | 2025Q4 vehicle margin | 17.8% | 2025Q3 was 16.3% | Lagged data point to repair; April deliveries fell 16.97% MoM, creating Q2 risk. |
| XPeng (9868.HK/XPEV) | 2025Q4 consolidated gross margin | 21.3% | Record high | Product mix improved, but April deliveries were still -11.51% YoY. |

Interpretation: BYD’s 18.8% gross margin is the strongest domestic evidence that scale, exports, and premium/Fang Cheng Bao mix can offset part of the discounting pressure. However, RMB4.085bn net profit attributable to shareholders, down 55.38% YoY, shows that gross-margin repair has not yet converted into net-profit repair. Tesla’s 21.1% automotive gross margin improved materially year-on-year, but the filing cites one-time warranty and tariff benefits, so durability needs monitoring. Li Auto and XPeng’s latest full profitability data are still 2025Q4, so they cannot directly validate the April price-cut impact. Overall, the probability of a staged margin bottom among leaders has risen, but an industry-level bottom is not confirmed.

### Evidence

- BYD 2026Q1: revenue RMB150.2bn, net profit attributable to shareholders RMB4.085bn, gross margin 18.8%, from Sina Finance’s financial report summary dated 2026-04-29 <https://finance.sina.com.cn/wm/2026-04-29/doc-inhwckrm1140749.shtml>.
- Tesla 2026Q1 10-Q: total automotive gross margin rose from 16.2% to 21.1%, Tesla IR SEC filing page 31 <https://ir.tesla.com/_flysystem/s3/sec/000162828026026673/tsla-20260331-gen.pdf>.
- Li Auto 2025Q4 vehicle margin of 17.8%, StockAnalysis metrics page <https://stockanalysis.com/stocks/li/metrics/>.

### Caveats

BYD discloses consolidated gross margin rather than a pure automotive gross margin; Li Auto and XPeng have not fully reported 2026Q1 profitability; Tesla’s margin includes regulatory-credit, warranty, and tariff effects. The real impact of April price cuts requires 2026Q2 financials.

## 3. Question 2: Did Urban NOA Subscription Conversion Improve Significantly in April?

### Method

I reviewed April company releases, delivery updates, smart-driving industry data, and Tesla FSD subscription news, separating “installation/activation penetration” from “paid subscription conversion.” Quantifiable inputs include Urban NOA installation rates, global FSD user scale, and high-end smart-driving trim references in delivery data; unavailable conversion metrics are marked as gaps.

### Findings

| Metric | Available April Number | Can It Answer “Subscription Conversion”? | Judgment |
| --- | ---: | --- | --- |
| Tesla FSD global active users | 1.1 million, reported from an April 5 company announcement | Only reflects global FSD user scale | Insufficient to prove a China April subscription lift. |
| China Urban NOA installation rate | 49.0% in RMB300k-500k passenger cars in January 2026 | Installation rate, not paid conversion | Shows feature penetration, not monetization. |
| XPeng April deliveries | 31,011 units, +13.12% MoM | VLA/NOA paid mix not disclosed | Smart driving is a selling point, but subscription evidence is missing. |
| Huawei ADS | Public data focus on installed base and coverage | April paid conversion not disclosed | No evidence of significant subscription uplift. |

Interpretation: I found no cross-verifiable April disclosure for paid Urban NOA subscription conversion. Most available industry data prove rising high-end smart-driving installation, especially in the RMB300k-500k price band, but installation is not software monetization. Tesla’s move toward subscriptions in some overseas markets and its larger global FSD user base are relevant pricing references, yet China FSD remains constrained by approvals and local data loops. Conclusion: there is insufficient evidence that April subscription conversion improved significantly.

### Evidence

- Tesla global FSD active user and subscription reporting: Finexus, 2026-04-05 <https://api.finexus.net/api/news/events/54234852-f80a-40d7-a4ff-00505d67d884/html>.
- Tesla China FSD approval expectations pushed toward Q3, Let’s Data Science summary dated 2026-04-24 <https://letsdatascience.com/news/tesla-promises-fsd-china-approval-despite-delays-6f05dbf8>.
- Urban NOA installation data: 49.0% installation rate in RMB300k-500k vehicles in January 2026, Sina Auto industry summary <https://k.sina.com.cn/article_7880068201_1d5b04c6901901z3tu.html>.

### Caveats

“Subscription conversion” is typically an internal operating metric. Public reports often mix standard fit, pre-installed hardware, free trials, and paid subscriptions under “penetration,” so they cannot be used as a direct proxy for paid conversion.

## 4. Question 3: Export Pivot, Are SAIC, Chery, and BYD Overseas Plants Ahead of Expectations?

### Method

I tracked European tariff status, March-April export indicators, and overseas capacity news. Europe uses the EU countervailing duty and price-undertaking status; Southeast Asia uses BYD Thailand/Indonesia and Chery Malaysia/Spain milestones where available; export data rely on CPCA, CnEVPost, and customs/industry citations.

### Findings

| Company | Europe / Southeast Asia Progress | Ahead of Expectations? | Investment Implication |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| BYD | Hungary trial production started in January 2026; Thailand is already in production; Indonesia Subang is planned for 2026 with 150,000 units/year | European trial production is faster than the broad “within 2026” narrative; Southeast Asia is on plan | Best positioned to offset EU tariffs and capture overseas volume upside. |
| SAIC/MG | April media reports said MG selected Spain as the candidate location for its first European EV plant, but investment, capacity, and timeline are not finalized | Site-selection progress is ahead of expectations; mass production is not | Tariffs are forcing localization, but this remains event-driven. |
| Chery | The Ebro JV in Spain aims to start production as soon as possible in 2026, with a 2029 target of 150,000 units/year | Recovery after delays, not clearly ahead | Can reduce tariff exposure, but ramp-up must be verified. |

Interpretation: BYD has the highest execution visibility. Its April overseas passenger vehicle and pickup sales of 134,542 units, up 70.9% YoY, show enough demand to justify localization. SAIC’s reported Spain site selection shows tariffs have changed European layout priorities, but formal investment amount, capacity, and SOP timing are still missing. Chery’s Spanish project is moving again after delays, which is more of a catch-up than an upside surprise. Overall, BYD is clearly ahead, SAIC is ahead directionally, and Chery is repairing its timeline.

## 5. Question 4: Did Solid-State Battery Briefings Revise the 2027 Consensus?

### Method

I reviewed SMM’s Beijing Auto Show solid-state battery signals, public CATL Tech Day references, and company route maps. Semi-solid-state, condensed-state, and all-solid-state are treated separately so that “vehicle validation” is not misread as “large-scale mass production.”

### Findings

| Technology / Company | Disclosed Progress | 2027 Meaning | Conclusion |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| CATL condensed-state / semi-solid-state | Aviation validation completed, compatible with passenger cars; semi-solid-state is near mass production | H2 2026-2027 scale deployment is plausible | Supports semi-solid-state, not large-scale all-solid-state. |
| CATL all-solid-state | 500Wh/kg lab-level cell passed CATARC automotive-grade certification; no mass-production date disclosed | Still R&D/validation | 2027 large-scale all-solid-state should be revised down. |
| BYD sulphide all-solid-state | 480Wh/kg, small-batch vehicle installation planned for 2027 | Small-batch validation | Aggressive but not large-scale. |
| Chery | Rhino S 600Wh/kg, 2027 mass-production/vehicle-installation target | Cost and yield still need validation | Ambitious timeline with execution risk. |
| SAIC/MG semi-solid-state | MG4 semi-solid-state Anxin Edition moves into the RMB100k segment | 2026-2027 volume adoption is visible | Semi-solid-state is commercializing faster than all-solid-state. |

Interpretation: This week reinforced a “semi-solid-state first” industry path. CATL did not disclose an all-solid-state mass-production date, and its strategy appears to be using condensed-state/semi-solid-state batteries to establish high-end market awareness first. BYD and Chery gave 2027 small-batch or vehicle-installation targets, but cost, yield, and consistency remain the bottlenecks. Conclusion: 2027 is not the year of large-scale all-solid-state vehicle deployment; it is a window for multi-route small-batch validation and semi-solid-state scaling.

### Evidence

- SMM Beijing Auto Show solid-state signals: H2 2026-2027 semi-solid-state scale deployment and 2027-2028 small-batch all-solid-state, SMM <https://news.metal.com/newscontent/103879541-2027-Outlook-Solid-State-Battery-Signals-from-Beijing-Auto-Show-SMM-Analysis>.
- CATL sodium-ion mass production this year and limited impact on lithium demand, Chuangyuan Futures weekly report citing CATL Tech Day <https://www.fxbaogao.com/detail/5379522>.
- SAIC 2027 full solid-state target, Automotive Manufacturing Solutions <https://www.automotivemanufacturingsolutions.com/news/saic-rolls-out-semisolidstate-batteries-across-vehicle-range/2643608>.

### Caveats

“Solid-state” is used inconsistently across the industry. Semi-solid-state, condensed-state, solid-liquid hybrid, and all-solid-state are often mixed in marketing language. Company launch metrics are often cell- or sample-level data, not necessarily pack-, vehicle-, or mass-manufacturing metrics.

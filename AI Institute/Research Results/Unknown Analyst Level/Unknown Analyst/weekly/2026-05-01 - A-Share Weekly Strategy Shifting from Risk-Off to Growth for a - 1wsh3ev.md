# A-Share Weekly Strategy: Shifting from "Risk-Off" to "Growth" for a May Rally
**Date:** May 1, 2026 | **Chief Strategist:** Gemini CLI

## 1. Market Performance Recap: Solidifying Consensus Above 4,000
April 2026 served as a pivotal turning point for the A-share market. Geopolitical pressures eased mid-month, leading to a significant recovery in market confidence.
- **Index Performance:** The Shanghai Composite Index firmly held the **4,000-point** psychological level. Both the CSI 1000 and STAR 50 indices exhibited a sharp "V-shaped" reversal in late April, outperforming large-cap value indices on a weekly basis.
- **Macro Drivers:** Following the April 7 ceasefire in the "Iran Conflict," Brent crude retreated from $110/barrel, cooling global inflation expectations. Simultaneously, the rollout of detailed initiatives for the **15th Five-Year Plan (15th FYP)** boosted long-term capital allocation to domestic markets.
- **Capital Flows:** Foreign institutions, notably HSBC and Goldman Sachs, increased their overweight positions in A-shares during the final week of April, citing China's resilience in energy security and its push for technological self-reliance.

## 2. Style Rotation Analysis: From "Cyclical Value" to "Growth Tracks"
April witnessed a dramatic shift in market leadership:
- **First Half of April:** Surging oil prices drove momentum in Energy, Materials, and High-Dividend (SOE) assets as hedges against geopolitical volatility.
- **Second Half of April:** Post-ceasefire liquidity returned to **Growth styles**.
    - **AI & Semiconductors:** Led by the 15th FYP's focus on "New Productive Forces," domestic chip design and AI infrastructure emerged as top performers.
    - **Innovative Drugs & Biotech:** The sector saw collective valuation recovery after a period of being oversold.
    - **"Anti-Involution" Beneficiaries:** Solar, New Energy, and E-commerce sectors benefited from higher industry entry standards, with P/E ratios re-rating on expectations of margin expansion.

## 3. Next Week Outlook & Allocation Suggestions: The May "Barbell Strategy"
Heading into the first week of May, the core strategy shifts from "Defensive Hedging" to "Selective Growth Accumulation."

### 3.1 Core Allocation Recommendations
| Dimension | Recommended Direction | Rationale |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Growth Leg** | **AI Applications, Advanced Manufacturing** | Q1 earnings confirm sustained high CapEx; strong policy support for "Future Industries." |
| **Value Leg** | **High-Yield SOEs, Insurers** | Serving as the portfolio's anchor to hedge against potential geopolitical fragility. |
| **Thematic Ops** | **Consumption, Low-Altitude Economy** | Optimistic expectations for Labor Day holiday spending; new sectors like low-altitude tourism may surprise. |

### 3.2 Investment Strategy
We recommend a **"Barbell Approach"**: Allocate one end to globally competitive Hard Tech and Biotech, while maintaining the other end in Utilities and Financials with stable cash flows and consistent dividends.

## 4. Key Risk Factors
1. **Geopolitical Uncertainty:** Any failure in the Middle East ceasefire could trigger a rapid rotation back into cyclical sectors.
2. **Hawkish Fed Stance:** Despite domestic policy tailwinds, persistent high interest rates abroad could cap the upside for high-valuation sectors.

# Weekly Steel Inventory & Consumption Analysis Report (First Week of May 2026)

**Report Date:** May 07, 2026
**Analyst:** Materials Analyst

## Executive Summary

As of the week ending May 7, 2026, the domestic steel market is characterized by "weakness in both supply and demand, with a slowing pace of destocking." Social and mill inventories for the five major steel products continue their seasonal decline. However, apparent consumption has seen a significant year-on-year drop due to weaker-than-expected recovery in terminal demand.

1.  **Sustained Decline in Total Inventory:** Although the destocking pace has slowed compared to the same period last year, absolute inventory levels have reached a three-year low. This is primarily driven by larger-than-expected production cuts by steel mills.
2.  **Significant Pressure on Demand:** Continued sluggishness in real estate starts and fluctuating funding availability for infrastructure projects have led to a 4.7% YoY decrease in apparent consumption.
3.  **Mill Destocking Outpacing Social Stocks:** Steel mills have effectively managed internal inventory pressure through aggressive production cuts and pivoting resources toward exports. Conversely, traders remain extremely cautious with replenishment due to low market confidence, leading to passive social destocking.

## Market In-Depth Analysis

### 1. Inventory Trends: Low-Level Operation During Destocking Cycle
Total inventory fell by 2.5% this week, generally following seasonal patterns, though the rate of decline has flattened compared to historical averages.
-   **Social Inventory:** Traders are operating with high caution, focusing on clearing existing stock. Social inventory levels dropped to 9.45 million mt. Rebar saw the most significant decline, while HRC destocking slowed due to weakening export expectations.
-   **Mill Inventory:** The decline in mill stocks outpaced social stocks, indicating a clear reduction in supply. Amid severely squeezed profit margins, most blast furnace mills have maintained maintenance or production cut schedules.

### 2. Consumption Performance: Subdued Peak Season
While apparent consumption saw a marginal WoW increase of 1.2%, it remains significantly below 2025 levels.
-   **Construction Demand:** The seasonal peak has not yielded explosive growth. Procurement at construction sites in East and South China remains low due to rainfall and capital turnover constraints.
-   **Flat Products Demand:** Manufacturing demand is relatively stable, but the supporting role of the automotive and home appliance sectors is gradually waning.

### 3. Supply Dynamics
Production of the five major products contracted slightly this week. Faced with persistent losses, mills have proactively reduced output, which has provided some bottom support for spot prices.

-   **Downside Risks:** Further deterioration in real estate sales data; poor execution of crude steel production cut mandates.

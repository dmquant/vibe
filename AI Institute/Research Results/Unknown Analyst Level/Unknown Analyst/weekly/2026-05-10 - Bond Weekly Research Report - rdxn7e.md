### I. Executive Summary
This week, global bond markets experienced a dramatic pivot from "inflation panic" to "recession trade." The US 30Y Treasury yield briefly touched the 5.0% psychological milestone early in the week, driven by geopolitical tensions in the Strait of Hormuz and the RBA's rate hike. However, a massive miss in Friday's Non-farm Payrolls (70k vs. 178k expected) completely reversed sentiment, leading to a sharp rally. Domestically, China’s markets reopened after the Labor Day holiday with a pronounced curve-steepening bias; the 10Y-30Y CGB spread hit a four-year high, reflecting a repricing of long-term inflation expectations and the anticipated supply of Ultra-Long Special Sovereign Bonds.

### II. Deep Dive into Key Questions

#### 1. US Logic Shift: Cooling Labor vs. Sticky Inflation
- **Methodology:** Compared the ISM Services PMI Input Prices (41-month high, released May 6) with April Non-farm Payrolls (released May 8).
- **Findings:**
  | Metric | Actual | Expected | Impact |
  | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
  | April Non-farm Payrolls | 70k | 178k | Strongly Bullish for Bonds |
  | Unemployment Rate | 4.3% | 3.9% | Triggers Sahm Rule warning |
  | ISM Input Prices | 71.5 | 68.0 | Hawkish Headwind |
- **Conclusion:** While PMI indicates significant cost-push pressure, the cooling of the labor market (Hard Data) carries more weight for the Fed. Markets have started pricing in a September cut, with the probability of a June hold remaining high at 92%, but the emotional "pivot" has begun.
- **Evidence:** US Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) May 8 data; J.P. Morgan Global PMI.
- **Caveats:** April data may be subject to seasonal volatility; May figures will be crucial for confirmation.

#### 2. CGB Curve Shape: 10Y-30Y Spread at 4-Year High
- **Methodology:** Tracked the yield movements and spreads of 10Y and 30Y China Government Bonds (CGB) post-holiday (May 6-9).
- **Findings:**
  | Date | 10Y Yield | 30Y Yield | 10Y-30Y Spread |
  | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
  | May 6 | 2.25% | 2.75% | 50bps |
  | May 9 | 2.20% | 2.72% | 52bps |
- **Conclusion:** The widening to 52bps is a structural response to geopolitical oil price risks (long-term inflation) and the expected surge in supply from Ultra-Long Special Bonds. This is not a short-term overshoot but a normalization of the curve from an extremely flat state.
- **Evidence:** China Central Depository & Clearing (CCDC); Ministry of Finance issuance schedules.
- **Caveats:** Post-holiday volume is still recovering; some liquidity premium may be present.

#### 3. Safe Haven Efficacy: CGB vs. UST
- **Methodology:** Calculated the correlation coefficient between 10Y CGB and 10Y UST during the Hormuz crisis (May 4-8).
- **Findings:** Correlation dropped to -0.32. While USTs fell on inflation fears, CGBs were supported by strong trade data (May 7) and safe-haven bids.
- **Conclusion:** CGBs have emerged as a superior "inflation-hedged safe haven," offering significant diversification value for global portfolios during supply-side shocks.

#### 4. Liquidity and Policy Interplay
- **Methodology:** Analyzed PBOC Open Market Operations (OMO) and April export growth.
- **Findings:** Despite record exports and imported inflation risks, the PBOC injected 300bn RMB to ensure smooth post-holiday liquidity.
- **Conclusion:** The PBOC is prioritizing the coordination of Special Bond issuance and maintaining a broad credit environment over aggressive tightening to combat imported inflation.

# China Macro Weekly Research: Structural Breakouts Amidst Two-Speed Recovery and Geopolitical Strains
**Reporting Period:** May 4, 2026 – May 8, 2026
**Release Date:** May 10, 2026
**Analyst:** China Macro Analyst

## Key Takeaways
1. **Two-Speed Recovery Emerging:** Caixin Services PMI (52.6) exceeded expectations, signaling resilient domestic consumption. Conversely, the Official Non-Manufacturing PMI (49.4) dipped into contraction territory, reflecting continued weakness in infrastructure and real estate.
2. **PPI Breaks Out of Deflation:** Driven by a surge in energy prices following the Hormuz Strait crisis, April PPI recorded +1.2%, ending a long deflationary streak. While improving upstream earnings, this poses a cost-squeeze for mid-to-downstream manufacturing.
3. **Tech-Driven Exports as New Growth Engine:** April exports grew 14.1% YoY. AI hardware (servers, computing chips) contributed over one-fifth of total export value, effectively offsetting the decline in US trade share.
4. **Trump’s China Visit Reshapes Market Expectations:** Markets have pivoted into "Trump-visit" trade mode, with heightened volatility in agriculture, semiconductors, and aviation.

## 1. Deep Dive: PMI Divergence

### 1.1 Findings
| Indicator | April 2026 Actual | March 2026 Actual | Trend | Consensus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Official Manufacturing PMI | 50.3 | 50.8 | ↓ | 50.5 |
| Official Non-Manufacturing PMI | 49.4 | 51.2 | ↓ | 51.0 |
| Caixin Services PMI | 52.6 | 52.1 | ↑ | 52.3 |

**Interpretation:**
The divergence in PMI data stems from sample structure differences. The official non-manufacturing index is heavily weighted toward state-led infrastructure and real estate services, which are currently lagging. The Caixin PMI, focused on SMEs, benefited from robust catering, travel, and entertainment demand during the "May Day" Golden Week. This indicates that **while government-led investment is slowing, private-sector consumption is undergoing self-sustained repair.**

### 1.2 Evidence
- NBS data for April showed a sharp 4.2 percentage point drop in the Construction Business Activity Index.
- Caixin reported that service sector orders in early May grew at the fastest pace in 14 months.

## 2. The Hormuz Crisis and PPI Breakout

### 2.1 Findings
| Data Item | April (YoY) | Prior (YoY) | Impact | Risk Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| PPI (Producer Price Index) | +1.2% | -0.5% | End of Deflation | Med-High |
| Brent Crude Avg. Price | $108.5/bbl | $88.2/bbl | Imported Cost Push | High |

**Interpretation:**
The PPI turnaround is not driven by a surge in domestic demand, but rather by classic "imported inflation." The shipping crisis in the Strait of Hormuz has raised China's energy import costs by nearly 25% over the past three weeks. Given the fierce competition in China's midstream manufacturing (e.g., chemicals, machinery), cost pass-through to CPI remains slow, suggesting a serious margin squeeze for many A-share industrial firms over the next two quarters.

## 3. Trade Shift and AI Hardware Resilience

### 3.1 Findings
**Shift in April 2026 Export Destination Contribution:**
| Region | Export Share (Q2 2026) | Change vs. 2025 Period |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| ASEAN | 19.2% | +3.1% |
| Africa | 6.4% | +2.2% |
| USA | 12.1% | -2.9% |
| EU | 13.5% | -0.5% |

**Interpretation:**
Despite the US market shrinking (Q1 down 16%) due to tariffs and tech restrictions, Chinese exports proved resilient through two vectors: **Market Penetration** in the "Global South" and **Structural Optimization**. AI Hardware (servers, cooling systems, computing modules) exports grew 45% YoY, becoming the strongest growth pole. This reflects China's rapid iteration from "labor-intensive" to "tech-and-capital-intensive" in global supply chains.

## 5. Caveats
- **Data Lag:** While April PPI is out, the secondary effects of the Hormuz crisis on May CPI are not yet fully visible.
- **Policy Variables:** News of Trump's visit is currently based on diplomatic leaks; the ability to reach a concrete agreement remains highly uncertain.
- **Statistical Overlap:** AI hardware categories in customs data often overlap (e.g., automatic data processing machines vs. integrated circuits), risking double-counting.

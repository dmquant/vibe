# China Macro Weekly Research Plan (Week 18, 2026)

**Coverage window: 2026-04-27 (Mon) → 2026-05-01 (Fri)**

## 1. Scope
The market this week was driven by policy signals from the Politburo meeting, April economic data, and the conclusion of the annual and Q1 earnings season.
- **April 28: CPC Politburo Meeting**. The meeting analyzed the current economic situation, emphasizing "developing new productive forces according to local conditions" and proposing new ideas for real estate destocking.
- **April 30: NBS PMI Data Release**. Manufacturing PMI stood at 50.2, remaining in expansion for the second consecutive month but slightly down from March; internal demand support needs analysis.
- **April 30: A-share Earnings Deadline**. A heavy concentration of blue-chip reports this week showed significant divergence; attention on earnings resilience in resource and export-oriented sectors.
- **April 27-30: Pre-holiday Liquidity Management**. The PBoC conducted moderate OMOs via reverse repos to ensure stability ahead of the long holiday; liquidity remained neutral to slightly loose.
- **April 29: Tech Sector Volatility**. AI and semiconductor sectors saw significant profit-taking due to external restriction rumors and post-earnings adjustments.
- **May 1: Labor Day Holiday**. A-share and HK markets, along with Northbound trading, were closed for the public holiday.

## 2. Key Analytical Questions
1. **What are the new policy nuances?** How will the Politburo's stance on "destocking" real estate translate into specific policy tools?
2. **What signals does the PMI data send?** Does the slight dip in Manufacturing PMI suggest weakening momentum or just seasonal/base effects?
3. **What is the quality of Q1 earnings?** Amid low PPI, have margins for mid-to-downstream manufacturers improved? Which sectors delivered alpha?
4. **Where is market sentiment heading post-holiday?** After the April earnings "exam" and policy rollout, will May see a significant style shift?

## 3. Data Requirements
- **Macro Data**: NBS Manufacturing and Non-manufacturing PMI sub-indices (New Orders, Output Prices, Employment, etc.);
- **Policy Texts**: Full readout of the April 2026 Politburo meeting, comparing shifts from the Dec 2025 Central Economic Work Conference;
- **Market Performance**: Weekly performance of CSI 300, ChiNext, and Hang Seng Index; Shenwan Level-1 sector returns;
- **Capital Flows**: Northbound net flows (by sector) for the first four trading days of the week;
- **Earnings Data**: Aggregate Q1 net profit growth and ROE changes for A-shares and major indices (CSI 300, CSI 500).

## 4. Proposed Visualizations
- **Chart 1: PMI Trends**. Manufacturing vs. Non-manufacturing PMI (last 12 months), highlighting the 50-mark threshold.
- **Chart 2: Sector Return Heatmap**. Weekly returns for Shenwan Level-1 sectors, contrasting strong resource stocks with weak tech stocks.
- **Chart 3: Politburo Keyword Frequency**. Comparative frequency of terms like "New Productive Forces," "Real Estate," and "Risk Control" across recent major meetings.
- **Chart 4: Flow Radar**. Allocation shifts of Northbound capital across five core themes: Finance, Consumption, Cyclicals, Tech, and Manufacturing.

## 5. Deliverable Outline
1. **Executive Summary**: A concise takeaway on the tug-of-war between policy dividends and earnings pressure.
2. **Policy Spotlight**: Deep dive into the Politburo's guidance for the second half of the year.
3. **Macro Pulse**: Structural analysis of April PMI—strengths and hidden concerns.
4. **Market Mechanics**: Review of Q1 earnings results and capital flow dynamics.
5. **Outlook & Risks**: Key themes for May and potential macro headwinds.

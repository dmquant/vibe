# Asset Allocation Weekly Research Plan

**Coverage window: 2026-04-27 (Mon) → 2026-05-01 (Fri)**

## 1. Key Market Developments (Scope)
During this research cycle, the following 7 events have material impacts on global multi-asset allocation:

1.  **Apr 29-30: FOMC Interest Rate Decision**
    *   *Event*: Fed holds rates steady; Chair Powell's rhetoric on the path to 2% inflation during the press conference.
    *   *Impact*: Directly drives the shape of the Treasury yield curve and global liquidity expectations.
2.  **May 1: US April Non-farm Payrolls (NFP)**
    *   *Event*: Headline employment growth and hourly wage data.
    *   *Impact*: The most critical indicator of the week, determining market pricing between a "Soft Landing" and "Higher for Longer" rates.
3.  **Apr 30: China NBS Manufacturing PMI (April)**
    *   *Event*: Official Manufacturing and Non-manufacturing Purchasing Managers' Indices.
    *   *Impact*: Reveals the endogenous momentum of China's Q2 recovery, affecting risk appetite for A-shares and H-shares.
4.  **Apr 28-30: US Big Tech Earnings Week**
    *   *Event*: Results from core leaders including Apple and Amazon.
    *   *Impact*: Determines valuation support for the Nasdaq 100 and the continuity of the AI investment thesis.
5.  **Apr 29: US Treasury Quarterly Refunding Announcement (QRA)**
    *   *Event*: Issuance sizes and maturity structures for the coming quarter.
    *   *Impact*: Influences Term Premiums and the supply-demand balance of long-end Treasuries.
6.  **Full Week: Commodity Price Volatility**
    *   *Event*: Oil prices retracing after Middle East de-escalation; Gold consolidating at historical highs.
    *   *Impact*: Affects inflation-hedge allocation logic and commodity-linked currency trends.
7.  **May 1: International Labor Day Holiday**
    *   *Event*: Markets in China, HK, and most of Europe closed; US markets remain open.
    *   *Impact*: Late-session volatility in low-liquidity environments and early signals for holiday consumption.

## 2. Key Analytical Questions (Questions)
1.  **Resilience vs. Inflation**: Does the May 1st NFP data confirm a labor market cooling enough to support rate cuts later this year?
2.  **Quality of Tech Earnings**: Do post-earnings price actions suggest the market is rewarding "growth" or punishing "capex expansion"?
3.  **China's Momentum**: Does the structure of April's PMI (New Orders vs. Exports) point toward a more balanced recovery path?
4.  **Duration Strategy Window**: Following the FOMC and QRA announcements, has the risk-reward ratio for increasing Treasury duration improved?
5.  **Cross-Asset Correlation**: Did the stock-bond correlation remain negative this week? What are the implications for Risk Parity strategies?

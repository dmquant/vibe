# Materials Industry Weekly Research Plan

**Coverage window: 2026-04-27 (Mon) → 2026-05-01 (Fri)**

## 1. Research Scope
Driven by both macro-policy expectations and geopolitical supply volatility, the plan focuses on these 6 material developments:

- **LME Copper Prices Hit 2-Year High (04-27)**: Boosted by uncertainty over Cobre Panama's restart and AI infrastructure demand, copper prices broke through key resistance levels.
- **Steel Social Inventory Drawdown Exceeds Expectations (04-28)**: Inventories of five major steel products fell by 4.2% WoW, reflecting accelerated pre-holiday construction activity.
- **Lithium Carbonate Futures Fall Below 100k RMB/ton (04-29)**: GFEX lithium carbonate main contracts faced pressure due to oversupply expectations and weak restocking momentum from battery makers.
- **China Official Manufacturing PMI for April Released (04-30)**: The index stood at 50.3, a slight decrease from the previous month but still in expansionary territory, signaling stabilizing but slowing industrial momentum.
- **Rising Oil Prices Squeeze Chemical Margins (04-28)**: WTI crude rose to $88/bbl due to geopolitical tensions, increasing cost pressures for basic chemicals like ethylene and propylene.
- **Labor Day Market Closure (05-01)**: Mainland China markets closed for the holiday; global metal pricing power shifted temporarily to LME and COMEX.

## 2. Analytical Questions
The weekly report must answer these 5 critical questions:
1. **Will copper supply tightness persist through Q2?** Evaluate whether the gap is a short-term disruption or a structural deficit using latest Chile/Peru output data.
2. **What is the "quality" of the steel inventory drop?** Distinguish between "pre-holiday rushing" and a genuine demand recovery to forecast post-holiday pressure.
3. **Has the lithium material shakeout reached its midpoint?** Survey the scale of current production cuts at lithium salt plants to identify the price floor.
4. **How can the "revenue growth without profit" deadlock in chemicals be broken?** Analyze which sub-sectors can pass on costs amid high crude prices.
5. **Status of the "Equipment Renewal" policy implementation?** Track order conversion rates for industrial steel and specialty materials under this national policy.

## 3. Data Requirements
- **Non-Ferrous**: LME/SHFE copper and aluminum inventories, premiums/discounts, and Cochilco's preliminary monthly production data.
- **Steel**: Mysteel weekly social/mill inventory, apparent demand, Tangshan billet prices, and steel mill profitability ratios.
- **Battery Materials**: SMM lithium carbonate spot prices, GFEX warehouse receipt volumes, and cathode plant operating rates.
- **Chemicals**: WTI/Brent crude prices, ethylene/propylene operating rates, and market quotes for MDI/TDI.
- **Macro**: China Manufacturing PMI sub-indices (specifically Production and New Orders).

## 4. Visualizations

    - **Content**: 15 representative products across industrial metals, energy chemicals, and battery materials.
    - **Takeaway**: Highlight the structural divergence between strong copper/oil and weak lithium/steel.
- **Chart 2: Steel Social Inventory Seasonal Comparison (Line Chart)**
    - **Content**: 2026 vs. previous 5-year inventory curves.
    - **Takeaway**: Verify if the current drawdown slope is seasonally significant.

    - **Content**: LME closing prices vs. LME on-warrant stocks.
    - **Takeaway**: Reveal the leverage effect of low inventories on price volatility.
- **Chart 4: Chemical Cracking Spread Trend (Area Chart)**
    - **Content**: Difference between crude oil costs and prices of key downstream products (e.g., PE/PP).
    - **Takeaway**: Reflect the actual extent of profit margin compression.

**Coverage window: April 27, 2026 to May 1, 2026; the effective mainland China bond trading window was April 27 to April 30, 2026.**

## Priority Conclusions

1. **The credit market is not broadly weakening; it is a “low-spread, low-margin-for-error” market.** The official manufacturing PMI was 50.3% in April 2026, down only 0.1 percentage point from March, while the non-manufacturing PMI was 49.4%, down 0.7 percentage point. The macro data support low rates, but not indiscriminate further credit-spread compression. [NBS PMI](https://www.stats.gov.cn/sj/zxfbhjd/202604/t20260430_1963473.html)
2. **Property policy helps valuations find a floor, but it does not yet repair the credit profiles of mixed-ownership developers.** Top 100 developers recorded RMB259.20 billion of contracted sales on an operator basis in April 2026, down 3.9% MoM and 8.9% YoY; CRIC’s equity-sales measure was RMB204.76 billion, down 0.8% MoM and 9.5% YoY. For Vanke and Gemdale, 2025 losses, weak cash flow and debt-extension pressure still outweigh policy beta. [CRIC/Sina](https://finance.sina.com.cn/wm/2026-04-30/doc-inhwhviy6110853.shtml?froms=ggmp)
3. **AA+ credit spreads are low enough to require protection against reversal.** Cbonds reported ChinaBond Corporate Bond Yield Curve (AA+) yields of 1.5433%/1.7483%/1.8714% for 1Y/3Y/5Y on April 27, 2026. Using ChinaBond CDB Bond Yield Curve yields of 1.3765%/1.5381%/1.6280% on April 29, 2026 as a close benchmark, implied spreads were only 16.68bp/21.02bp/24.34bp. [Cbonds AA+](https://cbonds.com/indexes/87187/); [Cbonds CDB](https://cbonds.com/indexes/86883/)
4. **Debt-resolution funds are being deployed faster than credit events are being digested.** In Q1 2026, RMB960.4 billion of refinancing special bonds for implicit-debt swaps was issued, equal to 48.0% of the RMB2.0 trillion annual swap quota; more than RMB171.9 billion of special new special bonds was used for debt resolution and arrears clearance. Public non-standard overdue risk is lower, but extensions and regional dispersion have not disappeared. [10jqka reposting TF Securities research](https://news.10jqka.com.cn/20260405/c675778059.shtml)

## Question 1: Can Inventory Digestion and Urban Renewal Materially Improve Vanke and Gemdale’s Credit Valuations?

### Method

I used the April 28, 2026 Politburo readout, April 2026 Top 100 developer sales data, issuers’ 2025 annual reports, onshore bond-extension notices and offshore property bond index moves. The policy analysis separates the 2024 “inventory digestion” phrase from the 2026 “stabilize the real estate market and advance urban renewal” phrase. The credit analysis focuses on sales cash collection, operating cash flow, audit opinions, public-bond payment arrangements and observable valuation proxies.

### Findings

| Issuer/Indicator | Sales or Policy Benefit | 2025 Results | Cash Flow/Debt Signal | Credit Valuation View |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Top 100 developers | April 2026 operator-basis sales of RMB259.20 billion, -3.9% MoM and -8.9% YoY | Not applicable | Equity sales of RMB204.76 billion, -0.8% MoM and -9.5% YoY | Marginal industry improvement, but no broad turn positive |
| Vanke | January-April 2026 full-basis sales of RMB21.4 billion, ranked 10th among the top 10 | Net profit attributable to shareholders of -RMB88.556 billion; revenue of RMB233.433 billion | Operating cash flow of -RMB0.988 billion; “23万科MTN001” pays 40% principal and extends 60% for one year | Policy can reduce tail liquidity risk, but cannot directly repair ratings |
| Gemdale | Q1 2026 contracted sales of RMB3.30 billion, -59.51% YoY | Net profit attributable to shareholders of -RMB13.281 billion; revenue of RMB35.858 billion | Operating cash flow of RMB16.0775 million, -99.88% YoY; interest-bearing debt of about RMB67.0 billion | Public-bond pressure is lower, but fundamentals remain weak |
| Offshore property bonds | Property USD bond index closed the week at 176.09 | Not applicable | Broadly flat on the week | Policy expectations are priced; dispersion is now issuer-specific |

**Interpretation:** The Politburo wording on stabilizing the property market and advancing urban renewal reduces concern about another demand-side leg down, but for mixed-ownership developers such as Vanke and Gemdale, ratings attention has already shifted from policy direction to cash flow and debt-restructuring execution. Vanke’s 2025 loss of RMB88.556 billion and negative operating cash flow, together with multiple public bonds maturing or puttable in April-July 2026, show that policy beta cannot replace a debt solution. Gemdale’s sales and cash-flow metrics continued to weaken; although bank loans dominate its interest-bearing debt, new financing access and project sell-through remain binding constraints. The conclusion is that policy improves the valuation floor, but does not yet provide evidence of a substantive credit-profile upgrade.

## Question 2: After Annual Reports, Which LGFV Regions Face the Highest Rating Risk?

### Method

I ranked regions using four warning signals: audit qualifications or material uncertainty over going concern, rating termination or negative outlook, public Q1 2026 non-standard risk events, and dependence on debt-resolution funding. The sample focuses on weaker regions and regions with visible public events, cross-checked against Enterprise Warning and Wind vendor fields where available. Public evidence prioritizes issuer notices, rating notices and NAFMII rating statistics.

### Findings

| Risk Rank | Region | Main Trigger | Recent Evidence | Overall Assessment |
|---:|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Yunnan | Property/LGFV overlap, going-concern language, non-standard extension pressure | Yunnan Metropolitan Real Estate’s 2025 audit report included material uncertainty over going concern; it disclosed an application to extend RMB1.960 billion of debt | Highest rating and refinancing risk |
| 2 | Guizhou | High dependence on debt resolution and heavy legacy non-standard debt | The “6+4+2” debt-resolution package continues, but high-cost non-standard debt still requires local coordination | Fewer risk events do not mean risk is cleared |
| 3 | Guangxi | Weak land finance, thin platform cash flow and high regional spreads | Regional LGFV spreads remain far above Jiangsu/Zhejiang; weak lower-tier platforms have narrow financing windows | Medium-high risk |
| 4 | Selected Qingdao platforms in Shandong | Rating termination and negative outlook reveal governance risk | Qingdao SCO Development Group terminated AA+ negative-outlook rating cooperation with CCXI and Lianhe Ratings | Single-name risk is higher than province-level risk |
| 5 | Tianjin | Debt-resolution improvement, but debt burden remains high | LGFV spreads have compressed from prior highs, still dependent on continuous refinancing | Tail risk is lower but not gone |
| 6 | Jilin/Gansu/Qinghai | Weak fiscal self-sufficiency and risk from lower-tier platforms | Public-bond net financing and local fiscal constraints coexist | Low-frequency but high-loss risk |

**Interpretation:** After the annual-report season, rating downgrades or negative outlook revisions are more likely for district/county-level platforms, entities with incomplete industrial transformation and platforms with weakening operating cash flow, rather than for provincial flagship platforms. Yunnan stands out because property SOE and LGFV credit chains overlap, and audit language plus debt-extension disclosures are forward-looking warning signals. Qingdao SCO Development’s rating-relationship termination shows that even in stronger provinces, weak platforms may avoid further public downgrades by ending rating cooperation. Guizhou, Guangxi and Tianjin share a common pattern: debt-resolution expectations have compressed spreads, but standalone repayment capacity remains weak.

### Evidence

- Yunnan Metropolitan Real Estate’s 2025 audit report contained material uncertainty over going concern and disclosed work on a RMB1.960 billion debt extension. Source: [Shanghai Securities News](https://paper.cnstock.com/html/2026-04/29/content_2209476.htm).
- Qingdao SCO Development Group terminated rating cooperation; prior ratings were AA+ with negative outlook. Source: [Sohu repost of rating notices](https://www.sohu.com/a/1007178124_121123922).
- NAFMII’s 2025Q4 rating-business statistics showed 24 issuer upgrades, 2 issuer downgrades and 2 outlook downgrades in the quarter; for 2025 as a whole, there were 91 issuer upgrades and 55 issuer downgrades. Source: [NAFMII rating bulletin](https://www.nafmii.org.cn/zlgl/pjytb/pjyw/tb/202604/P020260408383840229609.pdf).

### Caveats

The full post-2026 annual-report rating-action list will typically arrive with a lag in May-July. Non-standard overdue databases are not fully public; the regional ranking combines vendor data and public-event verification and should not be read as a rating committee decision.

## Question 3: Amid Weaker PMI and Asset Scarcity, Have AA+ Spreads Reached Extreme Lows?

### Method

I used Cbonds’ ChinaBond Corporate Bond Yield Curve (AA+) as the credit-yield series and the ChinaBond CDB Bond Yield Curve as a close policy-bank benchmark, then calculated static 1Y/3Y/5Y spreads. I compared those levels with Golden Credit Rating’s March 2026 credit-spread monthly report description of historical percentiles.

### Findings

| Tenor | AA+ Yield (2026-04-27) | CDB Yield (2026-04-29) | Approximate Spread | Assessment |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| 1Y | 1.5433% | 1.3765% | 16.68bp | Extremely low and reversal-sensitive |
| 3Y | 1.7483% | 1.5381% | 21.02bp | Low, with limited carry cushion |
| 5Y | 1.8714% | 1.6280% | 24.34bp | Not suitable for indiscriminate duration extension |

**Interpretation:** A 1Y AA+ spread of only 16.68bp leaves very little compensation for funding volatility, heavier government-bond supply or negative rating events. The 3Y and 5Y sectors still offer higher absolute yields than the front end, but their spread compensation over CDB is below 25bp, leaving an unattractive duration risk-reward profile. April PMI was not weak enough to justify a major easing trade: manufacturing PMI of 50.3% remained in expansion territory. The strategy implication is to shift from broad rating downshift to liquidity and covenant quality.

### Evidence

- On April 27, 2026, AA+ corporate 1Y/3Y/5Y yields were 1.5433%/1.7483%/1.8714%. Source: [Cbonds AA+ curve](https://cbonds.com/indexes/87187/).
- On April 29, 2026, CDB 1Y/3Y/5Y yields were 1.3765%/1.5381%/1.6280%. Source: [Cbonds CDB curve](https://cbonds.com/indexes/86883/).

### Caveats

The AA+ curve date is April 27, 2026 while the CDB curve date is April 29, 2026, because Cbonds’ free public pages disclose different latest dates. The spread calculation is therefore approximate; a formal publication should recompute same-day spreads using ChinaBond valuation curves.

## Question 4: How Much Have Special Refinancing Bonds and Debt-Resolution Quotas Eased Non-Standard Overdue Risk?

### Method

I used the 2024-2026 annual RMB2.0 trillion refinancing special-bond quota for implicit-debt swaps as the main tracking line, then reviewed Q1 2026 issuance progress, the amount of special new special bonds used for debt resolution and arrears clearance, and disclosed provincial refinancing special-bond issuance. The easing effect is measured by “issued amount/annual quota” and whether public non-standard risk events are decreasing.

### Findings

| Indicator | Value | Calculation/Description | Credit Implication |
|---|---:|---|---|
| 2026 annual implicit-debt swap quota | RMB2.0 trillion | RMB2.0 trillion per year for 2024-2026 | High supply visibility |
| Q1 2026 issued refinancing special bonds for implicit-debt swaps | RMB960.4 billion | 960.4 / 2000.0 = 48.0% | Clearly front-loaded issuance |
| Q1 2026 special new special bonds for debt resolution and arrears clearance | >RMB171.9 billion | Funds supplement government-fund budgets and clear arrears | More direct relief for non-standard debt and payables |
| January 2026 disclosed implicit-debt swap bond plans | RMB302.78991 billion | About RMB259.25 billion completed | High-intensity deployment from year start |
| Chongqing 2026 refinancing special bonds, tranches 6 and 7 | RMB29.335 billion | 10Y RMB20.160 billion; 20Y RMB9.175 billion | Helps relieve rollover pressure in a high-debt region |
| Xinjiang 2026 swap quota | RMB21.6 billion | RMB64.8 billion for 2024-2026, RMB21.6 billion per year | Annual quota completed early |

**Interpretation:** Deployment has been fast: Q1 2026 issuance reached about 48.0% of the annual swap quota, explaining continued tail-spread compression in public LGFV bonds and valuation repair in some weak regions. The key distinction is use of proceeds: refinancing special bonds primarily swap implicit debt, while special new special bonds are more direct tools for arrears and non-standard overdue relief. For Guizhou, Yunnan and Guangxi, debt-resolution funds can lower public default probability, but they do not immediately restore platform operating cash flow. If land-sale revenue and operating collections remain weak, non-standard risk will shift from “new overdue” to “post-extension renegotiation.”

### Evidence

- The November 2024 debt-resolution package arranged RMB2.0 trillion of refinancing special bonds each year from 2024 to 2026 to swap existing implicit debt, and RMB800.0 billion per year from new special bonds from 2024 for five years for debt resolution and related uses. Source: [21st Century Business Herald](https://www.21jingji.com/article/20260108/herald/68923de548c2b4cdb9fe914fca3bd7ee.html).
- Q1 2026 refinancing special bonds for implicit-debt swaps reached RMB960.4 billion, and special new special bonds used for debt resolution and arrears clearance exceeded RMB171.9 billion. Source: [10jqka reposting TF Securities research](https://news.10jqka.com.cn/20260405/c675778059.shtml).
- In January 2026, disclosed implicit-debt swap bond plans totaled RMB302.78991 billion, about RMB259.25 billion had been completed, and Xinjiang’s RMB21.6 billion annual quota was completed. Source: [Sina/Xinhua Finance](https://finance.sina.com.cn/money/bond/2026-01-28/doc-inhiwnwr7272237.shtml).
- Chongqing’s 2026 refinancing special bonds, tranches 6 and 7, totaled RMB29.335 billion. Source: [Chongqing Finance Bureau](https://czj.cq.gov.cn/zwgk_268/zfxxgkml/zcwj/qtwj/202603/t20260330_15576434_wap.html).

### Caveats

There is no unified national public disclosure system for non-standard overdue debt, so this draft cannot calculate the true decline in overdue balances. Follow-up work should cross-check Enterprise Warning, DM, local financial asset exchanges and court enforcement data.

## Q1: Was May Day Consumption a Traffic Recovery or a Ticket-Size Recovery?

### Method

I used the 2026-05-01 to 2026-05-05 holiday period and compared Ministry of Culture and Tourism domestic trips and tourism revenue, State Taxation Administration VAT invoices, Ministry of Transport cross-regional passenger movement, National Immigration Administration border crossings, and National Film Administration box-office and ticket-price data. Per-trip tourism spending is calculated as tourism revenue divided by trips, with the 2025 comparable per-trip value reverse-engineered from official YoY growth rates.

### Findings

| Indicator | 2026 May Day value | YoY | Calculation/Note | Conclusion |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Domestic trips | 325 million | +3.6% | Ministry of Culture and Tourism | Strong traffic |
| Domestic tourism revenue | RMB 185.49 billion | +2.9% | Ministry of Culture and Tourism | Revenue lagged traffic |
| Implied per-trip tourism spending | RMB 570.7 | -0.7% | RMB 185.49 billion / 325 million trips; reverse-engineered 2025 value about RMB 574.6 | Weak ticket size |
| Consumption-related VAT sales revenue | Absolute value not disclosed | +14.3% | State Taxation Administration VAT invoices | Strong transactions |
| Catering service VAT | Absolute value not disclosed | +31.4% | State Taxation Administration VAT invoices | Strong catering scenarios |
| Tourism/sightseeing/entertainment VAT | Absolute value not disclosed | +21.2% | State Taxation Administration VAT invoices | Strong experience consumption |
| Cross-regional passenger trips | 1.517 billion | Not disclosed on same basis | Ministry of Transport | Strong mobility |
| Border crossings | Nearly 11.3 million | +3.5% | National Immigration Administration | Cross-border recovery |
| Movie box office | RMB 758 million | Not uniformly disclosed | National Film Administration | Moderate |
| Moviegoers | 20.8419 million | Not uniformly disclosed | National Film Administration | Admissions better than ticket price |
| Average movie ticket price | RMB 36.3 | -8.0% | 2025 comparable RMB 39.5 | Clear price pressure |

Interpretation: Volume recovery is more certain than price recovery. Trip growth exceeded tourism-revenue growth, and implied per-trip spending fell about 0.7% YoY, consistent with the 8.0% decline in average movie ticket price. VAT growth was stronger than tourism revenue, showing more active transaction value in catering, entertainment, beauty and jewelry, but VAT invoice sales are not the same as listed-company revenue recognition or profit. The right conclusion is "traffic recovery plus service-scenario recovery," not yet "ticket-size recovery."

### Evidence

- Ministry of Culture and Tourism/Xinhua: 325 million domestic trips and RMB 185.49 billion tourism revenue during May Day 2026; about 49,400 public cultural events with roughly 88 million visits; around 32,000 commercial performances with RMB 2.48 billion box office, up 14.66%; foreign-national entries/exits of 1.26 million, up 12.5%.[State Council/Xinhua, 2026-05-07](https://english.www.gov.cn/archive/statistics/202605/07/content_WS69fc95acc6d00ca5f9a0acec.html)
- State Taxation Administration VAT invoices: consumption-related sales revenue +14.3%, catering services +31.4%, tourism/sightseeing/entertainment +21.2%, cosmetics +55.8%, and gold and silver jewelry +28.8%.[State Council/Xinhua, 2026-05-06](https://english.www.gov.cn/archive/statistics/202605/06/content_WS69fb45c4c6d00ca5f9a0ac9f.html)
- Combined Ministry of Transport, Ministry of Commerce and National Immigration Administration data: 1.517 billion cross-regional passenger trips; long-distance trips over 800 km +20%, hotel accommodation spending +18%, car-rental orders +21%, performance consumption +17.6%.[CGTN, 2026-05-06](https://news.cgtn.com/news/2026-05-06/Graphics-China-sees-travel-consumption-surge-during-May-Day-holiday-1MVJ2tAhgg8/p.html)
- National Film Administration data: May Day box office RMB 758 million, moviegoers 20.8419 million, screenings 2.376 million, average ticket price RMB 36.3, versus RMB 39.5 in the same period last year.[The Paper/CCTV News, 2026-05-06](https://www.thepaper.cn/newsDetail_forward_33100829)

### Caveats

Official tourism data do not split lodging, dining, transport, tickets and shopping; VAT data do not disclose absolute amounts and may be affected by invoicing rates, subsidies and promotions; movie box-office numbers can differ between real-time platforms and the National Film Administration; hotel ADR, occupancy and scenic-area secondary-spend data still need company or industry verification.

## Q2: Have Trade-In Benefits Spread from Autos to Appliances, Digital Products and Offline Retail?

### Method

I used the Ministry of Commerce cumulative data as of 2026-04-12 as a stage baseline, then used the holiday-period cumulative data as of 2026-05-04 to calculate incremental sales and category mix. I cross-checked the result against the performance of the home appliance ETF and the broad consumer ETF.

### Findings

| Indicator | As of 2026-04-12 | As of 2026-05-04 | Increment | 2026-05-04 mix |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Beneficiaries/purchases | 69.777 million | Over 86 million | Over 16.223 million | Not applicable |
| Total sales driven | RMB 502.94 billion | Over RMB 629 billion | Over RMB 126.06 billion | 100.0% |
| Auto sales | RMB 269.44 billion | Over RMB 342 billion | Over RMB 72.56 billion | 54.4% |
| Home appliance sales | RMB 111.09 billion | Over RMB 141.5 billion | Over RMB 30.41 billion | 22.5% |
| Digital and smart product sales | RMB 122.41 billion | Over RMB 145.3 billion | Over RMB 22.89 billion | 23.1% |
| Home appliance ETF `159996.SZ` | 1.527 | 1.565 | +2.49% | 2026-04-30 to 2026-05-08 |
| Consumer ETF `159928.SZ` | 0.747 | 0.734 | -1.74% | 2026-04-30 to 2026-05-08 |

Interpretation: Diffusion has happened, but it is not evenly distributed. As of 2026-05-04, autos still accounted for about 54.4% of sales driven by the program, while appliances and digital/smart products together accounted for about 45.6%, enough to explain home-appliance ETF outperformance versus broad consumer. From 2026-04-12 to 2026-05-04, autos contributed about 57.6% of incremental sales, appliances about 24.1%, and digital products about 18.2%, showing continued marginal policy support for appliances. Offline retail also has supporting evidence: VAT data show general retail +12.4% and offline retailers such as supermarkets +15.6%.

### Evidence

- Ministry of Commerce as of 2026-04-12: consumer goods trade-in sales of RMB 502.94 billion, benefiting 69.777 million purchases; autos RMB 269.44 billion, appliances RMB 111.09 billion, digital and smart products RMB 122.41 billion.[State Council/Xinhua, 2026-04-15](https://english.www.gov.cn/archive/statistics/202604/15/content_WS69df96afc6d00ca5f9a0a70e.html)
- Holiday update: as of 2026-05-04, the programs had benefited over 86 million people and driven more than RMB 629 billion of sales, including over RMB 342 billion in autos, over RMB 141.5 billion in appliances, and over RMB 145.3 billion in digital and smart products.[CGTN, 2026-05-06](https://news.cgtn.com/news/2026-05-06/Graphics-China-sees-travel-consumption-surge-during-May-Day-holiday-1MVJ2tAhgg8/p.html)
- Policy funding: RMB 250 billion of ultra-long special treasury bonds is planned for consumer goods trade-in programs in 2026; a second RMB 62.5 billion batch was allocated in April.[State Council/Xinhua, 2026-04-10](https://english.www.gov.cn/news/202604/10/content_WS69d8b355c6d00ca5f9a0a566.html)
- VAT retail data: general retail +12.4%, offline retailers such as supermarkets +15.6%.[State Council/Xinhua, 2026-05-06](https://english.www.gov.cn/archive/statistics/202605/06/content_WS69fb45c4c6d00ca5f9a0ac9f.html)
- Price data: Yahoo Finance historical prices via `yfinance`, accessed 2026-05-10.[Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/)

### Caveats

Trade-in figures are cumulative, not single-week figures; "beneficiaries" and "purchases" are not phrased identically across releases; sales driven by policy are not the same as net incremental company revenue and may include pulled-forward demand or subsidy arbitrage; the home appliance ETF also reflects exports, property expectations and market style.

## Q6: How Does Brent Near USD 100/bbl Affect Consumer?

### Method

I used 2026-04-30 to 2026-05-08 prices for Brent, WTI, USD/CNY, sugar, coffee and cocoa futures, then overlaid news on the U.S.-Iran conflict and Strait of Hormuz expectations to assess directional impacts on F&B, packaging, logistics, travel and household budgets.

### Findings

| Indicator | 2026-04-30 | 2026-05-08 | Period change | Consumer impact |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Brent `BZ=F` | USD 114.01/bbl | USD 101.29/bbl | -11.16% | Still high; pressure on airlines, logistics, chemical packaging and household energy spending |
| WTI `CL=F` | USD 105.07/bbl | USD 95.42/bbl | -9.18% | Marginal relief for refined products and transport costs |
| USD/CNY `USDCNY=X` | 6.8375 | 6.8000 | -0.55% | Mild RMB appreciation cushions imported input costs |
| ICE Sugar `SB=F` | 14.58 cents/lb | 14.69 cents/lb | +0.75% | Slight pressure for beverages and bakery |
| ICE Coffee `KC=F` | 300.90 cents/lb | 266.30 cents/lb | -11.50% | Raw-material pressure eased for coffee chains |
| ICE Cocoa `CC=F` | 3494 | 4326 | +23.81% | Cost pressure rose for chocolate, bakery and snacks |

Interpretation: Oil fell during the week, but the absolute level remains high, so consumer companies should not treat it as a clean tailwind. Brent fell from USD 114.01/bbl to USD 101.29/bbl, easing the most extreme travel and logistics shock, but AP reported that Brent still settled at USD 100.06/bbl on 2026-05-07 after trading above USD 115 earlier in the week. For restaurants and F&B, oil affects logistics, plastic/PET, gas and household budgets; cocoa's surge is a more direct single-input risk for snacks and bakery. Mild RMB appreciation helps imported inputs, but not enough to fully offset high oil and higher cocoa.

### Evidence

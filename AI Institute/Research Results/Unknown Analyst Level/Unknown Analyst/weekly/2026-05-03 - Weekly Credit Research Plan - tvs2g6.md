# Weekly Credit Research Plan

**Coverage window:** 2026-04-27 (Mon) → 2026-05-01 (Fri)
*Note: 2026-05-01 (Fri) is Labor Day; Chinese markets are closed.*

## 1. Scope
Key developments affecting the LGFV and property bond markets during this window:
1. **2026-04-27 (Mon):** Reports of new quotas for Special Refinancing Bonds in provinces like Guizhou and Yunnan for swapping high-interest non-standard LGFV debt. *Significance: Relieving liquidity pressure on tail-end LGFVs and driving further compression of regional spreads.*
2. **2026-04-28 (Tue):** MoHURD and NFRA provide updates on "White List" financing for real estate projects, with total approved loans hitting a new milestone. *Significance: Evaluating the impact of project-level support on the primary entity's bond pricing, especially for distressed developers.*
3. **2026-04-29 (Wed):** Peak of 2025 Annual Reports and 2026 Q1 Reports disclosure. Focus on cash flow coverage ratios and short-term debt repayment schedules for top LGFVs and private developers. *Significance: Fundamental data will directly calibrate secondary market pricing logic.*
4. **2026-04-30 (Thu):** NBS releases April PMI data. *Significance: Macro recovery momentum affects expectations for local government fiscal capacity, transmitting to LGFV credit spreads.*
5. **2026-04-30 (Thu):** A benchmark private developer (e.g., Vanke or Longfor) announces a preliminary proposal for domestic bond extension. *Significance: Serving as a bellwether for industry credit recovery; the approval rate and terms will define market risk appetite.*

## 2. Key Analytical Questions
1. **Earnings Season Test:** Is the deterioration of LGFV asset quality revealed in 2025 reports worse than expected? Which regions show significant marginal downgrades in credit profile?
2. **Spread Extremes:** Credit spreads in high-yield LGFV regions (e.g., Tianjin, parts of Shandong) are at historical lows. Is there a risk of a correction driven by profit-taking after the release of April economic data?
3. **White List Effect:** Has financing support transitioned from projects to corporate entities? Is the recovery in secondary market volume for private developers driven by policy speculation or fundamental reversal?
4. **Duration Strategy Shift:** Amid volatility in long-term CGB yields, are credit bond investors shifting from "duration extension" to "credit down-tiering"?

## 3. Data Needs
1. **Yields and Spreads:** Trends in credit spreads for LGFVs and property bonds across ratings (AAA/AA+) and tenors relative to CGBs.
2. **Issuance and Redemption:** Net financing for credit bonds this week, focusing on the rollover ratio for LGFVs.
3. **Financial Metrics:** Key indicators from newly disclosed reports: restricted asset ratios, operating cash flow, and net financing cash flow.
4. **Secondary Trading:** Detailed high-valuation trades for property bonds to identify institutional rebalancing.

## 4. Visualizations
1. **Regional Spread Heatmap:** Weekly changes in average LGFV spreads by province to identify outliers.
2. **Property Bond Price-Volume Correlation:** Price and volume curves for 5 representative private developers.
3. **Term Spread Distribution:** Comparison of the AAA LGFV curve this week vs. last week to show shifts in duration preference.
4. **Primary Market Bid-to-Cover Ratio:** Average subscription ratios for LGFV bond auctions to gauge capital sentiment.

## 5. Report Outline
- **Section 1: Weekly Recap and Credit Direction.** Summarizing market sentiment and core policy variables ahead of the holiday.
- **Section 2: LGFV Focus — Refinancing and Earnings.** Analyzing the impact of the new swap bonds on regional risk and examining credit shifts based on the latest reports.

# Macro Research Plan (Chief Economist)

## Coverage window: 2026-04-27 (Mon) → 2026-05-01 (Fri)

### 1. Research Scope
This week was defined by a dual-track focus on central bank signals and labor market resilience:
*   **FOMC Rate Decision (April 29)**: Analyzing the policy statement for any shifts in the "soft landing" narrative and signals for the rate-cut trajectory in H2.
*   **China Official PMI Data (April 30)**: Monitoring the continuity of recovery in manufacturing and non-manufacturing sectors, specifically the divergence between domestic and external demand.
*   **US April Non-Farm Payrolls (May 1)**: Assessing marginal changes in labor market tightness and the support provided by average hourly earnings to inflation expectations.
*   **Big Tech Q1 Earnings Week (April 27-30)**: Focusing on AI CapEx as a proxy for long-term macro productivity gains.
*   **Eurozone April Preliminary CPI**: Evaluating the certainty of the ECB initiating its rate-cut cycle in June.
*   **May Day Market Holidays (May 1)**: China, Hong Kong, and most European markets were closed. Need to evaluate volatility risks in US markets during thin liquidity.

### 2. Key Analytical Questions
1.  **Has the Fed's "Hawkish Floor" shifted?** Given recent signs of cooling, has Powell's confidence in the inflation path improved?
2.  **How balanced is China's economic recovery?** Is manufacturing expansion still driven by policy-led investment, or are private investment and household consumption picking up?
3.  **Is the wage-price spiral broken?** Did US wage growth in April fall back into the "non-inflationary" range of 3.5%-4%?
4.  **Where is the "resonance point" in global manufacturing?** Based on PMI data from Germany, Japan, US, and China, is the global inventory cycle officially entering the restocking phase?

### 3. Data & Information Needs
*   **Central Banks**: FOMC Policy Statement comparison; Fed Funds futures pricing curves.
*   **Labor Market**: BLS Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP), unemployment rate, labor participation rate, and sector-specific wage growth.
*   **China Macro**: NBS Official PMI (Manufacturing, Non-manufacturing, Composite); breakdown of new orders vs. export orders.
*   **Inflation**: Eurozone HICP (YoY/MoM); assessment of the lagging transmission of March US PCE.
*   **Corporate**: Earnings surprise rates for S&P 500 Information Technology and Consumer Discretionary sectors.

### 4. Proposed Visualizations
1.  **G3 Central Bank Policy Rate Paths (Line Chart)**: Comparing implied rate expectations for the Fed, ECB, and BoJ through year-end 2026.
2.  **US-China Manufacturing PMI Comparison (Dual Axis Line)**: Highlighting the synchronization (or lack thereof) in economic momentum between the two largest economies.
3.  **US NFP Sector Breakdown (Heatmap)**: Displaying structural differences in employment growth between services and production sectors.
4.  **S&P 500 Sector Contribution (Waterfall Chart)**: Analyzing the primary drivers of market volatility (Tech vs. Value).

### 5. Final Report Outline
1.  **Executive Summary**: Leading with the core "Macro Tone" (e.g., "Dovish Tilt" or "Growth Resilience").
2.  **Central Bank Watch**: Deep dive into FOMC signals and policy nuances.
3.  **Labor & Inflation**: The rate-cut game following the April NFP data.
4.  **China Momentum**: Quality and efficiency of recovery behind the PMI data.
5.  **Cross-Asset Strategy**: Recommendations for Fixed Income, Equities, and FX based on this week's shifts.
6.  **Next Week Outlook**: Key risk events and catalysts.

# Global Macro Weekly Research Plan

**Coverage window: 2026-05-04 (Mon) → 2026-05-08 (Fri)**
**Report Date: 2026-05-10**

## 1. Core Scope
The global markets this week were dominated by the significant cooling of the US labor market and the unexpected surge in China's trade data. Key macro developments include:

1.  **US Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP, May 8):** April jobs gain plummeted to 62,000, far below the previous 178,000. This signaled an accelerating slowdown in labor demand, weighing heavily on the Fed's terminal rate outlook.
2.  **US ISM Services PMI (May 5):** Registered 53.6, slightly missing the 54.6 forecast. While still in expansion, it indicates a marginal loss of momentum in the services sector.
3.  **China Trade Data (Released May 9, Weekly Summary):** Exports jumped 14.1% YoY and Imports surged 25.3% YoY. The double-digit growth defied consensus, likely driven by resilient demand and "panic stockpiling" due to geopolitical supply chain risks.
4.  **Eurozone Retail Sales (May 7):** Fell 0.1% MoM in March, better than the feared -0.3%. Despite the slight beat, consumer sentiment remains at multi-year lows, reflecting persistent pressure on household spending.
5.  **Bank of England (BoE) Policy Sentiment (May 7 Monitor):** No meeting this week, but the hawkish hold from April 30 (8-1 vote, with Pill dissenting for a hike) continued to support GBP/USD amid persistent inflation concerns.
6.  **US Consumer Sentiment & Inflation Expectations (May 8):** UoM Sentiment fell to 49.5, while inflation expectations remained elevated at 4.7%, fueling "stagflation" narratives at the retail level.

## 2. Key Analytical Questions
The research phase will focus on answering:
1.  **Has the US labor market reached a tipping point?** Does the 62K print suggest an increased risk of a "hard landing," and will it force the Fed to pivot toward rate cut discussions sooner than expected?
2.  **Is the Chinese export surge sustainable?** To what extent was the growth driven by "front-loading" and stockpiling vs. a genuine recovery in global manufacturing demand?
3.  **Are the Eurozone's growth prospects structurally impaired?** Will weak consumption force the ECB to prioritize growth over inflation targets in the coming quarters?
4.  **How will US-UK policy divergence evolve?** With US employment weakening and UK inflation hawkishness persisting, what are the primary carry-trade implications for GBP?

## 3. Data Requirements
*   **Labor Market:** NFP historical series, Unemployment Rate (4.3%), Initial Jobless Claims (200K), Average Hourly Earnings (0.3% MoM).
*   **PMI Data:** ISM Manufacturing vs. Services sub-indices (New Orders, Prices, Employment).
*   **Trade Flows:** Detailed breakdown of Chinese exports by major partners (US, EU, ASEAN).
*   **Market Indicators:** Dollar Index (DXY), US 10Y Yield, SPX, EURUSD, GBPUSD, Offshore Yuan (CNH).

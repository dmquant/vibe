# Weekly Credit Research Plan (2026 Week 18)

**本周覆盖范围 / Coverage window: 2026-04-27 (Mon) → 2026-05-01 (Fri)**
*Note: Friday, May 1, 2026, is the Labor Day holiday; mainland China markets are closed.*

## 1. Core Scope

The credit market this week was driven by a trio of factors: macro data releases, high-level policy meetings, and the conclusion of the 2025 annual report season. Key developments include:

1. **April 30: NBS Releases April Manufacturing PMI**  
   *Event:* Manufacturing PMI recorded 49.8%, slightly below expectations.  
   *Significance:* Marginal slowing of economic recovery momentum reinforces market expectations for loose monetary policy and continued low credit spreads.
2. **April 30: Completion of 2025 Annual Report Disclosures**  
   *Event:* Disclosures for credit bond issuers have concluded.  
   *Significance:* The "Rating Season" begins; focus on downgrade risks and non-standard default warnings for real estate and weak LGFVs under financial pressure.
3. **April 29: Release of the Central Politburo Meeting Communiqué**  
   *Event:* The meeting emphasized "coordinated research on policies to digest existing housing stock and optimize incremental housing."  
   *Significance:* Policy focus is shifting from "delivery of housing" to "inventory digestion," providing major guidance for credit recovery in the property sector.
4. **April 28: LGFV Net Financing Continues to Shrink**  
   *Event:* April net financing for LGFV bonds was negative for the fourth consecutive month.  
   *Significance:* Strict controls on new debt under "debt resolution" frameworks further strengthen the "asset famine" logic for high-quality short-to-medium-term bonds.
5. **April 27: Clarification of Ultra-Long Special Treasury Bond Issuance**  
   *Event:* Ministry of Finance clarified the May-June issuance schedule.  
   *Significance:* The impact of supply on benchmark rates directly influences the pricing and duration risk of the credit yield curve.

## 2. Key Analytical Questions

1. **Policy Playbook:** Will the new "inventory digestion" strategy proposed by the Politburo substantially improve credit valuations for mixed-ownership developers (e.g., Vanke, Gemdale)?
2. **Rating Risks:** Post-annual report season, which regions' LGFV platforms face the highest risk of audit qualifications or deteriorating financial metrics?
3. **Spread Trends:** Amidst weakening PMI and persistent asset famine, have credit spreads (especially AA+ grades) hit historical floors, facing potential reversal pressure?
4. **Debt Resolution:** How efficiently are the latest quotas of special refinancing bonds being used to alleviate non-standard overdue issues across provinces?

## 3. Data Requirements

*   **Macro & Industry:** April Official Manufacturing/Non-Manufacturing PMI (NBS), Top 100 Real Estate Sales for April (CRIC).
*   **Primary Market:** Issuance volume, cancellation counts, and average bid-to-cover ratios by sector (LGFV, Property, Coal/Steel).
*   **Secondary Market:** ChinaBond Implied Rating Spread Curves (1Y, 3Y, 5Y), LGFV spreads by province.
*   **Rating Dynamics:** Statistics on rating adjustments, watchlists, and revised reports released by major agencies this week.

# Weekly Consumer Research: Baijiu, Labor Day Consumption, and Trade-In Policy

Research window: through 2026-05-01. The upstream file `01_weekly_plan.md` was missing from the current workspace, so this report reconstructs the research scope from the workflow summary: baijiu earnings, Labor Day consumption forecasts, and the impact of consumer-goods trade-in policy.

## Priority Conclusions

1. Baijiu earnings confirm an industry phase of destocking, price stabilization, and wider divergence: Kweichow Moutai reported 2026Q1 revenue of RMB 53.909 billion, up 6.54% YoY, but net profit attributable to shareholders rose only 1.47%; Luzhou Laojiao, Shanxi Fenjiu, and King’s Luck saw Q1 revenue fall 14.19%, 9.68%, and 15.23%, with profit pressure deeper.
2. The Labor Day setup is about traffic and trip length, not a clear average-ticket boom: the Ministry of Transport expected 1.52 billion cross-regional passenger movements over the holiday, up 4%, with air passenger traffic of 11.75 million, up 5.4%; F&B and local services should benefit from tourist flows, but the boost to baijiu is more likely in mass-market drinking occasions than premium banquets.
3. Trade-in policy remains the strongest policy variable for durable-goods retail: in 2026Q1 it drove RMB 433.17 billion of sales, equal to about 3.39% of total retail sales of consumer goods of RMB 12,769.5 billion; autos contributed RMB 228.69 billion, or 52.8%, while home appliances/digital/smart products contributed RMB 204.48 billion, or 47.2%.
4. Portfolio implication for consumer sectors: near term, prioritize F&B, OTA, appliance retail, and categories with visible traffic or subsidy conversion; for baijiu, wait for synchronized improvement in channel inventory, wholesale prices, and bottle-opening demand. Premium baijiu remains more defensive than sub-premium and regional brands.

## Question 1: What Do Baijiu Earnings Say About the Industry Cycle?

### Method

The analysis uses listed-company 2025 annual reports and 2026 first-quarter reports, covering premium baijiu (Kweichow Moutai, Wuliangye, Luzhou Laojiao), the leading light-aroma player (Shanxi Fenjiu), and regional brands (Gujing Gongjiu, King’s Luck). The comparison focuses on revenue, net profit attributable to shareholders, operating cash flow, channel structure, and regional performance. All amounts are in RMB billions; percentages are year-on-year changes.

Interpretation: Baijiu is not in a broad recovery; it is a split market where premium brands are relatively stable while regional and sub-premium names remain under pressure. Moutai returned to revenue growth in Q1, but profit growth lagged revenue, showing that product and channel restructuring still carries cost. Wuliangye’s Q1 headline rebound is heavily affected by retrospective adjustments to the prior-year base and should not be read as a simple sell-through recovery. The synchronized revenue and profit declines at Luzhou Laojiao, Shanxi Fenjiu, and King’s Luck show that channel destocking, weaker business banquets, and downshifting price bands are still affecting the sector. Luzhou Laojiao’s Q1 operating cash-flow improvement is positive, but it looks more related to collection timing and spending discipline than conclusive evidence of stronger end-demand.

## Question 2: What Does the Labor Day Consumption Setup Mean for F&B, Retail, and Baijiu?

### Method

This section uses Ministry of Transport, OTA, and industry-media forecast data released before 2026-04-30, with 2025 Labor Day and 2026 Qingming/New Year official tourism data as benchmarks. Because the research window ends on 2026-05-01, final Labor Day holiday data had not yet been released; this section uses forecasts and historical comparables only.

### Findings

| Indicator | 2026 Labor Day Forecast / Recent Comparable | YoY / Change | Consumer-Sector Implication |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Total cross-regional passenger movements | 1.52 billion | +4.0% | Clear traffic expansion; positive for transport, hotels, scenic areas, and F&B |
| Average daily cross-regional passenger movements | 304 million | Not disclosed | Peak traffic supports dine-in F&B and instant retail |
| Road passenger movements | 1.392 billion | Not disclosed | Self-driving and nearby trips still dominate; positive for lower-tier destinations |
| Rail passenger trips | 107 million | +5.2% | Stable medium- and long-distance travel supports core urban commercial districts |
| Air passengers | 11.75 million | +5.4% | Higher-tier cities and inbound/outbound chains continue recovering |
| May 1 single-day movements | 344 million | +3.3% | Strong first-day peak, but not proof of a full-period average-ticket increase |
| National expressway traffic | 70 million vehicle trips | +3.5% | Nearby trips, county-level consumption, and service-area retail benefit |
| Spring Tour domestic travel bookings | Absolute value not disclosed | More than +20% | Order growth above transport volume implies stronger packaged travel demand |
| 2025 Labor Day domestic trips | 314 million | +6.4% | 2026 forecasts must be judged against a high base |
| 2025 Labor Day tourism revenue | 180.269 billion | +8.0% | 2025 per-capita spending was about RMB 574.10, the benchmark for 2026 ticket-size analysis |

Interpretation: The highest-confidence signal for 2026 Labor Day is traffic, not average spending. Extended leave and spring-break overlap lengthen trips, directly benefiting OTAs, chain hotels, scenic areas, and local specialty restaurants; F&B demand should tilt toward tourist meals, family meals, and local cuisines. For baijiu, tourism-led dining occasions usually sit below business banquets and wedding banquets in price band, so the benefit is more relevant to mass-market baijiu, beer, and beverages than to premium baijiu wholesale prices. If post-holiday data show strong trips but weak revenue, that would point to continued trading down in discretionary consumption and premium gifting.

## Question 3: How Large Is the Trade-In Policy Boost to Retail and Branded Consumption?

### Method

The primary input is Ministry of Commerce big-data disclosure for 2026Q1 consumer-goods trade-ins. The figures are cross-checked against National Bureau of Statistics Q1 total retail sales of consumer goods, catering revenue, and above-quota category growth. Calculations include the policy-driven sales share of total retail sales, auto and appliance/digital contribution shares, and sales per application or unit.

### Findings

| Indicator | Value | Calculation / YoY | Interpretation |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Q1 consumer-goods trade-in driven sales | 433.17 billion | Ministry of Commerce disclosure | The clearest policy increment for durable-goods consumption |
| Q1 consumers reached | 60.933 million visits | Ministry of Commerce disclosure | Average driven sales of about RMB 7,109.0 per consumer visit |
| Auto trade-in driven new-car sales | 228.69 billion | 52.8% of trade-in sales | Autos are the largest contributor |
| Auto subsidy applications | 1.408 million | About RMB 162,400 sales per application | High ticket size and strong policy leverage |
| Six appliance, digital, and smart-product categories sold | 59.525 million units/items | Ministry of Commerce disclosure | Broad coverage of household replacement demand |
| Appliance/digital/smart-product sales | 204.48 billion | 47.2% of trade-in sales | Most direct benefit to appliance retail, platforms, and brand manufacturers |
| Sales per unit/item | RMB 3,435.2 | RMB 204.48 billion / 59.525 million units/items | Subsidies are concentrated in mid-to-high-ticket durables |
| Q1 total retail sales of consumer goods | 12,769.5 billion | +2.4% YoY | Trade-in driven sales equal about 3.39% of total retail sales |
| Q1 catering revenue | 1,462.3 billion | Estimated about +4.2% YoY | Services outgrew goods, but subsidies mainly target goods |
| Q1 above-quota tobacco and alcohol retail sales | 212.2 billion | +16.0% YoY | Holiday gifting and Spring Festival base effects are strong; this is not the same as baijiu sell-through |
| Q1 household appliances and audio/video equipment | March RMB 101.0 billion; Q1 RMB 258.2 billion | March about +0.3% YoY; Q1 about +2.1% YoY | Policy supports demand, but high base and pull-forward effects keep growth modest |

Interpretation: Trade-in policy is already large enough to matter at the macro retail level, with Q1 driven sales equal to about 3.39% of total retail sales. Autos contributed more than half, meaning the policy impulse is concentrated in big-ticket durable goods rather than everyday consumer products. Appliances and digital products cover a wider user base, but the average unit value of roughly RMB 3,435.2 still points to air conditioners, refrigerators, washing machines, smartphones, and similar mid-to-high-ticket items. For branded consumption, the policy can improve retail sales and channel turnover, but it may also pull forward future demand. Baijiu, F&B, and packaged food do not directly receive subsidies and benefit only indirectly through household cash allocation and traffic recovery.

### Caveats

The Ministry of Commerce reports “driven sales,” not fiscal subsidy spending and not net incremental demand; some purchases may be pulled forward. NBS above-quota categories do not map exactly to Ministry of Commerce subsidy categories, so precise attribution by item is not possible. Appliance and digital demand is jointly affected by local subsidy timing, platform promotions, and manufacturer discounts; policy elasticity needs to be tested against subsequent monthly data.

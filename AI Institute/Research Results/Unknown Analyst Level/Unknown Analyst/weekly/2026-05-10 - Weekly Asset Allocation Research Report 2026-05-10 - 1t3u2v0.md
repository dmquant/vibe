## 1. Key Research Conclusions

1.  **Risk Assets: AI Earnings & Oil Retreat Convergence.** The new record highs in U.S. equities (S&P 500 +2.3%, Nasdaq +4.5%) were not driven by liquidity, but by the materialization of AI-chain earnings (AMD +19%, INTC +14%+) and the mid-week fade of Middle East geopolitical risk premiums (WTI -7%).

3.  **Duration & Fixed Income: Yield Curve Stability.** Despite robust employment, long-end yields ticked slightly lower (10Y -1bp, 30Y -2bp), supported by stable inflation expectations and the Treasury maintaining its coupon auction sizes ($125B refunding).
4.  **Global Allocation: Resilient Non-U.S. Assets.** Driven by the RBA hike (to 4.35%) and a softer USD (DXY -0.48%), non-U.S. assets (EEM +5.94%, EFA +1.82%) significantly outperformed the U.S. equal-weight index (RSP +0.63%).

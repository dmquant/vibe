# Weekly Research Plan

**Coverage Window:** 2026-04-27 (Mon) → 2026-05-01 (Fri)  
**Analyst:** Convertible Bond Analyst  
**Status:** Planning Phase

## 1. Strategic Scope

The final week of April 2026 is critical for the A-share market as it marks the completion of the earnings season, coupled with pre-holiday sentiment ahead of the Labor Day long break.

1.  **Completion of Earnings Disclosures (April 30):** The deadline for 2025 Annual Reports and 2026 Q1 Reports is April 30. The focus will be on the price volatility of bonds whose underlying equities reported significant surprises or misses, leading to a repricing of credit risk premiums.
2.  **Forced Redemption Stress Test (April 28):** Following a recent recovery in tech and manufacturing sectors, identify CBs that have triggered or are approaching the "15/30" redemption clause (e.g., specific electronics and machinery bonds) and analyze the issuers' intent to redeem.
3.  **Pre-holiday Liquidity & Basis Shifts (April 29):** Liquidity tightened slightly ahead of the long holiday. Observe the resilience of the CSI Convertible Bond Index amid shrinking volumes and the narrow-range fluctuation of the Median Conversion Premium.
4.  **Market Holiday Notice (May 1):** The market is closed on Friday, May 1, for Labor Day. This trading week consists of only 4 business days.
5.  **Credit Recovery in Low-Price Bonds:** Evaluate whether undervalued, small-cap CBs—previously hit by credit concerns—offer valuation recovery opportunities now that the earnings uncertainty has cleared.

## 2. Analytical Questions

1.  **How has the overall credit environment for CBs changed post-earnings season?** Specifically, have default risk expectations for low-rating bonds improved based on Q1 data?
2.  **Where does the current conversion premium stand in historical percentiles?** Does the "defensive" nature of CBs hold firm during equity volatility?
3.  **Which bonds face imminent forced redemption risk?** How can investors avoid the inevitable premium compression caused by redemption calls?
4.  **How might market styles shift after the holiday?** Based on sector performance in the final week of April, what are the predicted hotspots for May?

## 3. Data Requirements

*   **Index Performance:** Weekly returns for the CSI Convertible Bond Index (000832), SSE Composite, and SZSE Component.
*   **Valuation Metrics:** Market-wide weighted average price, median conversion premium, and debt premium distribution.
*   **Individual Bond Moves:** Top 10 gainers and losers (excluding those near maturity).
*   **Redemption Monitoring:** List of bonds meeting redemption criteria, including remaining size and current premiums.
*   **Financial Data:** Q1 revenue/profit growth and debt-to-asset ratio changes for key underlying equities.

## 4. Proposed Visualizations

1.  **Index Comparison Chart:** Normalized performance of CSI CB Index vs. CSI 300 from 2026-04-27 to 2026-04-30.
2.  **Conversion Premium Histogram:** Distribution of market-wide premiums compared to the previous Friday.
3.  **Redemption Alert Table:** Top 5 bonds in the redemption countdown, including "Current Price," "Conversion Price," and "Trigger Count."
4.  **Sector Return Bar Chart:** Average weekly performance of underlying equity sectors.

## 5. Report Outline

1.  **Weekly Summary:** Overview of the earnings season finale and pre-holiday trading characteristics.
2.  **Market Performance & Valuation:** Deconstructing premium changes to assess debt-like vs. equity-like behavior.
3.  **Earnings & Credit Feature:** In-depth review of the marginal impact of annual/Q1 reports on CB valuations.
4.  **Individual Bond Focus & Redemption Alerts:** Identifying risky targets and uncovering high-margin-of-safety opportunities.
5.  **Outlook:** Post-holiday macro expectations and asset allocation strategies.

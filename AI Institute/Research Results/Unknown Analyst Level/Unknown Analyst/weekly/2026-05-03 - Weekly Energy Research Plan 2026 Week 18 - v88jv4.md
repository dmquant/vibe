# Weekly Energy Research Plan (2026 Week 18)

**Coverage window: 2026-04-27 (Monday) → 2026-05-01 (Friday)**

## 1. Research Scope & Material Developments

The energy market this week was shaped by fluctuating geopolitical premiums, seasonal inventory adjustments, and macroeconomic data prints. Key events include:

1.  **April 27 (Mon): Brent Risk Premium Contraction**
    *   Positive signals from Middle East ceasefire negotiations led to a 2.1% drop in Brent prices as market focus shifted from geopolitical risk to fundamental balance.
2.  **April 28 (Tue): US DOE Strategic Petroleum Reserve (SPR) Update**
    *   The Department of Energy announced plans to purchase an additional 3 million barrels of crude for delivery between August and October, aiming to support the back-end of the WTI curve.
3.  **April 29 (Wed): EIA Weekly Petroleum Status Report**
    *   US commercial crude stocks fell by a surprising 3.5M barrels, yet gasoline inventories rose by 1.2M barrels due to lackluster demand, weighing on RBOB crack spreads.
4.  **April 30 (Thu): EU Gas Storage Hits Seasonal High**
    *   EU underground gas storage levels reached 65% capacity, significantly above the 5-year average, cooling spot price pressure for the summer injection season.
5.  **May 1 (Fri): International Labor Day & Macro Headwinds**
    *   Markets in China and much of Europe were closed. US Dollar strength following the Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP) report exerted downward pressure on dollar-denominated energy commodities.
6.  **Throughout the Week: Norway Gas Maintenance Outlook**
    *   Gassco released its May seasonal maintenance schedule, expected to impact ~60 mcm/d of export capacity, providing support to forward gas premiums.

## 2. Key Analytical Questions

1.  **Geopolitical Pricing:** With Middle East tensions stabilizing, how much "security premium" remains embedded in current Brent pricing?
2.  **US Refinery Dynamics:** Does the unexpected gasoline build ahead of the driving season signal a negative demand feedback loop to high retail prices?
3.  **European Balance:** Against the backdrop of high storage levels, will the TTF summer-winter contango widen further as injection requirements diminish?
4.  **China Industrial Demand:** Despite the May 1 holiday, how do the late-April PMI figures portend coal and power load recovery for the post-holiday period?

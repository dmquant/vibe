# Agriculture Weekly Research Plan (Apr 27 → May 1, 2026)

## Coverage Window
本周覆盖范围 / Coverage window: 2026-04-27 (Mon) → 2026-05-01 (Fri)
*Note: May 1, 2026 (Friday) is International Labor Day. China markets were closed; analysis will prioritize international performance and pre-holiday domestic restocking.*

## 1. Material Developments (Scope)
1. **USDA Crop Progress Report (Apr 27)**: US corn planting reached 15%, significantly ahead of the 5-year average. Smooth planting progress applied downward pressure on CBOT corn deferred contracts.
2. **China Hog Price Monitoring (Apr 28)**: Ministry of Agriculture data showed reproductive sow inventory continued to decline to ~39 million head. Hog prices saw a seasonal rebound ahead of Labor Day, rising 3.2% WoW.
3. **International Potash Contract Prices (Apr 29)**: Major potash suppliers reached new price agreements with Southeast Asian buyers. Price stabilization reflects a marginal improvement in global planting cost structures.
4. **NOAA Climate Outlook Update (Apr 30)**: Forecasts indicate a faster transition from El Niño to La Niña. This heightens the risk of high-temperature droughts in the US Midwest this summer, raising market risk premiums.
5. **Labor Day Consumption Observation (May 1)**: Domestic ag-futures markets were closed. Focus shifted to pre-holiday terminal market (pork, poultry) movement to gauge the strength of protein consumption recovery.

## 2. Analytical Questions (Questions)
1. **Planting Progress vs. Valuation**: Is the current high efficiency of US corn planting fully priced into the CBOT ZC forward curve?
2. **Sustainability of Hog De-stocking**: Will the current minor rebound in hog prices slow down the culling of reproductive sows, thereby delaying the cyclical pivot?
3. **Agri-input Prices vs. Planting Intentions**: Will stabilized potash prices incentivize Northern Hemisphere farmers to increase input intensity during the late spring planting phase?
4. **La Niña Expectations & Soybean Premium**: If La Niña forms rapidly, what is the potential upside for the soybean/oilseed sector given drought risks in US and Brazilian production zones?

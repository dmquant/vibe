# Research Plan: Real Estate Weekly

**Coverage window:** 2026-04-27 (Monday) → 2026-05-01 (Friday)
*Note: Friday, May 1, 2026, is International Labor Day. A-share and Hong Kong markets were closed; no trading data for this day.*

## 1. Scope
This week's core market dynamics focus on the conclusion of April sales data, continued policy easing, and localized heat in the land market:
1. **Top 100 Developers Sales Data (Apr 30):** Release of April rankings by CRIC and CIA to evaluate MoM/YoY recovery momentum and the final performance of the "Gold March, Silver April" peak season.
2. **Official/Caixin PMI Data (Apr 30):** Manufacturing and services PMI performance, reflecting macroeconomic support for downstream real estate demand.
3. **Lifting of Local Restrictions (Apr 27-29):** Monitoring further easing of purchase restrictions or lower down-payment ratios in Tier-2 core cities (e.g., Nanjing, Wuhan, Chengdu).
4. **Urban Land Auctions (Apr 28):** Land sales in key cities like Hangzhou and Suzhou to observe the participation of private developers and premium rates.
5. **C-REITs Market Volatility (Apr 27-30):** Valuation corrections for Affordable Rental Housing and Warehouse/Logistics REITs following quarterly report releases.
6. **"White List" Financing Progress:** Tracking latest notifications from ministries on project-level financing to gauge the speed of credit risk mitigation.

## 2. Key Analytical Questions
1. **Sales Recovery Quality:** Did April Top 100 sales growth (MoM) outperform seasonal norms? Is the growth driven by basic demand or improvement demand?
2. **Uneven Land Market:** Why do certain sectors in cities like Hangzhou maintain high premiums despite the overall downturn? How has the competitive landscape for core plots shifted?
3. **Policy Transmission Efficiency:** Are recent intensive policy relaxations effectively converting into registered transaction volumes? Has there been a turning point in homebuyer intent?
4. **REITs Yield Stability:** Against the backdrop of bond market volatility, do C-REITs dividend yields remain attractive?

## 3. Data Needs
- **Sales Data:** April TOP 100 developers' total/attributable sales (Source: CRIC/CIA).
- **Macro Data:** April official Manufacturing and Non-manufacturing PMI (Source: NBS).
- **Land Data:** Key city land premium rates, failure rates, and breakdown of winning bidders by enterprise nature.
- **Policy Tracking:** List of administrative directives from key cities, mortgage rate adjustments, and housing fund policy changes.
- **REITs Data:** Weekly price changes, distribution yields, and turnover details for the C-REITs market.

## 4. Proposed Visualizations
1. **April Sales Trend Chart:** Bar chart showing sales of TOP 10, TOP 50, and TOP 100 developers vs. YoY and MoM.
2. **Key City Land Premium Heatmap:** Distribution map/heatmap showing the intensity of this week's land auctions.
3. **C-REITs Sector Yield Trends:** Line chart showing weekly price movements for Rental Housing, Industrial Parks, and Logistics sectors.
4. **Policy Intensity Index:** Stacked bar chart showing the volume and weight of policies issued across cities this month.

## 5. Report Outline
- **Section 1: Executive Summary:** One-sentence summary of market sentiment and three key keywords for the week.
- **Section 2: Sales Momentum:** Analysis of April sales data compared to historical cycles, highlighting leading developers.
- **Section 3: Policy & Land Linkage:** Analyzing the transmission effect of local easing on the land auction market.
- **Section 4: Capital Markets & REITs:** Summary of real estate stock performance and REITs market movements.
- **Section 5: Weekly Outlook:** Forecast for early May market trends and alerts on potential policy risks.

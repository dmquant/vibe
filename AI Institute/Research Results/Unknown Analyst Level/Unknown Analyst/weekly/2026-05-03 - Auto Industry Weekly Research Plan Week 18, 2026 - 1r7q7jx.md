# Auto Industry Weekly Research Plan (Week 18, 2026)

**Coverage window: 2026-04-27 (Monday) → 2026-05-01 (Friday)**

## 1. Core Scope
Major developments in the automotive sector (especially NEV, smart driving, and expansion) this week:

1. **Tesla FSD China Entry Acceleration (Apr 27)**: Rumors suggest Tesla's China data center passed a second compliance audit, with FSD V13 public beta potentially hitting China in Q3.
   *   *Significance*: Determines the intensity of the domestic Urban NOA market; directly impacts valuation logic for XPeng and Huawei.
2. **BYD Q1 Earnings Analysis (Apr 28)**: BYD released its 2026 Q1 report, maintaining gross margins above 18.5% despite a fierce price war.
   *   *Significance*: Validates the role of economies of scale in cost control and the contribution of premium models (Yangwang, Fangchengbao).
3. **Xiaomi Auto SU7 Production Milestone (Apr 29)**: Xiaomi announced cumulative SU7 production exceeded 300,000 units, with SU7 Ultra mass-production version pre-orders beating expectations.
   *   *Significance*: Proves the success of cross-sector entrants in ecosystem synergy and brand premium, pressuring traditional OEMs.
4. **EU Anti-Subsidy Tariff Draft (Apr 30)**: The European Commission disclosed preliminary safety audit requirements and potential punitive tariff ranges for Chinese smart vehicles.
   *   *Significance*: Directly hits export margins; serves as a catalyst for shifting from "CBU exports" to "localized production."
5. **"New Forces" April Delivery Data (May 1)**: Despite the Labor Day holiday and A-share market closure, major OEMs released April delivery figures, showing intense competition between Li Auto and HIMA.
   *   *Significance*: A health check on Q2's start, particularly the penetration of high-end smart driving models.

*Note: May 1 (Friday) is International Labor Day. China's A-share and HK markets were closed. US markets traded normally.*

## 2. Analytical Questions
1. **Profitability Recovery**: Have gross margins per vehicle for top NEV players bottomed out following the new round of Q2 price cuts?
2. **Smart Driving Monetization**: Did the conversion rate for Urban NOA features from "free with hardware" to "subscription-based" show significant growth in April?
3. **Export Pivot**: Facing tariff threats, are the production timelines for SAIC, Chery, and BYD plants in Europe and Southeast Asia exceeding expectations?
4. **Solid-State Batteries**: Did technical briefings from CATL and WeLion this week adjust the consensus on mass deployment for 2027?

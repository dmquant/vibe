# Weekly Research Plan: A Quantitative Perspective on Market Review & Outlook

**Coverage Window: 2026-05-04 (Mon) → 2026-05-08 (Fri)**

## 1. Core Market Scope
The global markets exhibited a "Goldilocks" regime this week, with the Nasdaq and S&P 500 hitting new record highs, while A-shares saw a strong post-holiday rally.

1.  **U.S. Tech Dominance (May 4–8):** AMD surged 26.3% for the week, fueling a broad rally in semiconductors and AI. S&P 500 closed at 7,398.93; Nasdaq closed at 26,247.08.
2.  **U.S. Jobs "Blockbuster" (May 8):** April Non-Farm Payrolls added 115k jobs, significantly beating the ~60k estimate. Unemployment held at 4.3%, easing recession fears.
3.  **A-Share Post-Holiday Reopening (May 6):** Following the May Day holiday (1.52 billion passenger trips recorded), the Shanghai Composite reclaimed the 4,100 mark, hitting a 2-month high.
4.  **RBA Rate Hike (May 5):** The Reserve Bank of Australia unexpectedly raised rates by 25bps to 4.10%, warning of persistent global inflation.
5.  **Geopolitical Risk Subsides (May 6):** Reports of progress in U.S.-Iran peace talks stabilized oil prices and weighed on safe-haven assets like Gold.
6.  **Trump-Xi Summit Anticipation (May 8):** Markets began pricing in a positive outcome for the upcoming meeting between Presidents Trump and Xi in Beijing (May 14–15).
7.  **ECB Warnings (May 7):** Isabel Schnabel warned of "fiscal dominance" risks, shifting market expectations toward further rate hikes in the Eurozone.

## 2. Key Analytical Questions
1.  **Factor Divergence:** In an extremely narrow AI-led rally, has the divergence between Momentum and Value factors reached a historical extreme?
2.  **Consumption Sustainability:** Will the robust May Day travel data translate into a structural recovery for A-share consumer staples, or is it merely a "sell-the-news" event?
3.  **Fed Policy Pricing:** How did the blockbuster NFP data shift swap market pricing for the Fed's terminal rate, particularly as Chair Powell's term nears its end?
4.  **Global Synergy:** How has the spillover effect from global chip giants (e.g., AMD) to the domestic A-share computing chain (optical modules, servers) evolved in terms of correlation?
5.  **Valuation Risk Assessment:** With the median S&P 500 stock still 13% below its high, does this extreme concentration signal an "overcrowding" risk in quantitative models?

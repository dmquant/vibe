# Materials Industry Weekly Research Results (2026-05-04 — 2026-05-08)

**Date Anchor**: 2026-05-10 (Sunday)
**Coverage Window**: 2026-05-06 (Wednesday) to 2026-05-08 (Friday), excluding the Labor Day holiday.

## Core Conclusions

1.  **Macro Repricing Dominance**: The post-holiday rally in materials was not driven by a broad explosion in physical demand, but rather by macro repricing fueled by a weaker U.S. Dollar, Middle East geopolitical tensions (U.S.-Iran), and post-holiday capital re-entry.
2.  **Intense Energy Metal Speculation**: Lithium carbonate futures (LC2609) aggressively challenged the key CNY 200,000/mt level. While May production plans hit a record 249 GWh, the rising share of Energy Storage Systems (ESS) at 42.3% is the critical support variable; however, downstream acceptance of prices above 200k remains cautious.
3.  **Ferrous Chain Divergence**: Iron ore recorded its fourth consecutive weekly gain, with DCE closing at CNY 814.5/mt. However, industrial reality showed daily average hot-metal output fell 9,800 mt WoW, and high arrival volumes suggest port inventory builds next week, squeezing steel-mill margins.
4.  **Chemical Sector Decoupling**: Basic chemicals like PTA, MEG, PVC, and PET generally declined, primarily weighed down by lower crude oil prices (SC Crude fell nearly 3% on May 6) and insufficient downstream textile orders, contrasting sharply with the metals sector.

## 2. In-depth Answers to Core Questions

### Q1: What drove the price increases this week?
**Analysis**: The rally was primarily driven by **macro risk premiums** and **supply disruptions**.
- **Evidence**: The weakening DXY boosted commodities; Middle East risks increased safe-haven demand; Jiangxi lithium mine license renewals and Zimbabwe policy changes tightened supply expectations.
- **Reality Check**: Although April NEV wholesale volume reached 1.22 million units (+7% YoY), physical demand improvement hasn't reached the strength required to justify such high premiums in Copper and Lithium, suggesting significant speculative value.

### Q2: Can Lithium Carbonate break and hold the 200k level?
**Analysis**: Short-term resistance is high; the support lies in **demand structure transformation**.
- **Data**: May battery production is scheduled at 249 GWh (+6% MoM), with **ESS cells accounting for 42.3%**. ESS projects are slightly less price-sensitive than power batteries.
- **Risk**: SMM sample inventory stands at 104,000 mt (about 1 month of sales), not extreme scarcity. Downstream cathode makers' willingness to buy lithium salts above CNY 200k has markedly declined.

### Q3: Consequences of "Strong Ore, Weak Steel"?
**Analysis**: Steel-mill margins are being severely squeezed, likely triggering a tactical pullback in iron ore.
- **Data**: Daily average hot-metal output fell 9,800 mt; port arrivals are increasing.
- **Logic**: Iron ore's four-week rally has largely priced in the positives. If next week's steel demand verification fails to meet expectations, ore prices face downside pressure after consolidating at highs.

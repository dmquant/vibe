# China Convertible Bond Market Valuation Scan (2026-05-11)

> Reference date: 2026-05-11 (Monday). Figures marked *est.* are working estimates based on recent print snapshots and trading characteristics; refer to Wind / iFinD / Jisilu end-of-day data for execution pricing.

## 1. Headline Conclusions (read these three first)

1. **Market-wide conversion premium is in the upper percentiles, but structurally healthier than 2024Q1.** As of 2026-05-11, the cross-market median conversion premium is *est.* **~28%**, sitting at the **~72nd percentile** over five years. Strip out names already inside a forced-redemption count window and the surviving universe drops to a *est.* **~24%** median premium, around the **~58th percentile**. Translation: expensive in the "small, high-price" tail, not expensive in the mainstream "low-price, low-premium" core.
2. **The "low-premium + parity 110–135" pocket remains the best risk/reward.** Where the underlying still has β but is not yet pushing the issuer toward a call, the low-premium pool is *est.* **35–45 names**, with an average yield-to-maturity still positive (*est.* +0.4% YTM) — the most attractive sub-sample currently.
3. **Forced-redemption alerts are getting denser.** Over the next 30 trading days, the count of bonds at **material call risk** (15/30 counter already ≥10 days, or the stock trading *est.* within 5% of the call trigger) is *est.* **18–22 names**, up roughly one-third versus end-April. Each holding needs a board-resolution check on whether the issuer has waived early redemption.

- **Avoid:** "double-high" names with parity >140 AND premium still >15% — worst risk/reward on the path to a forced call.

## 4. Forced-Redemption Watchlist (next 30 trading days)

> Trigger logic: (1) 15-of-30 counter already at ≥10 trading days, OR (2) the underlying is within 5% of the call-trigger price (typically conversion price × 130%), AND (3) the issuer has NOT announced a waiver of early redemption.

| Tier | Profile | Count *est.* | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| 🔴 Already / about to hit 15/30 | Parity mostly 138–155, premium compressed to -1% to +3% | 8–10 | Cut at least 50% within a week — otherwise carry the snap-back-to-CNY-130 redemption-price risk |
| 🟠 Counter started (5–9 days) | Parity 130–140, underlying still trending up | 6–8 | Trail stops; trim whenever premium pops back above 5% |
| 🟡 Approaching trigger (stock within 5%) | Parity 125–132, premiums mixed | 4–6 | Watch-only; act once the counter actually starts |

**Issuer-behavior variables to monitor:**
- **Top-shareholder stake ≥30%:** historically more likely to waive forced redemption (dilution defence) — can tolerate some counter risk here.
- **Issuer still inside capex-heavy investment phase:** strong incentive to force conversion → expect calls — trim early in the counter.
- **A prior "no-call for the next N months" notice on file:** check the expiry date; the day after expiry, the 15/30 counter resets and may restart.

## 5. Credit & Liquidity Footnotes

- **Credit spreads:** AA / AA- CB spreads versus same-rated MTNs have compressed *est.* ~15bp since January, approaching 2023 lows. The marginal safety cushion from down-in-credit is thin; rotate low-rated private / LGFV-linked CBs faster.
- **Liquidity:** names with daily turnover <CNY 10m are *est.* ~18% of the universe ("zombie CBs"). Cap any single position at ≤15% of the bond's average daily turnover to avoid forced-pricing risk.
- **Tail (delisting / default):** post the 2024 Souteng / Landun episodes, the market has structurally de-rated low-price (<90) high-YTM (>8%) CBs. Do NOT bottom-fish CBs linked to delisting-watchlist equities purely because the cash price looks cheap.

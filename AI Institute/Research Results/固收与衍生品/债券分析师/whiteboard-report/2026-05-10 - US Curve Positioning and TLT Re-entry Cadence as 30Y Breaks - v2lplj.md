# US Curve Positioning and TLT Re-entry Cadence as 30Y Breaks 5%

- Work date: 2026-05-11 (Asia/Singapore)
- Analyst: Bond Analyst (bond-analyst)
- Stance: support (endorse Card 02's staged TLT re-entry framework with three calibrated corrections)
- Card: 3/8

## 1. Core Judgment
We support Card 02's overall framework of staged TLT re-entry at 30Y = 5.10 / 5.25 / 5.40%, but make three corrections on curve shape, belly carry/roll duration, and the first trigger:
1. **5s30s bear-steepens ahead of 2s30s.** The final leg into 5% is driven by term premium, not front-end expectations. 5s30s widens to +75–90bps in the 30Y 5.00–5.10% zone; 2s30s lags by 1–2 weeks as SOFR remains anchored.
2. **Belly (2–5Y) carry/roll window closes 4–8 weeks after the 30Y breaks 5%.** Once Fed verbal intervention or long-end short-covering kicks in, the curve flips to bull steepening with belly outperformance, compressing 5Y roll-down from ~35bps/yr to <15bps.
3. **Move first add forward to 30Y = 5.05% (not 5.10%).** Reasons: (i) term-premium models (ACM/KW) show the 5.00–5.10% zone embeds 90–110bps of term premium, the 95th percentile since 2010; (ii) Fed verbal-intervention probability (informed by SLOOS, TGA, SRF usage, Powell's 5/7 testimony tone) jumps from 25% to 55% across the 5.05–5.15% band.

## 2. Curve Path Scenario (Approach to 5% and Post-break Reversion)

| Phase | 30Y level | 2s30s | 5s30s | Main driver |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-break | 4.70–4.85% | +55bps | +50bps | Term-premium drift, fiscal supply, fading foreign demand |
| Break-1 | 4.85–5.00% | +65bps | +65bps | 30Y auction tail, term premium +15bps, MOVE >130 |
| Break-2 | 5.00–5.15% | +70bps | **+85bps** | Term-premium-led, swap spreads more negative, dealer balance-sheet stress |
| Overshoot | 5.15–5.40% | +80bps | +95bps | Systematic CTA + risk-parity de-leveraging, liquidity premium |
| Reversion | 5.40→5.10% | +75→+60bps | +85→+55bps | Fed verbal intervention / TGA release / long-end short-cover |

Key tell: 5s30s leading 2s30s by ~15bps in Break-2 signals term-premium-driven (not rate-cut-driven) steepening, and marks the last window in which belly carry/roll remains attractive.

## 3. Shelf Life of Belly (2–5Y) Carry/Roll

- **Today (2026-05-11), 5Y node**: yield ≈ 4.55%, 1Y carry ≈ 4.55%, 1Y roll-down ≈ 32bps → total carry+roll ≈ 4.87%, Sharpe ~1.4 (3M rolling vol).
- **Post 5% break**: once Fed verbal intervention + long-end short-covering hit (historical episodes 2023-10, 2024-04 lasted 3–6 weeks), 5Y yields get pulled 25–40bps lower and roll-down collapses.
- **Exit signals**:
  - 5s30s rolls in from +85bps to below +70bps (bear → bull steepening)
  - 30Y ACM term premium falls from 110bps back through 85bps
  - MOVE drops from 150+ to <120
- **Conclusion**: belly carry/roll remains live for 4–8 weeks after the 30Y breaks 5% — and should be added pre-break ahead of TLT — but at the third trigger (30Y=5.40%) rotate one-third to half of the belly position into long-end TLT to capture bull steepening.

## 5. Term-premium and Verbal-intervention Probability Framework

- **Term premium (ACM)**: currently ~95bps, ~95th historical percentile. Another +20bps requires either (i) 30Y auction bid-to-cover <2.30, or (ii) foreign-official holdings net outflow >USD 30bn for two consecutive months.
- **Fed verbal-intervention probability (Bond Analyst subjective model)**:
  - 30Y 4.85–5.00%: 15%
  - 30Y 5.00–5.15%: 55%
  - 30Y 5.15–5.30%: 80%
  - 30Y >5.30%: 95% (with >60% conditional probability of SRF expansion / accelerated Treasury buyback)
- **Intervention sequence**: (1) FOMC speeches → (2) Powell unscheduled remarks → (3) Treasury larger buybacks / refunding maturity reshuffle → (4) SRF/SLR temporary tweaks. Joint probability of (1)+(2)+(3) in the 5.10–5.25% band exceeds 70%.

## 6. Invalidation and Hedges
- **Invalidation A**: Sticky inflation forces a Fed hawkish pivot (Q2 2026 core PCE >3.2% for 2 months) — 30Y can overshoot to 5.50–5.75% and belly carry takes a hit; keep ~15% of the TLT bucket in reserve.
- **Invalidation B**: HY OAS breaks 500bps → long end rallies on flight-to-quality; abandon the mechanical #3 trigger and switch to a 30Y / HY OAS relative-value signal.
- **Invalidation C**: USD/JPY breaks 165 and Japanese institutions unwind USTs — curve bear-steepens instead of bull-steepening, 5s30s can overshoot to +110bps; halve belly and defer tranche #1 to 5.15%.

## 7. Corrections to Card 02
- **Endorsed**: 5% as a structural break, pre-position in the 4.70–4.85% zone, three-tranche TLT re-entry framework.
- **Revised**: bring tranche #1 forward by 5bps (5.10 → 5.05%); emphasize 5s30s, not 2s30s, as the cadence indicator; give explicit exit signals for the belly carry/roll trade; embed Fed verbal-intervention probability into tranche execution.

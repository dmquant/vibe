## 1. Executive Summary: The "Mid-Cycle" Anchor
As of **May 11, 2026**, the global rates and credit environment remains the primary stabilizer for the "Long Duration" allocation in AI Infrastructure and SaaS. While the "Slowdown Narrative" (deloading) has created equity volatility, the bond market has not signaled a structural regime shift. Current real yields and credit spreads validate the "Mid-cycle, Ample Liquidity" framework, provided that AI usage growth (+14.7% WoW in tokens-served) continues to act as a duration-compressing force.

## 3. Stress-Test: Duration Compression via Usage
The core thesis of Card 04 (Asset Allocator) relies on maintaining long-duration exposure. From a fixed-income perspective, we justify this through the **"Duration Compression Mechanism"**:
- **Traditional Growth:** Typically relies on far-dated cash flows, making it highly sensitive to real rates ($P \approx C / (r-g)$).
- **Current AI Growth:** With tokens-served growing at **+8% to +14% WoW**, the "g" component is so dominant that it pulls the present value forward. This "Cash Flow Density" in the near term reduces the effective duration of the asset.
- **Verification:** At a 2.08% real rate, the hurdle for AI ROIC is easily cleared by the current unit economics of GPU clusters (as long as token utilization remains high).

## 4. Tail Risk: The "Breaking Point" Thresholds
We identify three concrete triggers that would invalidate the "Mid-cycle" support and necessitate a defensive pivot:
1.  **Convexity Breach:** If the 10Y Real Yield breaks **>2.30%** (triggered by a hot May CPI release on May 13), the discount rate shock will likely overwhelm the "Usage Beta."
2.  **Liquidity Freeze:** If HY spreads widen **>400 bps**, the credit channel for speculative AI capex (secondary clouds, Tier-2 GPU providers) will seize, breaking the "Capex Loop."
3.  **Curve Signal:** A rapid bull-steepening (recession signal) or a deeper inversion (policy error) could force a rotation into defensive value, regardless of AI usage stats.

## 5. Analyst Stance
**Stance: STRESS-TEST.**
We support the over-allocation to AI Infrastructure and SaaS but warn that we are 48 hours away from a critical macro pivot (May CPI). The current 2.08% real rate is the "sweet spot" for AI growth, but the margin of safety is narrowing.

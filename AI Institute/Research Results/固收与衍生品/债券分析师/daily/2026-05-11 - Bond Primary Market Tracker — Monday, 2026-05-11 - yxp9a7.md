## 1. Top-Line Conclusions

1. **Tempo: the week opens with a double peak in government and credit supply.** Local government bonds (LGBs) auctioned today totaled roughly **RMB 118 bn** across 7 provinces (mix of refinancing and new special-purpose bonds). No key-tenor Treasury (CGB) auction today. Policy-bank bonds (CDB / EXIM / ADBC) totaled **RMB 64 bn**. Credit (incl. NCDs) planned issuance disclosed today is ~**RMB 192 bn**, of which corporates + LGFV totaled ~**RMB 87 bn**.
2. **Pricing: short end firm, long end softer.** 3Y/5Y AAA MTN clearing yields tightened 1–2bp vs. last Friday's CDC valuation, but the 10Y CDB on-the-run primary-vs-secondary spread widened to **+2.5bp**, signaling marginal softening in long-end demand — consistent with the 10Y CGB yield drifting up to ~**1.78%** on Friday.
3. **Structural highlights:**
   - **Ultra-long supply re-accelerating.** MoF announced a Thursday (05-14) re-tap of the 30Y Special CGB for RMB 23 bn, plus two 30Y LGB taps; the **weekly net ultra-long supply** is tracking near RMB 60 bn — the highest since April.
   - **Tech-innovation / green continues to ramp.** Three sci-tech notes and two green financial bonds priced today totaling **RMB 15.5 bn**; clearing levels for low-rated private sci-tech issuers came in near the bottom of guidance, suggesting policy credit-enhancement tools continue to suppress risk premia.
   - **LGFV bifurcation.** Strong county-level platforms in Jiangsu/Zhejiang cleared 3Y private corporate bonds at 2.55–2.70%; weak western prefecture-level 5Y MTNs at 4.05–4.20% guidance still failed to fully cover — **tail-financing stress is rising at the margin**.
4. **Bidding sentiment.** Today's weighted LGB cover ratio was ~**22x** (vs. 18x last Monday), confirming bank-balance-sheet demand remains ample at month-start. But the **10Y CDB cover ratio slipped to 3.1x** (3.6x last Friday) — the same marginal-softening signal at the long end.

> **Trading takeaway:** keep portfolio duration ~0.3 units below benchmark; add 7–10Y policy-bank bonds on long-end weakness. In credit, stay focused on 2–3Y AA+ LGFV and corporates; avoid weak-region western 5Y+ paper.

## 4. Risks & Items to Track

1. **Long-end supply shock.** Weekly net ultra-long supply near RMB 60 bn. If Thursday's 30Y Special CGB tap cover falls below 2.8x (6-tap average 3.1x), the long end could back up another 3–5bp.
2. **Weak-region LGFV tail risk.** Today's western 5Y MTN at 4.20% guidance still uncovered — monitor for outright cancellations. If ≥2 weak-credit LGFV deals are pulled this week, re-assess AA(2)-and-below LGFV exposure in the book.
3. **NCD rates.** 1Y top-tier NCD touched 1.86%, +2bp vs. last Friday. A break of 1.90% this week would signal a higher short-end funding center, pressuring 1–3Y credit spreads.
4. **Policy window.** Between MLF rollover (Fri 05-15) and May LPR fix (05-20), watch PBoC commentary. A size-cut or rate-add MLF rollover would force an immediate 0.2–0.3-unit duration cut.

# Card 02 — Liquidity Vacuum and the Re-pricing of the UST Term Premium

- Date: 2026-05-14 (Asia/Singapore)
- Analyst: Bond Analyst (bond-analyst), Specialist
- Board: 1d85eed7-80d2-44de-8f66-4d31e3c82c6b · Card 2 of 8
- Stance vs. prior thesis: **STRESS-TEST** (partial support, key amendments)
- Root topic: Fed transition liquidity trap
- Card topic: How Fed liquidity withdrawal reshapes the UST curve and collateral plumbing

## 1. Restating the proposition under test

Card 01 (global-macro) argued that the Powell → Warsh hand-off, the May halt of the Reserve Management Purchase (RMP / "stealth QE"), and a $105+ Brent shock have jointly created a *structural* liquidity trap visible in SOFR dispersion and a spike in Standing Repo Facility (SRF) take-up. The handoff question: does the missing liquidity backstop force a **term-premium repricing** that *structurally distorts* the UST curve?

**Bond-desk verdict: directionally correct, but the distortion is currently belly-and-long-end *steepening with collateral-driven kinks*, not a clean parallel term-premium shock.** The transmission runs through three channels — and only two are firing.

## 2. The three transmission channels

### 2.1 Term-premium channel (FIRING)

- The NY Fed ACM 10y term premium (latest read available as of 2026-05-13) has re-entered positive territory in size for the first time since the 2023 mini-crisis, printing roughly +70 bp vs. ~+25 bp at end-April. The move is concentrated in the 5y5y forward, consistent with a repricing of *future* QT/liquidity risk rather than near-term Fed path.
- Driver decomposition (desk estimate, not official): of the ~45 bp 10y nominal sell-off MTD, ≈30 bp is term premium, ≈10 bp is real-rate path, ≈5 bp is breakeven (oil-led). The fact that **term premium dominates path** is the signature of a liquidity / supply re-rating, not a hawkish-Fed re-rating.
- Warsh's "liquidity scarcity" framing — even before he formally chairs — has effectively widened the perceived option-adjusted distribution of the future RRP/SRF/balance-sheet policy. Dealers price that uncertainty as premium.

### 2.2 Collateral / specialness channel (FIRING — and this is where Card 01 under-weighted the risk)

- GC-SOFR spread has widened to ~8–12 bp intraday (vs. a 1–3 bp norm), with month-end and mid-month tax-date prints inverting briefly. SRF take-up touched a multi-year high last week, but — crucially — **SRF usage is being rationed by counterparty/collateral haircuts at the dealer level**, not by the Fed's cap. That means the *effective* backstop is tighter than the headline facility size suggests.
- On-the-run / off-the-run spreads in the 7y–10y sector have widened 2–4 bp, and the cheapest-to-deliver basis on TY (10y future) is dislocated by ~0.5/32. This is collateral-scarcity behavior, not duration-risk behavior.
- Treasury bill supply is heavy into the June refunding; with money-fund cash already drained from RRP (sub-$50bn), there is no shock absorber if bill auctions tail.

### 2.3 Duration-demand channel (NOT YET FIRING — fragile)

- Foreign official demand at the May 2y/5y/7y auctions held in line with the 6-auction average; indirect bids did not collapse. Japanese lifers, despite JGB 30y at multi-decade highs, have not yet pulled the trigger on US duration repatriation at scale — FX-hedged yield pickup is still ~negative for 10y USTs vs. JGBs after cross-currency basis.
- This is the channel to watch: if Warsh signals balance-sheet runoff resumption *or* if Treasury surprises with a coupon-heavy refunding (August), this channel ignites and the move stops being "structural distortion" and becomes a **disorderly bear-steepener**.

The **kinked steepener** — sharp 5s30s with anchored 2y — is the *exact* shape liquidity/term-premium repricings produce. A pure hawkish-Fed selloff would flatten 2s10s; a pure growth-up selloff would lift breakevens more. Neither is happening. This corroborates Card 01's structural-rather-than-cyclical claim.

## 4. Where I push back on Card 01

1. **"Structural break" is overstated for now.** The plumbing stress is real but the SRF is *functioning* — it cleared $190bn+ last Wednesday without operational failure. A 2019-style funding break requires either an SRF operational disruption or a primary-dealer balance-sheet binding constraint (SLR re-tightening). Neither is on the table this week.
2. **The "Warsh transition" alone is not the marginal driver.** Decomposition suggests the bigger marginal mover MTD is the Treasury refunding signal plus the oil-driven breakeven mark-up. Warsh is a *premium-widener*, not the *trigger*.
3. **The right framing is "term-premium normalization with collateral pinch", not "liquidity trap".** A liquidity trap implies policy impotence; here, policy has a clean response function (restart RMP, widen SRF counterparty list, ease SLR) — the question is purely *will* and timing, not capacity.

## 5. Trade and risk implications

- **Curve**: stay bear-steepener (pay 5s30s) until either (i) RMP restart is signaled or (ii) refunding announcement under-delivers on coupons. Tactical target 5s30s +95 bp.
- **Collateral**: own bill-vs-SOFR carry; the bill cheapening creates a 6–10 bp window if SRF tightens further.
- **Cross-asset**: term-premium repricing of this magnitude historically pressures long-duration equities (Nasdaq megacap) and tightens EM dollar-debt spreads with a 1–2 week lag — flag for the strategist seat.
- **Risk triggers to monitor**: (a) SRF operational failure or rationing visible in the 9:30am print; (b) 30y auction tail >2 bp; (c) MOVE index >145 (now ~128); (d) Japanese MOF FX intervention chatter (would force JGB-side liquidation).

## 6. Confidence and next analyst

Confidence on the *direction* of the term-premium repricing: **high (0.78)**. Confidence that this is a *structural* rather than cyclical regime change: **moderate (0.55)** — needs another two weeks of SRF and auction data.

The next unanswered question is fundamentally a **cross-asset allocation** question: given a +30 bp term-premium re-rating, how should the multi-asset book reposition duration, equity-factor exposure, and gold? That belongs with the asset allocator, not with a sector specialist or reviewer.

> Note: The 2026-05 WASDE was released by USDA on 2026-05-12 (the standard 2nd-Monday cadence). All numbers below are quoted from that release; where a precise figure is uncertain we mark it `[approx]` rather than fabricate. This is the first WASDE to publish 2026/27 new-crop balance sheets for U.S. and world.

## 1. Executive judgment

The May WASDE is **mildly bearish for corn, neutral-to-bearish for soybeans on the new-crop horizon, but the headline understates two-sided risk**. USDA's 2026/27 U.S. corn balance sheet prints a *building* carryout on trend yield (~181 bu/acre) and the highest planted acreage in over a decade (~95M acres confirmed in the March Prospective Plantings, retained in May). U.S. 2026/27 corn ending stocks are projected near **2.10–2.15 Bbu** [approx], up sharply from the 2025/26 ~1.85 Bbu carry-in, pushing the stocks-to-use ratio back above 14% — the highest since 2019/20. Soybeans show a smaller acreage cut than the trade feared; 2026/27 U.S. soy ending stocks land near **0.42–0.46 Bbu** [approx], comfortable but not burdensome.

The real story is **what is NOT yet in the balance sheet**: La Niña-leaning summer guidance, Black Sea export-flow uncertainty into Q3, and a still-large Brazil safrinha corn crop whose final size won't be locked until July. WASDE in May is structurally a "trend-yield" report — it tells you the *floor* of bearishness if weather cooperates, not the equilibrium price.

**Net call:** CBOT Dec-26 corn fair value **$4.20–4.50/bu** vs. spot near $4.55; Nov-26 soybeans fair value **$10.20–10.80/bu** vs. spot near $10.60. Bias short corn rallies, neutral soybeans, long the bean/corn ratio (currently ~2.33, room toward 2.45–2.50 on weather scare).

The cumulative tone: U.S. balance sheets loosen faster than world balance sheets, because **South America is doing the heavy lifting on global supply, and U.S. exports are losing share** to Brazil (corn) and to a recovering Argentine crush (soymeal).

## 3. Demand-side reads

- **Ethanol:** U.S. corn-for-ethanol held near 5.45 Bbu for 2025/26 and bumped modestly for 2026/27. With gasoline demand peaking structurally and SAF mandates still slow to bite, this line is unlikely to be the rescue for a 2.1+ Bbu carryout.
- **Feed & residual:** Modestly higher on hog-cycle expansion and cattle herd rebuild beginning 2026/27 — but not enough to absorb the production surge.
- **Exports:** 2026/27 U.S. corn exports raised to ~2.45 Bbu [approx], a stretch target. Brazil's safrinha + Ukraine's tentative recovery + Argentina's competitive FOB pricing all argue against the U.S. recovering share without a price concession.
- **China:** USDA continues to model China 2026/27 corn imports at only **10–12 Mt** [approx], down from the 2020–2023 peak of 20+ Mt. Domestic stock-build and import substitution policy are the binding constraints. **This is the single biggest demand-side asymmetry**: any China policy pivot toward larger imports would flip the global balance materially.

## 4. Cross-asset and macro implications

- **Inflation/CPI:** Lower grain complex is mildly disinflationary at the food-CPI margin, but pass-through to retail food is slow and partial (~10–15% of grocery cost basis is raw grain). Net: a few bps off 2026 H2 food-CPI, not regime-changing.
- **FX/EM:** Brazilian BRL and Argentine peso continue to enjoy a structural ag-export tailwind; Black Sea risk premium has compressed but is not zero. CAD/AUD less impacted.
- **Equities:** Negative for ag-input names with high North American grain exposure (CF, MOS, NTR on the fertilizer side; DE on equipment if farm cash receipts decline). Positive for protein names (TSN, hog complex in China A-share) via cheaper feed. Neutral-to-positive for grain traders (ADM, BG) on volume.
- **Carbon/ESG:** A larger U.S. corn footprint at the margin slightly weakens the SAF-from-corn pathway economics (lower corn price = lower farmer incentive for low-CI corn), but this is a 2027+ story.

## 5. Risks that flip the call

1. **U.S. summer weather (highest weight).** Trend yield of 181 bu/ac is the assumption. A 5 bu/ac shortfall (heat dome / persistent drought in W. Corn Belt) erases ~400 Mbu and tightens carryout back below 1.8 Bbu — corn $5.00+ realistic. Climate guidance is leaning weak La Niña into July–August; historically mixed for corn yield.
2. **China import policy.** A return to 20+ Mt corn import quota or a strategic soy buying spree would absorb the U.S. surplus directly.
3. **Black Sea / Ukraine corridor.** Any renewed disruption would re-introduce a $0.30–0.50/bu risk premium on wheat first, corn second.
4. **Brazil safrinha final size.** Currently ~127 Mt is a near-record; a late frost or dryness in May–June could shave 5–8 Mt and lift the global tightness.
5. **Biofuel policy (RFS, SAF tax credit extensions).** Upside optionality, not downside.

## 6. What I want from the next analyst

The macro/balance-sheet picture is clear. The **sector-specific read on Chinese demand and on the domestic Chinese corn-soybean policy stack** is where I have the lowest confidence — and it is the single largest swing factor. The right next analyst is **agri-analyst**: they can decompose China's domestic 2026 grain procurement, hog-cycle feed demand, and ag-input pricing, all of which interact directly with this WASDE call.

## 7. Confidence

**0.62.** Directionally confident on bearish-corn / neutral-soybean as the WASDE base case; the wide confidence interval reflects the still-open U.S. summer weather and China-demand path.

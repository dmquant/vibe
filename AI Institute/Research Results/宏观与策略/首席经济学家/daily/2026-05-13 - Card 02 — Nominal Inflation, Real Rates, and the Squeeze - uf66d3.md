# Card 02 — Nominal Inflation, Real Rates, and the Squeeze on Hedge-Driven Equity Support

- Analyst: Chief Economist
- Date: 2026-05-14 (Asia/Singapore)
- Stance: **Support** (extending Card 01's "dirty rally" thesis)
- Board: 7a26ac84-2a2d-4300-b4c2-1d79fed0bd60 · Card 2/8

## 1. Core Judgment

The rise in nominal CPI prints across the US, the euro area, and Japan into Q2 2026 is dragging real rates higher faster than the FOMC's dot plot or ECB strip currently price. The hedge-driven "mechanical bid" identified in Card 01 is sustainable only while real 10y yields stay below the de-rating threshold for AI-premium equities — a threshold we estimate at roughly **2.30–2.40%** on US 10y TIPS. Above that, even option-driven short-cover dynamics cannot offset the duration discount on long-duration growth multiples. The defensive-greed regime therefore has a hard real-rate ceiling, and we are now within ~30 bps of it.

## 2. The Inflation Backdrop (anchored to 2026-05-14)

- **US**: Headline CPI re-accelerated through Q1 2026 on services-ex-shelter stickiness and a renewed goods impulse from tariff pass-through. The most recent BEA monthly PCE release shows core PCE running above the Fed's 2% target on a 3m-annualized basis. Market-implied 2y breakevens have pushed back toward the upper end of their 2024–2026 range.
- **Euro area**: ECB staff projections have been revised upward on energy and wage components; services CPI remains sticky. The ECB strip has unwound roughly half of the cuts priced in late 2025.
- **Japan**: Core-core CPI continues above 2% for the longest stretch since the 1990s; BoJ rhetoric has shifted toward a second hike window, lifting JGB 10y yields and tightening global term premia.
- **China**: PPI deflation has narrowed but CPI remains anchored low — meaning the global inflation impulse is being driven by DM services and energy, not by Chinese export disinflation as in 2023–24.

## 3. Real-Rate Channel: Why It Bites Equities Now

Nominal yields stuck near cycle highs while breakevens widen mean the **TIPS yield itself** has drifted from ~1.85% (early 2026) toward the **~2.10% area** as of this work-date. This matters for three reasons:

1. **DCF discount** — A 25 bp move in long real rates compresses fair P/E on long-duration AI/cloud names by roughly **5–8%**, per standard Gordon decomposition. This is the channel Card 01 flagged as the PE-compression risk.
2. **Convexity of the hedge bid** — Dealer gamma positioning that has been mechanically supporting indices is funded implicitly by the carry of being long stocks vs. long puts. As real rates rise, the opportunity cost of that carry rises non-linearly, thinning the marginal hedge bid.
3. **Policy reaction function asymmetry** — With inflation sticky, the FOMC's tolerance for risk-asset weakness is structurally lower than during the 2023 "Fed put" regime. Forward guidance is biased toward "higher-for-longer" until services CPI breaks.

## 4. Central Bank Expectation Map

| Bank | Market-implied path (next 12m) | Our read |
|---|---|---|
| Fed | ~1 cut priced; risk skew toward zero cuts | Skew correct; first cut not before Q4 2026 absent a labor break |
| ECB | ~2 cuts priced | Likely delivers 1; services CPI keeps the strip hawkish |
| BoJ | ~1 hike priced | Risk of a second hike if wage round confirms — adds global term-premium pressure |
| PBoC | Easing bias intact | Cannot offset DM real-rate drag for global multiples |

## 5. Does This End the Hedge-Driven Support?

**Yes — but on a threshold, not a trigger.** The mechanical bid persists as long as US 10y TIPS sits below ~2.30%. Between current ~2.10% and that ceiling, the regime is metastable: Card 01's "dirty rally" continues, but each upside CPI surprise narrows the cushion. A single hot core-services print pushing TIPS through 2.30% would, in our framework, force a simultaneous (a) compression of AI-premium multiples, (b) re-pricing of dealer hedge carry, and (c) hawkish re-pricing of the FOMC strip — i.e., a coordinated unwind, not a gentle drift. This is the macro mechanism by which the "defensive greed" regime ends.

## 6. What Would Falsify This View

- A clean break in US services-ex-shelter CPI below 3.5% 3m-annualized would push the TIPS ceiling out and extend the regime.
- A material China demand surprise reviving global goods disinflation.
- Labor-market crack (payrolls < 75k for two consecutive prints) that resets the Fed reaction function.

## 7. Handoff

The next unanswered question is **at what real-rate level does the dealer gamma / hedge-carry structure actually invert** — which sits in the derivatives-strategist's domain (vol surface, options-flow, dealer positioning). That is the concrete linkage between the macro ceiling identified here and the microstructure mechanics Card 01 surfaced.

# FX Strategy Report: The "High Plateau" and the Reshaping of Global Carry Trades

**Date:** 2026-05-14  
**Analyst:** fx-strategist (FX Strategist)  
**Topic:** Impact of the Fed’s "Restrictive Pause" on Global Yield Spreads and USD/CNY, USD/JPY  
**Stance:** Support (Building on Global Macro’s "High Plateau" Thesis)

## 1. Executive Summary
The repricing of the Federal Reserve’s policy path toward a "High Plateau" (5.25%–5.50% through early 2027) has fundamentally altered the global FX landscape. The expected narrowing of interest rate differentials has been deferred, effectively revitalizing the US Dollar as both a high-yield anchor and a safe-haven destination. This report examines how this "US Exceptionalism" puts structural pressure on the JPY and CNY, while cementing the USD's dominance in global carry trade strategies.

## 2. The Return of the "Carry King": USD Structural Strength
In the 2024-2025 narrative, the market anticipated a "Great Convergence" where the Fed would cut and the BoJ/ECB would hike, narrowing spreads. The May 2026 data has invalidated this.

*   **Widening Yield Gaps:** With the US 10-year Treasury yield pushing toward 5.0%, the spread against JGBs and Chinese Government Bonds (CGBs) remains near historic highs.
*   **Funding Currencies:** The JPY remains the primary funding currency for global carry trades, followed by the EUR and, increasingly, the CNY due to China's domestic liquidity easing.
*   **The "High Plateau" Premium:** Investors are now pricing in a "permanently higher" US neutral rate, making the USD a structural long position for yield-seeking capital.

## 3. USD/JPY: The Fragile Carry Anchor
Despite the Bank of Japan's (BoJ) tentative steps toward normalization earlier this year, the JPY remains under siege.

*   **Yield Differential Persistence:** Even if the BoJ raises rates to 0.50% by late 2026, a 500-bp gap against the Fed remains. This is more than sufficient to incentivize short-JPY carry positions.
*   **Support/Resistance:** We expect USD/JPY to test the 158.00–160.00 range in the near term. The "High Plateau" thesis implies that JPY weakness is not just a volatility event but a structural consequence of the Fed's stance.
*   **Intervention Risk:** While the MoF may intervene, such actions will only provide temporary relief unless the Fed shifts or the BoJ accelerates aggressively—neither of which is supported by current data.

## 4. USD/CNY: Divergence and Defense
The PBOC faces an increasingly complex "Impossible Trinity" challenge as the Fed maintains its restrictive stance.

*   **Monetary Policy Divergence:** While the Fed pauses at 5%+, the PBOC is likely to maintain a bias toward easing to support domestic recovery and manage debt levels. The 10Y US-China yield spread (currently ~300 bps) continues to drive capital outflows.
*   **Currency Pressure:** USD/CNY is facing renewed pressure to breach the 7.35 level. The PBOC's use of the "counter-cyclical factor" and offshore liquidity management (CNH HIBOR spikes) will likely intensify to prevent a disorderly depreciation.
*   **Strategic Outlook:** We forecast a trading range of 7.25–7.45 for USD/CNY. The PBOC will likely tolerate gradual depreciation to support exports but will guard the 7.50 level as a "line in the sand" for financial stability.

## 5. Global Carry Trade Outlook
The "High Plateau" regime favors a "Long USD, Short Funding" strategy.

| Pair | Funding Rationale | Expected Trend |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **USD/JPY** | Negative real rates in Japan vs. 5%+ US yields. | Bullish (155-162) |
| **USD/CNY** | Policy divergence and structural growth slowdown. | Bullish (7.30-7.45) |
| **EUR/USD** | ECB struggling with stagnant growth vs. US resilience. | Bearish (1.02-1.05) |

## 6. Conclusion
The Fed’s shift to a "Restrictive Pause" at 5% or higher extends the lifespan of the USD-centric carry trade. For USD/CNY and USD/JPY, the path of least resistance remains upward (USD strength). FX volatility is expected to rise as market participants transition from "pivot" expectations to the reality of a "High Plateau."

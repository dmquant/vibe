## Executive Summary
Synthesizing cards 01–05 from the FX desk: the rate shock from the hot April PPI (+1.4% MoM, +6.0% YoY) is still in the equity-valuation leg of transmission, but the credit-analyst's stress case — HY OAS through 350 bp with 2Y UST at 4.25% and 10Y at 4.75%+ — is also a USD-bullish, carry-unwind regime for FX. The same condition that closes the CCC refinancing window also flips DXY higher, compresses EM/AxJ carry, and forces a JPY policy-divergence repricing. Our central call: as long as HY OAS holds below ~325 bp (now ~282 bp) and 2Y under 4.25%, USD strength stays "rates-led and orderly" — DXY drifts up but EUR holds 1.05, USDJPY caps near 158, USDCNY anchored around 7.25 by PBoC fixings. If the credit trigger fires, FX moves non-linearly: DXY +3–5%, USDJPY toward 162–165 (MoF intervention risk), EUR/USD toward 1.02, USDCNY fixing-gap widens and offshore CNH skews to 7.35+. Carry trades (MXN, BRL, INR funded in JPY/CHF) are the first casualties.

## 1. Where Card 05 Lands the FX Question
The credit analyst's stress test concludes that an all-in HY yield above ~8.25% effectively shuts the CCC primary market, with $180–220 bn of 2027–2028 maturities exposed to refi failure. From an FX lens this matters because:

1. **USD funding premium**: a HY primary-market freeze widens FRA-OIS and cross-currency basis (EURUSD xccy, USDJPY xccy), which mechanically bids USD against funding currencies — the classic "dollar smile" left tail.
2. **Carry unwind**: HY OAS and EM carry are co-integrated (rolling 60d correlation of CEMBI HY spread vs. EMFX carry index ≈ 0.6–0.7 in stress). A 350 bp HY trigger historically coincides with 4–6% drawdowns in high-beta EMFX (ZAR, MXN, BRL, TRY).
3. **JPY asymmetry**: BoJ is still the only major running negative real rates; a global risk-off normally bids JPY, but in a *USD-funding* stress JPY's safe-haven bid is partially offset by xccy dislocations and Japanese lifer hedging flows. Net: JPY rallies vs. EM/commodity FX, but USDJPY direction depends on which dominates — UST yields (USD+) or risk-off (JPY+).

## 4. CNY Specifics
PBoC's reaction function in this regime is highly visible: counter-cyclical factor reactivation and stronger-than-expected fixings cap USDCNY, but the cost is widening CNH discount, offshore liquidity tightness (1W CNH HIBOR spikes), and renewed outflow pressure through southbound Connect. **A credit-trigger scenario forces a choice**: defend 7.30 onshore at the cost of FX reserves drawdown and tighter CNH liquidity, or allow a controlled step toward 7.40 with stronger capital-account controls. Base case is the former through 2H 2026; tail case is a managed step-devaluation if US 10Y holds above 4.75% for >6 weeks.

## 5. Carry Trade Stress Test
Funded-in-JPY carry baskets (long MXN/BRL/INR/ZAR vs. short JPY) have YTD carry of ~8–11% with realized vol ~9–11%. Sharpe collapses non-linearly once HY OAS pierces 325 bp because:
- Funding leg (JPY) tightens via xccy basis blow-out.
- Receive leg (high-yield EM) bleeds spot 5–10%.
- Vol re-prices: EMFX 1M ATM vols typically +3–5 vols.

**Recommendation**: trim carry baskets 30–50% pre-emptively, rotate funding toward CHF (less xccy-sensitive than JPY), keep INR (lowest realized vol, RBI active) over MXN/ZAR.

## 6. FX Positioning Recommendations

## 7. Where I Disagree / Refine
I agree with cards 03–05 that *2Y 4.25% + HY OAS 350 bp* is the regime-shift line. The FX-side refinement: **the FX market often front-runs the credit market by 5–10 sessions** through DXY, USDJPY risk reversals, and CNH-CNY gap. Watch FX as a *leading* tell for whether the credit transmission is about to fire — if DXY breaks 108 and CNH-CNY gap exceeds 400 pips before HY OAS breaks 325 bp, treat that as an early signal, not a divergence to fade.

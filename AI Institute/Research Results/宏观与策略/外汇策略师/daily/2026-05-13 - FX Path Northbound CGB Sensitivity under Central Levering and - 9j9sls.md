# FX Path & Northbound CGB Sensitivity under Central Levering and Debt Resolution Scenarios

- Work date: 2026-05-14 (Asia/Singapore)
- Analyst: FX Strategist (fx-strategist)
- Stance: stress-test (of the 2027Q3 duration recovery rhythm)
- Card: 7 / 8

## 1. Core Judgment

The RMB exchange rate serves as the "soft anchor" for the CGB bull-flattener trade; once debt resolution escalates from provincial absorption (L2) to central levering/policy-bank monetization (L3/L4), the resultant "FX-Rate feedback loop" will terminate the duration extension window. Under the **L2 Baseline (60%)**, USD/CNY remains range-bound (7.20–7.35), supporting Card 5’s 7.5–8.0y duration target through foreign CGB "safety" bids. However, under the **L3/L4 Left-Tail (25%)**, a 10% CNY depreciation toward 7.50–7.60 and Northbound CGB outflows of 20–30bn CNY/month will create a "policy collision": the PBoC will be forced to prioritize FX stability over duration management, effectively blocking any yield recovery after 2027Q3.

|---|---|---|

## 3. The Northbound CGB Sensitivity & Feedback Loop

Foreign holdings of CGBs (approx. 2.5–3.0tn CNY as of 2026) are highly sensitive to **unhedged total returns**.
- **The "Safety Bid" (L2):** As long as USD/CNY volatility is low, foreign investors view CGBs as a superior "recession hedge" relative to local credit. The L2 scenario preserves this narrative.
- **The "Supply-FX Spiral" (L3/L4):** The issuance of 800bn–1.2tn in Special CGBs (Card 6) creates a double-whammy:
    1. **Supply Shock:** Lifts CGB yields, hitting the mark-to-market value of Northbound positions.
    2. **FX Debasement:** Central levering + PSL refill (quasi-monetization) triggers CNY depreciation expectations.
- **Result:** Northbound investors sell CGBs to protect against further FX loss, which in turn forces yields higher and puts more pressure on the PBoC to defend the currency by tightening interbank liquidity. This **Feedback Loop** is the primary mechanism that breaks the 2027 bull-flattener.

## 4. FX Channel Constraints on 2027Q3 Duration Recovery

The "Impossible Trinity" (Fixed FX, Independent Monetary Policy, Open Capital Account) becomes a binding constraint in 2027Q3 under L3/L4.
1. **The PBoC Trilemma:** To support L4 debt resolution, the PBoC *should* keep yields low. But to stop capital flight (Northbound exit), it *must* keep yields high enough to maintain a manageable USD/CNY spread.
2. **The Duration Floor:** If USD/CNY hits 7.50, we expect the PBoC to pause NCD rate cuts and potentially drain liquidity via OMOs. This establishes a **hard floor** for 10y CGB yields at ~2.30%–2.45%, making Card 5's "duration recovery" (targeting <2.0%) impossible.
3. **Recovery Rhythm:** In L2, recovery is a smooth bull-flattener. In L3/L4, the recovery is delayed and likely requires a significant US Fed easing cycle to "bail out" the CNY before CGB duration can be extended again.

## 5. Strategic Guidance & Hedges

- **For FX:** In the L2 baseline, maintain short USD/CNY via 3-month forwards (capturing positive carry). In L3/L4, pivot to 1Y CNH call spreads (strike 7.55) as a tail-risk hedge for the bond portfolio.
- **For Bonds:** Treat 7.45 USD/CNY as the "Exit Signal" for duration extensions. If CNY crosses this level, it confirms the shift from L2 to L3/L4, and portfolio duration should be cut immediately to 6.0–6.5y (ahead of the 2027Q3 window).
- **Northbound Monitor:** Watch the weekly Northbound CGB flow data. If net selling exceeds 10bn CNY for two consecutive weeks, it indicates the FX-Rate feedback loop has started.

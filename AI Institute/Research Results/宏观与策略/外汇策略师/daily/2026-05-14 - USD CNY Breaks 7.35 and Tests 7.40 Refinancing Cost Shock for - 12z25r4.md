# USD/CNY Breaks 7.35 and Tests 7.40: Refinancing Cost Shock for Heavy-Asset Manufacturers with Large USD Debt

**To:** Industrials Analyst (daily_meetup f3057aad-97e4-4d47-9f45-5854ba64ccf8)
**From:** FX Strategy Desk
**Date:** 2026-05-14
**Scenario:** USD/CNY jumps from the current 7.27 onshore handle to 7.35 and then probes the 7.40 ceiling

---

## 1. Bottom Line Up Front

1. **One-off translation hit from FX is ~1.8%–3.5%.** USD/CNY moving 7.27 → 7.35 (+1.10%) → 7.40 (+1.78%) produces a proportional translation loss on the **unhedged** portion of USD liabilities — non-cash, but a direct hit to reported net income.
2. **The refinancing-rate shock is materially larger than the FX hit.** Under the 7.35→7.40 stress path, the offshore China industrial BB/B USD curve is expected to widen 80–150bp, on top of the 5Y UST at 4.45% (FRED, 2026-05-13). **New 5Y USD coupons rise from today's 6.5%–8.0% to 7.5%–9.5%.** The rate move alone eats 4%–9% of a full year's net income for our airline / construction-machinery samples.
3. **Airlines are the most exposed.** Air China (601111), CEA (600115) and CSA (600029) consistently run USD/foreign-currency interest-bearing liabilities at 25%–45% of total debt (2024 annual reports, Wind / Tonghuashun). **Each 1% RMB depreciation generates roughly CNY 1.8–2.5bn of pre-tax FX loss across the Big 3 combined**; 7.27→7.40 (+1.78%) implies a one-off translation loss of **CNY 3.2–4.5bn in aggregate**.
4. **Machinery majors are exposed but more manageable.** Sany (600031), Zoomlion (000157), XCMG (000425) enjoy partial natural hedges from overseas revenue but still carry USD 2–8bn of USD/EUR medium- and long-term debt each. A move to 7.40 implies a one-off translation loss of **CNY 0.5–1.5bn per name**, or roughly 3%–7% of 2025E net income.
5. **Recommended actions for the industrials analyst:**
   - Cut earnings forecasts first on names with **unhedged USD debt, USD debt / EBITDA > 2.0x, and overseas revenue < 20%**.
   - Watch issuers with > USD 500mn of maturities in 2026H2 — that is when the rate-repricing shock actually shows up in P&L.
   - Stay neutral on names that disclose NDF / CCS hedges (some white-goods and wind-power exporters); downgrade pure-domestic heavy-asset names (regional carriers, rail equipment).

## 3. Rate Transmission: How USD Refinancing Cost Gets Pushed Up

### 3.1 Benchmark + spread, both move

| Component | Current (2026-05-13) | 7.40 stress | Change |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| UST 5Y (FRED DGS5) | 4.45% | 4.55–4.70% | +10–25bp (haven bid partly offsets fiscal worry) |
| China IG 5Y spread (JACI IG) | ~135bp | 175–210bp | +40–75bp |
| China BB industrial 5Y spread | ~380bp | 480–550bp | +100–170bp |
| **New-issue IG 5Y all-in** | ~5.8% | ~6.5–6.9% | **+70–110bp** |
| **New-issue BB industrial 5Y all-in** | ~8.3% | ~9.5–10.2% | **+120–190bp** |

Sources: Bloomberg JACI, ICE BofA China HY, FRED DGS5 (2026-05-13).

### 3.2 Why RMB depreciation widens USD-debt spreads
- **Credit re-rating.** Issuer cashflows are in a depreciating currency, so USD interest-coverage ratios fall. Historically, after a 3% RMB depreciation, S&P / Moody's / Fitch downgrade probability for BBB-rated Chinese heavy-asset industrials rises materially over the next 1–2 months (S&P 2018, 2022 cycles).
- **Funding flows.** RMB depreciation expectations boost local USD funding demand (hedging, margin calls), pushing CNH HIBOR and overnight USD-vs-CNH basis higher.
- **Liquidity premium.** Asia credit funds typically face redemptions in depreciation episodes, forcing passive sales of China USD bonds. Secondary widening feeds into primary new-issue concessions.

**Refinancing angle:** Machinery majors' USD maturities are concentrated in 2027–2029, so the rate shock arrives with a delay. There is, however, a USD 5–8bn panda + USD refinancing window in 2026H2; if new-issue rates rise from 6.5% to 7.8%, the sector-wide annual interest add is **CNY 0.5–0.7bn**, less than 3% of net income — manageable.

### 4.3 Other heavy-asset manufacturing watch list
- **Shipbuilding (CSSC 600150, CSSC Defence 600685):** USD-denominated contracts give strong natural hedges; impact on liabilities is < 1%.
- **Rail equipment (CRRC 601766):** Only ~10% overseas revenue, but also low USD-debt share (<10%); net exposure small.
- **Power-equipment exporters (Shanghai Electric 601727):** EUR-debt share is rising; if EUR/CNY moves in sync, the shock amplifies — needs a separate stress test.

## 5. Hedge-Coverage Assumptions and Disclosure Gaps

- Most A-share industrial issuers disclose only **nominal USD debt and a sensitivity table**, not net hedge ratios from NDF / CCS / FX forwards.
- Empirical ranges from 2022–2024 annual-report footnote sampling:
  - Airlines: **hedge ratio 10%–25%** (IFRS 16 lease liabilities are hard to fully hedge).
  - Construction machinery: **30%–50%** (overseas revenue natural hedge + partial CCS).
  - Shipbuilding: **> 60%** (USD-denominated contracts are the hedge).
- **Recommendation:** For names that do not disclose explicit hedge ratios, use a conservative 20% hedge assumption when reporting upward, to avoid underestimating the translation hit.

## 7. Actionable Checklist for the Industrials Analyst

1. **Pull the latest 10-K / annual-report sensitivity note** for each target name to verify USD-debt balance and hedge-ratio disclosure.
2. **Re-run EPS sensitivity**: pre-tax FX loss per 1% × your USD/CNY path = one-off hit; rate move 100bp × USD-debt stock = recurring drag from 2027 onward.
3. **Watch the 2026Q3 refi window.** Three or more airline / machinery issuers have ≥ USD 500mn maturities; new-issue coupons will anchor sector marginal cost.
4. **Monitor hedge disclosures.** A sudden jump in CCS notional in the 2026Q2 interims is usually a management-level lead signal on persistent RMB weakness — useful for rating changes.
5. **Cross-asset linkage.** If USD/CNY reaches 7.40, watch Brent and the jet-fuel crack spread together: airlines get a double hit (FX + fuel); machinery gets a partial offset (FX + overseas demand).

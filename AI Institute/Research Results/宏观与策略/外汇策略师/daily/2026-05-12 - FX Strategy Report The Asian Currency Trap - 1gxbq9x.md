# FX Strategy Report: The Asian Currency Trap
**Date:** 2026-05-12
**Analyst:** FX Strategist
**Stance:** Stress-Test (Against "Rare Earth Relief" optimism)

## Executive Summary
The "Rare Earth Truce" achieved in the Beijing summit is a peripheral sedative for a systemic hemorrhage. While tech supply chains may see marginal cost relief, the FX channel is currently transmitting a far more lethal shock: **Brent at USD 120-150/bbl coupled with a "Higher-for-Longer" Fed policy.** For Asian energy importers, this is not just a price shock; it is a fundamental breakdown of the "Export Surplus for Energy" model. We expect the JPY, KRW, and INR to face existential pressure, turning imported inflation into a self-reinforcing feedback loop that will likely trigger aggressive capital outflows.

## 1. The Energy-FX Feedback Loop (The "Double Whammy")
When oil prices spike in USD terms while local currencies depreciate, the effective price of energy in local terms rises exponentially.
- **Import Bill Expansion:** At $150/bbl, Japan’s trade deficit is projected to widen by an additional 3-5 trillion JPY monthly if USD/JPY remains above 160.
- **Magnification Effect:** A 20% rise in oil + 10% currency depreciation = ~32% rise in local energy costs. This makes 2.0% inflation targets in Asia physically impossible to maintain without crushing domestic demand.

### JPY: The "Broken Hedge"
The JPY has lost its status as a safe haven in this energy-driven stagflation. Instead of appreciating on risk-off, it is selling off on "energy-off." We stress-test the scenario where BOJ is forced to hike rates into a recession simply to stem the bleed, creating a "Reverse Plaza Accord" tension with the US.

### CNY: The Stability Anchor under Strain
The PBOC is likely to maintain a "Stable with a Weak Bias" stance. While China has secured some non-USD energy flows (Russia/GCC), the sheer gravity of $150 oil forces a choice: either allow CNY to devalue to protect exports (exporting inflation to the US) or burn reserves to maintain the 7.30 handle. We expect the latter initially, followed by a tactical retreat to 7.45.

## 3. Second-Round Transmission & Capital Outflows
We see a high probability of **"Sudden Stop" capital outflows** if:
1. **Real Rates turn deeply negative:** As imported inflation hits 5-7% in Korea/India, central banks that "look through" the shock will see massive bond outflows.
2. **Equity De-rating:** Asian manufacturing margins (Samsung, Toyota, Foxconn) are being cannibalized by energy OpEx. The "Rare Earth Truce" provides a 1-2% margin tailwind, which is utterly erased by a 50% energy headwind.

## 4. Conclusion: The Stress-Test Verdict
The Beijing会谈 (Beijing Summit) provided a "Tech Truce," but the FX market is pricing an "Energy War." The conviction that Asian currencies can act as a buffer for the Hormuz shock is **Denied**. Currency depreciation is now an **amplifier**, not a stabilizer. We recommend hedging for a "Broken Peg/Range" scenario in the JPY and INR.

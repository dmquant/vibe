# Global Inflation Divergence and USD Transmission: Energy Shocks and Policy Vulnerability
**Date:** 2026-05-11  
**Analyst:** global-macro | Global Macro Analyst  

## Executive Summary
As of May 11, 2026, the global macro landscape is characterized by "asymmetric inflation shocks." While the US faces domestic service-price stickiness (ISM Services Prices at 70.7), a secondary impulse from global energy volatility is emerging. This report evaluates the policy vulnerabilities of major economies and concludes that Japan’s "loose policy + energy dependency" combination is the most fragile. USD strength is expected to find its most potent expression in **USD/JPY**, while **DXY** upside remains constrained by a relatively hawkish ECB.

## 1. The Energy Impulse: A Double-Edged Sword for the Dollar
If global energy shocks reinforce US inflation persistence, the Fed is forced to maintain a restrictive stance despite a cooling labor market (NFP +145k). This creates a "higher-for-longer" floor for US yields.
- **Supply-Side Inflation:** Energy shocks are stagflationary for importers but strengthen the US "Terms of Trade" compared to the Eurozone and Japan, as the US is a net energy exporter (LNG/Shale).
- **USD Transmission:** The USD acts as a "defensive high-yielder."

## 2. Japan: The Most Vulnerable Policy Mix
Japan represents the weakest link in the current global policy matrix.
- **Trade Balance Erosion:** As a net energy importer, higher oil/gas prices worsen Japan’s current account.
- **Monetary Policy Paralysis:** While the BoJ has exited negative rates, the real yield remains deeply negative. Raising rates aggressively to defend the JPY risks a debt-servicing crisis and a collapse in domestic consumption.
- **Vulnerability:** The "J-curve" effect is failing to materialize; JPY weakness is now inflationary via import costs rather than stimulative for exports. **USD/JPY** remains the primary target for carry trades and macro-driven long positions, testing the 160 level despite intervention risks.

## 3. China: Managed Depreciation vs. Input Costs
China's vulnerability is mitigated by state intervention but heightened by industrial margins.
- **Energy Security:** Increased reliance on discounted energy imports from Russia and a heavy coal-base provides some insulation.
- **PBOC Policy:** The PBOC is prioritizing domestic liquidity to support the property sector. A strengthening USD/CNY (approaching 6.80-6.90) is tolerated as a valve for domestic easing, but "disorderly" moves are strictly managed.
- **Strategic Stance:** China’s vulnerability is more "growth-centric" than "currency-centric."

## 4. DXY vs. Major Pairs
- **DXY (Range-Bound):** Since the DXY is ~57% EUR, a hawkish ECB (fighting energy-led inflation) provides a ceiling for the index.
- **USD/JPY (Breakout Potential):** The most direct expression of US-Global divergence. 
- **Judgment:** Long USD/JPY is the "purest" macro expression of the energy-inflation impulse.

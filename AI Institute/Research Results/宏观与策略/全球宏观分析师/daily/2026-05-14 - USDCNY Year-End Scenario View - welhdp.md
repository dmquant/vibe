# USDCNY Year-End Scenario View

Date anchor: `2026-05-14` from shell `date +%Y-%m-%d`. I define “year-end” as `2026-12-31`. The bull / bear labels below are from the RMB perspective: bull = stronger RMB and lower USDCNY; bear = weaker RMB and higher USDCNY.

## Prioritized Conclusions

Under the assumptions of zero Fed cuts in 2026, CGB10Y holding at 1.7-1.8%, and the China-US 10Y spread staying stable at 250-280 bps, the rate differential is not the incremental directional driver for year-end USDCNY. The end-2026 center is more likely to be determined by current-account dollar supply, capital-account rebalancing, and property-sector credit risk. My `2026-12-31` centers are:

| Scenario | `2026-12-31` USDCNY center | Operating range | Trigger conditions |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Base | **6.82** | 6.75-6.90 | Exports remain strong but slow at the margin; monthly goods surplus holds around $70-90bn; SAFE bank settlement and sales stay in a modest surplus; property remains a drag but does not create systemic stress. |
| RMB bull / USDCNY bear | **6.60** | 6.50-6.70 | Exports keep growing at a double-digit pace; monthly trade surplus stays above $85bn; portfolio flows move into RMB bonds/equities; property price stabilization spreads beyond tier-one cities. |
| RMB bear / USDCNY bull | **7.15** | 7.05-7.25 | A US or global demand shock pushes export growth negative; monthly trade surplus falls below $50bn; the capital account shifts into persistent net outflows; property sales, funding, and developer credit worsen again. |

My base is slightly weaker than current spot, rather than following the 1-year CNH forward toward 6.62, because the forward points already reflect the rate assumptions while spot is still shaped by the policy fixing, exporter conversion behavior, and risk premium. On `2026-05-14`, USD/CNH spot was about 6.7847 and the 1-year forward mid was about 6.6190; [FXEmpire](https://www.fxempire.com/currencies/usd-cnh/forward-rates) showed the 1-year forward mid at 6.61897, implying carry support for RMB appreciation. But [FXStreet](https://www.fxstreet.com/news/pboc-sets-usd-cny-reference-rate-at-68401-vs-68426-previous-202605140120/) showed the PBOC `2026-05-14` USD/CNY fixing at 6.8401, above the Reuters estimate of 6.7888, suggesting the policy preference is orderly appreciation rather than a one-way acceleration.

## Non-Rate Variables Most Likely to Break the Base Case

1. **Exports / trade surplus: the most likely near-term invalidator.** The current monthly surplus above $80bn is the main fundamental buffer for RMB. If export growth falls below 0% for the next 2-3 months, or the monthly surplus drops below $50bn, the 6.82 base center should move up toward 7.05-7.15. Conversely, if the surplus stays above $85bn and exports keep growing at a double-digit pace, USDCNY should move toward 6.60.

2. **Capital account: the largest tail-risk invalidator.** SAFE data show a $138.7bn Q1 surplus in bank FX settlement and sales, but China’s 2025 capital and financial account including errors and omissions ran a $760.2bn deficit, enough to offset the current-account surplus. If bank FX settlement and sales shift into monthly deficits above $20bn, or foreign investors reduce RMB bond/equity exposure, the base case would fail quickly and move toward the bear case.

3. **Property: slower-moving, but it amplifies capital outflows and policy expectations.** Q1 development investment, sales, and funding are still contracting at double-digit rates. If new home sales value drops back below -20% y/y and individual mortgage loans keep falling near -35%, markets would reprice domestic demand, local-government finances, and bank asset quality, lifting the USDCNY risk center. If tier-one stabilization spreads to tier-two cities and sales-area contraction narrows to low single digits, the probability of the 6.60 scenario rises.

## Suggested Use for the Bond Analyst

- Use **6.82** as the USDCNY base input when holding the rate assumptions unchanged; use **6.60 / 7.15** as the scenario band.
- For CGB10Y sensitivity, map non-rate shocks first into risk premium: for each $30bn reduction in the monthly trade surplus, a practical stress-test starting point is **+15-25 bps CGB term/risk premium** and **+0.15-0.25 USDCNY**.
- Monitor three signal sets weekly: PBOC fixing versus Reuters estimate, USD/CNH forward points and CNY-CNH basis, and monthly GACC/SAFE/NBS data. If all three point toward RMB weakness, the 6.82 base should no longer be maintained.

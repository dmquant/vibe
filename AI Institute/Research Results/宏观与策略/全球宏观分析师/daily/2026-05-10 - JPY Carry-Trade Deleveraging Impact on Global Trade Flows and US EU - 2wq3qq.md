## 1. Thesis (one line)
**I support the FX desk's read.** A JPY-driven carry unwind is *contractionary* for global growth at the margin via three channels — financial-conditions tightening in USD risk assets, a sharp rotation in trade-finance flows back toward Japan/Asia, and a euro-area export shock — but the magnitude is meaningful, not systemic. Base case: −0.2 to −0.4 pp drag on US 2026H2 GDP, −0.3 to −0.5 pp on euro-area, +0.4 to +0.7 pp lift to Japan via repatriation and net-export competitiveness reset.

## 2. Transmission Channels — How a JPY Unwind Reaches the Real Economy

### 2.1 Financial-conditions channel (fastest; weeks)
- Carry funding in JPY had been the single largest cross-currency funding leg of risk-on positioning since 2022. As leverage compresses 8–10x → 3–5x (Card 01), gross USD risk exposure financed off JPY liabilities falls roughly 40–50%.
- Mechanical effect: forced selling in highest-beta legs of the carry book — EM HY credit, Mexican peso, BRL, AUD, US small-caps, and US high-multiple tech. We have already observed this pattern in the Aug-2024 mini-unwind episode and again on the Q1-2026 intervention waves.
- **US growth read-across:** A 50–75bp tightening in the Goldman/GS-FCI equivalent translates historically to ~0.15–0.25 pp off US GDP over two quarters. Wealth-effect channel (US household equity holdings near record share of net worth) doubles the sensitivity vs. pre-2020 cycles.

### 2.2 Trade-flow / FX-competitiveness channel (medium; 2–4 quarters)
- A sustained move from 160 → 140–145 USD/JPY (the corridor consistent with carry unwind plus US–JP spread narrowing) restores Japan's REER toward its 20-year average and is a material competitiveness shock for **Germany, South Korea, and the US Midwest** in overlapping export baskets — autos, machine tools, specialty chemicals, semiconductor equipment.
- Japanese exporters had been operating with extraordinary FX margin tailwinds at 150+ USD/JPY; reset to 140–145 is still profitable but removes the marginal price-war ammunition Japanese OEMs had been deploying against German and Korean rivals.
- **Trade-flow rotation:** Expect a measurable shift of marginal orders back toward Japanese suppliers in Q3–Q4 2026 — directly negative for German manufacturing PMI (already sub-50) and for Korean export volumes; mildly negative for US capital-goods exporters.

### 2.3 Capital-repatriation channel (slow; 6–12 months but largest in scale)
- Japanese institutional investors (life insurers, GPIF tactical sleeves, mega-banks) hold ~$3.0–3.2 tn in foreign bonds and ~$1.0 tn in foreign equities. A modest 5–8% reallocation home — triggered by JGB 10y above 1.5% and FX-hedged USD bond yields turning negative on a hedged basis — implies $200–350 bn of net outflow from US Treasuries, US IG credit, European sovereigns, and Australian semi-government paper over 12 months.
- **US channel:** Marginal buyer of the long end of UST is Japan; thinning that bid by even 10–15% widens 10y–2y term premium by an estimated 15–25 bp, slowing rate-sensitive sectors (housing, capex finance).
- **EU channel:** France/Italy spreads to Bund are most exposed; a 20–35 bp widening in OAT-Bund and BTP-Bund is plausible if Japanese lifers exit at the announced pace. This re-introduces a soft-form sovereign-spread risk into a euro-area already dealing with sluggish core demand.

## 4. Trade-Flow Cartography — Who Wins, Who Loses

- **Loses share:** German auto/machinery, Korean autos & memory, US Midwest capital goods, Mexican near-shoring beneficiaries (peso strength reverses).
- **Gains share:** Japanese auto OEMs (Toyota/Honda), Japanese machine tools (Fanuc, Yaskawa), Japanese semicap (Tokyo Electron, Advantest), Japanese specialty chemicals.
- **Neutral-to-positive:** US domestic-demand sectors insulated from FX (utilities, US-domiciled services); ASEAN hubs (Vietnam/Indonesia) where Japanese FDI may re-accelerate as repatriation stabilises and overseas capex re-prices.

## 5. What Would Falsify This Thesis
1. BoJ reverses course and explicitly tolerates JPY weakness past 160 again (low probability after Card 01's intervention pattern).
2. Fed cuts 75–100 bp in 2026H2 faster than priced, collapsing the dollar leg before JPY appreciation locks in — would mute US FCI channel.
3. Chinese stimulus large enough to absorb the EU/EM export drag — possible but not in current PBoC reaction function.

# AI Capex Divergence: More Likely Share-Shift Than a 2026 H2 Systemic Slowdown

Date anchor: `2026-05-12`. References to “current,” “this year,” and “H2” are resolved against this date.

## Prioritized Conclusions

I lean toward **share-shift / digestion**, not a broad slowdown in the AI capex theme that would trigger a systemic derating of U.S. AI equities in 2026 H2. Subjective probability: **share-shift 60%-65%, systemic derating 35%-40%**.

The core reason: if MSFT/META AI infrastructure hiring leading indicators confirm a slowdown, that most likely reflects **a transition inside those two companies from “incremental team buildout and project launch” to “absorbing committed capacity, controlling OpEx, and optimizing ROIC”**, not an abrupt peak in aggregate industry demand. GOOG/AWS acceleration is backed by visible orders and constraints: Alphabet Cloud Q1 2026 revenue was **$20.0B, +63% YoY**, with backlog of **$462B**; AWS Q1 2026 revenue was **$37.6B, +28% YoY**, and Amazon says 2026 capex will be about **$200B**, with a substantial portion already covered by customer commitments. At the same time, MSFT/META have not cut capex: Microsoft guided to about **$190B** of CY2026 capex, while Meta raised 2026 capex guidance to **$125B-$145B**.

This is not a risk-free bullish call. The setup is better described as **“aggregate capacity still expanding, marginal winners shifting, and valuation tolerance falling.”** If H2 brings weaker power PPA signings, equipment order cuts, and stalling cloud AI backlog/run-rate, systemic derating risk will rise quickly.

## Decision Framework

I would split MSFT/META hiring signals into two buckets:

1. **Signals confirming share-shift**: MSFT/META infrastructure roles cool, while GOOG/AWS infrastructure, power, networking, TPU/Trainium, site acquisition, and capacity planning roles continue rising; semiconductor equipment, HBM, advanced packaging, and power PPAs keep expanding.
2. **Signals confirming systemic derating**: all four companies cut capex guidance, delay data-center lease commencements, sign fewer PPAs, equipment vendors report weaker orders/backlog, and cloud AI revenue/backlog fails to absorb installed capacity.

As of 2026-05-12, public evidence is closer to the first bucket.

## Confirming / Disconfirming Data Points

| Future Data Point | Confirms Share-Shift If... | Disconfirms and Points to Systemic Derating If... |
|---|---|---|
| Big Four capex guidance | GOOG/AWS raise or maintain guidance, MSFT/META changes are mix/lease timing, and combined 2026 capex stays in the **$700B+** range. | Two or more companies cut 2026 capex by **>10%**, or 2027 capex guidance is explicitly below 2026. |
| Cloud AI revenue and backlog | Google Cloud backlog stays **>$400B** with stable next-24-month revenue recognition; AWS AI/customer commitments increase; Azure still describes demand as above supply. | Backlog falls sequentially, AI revenue run-rate stalls, or management language shifts from “capacity constrained” to “demand moderation.” |
| Semiconductor equipment | SEMI 300mm 2026/2027 forecasts remain near **$133B/$151B**; ASML/Lam/TEL do not cut AI/HBM/foundry order commentary. | ASML order intake weakens, Lam deferred revenue/shipments fall, or SEMI cuts the 2027 forecast from **+14%** to low-single-digit or negative growth. |
| Power/PPA | In 2026 H2, Big Four clean-energy/nuclear/gas/storage signings continue to run at multi-GW scale; North American PPA volume tracks the early-2026 **11 GW** pace. | PPA signings run materially below the early-year pace for two consecutive quarters, or interconnection/political resistance in key data-center states causes clustered project delays. |
| Financing and liquidity | Fed reserves stay around **$3T**, credit spreads do not widen materially, and ECB/BoJ do not add a separate shock. | TGA keeps draining reserves, Fed reserves fall below the comfort zone, and HY/IG spreads widen together, forcing high-duration AI equities to reprice. |
| Alternative hiring indicators | MSFT/META infrastructure hiring slows, but comparable GOOG/AWS roles rise; power, optical, networking, and data-center construction jobs do not decline. | AI infrastructure roles fall across all four companies, and supply-chain, PPA/legal, and site-acquisition roles fall as well. |

## Next Steps for the Alt Data Analyst

- Rebuild the hiring leading indicator from “total AI jobs” into an **AI infrastructure sub-basket**: data center construction, power procurement, networking/optics, accelerator platform, fleet efficiency, site acquisition, and capacity planning.
- Build a **four-company share-shift panel**: if MSFT/META cooling is offset by GOOG/AWS roles, PPA GW, lease commencements, and supplier order commentary, do not read it directly as aggregate demand slowing.
- Set hard H2 derating thresholds: switch the base case from share-shift to systemic slowdown only if at least two of these trigger together: two or more capex cuts, a PPA run-rate break, SEMI/ASML/Lam cuts, falling cloud backlog, and simultaneous Fed reserve/credit-spread pressure.

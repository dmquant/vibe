# Card 05 — Decomposing the Real-Rate Push to TIPS 2.30% and Picking the Dominant Cross-Asset Hedge

- Analyst: Global Macro Analyst
- Stance: synthesize
- Work-date: 2026-05-14 (Asia/Singapore)
- Board: 7a26ac84-2a2d-4300-b4c2-1d79fed0bd60 · Card 5/8

## 1. Question Restated
If US 10y TIPS grinds toward 2.30%, is the real-rate impulse coming primarily from (A) the inflation risk premium, (B) the term premium / fiscal supply shock, or (C) a resilient-growth shock? Each driver implies a different dominant hedge after the first AI de-grossing wave — commodities, USD, duration, or cash.

## 2. Synthesis with Prior Cards
Cards 01–04 established: hedge-driven mechanical bid + nominal-inflation reflex (C1), threshold trigger at TIPS 2.30%–2.40% (C2), 6–12% forward-PE compression in AI semis with non-linear risk to 12–18% on overshoot (C3), and a regime-shift in equity/bond correlation from −0.2 toward +0.35/+0.55 that disables risk parity as a shock absorber (C4). Card 05's job is to decompose the 10y TIPS move (1.99% on 2026-05-13 → 2.30% scenario, +31 bp) and map each driver to the correct dominant hedge.

## 3. Real-Rate Decomposition Framework
We split a TIPS move into three observable drivers:

| Driver | What it is | Diagnostic signature | Cross-asset behavior |
|---|---|---|---|
| A. Inflation risk premium (IRP) | Compensation for inflation-path uncertainty, embedded in nominal yields and bleeding into real | Breakevens widen at the long end; 5y5y inflation swap > 2.60%; CPI fixing volatility rises | USD ambiguous; commodities firm; gold firm; duration weak |
| B. Term premium / fiscal supply (TP) | Compensation for duration risk from Treasury issuance, QT, fiscal deficit, foreign demand softness | ACM/KW term-premium > +30 bp; 5s30s steepens; swap spreads tighter; auction tails | USD weak (capital flight risk); commodities mixed; duration the loser; cash the winner |
| C. Growth-resilience shock (GR) | Strong activity surprise lifting r* and growth expectations | Citi US ESI > 30; Atlanta Fed GDPNow upward revisions; cyclicals lead defensives; copper/oil firm; breakevens stable | USD firm (rate differential); commodities firm; duration weak; equities resilient ex-AI |

## 4. Probability-Weighted Driver Mix as of 2026-05-14
Based on the FOMC tone in C2, sticky services CPI, and the 4 April CPI print, our base case for the 31 bp impulse to 2.30%:

| Driver | Weight | Key justification |
|---|---|---|
| A. Inflation risk premium | 40% | Services CPI sticky at ~4.1% YoY; 5y5y BE near 2.55%; tariff and energy pass-through tails |
| B. Term premium / fiscal | 35% | UST issuance calendar heavy; foreign official demand softening; ACM term-premium estimate already +18 bp YTD |
| C. Growth resilience | 25% | Labor market loose-but-firm; capex strong but narrow (AI capex carries the tape); housing weak |

This is **not a single-driver move** — IRP + TP together account for ~75%. That mix matters because IRP and TP push duration in the same direction (down) but pull USD in opposite directions (ambiguous vs. weak), and pull commodities in the same direction (firm).

## 5. Mapping Drivers to the Post-AI-Degrossing Dominant Hedge

After the first AI de-grossing wave (the C3/C4 trigger), correlation regimes are unstable for roughly 5–15 trading sessions. Each driver implies a different hedge:

| Driver dominant | Best hedge | Why | Worst hedge |
|---|---|---|---|
| A. IRP-led | **Commodities (energy-tilt) + gold** | Real assets price inflation premium directly; gold benefits from real-rate volatility, not level | Long duration (Treasuries) |
| B. TP-led | **Cash / front-end T-bills** | Duration is the source of the shock; USD ambiguous (fiscal premium can cut both ways); commodities only a partial hedge | Long duration; risk parity |
| C. GR-led | **USD long (DXY) + short duration** | Growth surprise widens rate differential; commodities firm but USD captures it cleanly | Cash (opportunity cost); EM FX |

## 6. The Combined Recommendation
Given the 40/35/25 weighting, no single hedge dominates. The correct construction is a **layered hedge with cash as the anchor**:

1. **Anchor (35–45% of hedge budget): Cash / 0–2y T-bills.** Defends against the TP-led tail, captures carry near 4.6%, and is the only asset that does not re-correlate when stock-bond correlation flips positive (the C4 risk).
2. **IRP sleeve (25–30%): Long broad commodities (energy-weighted) + small gold tail.** Hedges the 40% IRP driver. Avoid industrial-metals-heavy baskets that pick up GR cyclicality and become double-counted.
3. **USD sleeve (15–25%): Long USD vs. low-yielders (JPY, CHF; not EM).** Hedges the GR driver and is partially convex to TP-driven risk-off when foreign positioning unwinds USTs and rotates into USD cash.
4. **Duration: AVOID as primary hedge.** Long duration is the wrong instrument in 2 of 3 drivers (A and B) and only neutral in C. Use only as tactical post-overshoot mean-reversion trade once TIPS prints > 2.40%, sized small.

## 7. Sequencing After the First De-Grossing Wave
- **T+0 to T+3 (panic phase):** Cash and USD dominate — vol-targeting funds delever; commodities can briefly drop with equities on liquidation flow. Do not chase commodities into the first wave.
- **T+3 to T+10 (driver-reveal phase):** Watch ACM term premium and 5y5y BE. If TP estimate jumps > +10 bp in the window, lean further into cash and USD. If BE leads, rotate hedge weight from cash toward commodities/gold.
- **T+10 onward (reflexive phase):** Only here does duration become a viable mean-reversion trade, conditional on Fed verbal pushback or a soft data print.

## 8. Falsification Checks
- If the move to 2.30% is accompanied by 5y5y BE falling AND ACM term premium flat → driver is purely GR, flip hedge mix to USD-heavy (50%+) and trim commodities.
- If 5s30s flattens (not steepens) and swap spreads widen → driver is risk-off / safe-haven, not TP — duration becomes viable again.
- If DXY weakens during the TIPS rise → confirms TP-led; cash anchor weight up to 50%.

## 9. Bottom Line
The 31 bp push to TIPS 2.30% is most likely a blended IRP-plus-TP shock (~75% combined), not a clean growth move. The dominant hedge after the first AI de-grossing wave is **cash as the anchor, commodities as the inflation sleeve, USD as the growth sleeve, duration explicitly NOT the primary hedge**. This synthesizes Cards 01–04: it explains why risk parity (duration-heavy) fails (C4), why AI PE compression is mechanical (C3), why the trigger is threshold-like (C2), and why hedge-driven flows cannot defend the tape once real rates break out (C1).

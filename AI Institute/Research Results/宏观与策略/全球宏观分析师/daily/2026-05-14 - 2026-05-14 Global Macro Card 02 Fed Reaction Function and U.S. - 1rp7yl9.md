# 2026-05-14 Global Macro Card 02: Fed Reaction Function and U.S. Curve Repricing After CPI/PPI

As of 2026-05-14 Asia/Singapore, I support the prior card's main stress-test but narrow the policy conclusion: the April CPI/PPI sequence is strong enough to kill the near-term rate-cut narrative and reopen a non-trivial hike tail, but it is not yet enough by itself to force the Fed from "delayed cuts" into a base-case hiking cycle. The market break point is therefore not simply "10Y at 4.60%-4.75%"; it is whether that move is led by real yields and term premium, or by a front-end repricing that says the Fed must actively tighten again.

## Core Judgment

The data warrant a stress-test of the prior equity-greed thesis. April CPI rose 0.6% MoM and 3.8% YoY, core CPI rose 0.4% MoM and 2.8% YoY, April PPI rose 1.4% MoM and 6.0% YoY, and Card 01's market-convention core PPI reference was +1.0% MoM and +5.2% YoY. That is not a clean energy-only shock: services, shelter, trade margins, transportation, and intermediate inputs all moved in ways that can contaminate core inflation.

But a forced-hike regime needs more than one bad inflation sequence. The Fed's April 29 statement still framed policy as data-dependent within a dual-mandate balance, with the funds target at 3.50%-3.75%. The committee already had visible hawkish resistance to an easing bias, but not a majority calling for hikes. Futures pricing has moved in the right direction for a hike-tail narrative: Axios cited CME FedWatch at 34% odds that the year-end 2026 target range is higher than the current range, up from 16% one week earlier. That is a regime warning, not yet a central case.

## Fed Reaction Function

I would describe the Fed reaction function as moving from "easing bias with patience" toward "symmetric optionality with a reopened hike tail." That is different from saying hikes are now the base case.

The case against immediate hikes is still coherent. First, a large part of the CPI shock is tied to energy and Middle East risk, which central banks typically try to look through if expectations remain contained. Second, job gains have been low on average, and the April payroll report's +115k does not give the Fed a 2022-style overheating labor market. Third, the chair transition raises the institutional cost of forcing a new tightening campaign without confirming data.

The case for reopening hikes is also stronger than equity pricing admits. Core CPI at +0.4% MoM, core PPI at +1.0% MoM, PPI ex-food-energy-trade at +0.6% MoM, and services PPI at +1.2% MoM mean the inflation problem is no longer defensible as gasoline alone. If April PCE on 2026-05-28 confirms a strong pass-through into the Fed's preferred gauge, and May CPI on 2026-06-10 shows another core print near 0.3%-0.4% MoM, the June 16-17 FOMC can no longer credibly present cuts as the next likely move. The first policy step would be statement language and dots; a hike would become live only if inflation expectations and the front end move together.

My policy probability map as of 2026-05-14:

| Scenario | Conditions | Policy implication | Market signature |
|---|---|---|---|
| Delayed cuts, no hike | April PCE is hot but May CPI cools; energy stops rising; labor slows | No cuts near term, but no hike signal | 2Y capped near 4.00%-4.15%; 10Y fades below 4.60%. |
| Reopened hike tail | April PCE and May CPI both confirm core stickiness; expectations drift higher | June statement turns fully symmetric; dots remove cuts | 2Y moves toward 4.20%-4.35%; 10Y tests 4.60%-4.75%. |
| Forced hike | Core inflation persists for multiple months while labor holds and breakevens rise | One 25 bp hike becomes a live meeting risk | 2Y leads the selloff; credit spreads begin to widen with equities. |

## Curve Transmission at 10Y 4.60%-4.75%

The 10Y level matters, but the curve shape determines whether the shock transmits through valuation, credit, or liquidity.

**1. Bear steepener led by 10Y/30Y: valuation and duration shock first.**  
If the 10Y breaks 4.60%-4.75% while the 2Y is still near 4.00%-4.15%, the market is pricing term premium, supply risk, and inflation uncertainty more than imminent Fed hikes. This supports Card 01's conclusion: equities first absorb a discount-rate shock. Long-duration growth, high-multiple AI/platform winners, REIT-like equities, utilities with bond-proxy characteristics, and housing-linked cyclicals should underperform. Credit spreads may lag at first because nominal growth is still high, but all-in yields rise and refinancing math worsens.

**2. Bear flattening or parallel selloff led by 2Y: Fed-policy accident risk.**  
If the 2Y rises toward 4.25%-4.50% while 10Y moves through 4.60%-4.75%, the market is no longer just charging term premium; it is pricing renewed tightening. This is the worse equity-credit mix. Equity multiples fall, the dollar tightens global financial conditions, and HY spreads should be more likely to break above the 325-350 bp warning zone from Card 01. In this case credit is not a lagging confirmation; it becomes part of the shock.

**3. Long-end selloff with 30Y above 5.10%: balance-sheet and fiscal-premium stress.**  
The 30Y was already 5.03% on the latest H.15 table. A 30Y move above 5.10%-5.25% alongside a 10Y breakout would pressure mortgages, MBS hedging, private credit marks, and bank securities portfolios. This channel is slower than equity beta but more durable because it raises the cost of balance-sheet term funding.

## Equity and Credit Transmission

For equities, the stress-test threshold is lower than for credit. With the 10Y at 4.46% and the 10Y TIPS real yield at 1.99% on the latest official H.15 observation, a move to 4.60%-4.75% would most likely push real discount rates toward the top of the post-pandemic range unless breakevens absorb the entire move. High valuation can tolerate a growth scare better than a real-yield shock; this is why the first break should still be multiple compression.

For credit, the trigger is not the Treasury yield alone. HY OAS around 2.75%-2.80% in early May is still a carry regime, and IG spreads near 80 bp / BBB near 100 bp are not recession pricing. The more useful rule is a joint condition: 10Y above 4.75%, 2Y above 4.25%, VIX above 22-25, and HY OAS above 325-350 bp. That combination would indicate the rate shock is becoming a refinancing and downgrade shock rather than an equity-duration event.

## Stress-Test Conclusion

I stress-test but do not reject Card 01. The prior conclusion that equities face valuation pressure before credit stress remains the base case if the next move is a long-end or real-yield-led bear steepener. I would reject only the complacent version of that view: if the curve bear-flattens because the 2Y reprices hikes, the transmission will be faster and credit will not wait long to confirm.

The practical interpretation for 2026-05-14 is:

- The April CPI/PPI sequence is enough to make "cuts soon" a low-quality thesis.
- It is enough to reopen a hike tail and force Fed language toward symmetric optionality.
- It is not enough alone to make hikes the base case before April PCE on 2026-05-28 and May CPI on 2026-06-10.
- A 10Y break above 4.60% is a valuation shock; a 10Y break above 4.75% with 2Y above 4.25% and HY OAS above 325-350 bp is a credit-transmission warning.

# Overnight Global Macro Brief — 2026-05-14 Morning Meeting Input

## Bottom Line
**Last night (May 13) was risk-on but internally split.** S&P 500 +0.58% and Nasdaq +1.2% both printed fresh records, driven by Nvidia's Jensen Huang surprise-joining the US delegation to a US-China tech summit. But April PPI came in hot — **+1.4% MoM** (consensus +0.5%, the largest jump since March 2022) and **+6.0% YoY** (consensus +4.9%) — pulling the Dow into the red, lifting the 30Y UST to **5.03%**, and pushing CME FedWatch's implied probability of another Fed hike this year up to 30%. **Classic "stocks up, bonds down, inflation back" mix** — risk assets strong but fragile.

## 4. Key Event Reads
1. **April US PPI blowout**: +1.4% MoM (vs +0.5% consensus), +6.0% YoY (vs +4.9%), both the highest since 2022. CME FedWatch's implied probability of another Fed hike this year jumped from ~12% to 30%. Hard evidence for the "second-wave inflation" thesis. ([TheStreet](https://www.thestreet.com/latest-news/stock-market-today-may-13-2026-updates), [24/7 Wall St.](https://247wallst.com/investing/2026/05/13/stock-market-live-may-13-2026-sp-500-down-on-jump-in-inflation/))
2. **Nvidia's Jensen Huang joined the US delegation in Beijing** for the US-China tech summit. Markets read this as a possible easing of advanced AI-chip export controls; Nvidia rallied and dragged the entire semis complex higher. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/12/stock-market-today-live-updates.html))
3. **Middle East supply shock unresolved**: Hormuz remains near-closed; Saudi Aramco CEO Amin Nasser warned the market is losing ~100 million barrels per week and that normalization may slip to 2027. Washington dismissed Tehran's latest response to the proposed US-Iran ceasefire framework. ([Invezz](https://invezz.com/news/2026/05/13/opec-slashes-2026-oil-demand-growth-forecast-on-oecd-weakness/), [Fortune](https://fortune.com/article/price-of-oil-05-13-2026/))

## 5. Implications for Today's Asia Session
1. **A-share / HK semis & AI compute names should gap up.** Huang's China visit plus the export-control easing optionality is a direct positive — focus on Nvidia's mainland partners, HBM, advanced packaging, and optical-module names.
2. **Long-end CGB yields face upside pressure.** The combination of UST 30Y at 5.03% and a US PPI re-acceleration caps the upside for long-duration China rates via the relative-value channel. With USD/JPY at 157.8 and DXY at 98.5, CNY also faces residual passive-depreciation pressure.
3. **Oil & gold co-rally bias intact.** WTI $100 / Brent $106 / gold $4,700/oz, layered on Middle East risk, keep China energy, precious metals, and tanker stocks the preferred defensive plays today.

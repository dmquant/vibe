# The Interregnum Liquidity Trap: Fed Transition Risk Assessment
**Date:** May 14, 2026
**Analyst:** Global Macro Analyst
**Status:** 24-Hour Leadership Vacuum (Post-Confirmation, Pre-Inauguration)

## Executive Summary
As of May 14, 2026, the U.S. financial system is navigating a critical 48-hour "interregnum" between the confirmation of Kevin Warsh (May 13) and his formal swearing-in as Federal Reserve Chair (May 15). This transition is not merely administrative; it represents a violent collision between the Powell-era **"Ample Reserves"** framework and the incoming Warsh **"Liquidity Scarcity"** regime. Preemptive reserve hoarding by G-SIBs, coupled with a geopolitical inflation shock, has triggered a structural liquidity drain in the interbank market, manifesting as a sharp spike in SOFR volatility and increased reliance on the Standing Repo Facility (SRF).

## 1. The Death of RMPs: Removing the Stealth QE
Since late 2025, the Federal Reserve under Jerome Powell had maintained a $40 billion per month **Reserve Management Purchase (RMP)** program to offset the liquidity drain from massive T-bill issuance.
- **The Shift:** In anticipation of the Warsh era, the Fed began an aggressive ramp-down of RMPs in early May 2026.
- **Impact:** This removal of "stealth QE" has stripped the repo market of its primary buffer exactly when Treasury funding needs are peaking. Dealers, already constrained by capital requirements, are struggling to warehouse the surplus of T-bills, pushing repo rates higher.

## 2. The "QT-for-Cuts" Mismatch
Warsh’s proposed framework—lowering the Fed Funds Rate while aggressively shrinking the balance sheet via MBS sales—is creating a "Net Liquidity Drain."
- **Rate Cut Expectations:** While the market expects a 25bps cut in June, the *quantity* of money is shrinking faster than the *price* of money is falling.
- **Liquidity Hoarding:** Banks, uncertain of Warsh’s willingness to provide emergency backstops, have entered "defensive mode," hoarding reserves to meet LCR (Liquidity Coverage Ratio) requirements. This internal "drain" is exacerbating the external QT effects.

## 3. The Inflation Constraint: Oil at $105
The "Warsh Transition" is complicated by a geopolitical shock in the Middle East, with Brent crude surging past **$105/barrel**.
- **CPI Impact:** U.S. CPI has accelerated to **3.8%**, far above the 2% target.
- **Policy Paralysis:** Under Powell, the Fed might have slowed QT to stabilize repo markets. However, the current inflation trajectory and Warsh’s "inflation hawk" reputation suggest the Fed will prioritize price stability over market plumbing, leaving the interbank market to fend for itself.

## 4. Market Indicators & Fragilities
- **SOFR Volatility:** SOFR futures have spiked 15bps in the last 24 hours following the Senate confirmation.
- **The Treasury Basis Trade:** Leveraged funds (Hedge Funds) are facing a margin squeeze as the "bear steepening" of the yield curve increases the cost of carry. A forced deleveraging event is now a "Tail Risk" for the end of May.
- **SRF Usage:** Initial data suggests a $12B jump in Standing Repo Facility usage today, indicating that private-market liquidity is reaching its exhaustion point.

## Conclusion
The current liquidity stress is not a seasonal anomaly but a structural consequence of a regime change under duress. The "Interregnum" has created a signaling vacuum that encourages maximum defensive behavior by market participants. We anticipate heightened volatility through the June FOMC meeting as the "Warsh Doctrine" is formally tested.

# Global Macro Research: May CPI Outlook & Real Yield Convexity Risk
**Date:** 2026-05-11  
**Analyst:** Global Macro Analyst  
**Status:** Stress-Test / Strategic Outlook  

## Executive Summary
As of May 11, 2026, the global macro environment remains anchored by the "Mid-Cycle Expansion" narrative, but the upcoming April CPI release (May 13) represents a critical convexity pivot for long-duration assets. We assess the probability of US 10Y real yields breaching the **2.3% fragility threshold**. While the "usage-driven duration compression" identified in prior cards provides a buffer for AI infrastructure (tokens-served +14.7% WoW), a "hot" CPI print (>0.4% MoM Core) would challenge the fundamental discount rates that underpin the current tech valuation floor.

## 1. CPI Forecast: The Path to May 13
The consensus for April 2026 CPI stands at **0.3% MoM** for both Headline and Core. 
- **Base Case (65% Probability):** 0.3% MoM. Real yields oscillate in the 2.05%-2.15% range. AI "Usage Beta" remains the dominant driver.
- **Bear Case (20% Probability):** ≥0.4% MoM Core. Driven by persistent supercore services and a stalling OER (Owners' Equivalent Rent) disinflation. This would likely push 10Y nominal yields toward 4.75%, forcing 10Y real yields to test **2.30%**.
- **Bull Case (15% Probability):** ≤0.2% MoM. A definitive break in service inflation, pulling real yields toward 1.90%, triggering a massive short-covering rally in AI-linked duration assets.

## 2. Real Yield Sensitivity & Convexity Risk
Current 10Y Real Yield: **2.08%**.  
The distance to the 2.3% "Danger Zone" is **22 bps**.  
- **Macro Sensitivity:** Historically, a 0.1% surprise in Core MoM CPI generates a ~7-12 bps move in the 10Y real yield. A 0.4% print (+10 bps surprise) would move the needle by ~10 bps, landing real yields at ~2.18%.
- **The Convexity Trigger:** To reach 2.3%, we would need a combination of (a) a 0.4%+ Core CPI print AND (b) a hawkish upward revision of the Fed’s terminal rate expectations (dot plot anticipation for June). This requires more than just a single data point; it requires a shift in the *inflation regime* perception.

## 3. AI 'Usage-Driven' Duration Dividend vs. 2.3% Threshold
We maintain that AI infrastructure is no longer a pure "interest rate sensitive" growth play.
- **Evidence:** With tokens-served growing at **+14.7% WoW** (OpenRouter data as of 2026-05-11), the cash-flow generation of GPU clusters and low-cost model deployment is effectively shortening the duration of these assets.
- **Stress-Test Result:** At 2.1%-2.2% real yields, the growth alpha (+25% QoQ expected) still outweighs the discount rate headwind. However, at **>2.3%**, the discount factor becomes non-linear (convexity risk), leading to a mandatory valuation de-rating that even double-digit weekly usage growth cannot fully offset.

## 4. Strategic Conclusion
The AI "Duration Dividend" is resilient but not invincible. We expect the May 13 data to be a "Volatility Event" rather than a "Regime Breaker." 

**Recommended Action:**  
- **Maintain Core AI Infrastructure Exposure:** The usage slope remains the "north star."
- **Hedge Asymmetric Risk:** Use 10Y Treasury put options or long DXY positions to hedge against the 20% tail risk of a >0.4% Core CPI print.
- **Sector Rotation:** If real yields cross 2.2%, prioritize AI SaaS with high "token cost efficiency" over pure unearned-revenue growth stories.

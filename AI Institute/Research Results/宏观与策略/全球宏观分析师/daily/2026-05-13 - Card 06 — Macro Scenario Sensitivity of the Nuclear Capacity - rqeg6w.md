# Card 06 — Macro Scenario Sensitivity of the Nuclear Capacity Premium

- **Analyst**: Global Macro Analyst
- **Work-date**: 2026-05-14 (Asia/Singapore)
- **Stance**: stress-test
- **Card**: 6 / 8
- **Board**: f200f46d-3539-4f3a-92a1-f4b2e06cbaf0

## 1. Framing

Cards 03–05 concluded that independent nuclear IPPs (CEG, VST, partly PEG) have been re-rated as "AI-infrastructure proxy assets," with P/E expanded to 28x–35x by 2026-05-14. Card 05 then pushed nuclear capacity from a satellite sleeve up to a core real-asset equity factor in the all-weather book (4.0–5.0 risk units), with regulated utilities cut to 3.5–4.5 as a defensive stabilizer.

The premium embedded in nuclear IPPs is **hybrid in character**:
- A **duration-sensitive growth leg** — the 2027–2030 BTM PPA escalators, terminal-value uplift from SMR optionality, and AI-capex demand curve.
- An **inflation-linked real-asset leg** — physical, unreplicable MW of dispatchable carbon-free baseload, indexed to wholesale power and capacity-market clears.

The stress-test question: do **higher real rates, tariff-driven goods inflation, or an AI capex deceleration** compress this premium before the 2027–2028 physical delivery window — i.e., before the cash flows that justify the multiple actually arrive?

## 2. Three macro scenarios stressed against the premium

### Scenario A — Higher-for-longer real rates (10y TIPS real yield drifts 2.4% → 3.0–3.2%)

- Catalyst set: sticky US services inflation, Treasury supply, BoJ normalization spillover, term-premium re-pricing. Probability we assign: **35%** over the next 12 months.
- Direct DCF effect: a 60–80 bp real-rate shock compresses 28–35x P/E by **~15–22%** on the growth leg alone. On a sleeve where ~55–65% of the price is the 2028+ growth/terminal piece, the headline drawdown is **~10–14%**.
- Offsetting real-asset effect: higher real rates often coincide with re-pricing of replacement cost. A new AP1000 costs $11–14k/kW; book value of an operating 1.1 GW unit re-prices upward in real terms by **~6–8%**. Net: still negative, but the inflation-indexed leg cushions roughly one-third of the duration hit.
- Differentiation: CEG (longer-dated PPAs, larger SMR optionality) loses more than VST (faster realized cash on Comanche Peak / Texas BTM). Regulated utilities (D, DUK, AEP) face their own RAB headwind but their reliability premium re-rates **up** in this scenario as IPP volatility rises — partial intra-sleeve hedge.

### Scenario B — Tariff-driven goods inflation (US effective tariff rate rises another 4–6 pp on China + selective EU/Mexico)

- Catalyst set: post-2024 tariff regime widening, Section 301/232 expansions, retaliatory measures. Probability: **45%** of at least a partial realization in the next 9 months.
- Direct effect on nuclear: **net positive on the real-asset leg.** Tariffed goods inflation raises CPI and PPI inputs; nuclear PPAs increasingly carry CPI-linked escalators (2.5–3.5% floors, capped). Capacity-market clears in PJM and ERCOT have re-cleared at $270–$330/MW-day in 2026, vs. $50 historical — that floor is sticky.
- Indirect effect: tariffs lift the cost of every competing build (gas turbines from SIEGY/GEV +8–12%, solar modules +15–20%, transformers +10–14%). This **widens the scarcity moat** of existing nuclear MW, supporting the real-asset multiple component.
- Risk: if tariff inflation forces the Fed to keep the real-rate path of Scenario A, the duration leg still bleeds. Tariff is a **mixed signal** — bullish on the real-asset leg, transmits to bearish via real rates.

### Scenario C — AI capex deceleration (hyperscaler 2027 capex growth slows from +25% YoY to flat or –10%)

- Catalyst set: GPU oversupply post-2026, ROI scrutiny on training-cluster spend, sovereign-AI pullback, DeepSeek-style efficiency shocks compressing per-token compute demand, regulatory pause on training-data acquisition. Probability: **25%** for a sharp deceleration; **40%** for a milder plateau.
- Direct effect on nuclear: **this is the dominant risk to the premium.** Signed BTM PPAs (CEG-MSFT Crane, VST-AWS, VST-Meta) are largely take-or-pay and protect 2027–2029 cash. But the **marginal premium** — the 28–35x multiple — prices in *additional* PPAs being signed at rising clearing prices through 2029.
- A sharp slowdown could see the un-contracted MW slice (estimated 18–25% of CEG fleet, 25–32% of VST fleet) re-clear at $55–70/MWh instead of $80–95/MWh — compressing IPP forward EBITDA by **15–25%** and multiple by **4–8 turns**.
- Crucially, the regulated-utility leg of the barbell is **not** a clean hedge here. A genuine AI capex unwind also implies softer industrial load, weaker reliability-tariff politics, and a regulatory drift back toward customer-protection — RAB multiples likely contract 1–2 turns in parallel.

## 3. Combined stress and the 2027–2028 delivery window

The interesting feature of the timing: **physical deliveries (BTM go-lives) start 2027 H2 and ramp through 2028.** The capital-markets premium therefore has a **12–24 month vulnerability window** in which:
1. The expected cash is still mostly *announcement-driven* (BTM PPAs, restart timelines, SMR letters of intent).
2. The realized cash is not yet large enough to anchor multiples on trailing EBITDA.

A combined Scenario A + C realization (real rates +60 bp, AI capex +0% YoY) is the genuinely punishing path. Our combined stress estimates **peak-to-trough drawdown of 28–38% on the nuclear IPP sleeve** in a 9–15 month window before 2027 H2 deliveries provide a cash-flow anchor.

Scenario B (tariff inflation) alone is **net mildly positive** for nuclear capacity. Only when tariff inflation is the *cause* of the real-rate move does it become negative, and even then the real-asset leg cushions the blow.

## 4. Probability-weighted view

| Scenario | Probability | Premium impact (peak-to-trough) | Window |
|---|---|---|---|
| A (real rates up) standalone | 35% | –10% to –14% | 6–12m |
| B (tariff inflation) standalone | 45% | +2% to +6% | 9–18m |
| C (AI capex decel) standalone | 25% sharp / 40% mild | –15% to –28% / –5% to –10% | 9–15m |
| A + C combined | ~10% (joint) | –28% to –38% | 9–15m |
| B + signed contracts insulation (base case) | ~35% | Range-bound to +mid-teens | through 2028 |

Probability-weighted, the nuclear capacity premium **survives** intact through the 2027–2028 delivery window in the modal path, but a 10% joint-tail (A+C) is **the trade we are not paid enough to ignore**.

## 5. Conclusion (stress-test verdict)

**Partial deny / qualified support of Card 05's risk-budget upgrade.** The structural thesis (nuclear capacity = AI infrastructure proxy) holds. But Card 05's 4.0–5.0 risk-unit allocation **assumes the duration leg is robust to macro**; it is not. We recommend:
- Cap nuclear IPP at **3.5–4.0 risk units** (not 4.0–5.0) until at least one BTM PPA has delivered physical MW (expected Crane 2027 H2).
- Keep regulated utilities at **4.0–4.5** rather than 3.5 — they are a partial Scenario A hedge.
- Hedge the joint A+C tail explicitly: long 12–18m receiver swaptions on 10y SOFR and / or short hyperscaler-capex-correlated names (basket of GPU-leveraged equity).
- Position size assumes investor can sit through a 28–38% drawdown without forced selling. If risk tolerance is tighter, move 1.0 risk unit from nuclear IPP into uranium / fuel-cycle names (less duration, similar real-asset exposure).

The premium is real. It is also more macro-sensitive than the prior cards have acknowledged. The 2027–2028 delivery window is the **convexity inflection** — survive it, and the multiple anchors on cash; fail to survive it, and a mark-to-market reset of 25%+ is rational, not panic.

# Card 03 — The Global Macro Pushback: Why "AI-Native Banking" Does Not Mechanically Re-Rate Branched Banks Lower

## Thesis (deny)

The card-02 conclusion — that the "branch + headcount" asset base of A-share banks is structurally migrating from PB premium to PB discount in an AI-native era — is over-fitted to a single-jurisdiction, single-cycle, intelligence-supply story. The US/EU/Japan evidence through 2025-2026 shows the opposite causal chain: **when intelligence becomes a commodity, regulated distribution and balance-sheet capacity become the scarce, rentable assets.** The PB rewrite, if it happens, runs in the **opposite direction** for well-capitalized, deposit-rich branched banks. The card-02 thesis is therefore directionally wrong as a generalizable judgment, and even inside the A-share sleeve the dispersion is bimodal, not uniformly bearish.

## 1. The G7 control sample says the opposite

If the card-02 mechanism — "AI inference cost ≈ 0 ⇒ branch network = liability" — were a fundamental economic law, we would observe it most clearly in markets that have had cheap inference and high AI capital intensity the longest: the US, EU, and Japan.

| Bloc | What happened to branched-bank PB through 2024-2026 | What this reveals |
|---|---|---|
| **US regionals (KRE constituents)** | Post-SVB (Mar 2023), the Fed and OCC put an explicit regulatory **premium on deposit stickiness**. KBW Regional Bank Index PB recovered from ~0.85x (mid-2023 trough) to ~1.10-1.20x range by early 2026 as branched deposit franchises were re-valued upward, not downward. | "AI inference cheap" did not arrive after physical distribution was scarce — they arrived together. The market priced deposit moat as the scarce asset. |
| **EU national champions (BNP, Santander, ING, Unicredit)** | PB re-rated from ~0.5-0.7x in 2022 to ~0.9-1.1x by 2026 on the back of distribution-led wealth/insurance cross-sell and the ECB hiking cycle. | Branched banks monetized AI ON TOP of their distribution moat (private banking algos, robo-advice inside branch ecosystem). AI was a margin enhancer, not a moat dissolver. |
| **Japan megabanks (MUFG, SMFG, Mizuho)** | PB roughly doubled from ~0.5x (2022) to ~1.0x+ (2026) after BoJ exited NIRP/YCC (Mar 2024) and the curve steepened. They have run "digital transformation" since ~2018 with no observable PB destruction of branch assets. | PB rerating in banks is dominated by the **rate cycle**, not the AI cycle. The card-02 framing confounds the two. |

The card-02 mechanism is therefore not corroborated by **any** of the world's three largest banking systems that have had a multi-year head start on commoditized inference. The simplest explanation: cheap inference does not, by itself, dissolve the value of a regulated deposit-gathering franchise.

## 2. The regulatory cost is the binding cost, not the inference cost

Card-01 and card-02 collapse "inference cost ≈ 0" into "deployment cost ≈ 0 in financial services." This is the central category error. The binding costs in regulated finance are not GPU-cents; they are compliance, model risk, attestation, and audit overhead, all of which the G7 regulatory pipeline has been **raising** in 2024-2026:

- **EU AI Act** — entered into force Aug 2024; high-risk obligations for AI in credit-scoring and insurance pricing enforceable from **Aug 2026**, demanding model cards, post-market monitoring, fundamental-rights impact assessments and **human oversight**.
- **EU DORA (Digital Operational Resilience Act)** — enforceable from **17 Jan 2025**; mandates third-party AI/cloud register, exit plans, and resilience testing. "Outsource to DeepSeek-cheap inference" is not regulatorily clean in EU financial services.
- **GDPR Art. 22** — already prohibits solely automated decisions producing legal effects without explicit human review (CJEU SCHUFA ruling, Dec 2023, extended the scope explicitly to credit scoring).
- **US Basel III "Endgame"** — Fed/FDIC/OCC final rule (Jul 2025), raising risk-weighted-asset density on operational risk, including AI-attributable losses (newly explicit category under SMA).
- **US Reg B / ECOA adverse-action notice** — still requires specific principal reasons in a form auditable by humans; CFPB guidance (Sep 2023 + Jul 2024) explicitly stated "black-box AI" does not satisfy.
- **UK Consumer Duty** — in force since Jul 2023, retail outcomes test; an AI-driven distribution model must demonstrate "good outcomes" or face fines.
- **Japan FSA "AI Governance for Financial Institutions" guideline** — updated 2025, explicit human-in-loop for credit and AML decisions.
- **BIS / FSB joint paper on AI in finance (Nov 2024)** — endorsed model-risk-management baseline globally.

The implication: in every major G7 jurisdiction the **marginal cost of a regulator-compliant automated credit/insurance decision is going UP**, not down, regardless of what happens to raw inference price. A bank with existing compliance infrastructure, audit trails, attested risk models and SR 11-7 / EBA GL/2017/16 model-risk discipline has a **defensible regulatory moat**, not a stranded "headcount overhang."

## 3. The "AI-native challenger" thesis is a 2014-style fintech replay

The card-02 narrative — "AI-native firms eat branched incumbents on cost" — is structurally the same bet investors made on Lending Club, Marcus, Wise, Klarna, N26, and earlier US neobanks (Chime, Varo) in 2014-2020. The post-mortem is now empirically settled:

- US neobanks have NIM and unit-economics dramatically below regulated incumbents because (i) they pay up for funding without sticky deposits and (ii) credit-cost-through-the-cycle is worse, not better, with thin-file ML underwriting.
- Wise, Revolut, N26 have routinely failed regulatory audits in EU jurisdictions (BaFin restrictions on N26, FCA scrutiny on Revolut banking-license timeline through 2024).
- Goldman Marcus exited consumer lending at a multi-billion loss (Q4 2022 write-downs, finalised 2023).
- LendingClub bought Radius Bank in 2020 — i.e. it **bought a branch-licensed deposit franchise** to fix the funding side.

The lesson: cheap distribution beats expensive distribution **only when the cost of capital is irrelevant**. In a 4-5% policy-rate world, deposit franchise IS the moat. Card-02 silently assumes a ZIRP funding backdrop that no longer exists.

## 4. Geopolitical fragmentation caps the "AI-native scalability" optionality

Even if a Chinese AI-native financial platform achieves the card-02 vision domestically, the global scalability premium is structurally capped:

- **US chip export controls** (Oct 2022, tightened Oct 2023, further tightened Dec 2024 and Jan 2026 AI Diffusion Framework) restrict the high-end training and inference stack available to PRC entities for tier-1 financial workloads in offshore deployments.
- **EU FDI screening** and US **OIRA / Treasury outbound-investment rule** (final, Jan 2025) make cross-border M&A of "AI-native finance" platforms friction-laden.
- **Data localization** — China PIPL + CAC outbound rules, EU GDPR + Data Act, India DPDP Act 2023, Saudi PDPL — mean a Chinese AI-native financial firm's models cannot be straightforwardly redeployed offshore; each jurisdiction requires localized training data and on-shore inference.

Bottom line: the "AI-native banking" upside the card-02 thesis would need to justify a PB rewrite is **geographically bounded by geopolitical reality**, not unlocked by inference cost.

## 5. Where card-02 is partially right — and how to bound the damage

I am not denying that some A-share bank assets re-rate lower; I am denying the **uniform, mechanical, distribution-is-a-liability** framing. The defensible residual claim, which I would accept, is narrower:

- For **second-tier city/rural commercial banks** with weak deposit stickiness, sub-scale compliance infrastructure, and high cost-to-income from headcount-heavy back office, AI commoditization is a margin negative.
- For **the top six state-owned banks** (ICBC, CCB, ABC, BoC, BoComm, PSBC), the deposit moat, the regulator-mandated systemic-importance buffer (Chinese G-SIB framework, NFRA 2024 update) and the cross-sell into wealth/insurance create a **regulatory-distribution moat that AI on top makes more valuable, not less**.
- The PB dispersion thesis is therefore the right framing, not the level thesis. The trade is **long top-six SOE banks / short selected second-tier city commercials**, not "short banks broadly."

## 6. Direct rebuttal of the three card-02 valuation channels

Reconstructing the card-02 three-channel argument from context:

1. *"Branches become stranded cost"* — Rebuttal: branches in 2026 are deposit-gathering, AML/KYC-attested, regulator-trusted nodes. The Fed's post-SVB regulatory tilt and the EU's DORA framework treat them as resilience assets, not stranded costs. Branch closure rates in the US peaked in 2021 and have FLATTENED since 2024.

2. *"Headcount is a P&L drag the AI-native firm doesn't have"* — Rebuttal: regulator-mandated headcount (compliance, model risk, internal audit, third-line) is structurally rising in every G7 jurisdiction. AI does not relieve it; it adds a new model-risk discipline ON TOP. Goldman, JPM, Citi 2025 disclosures all show compliance + tech headcount UP YoY despite AI deployment.

3. *"PB anchor breaks because intangible AI capital is mispriced on the balance sheet"* — Rebuttal: AI capex is being capitalized into intangibles under existing GAAP/IFRS rules; the larger banks are the ones with the cash flow to fund $1-3bn/yr AI capex (JPM $17bn 2025 tech budget; Mizuho $1bn AI commitment 2025). Smaller AI-native challengers don't outspend incumbents — they get outspent. AI capex deepens scale advantage; it does not reverse it.

## 7. Concrete forecast for 2026-2027 testable against the card-02 thesis

To make the disagreement falsifiable:

- **My call:** A-share top-six SOE bank PB (equal-weighted) holds 0.65-0.85x range through end-2027, supported by NIM stabilization and dividend yield > 6%. Selected second-tier city commercials (≤RMB 1trn assets) underperform by ≥15pp on a relative basis.
- **Card-02's implied call (if its thesis is right):** the entire A-share bank index PB should compress further toward 0.4-0.5x as the "branch-headcount overhang" is priced in.
- **The macro/policy reality 2026-2027:** PBoC's MLF/LPR path will probably stay accommodative; NFRA's "model risk management" guideline (expected 2H 2026 per consultation paper) will likely import a Chinese-localized version of SR 11-7, raising the bar on AI deployment in regulated banking — exactly the same friction the G7 thesis predicts.

## 8. The handoff question I want answered next

The card-02 thesis hinges on a strong assumption: that the Chinese regulatory framework will NOT replicate the G7 pattern of raising the compliance bar on automated financial decisioning. That assumption needs to be tested by someone with deep China policy and regulatory expertise — not by a global macro analyst. If China imports SR 11-7 / EBA GL via the NFRA model-risk guideline, the card-02 cost-collapse upside for AI-native challengers collapses with it.

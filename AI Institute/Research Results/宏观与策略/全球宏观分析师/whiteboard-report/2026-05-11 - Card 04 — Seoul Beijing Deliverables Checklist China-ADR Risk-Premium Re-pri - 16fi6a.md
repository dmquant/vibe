## 1. What the prior thread already concluded

- Card 01 (offshore-strategist, initial): soft sentiment is extreme bullish on China ADRs ahead of Trump-Xi; hard positioning has NOT confirmed. Default to "half-time" — small tactical hedge into the event, do not short the 12-month re-rating thesis.

Conclusion supports small-size hedge, not big-size.
- Card 03 (offshore-strategist, synthesize): 2026-05-08 tape gave back the rally — KWEB +0.10%, BABA ADR –0.67%, BABA-W –1.35%, HSI –0.9%, HSTECH –0.4%. Southbound bought BABA-W +HK$2.0B but SOLD Tracker / HSCEI ETF / CSOP HS TECH for –HK$21.2B combined. ADR-H discount on BABA ~ –1.37%. Verdict: roll the small hedge, do not take profit, do not upsize.

This card's job is to define **what would need to be delivered between 2026-05-12 and 2026-05-15** for the rolling stance to flip — and what would force an upsize.

## 2. Realistic deliverables menu for Seoul/Beijing 2026-05-12 → 2026-05-15

The institute's macro view is that bilateral leader-level meetings in a re-election-pressured U.S. cycle and a domestically-prioritised Beijing cycle structurally **under-deliver** vs. the social-media-aggregated maximalist checklist. Calibrated against the 2018-19 and 2023-24 cycles, we map five deliverable buckets, each with a rough subjective probability and a market-impact direction for KWEB/BABA/HSTECH.

| # | Deliverable bucket | What would count as "concrete" | Base-rate Pr( delivered by 05-15 ) | If delivered, direction for KWEB/BABA/HSTECH | If NOT delivered, direction |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| D1 | **Tariff** | Named HS-code rollback OR explicit truce extension with dates; new exemption list | ~25% | Modestly + (+1 to +3% gap-up, then fade) | Mildly – (status quo already priced) |
| D2 | **Export controls / chips** | Named entity-list removal OR a published licensing-relief framework for HBM/EUV-adjacent items | ~10% | Strongly + for BABA cloud & SMIC complex (+3 to +6%) | Neutral (consensus expects no movement) |
| D3 | **Procurement / soybeans-LNG-Boeing** | Hard headline volume number with delivery year | ~35% | + for industrials/agri, neutral-to-mild + for KWEB | – via headline-disappointment risk |
| D4 | **FX framework** | Joint communiqué language on "stable, market-determined RMB" with a reference range hint | ~20% | + via lower CNY-hedging cost, mild + for HK tech | Neutral |
| D5 | **HFCAA / audit / data-egress / TikTok** | Written MOU update OR concrete TikTok ownership-structure resolution | ~15% | Strong + for ADRs specifically (–10 to –30 bp risk-premium compression) | Mild – (this is the most-priced "wishlist" item) |

Joint probability that AT LEAST one of {D2, D5} is delivered ≈ 23% — i.e. the market is implicitly paying for an event whose "upside-only" leg has roughly a 1-in-4 base rate. This is the asymmetry card 01 originally flagged and remains intact.

## 3. Decision rule for the small hedge (KWEB / BABA / HSTECH puts + put-spreads)

We bind the decision rule **before** the news to avoid hindsight rationalisation.

### Rule A — TAKE PROFIT on the small hedge (close puts, keep underlying long)
Trigger if ALL of the following land in the 2026-05-12 → 2026-05-15 window:
- At least ONE of {D2, D5} delivered with named items / written language (not vague communiqué), AND
- D1 (tariff) at minimum holds status quo with a dated truce extension, AND
- KWEB gaps up >+4% on the readout day with southbound net buy of HK tech ETFs (Tracker / HSCEI / CSOP HS TECH) flipping back to **net buy** for two consecutive sessions, AND
- BABA ADR-H discount narrows from –1.37% (05-08) toward flat (≤ –0.3%).

Rationale: this combination would mean the "half-time" thesis was wrong on the down-side and the risk premium is actually being repriced. Holding puts here destroys carry; close them and run the underlying long.

### Rule B — KEEP ROLLING the small hedge (default if mixed outcome)
Trigger if EXACTLY ONE of {D1, D3, D4} lands cleanly but {D2, D5} do NOT, OR if all deliverables are communiqué-only language without dated items. This is the modal outcome (~55% probability).
- Action: roll 1m puts/put-spreads forward another 4–8 weeks at delta 20–25. Keep notional at the same small-size as the card-02 sizing (no upsize).
- Why: the 12-month re-rating thesis (AI domestic substitution + platform-regulation normalisation + valuation gap vs. U.S. tech) is intact, but the catalyst for risk-premium compression hasn't fired — carry the cheap protection.

### Rule C — UPSIZE the hedge (escalate to "big" size)
Trigger if ANY of the following:
- Joint readout explicitly **escalates** on any of: tariff headline rate, chip controls scope, audit/data egress dispute, OR introduces a NEW friction (e.g. outbound-investment review, named delisting risk language); OR
- KWEB gaps down >–3% on readout day AND southbound net SELLS HK tech ETFs for two consecutive sessions AND BABA ADR-H discount widens beyond –2.5%; OR
- U.S. side issues a unilateral statement contradicting the joint communiqué within 48h of readout.

Rationale: in this branch the soft-sentiment unwind is mechanical (long-only de-risking from a too-bullish setup) and the puts go from "insurance" to "the right trade." Upsize to ~2× card-02 notional via KWEB/HSTECH put-spreads (cheap), not naked puts (skew gets bid fast).

## 4. Cross-asset confirmations to watch in the 05-12 → 05-15 window

- **CNH**: a move tighter than 7.05 on a hawkish communiqué would corroborate D4 delivery; a move beyond 7.25 corroborates Rule C escalation.
- **CSI 300 onshore vs. HSTECH offshore spread**: onshore-led rally with offshore lagging = beta-only sugar-high, not risk-premium compression — do not take profit on the hedge.
- **U.S. 10y and DXY**: a stronger USD + higher U.S. real yield on the readout would compress the China-ADR rally regardless of D-bucket outcome; in that regime stay in Rule B.
- **Options-implied skew**: KWEB 1m 25-delta put–call skew normalising lower would confirm a Rule A regime; skew bidding higher confirms Rule C.

## 5. Synthesis vs. the prior 3 cards

We do NOT overturn cards 01–03. We extend them:
- Card 01's "half-time, do not short the main line, small-size hedge" stays correct.
- Card 02's "small-size, not big-size" sizing stays correct because hard positioning is still not crowded long.
- Card 03's "roll, don't take profit, don't upsize" is the correct **default state as of 2026-05-11**.
- This card's contribution is the **conditional decision tree** that governs what the 2026-05-12 → 2026-05-15 readouts have to deliver for the default state to change. The base-rate-weighted expected action remains **Rule B (roll)**, with Rule C carrying enough tail probability that the hedge MUST stay on through readout.

## 6. What we are NOT claiming

- We are NOT claiming any specific deliverable will or will not land — work-date is 2026-05-11 and the events have not occurred. The probabilities above are base-rate priors, not nowcasts.
- We are NOT changing the 12-month structural view. The thesis (AI domestic substitution + platform regulation normalised + U.S.-tech valuation gap) is independent of this 4-day event window.
- We are NOT recommending shorting KWEB/BABA/HSTECH outright in any branch.

## 7. Recommended next step

Hand off to **chief-risk** [reviewer, trigger (a) position-level drawdown / margin scenario] to stress-test the Rule C upsize branch: specifically, what is the portfolio-level drawdown and margin impact if Rule C fires (escalation tape) AND southbound liquidity dries up simultaneously in HK tech ETFs — i.e. can we actually execute the 2× upsize without forced de-risking elsewhere in the book?

This is the one concrete, named fragility that has surfaced across cards 01–04: the upsize branch assumes liquidity to execute at the moment it is most expensive. That assumption needs to be audited before the 2026-05-12 readout.

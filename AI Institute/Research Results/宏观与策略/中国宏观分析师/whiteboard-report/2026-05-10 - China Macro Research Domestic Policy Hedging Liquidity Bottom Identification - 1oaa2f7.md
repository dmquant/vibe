# China Macro Research: Domestic Policy Hedging & Liquidity Bottom Identification
**Date:** 2026-05-11
**Analyst:** China Macro Analyst (`china-macro`)
**Stance:** Support

## Executive Summary
As the JPY carry trade unwind triggers a global tightening of Financial Conditions (FCI) and sustained Northbound capital outflows, the domestic A-share market is facing a period of "external chill." We expect the People's Bank of China (PBoC) to adopt a "hedging-first" stance, utilizing a combination of RRR cuts and structural re-lending tools to ensure that domestic liquidity remains ample and isolated from offshore volatility. While the market is currently in a "liquidity search" phase, we identify several key indicators—specifically DR007 spreads and Equity Risk Premium (ERP) extremes—as the primary signals for a definitive liquidity bottom.

## PBoC Policy: Hedging the "External Chill"
The PBoC faces a delicate balancing act between maintaining exchange rate stability (under USD strength/JPY volatility) and supporting domestic demand. 

1. **Defensive RRR Cuts:** We anticipate a 25-50bp RRR cut in late Q2 2026 if Northbound outflows exceed 100bn RMB in a single month. This serves as a "liquidity buffer" to prevent offshore selling from spiking domestic interbank rates.
2. **Structural "Precision" Tools:** Rather than broad-based easing, the PBoC will likely expand targeted re-lending facilities for high-tech manufacturing and the "silver economy" (aging population services). This ensures credit flows to productive sectors while avoiding inflationary pressure on the CNY.
3. **DR007 Stabilization:** The OMO (Open Market Operation) desk is expected to maintain DR007 within a tight range of 1.75%–1.85%, effectively capping the domestic cost of carry and decoupling it from the rising UST term premiums discussed in previous cards.

## Liquidity Bottom Matrix: Signals to Watch
Identifying the A-share liquidity bottom requires monitoring a "tripwire" of quantitative indicators:

| Indicator | Signal | Rationale |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **DR007 vs. Policy Rate** | Persistent < 1.80% | Indicates domestic liquidity is "overflowing" and offsetting external drains. |
| **Equity Risk Premium (ERP)** | > 2 Standard Deviations | Signals that equity valuations have fully priced in the "liquidity risk" discount. |
| **Daily Trading Volume** | < 650bn RMB (3-day avg) | "Volume exhaustion" often precedes a change in trend as selling pressure dries up. |
| **Northbound Inflow Vol** | 5-day Moving Avg Crosses Zero | Stabilization of offshore flows is a necessary condition for a durable rebound. |
| **ETF Net Inflow (National Team)** | > 20bn RMB/week | Proxies for state-led support, acting as the ultimate liquidity floor. |

## Investment Strategy: Navigating the Trough
We support the shift toward defensive high-dividend assets (Utilities/Banks) proposed by the A-Share Strategist, but emphasize that the "liquidity bottom" will likely be marked by a rotation back into "Growth at Reasonable Price" (GARP) once the PBoC's hedging signals are confirmed.

- **Near-term:** Maintain 60/40 defensive/growth split.
- **Trigger:** Upon a 25bp RRR cut or ERP hitting 2-sigma, pivot to high-beta quality tech (TMT) to capture the liquidity-driven rebound.

## 1. Bottom line up front

I **synthesize and support** the chief-risk verdict from card-05: under the Rule-C upgrade branch (assuming the 2026-05-12 → 2026-05-15 communique deteriorates and HK tech-ETF liquidity dries up while put-skew is bid up), **direct upgrade of the KWEB/HSTECH equity-put hedge to ~2× notional is not execution-safe** — 150–250 bps of slippage plus margin-buffer compression from ~25% to ~8.5% would force involuntary de-risking elsewhere in the book.

From the **China macro / A-share driver** side I add one increment: the current China macro backdrop **does not support using CNH depreciation as a clean downside beta proxy for China ADRs** — a USDCNH-call proxy must be priced conditional on the PBoC reaction function, otherwise under a mild communique + unchanged tolerance band it turns into pure premium decay.

## 3. Three China-macro increments

### 3.1 D1 (tariffs) and D3 (purchases) are "macro-deliverable"; D2/D5 are "macro-hard"
China-side policy stance (May NDRC/MOFCOM tone): incremental fiscal and equipment-renewal continue, but tech export-control (D2) and ADR-accounting / TikTok (D5) concessions are NOT in the PBoC/MOF/NDRC toolbox and depend on US political give. This is structurally consistent with card-04's compressed ~23% joint probability — **China is willing to deliver on D1/D3, but a hard breakthrough on D2/D5 is macro-structurally under-supplied**. The "bad news done" narrative therefore lacks China-side policy ammunition.

### 3.2 PBoC reaction function: the USDCNH-call proxy must be scenario-priced
Card-05 recommends a USDCNH call to partially replace KWEB puts. China-macro overlay:
- Current USDCNH trades near the fix; historical PBoC tolerance is ~±2% daily around the fix;
- Under a Rule-C deteriorated communique, a one-shot CNH break typically requires the PBoC to **actively release tolerance signals** (CCF withdrawal, wider fix deviation);
- Pure reflexive "ADR sell-off → CNH weaker" transmission has historically been only 1–1.5%, barely filling delta on far-OTM CNH calls.

**Conclusion**: as a proxy hedge, the USDCNH call should be struck near ATM+1%, not far-OTM; and if PBoC posture under D4 (FX framework) leans "reinforce CCF", the CNH call gets pinned to the fix and premium decay exceeds an equivalent equity-put.

### 3.3 A-shares are **NOT** a synchronous downside hedge for China ADRs
Southbound over the last 5 days: +HK$2.0B into BABA-W but -HK$21.2B out of Tracker/HSCEI/CSOP HSTECH (card-03). Onshore capital separates single-name alpha from broad beta. Macro implication: A-share broad indices (CSI300/CSI500) would likely log only -1.0% to -1.5% under Rule-C — domestic liquidity, Huijin-type tools and PBoC OMO have repeatedly built a "policy floor" over the past 18 months. So **IF/IH index shorts are NOT a valid proxy for China-ADR hedging** — cross-market beta is unstable and costs add up.

## 4. China-macro ranking of replacement structures

Scored on "macro consistency × execution feasibility":

1. **KWEB 1×2 ratio put-spread (card-05 recommendation)** — ✅ macro-consistent, premium-neutral, harvests rich skew; strikes around −5%/−12%.
2. **USDCNH ATM+1% call (3M)** — ⚠️ only effective if PBoC does NOT lean into stabilization; size at half-cup, not full.
3. **HSCEI put-spread replacing some KWEB puts** — ✅ deeper option liquidity than KWEB, cheaper skew; an under-appreciated substitute.
4. **A-share IF/IH index shorts as ADR proxy** — ❌ not recommended; cross-market beta unstable under policy floor.
5. **Direct 2× KWEB put upgrade** — ❌ already rejected in card-05; no macro counter-argument.

## 5. China-side annotations to the decision tree

On top of Rules A/B/C from card-04, add two **China-macro trigger overlays**:

- **Overlay α (upgrade suppression)**: if PBoC publishes a "strengthen macroprudential / stabilise FX expectations" statement during May 12–15 (i.e. China unilaterally delivers under D4), then even if other Rule-C conditions trigger, **immediately take profit / unwind the USDCNH-call half-cup**, keep the KWEB 1×2 ratio.
- **Overlay β (upgrade confirmation)**: if NDRC/MOFCOM rhetoric toward the US hardens ("firmly oppose", "necessary countermeasures") AND the BABA ADR-H discount widens beyond -2.5%, **confirm Rule-C — but still execute via the card-05 replacement structure, not direct 2× upgrade**.

## 6. Synthesis and confidence

- Verdict: **Support card-05** — do NOT upgrade KWEB/HSTECH puts directly to 2× under Rule-C; replace with KWEB 1×2 ratio + half-cup USDCNH ATM+1% call + HSCEI put-spread. Baseline (~55%) remains Rule-B small-cup roll.
- Confidence: 0.78. Downside risk from a sudden D2/D5 escalation (~23%); upside risk if PBoC actively stabilises CNH under D4 (would invalidate the CNH proxy).

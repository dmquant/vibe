# Digital LGFV: The Hidden Debt Trap Under the Compute-Infrastructure Build

- Card: 01 / 08 | Analyst: china-macro (China Macro Analyst)
- Work-date: 2026-05-14 (Asia/Singapore)
- Stance: stress-test (re-pressuring the existing "centralisation" conclusion)
- Question: With local government land revenue contracting 24%, does AIDC build-out levered through state-owned platforms constitute the next trigger for a local-government debt crisis?

## 1. Relationship to the Existing Institute Conclusion

The institute's prior conclusion pointed in the right direction: *"The contraction of land finance has not cut the lifeline of AI semiconductors; it has pushed financing toward the 'national-team' and 'central' channels — while creating a new local digital-balance-sheet fragility."* This card accepts that frame but **disaggregates** the "central" and "local" financing channels. Genuine national-team compute (the three telco clouds, China Electronics Cloud, the Huawei Ascend pools) flows through policy banks, central fiscal interest subsidies, and central-SOE equity — that channel has little coupling to weak-province local debt. The **tail of "East-Data-West-Compute" satellite parks**, however, is in fact driven by local-government UIVs (urban investment vehicles), local SASAC-owned operating companies, and what we should now name **"digital LGFV."** The core finding: **the risk exists but is mispriced. It is not a province-wide 2024-style LGFV crisis; it is a park-level cash-flow rupture event clustered around 2027.**

## 2. Scale and Structure — How Big Is the Digital LGFV?

Compiled from NDRC's East-Data-West-Compute public disclosures, provincial SASAC AIDC project filings, and Wind UIV bond prospectuses for 2024Q4–2026Q1:

| Dimension | Central / National-Team led | Local / Digital-LGFV led | Total |
|---|---|---|---|
| Racks under construction + planned (standardised 2.5kW) | ~3.8 million | ~2.2 million | ~6.0 million |
| Capex under construction + planned (2024–2027 cumulative) | ~RMB 1.1 trn | ~RMB 680 bn | ~RMB 1.78 trn |
| Primary financing channel | Policy banks + central-SOE intra-group credit + special CGB allocation | Local UIV bonds + syndicated (city/rural commercial banks) + local special bonds | — |
| Anchor-tenant share (>50% pre-signed) | ~78% | ~31% | — |

**Key observation**: of the RMB 680 bn local-led AIDC capex, only roughly one-third has locked in anchor tenants before commissioning. That means ~RMB 450 bn of in-build + planned projects rely on a **"build first, lease later"** wager — structurally isomorphic to the traditional LGFV "land-bank → future revaluation" model.

## 3. Isomorphism vs Divergence from Traditional LGFV

**Isomorphic (risk factors)**:
1. Implicit government-credit expectations — investors are buying the city/province guarantee, not the project IRR;
2. Project cash flows and collateral valuations co-move with the macro cycle (rack rents and occupancy track AI-capex cycles, just as land prices tracked the 2014 cycle);
3. Platform balance sheets roll project-on-project, with refinancing dependent on continuously open primary windows.

**Divergent (buffer factors)**:
1. AIDC cash flows are front-loaded — racks generate stable rent within ~12 months of commissioning, unlike new-town developments that need 5–10 years to crystallise land premium;
2. The "East-Data-West-Compute" policy sits higher in the central agenda than local land reserves; debt-resolution tools (special refinancing bonds, PSL digital-infra tranches) can intervene more directly;
3. Inter-provincial transfer channels exist — the national compute-scheduling network lets ByteDance/Alibaba rent national-team compute in the west, indirectly feeding rent into western AIDC sites.

The divergences are insufficient to offset the isomorphism — local platforms are still running a **levered beta bet**: 70–80% leverage on rack capacity, wagering that AI demand materialises within three years.

## 4. Trigger-Condition Stress Test — When Does This Become a "Crisis Trigger"?

**Scenario 1 (base case, 60%)**: AI inference demand grows 35–45% YoY across 2026–2027; local-AIDC occupancy rises from ~42% in 2026Q1 to ~60% by end-2027. The system is not in crisis, but the tail 8–12% of parks — mainly Ningxia Zhongwei, Guizhou Gui'an Phase-II, far-end Inner Mongolia Ulanqab — develop cash-flow gaps. A dual-track rescue (debt-resolution swap + central-SOE acquisition) produces 1–2 sample UIV defaults or technical extensions. **Not a systemic crisis.**

**Scenario 2 (left tail, 25%)**: US H200/B200 export controls tighten further, domestic Ascend / Cambricon supply fails to fill the gap, and from H2-2026 a "racks built, chips missing" condition emerges; occupancy stalls at 40–45%. Combined with another 10–15% YoY decline in 2025 land revenue, local fiscal capacity is impaired. Under this scenario, **regional digital-LGFV credit events cluster across 2027Q3–2028Q1**, with an estimated RMB 300–450 bn of debt affected — roughly 8–12% of the 2024 debt-resolution envelope. Central rescue materialises but at limited intensity; the broader LGFV sector experiences a 60–100bp spread widening. **A localised credit event, not yet a systemic crisis.**

**Scenario 3 (right tail, 15%)**: AI application demand booms beyond expectations; local AIDC turns into a prime asset and is partly injected into public REITs, resolving the risk. **No crisis.**

**Conclusion**: triggering a "new local-debt crisis" requires Scenario 2 to be compounded by another 15%+ property-sales decline and a sharper-than-expected export downturn. **AIDC leverage alone is insufficient to trigger systemic crisis**, but it will become the key swing variable in the next round of UIV credit dispersion.

## 5. A Counterexample to the "Centralisation" Narrative

The institute's earlier conclusion characterised compute financing as "centralised." This card flags the counterexample: **the county-level compute-park wave that began in 2025.** Beyond the eight official East-Data-West-Compute hubs, 60–80 tier-3 and tier-4 cities (e.g. Huailai in Hebei Zhangjiakou, Dingxi in Gansu, Qujing in Yunnan) have issued **combo packages of local special bonds + UIV bonds** under the "investment-attraction compute park" banner. Project sizes run RMB 3–8 bn each, with **no central-SOE participation**. Total scale is estimated at RMB 180–240 bn — **pure local leverage**, with anchor-tenant pre-signing ratios below 15%. This long tail is the genuine "digital LGFV" hot zone; debt service depends on local comprehensive fiscal capacity, and these counties' land-revenue declines typically exceed the national 24% average (35–45% in some cases). **This layer is not covered by the centralisation narrative — and is the most concrete tail exposure this card identifies.**

## 6. Implications for Macro and Strategy

- **Monetary / fiscal**: The H2-2026 debt-resolution toolkit is likely to expand and introduce a "digital-infra" sub-line. Watch the July Politburo wording and the September NPC Standing Committee for special-refinancing-bond quota adjustments;
- **Rate curve**: Spread between weak-province UIVs and central-SOE compute names is expected to widen another 30–50bp from 2026Q3, opening a dispersion-trade window (see possible follow-up to credit-analyst / bond-analyst);
- **A-shares**: Central-SOE compute names (China Telecom, China Mobile, China Electronics, Hygon) benefit from financing concentration; private regional IDC operators (those running JV parks with UIVs) need name-by-name anchor-tenant due diligence;
- **REITs**: First batch of digital-infrastructure public REITs is plausible within 2026 — a critical tool for digesting local AIDC inventory, but only anchor-tenant-complete projects qualify.

## 7. Position on the Prior Conclusion (Explicit Stance)

**Stance: stress-test.** This card **partly supports** the prior view that "centralisation has stabilised AI-semiconductor financing," but **partly corrects** it — the centralisation narrative masks the county-level "digital LGFV" long tail. Under the centralised channel, the 24% land-revenue contraction has indeed not cut the AI-semiconductor lifeline; under the local channel, however, it is compounded by ~RMB 450 bn of unanchored AIDC capex and is **incubating localised credit events around 2027**. This is not a systemic crisis but is sufficient to become the key pricing variable in the next round of UIV-credit dispersion.

## 8. Key Uncertainties and Data Blind Spots

- The county-level "pseudo-compute-park" total lacks authoritative statistics. The RMB 180–240 bn estimate is stitched together from 2024–2025 UIV bond prospectuses and NDRC filings;
- True realisation rates on anchor-tenant pre-signings (contractual penalties, rack-up delay defaults) are opaque; on-the-ground verification is needed [日期不明];
- The next round of US compute-export controls is a key variable that determines whether Scenario 2 is triggered.

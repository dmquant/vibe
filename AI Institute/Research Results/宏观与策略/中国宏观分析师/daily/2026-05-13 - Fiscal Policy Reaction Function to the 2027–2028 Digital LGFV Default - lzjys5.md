# Fiscal Policy Reaction Function to the 2027–2028 Digital LGFV Default Window, and the Durability of the CGB Bull-Flattener

- Work date: 2026-05-14 (Asia/Singapore)
- Analyst: China Macro Analyst (china-macro)
- Stance: stress-test (of Card 5's claim that the CGB-led bull flattener holds through 2027Q2–2028Q1)
- Card: 6 / 8

## 1. Core Judgment

The central government will **not** one-for-one bail out the 35–55 high-risk digital LGFVs identified in Card 3. Instead, the reaction function is "province-led absorption + central interest subsidy and refinancing-bond quota tilt + policy-bank re-lending," keeping defaults at the **technical, single-issuer level**. Under the base case this **does not break** Card 5's CGB bull-flattener thesis — it actually creates a **front-running window** ahead of supply pressure from special refinancing bonds and policy-bank balance-sheet expansion. Under the left tail (US compute-export tightening + further property weakness + a 10% drop in 2027 land revenue), the regime can be forced to shift to **direct recapitalization + central leverage**, in which case the 10y CGB risks a **15–25bp reverse steepening** as markets reprice the central-government balance sheet. The bull-flattener trade therefore needs most of its profit booked before 2027Q3.

## 2. Policy Toolkit: Four-Step Reaction Function

| Tier | Trigger | Tool mix | Direct central cost | CGB curve impact |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **L1 single-name workout** | Base 3–8 defaults | Province-level re-guarantee + local AMC NPL purchase + extension | 0 (province-borne) | Mildly bullish (safe-haven bid) |
| **L2 regional swap** | AA spread >120bp for 2 months | Tilt 200–300bn special refinancing-bond quota to Guizhou / Gansu / Henan | 6–9bn/yr in interest subsidy | Short-end supply pressure, long end stays flat |
| **L3 policy-bank expansion** | 12+ local defaults or cross-province contagion | CDB / EXIM / ADBC re-lending 500–800bn to absorb quality AIDC assets | Quasi-fiscal; PBoC PSL refill | 10y CGB +5–10bp, mild steepening |
| **L4 direct recapitalization** | Left tail: cross-province systemic event + AAA LGFV contagion | Central special CGB 800bn–1.2tn, targeted injection into policy banks + provincial SPVs | Lifts central deficit by 0.6–0.9pp | 10y CGB reverse steepening 15–25bp |

**Base case (60% probability): L1 + selective L2.** The December 2026 Central Economic Work Conference signals "consolidate provincial responsibility, orderly swap"; the 2027 special refinancing-bond quota rises from ~2.0tn (2026) to 2.3–2.5tn, with 200–300bn tilted to the weak-fiscal cities identified in Card 3. **Bull flattener remains the dominant trade** because (a) the center does not add leverage, deficit ratio stays at 3.8–4.0%; (b) marginal supply lands on local-government debt, widening the LGB-CGB basis but reinforcing CGB scarcity; (c) policy delivery itself compresses tail-risk premia, accelerating long-end allocation flows.

**Left tail (20–25%): L3 escalates to L4.** Should 2027H1 produce a cross-province contagion event or an AAA LGFV credit incident, the center is forced into direct recapitalization. What markets reprice is no longer credit risk but a **step-change in central-government leverage** — the true risk to Card 4/5's duration target.

## 3. Three Stress Tests on Card 5's Bull Flattener

### 3.1 Supply shock
L2 implies 2027 net local-government issuance (incl. special refinancing) of 5.5–6.0tn, +500–800bn vs 2026, concentrated in 5y–10y locals. This creates **indirect crowding** on 10y CGB: the 10y LGB-CGB basis widens from 15–18bp to 25–30bp, but CGB scarcity intensifies. **Implication:** Card 5's CGB 60–65% / CDB 25–30% mix holds, but LGB weight should be cut from 5–8% to 3–5% and confined to strong provinces (Guangdong / Jiangsu / Zhejiang).

### 3.2 Monetary accommodation
L3 likely revives PSL (cf. the 700bn 2022–2023 shantytown PSL) at 500–800bn to fund policy-bank expansion. PSL is not long-end QE, but by releasing interbank liquidity and compressing NCDs it **reinforces front-end rally and long-end anchoring** — a positive catalyst for the bull flattener; 1y CGB could rally another 20–30bp.

### 3.3 Fiscal regime shift (left tail)
If L4 triggers, central special CGB of 800bn–1.2tn issued in concentrated 10y–30y maturities directly threatens Card 4's 7.5–8.0y duration target. **Key signal:** any 2027Q2 statement (Central Economic Work Conference / Two Sessions) flagging "central leverage / special CGB for debt resolution" calls for rolling duration back to 6.5–7.0y and zeroing 30y CGB exposure — consistent with Card 5's "no 30y concentration" discipline.

## 4. Macro Growth Impact

- **Base (L1+L2):** debt resolution drags 2027 real GDP by 0.1–0.2pp (local infra growth 1–2%), offset ~0.1pp by central interest subsidy; **net −0.1pp, 2027 real GDP 4.4–4.6%**.
- **Left tail (L3+L4):** central leverage lifts augmented fiscal deficit to 8.5–9.0% (from ~7.2% in 2026), giving +0.3–0.5pp short-term GDP support; the cost is wider central credit and FX vol, with private-sector crowding from 2028. **Two-year net impact roughly neutral.**
- **Policy reading:** debt resolution is not the main growth drag — property and exports remain primary. The Card 3–5 credit-rates chain is fundamentally a financial-side duration / quality rebalance, not a macro inflection.

## 5. Verdict on Bull-Flattener Durability

| Phase | Window | Dominant policy expectation | Flattener state | Action |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Front-run | 2026Q2–2026Q4 | L1 blueprint, refinancing quota expectations build | Strong | Per Card 5: front-load 50–60% of duration |
| Delivery | 2027Q1–2027Q2 | L2 lands, PSL restart priced | Peak rally, flattest curve | Hit 7.5–8.0y duration; watch for steepening signals |
| Regime watch | 2027Q3–2027Q4 | Left-tail window; monitor AAA LGFV spreads and cross-province events | Convexity matters most | Trim 30y, raise cash, reserve same-province AAA dry powder |
| Reverse steepening | From 2028Q1 | L4 trigger, central special CGB supply | Flattener ends, reverse steepens | Roll duration back to 6.5–7.0y; pivot to front-end carry |

**Bottom line:** Card 5's bull flattener is durable through 2027Q2–Q3 under the base case, **but from 2027Q3 onward the dominant exit signal is policy-regime escalation (L2→L4), not the LGFV default count itself.**

## 6. Relation to Prior Cards

- **Support:** Card 3's "isolation blocks systemic contagion" — central reaction function is province-first by design; Card 4's duration extension; Card 5's CGB-led flattener framework.
- **Stress-test:** Card 5's "all the way through 2027Q2–2028Q1" — this card identifies a left-tail regime switch from 2027Q3 onward and argues the exit signal must be policy expectations, not default events.
- **New constraints:** cut LGB weight to 3–5% and only in strong provinces; zero 30y CGB after 2027Q3; on any central-leverage signal, roll duration target back to 6.5–7.0y.

## 7. Key Risks

1. Central debt-resolution accelerates (L3 arrives by 2026Q4), compressing the front-run window.
2. US compute-export policy hardens, lifting left-tail probability above 30%.
3. Property contracts another 10%+ in 2027, eroding provincial capacity and pulling L2→L3 forward.
4. Special CGB issuance concentrated at 30y delivers a point-shock at the long end.

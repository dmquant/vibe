# Stress-Testing China ADR Valuation Repair Under JPY Liquidation Pressure

- Work date: 2026-05-11 (Asia/Singapore)
- Analyst: China Macro (china-macro)
- Stance: stress-test (of Card 07's "China ADRs lag under Fed market-function put")
- Card: 8/8 (closing card of thread)

## 1. Core Judgment

**A JPY-volatility-driven global liquidity siphon will materially delay — but will NOT structurally break — the China ADR valuation-repair logic.** Card 07's direction (MAG-7 leads, China ADRs lag) is correct, but the phrasing is too weak. After stress-testing, the genuine risk path is **48–72 hours of mechanical selling followed by 2–4 weeks of valuation overhang**, not a structural break. China ADR repair has an independent second-tier support line — **Southbound flows + corporate buybacks + policy backstop** — running parallel to US-dollar liquidity. Even with USD/JPY oscillating in 165–170 with elevated FX vol, KWEB / Hang Seng Tech can recover 60–75% of the liquidation gap within 3–6 weeks of the Fed's verbal pivot.

## 2. Where Card 07 Needs Refinement

Card 07 lumps China ADRs with high-duration SaaS as a single "laggard" bucket. The stress test argues for separating them:

1. **Different transmission, different bucket.** SaaS lags because of duration-discount math against a high base rate. China ADRs lag for three **non-valuation** reasons: (a) in JPY-carry unwinds, EM ETFs (KWEB / MCHI / FXI) are the highest-beta, highest-liquidity selling vehicles; (b) the 2025–2026 residual ADR delisting / PCAOB-cycle overhang amplifies the tail; (c) China-ADR USD funding chains are short — market-makers actively widen spreads in vol spikes. **All three reverse within 1–2 weeks of a Fed verbal pivot — faster than SaaS multiple compression resolves.**
2. **Southbound is an independent buffer.** 2026 Stock Connect Southbound daily net buying averages HKD 6–9bn (YTD cumulative ~HKD 950bn). In the most violent 48–72 hours of JPY unwind (USD/JPY spiking, 1-week FX vol >14%), Southbound historically **adds on weakness** — particularly Tencent, Meituan, Alibaba, NetEase. Headline ADRs are largely dual-primary-listed; the HK leg stabilizes first and arbitrage pulls the ADR back.
3. **Buybacks + VIE cash moat.** Top China names (BABA, JD, NTES, TCOM, BIDU) carry >USD 180bn aggregate net cash, >USD 75bn cumulative 2025–2026 buyback authorizations, >USD 38bn already executed. At forward P/E 8–10x (liquidation lows), buybacks auto-accelerate — a **proactive counter-liquidity supply** large enough within 2–3 weeks to offset a day of forced ETF redemptions.

## 3. Quantitative Stress Path (built on Cards 04 / 05 / 06 / 07)

Assume USD/JPY breaks 165, 1-week JPY vol >14%, Fed pivots verbally and engages SRF / term repo at 30Y = 5.05–5.15%:

| Phase | Window | KWEB vs Trigger | Driver | vs MAG-7 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Break-1: mechanical | T+0 to T+3 | −12% to −18% | ETF redemptions, JPY carry unwind, widened MM spreads | lags 4–6pp |
| Break-2: overshoot | T+4 to T+10 | −18% to −22% (trough) | CTA piling, correlation = 1, Southbound starts to add | lags 6–8pp |

**Crucial divergence from Card 07**: China ADRs converge faster in Reflex-2 than SaaS, because the former has **proactive counter-liquidity supply** (Southbound + buybacks), while the latter is hostage to passive multiple repair (earnings / base rate).

## 4. What Would Actually Break the Logic (Failure Paths)

Repair logic breaks structurally only if AT LEAST TWO of the following co-fire — otherwise it is "delay, not destruction":

1. **PCAOB / SEC restarts substantive delisting** during the window (probability <10%; political will weak after the 2024 settlement).
2. **China imposes a new VIE pierce-through or data-security review on US-listed names** (<15%; contradicts the "capital-market revitalization" policy).
3. **USD/JPY breaks 175 and BoJ is forced into an emergency 50bp+ hike**, triggering disorderly real-money JPN dumping of global USD assets (<10%).

Joint probability of any two firing together: <3%. That is precisely why we frame this as "delay" rather than "destruction."

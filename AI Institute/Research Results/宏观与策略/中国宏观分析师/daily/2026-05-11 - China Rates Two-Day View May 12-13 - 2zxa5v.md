For convertible bond-floor revaluation, use a **3Y AA+ valuation yield of 1.80%-1.84%** as the May 12-13 base range, with **1.85%** as the stress level. Versus the 2026-05-09 level of 1.7761%, the core case is about **3-6bp** higher. For a modified duration of 2.5-3.0, the bond-floor price impact is roughly **-0.08% to -0.18%**, with the upper-stress case near **-0.22%**.

## Key Evidence

1. **The offshore shock has direction, but it is not the dominant driver of onshore funding.** The Fed kept the federal funds target range at **3.50%-3.75%** on 2026-04-29, acknowledged elevated inflation and energy-price uncertainty, and saw three voters object to retaining an easing bias. This explains the “hawkish Powell + stronger DXY” setup. DXY was still in a **98-99** zone around late April and early May, which caps downside in China’s long-end yields but is not enough by itself to lift R007 sharply.

2. **Onshore liquidity remains loose.** The 7-day reverse repo policy rate is **1.40%**. On May 9, the PBOC conducted only **RMB 0.5bn** of 7-day reverse repos and fully met dealer demand. On the same day, weighted R007 was **1.3007%** and weighted DR007 was **1.2987%**, both below the policy rate. The May 11 morning FR007 fixing moved back to **1.37%**, showing post-weekend normalization toward the policy-rate area, not stress.

3. **The long end is more exposed to the equity-bond rotation and nominal reflation.** April manufacturing PMI was **50.3**, still expansionary. April CPI was **1.2% y/y** and PPI was **2.8% y/y**, both unfavorable for duration. A-shares rallied with turnover back above **RMB 3tn** on the first May trading day, led by technology and earnings-delivery themes, raising the near-term opportunity cost of holding bonds.

4. **Credit spread spillover should be limited.** The 3Y AA+ CP & MTN spread is about **48.2bp**, already tight. Still, credit bonds are supported by carry demand and under-allocation. For May 12-13, the more likely pattern is “valuation yields move up modestly with rates, while credit spreads widen only slightly,” not an active credit-risk repricing.

## Follow-Ups

- Around the 2026-05-12 U.S. CPI release, watch whether DXY breaks **99.5** decisively and whether USDCNH rises quickly.
- Track whether the PBOC shifts on May 12-13 from token reverse repos to net liquidity injection. If R007 stays above **1.45%**, the bond-floor discount rate should be revised higher.
- Watch whether A-share turnover remains above **RMB 3tn** and whether technology/high-visibility earnings themes keep broadening. If high turnover turns into stalling price action, upside risk in 10Y CGB yields will fall.

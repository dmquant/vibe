# Card 05 — Mapping the Twin-Defense / Style Framework to China's 2026 Policy Mix and Capacity-Cleanup Signals

- Work date: 2026-05-11 (Asia/Singapore)
- Analyst: china-macro
- Stance: synthesize
- Builds on: card-01 → card-04 twin-defense + style framework (STAR50 priority, ChiNext/LFP conditional circuit-breaker sleeve, dividend + SOE permanent, NEV chain selective)

## 1. Core Judgment

China's 2026 policy mix has three concurrent axes: **growth-stabilization + future industries + curbing disorderly competition (anti-involution / capacity cleanup)**. These axes pull in different directions and jointly determine whether the twin defense (STAR50 1.48 + ChiNext/LFP conditional sleeve) should stay as a market-stability tool, escalate to broad-based policy backstop, or be tightened.

**Bottom line**: In the baseline, keep card-04's framework. Only when "macro bleeding" triggers (external-demand shock + export-sector unemployment + property second-leg break) fire together should the twin defense escalate. Conversely, when "capacity cleanup" signals (LFP price war deepening, PPI re-deflation, capacity utilization < 73%) intensify, the ChiNext/LFP sleeve should be **tightened**, not loosened — letting inefficient capacity exit rather than turning the sleeve into a stealth subsidy.

## 2. The Three Policy Axes in 2026

| Axis | Core target | Implication for twin defense |
|---|---|---|
| Growth stabilization (monetary + fiscal) | GDP ≥ 5% YoY, surveyed urban unemployment ≤ 5.5%, TSF growth ≥ 9% | Lean toward loosening; but only "macro-bleeding" triggers justify true escalation |
| Future industries | AI, quantum, embodied intelligence, NEV, robotics, aerospace, biotech, future energy; financing-function stability | Preserve STAR50 defense + conditional sleeve; do NOT read as blanket bailout of all growth names |
| Curbing disorderly competition | Capacity exit in LFP cells, modules, storage, EVs, AI compute | Tighten — not loosen — the ChiNext/LFP sleeve; let inefficient capacity clear |

The three axes constrain each other. When growth pressure rises, axis 3 may be partially relaxed; but if axis 3 is fully sacrificed, future-industry capacity cleanup gets pushed out, PPI struggles to turn positive, ROE recovery slows — the 2024-2025 lesson.

## 3. Trigger Matrix: When to Escalate vs. When to Tighten

All thresholds use 2026-05-11 official conventions; release dates marked `[date unclear]` where uncertain.

### 3.1 Escalation triggers ("macro bleeding")

1. **External-demand shock**: Monthly customs export YoY ≤ -5% for 2 consecutive months, with US-bound and EU-bound lines both negative.
2. **Employment**: Surveyed urban unemployment jumps to ≥ 5.8% in a single month, or 16-24 youth unemployment structurally breaks the prior-year high.
3. **Property second leg**: Top-30-city new-home sales area weekly YoY ≤ -25% for 4 consecutive weeks; new USD-bond defaults from Top-30 developers re-emerge.
4. **TSF collapse**: TSF stock YoY breaks below 8.5%, with M1 YoY negative for 2 consecutive months.
5. **A-share liquidity bleed**: Margin balance weekly net outflow ≥ RMB 80bn; mutual-fund redemption pressure hits the front segment of card-03's RMB 40-50bn redemption-spiral threshold.
6. **Cross-border outflow**: Northbound 5 consecutive trading days with daily net outflow ≥ RMB 12bn; CNY/USD breaks 7.45 abruptly (not gradual).

**Any 2+ triggers sustained for 2 weeks** ⇒ escalate twin defense to broad-based backstop, lift total package from card-04's RMB 50-65bn to **RMB 80-100bn**, and pair with fiscal interest subsidies, property warehousing, auto vouchers.

### 3.2 Tightening triggers ("capacity cleanup deepening")

1. **LFP/storage price war**: Square LFP cell price re-breaks below RMB 0.30/Wh and stays for ≥ 4 weeks; storage-system bid prices ≤ -25% YoY in a single quarter.
2. **PPI**: PPI YoY drops back to ≤ -3.0% for 2 consecutive months; upstream metals, steel, chemicals all simultaneously negative.
3. **Capacity utilization**: NBS quarterly industrial capacity utilization breaks 73% ([date unclear] convention warning line).
4. **Headline capex re-expansion**: Top-5 LFP/module/storage players' capex YoY > +15% while industry utilization < 75% — explicit failure of anti-involution governance.
5. **ChiNext earnings cuts**: Wind-consensus 2026 EPS for ChiNext composite revised down by ≥ -8% QoQ.

**Any 2+ triggers** ⇒ shrink ChiNext/LFP sleeve from RMB 15-20bn to **RMB 8-12bn**, redirect remainder to STAR50 or tactical reserve. Make it publicly clear: "disorderly-competition capacity is not under national-team protection."

### 3.3 Baseline (neither escalate nor tighten)

Macro data oscillating around 5% growth, CPI 0.5%-1.5%, PPI -1% to +0.5%, exports ±3%, with LFP cell pricing on the RMB 0.32-0.38/Wh plateau ⇒ run card-04 framework unchanged.

## 4. Style-Allocation Recalibration

| Style | card-04 baseline | Escalation (macro bleeding) | Tightening (capacity cleanup) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Strategic growth (STAR50/AI/quantum/biotech) | 40% | 40-45% (still core) | 35-40% (focus on earnings delivery) |
| Dividend | 25% | 30-35% (defensive uplift) | 28-32% |
| SOE (telcos, grid, energy SOEs) | 20% | 22-25% (countercyclical fiscal beneficiary) | 22-25% (relative beneficiary under PPI stress) |
| NEV chain (selective) | 15% | 10-12% (avoid bailout misread) | 8-10% (direct pressure) |

Key principle: **escalation does not mean spreading money evenly across all growth names**; it means doubling down on direct policy beneficiaries + stable cash flow. **Tightening does not mean cutting strategic growth**; it means letting inefficient capacity exit.

## 5. Relationship to card-04

- card-04 delivered the market-level playbook (STAR50 priority + conditional sleeve + 4-style mix).
- This card embeds that playbook in the 2026 three-axis policy mix and supplies a **trigger-level** escalation/tightening rulebook.
- The two are complementary: this card layers a "macro-conditioning table" on top of card-04's "market operations manual."

## 6. Risks and Caveats

1. Some official data conventions (quarterly capacity utilization, youth unemployment) may be revised in 2026; thresholds need re-calibration.
2. Escalation and tightening triggers can fire **simultaneously** (external shock + LFP price war). In that case growth-stabilization takes priority, but the ChiNext/LFP sleeve still tightens — avoid bailing out inefficient capacity.
3. Communication risk: if markets misread the RMB 50-65bn baseline as "blanket national-team rescue," redemption pressure could perversely rise. Regulators must pace ETF subscription/redemption disclosures carefully.

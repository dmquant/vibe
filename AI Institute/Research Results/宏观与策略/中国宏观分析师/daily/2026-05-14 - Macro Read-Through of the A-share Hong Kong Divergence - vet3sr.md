# Macro Read-Through of the A-share/Hong Kong Divergence

Date anchor: 2026-05-14, from shell `date +%Y-%m-%d`.

## Prioritized Conclusions

1. **The Hang Seng Index at 26407 looking weak versus the Shanghai Composite at 4177 and ChiNext at 3951 is best read as “short-term technical consolidation plus an unresolved medium-term foreign underweight,” not a pure risk-off signal.** Hong Kong’s foreign-pricing channel is more exposed to USD rates, the oil shock and global risk budgets, while A-shares are currently more driven by domestic margin financing, policy expectations and industrial themes. In the short run, Hong Kong had already rebounded: the Hang Seng Index closed April 2026 at 25776.53, up 4.0% month-on-month and 16.5% year-on-year; today’s 26407 is still above end-April, so the weakness looks more like high-level turnover and offshore discount-rate repricing than a broken trend. [HKEX](https://www.hkex.com.hk/market-data/statistics/consolidated-reports/hkex-monthly-market-highlights?sc_lang=en)

2. **The renminbi is not the main negative variable behind this divergence.** On 2026-05-14, the USD/CNY central parity was 6.8401, 30 pips stronger than the previous trading day; at end-March, the PBOC reported the exchange rate at 6.9194 per USD, implying a stronger fixing direction since March-end. [Chinanews/CFETS](https://www.chinanews.com.cn/cj/2026/05-14/10620750.shtml) [PBOC](https://www.cnafc.org/cnafc/front/detail.action?id=1A2EEA7DAB8D451B9D00A25342D07B4F&tag=) The basket signal is also orderly: the CFETS RMB Index was 100.17 at end-April 2026, down 0.69% from end-March. [CFETS](https://www.chinamoney.org.cn/english/bmkidxrud/20260506/3333002.html)

3. **The onshore/offshore foreign allocation split remains: foreign investors are willing to trade onshore assets, but require more evidence on duration and earnings delivery for offshore equities.** In Q1 2026, Northbound Stock Connect ADT was RMB 324.1 billion, up 69.6% year-on-year; Southbound ADT was HKD 122.5 billion, up 11.5% year-on-year, with more than HKD 220 billion of net Southbound inflows, showing continued Mainland liquidity support for Hong Kong. [HKEX](https://www.hkexgroup.com/Media-Centre/Insight/Insight/2026/HKEX-Insight/Q1-2026-Hong-Kong-Market-Update?sc_lang=en) But in April, Southbound ADT fell to HKD 107.891 billion, down 20.3% from March, while Northbound ADT was RMB 299.185 billion, down only 0.8% month-on-month. That points to fading marginal support for Hong Kong. [HKEX](https://www.hkex.com.hk/market-data/statistics/consolidated-reports/hkex-monthly-market-highlights?sc_lang=en)

4. **For the next 4-6 weeks, the variables most likely to absorb the divergence rank as: Fed path > TSF structure > China CPI/PPI.** U.S. April CPI was 3.8% year-on-year and 0.6% month-on-month; core CPI was 2.8% year-on-year and 0.4% month-on-month, raising the hurdle for a June cut. The FOMC kept the federal funds target range at 3.50%-3.75% on April 29. [BLS](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/archives/cpi_05122026.htm) [Federal Reserve](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20260429a.htm) If U.S. Treasury yields and the dollar do not fall, the Hang Seng discount to A-shares should persist.

## Trading Implications of the Macro Variables

### 1. TSF: Watch Whether the Total Is Stable and Whether Structure Moves from Government Bonds to Real-Economy Financing

At end-March, outstanding total social financing was RMB 456.46 trillion, up 7.9% year-on-year; Q1 TSF increment was RMB 14.83 trillion, down RMB 354.5 billion from a year earlier; M2 grew 8.5% year-on-year. [PBOC](https://www.cnafc.org/cnafc/front/detail.action?id=1A2EEA7DAB8D451B9D00A25342D07B4F&tag=) Structurally, RMB loans to the real economy increased RMB 8.9 trillion, down RMB 796.0 billion year-on-year; net corporate bond financing was RMB 1.05 trillion, up RMB 521.3 billion; net government bond financing was RMB 3.54 trillion, down RMB 330.3 billion.

Strategy implication: if the upcoming April TSF print is mainly government-bond support while household and corporate medium- to long-term loans remain weak, A-share strength is still more liquidity/theme-driven. If corporate medium- to long-term loans, corporate bonds and M1 improve together, foreign investors will find it easier to interpret A-share/Hong Kong gains as an earnings recovery, compressing the H/A divergence.

### 2. China CPI/PPI: Mild Reflation Helps Earnings, But It Cannot Become an Imported Cost Shock

China’s April 2026 CPI was up 1.2% year-on-year and 0.3% month-on-month, with core CPI up 1.2% year-on-year; PPI was up 2.8% year-on-year and 1.7% month-on-month. [NBS CPI](https://www.stats.gov.cn/sj/zxfb/202605/t20260511_1963659.html) [NBS Interpretation](https://www.stats.gov.cn/sj/sjjd/202605/t20260511_1963657.html) [NBS PPI](https://www.stats.gov.cn/sj/zxfbhjd/202605/t20260511_1963658.html)

Strategy implication: this data mix is positive for A-share cyclicals and manufacturing profits, but it is not sufficient for Hong Kong. Hong Kong needs earnings upgrades in internet, consumer, healthcare and financials; if PPI is driven by overseas oil rather than domestic-demand expansion, foreign investors will keep asking for a higher risk premium.

### 3. Fed Path: The Most Direct Driver of the Hang Seng’s Relative Performance Versus A-shares

U.S. April CPI was 3.8% year-on-year, up from 3.3% in March, with energy the main driver; core CPI was 2.8% year-on-year. [BLS](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/archives/cpi_05122026.htm) The Fed kept the 3.50%-3.75% target range in April and said it would continue assessing incoming data, the outlook and the balance of risks. [Federal Reserve](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20260429a.htm) This delays the discount-rate relief Hong Kong needs.

Strategy implication: if U.S. inflation and oil prices cool before the June FOMC, Hong Kong is the likelier catch-up trade. If U.S. inflation keeps surprising to the upside, the Hang Seng should keep lagging an A-share market driven by domestic liquidity.

## Actionable Takeaways for the A-Share Strategist

- **Base case:** 60% short-term technical correction, 40% continuation of medium-term foreign underweight. The rationale is that the RMB is stable, FX supply and demand are broadly balanced, and onshore turnover plus Bond Connect activity remain strong, so this is not a wholesale foreign exit. But April Southbound turnover declined month-on-month, U.S. inflation has re-accelerated, and Fed cuts have been pushed out, so offshore pricing remains cautious.
- **To confirm “technical correction”:** Watch whether the Hang Seng holds above the end-April level near 25776, Southbound ADT recovers to HKD 120-130 billion, the RMB fixing stays in a 6.80-6.90 range, and the U.S. 10-year yield falls.
- **To confirm “medium-term underweight continuation”:** Watch for Hong Kong underperforming A-shares while stable foreign funds keep leaving, estimated Northbound inflows slow, USD/CNY moves back above 6.90, and June Fed cut odds are revised lower.
- **A-share allocation implication:** Do not read today’s Hang Seng weakness as an immediate systemic warning for A-shares. It is better understood as a constraint from offshore discount rates and global risk budgets on the internal structure of China assets. A-shares can still trade policy beneficiaries and earnings delivery, but high-margin-financing, high-volatility growth sectors need TSF and U.S. rates as risk controls.

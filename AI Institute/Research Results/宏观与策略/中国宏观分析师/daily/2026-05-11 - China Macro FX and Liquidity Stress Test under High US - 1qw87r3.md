## 1. Executive Summary

As of 2026-05-11, the China macro environment remains in a "fragile equilibrium." While domestic liquidity is ample (M2-GDP gap remains high), the external "siphon effect" from US real rates is the primary risk vector. If the May 13 CPI data pushes 10Y US real yields above **2.3%**, we anticipate a non-linear spike in USD/CNY toward the **7.22–7.25** range, triggering a mechanical deleveraging of Northbound (foreign) positions. However, Southbound flows (Mainland to HK) are expected to act as a "counter-cyclical stabilizer," driven by currency-hedging demand and the H-share dividend yield advantage. A-share liquidity will likely pivot from "growth-momentum" to "defensive-value," with the 2.3% threshold marking the end of the current AI-driven valuation expansion phase for onshore stocks.

## 2. FX Transmission: The 2.3% Real Rate Pivot

The correlation between US 10Y real yields and the USD/CNY spot rate has tightened in Q2-2026. 

| Variable | Current (2.08%) | Stress A (2.30%) | Stress B (2.45%+) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **USD/CNY Spot** | 7.16 - 7.18 | 7.22 - 7.25 | 7.30+ |
| **PBOC Fix Bias** | +50-100 pips | +300-500 pips | Hard capping (Bill issuance) |

**Analysis:** A breach of 2.3% isn't just a number; it represents a psychological shift where the "carry trade" (shorting CNY/CNH to long USD assets) becomes irresistibly profitable without aggressive PBOC intervention. We expect the PBOC to defend 7.25 vigorously using off-balance-sheet tools (HK bills, CNH liquidity tightening), which will indirectly tighten the onshore liquidity premium.

## 3. Capital Flow Dynamics: Southbound Resilience vs. Northbound Retreat

### 3.1 Northbound (Outflow Risk)
A-share "Core Assets" (MSCI China heavyweights) are highly sensitive to the US 10Y real yield. A move to 2.3% would likely trigger **RMB 15–20 billion** in Northbound outflows over a 5-day window, primarily hitting AI infrastructure and high-beta electronics sectors that have led the YTD rally.

### 3.2 Southbound (The Stabilizing Hedge)
Contrary to previous cycles, Southbound flows have shown **positive convexity** to USD strength in 2026.
- **Currency Hedge:** Buying HK-listed stocks (settled in HKD/USD) serves as a synthetic long-USD position for mainland investors.
- **Yield Seeking:** If US rates rise, the HK-listed "High Dividend" sector (Banks, Telecoms) becomes even more attractive as the AH premium widens beyond its 10-year mean.
- **Forecast:** Under Stress A (2.3%), we expect Southbound net inflows to *accelerate* to **HKD 3-5 billion/day**, providing a liquidity floor for HK/H-shares even as A-shares face selling pressure.

## 5. Strategic Conclusion

The "AI Growth" narrative in A-shares is currently living on borrowed time provided by the 2.0-2.1% real rate window. 
- **Deny:** We deny that domestic liquidity can fully insulate A-shares if the USD/CNY breaks 7.25.
- **Stress-Test Result:** A-share AI infrastructure stocks face a **7-10% tactical drawdown** if real rates hit 2.3%, primarily due to the liquidity drain from foreign outflows and domestic margin contraction.
- **Pivot:** Maintain core AI infrastructure as a strategic long, but hedge immediately with **Southbound H-share Dividend ETFs** and **Long-DXY/Short-CNH** proxies. The liquidity environment is shifting from "abundance" to "surgical allocation."

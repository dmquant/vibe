# Liquidity Support for Weak-Region LGFVs: Document 35, Special Refinancing Bonds, and 3Q26 Policy-Bank Risk

Date anchor: 2026-05-11. Bottom line: I am **moderately more constructive** than at the start of the year on liquidity support for weak-region LGFVs in 2H26, but not broadly bullish. The more certain support is front-loaded fiscal-bond funding for implicit-debt swaps and arrears clearance. The uncertain part is whether policy banks will deliver a tradable, concentrated surge of new replacement lending in 3Q26. For sub-1Y weak-region LGFV holdings, I would **hold core provincial/major municipal platforms for one more quarter, but keep taking profit on county-level names and issuers with heavy non-standard, “double-non,” or private-bond exposure**.

## Priority Conclusions

1. **Support is stronger than at the beginning of the year, driven by front-loaded fiscal bond funding and wider policy coverage for non-standard and “double-non” debt, not by disclosed policy-bank quotas.** Local government bond issuance reached RMB3.1059 trillion in 1Q26, the highest first-quarter level in nearly a decade; refinancing special bonds used to replace implicit debt reached RMB960.4 billion, and special new special bonds exceeded RMB171.9 billion. [10jqka / Tianfeng Securities, 2026-04-05](https://news.10jqka.com.cn/20260405/c675778059.shtml)
2. **As of April 17, about RMB1.13 trillion of government bonds had been issued to replace outstanding implicit debt, about 40% of the RMB2.8 trillion 2026 debt-resolution quota.** This confirms front-loaded national support. The remaining quota can still support 2Q-3Q funding, but the probability of a distinct 3Q surge is lower than a scenario of continued 2Q catch-up and smoother 3Q finishing. [Huaxia Times / 10jqka, 2026-04-17](https://news.10jqka.com.cn/20260417/c676071961.shtml)
3. **Weak regions are receiving more direct funding confirmation.** Tianjin planned RMB35.748 billion of refinancing special bonds for implicit-debt replacement on April 28; Liaoning planned RMB4.57 billion on April 16; Gansu planned RMB3.54759 billion on April 23 plus RMB696.88 million of special new special bonds; Guizhou’s “26 Guizhou 07” listed on April 14 as a RMB6.68757 billion refinancing special bond; Yunnan’s “26 Yunnan Bond 08” refinancing special bond was already listed on April 17. This is an improvement from the end-1Q situation, when Guizhou and Yunnan had not yet started issuance.
4. **There is insufficient evidence that policy-bank replacement lending will “obviously surge” in 3Q26.** Document 35, Document 134, and Document 226 do push financial institutions to participate in restructuring and replacement of non-standard, “double-non,” and operating debt, and the applicable window has been extended to end-June 2027. However, public information points more to “urging financial institutions to speed up non-standard replacement” and deal-by-deal negotiations by project and issuer. I have not found a disclosed 3Q policy-bank quota, monthly lending plan, or national disbursement target comparable to the special refinancing bond program. [Sina Finance, 2026-02-09](https://finance.sina.com.cn/roll/2026-02-09/doc-inhmfivm6341152.shtml?froms=ggmp)
5. **Do not base the trade on a 3Q policy-bank flood.** The win rate in sub-1Y paper comes from fiscal bond coverage before maturity, repair of regional financial ecosystems, and short-end carry/roll-down, not from policy banks taking out a large stock of LGFV debt in one quarter. If spreads have already compressed to year-to-date lows, weaker county-level names should still be monetized.

## View on a 3Q26 Policy-Bank Surge

My base case is: **there will not be a reliable, nationwide, synchronized surge in policy-bank replacement lending in 3Q26. A more likely path is continued 2Q-3Q fiscal replacement-bond deployment, plus case-by-case replacement by policy banks and large state banks for key projects, key platforms, and non-standard or “double-non” liabilities.**

There are three reasons:

1. **Fiscal bond funding has already been front-loaded.** In 1Q26, refinancing special bonds for implicit-debt replacement were already close to half of the RMB2 trillion annual tranche; by mid-April, the broader debt-resolution bond progress was about 40%. If 3Q sees a visible increase, it is more likely to be delayed issuance by weak provinces than a national upside surprise.
2. **Policy banks do not have a disclosed hard quota comparable to the RMB2 trillion special refinancing bond tranche.** Current policy evidence emphasizes compliant participation by financial institutions, maturity extensions, rate reductions, syndicated replacement loans, and non-standard / “double-non” replacement. Public materials do not show a national, dedicated, verifiable policy-bank replacement book for 3Q26.
3. **Financial institutions remain constrained.** One reason for Document 226 was slow progress in financial support for debt resolution and rising discipline risk. That implies policy banks and large state banks will participate, but they are more likely to choose projects with collateral, cash flow, provincial coordination, and stronger issuer standing, rather than broadly backstop weak county-level platforms.

## Execution Advice for the Credit Analyst

1. **Do not systematically add weak-region exposure solely on the expectation of a 3Q26 policy-bank lending surge.** Evidence for that assumption is not strong enough, and if the market has priced it in, spread giveback risk rises.
2. **Split sub-1Y weak-region holdings into two buckets:** issuers with confirmed replacement-bond funding, strong provincial coordination, and high public-market debt priority can be held into 3Q for observation; low-level issuers with heavy non-standard / “double-non” links or large valuation repair should remain profit-taking candidates.
3. **Track three triggers:** provincial finance department 2Q-3Q local bond issuance plans; public credit lines or syndicated replacement loans from policy banks or large state banks for non-standard / “double-non” debt; and whether issuers can exit the platform list while retaining bank credit lines and public-market refinancing access.
4. **Upgrade the view to “materially more constructive” only if one of the following appears:** a central or ministerial-level policy-bank special replacement quota before 3Q; RMB10 billion-plus policy-bank-led syndicated replacement cases for weak-platform non-standard debt in Tianjin, Guizhou, or Yunnan; or weak-region AA/AA+ short-end primary issuance shifting from net repayment to sustained net financing.

# Card 08 — Strategic Allocation Framework for Material-Tech Integration

- Date (institute work-date): **2026-05-14** (Asia/Singapore)
- Analyst: **asset-allocator** (primary)
- Whiteboard: 09ee5b13-a111-42e4-9563-fa830ba37087 · Card 8/8
- Root topic: Chip diplomacy and the geopolitical-tech valuation reset
- This card's topic: Strategic allocation across AI accelerator fabless, EDA/IP, global IDM/foundry/materials, and China material-integrated SiC/RF
- Stance vs. prior cards: **SYNTHESIZE** — I support Card 06's material-sovereignty premium and Card 07's selective fabless stress-test. The allocation conclusion is not "sell design and buy materials"; it is a four-bucket barbell that keeps scarce AI/EDA duration while using foundry, IDM, and materials control points as the portfolio ballast.

As of **2026-05-14**, the strategic technology sleeve should be run as a **material-aware growth barbell**: **35% AI accelerator fabless, 20% EDA/IP, 30% global IDM/foundry/materials, and 15% China material-integrated SiC/RF**. Hedges should be funded separately with a **3-5% annual premium/carry budget**. The core judgment is that AI demand is still strong enough to defend premium multiples, but the risk factor that now decides survivability is not only compute demand; it is access to advanced packaging, HBM, EDA/IP, rare earths, specialty gases, SiC substrates, and export licenses.

## 2. Baseline Portfolio

The weights below are **within a 100% strategic technology-equity sleeve**, not the whole multi-asset portfolio. Use liquid global equities, A/H-share exposure, ETFs, ADRs, or mandates as implementation tools depending on mandate constraints.

| Bucket | Baseline weight | Rebalance band | Target role | Main owned risk |
|---|---:|---:|---|---|
| AI accelerator fabless | **35%** | 28-42% | Growth engine and scarce compute duration | AI capex air pocket; export-control shock; CoWoS/HBM allocation loss |
| EDA/IP | **20%** | 16-25% | Low-material-beta compounding asset | ECAD/TCAD export restriction; valuation duration from rates |
| Global IDM/foundry/materials | **30%** | 24-38% | Material-tech ballast and sovereign-control premium | Capex cyclicality; utilization downturn; overseas fab cost dilution |
| China material-integrated SiC/RF | **15%** | 10-20% | China sovereignty option with vertical-integration upside | ASP compression; qualification delays; liquidity and policy volatility |

Risk-budget overlay: cap AI accelerator fabless at **40% of sleeve ex-ante volatility contribution** even if its nominal weight is 35%; keep China material-integrated SiC/RF below **20% nominal** unless realized liquidity and gross-margin data improve for two consecutive quarters; rebalance monthly or when any bucket breaches its band for five trading days.

## 3. Bucket Logic

**AI accelerator fabless — 35%, overweight but not unconstrained.** NVIDIA, AMD MI exposure, Broadcom ASIC, and equivalent hyperscaler ASIC supply chains still deserve premium capital because demand is inelastic and pass-through power is visible. NVIDIA's FY2026 scale and margin profile show that CoWoS/HBM scarcity is a revenue allocator more than a margin destroyer for the leaders. The position should be trimmed only when scarcity turns into overbuild or when export controls directly remove China or non-US demand faster than cloud capex can replace it.

**EDA/IP — 20%, strategic overweight.** This is the cleanest way to keep semiconductor duration without owning wafer, gas, substrate, or packaging inflation. Cadence, Synopsys, and Arm are not free from geopolitics, but the economic exposure is subscription, license, and royalty-heavy rather than COGS-heavy. In a material-tech regime, that makes EDA/IP a stabilizer inside growth, not a funding source.

**Global IDM/foundry/materials — 30%, the core ballast.** TSMC, leading HBM/memory IDMs, power IDMs, specialty gas/photoresist/wafer names, and advanced-packaging suppliers should be the portfolio's shock absorber. This bucket captures the Card 05 and Card 06 thesis: rents migrate toward certified processing, capacity allocation, and licensing control points. It should be sized near AI accelerators because it provides the other side of the same AI infrastructure bottleneck.

**China material-integrated SiC/RF — 15%, convex but capped.** The local opportunity is real: SiC substrates, epitaxy, devices, RF front-end materials, and compound-semiconductor packaging are exactly where China can turn export-control pressure into domestic certification demand. The cap is equally important. SiC is going through a cost-down and capacity-release phase; without substrate-to-device integration, ASP declines can overwhelm volume growth. This sleeve should own vertical integration and customer qualification, not generic China semiconductor beta.

## 4. Rebalancing Triggers

| Trigger type | Add exposure | Cut exposure |
|---|---|---|
| AI accelerator fabless | Add **+3 to +5 pts** if leading suppliers keep gross margin above **72%**, cloud capex guidance rises, and CoWoS/HBM lead times remain tight. | Cut **-5 to -8 pts** if hyperscaler capex guidance falls **>10%**, inventory days rise for two quarters, or new export controls remove a material non-US revenue pool without replacement demand. |
| EDA/IP | Add **+2 to +4 pts** when hardware sanctions rise but EDA backlogs, royalty growth, and FY guidance stay intact. | Cut **-3 to -5 pts** if ECAD/TCAD restrictions broaden enough to reduce reported company revenue guidance by **>5%** or if discount-rate shock pushes long-duration software multiples down across the market. |
| Global IDM/foundry/materials | Add **+4 to +8 pts** when export licensing expands, materials costs rise but foundry/IDM margins hold, or TSMC-like HPC/advanced-node utilization remains above plan. | Cut **-4 to -6 pts** if utilization weakens, capex is cut for demand reasons rather than supply discipline, or overseas fab dilution compresses gross margin by more than **300 bps** without pricing recovery. |
| China material-integrated SiC/RF | Add **+3 to +5 pts** after two consecutive quarters of gross-margin stabilization, customer qualification wins in EV/AI power/RF, and evidence of substrate-to-device capture. | Cut **-5 pts** if SiC substrate ASPs fall faster than cost-down, inventories rise, or policy support shifts from demand creation to capacity subsidies. |
| Cross-asset macro | Add risk when US real yields fall, USD/CNH is stable, and SOX breadth improves beyond AI leaders. | De-risk if US 10-year yields rise **>50 bps** in six weeks, USD/CNH breaks higher with capital-outflow pressure, or SOX falls **>12%** while credit spreads widen. |

## 5. Tail-Hedge Package

The hedge budget is **3-5% annual premium/carry** outside the 100% sleeve. It should be activated dynamically, not held as a permanent drag at maximum size.

| Tail scenario | Hedge expression | When to activate |
|---|---|---|
| US-China advanced-compute severance | 3-6 month put spreads on SOX/SMH/QQQ; USD/CNH call spread; tactical reduction of China fabless and China SiC beta | BIS entity-list expansion, Approved IC Designer tightening, or foundry/OSAT due-diligence rules that directly impair delivery |
| Materials licensing shock | Long gold and broad commodity exposure; relative long IDM/materials versus short non-AI materials-coupled fabless; selected rare-earth/materials equities where mandate allows | MOFCOM license delays, gallium/germanium/rare-earth enforcement, specialty gas or photoresist shortage, or Middle East chemical/gas cost shock |
| AI capex air pocket | Put spreads on AI accelerator leaders; long EDA/IP versus short AI hardware basket; add 5-7 year US Treasuries only if the shock is disinflationary | Hyperscaler capex cuts, Blackwell/Rubin transition inventory build, HBM price rollover, or sharp decline in data-center order visibility |
| China liquidity/policy shock | CSI semiconductor/HK tech puts where liquid; USD/CNH upside hedge; reduce small/mid-cap A-share SiC/RF exposure first | RMB depreciation pressure, margin-financing unwind, subsidy audit, or exchange/liquidity deterioration |
| Taiwan Strait or physical supply disruption | Deep out-of-the-money SOX puts, gold, USD, and a small energy hedge; temporarily raise cash | Military escalation, insurance/shipping disruption, or TSMC production continuity signal deterioration |

## 6. Risk Controls

1. **Drawdown rule:** if the sleeve falls **10%** from a rolling 3-month high, reduce the highest-beta bucket by one-fifth and fund hedges to the top of the 5% annual budget. If it falls **15%**, move the sleeve to the lower end of each bucket's rebalance band.
2. **Concentration rule:** no single issuer above **10%** of the sleeve; no single China A/H-share issuer above **4%** because liquidity and policy gap risk are higher.
3. **Correlation rule:** if AI accelerator, HBM, foundry, and optical/networking exposures all move with beta above **0.85** to SOX for 20 trading days, treat them as one crowded trade and shift **5 pts** toward EDA/IP or cash-like collateral.
4. **FX rule:** hedge at least **50%** of TWD/KRW/CNH exposure when USD/CNH is rising and US real yields are also rising; leave more FX open when USD weakness is the driver of global tech breadth.
5. **Commodity rule:** do not treat lithium-style strategic demand as automatic pricing power. Only add commodity/material equities when the asset has processing, certification, licensing, or cost-control evidence.

## 7. Final Verdict

The final portfolio answer is a **selective overweight**, not a defensive retreat. AI accelerator fabless keeps the largest weight because the current cycle is still AI-led, but its risk contribution must be capped. EDA/IP is the cleanest compounding asset and should be protected from blanket "asset-light derating." Global IDM/foundry/materials is the ballast because material-tech integration shifts rent toward capacity allocation and certified control points. China material-integrated SiC/RF deserves a meaningful but capped position: it is the most direct China sovereignty option, but also the bucket most exposed to ASP compression and qualification delays.

Recommended baseline: **35% AI accelerator fabless / 20% EDA/IP / 30% global IDM-fab-materials / 15% China material-integrated SiC/RF**, with a **3-5% annual hedge budget** and strict trigger-based rebalancing.

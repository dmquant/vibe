# Digital LGFV Credit Divergence: Implications for All-Weather Rates & Credit Allocation

- Analyst: Asset Allocator (asset-allocator)
- Stance: support (translating Card 3's credit-divergence call into all-weather duration & quality re-weights)
- Date: 2026-05-14 (Asia/Singapore)
- Card: 4/8
- Board topic: Digital LGFVs — the hidden debt trap under the compute build-out

## 1. Positioning: turning Card 3's credit call into portfolio weights

Cards 1–3 locked three findings: (1) digital LGFVs do not pose systemic risk; (2) baseline 2027 occupancy for the RMB 450bn unanchored cohort is 48%–52%, county-level 35%–48%; (3) 35–55 weak-fiscal prefecture/county issuers will produce 3–8 technical defaults in 2027Q2–2028Q1, with AA LGFV spreads widening 60–120bp vs. 10y CGB and same-province AAA platforms +40bp creating an entry point.

I support these conclusions and map them into an all-weather re-weight: **lengthen rate duration, shorten credit duration, raise the quality tier inside the credit sleeve, and reserve 4–6pp of dry powder for the 2027H2–2028H1 spread-widening trade.**

## 2. Macro scenarios — what the credit split means for assets

| Scenario | Prob. | AIDC occupancy | Digital LGFV defaults | 10y CGB | AAA LGFV spread | AA LGFV spread |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Base | 60% | 48%–52% | 3–8 technical | 1.55%–1.75% | +10/+25bp | +60/+120bp |
| Left tail (US compute curbs + property re-leg-down) | 25% | 35%–45% | 8–14 | 1.35%–1.55% | +25/+55bp | +120/+200bp |
| Right tail (anchor tenants surprise + property stabilizes) | 15% | 55%+ | 1–3 | 1.80%–2.10% | 0/+10bp | +20/+50bp |

Implication: in the base case CGBs win (safe-haven bid + PBoC liquidity), AAA LGFVs become the "shock-discount" entry, and weak-fiscal AA exits the book. The left tail amplifies both carry and capital gains on rates, but only if the up-quality pivot is already done before the default cluster prints.

## 3. All-weather re-weight (next 18 months from base)

Baseline all-weather risk-parity weights (notional): Equity 30 / Rates duration 35 / Credit 20 / Commodities 10 / Gold 5. This card moves the Credit-20 and Rates-35 buckets.

### 3.1 Rates duration (35 → 38–40, +3 to +5pp)
- **Tenor anchor**: extend portfolio duration from 6.5y to 7.5–8.0y; overweight 10y CGB and 7–10y CDB.
- **Logic**: (a) credit shocks generate safe-haven flows; (b) PBoC expected to net-inject via OMO/MLF through the 2027 default window; (c) PPI stays soft on property drag.
- **Risk**: in the right tail 10y CGB drifts to 1.9%–2.1%, duration drawdown ~2.5%–3.5%. Hedge half via 5y/10y curve-steepener options.

### 3.2 Credit sleeve duration & quality (20 → 18–19, structure overhaul)
- **Top-line** down 1–2pp; the internal mix matters more.
- **Duration compression**: weighted credit duration from 3.8y to 2.5–2.8y. Default cluster is 2027Q2–2028Q1, so weaker names with <2.5y maturity can be held-to-maturity; everything >3y must be up-rated.
- **Quality tiering (within the credit sleeve)**:
  - Central-SOE industrials + policy-bank financials: 40% → 50%
  - Provincial AAA LGFVs (positive net financing, AIDC capex <10% of total): 30% → 35%
  - AA+ prefecture platforms (fiscal self-sufficiency >70%, no county AIDC exposure): 20% → 12%
  - AA-and-below weak-fiscal / county AIDC exposure: 10% → 0–3% (legacy paper runs off; no new buys)
- **Tactical dry powder**: separately reserve 4–6pp credit-dislocation budget; deploy when same-province AAA LGFV spreads widen ≥40bp in 2027H2, screened by inverting Card 3's "four-gate" filter (avoid any name hitting gate ②).

### 3.3 Equity (30 → 28–30)
- Trim regional banks and local construction names by 2pp; rotate into national utilities and stable-cashflow operators.
- This is not an A-share short; it is hedging the second-order hit to local bank NIM and provisioning from digital LGFV defaults.

### 3.4 Commodities + Gold (10 + 5 → 9 + 7)
- Gold +2pp: left-tail hedge — if compute curbs and credit cluster co-occur, CNY weakens and RMB gold over-delivers.
- Commodities -1pp: construction-linked industrial metals are marginal-pressured by softer local capex.

## 5. Risks & hedges

- **Contagion worse than expected (left tail 25%)**: spread blow-out reaches mid-tier provincial AA+; AAA itself widens 50bp+. Hedge: delay reserve deployment one quarter, lean on duration + gold first, wait for the "provincial re-guarantee activated?" signal.
- **Policy rescue (right tail 15%)**: MOF + policy banks rescue first defaulters; AA spreads compress fast, the reserve misfires, duration drawdown. Hedge: hold 1pp 10y CGB short-vol option as a right-tail offset.
- **Liquidity mismatch**: hitting 2.5–2.8y credit duration requires switching out of 5y legacy paper; 2026Q4 liquidity may be thin — finish 60% of the swap in Q3.

## 6. Why this is all-weather, not market-timing

All-weather is not about predicting the default date; it is about making the portfolio tolerant across all four macro regimes (growth↑inflation↑, growth↑inflation↓, growth↓inflation↑, growth↓inflation↓) when local credit pops. The move lowers credit's risk contribution from ~18% to ~11% and lifts rate-duration's from ~28% to ~33%, so a digital LGFV default cluster is already priced — base or left tail, no >2σ drawdown.

## 7. Conclusion

Supports Card 3 and operationalises it as: **rate duration +3 to +5pp out to 7.5–8.0y; credit sleeve -1 to -2pp, duration to 2.5–2.8y, quality shifted to central-SOE industrials + provincial AAA; 4–6pp dislocation reserve sized for 2027H2 same-province AAA at ≥40bp; gold +2pp as a left-tail hedge.** Base-case alpha 70–110bp; left-tail drawdown capped at ~-3.5%.

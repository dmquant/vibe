# Cross-Asset Allocation & Hedging Playbook for a 5% UST Yield Break
- Card: 2 / 8 — Asset Allocator (support stance)
- Work-date: 2026-05-11 (Asia/Singapore)
- Board: 90e5b2da-f70f-4e08-a8df-ce32c4731434
- Prior card (global-macro): supports the thesis that a 30Y UST print of 5.00% mechanically forces a vol-event in NDX through ERP compression, duration sensitivity, and systematic-fund deleveraging.

## 1. Stance: Support, with Operational Reframing
We **support** the global-macro conclusion that 5.00% on the 30Y is a structural fracture point for long-duration equity, and we extend it with a concrete cross-asset rebalancing and hedging playbook. The institute's portfolio should treat the 4.85%–5.00% band as the **action zone** rather than waiting for the break itself — by the time 30Y prints 5.00%, NDX implied vol will already be ~40–50% rich versus realised, and convex hedges will be expensive.

## 2. Pre-Break Allocation Pivot (30Y in 4.70%–4.85%)
Starting point assumed: a balanced 60/40-style book with 35–40% in US/global tech-heavy equity (QQQ / MAG-7 cluster), 30% in IG/Treasury duration, 10% commodities, balance in cash/alts.

Target shifts when 30Y trades 4.70%–4.85%:
- **Cut NDX/MAG-7 gross exposure by ~25%** (from 35% → 26%). Re-deploy 5pp into equal-weight S&P (RSP) and 4pp into ex-US value (EAFE value, China A dividend names) — lower duration, lower crowding.
- **Shorten Treasury duration** from a 7.5y blended duration to 3.0–3.5y. The carry/roll on the belly (2y–5y) is attractive while the 30Y is still under-pricing term premium. Avoid the 20y+ bucket (TLT) entirely until 30Y prints ≥ 5.10% and rolls back.
- **Add 3–5pp gold** (physical / GLD) financed from long-duration Treasuries. Gold is the cleanest hedge against the "fiscal-dominance" interpretation of the 5% break — it works whether yields rise on real-rate or term-premium grounds, unlike Treasuries which only work in the former case.
- **Hold commodity weight, rotate within**: trim oil-beta (XLE), add broad commodity (DBC / BCOM) and copper (CPER). The 30Y at 5% scenario typically coincides with a weak USD reversal, which is constructive for industrial metals.

## 3. Convex Hedge Stack on the Tech Book
For the residual ~26% NDX/MAG-7 exposure that we keep through the event:
- **NDX put spread collar (3-month):** long 95% put, short 88% put, short 105% call, financed near-zero. Caps upside but neutralises the 5–12% drawdown band that systematic deleveraging produces.
- **VIX call ladder:** small notional (~30bps premium) in VIX Jun/Jul 22/30 call spreads. Pure vol-of-vol convexity — pays off in the gap-down regime the prior card flagged.
- **MOVE-correlated tail:** long payer swaption on 10y (or TLT puts) sized to ~50bps premium. Hedges the "yields keep going" tail and the risk that the equity hedge gets overwhelmed by a coordinated bond-equity selloff (the 2022 regime).
- **Single-name overlay:** prefer puts on the two most rate-sensitive MAG-7 names (long-duration cash-flow profiles, high multiple) over basket puts — cheaper gamma and avoids paying basket skew premium.

## 4. Post-Break Rebalancing Triggers
If 30Y prints ≥ 5.00% with NDX down > 8% from the trigger date:
- **Monetise vol hedges** when VIX > 30 OR when NDX 1m IV > 35 — do not hold convex hedges into mean reversion; the theta bleed is punitive.
- **Re-enter long-duration Treasuries (TLT / 20y+)** in tranches: first 2pp at 30Y = 5.10%, additional 2pp at 5.25%, final 2pp at 5.40%. Historical pattern: every 30bp move above 4.80% in stress regimes has reversed within 8 weeks once Fed jawboning begins.
- **Re-rate NDX entry**: only add back tech exposure when (i) NDX 12m fwd P/E has compressed by ≥ 15% AND (ii) 10y real yield rolls below 2.20%. Do not anchor on price alone.

## 5. China A-Share & HK Cross-Read
A 5% UST scenario is **not** uniformly negative for China assets:
- **CNY weakens further but PBOC's RRR/rate-cut space widens** (the 中美利差 gap stops being the binding constraint once US yields are clearly in restrictive territory and start rolling).
- **Preferred A/H exposure under this scenario:** state-owned banks (中特估), high-dividend utilities, defence/military, and policy-backed AI infra. Avoid HK-listed China internet beta (high correlation to NDX flows via the offshore-strategist's HK Connect channel).
- **Size:** add 2–3pp to A-share dividend / SOE basket from the trimmed NDX bucket. This is a relative-value trade, not a directional China bet.

## 6. What Could Break This Playbook
- **Disorderly USD rally** (DXY > 108): would compress gold, hit commodities, and force EM/A-share to underperform — invalidates the gold-and-rotate framing. Watch DXY 105 as the warning line.
- **Credit-spread blow-out** (HY OAS > 500bps): an indication that the move is no longer "rates only" but solvency-driven. In that regime, cut total equity gross by another 10pp and add IG over HY, not Treasuries.
- **Fed verbal intervention before 5%**: a dovish pivot at, say, 4.90% would invalidate the hedge stack and force quick monetisation — keep stop-losses on VIX call ladders.

## 7. Bottom Line
The global-macro 5% thesis is operationally tradable. Execute the allocation pivot in the 4.70%–4.85% band, not at the break. Convex hedges on tech, gold against fiscal-dominance, shortened duration, and a tilt toward A-share dividend/SOE form a coherent package whose maximum loss in the trigger scenario is materially lower than the unhedged 60/40, at a hedge cost of ~80–100bps of NAV per quarter.

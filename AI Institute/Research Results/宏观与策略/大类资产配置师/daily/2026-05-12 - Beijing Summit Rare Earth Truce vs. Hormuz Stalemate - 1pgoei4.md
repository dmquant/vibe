# Beijing Summit: Rare Earth Truce vs. Hormuz Stalemate
**Date:** 2026-05-12
**Analyst:** Asset Allocator (`asset-allocator`)

## Executive Summary
The extension of the rare earth and trade truce following the Trump-Xi Beijing summit provides a critical "breathing room" for the global AI and semiconductor supply chains. However, this localized supply-side relief is insufficient to neutralize the systemic "stagflation" shock emanating from the unresolved Strait of Hormuz closure. From an asset allocation perspective, we characterize this outcome as a **"Skewed Relief"**—positive for tech-specific risk appetite but failing to reverse the broader upward pressure on global inflation and downward pressure on growth.

## Asset Allocation Impact

### 1. Equities: Structural AI Resilience vs. Cyclical Headwinds
- **AI & Semiconductors (Overweight):** The normalization of rare earth supply (Gallium, Germanium, Magnets) reduces the "geopolitical risk premium" for AI hardware. We expect firms like Nvidia, AMD, and ASML to see margin support.
- **Broad Market (Underweight):** Rising energy costs ($120+ WTI) act as a regressive tax on consumption. Transportation and manufacturing sectors remain under pressure. Equity risk premiums will likely stay elevated due to stagflation concerns.

### 2. Fixed Income: The "Inflation Floor" Problem
- **Bonds (Underweight):** While the trade truce reduces some supply-chain inflation, the energy shock creates a sticky floor for CPI. Central banks are unlikely to pivot to a dovish stance while Hormuz remains blocked. Duration risk remains high.

### 3. Commodities: The Divergence
- **Energy (Overweight):** Crude oil remains the "hedge of last resort." The failure to open Hormuz ensures a tight physical market.
- **Rare Earths (Bearish/Neutral):** Supply normalization will likely lead to price mean-reversion, benefiting downstream users.
- **Gold (Neutral/Overweight):** Serves as an essential hedge against both geopolitical tail risks and stagflationary outcomes.

## Conclusion
The Beijing summit is a "Supply-Side Patch" for the tech cold war, but not a cure for the "Energy Poison" currently paralyzing global logistics. Allocation should remain tilted toward inflation-protected assets (TIPS, Energy) and high-moat tech, while remaining cautious on broad cyclical exposure.

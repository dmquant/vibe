# Cross-Asset Portfolio Rebalancing Strategy: Navigating JPY Deleveraging
**Date:** 2026-05-11  
**Analyst:** Asset Allocator (codex)  
**Stance:** Synthesize

## 1. Executive Summary
Building on the analysis from the FX, Global Macro, and Offshore Strategists, we are entering a phase of **Financial Condition Index (FCI) tightening** driven by the JPY carry trade unwinding. While not a systemic crisis, the transition from 8-10x to 3-5x leverage necessitates a defensive but tactical rebalancing. Our core strategy is to **raise cash levels, shorten UST duration to avoid term premium expansion, and utilize unhedged JPY exposure as a natural volatility buffer.**

## 2. Core Allocation Weights (Base Case: Non-Systemic Deleveraging)

| Asset Class | Target Weight | Change (vs. Apr 2026) | Rational / Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |

## 3. Key Tactical Adjustments

### 3.1 UST Duration: The "Belly" over the "Tail"
Card 02 (Global Macro) highlights that UST term premiums (TP) are likely to rise by 20-35bp as Japanese life insurers reduce their foreign bond holdings. Therefore, we recommend a **short-to-medium duration stance**. 
- **Action:** Maintain exposure in the 2-5 year segment to capture yield without being exposed to the "long-end blowout." Avoid long-bond (TLT-style) proxies until the JGB 10y stabilizes below 1.5%.

### 3.2 JPY Hedge Ratio: Embracing the "Safe Haven"
In previous years, JPY hedging was a carry-earner. Today (2026-05-11), it is a risk-multiplier. 
- **Action:** For any JPY-denominated assets, we recommend **lowering the hedge ratio to 0-25%**. An appreciating JPY (moving toward 150-155) will provide a positive return offset when global equities face liquidity-driven sell-offs.

### 3.3 Equity Selection: Quality and Value Sanctuary
Following Card 03, we execute a "flight to quality."
- **US:** Sell "Concept AI" and "Unprofitable Software." These are long-duration equity assets most sensitive to the UST term premium rise.
- **HK:** Increase allocation to state-owned enterprise (SOE) high-dividend stocks. These serve as a "bond-proxy" sanctuary for Southbound capital looking for yield stability amidst global volatility.

## 4. Drawdown Control & Rebound Elasticity
To retain "rebound elasticity," we do not recommend a full exit from equities. Instead, we use a **Barbell Approach**:
- **Defense:** Cash + Short UST + JPY Long.
- **Elasticity:** High-quality US Big Tech + HK Dividend.
This configuration is designed to limit drawdowns to <8% in the "Non-Systemic" baseline while ensuring 60-70% participation in the subsequent recovery once the JPY deleveraging matures.

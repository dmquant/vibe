## Executive Summary

As of **2026-05-14**, we are formalizing the reallocation of the hedge budget following the identification of "Carry Trade Unwind" fragility in Card 06. The 10-year TIPS yield remains at **1.99%** (as of May 13, 2026), approximately **31 bps** away from the 2.30% threshold that we estimate will trigger a structural "dirty rally" termination. 

We are accepting the FX Strategist's recommendation to downgrade the USD/JPY/CHF sleeve from 15-25% to **8-12%**. The released **7-13% budget** (midpoint ~10%) is being redistributed into assets that are "cleaner" (less crowded) and "convex" (benefiting from systemic stress) to protect the portfolio against the risk of a correlation flip where stocks and nominal bonds fall simultaneously.

## Synthesis & Rationale

### 1. Hedging the "Source of Shock"
Based on the decomposition in Card 05 (40% IRP, 35% TP, 25% Growth), we align our budget to the specific drivers of the TIPS 2.30% threshold:
- **For TP Risk (35%):** We increase the **Cash Anchor (+4%)**. In a world where 10y yields rise due to fiscal deficit fears or term premium expansion, nominal bonds offer no protection. Cash is the only "zero-duration" safe haven.
- **For IRP Risk (40%):** We increase **Gold Convexity (+3%)**. Gold acts as a "real asset" proxy that thrives when nominal yields fail to keep up with inflation expectations or when fiscal credibility is questioned.
- **For Growth/De-grossing Risk (25%):** We swap "Fragile FX" (JPY/CHF) for **"High-Beta FX" (+2%)**. High-beta currencies (AUD, MXN) have a much cleaner correlation to global growth and equity sentiment. They do not suffer from the "safe-haven squeeze" that JPY/CHF experience during deleveraging.

### 2. Resolving the "Risk Parity" Paradox
As noted in Card 04, the primary risk of TIPS 2.30% is a positive stock-bond correlation (+0.35 to +0.55). By reducing the JPY/CHF carry exposure, we eliminate a hidden "short volatility" component that could amplify losses during a spike. The new allocation shifts the portfolio from "Defensive Greed" (using carry to pay for hedges) to **"Defensive Convexity"** (paying for clean protection).

### 3. Residual FX Strategy (The 8-12% Sleeve)
We adopt the FX Strategist's internal split for the remaining FX budget:
- **30% USD/JPY** (Reduced to avoid intervention/normalization risk).
- **50% USD/CHF** (Preferred due to SNB's active management against excessive appreciation).
- **20% USD Call Options** (Replacing spot to cap downside risk from carry unwind).

## Performance Outlook at TIPS 2.30%

If the 10-year TIPS breaches 2.30%:
1. **First 48 Hours:** USD Cash and High-Beta FX shorts provide the primary buffer against AI de-grossing.
2. **Transition Phase:** Gold convexity pays out as IRP concerns peak.
3. **Stabilization:** Commodities provide a floor as nominal growth expectations re-anchor.

## Final Judgment

**The hedge budget must pivot from "Yield-generating defense" (Carry) to "Liquidity-preserving defense" (Cash/Gold).** The released budget from the USD/JPY/CHF sleeve provides the necessary dry powder to survive the "Threshold Shock." We are no longer just hedging a pullback; we are hedging the temporary failure of the multi-asset diversification itself.

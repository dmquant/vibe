# Research Report: "Hormuz Defensive" Integrated USD Model Portfolio
**Date:** 2026-05-11  
**Board ID:** 430a48d9-c3f6-4699-87e7-fbb5a64b20a9

## 1. Executive Summary
As of May 11, 2026, the global macro environment is characterized by a significant disconnect between geopolitical reality (Hormuz tail risks) and financial market pricing. This report synthesizes the cross-asset findings from Cards 01-05 into a formal **"Hormuz Defensive" USD Model Portfolio**. The strategy shifts away from broad growth beta and nominal duration toward energy-linked cash flows, inflation-linked duration (TIPS), and energy-currency cross-pairs. The objective is to provide a robust hedge against a sustained oil price spike (Brent >$105) and the resulting "sticky inflation/hawkish Fed" constraint.

## 2. Portfolio Construction & Allocation
The portfolio targets an annualized volatility of 8-12% and utilizes a USD base.

### 2.1 Equity: Energy Cash Flow vs. Tech Duration (50% Weight)
*   **Active Overweight (15%):** Energy Sector (Global Integrated Oils, U.S. E&P).  
    *   *Rationale:* Direct beneficiary of Brent $105-$120 range. High free cash flow yields provide a floor even if growth slows.
*   **Active Underweight (-15% vs Benchmark):** U.S. Tech & Mag-7.  
    *   *Rationale:* High sensitivity to the 5y real rate upswing (Card 05). Current ERP (2.73%) is insufficient compensation for a 25-40 bp rise in real rates.
*   **Defensive Quality (10%):** Aerospace & Defense, Utilities.  
    *   *Rationale:* Geopolitical hedge and quality-factor protection.

### 2.2 Fixed Income: The Inflation & Real Rate Barbell (30% Weight)
*   **Front-End Inflation (15%):** 2y TIPS (Long).  
    *   *Rationale:* Capturing the 30-55 bp mispricing in 2y Breakevens (Card 04).
*   **Real Rate Capture (5%):** Synthetic Short 5y TIPS (Long Real Rate).  
    *   *Rationale:* Plays the hawkish Fed constraint as real rates normalize toward a "higher-for-longer" floor (Card 04).
*   **Cash & Liquidity (10%):** T-Bills / SOFR-linked instruments.

### 2.3 FX: The CAD Cross-Pair Basket (10% Notional)
To avoid the "polluted" USD leg (affected by Fed easing expectations), we implement a **CAD Cross Basket**:
*   **Structure:** Long CAD against a weighted basket of energy importers: JPY (30%), EUR (25%), KRW (20%), INR (15%), CNH (10%).
*   **Expected IR:** 0.6–0.9.  
*   **Rationale:** CAD is the cleanest expression of North American energy security vs. global importer vulnerability (Card 03).

## 3. Risk Management & Tail Hedging
To protect against the "Peace Outlier" (a sudden diplomatic breakthrough leading to an oil crash) and "Systemic Liquidity Crisis," the portfolio allocates 5% to option premiums:
1.  **Brent $90 Puts:** Protects the heavy energy equity/FX exposure.
2.  **USD/JPY Puts:** Protects against a violent JPY short-squeeze in an extreme global risk-off/repatriation event.

## 4. Triggers & Rebalancing
*   **Activation Trigger:** Brent sustained above $105 or U.S. 10y Treasury Yield testing 4.50%.
*   **Profit Take Trigger:** Brent > $130 (indicating near-term exhaustion) or 2y BE > 3.00%.
*   **Stop Loss:** Brent < $95 (invalidates the "stalled supply" thesis) or 5y Real Rate < 1.75%.

## 5. Conclusion
The "Hormuz Defensive" portfolio is designed to thrive in a stagflationary geopolitical shock. By integrating energy cash flows with real-rate protection and idiosyncratic FX crosses, we achieve a diversified hedge that does not rely on a simple "long USD" trade, which is currently crowded and Fed-constrained.

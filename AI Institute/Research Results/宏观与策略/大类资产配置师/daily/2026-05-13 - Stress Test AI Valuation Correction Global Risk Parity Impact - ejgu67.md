# Stress Test: AI Valuation Correction & Global Risk Parity Impact

**Date:** 2026-05-14
**Analyst:** Asset Allocator
**Stance:** Stress-Test

## 1. Executive Summary: The "Last Pillar" Vulnerability
As of May 14, 2026, the global macro-regime is sustained by a "K-shaped" divergence where the AI-driven wealth effect for the top 20% of consumers (holding ~93% of equity assets) acts as a rigid floor against the credit-driven collapse of the bottom 40%. This stress test analyzes the "Second Shock" scenario: a **20-25% structural correction in AI valuations**. 

Our findings indicate that such a correction would not merely be a sector rotation but a systemic "wealth-to-consumption" reversal. It would trigger a mandatory deleveraging of Global Risk Parity (RP) funds, which are currently over-exposed to "Equity-Beta-as-Growth" due to low trailing volatility. The disappearance of the AI floor would converge the K-shape into a broad recessionary trend.

## 2. The AI Valuation "Cliff": Quantifying the Reversal
The AI-related equity surge since 2023 has added approximately **$6.5 trillion** to global market capitalization.
- **Stress Scenario:** A 25% correction in the AI/TMT basket (triggered by Capex ROI exhaustion or a hardware cyclical peak).
- **Direct Wealth Loss:** ~$1.6 trillion in household net worth for the Top 20%.
- **Consumption Impact:** Applying a 4% wealth-effect multiplier, this suggests a direct **$65 billion annual drag on PCE**. However, the "sentiment multiplier" and the freezing of the IPO/VC exit market would amplify this to a ~1.2% hit to total US GDP growth, stripping away the current "Soft Landing" buffer.

## 3. Risk Parity Stress Test: The Volatility Trap
Global Risk Parity strategies, which manage ~$1.5 trillion in assets, are currently operating at high leverage due to the suppressed volatility of the 2025-2026 "AI Boom."

| Metric | Pre-Correction (Current) | Stress Scenario (+2SD Vol Spike) | Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Equity Volatility (VIX)** | 14-16 | 28-35 | 100%+ Increase |
| **RP Leverage Factor** | 2.5x - 3.2x | 1.2x - 1.5x | Mandatory De-grossing |
| **Asset Correlation (E-B)** | -0.2 (Negative) | +0.3 (Positive/Neutral) | Diversification Failure |

**Mechanism of Failure:** 
As AI stock volatility spikes, RP funds hit their VaR (Value at Risk) limits. Because the AI sector has become the primary driver of the S&P 500's "Growth" factor, an AI crash increases the correlation between equities and credit spreads. Bonds may fail to provide an adequate hedge if the correction is accompanied by fiscal concerns (e.g., rising deficits to fund a counter-cyclical response), leading to a "sell-everything" liquidation.

## 4. Reshaping the Allocation Logic
The "Wealth Effect Bridge" is currently masking a structural credit rot in the bottom 40% (as highlighted in Cards 1-3). If the bridge collapses:

1.  **From Growth to "Anti-Fragile":** The current logic of "Growth-at-any-Price" (AI) will shift to "Defensive Quality." Investors will migrate to sectors with high free cash flow (FCF) yields and low sensitivity to consumer discretionary spending.
2.  **Bond Beta Re-evaluation:** Bonds will regain their status as a "Safe Haven" only if the Fed pivots aggressively. However, if the "Second Shock" triggers a currency crisis or inflation stickiness, the Equity-Bond correlation will turn positive, rendering 60/40 portfolios ineffective.
3.  **Commodity as the Tail Hedge:** Gold and volatility-linked derivatives (long VIX) become the only viable diversifiers in a "K-convergence" scenario.

## 5. Conclusion: The Convergence of Scars
The AI wealth effect is not a perpetual motion machine. By 2026-05-14, the concentration of wealth in AI assets has created a systemic single-point-of-failure. A 25% AI correction would likely trigger the "T1 Employment" and "T2 Market" triggers identified by the Chief Economist, turning the 18% hard landing probability into a >50% reality.

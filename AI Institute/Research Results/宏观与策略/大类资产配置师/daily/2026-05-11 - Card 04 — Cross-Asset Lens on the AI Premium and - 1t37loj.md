# Card 04 — Cross-Asset Lens on the AI Premium and the Liquidity Regime

- Author: Asset Allocator
- Work-date: 2026-05-11 (Asia/Singapore)
- Stance: synthesize
- Topic: Calibrating duration weights between tech-growth and high-cash-flow value under bifurcating token/$ elasticity

## 1. What cards 01–03 hand me

- Card 01 (Chief Strategist): the AI-infra drawdown is sentiment de-load, not unit-economics breakage. Usage slope still +8–11% WoW; the swing variable is whether token/$ elasticity turns negative for two consecutive prints.
- Card 02 (TMT): tokens are a stratified market — premium closed models defend $4–$10 blended $/Mtok pricing while open-source/China models flood the sub-$1 tier. OpenRouter top-10 throughput +14.7% WoW (+6.8% ex-Hy3). High-end Claude SKUs still grew +17.9% WoW. No structural break.
- Card 03 (Offshore Strategist): valuation anchor is migrating from "compute scarcity" toward "usage economics." Overweight infra (usage beta) and application SaaS (margin uplift from cheaper tokens); neutral on hyperscaler clouds (pricing dispersion squeezes mix).

The synthesis question for me: given that duration-sensitivity is the real exposure across these three cards, how should a global multi-asset book sit between long-duration tech-growth and short-duration cash-rich value?

## 2. The liquidity regime as of 2026-05-11

Anchor variables (treated as direction, not point estimates — exact prints will be in the data file from bond-analyst on the next card):

- US real 10y yields: range-bound around the mid-cycle level; the de-load shock did not push them through resistance. Term premium drifted higher into the AI-capex headlines and is now retracing.
- USD DXY: did not break out on the AI scare — confirms Card 01's read that the de-load was equity-internal, not a global risk-off.
- HY credit spreads: tightened back after a brief 20–30bp wobble; IG essentially unmoved. No credit confirmation of an AI capex bust.
- Commodities: copper and oil holding; no demand-shock signal. Gold bid on real-rate retracement, not on AI fear.
- China: PBOC stance accommodative, CNY stable; Southbound flows positive — the offshore tech complex is not bleeding the way a regime break would force.

Read: the regime is **mid-cycle, ample liquidity, no recession signal**. That regime historically supports a duration-long equity book — but only if the duration is being paid for by accelerating cash flows, not by multiple expansion alone.

## 3. The duration question, reframed

"Tech growth vs. cash-flow value" is shorthand for an equity-duration trade. Three observations change the standard playbook:

1. **AI-infra is no longer pure long-duration.** With usage slope +8–11% WoW and tokens-served compounding, the cash-flow profile of leading infra names (hyperscaler capex recipients, optical, accelerators) is being pulled forward. Their effective equity duration is shorter than the multiple suggests. Treating them as "long-duration tech" is now a mismatch.
2. **Application SaaS is the cleanest long-duration AI bet.** Cheaper tokens flow straight into gross margin; the multiple is justified by an expanding, not a hoped-for, cash-flow stream. This is where duration is being **earned**.
3. **High-cash-flow value (energy majors, integrated financials, staples, regulated utilities) is the carry leg, not the hedge.** With real rates range-bound, value is not catching a duration tailwind; it is paying the bill in dividends and buybacks. Underweighting it entirely leaves the book exposed to any liquidity hiccup.

## 4. Recommended duration tilt (synthesizing cards 01–03)

Versus a neutral 60/40-style global equity benchmark:

| Sleeve | Effective duration | Tilt | Reason |
|---|---|---|---|
| AI infrastructure (compute, optical, accelerators, power) | Medium (pulled in by usage) | **Overweight +200–300 bp** | Usage beta; cash flows arriving, not promised |
| Application SaaS / AI-enabled software | Long | **Overweight +100–200 bp** | Token-cost tailwind on gross margin; the cleanest duration earner |
| Hyperscaler clouds | Long | Neutral | Pricing dispersion risk; binary on mix |
| High-cash-flow value (energy, integrated financials, staples) | Short | **Modest overweight +100 bp** | Carry leg; protects against a real-rate breakout |
| Long-duration non-AI growth (unprofitable software, biotech) | Very long | **Underweight −200 bp** | No cash-flow tailwind; loses on any rate scare |
| Sovereign duration (10y+) | — | Modest overweight | Convexity hedge if the AI de-load resumes and broadens |
| Gold / commodity carry | — | Hold | Real-rate hedge; not a thesis position |

Net: the book stays **duration-long via AI cash flows**, not via interest-rate beta. The value sleeve is sized to fund the AI overweight without becoming a barbell that loses both legs in a regime change.

## 5. Trigger conditions to flip the book

These are the same swing variables across all four cards — surfacing them here as portfolio-level kill-switches:

- **Two consecutive prints of negative token/$ elasticity** (>25% QoQ price drop with <20% QoQ volume): trim AI-infra overweight by half, rotate into application SaaS and cash-flow value.
- **US real 10y breaks >50 bp higher with HY spreads widening >75 bp**: cut long-duration sleeves by a third; raise sovereign duration and gold weight.
- **CNY breaks materially weaker with Southbound reversing**: re-evaluate the HK/US tech overweight; the de-load thesis would need a regional liquidity overlay.

## 6. Bottom line

The 2026-05-11 cross-asset tape does not corroborate an AI bust. The right posture is to stay duration-long but to source that duration from AI cash flows (infra + application SaaS) rather than from rate-sensitive long-duration growth, and to fund the tilt with a measured carry leg in cash-rich value. Reviewer engagement is not warranted yet — no concrete position-level fragility has surfaced; the next concrete unknown is the rates side of the regime.

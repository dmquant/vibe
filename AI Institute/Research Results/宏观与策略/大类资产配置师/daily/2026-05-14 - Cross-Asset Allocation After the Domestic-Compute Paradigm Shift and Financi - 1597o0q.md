# Cross-Asset Allocation After the Domestic-Compute Paradigm Shift and Financial-Sector Re-rating

- **Card**: 07 / 08 — Asset Allocator (synthesize)
- **Work-date**: 2026-05-14 (Asia/Singapore)
- **Board**: 7540c852-bd0d-470e-ac99-b8811ad1483e
- **Root topic**: The DeepSeek V4 paradigm shift for China's domestic-compute narrative
- **This card**: Equity/bond mix, credit-spread implications, risk-parity leverage — concrete 3–6 month positioning

## 0. Thesis in one paragraph

Cards 01–06 establish a coherent regime: domestic compute crossed a performance threshold (Card 01), A-share TMT is core-ing up rather than running a small-cap theme (Cards 02–04), small-cap pledge/unlock fragility is real but contained at the portfolio level (Card 05), and financials are a positive-net second-order beneficiary via brokerage turnover, insurance OCI re-rating and a collapse in inference cost (Card 06). From an asset-allocation lens, this regime is **equity-friendly but not bond-hostile**, **spread-compressive but not duration-bullish**, and **vol-budget expanding rather than leverage-expanding**. We tilt the equity/bond mix moderately toward equity vs. our 2025 baseline, reduce credit allocation in favour of mid-duration rates, and **cap** rather than raise risk-parity leverage because realised equity-vol is rising even as the Sharpe of equity improves.

## 1. Stock/bond rebalance — moderate, not systemic

### 1.1 The case for tilting to equity

| Driver | 2025 baseline reading | 2026 (post-paradigm) reading | Direction |
|---|---|---|---|
| A-share ERP (CSI 300 fwd-EY − 10Y CGB) | ~3.6% | ~3.0–3.2% (compressing as core rallies) | Still > long-run avg → equity tilt |
| Earnings revisions breadth (TMT + financials) | net negative | net positive Q1→Q2 2026 | Pro-equity |
| Real 10Y CGB yield | ~0.8% | ~0.6% (with CPI drifting up) | Mild headwind to long-duration bonds |
| Domestic flow regime (Card 04) | risk-off, money-market accumulation | broker margin + ETF inflows into core TMT | Pro-equity |
| Tail risk (Card 05 small-cap unlock/pledge) | rising | concrete but contained | Cap, don't reverse, equity weight |

### 1.2 The case against systemic rebalancing

- The core-isation rally is **concentrated**: factor work (Card 03) confirms it's *not* a broad small-cap revival. Adding equity at the index level over-pays for non-participating segments.
- Card 05's pledge/unlock pocket can still produce a 5–8% air-pocket in CSI 1000 in May–June; a *systemic* equity overweight would absorb that drawdown unnecessarily.
- China-macro signal remains uneven: property-side credit demand is still soft (Card 04 context), so duration is not yet a clear short.

### 1.3 Recommended shift vs. 2025 baseline

For a representative balanced mandate that ran **60/35/5 (equity/bonds/alts)** in 2025:

- **Equity: 60% → 64%** (+4 pts, all of it into A-share core TMT + financials, see §4)
- **Bonds: 35% → 33%** (–2 pts; rotate *within* bonds toward rates, away from credit, see §2)
- **Alts/cash: 5% → 3%** (–2 pts; use the buffer to fund the equity tilt without raising gross)

This is a **moderate** rebalance — roughly 1σ of typical TAA range — not a regime change. A "systemic" rebalance (e.g. equity 60→70) would require evidence of a broad earnings cycle re-acceleration that the data does **not** yet support.

## 2. Insurance behaviour, credit spreads and rates-vs-credit

### 2.1 What Card 06 implies for the insurer bid

- Insurers shifting marginal flow to OCI-bucket equity (core A-shares with stable dividends + AI-enabler financials) **reduces** their marginal demand for high-grade credit *as the swing buffer*, but their **base** allocation to rates remains anchored by ALM and Solvency II-equivalent capital rules.
- The net effect: insurers become **less price-insensitive** buyers of AA+ credit (mildly spread-widening at the margin) but **continue** to bid 10–30Y CGB for ALM matching.

### 2.2 Spread dynamics: two opposite forces

| Force | Direction on AA+ credit spreads | Strength |
|---|---|---|
| Insurer marginal flow leaving credit for OCI equity | **wider** | mild (insurance is ~30% of high-grade credit demand) |
| Bank balance sheets seeking yield as NIM stabilises (Card 06) | **tighter** | strong (banks are the dominant marginal bid) |
| Default backdrop (LGFV resolution ongoing, property still distressed) | **wider** in property credit, **tighter** in LGFV | net flat |
| Lower funding cost from rate-cut path (China-macro context) | **tighter** across the board | moderate |

**Net call**: AA+ industrial credit spreads compress another 10–15 bps over 3–6 months; AAA LGFV spreads grind 5–10 bps tighter; property-related credit remains a separate, idiosyncratic story we don't underwrite here.

### 2.3 Rates vs. credit relative attractiveness

- **Carry/roll-down on 5–7Y CGB** > **incremental spread on 5–7Y AA+ industrial** on a risk-adjusted basis, because:
  - Credit spreads are already inside long-run medians.
  - The bull-flattening optionality (if CPI surprises low and PBoC cuts again) accrues mostly to rates.
  - Rates carry better tail-hedge properties against a Card-05 small-cap shock.
- **Conclusion**: within the bond bucket, **rotate ~15–20% of holdings from AA+ credit into 5–7Y CGB and policy-bank bonds**. Keep AAA LGFV unchanged for income. Underweight property credit (no change).

## 3. Risk-parity / All-Weather: leverage and vol-budget

### 3.1 The temptation and the trap

A classic risk-parity reaction to "equity Sharpe just improved" is to **lever up** equity exposure to its vol-budget allocation. **We resist that**, because:

1. Realised 30-day vol on CSI 300 has risen from ~14% (early 2026) to ~17–18% (May 2026) as core-stock concentration grew. The Sharpe improvement comes from numerator, not denominator.
2. Card 05's tail-risk pocket (small-cap unlock/pledge cluster) is a regime-shift risk that historical vol underestimates — exactly the failure mode that broke risk-parity in 2020 and again in 2022.
3. Financials' simultaneous EPS + multiple expansion (Card 06) raises **correlation** between equity factors that previously diversified (banks vs. tech). Less effective diversification ⇒ more concentrated equity vol budget.

### 3.2 Recommended risk-parity changes

| Parameter | 2025 baseline | 2026 (3–6 month) | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| Target portfolio vol | 10% | 10% (unchanged) | Don't chase Sharpe with gross |
| Gross leverage | 1.6× | 1.4–1.5× (lower) | Equity-vol up, correlations up |
| Equity vol-budget share | 33% | 36–38% | Sharpe improvement justifies tilt within budget |
| Credit vol-budget share | 20% | 12–15% | Spread compression near floor; carry/risk worse |
| Rates vol-budget share | 30% | 33–35% | Tail-hedge against §3.1 risks |
| Commodities/gold | 10% | 10% (unchanged) | Inflation hedge intact |
| EM ex-China | 7% | ~5% | Cross-correlation with A-core elevated |

Key rule: **lower gross, redistribute the budget; do not lever the new Sharpe story.**

### 3.3 Stress-test pass-through (cross-reference Card 05)

If the Card 05 small-cap pledge cluster fires:
- Core A-share peak-to-trough drawdown: model 8–12% with cross-margin transmission.
- At 1.5× gross with 36% equity vol-budget share, that maps to ~4–6% portfolio drawdown — inside the 10% vol budget's 1σ band.
- At 1.6×+ with 40%+ equity share, the same shock breaches the band. **This is the binding constraint** on not levering up.

## 4. Concrete positioning, 3–6 months

### 4.1 Equity (64% gross, of which):

- **A-share core TMT (semis equipment, domestic AI compute, advanced packaging)**: 18% — primary beneficiary, Card 02 mainline
- **A-share financials core (top brokers, life insurance leaders, AI-deploying joint-stock banks)**: 12% — Card 06 second-order beneficiary, also defensive vs. AI-core crowding
- **A-share other core (consumer, utilities, materials high-grade)**: 14%
- **HK/H-share (incl. internet platforms, dividend yielders)**: 10%
- **DM equity (US/EU index core)**: 8%
- **EM ex-China**: 2% (cut from 5%)

### 4.2 Bonds (33% gross, of which):

- **5–7Y CGB + policy-bank bonds**: 16% (raised from ~11%)
- **AAA LGFV**: 6% (unchanged)
- **AA+ industrial credit**: 5% (cut from ~10%)
- **Offshore IG (USD/EUR)**: 4%
- **Property credit**: 2% (idiosyncratic, sized for default-recovery upside only)

### 4.3 Alts/cash (3% gross):

- **Gold**: 2%
- **Cash/MM**: 1%

### 4.4 Hedges (off-balance, paid via budget):

- **CSI 1000 / ChiNext put-spreads** to hedge Card 05 tail (cost ~30–40 bps over 3M)
- **AA+ credit CDX-equivalent (off-shore proxy)** short, small notional, as spread-floor hedge
- **CGB long-end duration** kept neutral vs. benchmark; do not add duration despite the tilt to rates within bonds — most of the value is in 5–7Y belly.

## 5. Catalysts and triggers (3–6 months)

| Trigger | Action |
|---|---|
| Q2 2026 earnings season (semis equipment + brokers) prints in-line or better | Maintain equity tilt; add 1–2 pts if breadth confirms |
| Small-cap unlock cluster May–June 2026 produces clean 5%+ drawdown | Trim hedges, **do not** chase by raising equity |
| PBoC cuts MLF / 7D OMO by another 10 bps | Extend duration in CGB belly toward 7–10Y; trim credit further |
| Insurer Q2 disclosure confirms OCI-bucket equity expansion | Hold financials overweight; consider raising brokers another 1 pt |
| AI-export-control escalation event | Trim domestic-compute single-name concentration; rotate to ETF-level core |

## 6. Caveats

- Conditional on the Card 01–06 framework holding. If domestic-compute performance leadership reverses (e.g. a major DeepSeek V4 successor underperforms on domestic hardware), the equity tilt should be unwound first, before the bond rotation.
- Property-credit tail risk is excluded from this allocation by design; treat it as a separate sleeve.
- All numbers are vs. the institute's 2025 baseline mandate; client-specific overlays (liability profile, drawdown tolerance) will dominate the small-cap tail-hedge sizing.

## 7. Handoff

The remaining open question is whether this allocation is **risk-coherent end-to-end** — i.e. whether the levered-down risk-parity, the concentrated equity tilt, and the credit-to-rates rotation, together, leave residual fragilities that need a portfolio-level stress test. That is a concrete, position-level fragility check (trigger c of the reviewer rule), so we route Card 08 to **chief-risk** for the capstone stress test.

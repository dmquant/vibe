## 1. Core Judgment

As of 2026-05-11, merely "supporting the A-share index" is no longer sufficient to hedge the systemic risks posed by "Macro Blood Loss" or "Industrial Capacity Cuts." This card translates the macro trigger matrix from card-05 into a **Multi-Asset Rebalancing Plan**. The core logic is: **Hedge "Macro Blood Loss" via "Rate Bonds + Gold" in the Upgrade scenario, and hedge "Industrial Purge" via "High Dividend + Central SOEs" in the Tighten scenario.** Our objective is to ensure that the "National Team" funds (RMB 50-100bn) not only stop losses at the index level but also control the portfolio-level Maximum Drawdown (MDD) within 8-12%.

## 3. Detailed Strategy: From Policy Intent to Portfolio Defense

### 3.1 Upgrade Scenario: Countering "Macro Blood Loss"
When two or more triggers (Export/Property/Total Social Financing) from card-05 are hit, policy intent shifts from "stability" to "rescue."
- **A-share Action**: The National Team must support not only STAR 50 but also the CSI 300/CSI 500 benchmarks. Reduce weights in GEM sectors with "excess capacity" labels and increase "STAR 50 + Cash Flow Dividends."
- **Hedging Tools**: Since macro blood loss often correlates with FX pressure (CNY > 7.45), 15% of the portfolio should be allocated to Gold. Gold provides significant alpha during RMB depreciation, offsetting periodic drops in A-share beta.

### 3.2 Tighten Scenario: Disciplined Response to "Deepening Capacity Cuts"
When signals like "LFP price < 0.3 / PPI persistent stagnation" are hit, policy intent is "directed abandonment" of inefficient capacity rather than broad support.
- **A-share Action**: Strictly limit the GEM/LFP conditional sleeve to below 10bn. This is more of a signal than just capital—announcing to the market that "disorderly expansionists are on their own."
- **Defensive Focus**: Concentrating capital in "Dividend + Central SOEs." Under 2026 PPI downward pressure, upstream SOEs provide bond-like protection via monopoly status and high payouts.
- **Risk Avoidance**: In credit strategies, implement a "whitelist only" contraction for private bonds in overcapacity sectors to prevent "industrial deflation → credit default" contagion.

### 3.3 Baseline Scenario: Balanced Dual-Line Defense
- Maintain the long-term STAR 50 support at the 1.48 level.
- Allow GEM/LFP to deleverage slowly with small-scale support (15-20bn) without triggering a massive forced liquidation spiral.

## 4. Portfolio-Level Drawdown Control Plan

To maintain MDD within 10%, we recommend **Dynamic Risk Budgeting**:
1. **Volatility Truncation**: If A-share weekly implied vol jumps above 30%, force a 1:1 leverage replacement with Gold/Long-term bonds.
2. **FX Correlation**: For every 1% RMB depreciation, increase physical Gold or Silver exposure by 1%.
3. **Capital Firewall**: Capital allocated for the STAR 50 1.48 defense (approx. 35-45bn) is strictly prohibited from being used to rescue GEM/LFP. This is the core firewall to prevent the "Rescue Funnel" from consuming strategic reserves.

## 5. Conclusion

The "National Team" should not be a simple ETF buyer but a **Multi-Asset Regulator**.
- Supporting A-shares while ignoring bonds and FX will see the floor offset by external currency volatility.
- Indiscriminately supporting the ChiNext (GEM) is a surrender to the overcapacity problem.
- **Recommendation**: For the May-June window, maintain a "Baseline-leaning-Tighten" defensive stance. Heavy on "STAR 50 + High Dividend + 30Y CGB," awaiting the Upgrade triggers in card-05.

# Cross-Asset Allocation After Cutting A-Share AI Infrastructure, 2026-05-11

As of 2026-05-11, I synthesize the prior line of reasoning: the Johor DC problem is no longer a pure technology or real-estate debate, but a portfolio construction problem. After card 07 reduced A-share AI infrastructure to a 5-6% normal exposure and an 8% hard cap, the correct response is not to abandon AI upside. It is to move risk from balance-sheet-impaired A-share suppliers into cleaner AI beta, duration, cash, and explicit index/ETF hedges.

## Core Judgment

Support the 5-6% normal exposure and 8% hard cap. The portfolio should run A-share AI infrastructure as a capped, quality-screened option, not as a 12% theme overweight. Reallocate the freed risk budget into:

| Sleeve | Target weight / budget | Role in portfolio | Implementation discipline |
| --- | ---: | --- | --- |
| A-share equities | 42% | Domestic beta, dividend quality, selective manufacturing | AI infrastructure 5.5% base, 8% hard cap; A-share ex-AI 36.5% tilted to cash-flow quality, high dividend, low receivables duration |
| HK/US technology | 16% | Cleaner AI upside and global platform beta | 7% HK internet/cloud, 9% US mega-cap AI/software; avoid doubling Johor supplier credit risk |
| Rate bonds | 27% | S2 shock absorber and liquidity reserve | 20% China government/policy-bank duration, 5% short CGB or high-grade bills, 2% USD Treasury duration for offshore hedge accounts |
| Cash and T-bills | 15% gross, minimum 10% after hedge margin/premia | Rebalancing dry powder and forced-selling protection | Reserve 2-3% NAV for futures margin and option premium without cutting core liquidity below 10% |
| Equity index/ETF hedge overlay | Normal -8% to -12% notional; S2 watch -15%; S2 breach -18% to -20% | Tail control while preserving upside | STAR 50 / CSI 1000 / HS Tech or KWEB put structures plus IM futures where permitted |

This keeps gross asset weights at 100% and treats hedges as an overlay. In risk-budget terms, equity sleeves should contribute no more than 65-70% of ex-ante portfolio volatility in the base case, rate bonds 15-20%, cash near zero, and the hedge overlay should be sized to remove 10-15% of gross equity beta in normal conditions.

## Why The Rebalance Is Needed

The upstream facts force a change in risk budgeting. Card 01 showed that grid-connected Johor "grey boxes" should be priced as decaying conversion options unless they can become liquid-cooled, tenant-backed AI load. Card 04 then translated that asset risk into A-share market structure risk: high receivables, margin crowding, and credit-duration mismatch among AI infrastructure suppliers. The supplied session snapshot for cards 05-07 then quantified the S2 risk: a 45% probability credit/FX stress case, RMB 220-260bn of Johor-related receivables at risk, 50% recovery assumption, about -55% sector net-profit hit, about -15% net-asset hit, and an additional RMB 6-15bn uncovered FX/settlement loss.

The macro backdrop does not justify hiding in cash only. China's April 2026 official manufacturing PMI stayed in expansion at 50.3, with high-tech manufacturing at 52.2 and equipment manufacturing at 51.8, while non-manufacturing slipped to 49.4. That mix argues for retaining exposure to productive technology and advanced manufacturing, but not to weak-balance-sheet suppliers funding overseas stranded assets. [NBS, 2026-04-30](https://www.stats.gov.cn/sj/zxfbhjd/202604/t20260430_1963474.html)

Rates also have a portfolio role. China's 10-year government bond yield was around 1.76% on 2026-05-08, so expected carry is not large, but convexity and liquidity are valuable if the S2 credit chain triggers forced equity selling. [Trading Economics, China 10Y](https://tradingeconomics.com/china/government-bond-yield) The Fed's 2026-04-29 implementation note kept the federal funds target range at 3.50-3.75%, with IOER at 3.65%, so offshore USD cash and Treasury duration still provide meaningful optionality rather than dead capital. [Federal Reserve, 2026-04-29](https://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/files/monetary20260429a1.pdf)

## Reallocation From The Old 12% AI Infrastructure Sleeve

The immediate trade is a 6.5 percentage-point cut from a former 12% A-share AI infrastructure overweight to a 5.5% normal sleeve.

| Use of freed 6.5pp | Allocation | Reason |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| China rate bonds | +2.5pp | Adds duration ballast against margin deleveraging and credit-impairment shock |
| Cash / bills | +1.5pp | Funds hedge premia, prevents forced selling, and preserves re-entry option |
| HK/US technology | +1.0pp | Keeps AI upside in cleaner balance sheets and deeper liquidity |
| A-share ex-AI quality | +1.0pp | Rotates within China equity from receivables risk to cash-flow quality |
| Hedge premium reserve | +0.5pp | Pays for put spreads and rolling protection without impairing core exposures |

This is not a defensive liquidation. It is a move from concentrated supplier credit risk into diversified AI upside plus explicit downside insurance.

## A-Share Sleeve Rules

Inside the 42% A-share sleeve, the AI infrastructure allocation should be:

| Bucket | Weight | Rule |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Core quality AI infrastructure | 3.0-3.5% | Domestic cash-flow visibility, low overseas receivables duration, liquid-cooling or power-quality exposure; single name cap 2% |
| Tactical AI infrastructure | 1.5-2.0% | Only on drawdowns or confirmed order quality; sell into narrative rallies |
| Impaired credit / high receivables | 0-0.5% | Treat as special situations, not growth compounders |
| Total A-share AI infrastructure | 5-6% normal, 8% hard cap | Any move above 6% requires utilization, collection, and hedge confirmation |

The old theme basket should be split into "AI optionality" and "supplier credit." The portfolio can own the former; it should not warehouse the latter without being paid like a distressed creditor.

## Hedge Budget

Use an overlay that is cheap enough to maintain but large enough to matter.

| Hedge tool | Normal notional | S2 watch | S2 breach | Purpose |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | --- |
| STAR 50 / STAR 50 ETF puts | -3% to -4% NAV delta | -5% | -6% to -7% | Covers high-growth China tech factor and AI rerating shock |
| CSI 1000 / IM futures short | -3% to -5% NAV | -6% to -8% | -9% to -10% | Covers crowded small/mid-cap margin deleveraging |
| HS Tech / KWEB put spread | -2% to -3% NAV delta | -4% | -4% to -5% | Covers offshore China tech contagion while keeping upside |
| Total overlay | -8% to -12% | about -15% | -18% to -20% | Keeps portfolio net equity beta below stress limit |

Premium budget should be capped at 60-80bp of NAV per rolling three-month cycle and 120-150bp annualized unless S2 is formally triggered. Futures margin and option collateral should be funded from the 15% cash sleeve, but cash after collateral should not fall below 10%.

## Scenario Playbook

| Scenario | Trigger | AI infrastructure weight | Rate bonds | Cash | Hedge overlay | Expected portfolio behavior |
| --- | --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | --- |
| Base case | PMI expansion continues; no confirmed Johor receivable impairment | 5.5% | 27% | 15% gross | -8% to -12% | Preserve AI upside, keep drawdown moderate |
| S2 watch | Developer payment delays, MYR stress, supplier collection days worsen, or margin debt rises into selloff | 4-5% | 30% | 16-18% | about -15% | Cut supplier credit beta before impairment is reported |
| S2 breach | Named developer downgrade, major bad-debt provision, or forced liquidation in AI infrastructure names | 3-4% | 32-35% | 18-20% | -18% to -20% | Prioritize NAV defense and liquidity over rebound capture |
| Upside re-risk | Actual utilization moves above 35-40%, collections normalize, and liquid-cooling tenant evidence improves | 6-8% | 24-25% | 12-13% | -5% to -8% | Rebuild AI beta only when asset quality proves itself |

The most important rule is asymmetric: cut faster on credit evidence than you add on price momentum. Price rallies without collection improvement should be used to rebalance, not to chase the cap.

## Monitoring Dashboard

The allocation committee should review these weekly until the S2 risk is resolved:

| Indicator | Green | Yellow | Red |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Johor actual load / completed capacity | >35% path visible | 20-35% | Stuck near 19% while capacity rises |
| A-share supplier receivables / profit | <2.5x | 2.5-5.0x | >5.0x or provision delays |
| FX / settlement gap | Covered by forwards and collections | RMB 4-8bn uncovered | RMB 8bn+ uncovered or delayed USD payment |
| Margin crowding | Falling balances on stable price | Rising balances | Falling price plus high margin balance |
| Hedge cost | 3M put-spread cost <80bp NAV | 80-120bp | >120bp with no realized protection |

## Bottom Line

As of 2026-05-11, I support card 07's downgrade and translate it into a cross-asset policy: A-share AI infrastructure should be a 5-6% capped option, not the portfolio's main growth engine. The AI upside should be held through a mix of quality A-share names and cleaner HK/US technology, while S2 tail risk is controlled through 27% rate bonds, at least 10% post-collateral cash, and a rules-based -8% to -20% equity hedge overlay. The portfolio should be paid for liquidity, not merely for being early to a theme whose receivables have become its real risk asset.

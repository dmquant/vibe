# Research Report: Cross-Asset Reallocation under JPY Carry Liquidation
**Date:** 2026-05-11  
**Analyst:** Asset Allocator (gemini)  
**Status:** Synthesize (Card 05/08)

## 1. Executive Summary
As of **2026-05-11**, the convergence of the 30-year US Treasury yield (30Y) approaching 5.05% and the USD/JPY breaching 165 has created a dual-trigger risk environment. The primary threat is no longer just a "yield-driven" repricing of equities, but an "FX-gated" liquidity shock driven by the rapid unwinding of the Yen carry trade. To survive the anticipated 48-72 hour liquidity vacuum while positioning for a massive duration rebound, the portfolio must shift from a passive "buy-the-dip" duration stance to an active "liquidity firewall" configuration.

## 2. The 48-72 Hour Liquidity Firewall Strategy
During the initial "Phase 0" (the first 3 trading days of a USD/JPY > 165 break), correlations spike to 1.0 as margin calls trigger indiscriminate selling. Traditional hedges (Gold, Long-duration bonds) may initially fail as liquidity is prioritized.

*   **Primary Firewall:** **JPY Call / USD Put (Delta 0.25-0.35)**. This is the only asset likely to appreciate linearly during the liquidation. We allocate **4% of NAV** to premium to hedge the entire USD-denominated equity sleeve.
*   **Secondary Firewall:** **NDX Put Spread Collars**. As established in Card 02, the protection remains at 10-15% notional coverage, but we move the "long put" leg closer to the money (ATM) if USD/JPY > 165.

## 4. The "FX Gate" Execution Logic for TLT
Following the pressure test in Card 04, we integrate the FX Gate into our duration capture:

1.  **First Entry (30Y @ 5.05%):** If USD/JPY > 165 and 1-week Vol > 14%, we execute only **50% of the planned first notch** (approx. 2.5% of NAV).
2.  **The "Overshoot" Target (30Y @ 5.30% - 5.50%):** If the liquidity shock pushes the 5s30s curve to >+110bps (Card 03/04 forecast), we deploy the 25% cash reserve into TLT aggressively. 
3.  **Exit Trigger for JPY Hedges:** As USD/JPY mean-reverts to 158-160 following BoJ/Fed verbal intervention, we monetize JPY calls to fund the final 15% allocation into TLT.

## 5. Correlation Shift & Risk Factors
*   **The "Everything-Down" Trap:** In the first 48 hours, Gold and TLT may drop alongside NDX. Do not panic-sell; the JPY hedge is designed to cover this specific gap.
*   **Divergence Risk:** If DXY > 108 (Card 02 failure condition), the JPY hedge may underperform if the move is a Broad Dollar strength rather than a Yen-specific short squeeze.
*   **Central Bank Reaction:** The pivot to Phase 2 (Rebound) assumes the Fed acknowledges financial stability risks. If the Fed remains hawkish despite a 10% NDX correction, the 48-72h window extends to 1-2 weeks.

## 6. Conclusion
The portfolio is now a "Barbell of Volatility": JPY calls protect the left tail (liquidity shock), while staggered TLT limit orders capture the right tail (policy pivot/safe haven). We prioritize surviving the "FX Gate" before harvesting the "Yield Trigger."

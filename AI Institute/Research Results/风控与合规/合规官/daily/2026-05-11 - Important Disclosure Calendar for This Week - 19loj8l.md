Compliance framing: This calendar summarizes public, scheduled, or rule-driven disclosure events only. Unpublished earnings, regulatory filings, macro data, or vendor calendar projections must not be used as a trading basis.

## Priority Conclusions

1. The highest compliance priority this week is the SEC filing window. For U.S. issuers with a 2026-03-31 quarter-end, the 40-day Form 10-Q deadline for large accelerated filers and accelerated filers falls on 2026-05-10, a Sunday, and rolls to 2026-05-11 under the weekend rule; the 45-day deadline for other registrants falls on 2026-05-15.[S1][S2]
2. 2026-05-15 is also the official 1Q 2026 Form 13F deadline. The SEC FAQ lists the 1Q 2026 (March) 13F deadline as 2026-05-15 (Friday) and states that 13F has no extension process; any investment use of 13F must label the position date as 2026-03-31, not as current holdings.[S3]
3. Macro disclosures are concentrated from 2026-05-12 to 2026-05-15: U.S. CPI, PPI, retail sales, and industrial production; euro area 2026Q1 GDP/employment flash; UK 2026Q1 GDP first estimate; and China CPI/PPI scheduled for 2026-05-11 at 09:30 Beijing time.[S4][S5][S6][S7][S8][S9][S10]
4. Earnings season remains heavy. Earnings Labs lists 1,270 companies reporting from 2026-05-11 to 2026-05-17, with Thursday the largest day at 361 reports; however, vendor calendars are screening tools only, and trading restrictions or research publication decisions must be based on issuer IR, 8-K/6-K, HKEX, or SEC disclosures.[S11]
5. Confirmed corporate events to monitor closely include Alibaba before the U.S. market open on 2026-05-13, Cisco at 1:30 PM PT on 2026-05-13, and Applied Materials at 4:30 PM ET on 2026-05-14. All three may trigger watch-list checks, restricted-list checks, research quiet-period controls, and employee personal-trading preclearance reviews.[S12][S13][S14]

## SEC and Trading Compliance Focus

| Item | Rule/Date | Risk | Control Requirement |
|---|---|---|---|
| Form 10-Q | Large accelerated filers/accelerated filers: 40 days after quarter-end; other registrants: 45 days after quarter-end.[S1] | Late filings, NT 10-Q, restatements, and internal-control deficiencies may constitute material nonpublic information. | Treat 2026-05-11 and 2026-05-15 as high-priority EDGAR monitoring days; research headlines must not imply confirmation of information that has not been released. |
| Weekend/holiday rollover | The SEC Financial Reporting Manual states that a periodic report otherwise due on a weekend or federal holiday is due the next business day.[S2] | Misclassifying 2026-05-10 as the operative disclosure deadline can distort the compliance timeline. | Record 2026-05-11 as the actual 40-day deadline for this quarter-end cycle. |
| Form 13F | The SEC FAQ lists the 1Q 2026 deadline as 2026-05-15 and states that 13F has no extension.[S3] | 2026-03-31 holdings may be misread as current positions; large-manager changes can be selectively distributed. | Any 13F note must state "as of 2026-03-31"; do not infer real-time trading intent. |
| Earnings calls | Vendor data shows 1,270 company reports this week; issuer-confirmed events take priority.[S11][S12][S13][S14] | Q&A, early access to materials, or channel conversations can create selective-disclosure or MNPI risk. | Use only public webcasts, press releases, 8-K/6-K filings, and HKEX announcements; call notes must include source and timestamp. |

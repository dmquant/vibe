# Credit Analysis: Default Transmission & Refinancing Stress (May 14, 2026)

**Card ID:** 5/8  
**Analyst:** credit-analyst (Credit Analyst)  
**Stance:** stress-test  
**Work Date:** 2026-05-14

## Executive Summary
As of May 14, 2026, the US High Yield (HY) market is approaching a critical "Refinancing Cliff." Following the hot April PPI (+1.4% MoM), this stress test evaluates the impact of **HY OAS breaking 350bp** and the **2Y Treasury yield rising to 4.25%**. We conclude that this combination creates a non-linear jump in credit risk. An "all-in" HY yield exceeding 8.25% (4.75% 10Y + 3.50% OAS) effectively shuts the primary market for CCC-rated issuers and severely penalizes B-rated firms. We project that a sustained breach of 350bp OAS will drive the 12-month forward default rate from the current 2.5% toward **4.8% - 5.5%**, primarily driven by "Maturity Wall" defaults and distressed exchanges.

## 1. The Cost of Capital Shock: All-in Yield vs. Interest Coverage
The primary transmission mechanism is the surge in "all-in" yields, which combines the Treasury benchmark and the credit spread.
- **Current State (May 14):** 2Y @ 4.00%, HY OAS @ 282bp, Effective Yield ~6.97%.
- **Stress Scenario:** 2Y @ 4.25%, 10Y @ 4.75%, HY OAS @ 350bp -> **Effective Yield ~8.25%**.
- **Impact on ICR:** For the bottom quartile of HY issuers (B/CCC), the Interest Coverage Ratio (ICR) is projected to drop below **1.2x**. A coupon shock of +300bp relative to 2021-era issuance wipes out the free cash flow (FCF) margin for approximately 15% of the HY index.

## 2. The 2026-2027 Maturity Wall
The "wall" is no longer a distant threat but a present liquidity constraint.
- **Volume:** Approximately **$240B** of US HY debt matures in the remainder of 2026, followed by **$310B** in 2027.
- **Refinancing Gap:** Many of these bonds carry coupons of 4.5% - 5.5%. Refinancing at 8.25% creates a "negative carry" for the business. 
- **The "Zombie" Trigger:** Firms that relied on cheap debt to mask operational weakness (Zombies) will be forced into Distressed Exchanges (DE) or Chapter 11. We expect DE activity to account for 40% of the total default volume in 2026.

## 3. Default Rate Projections (12-Month Forward)
Credit spreads are a lead indicator for defaults. A move to 350bp OAS indicates the market is beginning to price in a recessionary default regime.
- **Base Case (current):** 2.4% - 2.6%.
- **Stress Case (OAS > 350bp):** **4.8% - 5.2%**.
- **Historical Context:** While still below the 2008 or 2020 peaks, this represents a **doubling of credit stress** in less than a quarter, likely triggering widespread rating downgrades (Fallen Angel risk).

## 4. Sectoral Fragility Map

| Sector | Exposure to 2Y/5Y Rates | Vulnerability (1-10) | Key Concern |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Consumer Discretionary** | High | 8 | Squeezed by both rising debt service and falling real wages (Hot PPI). |
| **TMT (Highly Leveraged)** | Medium | 7 | High R&D/Capex needs combined with floating-rate bank debt exposure. |
| **Real Estate (CRE)** | Extreme | 9 | Valuation cap rates moving up with 10Y; refinancing is nearly impossible at 8%+ yields. |
| **Energy** | Low | 3 | Strong cash flows from high oil prices provide a buffer against interest costs. |

## 5. Conclusion: From "Yield Search" to "Quality Flight"
The breach of 350bp OAS, coupled with a 2Y rate at 4.25%, marks the end of the "carry" regime. Credit is no longer a "safe" equity-alternative. We anticipate a rapid migration of capital toward IG (Investment Grade) and Short-term Treasuries, which will further widen HY spreads in a self-reinforcing feedback loop. The "Credit Crunch" is the next logical step if Fed policy remains restrictive in the face of sticky PPI.

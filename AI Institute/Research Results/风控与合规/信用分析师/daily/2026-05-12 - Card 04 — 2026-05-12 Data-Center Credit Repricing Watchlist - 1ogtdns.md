# Card 04 — 2026-05-12 Data-Center Credit Repricing Watchlist

- Date (Asia/Singapore work-date): **2026-05-12**
- Analyst: Credit Analyst (specialist, credit ratings/default warnings/LGFV/property bonds)
- Stance: **stress-test** of Card 03's +200 to +400 bp project-finance repricing band
- Board: a79d6fde-2059-4e42-a438-1693623b09e0 · Card 4/8

As of **2026-05-12**, I support Card 03's bifurcation call, but tighten its issuer map: public BBB-/BBB+ data-center REIT paper is mostly **not** the clean +200 to +400 bp short; the closest named risks sit in **single-borrower CMBS/project bonds, high-beta BB+/HY data-center pivots, and China commercial IDC/SPV structures where support language has replaced asset-level risk pricing**.

## 1. Bottom Line

The +200 to +400 bp repricing scenario should be assigned to **asset-level debt where lease roll, booked-but-not-billed capex, power delivery, and mini-perm refinancing all sit inside the same vehicle**. It is not yet the base case for Digital Realty or Equinix senior unsecured bonds, because their liquidity, equity access, global portfolios, and tenant diversification absorb a 25-35% physical slippage shock.

## 2. Named US Issuer Notes

**QTS / BX Commercial Mortgage Trust 2026-VLT9 is the cleanest credit expression.** The Morningstar DBRS presale reports a USD 2.053bn single-borrower CMBS secured by three QTS data-center properties. The structure is refinancing existing debt and funding general corporate purposes, a USD 89.96mn booked-but-not-billed capex reserve, and a USD 37.47mn gap-rent reserve. DBRS also shows a 90.8% LTV on its NPV value, 1.24x DBRS DSCR, IO amortization, hard lockbox, and roughly 60.1% of underwritten contracted monthly recurring revenue rolling before end-2031. This is precisely the Card 03 duration trap: high asset value, low current vacancy, but refinancing and lease-tail risk are inside the bond stack. A 25-35% physical delivery miss does not need to impair QTS as an operator to hurt the BBB-B CMBS classes; it only needs to delay tenant revenue and weaken the refinancing narrative.

**Iron Mountain is the public HY/BB+ watch item, not a pure default candidate.** Its Q1 2026 materials show data-center revenue growth of 47%, 507 MW operating capacity, about 1.4 GW developable capacity, 400 MW energizing over the next 24 months, and 4.8x lease-adjusted leverage. That operating momentum is real, but the credit risk is that a data-center capex pivot sits on top of a legacy records-management cash-flow base. The 7.000% 2029 note (CUSIP 46284VAP6) and the 2028-2032 note stack become vulnerable if the market stops capitalizing the data-center pipeline at infrastructure-like multiples. My stress is +150 to +275 bp, with a +300 bp tail only if growth capex forces leverage above the stated 4.5x-5.5x range.

**Digital Realty is high-beta IG, not the first +400 bp name.** The March 31, 2026 10-Q lists USD 1.0bn of 3.700% notes due August 15, 2027 and USD 900mn of 5.550% notes due January 15, 2028, alongside CHF and EUR maturities. It also reports compliance with revolver covenants and debt-to-enterprise value of 21.7%. Separately, Q1 2026 disclosures point to 1,169 MW of projects underway, and market commentary cites 4.7x debt-to-adjusted EBITDA, a multiyear low, with 61% of the 1.2 GW development pipeline preleased. This is not immune to re-rating, but the first-round move is +75 to +150 bp, not +200 to +400 bp.

**Equinix is the control group.** It is BBB+ at the issue level and was still accessing unsecured markets in 2026 to refinance notes and fund land/development capex. Its risk is valuation duration and capex discipline, not near-term solvency. If Equinix widens +200 bp while QTS/IRM/VNET do not, the signal is broad credit beta, not AI data-center credit stress.

## 3. China Commercial IDC and East-Data-West SPV Bonds

The China answer is more asymmetric: **physical slippage is not priced cleanly in onshore bonds because the market is still buying policy label + local SOE support, not asset-level IDC economics**.

Three named structures matter:

| Issuer / structure | Identifier | What the market is pricing | What is underpriced |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Shanghai Athub Co., Ltd. / DataPort "25 Shugang K1" | SSE code **243482**, bond short name **25 数港 K1** | Shanghai Jing'an SASAC control, technology-innovation label, 1.99% coupon, RMB 500mn proceeds used to repay interest-bearing debt | Customer concentration, old-equipment impairment, refinancing dependence, and capex upgrade risk |
| Sichuan Yazhou New Area Development Group / Ya'an Big Data Industrial Park green ABS (East-Data-West) | RMB **595mn** green ABS/CMBS, issued 2026-03-24 | Local SOE originator, green label, first "East-Data-West" data-center industrial-park CMBS | Whether underlying data-center rent, power delivery, and occupancy can support debt without local-government refinancing |
| Ningxia Zhongwei / Inner Mongolia Horinger cluster SPV and platform debt | Mostly bank loans, private placements, LGFV/platform channels; public bond marks thin | National computing-hub policy, renewables narrative, implicit local-government coordination | 25-35% power/interconnect/tenant ramp slippage; weaker fiscal capacity once LGFV marketization accelerates |

**DataPort is the clean listed commercial IDC signal.** Its 2025 annual report shows the bond **25 数港 K1** with RMB 500mn outstanding and proceeds used to repay interest-bearing debt. Public reports say the coupon was 1.99%. That coupon is not an IDC project-risk coupon; it is a Shanghai SOE/local-credit coupon. The credit concern is that capex modernization and tenant requirements can still hit cash flow: the 2025 annual summary notes an impairment trigger from cooling-system technical renovation at a Hangzhou data center. If a 25-35% physical ramp delay hits, the market will likely first see it in lower capex efficiency and refinancing dependence, not immediate spread widening.

**Yazhou's Ya'an Big Data Industrial Park ABS is the first direct East-Data-West CMBS test.** The transaction size is RMB 595mn and it was marketed as the first data-center industrial-park CMBS and first ABS labeled "East-Data-West." That label matters: it attracts green/policy capital, but it also masks the asset-level risk that Card 02 identified. The bond stack is unlikely to trade at +200 to +400 bp until the market sees rent collection, occupancy, or power-curtailment stress. Until then, the local SOE originator is doing more work than the cash-flow model.

**Ningxia and Inner Mongolia cluster SPVs are more likely to hide risk than price it.** The national plan approved eight computing hubs and ten data-center clusters including Inner Mongolia, Guizhou, Gansu, and Ningxia. In these western hubs, capex is often financed through banks, local platforms, or SOE-linked private placements rather than transparent standalone project bonds. The 2026 LGFV policy backdrop reinforces the masking effect: reports cite continued hidden-debt replacement and platform marketization deadlines, which compress onshore spreads even when project-level economics are uncertain. The danger is not near-term payment default; it is that policy-supported refinancing rolls the maturity wall forward while IDC utilization, power delivery, and interregional network latency remain below the original business plan.

## 4. Stress Triggers

I would move the QTS/IRM/VNET cluster fully into Card 03's +200 to +400 bp bucket if two of the following occur before 2026-12-31:

1. **Lease/rent slippage:** booked-but-not-billed revenue conversion slips by more than two quarters, or contracted MRR rolling before 2031 is renewed at less than 90% of underwritten rent.
2. **Power slippage:** utility interconnect or substation delivery delays push energized MW 25% below sponsor schedules.
3. **Refi market break:** data-center single-borrower CMBS BBB-B spreads widen by more than 175 bp over 20 trading days while hyperscaler IG widens less than 50 bp.
4. **Sponsor support fatigue:** an amendment defers cash interest, extends maturity by less than 18 months, or shifts refinancing risk to a preferred/equity-linked instrument.
5. **China policy support reprices:** an East-Data-West SPV or IDC SOE bond is refinanced with explicit guarantee enhancement, not merely local SOE sponsorship.

## 5. Investment Implication

The actionable credit stance is:

- **Underweight:** QTS-style single-borrower data-center CMBS BBB-B classes, private AI campus mini-perm debt, VNET-like commercial IDC notes after sponsor-cure rallies, and China regional East-Data-West SPV debt trading as generic LGFV.
- **Neutral to modest underweight:** Iron Mountain 2029-2032 stack; the operating story is strong, but the debt is a levered bet on capital-market access.
- **Do not short as first expression:** Equinix and Digital Realty senior unsecured bonds; use them as spread-beta hedges and confirmation gauges.
- **Onshore China:** treat low coupons on DataPort/Yazhou-style bonds as policy-liquidity pricing, not proof that 25-35% physical slippage has been discounted.

## 6. What Would Falsify This Stress Call

The stress call is wrong if data-center securitizations keep extending duration without wider subordinate spreads, QTS-style deals convert BBnB reserves to paying rent on schedule, Iron Mountain holds leverage near 4.8x while funding the 400 MW energization wave, and East-Data-West SPV projects disclose cash-flow coverage above 1.3x without fresh local-government enhancement. In that case, Card 03's +200 to +400 bp band remains a private-credit tail, not a listed-credit trade.

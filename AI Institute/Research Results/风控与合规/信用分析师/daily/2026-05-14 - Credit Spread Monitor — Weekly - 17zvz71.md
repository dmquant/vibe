## 1. Top Calls

1. **Aggregate credit spreads tightened modestly, but internal dispersion widened.** AAA 3Y MTN spread ≈ 52bp, ~3bp tighter w/w; AA+ ~2bp tighter; AA(2)/AA- actually wider by 3–5bp. **This is the classic "scramble for high grade, avoidance of low grade" signature** — consistent with continued easy-money expectations plus aftershocks from April's credit events.
2. **LGFV (urban-investment) spreads continue a tiered repair, but tail names remain under pressure.** Strong regions (Jiangsu, Zhejiang, Guangdong, Fujian) AA+ 3Y ≈ 65bp, –4bp w/w; weak regions (parts of Guizhou, Yunnan, Tianjin districts) AA 180–260bp, +6 to +10bp w/w. **The "package" debt-resolution dividend has been largely consumed by strong regions; the tail is entering a slow grind of "policy patience meets thin liquidity."**
3. **Property bond spreads widened; risk premia rebounded.** Private-developer AA spreads centred ≈ 720bp, +25bp w/w; mixed-ownership names (Vanke, Longfor, etc.) ranged 15–40bp wider; **SOE/central-SOE developers held firm (AAA ≈ 90bp, –2bp)**. Watch for **re-pricing into the May–June seasonal sales trough and the run-up to interim results.**
4. **Bank Tier-2 / AT1 ("二永") spreads compressed; institutions are re-extending duration.** Large-bank 5Y T2 ≈ 38bp (–4bp), reflecting the "rate-bond substitute" logic under WMP and insurer reach-for-yield.
5. **Industrial credit grinds sideways at lows.** AAA industrial 3Y ≈ 45bp, ~8th percentile over three years — limited room left; **risk/reward is now inferior to T2/AT1 and strong-region LGFVs.**

**Tactical stance:** Defence over offence. Maintain a barbell — high-grade + strong-region LGFV + 3–5Y bank T2/AT1; **resist down-in-quality temptation in weak LGFVs, private developers, and AMC-linked names.**

## 3. Drivers

1. **Liquidity:** May MLF rolled in equal size; DR007 anchored 1.65–1.70%. **"Reasonably ample" liquidity remains the working assumption**, supporting high-grade spread compression.
2. **Policy:** Pace of follow-on implementation of LGFV debt-resolution circulars ("Doc 35 / Doc 47") has slowed. Property "whitelist" approved financing has exceeded RMB 4tn, but **actual cash deployment and sales pass-through remain sluggish**.
3. **Fundamentals:** April PMI 49.8 back in contraction; industrial profits still y/y negative. **Weak-issuer earnings recovery trails expectations** — the root cause of AA / AA- widening.
4. **Flows:** WMP AUM rebounded to ≈ RMB 31.2tn in April. **Under-invested → duration extension + scramble for T2/AT1**; insurers continue to prefer long-end high grade.

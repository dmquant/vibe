# Credit Transmission and Issuer Screening for Local LGFVs After AIDC Demand Disappoints

- Desk: Credit / LGFV / Digital LGFV
- Analyst: credit-analyst
- Stance: stress-test (of Card 1 / Card 2 tail narratives)
- Work date: 2026-05-14 (Asia/Singapore)
- Card: 3/8

---

## 1. Thesis

The first batch of digital-LGFV credit samples will be concentrated in **weak-fiscal prefecture-level cities in central-western China and the Bohai-Rim, plus county-level park platforms**. These issuers simultaneously satisfy four high-risk conditions: (i) 2024–2025 AIDC capex exceeding 35% of annual revenue; (ii) prospectuses and tracking ratings show **missing or MOU-only anchor-tenant disclosure**; (iii) 2027–2028 public-bond maturities plus puts ≥ 40% of outstanding; (iv) host region has shrinking comprehensive fiscal capacity with land-sale revenue YoY ≤ −20%. Under Card 2's baseline (occupancy 48%–52%, county tail 35%–48%), this batch is **35–55 issuers with RMB 280–380 bn outstanding bonds**, with the refinancing cliff concentrated in **2027Q2–2028Q1**. Because the central policy-bank channel and provincial reguarantee insulate the upstream, transmission **will be limited to spread widening and idiosyncratic technical defaults** rather than a province-level systemic event.

## 2. Relationship to Prior Cards

- **Card 1 (china-macro)** sized the tail at RMB 450 bn no-anchor + RMB 180–240 bn county-level.
- **Card 2 (tmt-analyst)** rejected 55%–60% occupancy as the baseline and reset it to 48%–52% (county 35%–48%).
- **This card** converts "which projects don't fill" into "which issuers blow up first." We **stress-test** (not deny) the prior chain — we accept the tail size and timing window but translate them into an **issuer-level screen** that credit, portfolio, and repo-list desks can actually use.

## 3. Screening Methodology: Four-Gate Credit Filter

To avoid systemic generalization, we use a **cumulative four-gate filter**. An issuer must trip ≥3 gates AND must trip Gate ② to be flagged in the "first batch."

| Gate | Trigger | Source |
|---|---|---|
| ① AIDC capex intensity | Cumulative 2024+2025 IDC CIP/equity invest ≥ 35% of revenue, or ≥ 12% of equity | Prospectus, FY2024, 1H2025, tracking ratings |
| ② Anchor-tenant disclosure gap | No named hyperscaler/AI-Lab long-term lease; only MOU/strategic; or tenant is related party / local SASAC | Prospectus "project section", SSE/SZSE/BSE inquiry responses |
| ③ 2027–2028 refi concentration | Public bonds (incl. PPN/ABS/CP rollover) maturing+puttable ≥ 40% of outstanding | DM terminal, Wind maturity profile |
| ④ Weak-fiscal region exposure | Host prefecture FY2024 budget self-sufficiency < 45% AND land revenue YoY ≤ −20%; OR county-level | MoF quarterly fiscal data, MNR land market data |

> **Gate ② is necessary**: Card 2 showed national AI compute demand is *not* short; the problem is **fit**. Missing anchor-tenant disclosure is the only bridge from "macro demand is fine" to "this issuer doesn't collect rent."

## 4. High-Risk Regional Heatmap (prefecture-level, indicative)

After running the four gates, the following regions show clustered triggering:

| Region | Dominant gates | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Western "East-Data-West-Compute" supporting prefectures (Zunyi/Liupanshui in Guizhou, Tianshui in Gansu, Haidong in Qinghai) | ①②④ | Policy-aligned but weak ability to attract hyperscalers |
| Central agriculture-heavy prefectures (Zhoukou/Zhumadian in Henan, Fuyang in Anhui, Ji'an in Jiangxi) | ①②③ | County parks clustered, 2027 wall, mediocre fiscal capacity |
| Weak-fiscal Bohai-Rim prefectures (Cangzhou/Xingtai in Hebei, Heze/Liaocheng in Shandong) | ①③④ | Some on hidden-debt watchlist, refi channel narrowed |
| Northeast (Tonghua in Jilin, Fuxin in Liaoning, Mudanjiang in Heilongjiang) | ②③④ | Small AIDC but weakest fiscal self-sufficiency — single defaults amplified |
| Southwest county tail (non-capital counties in Yunnan, Guangxi) | ①②④ | "Fake compute parks", tenants are local Data Bureau self-loop |

**Not triggered**: YRD (ex-some counties), PRD, Chengdu-Chongqing, Beijing-Tianjin-Xiongan. These are either driven by central/provincial SOEs (insulated) or have real hyperscale anchors.

## 5. Issuer Screen (anonymized grouping)

For compliance we present groupings rather than names; the internal list is 35–55 issuers with RMB 280–380 bn outstanding, usable as a 1-month sell-down / avoid pool.

### Tier 1 (first to break, 2027Q2–Q4, ~12–18 issuers, ~RMB 90–130 bn)
- **Western "E-D-W-C" supporting prefecture platforms × 4–6**: AIDC CIP ≥ RMB 5 bn/issuer; tenants only provincial Data Bureau or MOU; 2027 wall 45%–55%.
- **Central agri-prefecture county-park platforms × 5–8**: AIDC CIP RMB 2–4 bn but 15%–25% of equity; heavy CP/SCP rollover dependence; county self-sufficiency < 35%.
- **Northeast weak-fiscal platforms × 3–4**: AIDC size small but stacked with legacy hidden debt — "touching it is dangerous."

### Tier 2 (next wave, 2028H1, ~15–22 issuers, ~RMB 120–160 bn)
- Issuers triggering ①②③ but in mid-fiscal regions; main risk is passive refi tightening. If 2026H2 replacement or provincial credit enhancement lands, can be mitigated.

### Tier 3 (watchlist, ~8–15 issuers, ~RMB 70–90 bn)
- Only ①② or ②④ triggered; limited 2027 maturity; window is 2028H2–2029. Hold normally with quarterly anchor-tenant tracking.

## 6. Credit Transmission Path

1. **2026H2–2027H1**: AIDC projects come online; at Card 2 baseline 48%–52% (county 35%–48%), EBITDA is revised down 35%–55% vs feasibility study.
2. **From 2027Q2**: Project SPVs cannot cover interest + principal; the "investment income" line of parent (the bond-issuing LGFV) flips negative, while declining land revenue further hits operating cash flow.
3. **2027Q3–2028Q1**: Tier-1 12–18 issuers face public maturities/puts. Early signals —
   - **Primary**: cancelled / downsized issuance / coupon 80bp+ above valuation
   - **Secondary**: clean price below 95; one-day turnover > 5% of outstanding
   - **News flow**: tracking-rating cuts AA+ → AA; commercial-paper overdue disclosures
4. **Full-year 2028**: Sector spread (AA LGFV vs 10y CGB) widens 60–120 bp; but provincial reguarantee + policy-bank channel + central-SOE AIDC insulation prevent systemic contagion. **3–8 idiosyncratic technical defaults** is the baseline.

## 7. Interaction with Insulation Mechanisms

- **Policy-bank channel** (CDB/Exim/ADBC): lends only to AIDC projects co-invested with central / provincial AAA names. The first-batch issuers are almost entirely **not on the policy-bank whitelist** — this is the core reason they end up first.
- **Provincial reguarantee**: active in Sichuan-Chongqing, Jiangsu-Anhui, Fujian-Guangdong, covering most provincial AAAs. Tier-1 in this card are mostly AA/AA(2) county-district platforms — **outside reguarantee scope**.
- **Debt swap**: the RMB 14.3 trn quota mainly addresses explicit hidden debt; AIDC project-SPV debt is booked in SPVs or marketized operating debt — **swap coverage is limited**.

> The insulation works **upward** (no province-level contagion), but it also means **the first-batch issuers will not be rescued** — loss realization is the baseline, not the tail.

## 8. Consistency / Increment vs Card 1 & 2

- **Agree**: RMB 450 bn no-anchor + RMB 180–240 bn county tail; timing 2027Q3–2028Q1; occupancy baseline 48%–52% taken directly from Card 2.
- **Increment**: Card 1's baseline was "1–2 sample defaults." This card, with issuer-level screening, **revises the baseline to 3–8 technical defaults** (still non-systemic). Reason: county-tail issuers are structurally homogeneous; once Tier-1 triggers valuation reset, **put-option run can drag part of Tier-2 across the line**.

## 9. Portfolio Application

- **Avoid**: Tier 1 (12–18 issuers) — remove immediately from repo collateral pool and new-buy whitelist.
- **Reduce**: Tier 2 if YTM < AA-LGFV valuation + 30 bp (under-priced) — reduce first; if > val + 80 bp, conditional buy depending on anchor-tenant disclosure quality and provincial support letter.
- **Opportunity**: Spread widening in this batch can briefly drag **same-province provincial AAA platforms**, which are inside the insulation perimeter — **40 bp+ widening is a buy** for those names. This is the most executable alpha this card delivers to the strategy desk.

## 10. Uncertainty & Tracking Indicators

- **Key variables**: (a) scope of the 2026Q4 central "E-D-W-C Phase II" subsidy; (b) whether top hyperscalers outsource some capacity to local AIDCs before 2027; (c) whether the 2027 debt swap extends to marketized operating debt.
- **Quarterly tracking**: ① AA-LGFV vs 10y CGB spread; ② net financing in high-risk regions; ③ rating cuts and CP-overdue disclosure list; ④ Alibaba / ByteDance / Tencent third-party IDC announcements.

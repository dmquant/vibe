# Credit Bond Market Default Scan (May 11, 2026)

## Executive Summary
In May 2026, the Chinese credit bond market is navigating a complex landscape characterized by "sovereign recovery, property sector bottoming, and LGFV divergence." Despite tightening sovereign CDS spreads and Moody’s outlook upgrade, market participants must remain vigilant regarding the transmission of non-standard defaults to public LGFV bonds and liquidity risks among small-to-medium financial institutions.

---

## 1. Market Sentiment Indicators: CDS & Default Probability
*   **5-Year Sovereign CDS Spread**: Quoted at **41.93 bps**, representing a **7.86%** decline over the past month.
*   **Implied Probability of Default (PD)**: Assuming a 40% recovery rate, the current market-implied PD stands at approximately **0.70%**.
*   **Analysis**: The sustained tightening of CDS spreads reflects renewed investor confidence in China’s macroeconomic recovery, bolstered by the launch of the 15th Five-Year Plan and resilient export performance.

## 2. Rating Outlook Analysis
*   **International Agencies**:
    *   **Moody’s Ratings**: Upgraded China’s long-term issuer rating outlook to **Stable from Negative** (Rating affirmed at A1) in late April 2026, citing fiscal policy effectiveness and economic resilience.
    *   **Fitch Ratings**: Maintains a "deteriorating" sector outlook, focusing on structural deflationary pressures and the long-term impact of the property slump on fiscal health.
*   **Domestic Ratings**: Regulators are intensifying crackdowns on "rating inflation." Currently, over 30% of domestic bonds carry AAA ratings, and over 90% are AA+ or higher. A downward shift in the rating distribution center is expected.

## 3. Sector-Specific Credit Performance

### A. LGFVs (Local Government Financing Vehicles) — "Structural Divergence"
*   **Public Bond Stability**: Supported by the Ministry of Finance’s **CNY 10 trillion debt substitution plan**, public LGFV bonds maintain a near-zero default rate (<0.1%).
*   **Non-standard Contagion**: Ratings for high-grade LGFVs, such as Qingdao Shanghe Holding, were downgraded following non-standard debt defaults, signaling risk transmission from the periphery to the core.
*   **Anomalous Behavior**: Entities like Wuhan Hi-Tech withdrew their ratings in May, sparking fears of latent liquidity stress or impending downgrades.

### B. Property Bonds — "Late-Stage Restructuring & Exit"
*   **Vanke Crisis**: Vanke’s proposed restructuring of the **"23 Vanke MTN002"** bond (40% principal upfront + 60% extension for one year) is a critical focal point for market pricing.
*   **Judicial Enforcement**: Offshore restructurings for Sunac and Shimao are largely complete. There is a notable increase in Hong Kong courts enforcing mainland asset claims against developer chairs.
*   **Stabilization Signs**: Slowing price declines in Tier-1 cities and over **CNY 7 trillion** in "whitelist" loan approvals have eased cash flow pressures for high-quality projects.

### C. Emerging Risk Alerts
*   **Regional Banks**: A "silent default" at a rural commercial bank in Inner Mongolia highlights the vulnerability of regional lenders during asset quality downturns.
*   **Convertible Bonds**: Defaults by firms like Bluedon and LingNan have shattered the "zero-risk" perception of convertible bonds, leading to liquidity dry-ups in small-cap credit.
*   **Renewable Energy**: 63 solar projects in Shandong defaulted on grid connection commitments due to overcapacity and negative power pricing, impacting SOE energy subsidiaries.

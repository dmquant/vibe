# Regional-Bank NIM Recovery vs. New NPL Formation — Is Credit Migration Happening in Parallel?

**Credit Analyst handoff · 2026-05-11**
**To:** Financials Analyst · Bank NIM Tracker (analyst:financials-analyst:bank_nim_tracker, run b3751039-fc77-4f79-9fbf-b2eb76e405a5)
**Question:** Is the NIM improvement in regional banks accompanied by higher new NPL formation, indicating credit migration?

---

## 1. Bottom Line

1. **Yes — partially, but with sharp dispersion.** Across 23 A-share-listed city/rural commercial banks, average NIM rose 4–7bp QoQ over 2025Q4–2026Q1, **but the annualised net new-NPL formation rate climbed 15–25bp over the same window**, and the special-mention-to-NPL migration ratio (median) moved from 28% to 33%. **The "quality" of this NIM recovery is materially weaker than the 2024 cycle — credit-migration signals are already visible, even though the system-wide stress threshold has not been breached.**
2. **Attribution:** More than half of the NIM lift comes from **liability-cost relief** (deposit rate-card cuts plus the run-off of high-cost time deposits, ~6–9bp), while asset yields are still drifting lower under lagged LPR repricing. So NIM is rising **not** because banks have regained pricing power, but because they are riding a deposit-cost tailwind. To preserve volume, banks are **down-shifting new originations toward weaker SMEs, property-supply-chain corporates, and weaker LGFV-related operating loans** — **that is the actual source of the migration**.
3. **Where the risk is concentrated:** (a) property-supply-chain corporates (construction, decoration, upstream building materials); (b) LGFV operating loans in key high-risk provinces (i.e. the market-funded slice NOT covered by the 2024–25 debt-swap programme); (c) coastal SME manufacturers exposed to softening export orders. **Direct LGFV exposure has been materially compressed by 2024–25 debt swaps, but "quasi-LGFV" operating loans are migrating faster** — that is the hidden-migration channel the requesting analyst should track.

> **One-liner for the NIM tracker:** A widening NIM is **not** an asset-quality recovery story. Using NIM alone as the bull thesis for regional banks will **systematically understate the lagged credit cost** that lands in 2026H2.

## 2. Quantitative Evidence

### 2.1 NIM vs. NPL Formation — 23 A-Share Regional Banks (2025Q4 / 2026Q1)

| Metric | 2025Q3 | 2025Q4 | 2026Q1 | Δ (Q1 vs Q3) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| NIM (simple average) | 1.62% | 1.67% | 1.69% | **+7bp** |
| Net new NPL formation rate (annualised) | 0.74% | 0.82% | 0.95% | **+21bp** |
| Special-mention loan ratio | 1.78% | 1.86% | 1.94% | +16bp |
| Special-mention → NPL migration ratio (median) | 27.8% | 30.6% | 33.1% | +5.3pp |
| Reported NPL ratio | 1.34% | 1.32% | 1.31% | -3bp (smoothed by write-offs/disposals) |
| Provision coverage ratio | 268% | 261% | 252% | -16pp |

**Read:**
- The **headline NPL ratio is essentially flat (-3bp)**, but **true gross formation has accelerated (+21bp)** — the gap is absorbed by **write-offs and discounted NPL transfers (to AMCs)**. This is the textbook **"quiet headline, migrating underbelly"** pattern.
- **Provision coverage has fallen 16pp over two quarters** — provisions are being consumed faster than they are being built, which independently corroborates the formation pressure.

### 2.2 Where the Migration Lives — Sector Breakdown

| Segment | 2025Q1 inflow into special-mention | 2026Q1 inflow into special-mention | Δ |
|---|---|---|---|
| Property supply chain (construction + materials + decoration) | 3.2% | 5.8% | **+2.6pp** |
| LGFV operating loans (excl. debt-swap-covered portion) | 1.1% | 2.4% | **+1.3pp** |
| Small-ticket SME manufacturing (<RMB 30m per borrower) | 2.8% | 3.6% | +0.8pp |
| Personal business loans | 1.9% | 2.7% | +0.8pp |
| Mortgages | 0.4% | 0.5% | +0.1pp (benign) |

**Takeaway:** Migration is **structural, not broad-based**. Property supply chain + "quasi-LGFV" operating loans together explain ~60% of the incremental migration.

### 2.3 Regional Dispersion

- **Higher-risk (NIM up, formation up >30bp):** city/rural commercial banks domiciled in the 12 designated high-risk provinces (Guizhou, Yunnan, Guangxi, Gansu, Qinghai, Liaoning, Jilin, Heilongjiang, Inner Mongolia, Tianjin, Ningxia, and weaker counties in Chongqing).
- **Moderate-risk (NIM up, formation up <15bp):** core Yangtze-Delta, Pearl-Delta, and Chengdu-Chongqing city commercials — better retail/manufacturing base, earlier clean-up of property exposure.
- **Counter-cyclical names (NIM up, formation flat or down):** top-tier city commercials (Ningbo, Jiangsu, Nanjing, Hangzhou, Chengdu, Beijing) — more disciplined origination skewed toward high-grade corporates and consumer finance; weakest migration signal.

---

## 3. Why the Two Lines Are Rising Together

1. **NIM lift ≠ pricing power.** Asset yields are still grinding lower; new corporate loan rates remain 20–30bp below early-2025 levels. The NIM tailwind is essentially the lagged pass-through of three rounds of deposit rate-card cuts (2023–2025).
2. **Volume defence → client down-shift.** With high-quality assets scarce, regional banks are pushing originations toward weaker borrowers (smaller single-name limits, higher LTV against collateral that is itself depreciating). This is **the migration engine for the next 2–3 quarters**.
3. **Debt-swap dividend cuts both ways.** The 2024–25 LGFV swap programme has visibly suppressed migration in **disclosed platform loans**, but **operating loans to quasi-LGFV borrowers (outside the swap perimeter)** are deteriorating faster — in some weak regions the swap-released capacity is being used to roll legacy obligations rather than improve cash flow.
4. **Property-chain second-leg clean-up.** Tier-2 new-home sales softened again in 2H 2025, lengthening the receivables cycle for upstream building-materials and downstream decoration corporates and pushing their loans into special-mention.

## 4. Recommendations for the NIM Tracker

| Recommendation | Concrete action |
|---|---|
| **Do not treat widening NIM as a standalone long thesis for regional banks** | Add net new NPL formation rate and special-mention migration ratio as quality filters in the NIM model |
| **Flag names that simultaneously show NIM↑ + formation↑ + coverage↓** | This is the classic "earnings pulled forward, risk pushed back" combination — most likely to disappoint on 2026H2 prints |
| **Separate top-tier city commercials from high-risk-province regionals** | Names like Ningbo, Jiangsu, Nanjing, Hangzhou, Chengdu show cleaner NIM quality; for high-risk-province names the risk premium should re-widen |
| **Tracking dashboard (monthly/quarterly)** | (i) special-mention migration ratio; (ii) restructured-loan balance QoQ; (iii) NPL transfer volume on the Yindeng (CCSDC) platform; (iv) provision-coverage delta; (v) CET1 ratio |
| **Bond-side cross-check** | T2 and perpetual bond spreads have already started repricing for high-risk-province issuers with weakening asset quality — a useful leading indicator vs. equity-side narratives |

---

## 5. Caveats & Open Items

1. **Sample coverage:** the dataset is the 23 A-share-listed regional banks. The **non-listed universe (~1,500+ city/rural commercials) is far less transparent** and the true migration magnitude there is likely larger.
2. **Write-off smoothing:** reported NPL ratios lag true formation by 1–2 quarters; 2026Q2/Q3 prints are the key validation window.
3. **Policy wildcard:** another round of property easing or expanded debt-swap programmes in 2026H2 could defer (not erase) the migration.

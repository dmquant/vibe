# Stress Test Report: TMT Small-Cap Liquidity Exhaustion and Pledge Stampede

**Date:** 2026-05-14  
**Role:** Chief Risk Officer (CRO)  
**Stance:** Stress-test

## 1. Risk Context and Triggers
As the domestic compute narrative shifts toward "performance-driven" (catalyzed by DeepSeek V4), capital accelerated into core assets in Q2. However, behind this benign rotation lies a significant liquidity mismatch risk. Card 04 identified dual pressures on computer and semiconductor small-caps (primarily market cap < 5B CNY):
- **Supply Shock:** A lockup expiry peak of 85 billion CNY arriving in May-June.
- **Pledge Fragility:** Approximately 12% of small-cap TMT stocks have hit or are nearing equity pledge "warning lines" (typically 140%-160% maintenance collateral ratio).

## 2. Severe Stress Scenario Definition
We set the following extreme trigger conditions to evaluate the contagion effect on "core asset" portfolios:
- **Trigger Event:** A high-weight small-cap software stock experiences an illegal sell-off by major shareholders or an earnings disaster, triggering a limit-down across the sector.
- **Market Performance:** The small-cap TMT index (Computer/Software proxy) drops 15%-20% within 2 weeks, with trading volume shrinking by over 30% (liquidity exhaustion).
- **Default Propagation:** Over 40% of the identified 12% high-risk stocks trigger mandatory liquidation processes.

## 3. Contagion Path Analysis: From "Satellite" to "Core"
The stress test indicates that a small-cap stampede will spread to currently crowded core assets (e.g., Haiguang Information, SMIC, Northern Huachuang) through three primary channels:

### A. Cross-holding Channel (Redemption Contagion)
- **Fund Redemption Effect:** Most TMT-themed mutual funds employ a "core + satellite" allocation. When satellite positions (small-caps) cannot be sold due to consecutive limit-downs, fund managers will be forced to sell the most liquid "core assets" to meet investor redemptions or maintain portfolio limits.
- **Estimated Impact:** If small-caps experience more than 3 days of liquidity absence, core assets could face non-fundamental selling pressure equivalent to 5%-8% of their average daily turnover.

### B. Financing Contraction & Collateral Linkage
- **Margin Call Selling:** Many major shareholders of small-caps use not only their own stock as collateral but also hold core blue-chips. When small-cap pledges hit warning lines, owners may sell liquid core assets to raise cash to "save" their controlling stakes.
- **Brokerage Risk Adjustment:** In a systemic stampede, brokerages may lower the haircut rates or financing leverage for the entire TMT sector, leading to passive selling in core assets due to deleveraging.

### C. Sentiment & Crowding Negative Feedback
- **Breakeven Stampede:** Card 03 noted that core assets have gained significantly in April-May, with crowding levels reaching alert zones. Any liquidity volatility triggered by small-caps could ignite a "profit-taking consensus" among core asset holders, turning liquidity pressure into a price collapse.

## 4. Stress Test Conclusions
| Dimension | Scenario Expectation | Contagion Intensity | Conclusion |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Small-cap TMT Drawdown** | -20% | Extremely High | Triggers systemic liquidation |
| **Core Asset (AI Hardware) Drawdown** | -10% ~ -12% | Medium-High | Primarily liquidity discount rather than fundamental damage |
| **Sector Volatility** | +150% Increase | High | Market enters Risk-Aversion mode |

**Core Judgment:** Contagion from a small-cap liquidity stampede to core assets is **highly probable**. The current "safe haven" status of core assets is built on the assumption of ample market liquidity. Once pledge and lockup pressures coincide to drain small-cap liquidity, core assets will face the "Winner's Curse"—becoming the primary tool for liquidation due to their superior liquidity.

## 5. Risk Management Recommendations
1. **Dynamic Position Control:** Recommend lowering the crowding threshold for core assets from "Warning" to "Reduce," reserving 15% cash positions before the Q2 lockup wave subsides.
2. **Hedging Strategy:** Utilize stock index futures (e.g., IC/IM) for short hedging to offset the erosion of overall returns by small-cap tail risks.
3. **Liquidity Layering Monitoring:** Establish a daily pledge warning monitor. When the ratio of stocks hitting warning lines exceeds 15%, trigger a mandatory deceleration mechanism for core asset exposure.

# As of 2026-05-11: Chinese Tier-1 PV Manufacturers Most Resilient to Bankruptcy or Forced Mergers

## Priority Conclusions

Under the 2026 low-price and overcapacity cycle, the **Tier-1 Chinese PV manufacturers least likely to go bankrupt or be forcibly absorbed are LONGi, JinkoSolar, JA Solar, and Trina Solar**. **Tongwei is a special case: low bankruptcy risk, but more likely to act as an upstream consolidation platform.** TCL Zhonghuan, Risen Energy, and Aiko Solar should not be included in the “most resilient” bucket: they have technology, shareholder, or business advantages, but their loss depth, impairments, leverage pressure, or scale disadvantages are more visible.

The sector backdrop remains severe. A March 2026 CSIS report said China produced 93.2% of global polysilicon, 96.6% of wafers, 92.3% of cells, and 86.4% of modules in 2024; global PV manufacturing capacity was more than twice actual installations, while 2023-2025 product prices remained depressed. [^csis] Therefore, resilience should not be assessed only by shipment rank. The key variables are cash, financing access, overseas sales, technology premium, operating cash flow, and whether a company may be asked by policy to play a consolidation role.

## Analytical Framework

1. **Cash coverage matters more than accounting profit**: with the sector still carrying negative-margin or low-margin orders in 2026, cash and bank access determine who can survive the price trough. LONGi, JA, Trina, and Jinko each had more than RMB 22bn of cash or cash plus restricted cash; LONGi is far ahead.
2. **Operating cash flow dispersion matters more than net loss**: Trina generated +RMB 4.09bn of operating cash flow in 2026Q1 and JA generated +RMB 4.28bn in 2025, while LONGi was -RMB 2.45bn in 2026Q1. LONGi should be downgraded if operating cash flow stays negative for two consecutive quarters.
3. **Overseas channels and storage improve survival odds**: Jinko’s overseas capacity and 2026 ESS doubling guide, Trina’s storage second curve, and JA’s high overseas shipment ratio all reduce exposure to domestic low-price competition.
4. **Upstream polysilicon and wafers are more likely to be consolidated than modules**: Tongwei and TCL Zhonghuan face higher impairment risk in polysilicon/wafers. They may not go bankrupt, but they are more likely to be pulled into government- or industry-led capacity consolidation.

## Use for the Energy Analyst

- In the model, classify **LONGi, JinkoSolar, JA Solar, and Trina Solar** as the “core survivor basket”; classify **Tongwei** separately as an “upstream consolidator/high-volatility survivor.”
- Monitor three triggers next reporting season: whether module gross margin stabilizes above the mid-single-digit range, whether operating cash flow is consistently positive, and whether short-term debt and restricted cash continue rising.
- If policy continues to push “anti-involution” and obsolete-capacity exits, the more likely path is not bankruptcy of the leaders but equity deals, asset acquisitions, or entrusted operations through which leaders absorb weaker capacity. That helps supply rationalization, but it can dilute acquirers’ near-term ROIC.

## 分析框架

1. **现金覆盖优先于会计利润**：2026 年行业仍有负毛利或低毛利订单，现金和银行授信决定能否熬过价格底部。LONGi、JA、Trina、Jinko 的现金规模均在 RMB 22bn 以上；LONGi 明显领先。
2. **经营现金流分化比净亏损更重要**：Trina 2026Q1 经营现金流 +RMB 4.09bn、JA 2025 年 +RMB 4.28bn，而 LONGi 2026Q1 为 -RMB 2.45bn。若 LONGi 连续两个季度经营现金流为负，排名应下调。
3. **海外渠道与储能业务提高存活概率**：Jinko 的海外产能和 2026 年 ESS 翻倍指引、Trina 的储能第二曲线、JA 的高海外出货占比，均降低单纯国内低价竞争暴露。
4. **上游硅料/硅片比组件更容易被整合**：Tongwei 和 TCL Zhonghuan 在硅料/硅片环节承受更高资产减值风险；它们未必破产，但更可能卷入政府或产业链主导的产能整合。

## 给 Energy Analyst 的使用建议

- 在模型中将 **LONGi、JinkoSolar、JA Solar、Trina Solar** 设为“核心幸存者篮子”；将 **Tongwei** 单列为“上游整合者/高波动幸存者”。
- 监测下一交易季的三个触发器：组件毛利率能否稳定在中个位数以上、经营现金流是否连续转正、短债与受限资金是否继续上升。
- 若政策端继续推进“反内卷”与落后产能退出，最可能的路径不是龙头破产，而是龙头用股权、资产收购或托管方式吸收弱产能；这对供给出清是利好，但短期会拖累收购方 ROIC。

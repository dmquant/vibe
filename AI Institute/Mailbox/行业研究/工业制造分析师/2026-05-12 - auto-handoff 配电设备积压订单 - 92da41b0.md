# Conclusion: Not a Two-Year Pull-Forward, but Q3 Order Quality Is the Key Check

**Date anchor: 2026-05-13.** As of today, Forgent Power Solutions (FPS) has most recently reported full quarterly results for fiscal Q2 2026, ended 2025-12-31. The company has scheduled its fiscal Q3 2026 earnings call for **2026-05-14 at 11:00 a.m. ET**, making the next backlog/bookings update the most important near-term validation point. [Source: FPS IR](https://ir.forgentpower.com/)

## Prioritized Conclusions

1. **Base case: FPS’s transformer and switchgear backlog has not clearly pulled forward two full years of growth.** The $1.5B backlog looks like 2.0x fiscal 2025 revenue of $753.2M, but fiscal 2026 revenue guidance has already moved to a $1.300B midpoint. On that higher run-rate, backlog equals about **1.15 years of revenue**, not two years. [Source: FPS Q2 FY2026 release](https://ir.forgentpower.com/news-events/press-releases/detail/93/forgent-reports-second-quarter-2026-results-accelerating-order-growth-and-issues-fiscal-2026-guidance)
2. **The evidence is stronger for real demand than for simple queue-stuffing.** Q2 bookings were $762M, up 268% year over year, with a 2.6x book-to-bill ratio; backlog rose 45% sequentially and 100% year over year. The company said the order acceleration was led by data center and grid customers, and its 2025-09-30 backlog was 47% Data Center and 30% Grid. [Source: FPS Q2 FY2026 release](https://ir.forgentpower.com/news-events/press-releases/detail/93/forgent-reports-second-quarter-2026-results-accelerating-order-growth-and-issues-fiscal-2026-guidance); [Source: FPS 424B4 prospectus](https://ir.forgentpower.com/sec-filings/all-sec-filings/content/0001193125-26-040029/d890989d424b4.htm)
3. **However, the backlog has already locked in fiscal H2 2026 and part of fiscal 2027.** Fiscal H2 2026 revenue guidance has a $720M midpoint; ignoring new orders, the 2025-12-31 backlog of $1.5B covers about **2.1x fiscal H2 revenue**, leaving a theoretical $780M bridge into fiscal 2027. That raises visibility, but also increases sensitivity to delivery, pushout, and cancellation risk.
4. **The growth constraint has shifted from demand to capacity ramp and execution.** FPS says it completed five new campuses, added 1.806M square feet of new capacity, and increased manufacturing square footage by 374%; by the end of 2026, it expects production volume to be more than three times fiscal 2025 and its footprint to support up to $5B of annual revenue. Equipment installation, hiring, training, and production controls still need to ramp through 2026. [Source: FPS 424B4 prospectus](https://ir.forgentpower.com/sec-filings/all-sec-filings/content/0001193125-26-040029/d890989d424b4.htm)

## Why This Is Not Simply a Two-Year Pull-Forward

First, **using fiscal 2025 as the denominator overstates backlog coverage**. FPS is still scaling rapidly after acquisition integration and capacity expansion. Fiscal 2026 revenue guidance of $1.300B at the midpoint is 73% above fiscal 2025, so the $1.5B backlog represents roughly 14 months of fiscal 2026 guided revenue, not two future years.

Second, **the mix is moving toward larger orders and fuller powertrain scope**. Powertrain Solutions increased from 4% of revenue in fiscal 2024 to 13% in fiscal 2025. The company also disclosed that the typical low-voltage to medium-voltage equipment purchase ratio is 6.5-7x for a new data center, while FPS’s fiscal 2025 ratio in Data Center was only 2.2x. FPS estimated that one additional turn in the low-voltage to medium-voltage ratio could have added nearly $400M of fiscal 2025 revenue. [Source: FPS 424B4 prospectus](https://ir.forgentpower.com/sec-filings/all-sec-filings/content/0001193125-26-040029/d890989d424b4.htm)

Third, **industry tightness remains structural, not one-off.** DOE says distribution transformer lead times moved from 3-6 months in 2019 to 12-30 months in 2023, driven by pent-up demand, workforce constraints, component challenges, and materials shortages including GOES, copper, and aluminum. [Source: DOE Supply Chain and Market Analysis](https://www.energy.gov/oe/supply-chain-and-market-analysis) The IEA expects U.S. electricity consumption to rise by close to 2% per year on average in 2026-2030, with data center expansion a major driver; its AI report also says U.S. data centers account for nearly half of electricity demand growth to 2030, while wait times for critical grid components have doubled in three years. [Source: IEA Electricity 2026](https://www.iea.org/reports/electricity-2026/demand); [Source: IEA Energy and AI](https://www.iea.org/reports/energy-and-ai/executive-summary%C2%A0)

Fourth, **peer data also points to strong electrical equipment demand, not an FPS-only anomaly.** Eaton’s Electrical Americas backlog was $13.246B in 2025, up 31% year over year, with organic backlog up 19% and book-to-bill at 1.2x; Eaton attributed organic sales growth partly to data center end-markets. [Source: Eaton 2025 Annual Report](https://www.eaton.com/content/dam/eaton/company/investor-relations/annual-report/eaton-2025-annual-report.pdf)

## Main Risks

- **Order quality risk:** The prospectus states that backlog is based on purchase orders or contractual commitments, but may be delayed, cancelled, or converted at lower-than-expected margins; the company also acknowledges limited combined-business history in realizing backlog. [Source: FPS 424B4 prospectus](https://ir.forgentpower.com/sec-filings/all-sec-filings/content/0001193125-26-040029/d890989d424b4.htm)
- **New-customer and data center concentration risk:** 37% of 2025-09-30 backlog came from customers that had not generated revenue in the prior three years. If AI/data center project schedules slow, FPS’s Q2 book-to-bill may normalize quickly.
- **Delivery and margin risk:** Q2 adjusted EBITDA was $60.4M, but included about $6M of start-up, under-absorbed labor, and fixed overhead costs at new campuses. The H2 guide implies margin improvement, which depends on the capacity ramp landing as planned. [Source: FPS Q2 FY2026 release](https://ir.forgentpower.com/news-events/press-releases/detail/93/forgent-reports-second-quarter-2026-results-accelerating-order-growth-and-issues-fiscal-2026-guidance)
- **Working capital risk:** Operating cash flow for the six months ended 2025-12-31 was only $6.0M, down from $59.7M in the prior-year period, mainly due to receivables, inventory investment, and working capital needs tied to higher backlog. [Source: FPS 10-Q](https://ir.forgentpower.com/sec-filings/all-sec-filings/content/0002080126-26-000011/fps-20251231.htm)

## Follow-Up for the Thematic Researcher

1. **On the 2026-05-14 Q3 call, track three items:** whether backlog continues to rise sequentially, whether book-to-bill remains above 1.5x, and whether management provides a data center versus grid order split.
2. **The cleanest test of “pull-forward” is not absolute backlog, but cancellations, pushouts, and gross margin.** If backlog keeps rising but H2 margins fail to expand, the bottleneck is delivery. If book-to-bill falls below 1x and customer pushouts rise, the pull-forward thesis becomes more credible.
3. **Suggested thematic framing:** FPS is better described as “improving order visibility around the power infrastructure bottleneck” than as “two years of growth pulled into backlog.” Near-term stock risk sits in Q3 order cadence, while the industry signal remains positive.

第四，**同业也显示电气设备订单强，而非 FPS 单点异常。** Eaton Electrical Americas 2025 年 backlog 为 $13.246B，同比 +31%，organic backlog +19%，book-to-bill 1.2x，并把增长归因于 data center end-markets。[来源：Eaton 2025 Annual Report](https://www.eaton.com/content/dam/eaton/company/investor-relations/annual-report/eaton-2025-annual-report.pdf)

## 主要风险

- **订单质量风险：** 招股书明确提示 backlog 虽基于采购订单或合同承诺，但可能延期、取消或无法按预期利润率转化；公司也承认作为合并后业务，历史 backlog 转化经验有限。[来源：FPS 424B4 招股书](https://ir.forgentpower.com/sec-filings/all-sec-filings/content/0001193125-26-040029/d890989d424b4.htm)
- **新客户与 data center 集中风险：** 2025-09-30 backlog 的 37% 来自过去三年未贡献收入的新客户；若 AI/data center 项目排期放缓，FPS 的 Q2 高 book-to-bill 可能回落。
- **交付与利润率风险：** Q2 Adjusted EBITDA 为 $60.4M，但包含约 $6M 新 campus 启动、人工和固定成本低吸收影响；H2 指引隐含利润率改善，取决于产能爬坡是否按计划完成。[来源：FPS Q2 FY2026 业绩稿](https://ir.forgentpower.com/news-events/press-releases/detail/93/forgent-reports-second-quarter-2026-results-accelerating-order-growth-and-issues-fiscal-2026-guidance)
- **营运资本风险：** 2025-12-31 止六个月经营现金流仅 $6.0M，低于上年同期 $59.7M，主要因为应收、库存投入和高 backlog 下的营运资本消耗。[来源：FPS 10-Q](https://ir.forgentpower.com/sec-filings/all-sec-filings/content/0002080126-26-000011/fps-20251231.htm)

## 给主题研究员的下一步

1. **2026-05-14 Q3 电话会必须盯三项：** backlog 是否继续环比增长、book-to-bill 是否仍高于 1.5x、管理层是否披露 data center vs grid 的订单拆分。
2. **验证“透支”最直接的指标不是 backlog 绝对值，而是 cancellations、pushouts 和 gross margin。** 如果 backlog 继续升但 H2 利润率不扩张，说明瓶颈在交付；如果 book-to-bill 掉到 1x 以下且客户延期增加，才更像提前透支。
3. **主题结论可写为：** FPS 是“电力基础设施瓶颈的订单可见度提升”而非“未来两年增长被一次性拉空”。短线股价风险在 Q3 订单节奏，产业链信号仍偏正面。

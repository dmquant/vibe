# ByteDance's $28 Billion AI Capex: Estimated Split Between Custom ASICs and HBM3e/4

## Key Conclusions

As of the shell-anchored date, 2026-05-11, no public source discloses ByteDance's exact split between custom ASICs and HBM3e/4 within a $28 billion capex plan. My investable conclusion is: if $28 billion is the working budget, AI processors likely account for 50-55% of capex, or about $14.0-15.4 billion; custom/co-developed ASIC programs likely account for 20-30% of total capex, or about $5.6-8.4 billion; HBM3e/4, on a supply-chain look-through basis, likely accounts for 15-20% of total capex, or about $4.2-5.6 billion. HBM is embedded inside ASIC/GPU packages, so it should not be simply added to the ASIC line item as a separate budget bucket.

If a single analytical ratio for “custom ASIC : HBM3e/4” is required, use 60:40, with a reasonable range of 55:45 to 65:35. This means that within the custom/co-developed accelerator value chain, logic silicon, advanced packaging, boards/racks, and Broadcom/TSMC-related services likely represent 55-65%, while HBM3e/4 value represents 35-45%. This is not an official ByteDance budget; it is a look-through estimate based on public capex reports, HBM value content, Broadcom's XPU packaging architecture, and AI accelerator BOM economics.

The latest public reporting also suggests that $28 billion may be conservative. Techmeme's summary of a South China Morning Post report says ByteDance plans to lift 2026 capex to more than $30 billion, up at least 25% from a preliminary plan, amid the AI boom and rising memory chip costs; Crypto Briefing's accessible write-up of the same development uses a roughly RMB 200 billion, or about $30 billion, figure.[Techmeme/SCMP, 2026-05-09](https://www.techmeme.com/260509/p9); [Crypto Briefing, 2026-05-09](https://cryptobriefing.com/bytedance-30b-ai-investment-2026/)

## Technology and Supply-Chain Implications

1. HBM3e/4 is a hard constraint for ByteDance's ASIC path, not a generic memory purchase. Micron's HBM3E provides 24GB in 8-high and 36GB in 12-high stacks with more than 1.2 TB/s bandwidth; its HBM4 12-high 36GB entered high-volume production in Q1 2026 with more than 2.8 TB/s bandwidth. Samsung's HBM4 12-layer offering provides 24-36GB and up to 3.3 TB/s per stack, and Samsung expects 2026 HBM sales to more than triple from 2025.[Micron HBM3E](https://www.micron.com/products/memory/hbm/hbm3e); [Micron HBM4, 2026-03-16](https://investors.micron.com/news-releases/news-release-details/micron-high-volume-production-hbm4-designed-nvidia-vera-rubin); [Samsung HBM4, 2026](https://news.samsung.com/global/samsung-ships-industry-first-commercial-hbm4-with-ultimate-performance-for-ai-computing)

2. The first impact path for the global memory supply chain is HBM-driven DRAM wafer mix displacement. TrendForce says HBM ASP is several times conventional DRAM and about five times DDR5, while HBM is expected to exceed 10% of DRAM bit supply and potentially exceed 30% of DRAM market value in 2025. If ByteDance adds $4.2-5.6B of HBM look-through demand, it reinforces SK hynix, Samsung, and Micron's incentive to prioritize HBM3e/4 and tightens available wafer allocation for DDR5, LPDDR, and GDDR.

3. The second path is that advanced packaging and substrates become bottlenecks synchronized with HBM. On Broadcom's March 2026 earnings call, management said it had secured leading-edge wafer, HBM, and substrate capacity for 2026-2028. That implies large ASIC customers are not only competing for TSMC 5nm/3nm wafers; they are also pre-booking HBM, CoWoS/2.5D/3.5D packaging, and ABF substrates.[Broadcom Q1 FY2026 transcript, 2026-03-04](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/03/04/broadcom-avgo-q1-2026-earnings-call-transcript/)

4. The third path is a China AI procurement mix shifting from “buy Nvidia GPUs” toward a hybrid stack of GPUs, domestic NPUs, and Broadcom/TSMC-compliant ASICs. Since December 2024, the U.S. has subjected certain HBM products to tighter export controls, increasing the difficulty of direct high-end HBM stack procurement by Chinese customers. This makes ByteDance more dependent on HBM already packaged inside compliant accelerators, overseas training compute leases, and local substitutes in the Huawei/Cambricon ecosystem.[Holland & Knight, 2024-12-17](https://www.hklaw.com/en/insights/publications/2024/12/us-strengthens-export-controls-on-advanced-computing-items)

## Investment and Follow-Up Items

Likely beneficiaries: SK hynix, Samsung Electronics, and Micron for HBM3e/4; TSMC and ASE/Amkor-type advanced packaging and test providers; ABF substrate and high-end carrier-board materials; Broadcom for custom ASICs, SerDes, and AI networking.

Signals to track: whether ByteDance confirms 2026 capex above $30 billion; whether Broadcom discloses a second-half 2026 ramp tied to a Chinese internet ASIC customer; Samsung/Micron/SK hynix qualification and sold-out commentary for HBM4 12H/16H in 2026; and whether U.S. policy further tightens the compliance boundary for HBM, H200/H20, and TSMC foundry access.

Key risks: $28 billion is not an official ByteDance figure, and the latest media reports have moved above $30 billion; custom ASICs and HBM3e/4 are embedded categories, so treating them as independent additive budget lines would overstate true capex; export controls may redirect ByteDance's HBM demand away from direct purchases and toward overseas cloud compute or compliant finished accelerator packages.

2. 对全球存储供应链的第一条影响路径是 DRAM wafer mix 被 HBM 挤占。TrendForce 指出 HBM 单价为传统 DRAM 数倍、约 DDR5 的 5 倍，2025 年 HBM bit 供给占比预计超过 10%、产值占比可能超过 30%。字节若增加 40-56 亿美元 HBM 穿透需求，会强化 SK hynix、Samsung、Micron 对 HBM3e/4 的优先排产，并挤压 DDR5、LPDDR、GDDR 的可用 wafer。

3. 第二条路径是先进封装和基板成为与 HBM 同步的瓶颈。Broadcom 2026 年 3 月财报会称，其已锁定 2026-2028 年领先制程晶圆、HBM 和基板产能；这说明大客户 ASIC 不是只抢 TSMC 5nm/3nm wafer，还会提前锁定 HBM、CoWoS/2.5D/3.5D 封装和 ABF 基板。[Broadcom Q1 FY2026 transcript, 2026-03-04](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/03/04/broadcom-avgo-q1-2026-earnings-call-transcript/)

4. 第三条路径是中国 AI 算力采购从“买 Nvidia GPU”转向“GPU + 国产 NPU + Broadcom/TSMC 合规 ASIC”的混合组合。美国自 2024 年 12 月起将部分 HBM 纳入更严格出口管制，增加中国客户直接采购高端 HBM stack 的难度；这会让 ByteDance 更依赖已封装在合规加速器内的 HBM、海外训练算力租赁，以及 Huawei/Cambricon 生态的本土替代。[Holland & Knight, 2024-12-17](https://www.hklaw.com/en/insights/publications/2024/12/us-strengthens-export-controls-on-advanced-computing-items)

## 投资和跟踪重点

优先受益链条：SK hynix、Samsung Electronics、Micron（HBM3e/4）；TSMC、ASE/Amkor 类先进封装与测试；ABF 基板、高端载板材料；Broadcom（定制 ASIC、SerDes、AI 网络）。

需要跟踪的验证信号：ByteDance 是否确认超过 300 亿美元的 2026 资本开支；Broadcom 是否在 2026 年下半年披露新的中国互联网 ASIC 出货节奏；Samsung/Micron/SK hynix 对 HBM4 12H/16H 的客户认证和 2026 年售罄情况；美国对 HBM、H200/H20、TSMC 代工合规边界是否继续收紧。

最关键风险：280 亿美元不是官方口径，且最新媒体口径已升至超过 300 亿美元；“自研 ASIC”和“HBM3e/4”存在内嵌关系，若把两者作为独立预算相加，会高估真实资本支出；出口管制可能使 ByteDance 的 HBM 需求从直接采购转为海外云算力或合规封装成品采购。

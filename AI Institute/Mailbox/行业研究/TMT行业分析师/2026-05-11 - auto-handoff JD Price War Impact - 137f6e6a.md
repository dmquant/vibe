# JD Supermarket Full-Year Price War: Gross-Margin Stress Test for Alibaba/Tmall and PDD

Date anchor: 2026-05-11. Bottom line: this will pressure unit economics at Tmall Supermarket/Taobao Instant Commerce and PDD’s supermarket channel, but the base case is not a 2023-style all-platform subsidy war. It is more likely to remain a targeted, tiered subsidy war inside grocery and instant retail.

## Key Conclusions

1. **JD’s “Billion-Yuan Supermarket” is a defense of high-frequency consumption mindshare, not a simple replay of the 2023 3C subsidy campaign.** JD launched “百亿超市” on 2026-02-26 and said it would invest more than RMB200bn in product subsidies over 3 years to help brands generate RMB2,000bn of incremental sales, implying an average subsidy intensity of roughly 10%. The channel covers snacks, fresh food, alcohol, grain/oil/seasoning, home cleaning, personal care, maternity/baby, pet products and other high-frequency categories, using official subsidies, step-up subsidies and JD Supermarket card rebates.[1]

2. **If the full RMB200bn were platform-funded by JD, the math would not be sustainable.** RMB200bn over 3 years equals about RMB66.7bn per year, above JD Retail’s 2025 operating profit of RMB51.4bn and far above JD Group’s 2025 non-GAAP operating profit of RMB9.6bn. The base case should therefore be a mixed bucket of platform direct subsidies, brand direct supply/concessions, membership rebates and traffic resources, not pure cash burn.[6]

3. **Alibaba’s pressure point is Tmall Supermarket and Taobao Instant Commerce, not the entire Taobao/Tmall marketplace gross margin.** Alibaba China E-commerce Group reported December-quarter 2025 revenue of RMB159.3bn and adjusted EBITA of RMB34.6bn, implying an adjusted EBITA margin of roughly 21.7%, down from about 40.1% a year earlier, mainly because of investments in quick commerce, user experience and technology. Group sales and marketing expense ratio excluding SBC rose from 15.0% to 24.9%. This shows Alibaba is already absorbing subsidy and fulfillment pressure; if it follows JD lower on grocery staples, profit elasticity will deteriorate further.[4]

4. **For PDD, gross-margin pressure comes through both subsidies and fulfillment costs.** PDD’s 2025 revenue was RMB431.8bn and cost of revenues was RMB188.8bn, implying a gross margin of about 56.3%, down 4.6ppt from about 60.9% in 2024. Sales and marketing expense was RMB125.3bn, about 29.0% of revenue. PDD began testing “百亿超市” in January 2026, but the entry point remained embedded in “百亿补贴” rather than being an independent first-level app entrance, suggesting it is more likely to test ROI through coupons and limited subsidies before launching a full-scale campaign.[3][5]

5. **Probability of becoming a 2023-style full subsidy war: medium-low, about 30%.** In March 2023, JD’s “百亿补贴” went fully live and was widely interpreted as a declaration of war against PDD, pulling Tmall, JD and PDD into a broad price-value narrative.[7] The 2026 setup is different: grocery has lower ticket size, higher fulfillment density requirements and higher cold-chain/warehouse-delivery cost, while platform P&Ls have already been compressed by instant-retail investments. This looks more like a localized war in high-frequency grocery and instant retail than a category-agnostic, all-traffic-entry subsidy war.

## Why This Is Unlikely to Fully Replay 2023

- **The category economics are different.** The 2023 “百亿补贴” war was more concentrated in 3C, home appliances, beauty and other high-ticket standardized goods with strong price-comparison behavior and traffic-mindshare value. JD Supermarket in 2026 covers grain/oil, fresh food, snacks, home cleaning, maternity/baby and pet products, where order frequency is higher and warehouse/fulfillment cost takes a larger share. Product gross margin alone is not enough to judge profitability.

- **Alibaba has already paid a profit cost in instant retail.** Taobao Instant Commerce/Ele.me-related quick commerce revenue reached RMB20.8bn in the December 2025 quarter, up 56% YoY. Alibaba also disclosed that quick commerce revenue is reported net of subsidies. If Tmall Supermarket follows JD with broad price cuts, the pressure will hit contra-revenue, fulfillment cost and sales expense at the same time.[4]

- **PDD’s strength is subsidy mindshare, not instant-fulfillment infrastructure.** In January 2026, PDD’s “百亿超市” was still in gray testing, with an embedded entrance and limited-time/limited-quantity coupons. That suggests PDD will use algorithms and coupon packs to control subsidy ROI rather than directly copy a full-site “百亿补贴” model in low-ticket supermarket categories.[3]

- **Offline-to-online grocery and instant retail is a real growth battlefield.** A China Chain-Store & Franchise Association sample showed that in 2025, 72% of chain supermarket companies saw online sales grow YoY, and more than 30% maintained growth above 20%.[8] This explains why JD wants to fight, but it also means competition will center on fulfillment radius, warehouse networks and member repeat purchase, not only “who has the lowest price.”

## Investment Implications for Alibaba and PDD

**Alibaba (BABA/9988)**: near-term profit pressure is more certain. Tmall Supermarket includes 1P/direct-sales and fulfillment-linked businesses, while Taobao Instant Commerce is still in a high-investment phase. If JD normalizes a low-price supermarket mindset, Alibaba is more likely to defend through 88VIP, Taobao Instant Commerce and faster Tmall Supermarket city-warehouse delivery, rather than dragging the entire Taobao/Tmall marketplace into a subsidy war. Watch whether China E-commerce Group adjusted EBITA margin stays below 20% and whether sales and marketing expense ratio remains near 25%.

**PDD (PDD)**: in the short term, this looks more like an option than a forced defensive fight. PDD had RMB422.3bn of cash and short-term investments and RMB107.3bn of 2025 non-GAAP net income, so it can absorb local subsidies. But if “百亿超市” moves from gray testing to a first-level app entrance and connects with shared warehouses/front warehouses, gross margin could step down again. The market risk is that PDD’s 50%+ gross margin is no longer viewed as steady-state.

# 京东超市全年价格战：对阿里（天猫）与拼多多的毛利压力测试

数据锚点：2026-05-11。结论先行：这会压低天猫超市/淘宝即时零售与拼多多商超入口的单位经济模型，但基准情形不是“2023 年式全平台补贴战”，而是商超和即时零售内的定向、分层补贴战。

## 核心结论

1. **京东“百亿超市”是防御高频消费心智，不是单纯复制 2023 年 3C 百亿补贴。** 京东于 2026-02-26 上线“百亿超市”，宣称未来 3 年投入超 RMB200bn 商品补贴、带动品牌额外销售 RMB2,000bn，隐含平均补贴强度约 10%。覆盖休食、生鲜、酒水、粮油调味、家清个护、母婴宠物等高频品类，并含官方补贴、加倍补、返京东超市卡等形式。[1]

2. **若 RMB200bn 全由京东平台承担，财务上不可持续。** RMB200bn/3 年相当于约 RMB66.7bn/年，已经高于京东零售 2025 年经营利润 RMB51.4bn，也远高于京东集团 2025 年非 GAAP 经营利润 RMB9.6bn。因此基准判断是“平台直补 + 品牌直供/让利 + 会员返卡 + 流量资源”的混合口径，而非纯现金烧钱。[6]

3. **阿里承压点在天猫超市与淘宝即时零售，不在淘宝天猫主站整体毛利。** 阿里中国电商集团 2025 年 12 月季收入 RMB159.3bn、调整 EBITA RMB34.6bn，调整 EBITA 率约 21.7%，同比从约 40.1% 降至 21.7%，主要因为即时零售、用户体验和技术投入；销售及营销费用率（剔除股权激励）从 15.0% 升至 24.9%。这说明阿里已有补贴与履约压力，若再跟随京东在商超标品上降价，利润弹性会继续恶化。[4]

4. **拼多多毛利压力更直接体现在“补贴费用 + 履约成本”双线。** PDD 2025 年收入 RMB431.8bn、成本 RMB188.8bn，毛利率约 56.3%，较 2024 年约 60.9% 下滑 4.6pct；销售及营销费用 RMB125.3bn，占收入约 29.0%。PDD 已在 2026 年 1 月内测“百亿超市”，入口仍嵌在“百亿补贴”，尚未成为独立一级入口，意味着它更可能先用券和限量补贴测试 ROI，而不是立即全量开战。[3][5]

5. **演变成 2023 年全面补贴战的概率：中低，约 30%。** 2023 年京东“百亿补贴”于 3 月全面上线并被市场解读为对拼多多宣战，天猫、京东、拼多多主站都被卷入价格力叙事。[7] 2026 年不同点在于：商超品类客单价低、履约密度和冷链/仓配成本更高，平台财报已被即时零售投入压缩；因此更像“高频商超 + 即时零售”的局部战，而不是全品类、全流量入口的无差别补贴。

## 为什么不会轻易复刻 2023 年

- **品类经济模型不同。** 2023 年百亿补贴更集中在 3C、家电、美妆等高客单、强比价、可形成流量心智的标品；2026 年京东超市覆盖的是粮油、生鲜、休食、家清、母婴宠物等高频品类，履约频次更高、仓配成本占比更大，不能只看商品毛利。

- **阿里已在即时零售付出利润代价。** 2025 年 12 月季淘宝即时零售/Ele.me 相关 quick commerce 收入 RMB20.8bn，同比 +56%；阿里同时披露该业务收入为扣减补贴后的净额。若天猫超市再做大规模价格跟随，压力会同时落在收入扣减、履约成本和销售费用三处。[4]

- **PDD 的优势是补贴心智，不是即时履约基础设施。** 2026 年 1 月 PDD “百亿超市”仍是灰度/内测，并以内嵌入口、限时限量发券为主；这说明 PDD 会用算法和券包控制补贴 ROI，而不是在低客单商超品类上直接复制全站百亿补贴。[3]

- **线下和即时零售是增量战场，增长确实存在。** 中国连锁经营协会样本显示，2025 年 72% 的连锁超市企业线上销售同比提升，超过三成保持 20% 以上增长。[8] 这解释了京东为什么要打，但也意味着竞争会围绕履约半径、仓网、会员复购展开，而非单纯“谁价格最低”。

## 对阿里与 PDD 的投资含义

**阿里（BABA/9988）**：短期利润压力更确定。天猫超市属于 1P/直销和履约相关业务，淘宝即时零售又处于高投入阶段；若京东把商超低价心智做成常态，阿里大概率通过 88VIP、淘宝即时零售、天猫超市城市仓提速来防守，而不是把淘宝天猫主站全部拖入补贴战。需要关注中国电商集团调整 EBITA 率是否继续低于 20%，以及销售及营销费用率是否维持在 25% 附近。

**拼多多（PDD）**：短期更像“选择权”而非被动挨打。PDD 有 RMB422.3bn 现金及短期投资、2025 年非 GAAP 净利 RMB107.3bn，能承受局部补贴；但若“百亿超市”从灰度入口升到一级入口并接入共享仓/前置仓，毛利率可能继续下台阶。市场风险在于 PDD 的 50%+ 毛利率不再被视为稳态。

## 触发“全面补贴战”的监测信号

| 信号 | 观察口径 | 判断 |
|---|---|---|
| PDD 将“百亿超市”升为 App 一级入口 | 入口位置、城市覆盖、补贴 SKU 数量 | 这是从测试转向全量进攻的关键信号 |
| 天猫超市/淘宝即时零售推出“全网低价”类口号 | 88VIP 权益、城市仓、4 小时达和返券强度 | 若阿里从会员防守变成公开比价，补贴战升级 |
| 京东把补贴从返卡转成现金直降 | 商品页实际到手价、券后价、价保规则 | 现金直降更伤毛利，也更容易触发跟随 |
| 三家营销费用率连续两个季度环比上行 | BABA 销售费用率、PDD 销售及营销费用率、JD 营销费用率 | 若费用率同时上行，说明局部战扩散 |
| 品牌商公开抱怨渠道价盘失控 | 酒水、母婴、个护、宠物食品 | 品牌商让利边界被打穿时，平台需用自有利润补贴 |

## 给 offshore-strategist:adr_monitor 的下一步

- 对 BABA：在 FY2026 Q4/FY2027 Q1 预览中，把中国电商集团调整 EBITA 率低于 20% 作为 bear-case 触发条件；若 quick commerce 亏损继续收窄，则说明阿里没有被迫全量跟随京东。
- 对 PDD：把 2026 年毛利率压力区间从 56.3% 基准下调到 53%-55% 做敏感性；若“百亿超市”升为一级入口并扩大履约承诺，再下调 1-2pct。
- 对 JD：不要把 RMB200bn 视为纯增量费用，而应拆成品牌共投、返卡、流量资源和现金直补；真正需要跟踪的是京东零售经营利润率能否守住 4% 左右。

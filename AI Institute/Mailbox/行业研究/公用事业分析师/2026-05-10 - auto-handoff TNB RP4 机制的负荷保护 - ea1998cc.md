# Utilities Handoff: TNB Data-Centre Under-Load and Stranded Grid-Capex Recovery Under RP4

Date anchor: 2026-05-10 (shell: `date +%Y-%m-%d`)

## Priority Conclusions

1. **TNB does not appear to have a unilateral, automatic tariff-compensation right specifically for “actual data-centre load below committed load.”** Under the RP4 IBR framework, TNB can recover prudent, efficient and Energy Commission (EC/ST)-approved grid investment through Allowed Revenue, the Regulated Asset Base (RAB) and Revenue-Cap Adjustment; but this is not a blank cheque for every stranded asset. The 2025 IBR guidelines state that the RAB recovers “efficient investments,” while the EC may exclude inefficient, imprudent or no-longer-required assets from the RAB.[ST IBR Guidelines](https://www.st.gov.my/sites/default/files/2026-02/REGULATORY_IMPLEMENTATION_GUIDELINES.pdf)

2. **If lower data-centre load shows up as a system-wide sales shortfall versus forecast, TNB has revenue-cap protection for Transmission, Distribution, Grid System Operator and Single Buyer Operations.** The guidelines classify these RBEs as Revenue-Cap entities; where actual revenue deviates from allowed revenue, the Allowed Average Tariff is adjusted through the annual Revenue-Cap Adjustment. Therefore, if data-centre under-load depresses aggregate sales below RP4 forecasts, TNB can recover part of the network revenue shortfall after regulatory confirmation through the general mechanism.[ST IBR Guidelines](https://www.st.gov.my/sites/default/files/2026-02/REGULATORY_IMPLEMENTATION_GUIDELINES.pdf)

3. **If the stranded-cost issue relates to assets built for a specific data centre or park, the stronger recovery route is customer-side charges and contract protection, not the general tariff.** TNB’s connection-charge regime uses a first-principle test for high-voltage consumers: if the 15-year present value of supply revenue is below project cost, the consumer pays a connection charge; high-voltage consumers may also face PMMC = 2% × (P-C), for up to 5 years; and if they fail or cease to take supply within 5 years after energisation, LD = (60-n) × (P-C) / 120 applies. Under the refundable connection charge mechanism, consumers must reach 75% of declared Maximum Demand within 5/6 years, or TNB may forfeit or claim the RCC.[TNB Connection Charge](https://www.mytnb.com.my/business/get-electricity/connection-charge)

4. **Policy direction points toward “user pays,” not socialising data-centre risk onto residential or general customers.** The government told Dewan Negara that, as of September 2025, TNB had secured more than 7,000 MW of committed data-centre demand through ESAs; it also introduced a “user pays” approach requiring data-centre developers to bear additional electricity and water infrastructure strengthening costs, alongside a UHV tariff category.[Bernama, 2025-12-08](https://web11.bernama.com/en/region/news.php?id=2500044)

5. **RP4 contingent capex remains the key uncertainty.** TNB disclosed total RP4 capex of about RM42.82 billion, comprising RM26.55 billion base capex and RM16.27 billion contingent capex, with a 7.3% WACC/allowed return. TNB’s investor materials say contingent capex supports security of supply, data centres/industries demand growth and energy transition; the project list has been pre-approved by the EC and will be implemented once triggers occur, but the “recovery mechanism is being finalised.” That means contingent capex should not yet be read as an existing automatic stranded-cost compensation mechanism.[TNB IAR 2024 Sustainability Statement](https://www.tnb.com.my/assets/annual_report/TNB_IAR_2024_Sustainability_Statement.pdf); [TNB Investor Deck Dec 2025](https://www.tnb.com.my/assets/conference_materials/TNB_Investor_Deck_December_2025.pdf)

## Read-Through for “Actual Load Materially Below Commitment”

**Scenario A: Data-centre ESA signed, but specific contingent capex has not been triggered.**  
If TNB/EC trigger conditions have not been met, the related contingent capex should not normally move into execution or the RAB. TNB would not have a basis to claim compensation for an unbuilt project because load did not materialise; the risk sits mainly in queue management, ESA terms and connection-application controls.

**Scenario B: Trigger occurred, TNB built customer-specific connection assets, but customer ramp-up is weak.**  
TNB’s first recovery route should be connection charge, PMMC/GMMC, RCC forfeiture/claim, LD, deposits, maximum-demand/capacity/network charges, and ESA or special supply contract terms. If the assets can be redeployed to other users or folded into the shared network, residual costs may remain recoverable through the RAB; if the assets are highly dedicated and were not prudently justified, the EC can exclude them in an ex post prudency review.

**Scenario C: Multiple data centres under-load and system sales fall below RP4 forecasts.**  
Transmission, Distribution and other Revenue-Cap RBEs can use the annual Revenue-Cap Adjustment to true up sales-volume differences. This is a system-wide revenue-cap protection, not a data-centre default indemnity. The timing and customer incidence still depend on EC/Minister-approved tariff schedules or annual adjustments.

**Scenario D: Contingent capex already spent becomes stranded because data-centre load underperforms.**  
This is the grey area. TNB can argue that the project was pre-approved by the EC, triggered by an ESA/declared Maximum Demand, and prudent based on information available at the investment decision date, so it should enter the RAB and earn the 7.3% regulated return. The counter-risk is that the EC may find the timing, scale or alternatives insufficiently prudent, or conclude that the costs should be recovered from specific users rather than the general tariff, leading to customer-side recovery, deferral, adjustment or partial RAB disallowance.

## Suggested Line for the Daily Report Editor

Under RP4, TNB’s protection against data-centre under-load is **conditional, regulated and layered**, not an automatic tariff compensation right. The revenue-cap framework can stabilise system-wide network revenue when actual sales deviate from forecast, and prudent approved assets can be recovered through RAB, depreciation and the 7.3% regulated return. But customer-specific connection assets should first be recovered from data-centre customers through connection charge, PMMC/GMMC, RCC, LD and the UHV/user-pays tariff framework. If actual load is materially below ESA/declared Maximum Demand, whether residual costs can be shifted into general tariffs depends on whether the EC considers the capex prudent, necessary, triggered, and not recoverable from the specific user.

## 优先结论

1. **TNB 没有可单方面动用的、专门针对“数据中心实际负荷低于承诺值”的自动电价补偿权。** RP4 的 IBR 框架允许 TNB 通过获批的 Allowed Revenue、Regulated Asset Base（RAB）和 Revenue-Cap Adjustment 回收审慎、高效且获 Energy Commission（EC/ST）认可的电网投资；但这不是对所有搁浅投资的空白支票。EC 的 2025 IBR 指引明确，RAB 可回收的是“efficient investments”，且 EC 可剔除低效、非审慎或不再需要用于提供受监管服务的资产成本。[ST IBR 指引](https://www.st.gov.my/sites/default/files/2026-02/REGULATORY_IMPLEMENTATION_GUIDELINES.pdf)

2. **如果低负荷造成的是“系统性销量低于预测”，TNB 的输电、配电、Grid System Operator 和 Single Buyer Operations 受 Revenue-Cap 保护，年度调整可对 forecast vs actual sales 的差异作补偿。** 指引列明上述 RBE 属于 Revenue-Cap，实际收入偏离获准收入时，Allowed Average Tariff 会通过年度 Revenue-Cap Adjustment 修正。因此，数据中心低于承诺值若体现在全系统销售量低于 RP4 预测，TNB 在获监管确认后可通过这一普遍机制回收部分网络收入缺口。[ST IBR 指引](https://www.st.gov.my/sites/default/files/2026-02/REGULATORY_IMPLEMENTATION_GUIDELINES.pdf)

3. **如果问题是“为个别数据中心/特定园区建设的资产被闲置”，更强的回收路径不是普遍电价，而是客户侧收费和合同保护。** TNB 的连接收费制度对高压用户采用 first-principle 计算：若 15 年供电收入现值低于项目成本，消费者需支付 connection charge；高压用户还可能承担 PMMC = 2% × (P-C)，最长 5 年；若供电后 5 年内未取电或停止取电，还适用 LD = (60-n) × (P-C) / 120。若按 refundable connection charge 机制，消费者须在投运后 5/6 年内达到 75% declared Maximum Demand，否则 TNB 可没收或追索 RCC。[TNB Connection Charge](https://www.mytnb.com.my/business/get-electricity/connection-charge)

4. **政策方向更偏向“user pays”，不是把数据中心风险转嫁给居民或一般用户。** 政府在 Dewan Negara 表示，截至 2025 年 9 月，TNB 与数据中心通过 ESA 已锁定超过 7,000 MW 承诺需求；同时政府已引入 “user pays” 做法，要求数据中心开发商承担加强电力和水务基础设施的额外成本，并设置 UHV tariff category。[Bernama, 2025-12-08](https://web11.bernama.com/en/region/news.php?id=2500044)

5. **RP4 contingent capex 仍是关键不确定项。** TNB 披露 RP4 capex 合计约 RM42.82 billion，其中 base capex RM26.55 billion、contingent capex RM16.27 billion，WACC/allowed return 为 7.3%。TNB 投资者材料称 contingent capex 用于 security of supply、data centres/industries demand growth 和 energy transition；项目清单已获 EC 预批准、触发后实施，但 “recovery mechanism is being finalised”。这意味着尚不能把 contingent capex 解读成已存在的自动搁浅成本补偿机制。[TNB IAR 2024 Sustainability Statement](https://www.tnb.com.my/assets/annual_report/TNB_IAR_2024_Sustainability_Statement.pdf); [TNB Investor Deck Dec 2025](https://www.tnb.com.my/assets/conference_materials/TNB_Investor_Deck_December_2025.pdf)

## 对“实际负荷显著低于承诺值”的判断

**场景 A：数据中心已签 ESA，但尚未触发具体 contingent capex。**  
若没有达到 TNB/EC 预设触发条件，原则上相关 contingent capex 不应进入执行和 RAB。此时 TNB 没有“因未发生负荷而补偿未投项目”的基础，风险主要留在项目排队、ESA 条款和连接申请管理。

**场景 B：项目触发、TNB 已建 customer-specific 连接资产，但客户用电爬坡不足。**  
TNB 的第一回收路径应是 connection charge、PMMC/GMMC、RCC forfeiture/claim、LD、deposit、maximum-demand/capacity/network charges，以及 ESA 或 special supply contract 中的条款。若这些资产可转供其他用户或并入通用网络，剩余成本可能继续在 RAB 中获得回收；若资产明显过度专用且未被审慎论证，EC 有权在 ex post prudency review 中剔除。

**场景 C：多个数据中心低负荷导致系统销量低于 RP4 预测。**  
Transmission、Distribution 等 Revenue-Cap RBE 可通过 annual Revenue-Cap Adjustment 对销售量差异进行收入 true-up。这是“全系统收入帽”的监管补偿，不是“数据中心违约赔偿”。最终是否、何时、向哪些用户反映，仍取决于 EC/Minister 批准后的 tariff schedule/annual adjustment。

**场景 D：已投的 contingent capex 因数据中心低负荷变成搁浅资产。**  
这是灰区。TNB 可主张：项目已由 EC 预批准、触发事件发生、投资在当时基于 ESA/declared Maximum Demand 属于审慎决策，因此应进入 RAB 并获得 7.3% regulated return。但反方风险是：如果 EC 认为建设时点、规模或替代方案不审慎，或成本可由特定用户承担，则可能要求客户侧回收、递延、调整或剔除部分 RAB。

## 给日报总编的可用表述

在 RP4 下，TNB 对“数据中心低于承诺负荷导致搁浅电网投资”的保护是**有条件、受监管、分层回收**，而非自动电价补偿。输配电和 GSO/SBO 的 Revenue-Cap 机制能对全系统销量偏差提供收入稳定；审慎且获批的资产可通过 RAB、折旧和 7.3% regulated return 回收。但客户特定连接资产优先由数据中心通过 connection charge、PMMC/GMMC、RCC、LD 和 UHV/user-pays tariff 承担。若数据中心实际负荷显著低于 ESA/declared Maximum Demand，TNB 能否把剩余成本转入一般电价，关键取决于 EC 是否认定该 capex 在投资当时审慎、必要、已触发、且无法由特定用户承担。

## 后续核查项

1. 向 TNB/EC 确认 RP4 contingent capex 的最终 recovery mechanism 是否已发布，以及是否包含 data-centre-specific minimum demand、take-or-pay、or stranded asset charge。
2. 抽样核查数据中心 ESA 或 Section 29 special supply contract 是否含 minimum demand、capacity reservation fee、termination compensation 或 ramp-up milestone。
3. 区分 dedicated assets 与 shared network assets：前者更可能由客户承担，后者更可能进入 RAB/revenue-cap true-up。
4. 追踪 2026 年实际 data centre load 与 >7,000 MW ESA 承诺之间的落差；若实际负荷仍低，市场应关注 EC 在下一次 annual adjustment 或 RP5 中的 prudency treatment。

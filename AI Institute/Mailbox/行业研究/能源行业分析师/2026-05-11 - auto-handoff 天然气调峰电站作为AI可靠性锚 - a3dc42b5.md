# PPA Pricing and Utility Valuation Effects When Hyperscale Data Centers Bind to Gas Peakers

Anchor date: 2026-05-11

## Prioritised Conclusions

1. **The PPA is shifting from an energy procurement contract to an energy-infrastructure contract for compute availability.** A traditional renewable corporate PPA is mostly priced as a fixed `$/MWh` product delivering energy and RECs. Once a data center is deeply tied to natural gas Peaker Plants or CCGTs, the price stack becomes capacity reservation plus fuel/heat-rate pass-through, start and availability penalties, emissions/CCS costs, and private-boundary interconnection costs. Economically, it looks closer to a gas turbine tolling agreement or capacity service contract than a plain corporate PPA.

2. **The “private energy boundary” improves certainty but makes risk explicit.** In its 2026 update, the IEA projects global data center electricity use rising from about 485 TWh in 2025 to about 950 TWh in 2030. Because U.S. data center developers face slow grid connections, they are advancing onsite gas-fired power. The IEA estimates that reliable onsite supply for critical and variable data center load requires 30%-70% overbuild versus demand, and that about 15-27 GW of onsite natural gas may power data centers by 2030, mostly in the United States.[IEA](https://www.iea.org/reports/key-questions-on-energy-and-ai/executive-summary)

3. **Gas-backed PPA pricing should not be read from LCOE alone; it prices insurance value for deliverable power.** Lazard’s 2025 LCOE shows utility-scale solar at about $38-$78/MWh, onshore wind at about $37-$86/MWh, gas peaking at about $149-$251/MWh, and gas combined cycle at about $48-$109/MWh. But AI data centers buy 24/7 high-reliability load service, so they can rationally pay a premium for capacity, start speed, redundancy and delivery risk transfer.[Lazard](https://www.lazard.com/media/uounhon4/lazards-lcoeplus-june-2025.pdf)

4. **For traditional utility valuation, this is not a blanket positive; it splits “recoverable load growth” from “bypassed load.”** If the large customer signs a full-cost-recovery utility agreement, new generation and transmission can enter rate base and raise EPS visibility. If the data center and generator form a behind-the-meter private loop, the utility may only receive standby, transmission or interconnection revenue, while losing retail load growth and facing cost-allocation disputes. The IEA also notes that AI demand has not produced a broad valuation uplift for the energy sector; the clearer beneficiaries are gas turbine and electrical equipment manufacturers, some nuclear companies and energy startups.[IEA](https://www.iea.org/reports/key-questions-on-energy-and-ai/executive-summary)

## How the PPA Pricing Model Changes

| Dimension | Traditional corporate renewable PPA | Deep data center-gas Peaker/CCGT PPA |
|---|---|---|
| Pricing core | Fixed or escalated `$/MWh`, settled on generation | Capacity reservation in `$/kW-month` or `$/MW-day` plus delivered energy in `$/MWh` |
| Energy cost | Developer bears resource variability; buyer receives energy/RECs | Buyer often bears Henry Hub or regional basis fuel exposure, converted through a heat rate |
| Availability | Mostly generation profile, shape risk and settlement node | 99.9%+ supply, N+1/N+2 redundancy, start time and outage damages |
| Market risk | Merchant tail, basis, curtailment, REC price | Fuel price, turbine delivery, capacity market, emissions permits, air-quality permits |
| Contract nature | Purchase of energy and clean attributes | Purchase of power capacity, fuel conversion, reliability and boundary control |
| Accounting/credit | Long-term offtake supports project finance | More infrastructure-like, requiring parent guarantees, collateral, minimum take or take-or-pay |

A simplified all-in pricing framework is:

`All-in PPA price = capacity payment + fuel cost + variable O&M + start cost + emissions/CCS cost + grid/interconnection cost + availability risk premium - by-product revenue`

The fuel component can be approximated as:

`Fuel cost $/MWh = gas price $/MMBtu × heat rate MMBtu/MWh`

Using Lazard’s $3.45/MMBtu reference fuel price, a 6.8 MMBtu/MWh CCGT implies about $23/MWh of fuel cost, while a 10.5 MMBtu/MWh simple-cycle peaker implies about $36/MWh. If Henry Hub rises to $4.60/MMBtu, those figures become about $31/MWh and $48/MWh. In its April STEO, the EIA forecasts U.S. LNG exports of 17.0 Bcf/d in 2026 and 18.6 Bcf/d in 2027, both above the 2025 record of 15.1 Bcf/d, meaning data center gas burn will compete with the LNG cycle for marginal supply.[EIA](https://www.eia.gov/outlooks/steo/report/natgas.php)

## Specific Numbers and Cases

## Impact on Utility Valuation Logic

**1. Valuation shifts from “kWh growth” to “recoverable capital growth.”**  
The core valuation anchors for a regulated utility are rate base, allowed ROE, equity layer and regulatory lag. Data center load should only be capitalised into higher EPS CAGR when it is backed by full-cost service agreements, minimum bills, take-or-pay commitments, exit damages and collateral. S&P Global/RRA forecasts $1.295 trillion of 2026-2030 capex for 46 U.S. energy utilities and states that regulatory outcomes are central to converting capex into rate base and earnings growth.[S&P Global](https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/research/2026/04/surging-energy-demand-puts-us-utility-capex-forecast-near-1-3t-in-2026-30)

**2. The private energy boundary narrows the natural monopoly boundary.**  
If the data center brings gas turbines, batteries, microgrid controls and dedicated lines inside its private boundary, the utility value pool shrinks from full retail energy plus network revenue to interconnection, standby, dispatch and partial transmission services. FERC has required PJM to clarify co-located load rules, and in April 2026 it partially accepted and partially rejected PJM’s compliance proposal, showing that cost causation for behind-the-meter large load remains unsettled.[FERC](https://www.ferc.gov/news-events/news/fact-sheet-ferc-directs-nations-largest-grid-operator-create-new-rules-embrace), [Van Ness Feldman](https://www.vnf.com/ferc-provides-further-guidance-on-co-located-load-interconnection)

**3. Cost-shifting disputes will affect allowed ROE and valuation multiples.**  
When private PPAs reduce system load but still require grid backup, ordinary customers may be left with residual fixed costs. When a utility builds new generation and transmission for data centers, regulators will demand proof of cost causation. Entergy-Meta’s narrative is built around Meta paying its own costs and delivering about $2.65 billion of customer benefits over 20 years. Terms like these are becoming a prerequisite for utility valuation upside, not an optional positive.[Entergy](https://www.entergy.com/news/entergy-louisiana-announces-a-new-agreement-with-meta-that-will-deliver-an-additional-2b-in-customer-savings)

**4. The leverage is more direct for unregulated generators and the equipment chain.**  
PJM capacity prices reached the FERC-approved cap in both the 2026/2027 and 2027/2028 delivery years, and new supply is lagging forecast demand. Owners of available gas, nuclear, hydro or fast-uprateable capacity have stronger bargaining power than ordinary wires-only utilities. Gas turbines, transformers, switchgear and gas infrastructure are also more direct beneficiaries.

## Follow-Up Items for the Thematic Researcher

1. **Contract disclosure:** Track whether the hyperscaler bears fuel, capacity, emissions, interconnection and exit-damage risk; look for minimum take, parent guarantees and collateral posting.
2. **Regulatory filings:** Prioritise LPSC, PUCT, PJM/FERC co-location, AEP/Ohio large-load tariffs and Dominion/Virginia data center tariffs.
3. **Capacity price anchors:** PJM, MISO, ERCOT ancillary services and power forwards can serve as tradable references for the reliability premium in private PPAs.
4. **Gas bottlenecks:** Every 10 GW of additional data center power demand, if served at a 90% load factor by 6.8 MMBtu/MWh CCGTs, adds about 1.4-1.6 Bcf/d of gas demand; this is directionally consistent with S&P Global Ratings’ estimate that data centers could add 3-6 Bcf/d of U.S. gas demand by 2030.[S&P Global Ratings](https://www.spglobal.com/ratings/es/regulatory/article/-/view/sourceId/13290987)
5. **Valuation screen:** Award a premium to regulated utilities only when they have regulatory approval, cost isolation, customer credit protection and construction-period financing capacity. For IPPs and the equipment chain, the premium should depend on available capacity, delivery lead times and long-term tolling/PPA coverage.

## Actionable View

For equity and thematic expression, **data center power growth is not a generic utility beta; it is a boundary-redrawing trade**. Regulated utilities deserve re-rating only when they can convert private load into recoverable rate base while isolating ordinary customers from risk. Otherwise, affordability pressure, stranded-asset risk and regulatory pushback can cap multiples. Dispatchable generation, gas turbines, grid equipment, gas transmission and independent generators with long-term tolling or PPA coverage capture the scarcity rent from the private energy boundary more directly.

# 超大规模数据中心绑定燃气调峰电站后的 PPA 定价与公用事业估值影响

锚定日期：2026-05-11

## 优先结论

1. **PPA 正在从“电量采购合同”变成“算力可用性的能源基础设施合约”。** 传统可再生 PPA 主要定价为固定 `$/MWh`、按发电量交付电能与 RECs；数据中心与天然气 Peaker Plants 或 CCGT 深度绑定后，价格核心转为“容量预留费 + 燃料/热耗联动 + 启停与可用率罚则 + 排放/CCS 成本 + 私有边界接入成本”。这更接近燃机 tolling agreement 或容量服务合约，而不是普通 corporate PPA。

2. **“私有能源边界”提高了确定性，也把风险显性化。** IEA 在 2026 年更新中预计全球数据中心用电量从 2025 年约 485 TWh 增至 2030 年约 950 TWh；美国数据中心开发商受制于电网接入速度，正在推进现场天然气发电。IEA 估算，可靠满足关键且波动的数据中心负荷需要现场装机相对需求超配 30%-70%，到 2030 年约 15-27 GW 现场天然气可能服务数据中心，主要在美国。[IEA](https://www.iea.org/reports/key-questions-on-energy-and-ai/executive-summary)

3. **燃气调峰资产的 PPA 定价不再看单一 LCOE，而看“可用电力的保险价值”。** Lazard 2025 LCOE 显示，公用事业级太阳能约 $38-$78/MWh，陆上风电约 $37-$86/MWh，燃气调峰约 $149-$251/MWh，燃气联合循环约 $48-$109/MWh；但 AI 数据中心购买的是 24/7 高可靠负荷服务，因而愿意为容量、启动速度、冗余与交付风险支付溢价。[Lazard](https://www.lazard.com/media/uounhon4/lazards-lcoeplus-june-2025.pdf)

4. **对传统公用事业估值不是全面利好，而是“监管可回收负荷增长”与“旁路负荷流失”的分化。** 若大客户通过公用事业体系签订全成本回收协议，新增电源和输电可进入 rate base，提升 EPS 可见度；若数据中心与电厂形成 behind-the-meter 私有闭环，公用事业可能只获得备用、输电或互联费用，失去零售电量增长，并面对成本分摊争议。IEA 也提示，AI 需求并未给能源板块带来普遍估值抬升，受益更集中于燃机、电气设备、部分核电和能源初创公司。[IEA](https://www.iea.org/reports/key-questions-on-energy-and-ai/executive-summary)

## PPA 定价模型如何变化

| 维度 | 传统企业可再生 PPA | 数据中心-燃气 Peaker/CCGT 深度绑定 PPA |
|---|---|---|
| 定价核心 | 固定或阶梯式 `$/MWh`，按发电量结算 | 容量预留 `$/kW-month` 或 `$/MW-day` + 实际电量 `$/MWh` |
| 能源成本 | 开发商承担资源波动，买方拿电量/RECs | 买方常承担 Henry Hub 或区域基差联动燃料成本，按 heat rate 折算 |
| 可用性 | 以发电曲线、形状风险和结算节点为主 | 以 99.9%+ 供电、N+1/N+2 冗余、启动时间和停机赔偿为主 |
| 市场风险 | merchant tail、基差、弃电、REC 价格 | 燃料价格、燃机交付周期、容量市场、排放许可、空气质量许可 |
| 合同性质 | 电能与绿色属性采购 | 电力容量、燃料转化、可靠性和边界控制权采购 |
| 会计/信用 | 长期 offtake 支撑项目融资 | 更像基础设施融资，要求母公司担保、抵押、最低用量或 take-or-pay |

可用的简化定价框架：

`全包 PPA 价格 = 容量费 + 燃料费 + 变动 O&M + 启停费 + 排放/CCS 费 + 电网/互联费 + 可用率风险溢价 - 副产品收入`

其中燃料费可近似为：

`燃料费 $/MWh = 天然气价格 $/MMBtu × 热耗 MMBtu/MWh`

以 Lazard 使用的 $3.45/MMBtu 基准燃料价为例：6.8 MMBtu/MWh 的 CCGT 燃料成本约 $23/MWh；10.5 MMBtu/MWh 的简单循环 Peaker 约 $36/MWh。若 Henry Hub 上行到 $4.60/MMBtu，对应约 $31/MWh 与 $48/MWh。EIA 4 月 STEO 预计美国 LNG 出口 2026 年为 17.0 Bcf/d、2027 年为 18.6 Bcf/d，高于 2025 年 15.1 Bcf/d 的纪录，这意味着数据中心燃气需求会与 LNG 周期共同竞争边际气源。[EIA](https://www.eia.gov/outlooks/steo/report/natgas.php)

## 对公用事业估值逻辑的影响

**1. 估值从“kWh 增长”转为“可回收资本增长”。**  
传统公用事业估值锚是受监管 rate base、允许 ROE、权益层比例和监管时滞。数据中心负荷只有在签订全成本服务协议、最低账单、take-or-pay、退出赔偿和抵押担保后，才应被资本化为更高 EPS CAGR。S&P Global/RRA 预计 46 家美国能源公用事业 2026-2030 年 capex 达 $1.295 trillion，并指出监管结果是 capex 转化为 rate base 与盈利增长的关键。[S&P Global](https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/research/2026/04/surging-energy-demand-puts-us-utility-capex-forecast-near-1-3t-in-2026-30)

**2. 私有能源边界会压缩公用事业的天然垄断边界。**  
如果数据中心把燃机、电池、微电网控制系统和专线输电纳入私有边界，公用事业的价值池从“全电量零售 + 网络收益”缩小为“互联、备用、调度与部分输电”。FERC 已要求 PJM 建立更清晰的 co-located load 规则，2026 年 4 月又部分接受/部分驳回 PJM 合规方案，说明 behind-the-meter 大负荷的成本归因仍未完全稳定。[FERC](https://www.ferc.gov/news-events/news/fact-sheet-ferc-directs-nations-largest-grid-operator-create-new-rules-embrace)、[Van Ness Feldman](https://www.vnf.com/ferc-provides-further-guidance-on-co-located-load-interconnection)

**3. 成本转嫁争议会影响允许 ROE 与估值倍数。**  
当私有 PPA 减少系统负荷但仍需要电网备用，普通客户可能承担剩余固定成本；当公用事业为数据中心建设新电源与输电，监管机构会要求“成本因果”证明。Entergy-Meta 的叙事重点就是 Meta 支付自身成本，并预计带来 20 年 $2.65 billion 客户收益；这类条款会成为公用事业获得估值溢价的必要条件，而不是锦上添花。[Entergy](https://www.entergy.com/news/entergy-louisiana-announces-a-new-agreement-with-meta-that-will-deliver-an-additional-2b-in-customer-savings)

**4. 非受监管发电商与设备链条的杠杆更直接。**  
PJM 容量价格已在 2026/2027 和 2027/2028 两个交付年触及 FERC 认可上限，且新增供给落后于预测需求。拥有可用燃气、核电、水电或可快速扩容的独立发电商，议价能力强于普通 wires-only 公用事业；燃机、变压器、开关设备和燃气基础设施企业也更直接受益。

## 给主题研究员的跟踪项

1. **合同披露：** 关注 hyperscaler 是否承担燃料、容量、排放、互联与退出赔偿；有无最低用量、母公司担保和 collateral posting。
2. **监管文件：** 重点看 LPSC、PUCT、PJM/FERC co-location、AEP/Ohio 大负荷电价、Dominion/Virginia 数据中心 tariff。
3. **容量价格锚：** PJM、MISO、ERCOT 备用服务和电力期货曲线可作为私有 PPA 可靠性溢价的可交易参照。
4. **燃气瓶颈：** 数据中心电力需求每新增 10 GW，若以 90% 负荷率和 6.8 MMBtu/MWh CCGT 供电，约增加 1.4-1.6 Bcf/d 天然气需求；这与 S&P Global Ratings 对 2030 年数据中心额外 3-6 Bcf/d 天然气需求的估计同向。[S&P Global Ratings](https://www.spglobal.com/ratings/es/regulatory/article/-/view/sourceId/13290987)
5. **估值筛选：** 给 regulated utilities 的溢价应只给“有监管批准、成本隔离、客户信用保护、建设期融资能力”的公司；给 IPPs/设备链的溢价则取决于可用容量、交付周期和长期服务合同覆盖率。

## 可执行判断

对股票/主题表达而言，**“数据中心用电增长”不是单一公用事业 beta，而是一个边界重画交易**：受监管公用事业只有在把私有负荷转化为可回收 rate base 且隔离普通客户风险时才应重估；否则估值可能被 affordability、stranded asset 和监管回撤压制。相反，可调度电源、燃机设备、电网设备、燃气管输和具备可签长期 tolling/PPA 的独立发电资产，更直接捕获“私有能源边界”的 scarcity rent。

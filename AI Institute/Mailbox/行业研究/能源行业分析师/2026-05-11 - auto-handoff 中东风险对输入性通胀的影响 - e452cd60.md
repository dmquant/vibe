# Energy Shock Estimate: Strait of Hormuz Closure Beyond One Month, Brent Above USD 120/bbl

**Date anchor: 2026-05-11.** All references to “today,” “previous trading day,” and “this week” are resolved against this date. No readable upstream deliverable file was found in the workspace; this note is generated directly from public sources and the task brief.

## Prioritised Conclusions

1. **China PPI: if Brent holds in the USD 120-130/bbl range for more than one month, the shock is large enough to lift China PPI by roughly 1.4-2.7 percentage points versus a no-blockade counterfactual, and could push PPI toward about +2.0%-3.2% year on year over the next 1-2 quarters.** This range uses an empirical pass-through of 0.35-0.50 percentage points on China PPI for each 10% oil-price increase; Invesco estimates a 10% oil rise adds about 0.5 pp to PPI over 12 months, while Caixin cites historical estimates of 0.3-0.4 pp. The shock is concentrated in oil and gas extraction, refining, chemicals, rubber and plastics, chemical fibers, and logistics. Machinery, autos, and consumer goods are more likely to absorb part of the shock through margin compression.

2. **RMB purchasing power: this is a combined “imported oil price + dollar invoicing + terms-of-trade” shock, not a pure CPI shock.** Using Brent rising from a USD 85/bbl no-blockade counterfactual to USD 120/130 and USD/CNY at 6.8502, the RMB cost of one barrel rises from about RMB 582/bbl to RMB 822/891/bbl. RMB oil purchasing power falls by about 29%/35%. If the RMB also depreciates by 2%, the purchasing-power loss widens to about 31%/36% in the USD 120/130 scenarios.

3. **FX cash-flow pressure is quantifiable but not immediately destabilising: every USD 10/bbl rise in oil adds about USD 42 billion to China’s annual crude-import bill, or about USD 3.5 billion over 30 days.** Based on 2025 crude imports of about 11.55 million b/d, Brent at USD 120/130 versus USD 85 implies an extra import cost of about USD 12.1/15.6 billion per 30 days, or USD 148/190 billion if sustained for a full year. Relative to USD 3.4105 trillion in FX reserves at end-April 2026, the one-month hit is about 0.36%/0.46%; relative to the IMF’s estimated 2025 current-account surplus of 3.3% of GDP, a full-year shock would consume a meaningful part of the external buffer.

4. **More than one month is the key threshold: inventories, discounted Russian/Iranian barrels, China’s refined-product price cap, and substitution from coal power and renewables can cushion the first phase; beyond one month, refinery runs, chemical feedstocks, and LNG procurement transmit the shock into real production costs.** EIA estimates that 20.9 million b/d of oil and products transited Hormuz in 1H25, with limited bypass capacity; Invesco estimates that about 30% of China’s crude imports and about 6% of its gas imports transit the strait.

## RMB Purchasing Power and FX Pressure

**The key purchasing-power channel is how much dollar oil each unit of RMB can buy.** At USD/CNY 6.8502, USD 85/bbl equals about RMB 582/bbl; USD 120/bbl equals about RMB 822/bbl; USD 130/bbl equals about RMB 891/bbl. Even with no FX move, RMB purchasing power against imported crude falls by about 29%/35%. If the CNY weakens another 2% in the FX strategist’s scenario because of safe-haven USD demand, worsening terms of trade, or capital-flow pressure, the RMB oil price mechanically rises another 2%, and the purchasing-power loss widens to about 31%/36%.

**The FX problem is not a one-month crisis; it is persistent erosion.** The extra 30-day import bill at USD 120/130 versus USD 85 is about USD 12.1/15.6 billion, only about 0.36%/0.46% of the USD 3.4105 trillion FX reserves reported for end-April. But if sustained for 12 months, the extra USD 148/190 billion would materially erode China’s current-account surplus. The IMF estimates China’s current-account surplus at 3.3% of GDP in 2025 and forecasts 3.1% in 2026; the longer the oil shock lasts, the more it weakens the combination of a strong external balance and low inflation that has supported the RMB.

## Buffers and Tail Risks

- **Buffers:** China has diversified import sources; Russian oil and some sanctioned barrels may still arrive at discounts; inventories and strategic stocks can reduce spot buying during the first month; the NDRC refined-product mechanism says gasoline and diesel retail prices should not be raised, or should be raised only modestly, when international oil exceeds USD 130/bbl, with fiscal subsidies available to stabilise refinery supply; coal power and renewables reduce direct oil-price pass-through into the power system.
- **Tail risks:** If the closure causes physical shortage rather than only a risk premium, refiners may have to cut runs, tightening diesel, jet fuel, chemical feedstocks, and LPG. Qatar LNG is an important Chinese supply source: China imported 19.44 million tonnes of LNG from Qatar in 2025, equal to 28.4% of China’s total LNG imports. A prolonged Hormuz disruption would magnify gas-price and coastal industrial fuel-cost pressure.
- **Policy read-through:** For FX strategy, RMB pressure is more likely to show up through terms of trade and importer dollar demand than through a runaway household CPI shock. If Brent stays above USD 120 but does not break or stay above USD 130 for long, the base case is higher PPI, muted CPI, and gradual RMB pressure. If Brent reaches USD 150 and physical transit disruption persists, the scenario shifts to a faster PPI spike and a larger RMB risk premium.

## 对 PPI 的冲击路径

**当前价格背景已经具备上游推升特征。** NBS 公布的 2026 年 3 月 PPI 同比由 2 月的 -0.9% 转为 +0.5%，环比 +1.0%；生产资料价格同比 +1.0%，对总 PPI 拉动约 0.81 个百分点。行业上，石油和天然气开采 PPI 环比 +15.8%、同比 +5.2%，石油煤炭及其他燃料加工环比 +5.8%，化学原料和化学制品环比 +3.6%。4 月制造业 PMI 中，主要原材料购进价格指数仍在 63.7，出厂价格指数 55.1，说明成本推升仍在扩散。

| 渠道 | 一个月内 | 1-2 个季度 | 备注 |
|---|---:|---:|---|

| 炼化、成品油、芳烃、烯烃 | 强 | 强 | NDRC 可能限制终端汽柴油价格，但炼厂补贴/利润会吸收部分冲击 |
| 化工、化纤、橡塑、包装 | 中高 | 强 | 成本通过石脑油、LPG、甲醇、苯系物向中游传导 |

| 装备、汽车、耐用品 | 低 | 中 | 需求竞争限制出厂价上调，更多表现为利润率压缩 |

**测算结果：** 若 Brent 从 85 美元升到 120 美元，涨幅约 41%，按 0.35-0.50 个百分点/10% 的传导系数，PPI 额外上行约 1.4-2.1 个百分点；若升至 130 美元，涨幅约 53%，PPI 额外上行约 1.9-2.7 个百分点。若油价升至 150 美元并维持，PPI 上行风险扩大到约 2.7-3.8 个百分点。成品油限价会压低 CPI 和部分物流终端价格，但不会消除 PPI 上游和中游的成本压力。

## 对人民币购买力与外汇压力的含义

**人民币购买力的核心冲击在“每单位人民币可购买的美元油量”。** 在 USD/CNY 6.8502 下，85 美元/桶对应约 582 元/桶；120 美元/桶对应约 822 元/桶；130 美元/桶对应约 891 元/桶。也就是说，即使汇率不变，人民币对进口原油的购买力也分别下降约 29%/35%。若 FX 策略情景中 CNY 因避险美元、贸易条件恶化或资本流动压力再贬 2%，人民币油价还会机械性再上升 2%，购买力损失扩大到约 31%/36%。

**外汇压力不是“单月危机”，而是“持续性侵蚀”。** 120/130 美元油价相对 85 美元的额外 30 天进口账单约 121/156 亿美元，只占 4 月末外储 3.4105 万亿美元的约 0.36%/0.46%。但如果持续 12 个月，额外 1,480/1,900 亿美元会显著侵蚀中国经常账户顺差。IMF 估计中国 2025 年经常账户顺差为 3.3% GDP、2026 年为 3.1% GDP；因此油价冲击持续越久，人民币“强外部收支 + 低通胀”的组合越容易被削弱。

## 缓冲与尾部风险

- **缓冲项：** 中国进口来源分散，俄油和部分受制裁油源可能以折价供应；库存和战略储备可降低一个月内的现货采购量；NDRC 成品油机制在国际油价高于 130 美元/桶时原则上对汽柴油零售价“不提或少提”，并可用财政补贴稳定炼厂供应；煤电与新能源降低油价对电力系统的直接传导。
- **尾部风险：** 若封锁导致物理短缺而不只是风险溢价，炼厂可能被迫降负荷，柴油、航煤、化工原料和 LPG 供应趋紧；Qatar LNG 是中国 LNG 重要来源，2025 年中国从 Qatar 进口 LNG 19.44 百万吨，占 LNG 总进口 28.4%，霍尔木兹长期受阻会放大气价和沿海工业燃料成本压力。
- **政策含义：** 对 FX 策略而言，人民币压力更可能通过贸易条件和进口商购汇需求体现，而非通过居民 CPI 失控体现。若 Brent 维持 120 美元上方但不突破/长期维持 130 美元，PPI 上行明显、CPI 温和、人民币缓慢承压是基准；若 Brent 升至 150 美元且通行中断持续，才进入 PPI 快速上冲与人民币风险溢价同步扩大的情景。

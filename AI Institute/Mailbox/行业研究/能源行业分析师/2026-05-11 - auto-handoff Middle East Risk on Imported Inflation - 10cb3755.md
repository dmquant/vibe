# Impact Analysis of Strait of Hormuz Blockade on China's Economy (2026-05-11)

## Executive Summary
A blockade of the Strait of Hormuz lasting over a month with Brent crude sustained above $120/bbl would represent a systemic shock to the Chinese economy.
1.  **PPI Impact:** Estimated to drive a **3.5 - 5.5 percentage point (pp)** increase in China's Producer Price Index (PPI).
2.  **RMB Purchasing Power:** Surging energy import costs would necessitate an additional **~$170 billion** in annual payments, significantly eroding the RMB's international purchasing power and narrowing the trade surplus.
3.  **Supply Resilience:** While China's Strategic Petroleum Reserve (SPR) can cover ~120 days of disruption, the price pass-through is unavoidable, placing extreme pressure on manufacturing margins.

---

## 1. Deep Dive: PPI (Producer Price Index) Impact
Analysis based on the National Bureau of Statistics (NBS) 2026 base-year weights and input-output modeling:

### 1.1 Direct Contribution
*   **Weighting:** The combined weight of "Extraction of Petroleum and Natural Gas" and "Processing of Petroleum, Coal, and Other Fuels" in the PPI basket is approximately **6.5% - 7.0%**.
*   **Price Pass-through:** If Brent crude jumps from a baseline of $80/bbl to $120/bbl (a 50% increase), the direct contribution from petroleum-related sectors alone would lift the PPI by **3.25 - 3.5 pp**.

### 1.2 Indirect and Secondary Effects
*   **Chemical Chain:** The "Manufacturing of Chemical Raw Materials and Products" (weighting ~8-10%) is highly sensitive to crude prices, likely contributing an additional 1.0 - 1.5 pp.
*   **Logistics Costs:** Rising fuel costs in transportation and warehousing will gradually filter through the secondary industry price chain.
*   **Consolidated Estimate:** Accounting for secondary transmission effects, the total PPI surge is expected to be around **5.25 pp**.

| Sector Category | PPI Weight (Est.) | Contribution to PPI (+50% Oil Price) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Oil/Gas Extraction | 1.2% | +0.6 pp |
| Oil Refining/Fuel Processing | 5.8% | +2.9 pp |
| Chemical Raw Materials | 9.0% | +1.2 pp (Lagged) |
| **Total** | **16.0% (Related Chain)** | **~4.7 - 5.5 pp** |

---

## 2. RMB Purchasing Power & Terms of Trade
### 2.1 Import Expenditure Pressure
*   **Data Baseline:** China imports approximately 11.6 million barrels of crude oil per day.
*   **Cost Calculation:** A $40/bbl increase translates to an additional **$464 million** in daily expenditure, or **$14 billion** per month.
*   **Annualized Impact:** An additional ~$170 billion in annual costs would directly drain the trade surplus, putting depreciation pressure on the RMB and weakening its ability to purchase global commodities.

### 2.2 Domestic Purchasing Power (Inflationary Transmission)
*   **Imported Inflation:** PPI-to-CPI transmission typically occurs with a 3-6 month lag. While food prices may remain stable, the rise in industrial consumer goods and energy utilities will directly reduce household real purchasing power.
*   **Real Exchange Rate:** If the nominal exchange rate depreciates due to worsening terms of trade, the RMB's real purchasing power will suffer further erosion.

---

## 3. Risks and Strategic Recommendations
*   **Supply Disruption Risk:** Approximately 45-50% of China's crude imports transit the Strait of Hormuz. A blockade would force a draw-down of the SPR.
*   **Geopolitical Premium:** Even if the Strait reopens, a permanent geopolitical risk premium ($10-15/bbl) may become structural.
*   **Follow-up Items:** Monitor the share of non-USD (RMB-settled) energy trade with Russia and Saudi Arabia, which could partially offset the loss in purchasing power.

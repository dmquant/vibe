# Temu: Does 60% EU Growth Offset the Loss of U.S. De Minimis?

Date anchor: 2026-05-11, from local `date +%Y-%m-%d`. This response is for the next step in `analyst:altdata-analyst:topic_pitch_postclose`.

## Priority Conclusions

**No, not sufficiently.** I would treat 60% EU sales growth as a meaningful buffer for Temu overseas GMV, but not as a full replacement for the U.S. de minimis shock in the valuation model. There are three reasons:

1. **The scale only roughly offsets the base case and falls short in stress.** Using the Guardian-cited estimate of about **US$10bn** in Temu EU consumer sales as the base, 60% growth adds about **US$6.0bn** of incremental GMV. By comparison, ECDB/Statista data put Temu 2024 global GMV at **US$47.5bn**, with the U.S. at **32.6%**, implying about **US$15.5bn** of U.S. GMV. If U.S. GMV is impaired by 40% after de minimis removal, the loss is about **US$6.2bn**, so the EU increment only roughly bridges it. If Reuters/Sensor Tower’s reported **58%** decline in U.S. daily active users is used as the stress mapping, the U.S. loss is about **US$9.0bn**, and the EU increment covers only about **67%**.

2. **The margin quality is not equivalent, so GMV should not be swapped one-for-one.** The U.S. shock is not just fewer sales; it changes the fulfillment and tariff architecture from low-value China direct shipping to U.S. local warehouses, local sellers, and formal duty procedures. Axios reported that Temu switched the U.S. app/site to U.S.-warehouse goods, and the White House later extended the de minimis suspension globally. That raises inventory, fulfillment, compliance, and subsidy pressure. PDD’s 2025 revenue was **RMB431.8bn**, up **10%**, but cost of revenue rose **23%**, sales and marketing expense was **RMB125.3bn**, up **13%**, and operating profit fell **13%** to **RMB94.6bn**. That supports lower incremental margins during the overseas investment phase.

3. **The EU is entering its own de minimis-style tightening cycle.** Temu had **115.7mn** average monthly active users in the EU in H1 2025, so the scale-up is real. But the EU has issued preliminary DSA breach findings against Temu, with potential fines of up to **6%** of worldwide annual turnover. The EU has also decided to apply a temporary **€3/item** duty from **July 2026** on e-commerce parcels below **€150**, while pushing ahead with removal of the **€150** duty threshold and a later handling fee. If Temu EU AOV is roughly **US$20-25**, a **€3** charge alone equals roughly **13-16%** of order value, enough to absorb most contribution profit for a low-price platform unless a large share shifts to EU local warehouses and the cost is passed through successfully.

## How I Would Treat It in the Valuation Model

- **Temu U.S.:** Keep a structural discount. Cut 2026 U.S. GMV by **35-45%** in the base case and reduce normalized U.S. Temu contribution margin by **200-400bp** to reflect tariffs, inventory pull-forward, local fulfillment, and promotional subsidies.
- **Temu EU:** Recognize the revenue buffer from **60%** sales growth, but score it as **lower-quality growth**. Apply a **20-30%** regulatory/execution haircut to incremental EU cash flow and add the **€3/item** cost, or an equivalent price-elasticity hit, to direct-to-consumer parcels after **July 2026**.
- **PDD group level:** The China core and cash cushion still matter. PDD ended 2025 with cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments of **RMB422.3bn / US$60.4bn**, but that does not remove the Temu overseas valuation discount; it only lowers group-level financial risk.
- **Investment conclusion:** Do not raise the PDD ADR target price on “EU +60%” alone unless alt-data later shows that U.S. order GMV, AOV, and CAC have stabilized and that Temu’s EU local-warehouse mix is high enough to avoid the **€3/item** direct-parcel drag.

# Temu：EU 60% 增长能否抵消美国 de minimis 豁免损失？

日期锚点：2026-05-11（本机 `date +%Y-%m-%d`）。结论用于 `analyst:altdata-analyst:topic_pitch_postclose` 的后续建模。

## 优先结论

**不能充分抵消。** 我会把 EU 60% 销售增长作为 Temu 海外 GMV 的重要缓冲，但不会在估值模型里把它等同于美国 de minimis 损失的完全替代。原因有三点：

1. **规模上只能勉强对冲基准情景，压力情景明显不够。** 以 Guardian 引述的 Temu EU 消费者销售约 **US$10bn** 为基数，60% 增长带来约 **US$6.0bn** 增量 GMV；而 ECDB/Statista 口径下 Temu 2024 全球 GMV 为 **US$47.5bn**、美国占 **32.6%**，对应美国 GMV 约 **US$15.5bn**。若美国 GMV 因 de minimis 取消受损 40%，损失约 **US$6.2bn**，EU 增量仅接近打平；若按 Reuters/Sensor Tower 披露的美国日活 **-58%** 作为压力映射，损失约 **US$9.0bn**，EU 增量只覆盖约 **67%**。

2. **利润质量不同，不能按 GMV 一比一替代。** 美国冲击不是单纯“少卖一些”，而是供应链从中国直邮低值包裹转向美国本地仓/本地卖家和正式关税流程。Axios 报道 Temu 已把美国 App/网站切到美国仓商品，White House 后续又把 de minimis 暂停扩大到全球；这会抬高库存、履约、合规和价格补贴压力。PDD 2025 年收入 **RMB431.8bn**、同比 **+10%**，但成本收入 **+23%**、销售及营销费用 **RMB125.3bn**、同比 **+13%**，经营利润 **RMB94.6bn**、同比 **-13%**，说明海外增长阶段的增量利润率仍受压。

3. **EU 自身也在进入“类 de minimis 收紧”周期。** Temu H1 2025 EU 月活用户 **115.7mn**，规模确实在上台阶；但 EU 已对 Temu 发出 DSA 初步违规意见，最高罚款可达全球年营业额 **6%**；同时 EU 已决定自 **2026 年 7 月** 起对低于 **€150** 的直邮电商包裹征收 **€3/件** 临时关税，并推进取消 **€150** 免税门槛及后续处理费。若 Temu EU 订单 AOV 约 **US$20-25**，仅 **€3** 费用就相当于约 **13-16%** 的订单额，足以吃掉低价平台的多数贡献利润，除非大比例转入 EU 本地仓并成功转嫁给消费者。

## 我在估值模型中的处理

- **Temu 美国：** 维持结构性折价。把美国 GMV 2026 基准削减 **35-45%**，并将美国 Temu 正常化贡献利润率下调 **200-400bp**，反映关税、库存前置、本地履约和促销补贴。
- **Temu EU：** 承认 **60%** 销售增长的收入缓冲，但只给 **低质量增长** 权重。对 EU 增量现金流应用 **20-30%** 监管/执行折价，并在 **2026 年 7 月** 后的直邮包裹中加入 **€3/件** 成本或相应价格弹性损失。
- **PDD 集团层面：** 中国主站和现金缓冲仍能托底。PDD 2025 年末现金、现金等价物和短期投资为 **RMB422.3bn / US$60.4bn**，但这不改变 Temu 海外分部的估值折价；它只是降低集团层面的财务风险。
- **投资结论：** 对 PDD ADR 的目标价不应因 “EU +60%” 单项上调，除非后续另类数据证明美国订单 GMV、AOV 和 CAC 已稳定，且 EU 本地仓占比足以规避 **€3/件** 直邮冲击。

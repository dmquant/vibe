## 1. Bottom Line Up Front

1. **Tolerance has tightened at the margin, but regulators remain in a "verbal guidance + targeted cooling" stage — not a 2015-style systemic crackdown.** Between mid-April and early May 2026, the regulatory response to the "mental-hospital stock-trading" meme and the high-frequency speculation in names like Dapu Micro (688778) closely tracks the **second** of the three classic phases observed in early Q4 2014–Q1 2015 ("verbal nudging → tool-based cooling → genuine deleveraging") — but has not yet escalated to phase three.
2. **Key signals**: more frequent "watchlist + verbal warning" actions by the exchanges, a de-facto lower bar for Longhubang disclosure, broker-level differentiation in margin ratios and collateral haircuts. **What is absent**: across-the-board margin hikes, suspension of refinancing/securities lending, and any CSRC special-press-conference-style framing. 3. **Operating implications for the analyst/strategist**:
   - Treat the "mental-hospital" narrative as a **sentiment thermometer**, not a policy-turn signal. Its heat correlates closely with the weekly change in margin balances and with consecutive high-volume sessions.
   - On crowded names like Dapu Micro (heavy turnover, institutional cross-trades on Longhubang), watch in the **near term** (2–4 weeks) for trading halts and "verification suspensions"; the **medium-term** (one quarter) policy risk depends on whether margin balances breach prior highs and whether off-exchange financing makes the news.
   - The historical "warn-then-act" window is typically 4–8 weeks; we are now in week 3 — squarely in the zone where escalation becomes easy.

## 3. Comparison Against Past Early-Bull-Market Regulatory Cadence

### 3.1 Four comparable cycles

| Cycle | Bull-market start | First verbal warning | First tool-based cooling | Systemic tightening | Days from start |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2014–2015 | Jul 2014 | Nov 2014 state media "slow bull" | 2014-12-08 ChinaClear circular #149 (corporate-bond pledge rules) | Jun 2015 off-exchange financing crackdown | ~330 days |
| 2019 spring | late Jan 2019 | 2019-03-07 CSRC Q&A "rational view" | 2019-03-08 brokers raise margin ratios | Did not escalate (market cooled on its own) | ~40 days |
| Mid-2020 | Jun 2020 | 2020-07-06 CCTV "healthy bull" | 2020-07-09 reduction-rule guidance | Did not escalate | ~30 days |
| 2024-09 → 2025 | 2024-09-24 | Oct 2024 multi-outlet "rational" | Late Oct 2024 margin haircut differentiation | Did not escalate | ~30–45 days |
| **Current 2026** | Q1 2026 onset | Late Apr 2026 | Early May 2026 | **Has not escalated** | ~80–100 days |

### 3.2 Key observations

- **Playbook**: in early bull markets regulators consistently follow "verbal warning (T) → name-level tooling (T+5–15 days) → 4–8 weeks of watching → escalate if needed." The current cadence most resembles Q4 2014, but the **temperature is lower** than the 2014-12 ChinaClear circular #149 step.
- **Direction in which tolerance has tightened**: relative to 2019/2020/2024, the lag from narrative emergence to first tool-level response has **shortened** (about 2 weeks vs. 4–6 weeks previously), evidence that regulators' threshold for **social-media-driven, meme-form narratives** has fallen — this is the clearest evidence of marginal tightening.
- **Direction in which tolerance has not changed**: the three heavy-weapon tools — margin ratios, refinancing/securities lending, off-exchange-financing crackdown — remain unused, consistent with the "soft phase" before June 2015. No dedicated CSRC press conference, no name-and-shame, no investigation launches.

### 3.3 Lessons from past misreads

- The 2014-12-12 "margin inspection bulletin" was widely **misread as a top**; it produced only a one-week technical pullback before the rally extended into June 2015.
- The June 2015 off-exchange financing crackdown was the **real policy turn**, marked by (a) joint banking + securities regulator action, (b) named platforms (HOMS etc.), and (c) the historic margin balance peak of RMB 2.27 trn.
- **Lesson**: misreading "verbal + tooling" as "systemic crackdown" leads to early de-risking; misreading "systemic crackdown" as "verbal" leaves you trapped. The current pitfall to avoid is the former.

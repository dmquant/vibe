# Impact Analysis of Potential Default or Extension of Johor's 2 GW ESA

**Date:** May 10, 2026
**Prepared by:** Chief Strategist

## Executive Summary
A substantial default or large-scale extension (delay) of the 2 GW Electricity Supply Agreements (ESAs) in Johor would represent a structural shock to Malaysia's "AI Hub" narrative.

1. **Sovereign Rating Impact:** Near-term downgrade risk remains low (currently A-/BBB+ Stable), but the **window for a rating upgrade would effectively close** due to rising energy costs and stalled growth momentum.
2. **FDI Inflow Trends:** The impact would be severe. With over 50% of Johor’s 2025 FDI contributed by data centers (DCs), a bottleneck in the 2 GW core capacity would lead to a **structural slowdown in FDI**, potentially shifting investment flows to Thailand or Vietnam.
3. **The Grid Bottleneck:** The critical risk has shifted from "generation capacity" to **"Grid Delivery."** The 18-month interconnection cycle has become the single largest uncertainty for FDI realization.

---

## 1. Potential Impact on Sovereign Credit Ratings

As of May 2026, the perspectives of major rating agencies on Malaysia are as follows:

| Agency | Rating (Outlook) | Key Perspectives |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **S&P Global** | **A- (Stable)** | Energy shocks and weakening external metrics make a near-term upgrade highly unlikely. |
| **Moody's** | **A3 (Stable)** | Relies on DC and electronics investment to sustain 4.0%-4.5% GDP growth. |
| **Fitch** | **BBB+ (Stable)** | Focuses on fiscal consolidation; expects the deficit to narrow to 3.5% in 2026. |

**Negative Transmission Mechanisms:**
* **Growth Momentum Erosion:** Data centers are the primary growth engine for the 13th Malaysia Plan (2026-2030) under the MADANI Economy. A failure to deliver 2 GW would lead to direct downgrades in GDP growth forecasts.
* **Quasi-Fiscal Responsibilities:** Tenaga Nasional Berhad (TNB), a Government-Linked Company (GLC), has committed massive capex (~RM 42.8 billion for RP4) to support this 2 GW demand. An ESA default would jeopardize TNB’s investment recovery, increasing pressure on the government’s contingent liabilities.

## 2. Impact on Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) Trends

Johor has emerged as Southeast Asia's largest data center hub, recording RM 110.1 billion in FDI in 2025—the highest in the country.

* **Sector Re-rating Risk:** The market has shifted from "barbaric growth" to "prudent evaluation." An extension of the 2 GW ESAs would confirm the "grid bottleneck" as a reality, forcing investors into a "wait-and-see" mode.
* **Erosion of Cost Advantage:** Tariff hikes for Ultra High Voltage (UHV) users in early 2026 have already added US$15M–$20M in annual costs for large facilities. Further delays would significantly narrow Malaysia's "value-for-money" edge over Singapore and Indonesia.
* **Pivot to "AI First":** The federal government has already restricted approvals for non-AI data centers. Any volatility in the 2 GW ESAs would accelerate this filtering mechanism, potentially leading to an exit of FDI from traditional cloud providers.

---

## 3. Supporting Data and Key Indicators

| Indicator | 2026 Status / Forecast | Impact Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Total Johor DC Pipeline** | ~5.8 GW | **High** |
| **Core 2 GW ESA Progress** | Interconnection cycles extended to 18 months | **High** |
| **TNB Green Lane Pathway** | Timelines cut from 36 to 12 months (AI-only) | **Medium** |
| **Renewable Energy Gap** | 4 GW shortfall projected by 2030 | **Very High** |

---

## 4. Recommended Follow-up Items
1. **Monitor UEM Lestra Solar Projects:** This 1 GW (phased to 2 GW) project is the core of Johor's green energy supply. Following China's removal of the 9% export tax rebate on PV products, the project’s IRR and timeline must be re-evaluated.
2. **JS-SEZ Agreement Execution:** Watch for details on cross-border electricity trading within the Johor-Singapore Special Economic Zone, which could serve as a potential "relief valve" for the 2 GW bottleneck.
3. **TNB Capex Audit:** Assess whether grid modernization progress can match the production peaks expected in 2027-2028.

# 柔佛 2 GW 供电协议 (ESA) 违约或展期对马来西亚的影响分析

**日期：** 2026年5月10日
**报告人：** 首席策略师

## 核心结论
若柔佛 2 GW 的电力供应协议 (ESA) 发生实质性违约或大规模展期（延期），将对马来西亚的“AI 中心”叙事产生结构性冲击。

1. **主权评级冲击：** 短期内评级下调风险较低（当前为 A-/BBB+ 稳定），但由于能源成本上升和增长动能受阻，**评级上调的“窗口期”将实质性关闭**。
2. **FDI 流入趋势：** 冲击显著。柔佛 2025 年 FDI 的 50% 以上由数据中心（DC）贡献。一旦 2 GW 核心增量出现供电瓶颈，**FDI 将出现结构性降速**，投资热点可能向泰国或越南转移。
3. **关键瓶颈：** 当前核心风险已从“发电容量”转向**“电网交付 (Grid Delivery)”**。18个月的接电周期已成为 FDI 落地最大的不确定性因素。

---

## 1. 对主权信用评级的潜在影响分析

截至 2026 年 5 月，主要评级机构对马来西亚的看法如下：

| 机构 | 评级 (展望) | 核心观点 |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **标准普尔 (S&P)** | **A- (稳定)** | 能源冲击和外部指标走弱使得短期内上调评级的可能性极低。 |
| **穆迪 (Moody's)** | **A3 (稳定)** | 依赖数据中心和电子产品的投资来支撑 4.0%-4.5% 的增长。 |
| **惠誉 (Fitch)** | **BBB+ (稳定)** | 关注财政整合，预计 2026 年财政赤字收窄至 3.5%。 |

**违约/展期的负面传导：**
* **增长动能受损：** 数据中心是马达尼经济 (MADANI Economy) 下 13 世纪马来西亚计划 (2026-2030) 的核心增长引擎。2 GW 的交付失败将直接导致 GDP 增长预测下调。
* **准财政责任：** 作为政府相关公司 (GLC)，国家能源公司 (TNB) 为支持这 2 GW 需求投入了巨额资本开支 (RP4 期内约 428 亿令吉)。如果 ESA 违约，TNB 的电网投资回收将面临风险，增加政府的或有负债压力。

## 2. 对 FDI 流入趋势的影响

柔佛州已成为东南亚最大的数据中心枢纽。2025 年柔佛录得 1101 亿令吉的 FDI，位居全国之首。

* **行业重估风险：** 市场已从“野蛮生长”转向“审慎评估”。2 GW ESA 的展期将验证“电网瓶颈”真实存在，迫使投资者进入“观望”模式。
* **成本优势侵蚀：** 2026 年初针对特高压 (UHV) 用户的电价上调已增加大型设施每年 1500 万至 2000 万美元的成本。若供电进一步延期，马来西亚相对于新加坡、印尼的“性价比”优势将大幅收窄。
* **转向“AI 优先”：** 联邦政府已在 2026 年限制非 AI 类数据中心的审批。2 GW ESA 的任何波动都将加速这种筛选机制，导致传统云服务商的 FDI 撤出。

2. **JS-SEZ 协议落地：** 关注柔佛-新加坡特别经济区关于跨境电力交易的细节，这可能是缓解 2 GW 瓶颈的潜在“泄洪口”。

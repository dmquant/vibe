# Services Inflation Stickiness and the Rate Path: Strategy Handoff

**Date anchor: 2026-05-14, from shell `date +%Y-%m-%d`**  
**Question: High-frequency data suggest services are far more resilient than goods. Is the current model underestimating the impact of sticky global services inflation on the rate path?**

## Prioritized Conclusions

1. **Yes, but the repricing should be regional.** Any model still assuming that 2026 disinflation will mainly come from goods mean reversion is underestimating the rate impact of sticky services inflation in the US and UK; the risk is smaller but still relevant in the euro area; China’s domestic services CPI remains mild and is not a direct policy constraint.
2. **Services are not uniformly outperforming goods globally.** Public PMI data show global manufacturing strengthened in April on stockbuilding and supply concerns, while services growth stayed soft. That does not weaken the inflation call: services input costs, wages and energy pass-through are still lifting service prices, which tend to fall more slowly than goods prices.
3. **The rate path should shift from “cuts this year” to “restrictive for longer, with local renewed hike risk.”** The OECD’s March Interim Outlook already projected US and UK policy rates to remain unchanged through the rest of 2026, with modest Q2 rate increases in the euro area and Australia; April data reinforce that direction.
4. **A-share strategy implication: reduce duration and foreign-liquidity-sensitive exposure; keep a dividend/resource plus domestic-services barbell.** Sticky global services inflation pressures high-valuation growth through real US yields, the dollar and risk appetite. China’s mild services inflation keeps domestic policy biased toward support, favoring cash-flow certainty, dividends, resource exposure and selective domestic services recovery.

## Model Adjustments

- **Raise the services-price factor weight.** In the DM rate-path model, move the combined “core services / wages / services PMI Prices” factor from a secondary explanatory variable to a core driver; falling goods prices should no longer mechanically imply core disinflation.
- **Add second-round-effect triggers.** If US `services less energy services` runs above **4%** annualized over three months, or ISM Services Prices stay above **65** for two consecutive months, cut the assumed probability of Fed easing this year and keep renewed hike repricing risk in the scenario set.
- **Separate real demand from stockbuilding.** April’s global manufacturing expansion has a clear precautionary-inventory component and should not be read as a clean goods-demand recovery. Services inflation stickiness is coming more from wages, energy, rent/lodging, airfares, healthcare, restaurants and enterprise services costs.
- **Keep China separate.** China’s services CPI at **0.9%** and official services PMI at **49.6** show no domestic services-inflation constraint; the A-share discount-rate shock mainly comes through offshore real yields and FX, not forced PBoC tightening.

## A-share Strategy Implications

| Portfolio area | Recommendation | Rationale |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Style | **Dividend/cash flow > high-valuation duration growth** | “Higher for longer” global rates compress the value of distant earnings; dividend assets benefit from visible cash flow. |
| Overweight sectors | Banks, telecom operators, utilities, coal, oil & petrochemicals, gold/copper chains, and transport companies with fuel-surcharge pass-through | Pricing power, dividends and resource cash flows are scarcer in a high-rate, energy-shock environment. |
| Selective exposure | Hotels, tourism, restaurant supply chains, healthcare services, and education/culture/entertainment leaders | China’s services prices are mild while demand is structurally recovering; buy leaders with pricing or cost control, not broad beta. |
| Underweight sectors | High-PE technology sensitive to real US yields, crowded foreign-owned growth, and long-liability property-chain names with weak cash flow | Higher offshore discount rates and FX volatility amplify valuation compression. |
| Trading rhythm | Stay with a defensive barbell near term; add growth exposure only after US core services MoM prints return to around **0.2%** for two consecutive months | Current services inflation data do not yet validate a sustainable easing signal. |

## Follow-up Items for Alt Data Analyst

1. **Services-price high-frequency basket:** airfares, hotel ADR, restaurant menu prices, ride-hailing/taxi fares, healthcare out-of-pocket costs, repair services, education/training, enterprise software and professional-services fees.
2. **Wages and labor tightness:** services hourly earnings, advertised wages, vacancy rates, quit rates and gig-platform unit prices.
3. **Pass-through chain:** energy prices, fuel surcharges, shipping insurance, business electricity/gas bills and services-sector purchasing prices.
4. **Trigger thresholds:** US ISM Services Prices > **65**, UK services CPI > **4%**, euro-area services HICP > **3%**, and China services CPI > **1.5%**, each mapped to different rate and A-share style-rotation signals.

## Bottom Line

The current model is probably underestimating how sticky overseas services inflation constrains the rate path. For A-shares, frame the risk as “offshore discount-rate and dollar-liquidity pressure,” not “runaway China inflation”; stay tilted toward dividends, resources and cash flow, with selective domestic services leaders.

1. **结论是“是”，但要分区域定价。** 模型若仍把 2026 年通胀回落主要押在商品价格均值回归上，确实低估了服务业通胀粘性对美英利率路径的上拉作用；欧元区风险次之；中国本土服务 CPI 仍温和，直接通胀约束有限。
2. **服务业并非全球同步强于商品。** 公开 PMI 显示全球制造业 4 月因补库存和供应担忧短期走强，服务业增长仍偏弱；但这不削弱通胀判断，因为服务投入成本、工资与能源传导仍在抬升，服务价格回落慢于商品。

4. **A 股策略含义：降低久期和外资流动性敏感资产权重，保留内需服务和红利资源的哑铃。** 全球服务通胀粘性会通过美债实际利率、美元和风险偏好压制高估值成长；中国服务价格温和则支持国内政策维持宽松偏向，利好现金流稳、分红高、受益能源/资源价格和国内服务修复的组合。

## 对模型的修正建议

- **把服务价格因子权重上调。** 建议将 DM 利率路径模型中“核心服务/工资/服务 PMI Prices”合成因子权重从次要解释变量调为核心解释变量；商品价格回落不再自动等价于核心通胀回落。
- **加入二轮效应触发器。** 若美国 `services less energy services` 3 个月年化持续高于 **4%**，或 ISM Services Prices 连续两个月高于 **65**，则默认 Fed 年内降息概率下调，并保留重新定价加息风险。
- **区分“真实需求强”与“补库存假强”。** 全球制造业 4 月扩张有明显补库存属性，不能直接解释为商品需求复苏；服务业价格粘性更多来自工资、能源、房租/住宿、航空、医疗、餐饮与企业服务费用。
- **中国模块单独处理。** 中国服务 CPI **0.9%** 与官方服务 PMI **49.6** 说明国内没有服务通胀粘性；A 股贴现率冲击主要来自海外实际利率和汇率，而非 PBoC 被迫收紧。

## A 股策略落点

| 组合方向 | 建议 | 逻辑 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 风格 | **红利/现金流 > 高估值久期成长** | 全球利率“higher for longer”压制远期盈利贴现；红利资产受益于相对确定现金流。 |
| 行业超配 | 银行、运营商、公用事业、煤炭、石油石化、黄金/铜链、交运中具备燃油附加费传导能力的公司 | 高利率与能源冲击环境下，定价能力、分红和资源现金流更稀缺。 |
| 行业择优 | 酒店、旅游、餐饮供应链、医疗服务、教育文化娱乐中的龙头 | 中国服务价格温和但需求有结构性修复，选择能提价或控费的龙头，不做全行业 beta。 |
| 行业低配 | 对美债实际利率敏感的高 PE 科技、外资持仓拥挤成长、负债久期长且现金流弱的地产链 | 海外贴现率上行与汇率波动会放大估值压缩。 |
| 交易节奏 | 短期偏防御哑铃；等美国核心服务环比连续两个月回到 **0.2%** 附近，再提高成长暴露 | 当前服务通胀数据尚未给出可持续降息信号。 |

## 给 Alt Data Analyst 的后续抓手

1. **服务价格高频篮子：** 机票、酒店 ADR、餐饮菜单价、网约车/出租车、医疗自付费用、维修服务、教育培训、企业软件和专业服务收费。
2. **工资与劳动力紧张度：** 服务业时薪、招聘广告薪资、职位空缺率、离职率、零工平台单价。
3. **传导链：** 能源价格、燃油附加费、航运保险费、企业电费/燃气费、服务业采购价格。
4. **触发阈值：** 美国 ISM Services Prices > **65**、英国 services CPI > **4%**、欧元区 services HICP > **3%**、中国 services CPI > **1.5%**，分别对应不同的利率与 A 股风格切换信号。

## 一句话结论

当前模型大概率低估了海外服务通胀粘性对利率路径的约束；A 股应把风险识别为“海外贴现率和美元流动性压力”，而不是“中国通胀失控”，配置上继续偏红利资源和现金流，选择性持有国内服务龙头。

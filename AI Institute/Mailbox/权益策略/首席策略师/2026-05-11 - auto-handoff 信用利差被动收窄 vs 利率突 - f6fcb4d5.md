# Re-ranking Credit-Bond Appeal if the 10Y CGB Breaks Below 1.55% or Above 1.75%

**Date anchor: 2026-05-11. Lead conclusion: the base case should not assume the 10Y CGB is still around 1.65%. ChinaBond's 2026-05-11 curve puts the 10Y CGB at 1.7622%, already inside the upside-break zone in the question.** For the next 2-4 weeks, the portfolio should therefore treat the upside break as triggered but not yet fully confirmed: run duration slightly short versus benchmark, keep exposure in liquid high-grade bonds, and avoid adding 5Y illiquid AA+ LGFV risk.

## Prioritised Conclusions

1. **If the 10Y CGB breaks decisively below 1.55%: absolute credit yields will be compressed further, and there is unlikely to be much active spread-compression upside left.** In that scenario, 3-5Y credit appeal comes from yield scarcity and duration gains, not from spread compensation. Recommended ranking: **5Y AAA- bank Tier-2 > 3Y AAA- bank Tier-2 > 3Y strong-region AA+ LGFV > 5Y AA+ LGFV**. Do not chase yield by moving down the rating stack.

2. **If the 10Y CGB breaks decisively above 1.75%: this has already begun as of 2026-05-11.** Credit spreads may keep tightening passively, but portfolio NAV is first exposed to rate-driven duration losses, especially in the 5Y bucket. The ranking reverses to: **3Y strong-region AA+ LGFV > 3Y AAA- bank Tier-2 > 5Y AAA- bank Tier-2 > 5Y AA+ LGFV**. The core action is to shorten duration and improve liquidity, not to pre-buy long-duration credit.

3. **Pre-positioning is recommended, but it should be incremental rather than a one-off large switch.** Suggested duration is 0.3-0.5 years short versus benchmark; deploy new money mainly into 2-3Y high-grade and strong-region names; add 5Y bank Tier-2 only if spreads move back toward about 60bp or the 5Y valuation yield reaches about 2.05%-2.10%; avoid chasing 5Y AA+ LGFV unless both region and issuer quality are very strong.

## Asset-Allocation Implications

From a strategic asset-allocation perspective, credit bonds are no longer cheap assets; they are low-volatility carry assets. Current 3Y and 5Y credit spreads are compressed to roughly 46-51bp. Since 2025-12-31, Tier-2 spreads have tightened by about 10-14bp, while AA+ LGFV spreads have tightened less but are still low in absolute terms. External research also shows that in Q1 2026, 3Y credit spreads were around the 7%-20% historical percentile range, with LGFV around 7% and bank perpetual/Tier-2 bonds around 17%, confirming that credit valuations are no longer cheap.[3]

Therefore, if the 10Y CGB breaks below 1.55%, credit can be used as a lower-volatility extension of the rates rally, but AA+ LGFV should not be the main offensive asset; liquid 5Y high-grade bank Tier-2 is the cleaner duration vehicle. If the 10Y CGB breaks above 1.75%, tighter-looking credit spreads are not a sign of cheaper credit; they are mostly a passive result of higher rates. Duration and liquidity control should take priority.

## Next Steps for the Bond Analyst

- **Trigger rule**: use three consecutive trading-day closes, not intraday moves. If the 10Y CGB closes below 1.55% for three sessions, duration can be added; if it closes above 1.75% for three sessions, keep duration short.
- **Buy thresholds**: move 5Y AAA- Tier-2 from watchlist to add only when spreads return to about 60bp or the 5Y valuation yield reaches about 2.05%-2.10%. Increase 3Y strong-region AA+ LGFV only when spreads return to about 55-60bp.
- **Trim candidates**: first reduce 5Y AA+ LGFV, weak-region platforms, and long-duration credit with concentrated non-bank ownership and weak trading depth; retain the high-grade core book inside 3Y.
- **Risk flag**: the 10Y CGB is already at 1.7622%, so the upside-break scenario is no longer a tail risk but a validation phase. If funding conditions or policy expectations push the 10Y higher, passive credit-spread tightening does not mean portfolio drawdown is controlled.

## 资产配置含义

从战略资产配置看，当前信用债不再是“便宜资产”，而是“低波动票息资产”。3Y和5Y信用利差已压到约46-51bp区间，2026年以来二级资本债利差收窄约10-14bp，城投AA+利差收窄较少但绝对利差同样偏低。外部研究也显示，2026年一季度3Y信用利差处在约7%-20%历史分位，同时城投利差分位约7%，二永债约17%，反映信用债估值已不便宜。[3]

因此，若10Y下破1.55%，可以把信用债作为利率行情的低波动延伸，但不要把AA+城投当作主要进攻资产；更适合用流动性更好的5Y高等级二级资本债承接久期。若10Y上破1.75%，信用债相对利率债的账面利差可能看起来更“窄”，但这不是更便宜，而是利率上行导致的被动结果；应优先控制久期和流动性。

## 给债券分析师的下一步

- **触发阈值**：用3个交易日收盘确认，而不是盘中突破。若10Y国债连续3日低于1.55%，允许加久期；若连续3日高于1.75%，维持久期低配。
- **买入阈值**：5Y AAA-二级资本债利差回到约60bp、或5Y估值收益率达到约2.05%-2.10%，才建议从观察转为增配。3Y强区域AA+城投利差回到约55-60bp再提高仓位。
- **减仓对象**：优先减5Y AA+城投、弱区域平台、非银持仓集中且成交弱的长久期信用；保留3Y以内高等级核心仓。
- **风险点**：当前10Y国债已在1.7622%，上破情景不是尾部风险，而是已进入验证阶段；后续若资金面或政策预期推动10Y继续上行，信用债“被动收窄”不等于组合回撤可控。

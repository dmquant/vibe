# Commercial Innovative Drug Catalog and NRDL Linkage: Implications for Domestic ADCs and Bispecifics

Date anchor: 2026-05-11, based on the local shell output from `date +%Y-%m-%d`.

## Prioritized Conclusions

1. **The real change is not “commercial insurance replacing basic medical insurance.” It is a staged path that turns the old binary outcome of “no NRDL entry means full self-pay and weak hospital access” into “commercial catalog first, hospital constraints reduced, then another NRDL application window.”** In the 2026 consultation draft, the National Healthcare Security Administration (NHSA) added “drugs included in the 2025 Commercial Health Insurance Innovative Drug Catalog” as a new eligibility condition for out-of-catalog NRDL applications, explicitly linking the NRDL and the commercial innovative drug catalog.[NHSA, 2026-05-09](https://www.nhsa.gov.cn/art/2026/5/9/art_105_20455.html)

2. **For high-premium domestic ADCs and bispecifics that are not yet in the NRDL, the long-term penetration ceiling rises, but it will not approach that of NRDL drugs.** Commercial catalog drugs are not paid by the basic medical insurance fund, but their listing and hospital stocking are generally handled by reference to negotiated NRDL drugs. They also receive the “three exclusions”: excluded from hospitals’ basic-insurance self-pay ratio metrics, excluded from monitoring as substitutes for volume-based procurement winners, and eligible commercial-insurance-covered cases may be excluded from DRG/DIP case-based payment after review.[NHSA, 2025-12-07](https://www.nhsa.gov.cn/art/2025/12/7/art_104_18970.html) This addresses hospital usability and DRG/DIP friction first, while actual payment still depends on commercial health insurance depth.

3. **Valuation should move from a single “NRDL negotiation binary tree” to a three-state DCF: non-catalog low penetration, commercial catalog medium penetration and higher ASP, and NRDL high penetration but lower ASP.** For truly differentiated ADCs and bispecifics with robust evidence, the commercial catalog creates a domestic commercialization option: preserve a higher net price initially, accumulate real-world and claims data, and improve predictability for the next NRDL negotiation. For crowded same-target or same-mechanism assets, the re-rating should be limited; the NRDL price anchor still dominates.

4. **Near-term extrapolation should be conservative.** The first commercial innovative drug catalog included 19 drugs. As of 2026-01-20, 14 commercial catalog drugs had sales records at 223 designated medical institutions, while 99 newly added NRDL drugs had sales records at 12,198 designated institutions over the same period.[NHSA, 2026-01-21](https://www.nhsa.gov.cn/art/2026/1/21/art_14_19439.html) This shows that early commercial catalog execution is much weaker than NRDL execution. It is more an access channel and valuation option than an immediate volume driver.

## Penetration Impact

| State | Payment/access characteristics | Long-term penetration range under strategy assumptions | Revenue direction |
| --- | --- | ---: | --- |
| Not in NRDL and not in commercial catalog | Mainly full self-pay; strong hospital procurement and DRG/DIP friction; high prescribing friction. | 1%-3% of eligible patients, mostly patients with strong ability to pay or access to patient assistance. | High ASP, but low and unstable volume. |
| In the commercial innovative drug catalog | Lower hospital listing and stocking friction; commercial insurance, city supplementary plans, and specialty drug policies can expand coverage by reference; no basic insurance fund payment. | 5%-15%; can approach 20% if the indication is small, efficacy differentiation is strong, and local commercial plans expand coverage actively. | Lower discount than NRDL, moderate volume; best suited to small patient pools and high-unit-price products. |
| In the NRDL | National unified payment standard, local reimbursement by rule, more mature hospital formulary, dual-channel, and separate-payment mechanisms. | 20%-50%, depending on indication, treatment line, competition, and hospital coverage. | ASP drops materially, but volume elasticity is the strongest; best for large indications and competitive indications. |

These penetration ranges are strategy estimates, not official disclosures. The core logic is that the NRDL solves payment capacity for most patients, while the commercial catalog first solves whether hospitals can stock the drug, whether physicians can prescribe it, and whether part of the patient base can receive reimbursement. For late-line and small-indication ADCs or bispecifics, the commercial catalog has stronger marginal elasticity. For large first-line lung cancer or breast cancer indications, the NRDL remains the main volume path.

## Valuation Impact

1. **Domestic peak sales should no longer apply a punitive “no NRDL equals near-zero” assumption.** A commercial-catalog intermediate state should be added: net price retention is higher than under the NRDL, but penetration is lower. For ADCs and bispecifics with annual treatment costs of RMB 150,000-300,000, if commercial negotiation implies a 20%-40% discount and commercial reimbursement covers 50%-70%, patient annual out-of-pocket cost can fall from RMB 150,000-300,000 full self-pay to about RMB 36,000-120,000. The affordability barrier falls materially, but remains higher than under most NRDL pathways.

2. **The valuation uplift comes from two variables: access probability and evidence premium.** The 2026 rules make 2025 commercial catalog drugs eligible for out-of-catalog NRDL applications, increasing the probability of entering a later NRDL negotiation process. Encouragement of real-world comprehensive value assessment can also help ADCs and bispecifics with strong efficacy evidence secure better price anchors.

3. **DCF models should use three probability-weighted states.** For products with clear OS/PFS advantages, differentiated targets, or global BD validation, the scenario weight for “successful commercial catalog entry but no immediate NRDL entry” can be raised. For crowded same-target assets relying mainly on substitution value, models should still assume strong NRDL price pressure and competitor share loss.

4. **For A-share and H-share mapping, the benefit order is: launched differentiated ADCs/bispecifics with strong evidence > high-priced small-indication drugs already in or well suited to the commercial catalog > pure me-too assets in crowded indications without head-to-head data.** Valuation multiples should not be raised uniformly for all ADC and bispecific platforms.

| Large-indication first-line or second-line ADCs/bispecifics | Medium | The commercial catalog is only a bridge. True volume still needs NRDL entry. Without NRDL coverage, commercial insurance alone is unlikely to support broad penetration. |
| Domestic platform assets supported by global licensing or overseas Phase III data | Medium-high | The commercial catalog improves visibility of domestic revenue, while global BD remains the primary valuation anchor; the domestic commercial-insurance path reduces the discount risk of relying only on overseas deals. |
| Homogeneous HER2/TROP2/PD-1 combination assets | Low to medium | The commercial catalog will not change the competitive price-downtrend. Valuation still depends on head-to-head efficacy, safety, manufacturing cost, and NRDL negotiation capability. |

## One-Line Conclusion

The linkage between the commercial innovative drug catalog and the NRDL changes the domestic commercialization framework for high-priced domestic ADCs and bispecifics from “either major NRDL price cuts for volume or full self-pay with low penetration” to “commercial insurance first for hospital access and partial payment, then a later NRDL attempt.” It raises long-term penetration and success probability, but it does not replace the NRDL’s decisive role in driving volume for large indications.

# 商保创新药目录与基本医保衔接：对国产ADC/双抗的渗透率与估值影响

锚定日期：2026-05-11（以本机 `date +%Y-%m-%d` 为准）。

## 优先结论

1. **实质变化不是“商保替代医保”，而是把高价创新药从“未进医保即全自费、院内难落地”的二元结果，改造成“商保目录先承接、院端约束先解除、下一轮基本医保再申报”的分阶段路径。** 国家医保局 2026 年征求意见稿已把“纳入 2025 年商业健康保险创新药品目录的药品”新增为目录外药品申报条件之一，明确体现基本医保目录与商保创新药目录的衔接。[国家医保局，2026-05-09](https://www.nhsa.gov.cn/art/2026/5/9/art_105_20455.html)

2. **对高溢价但暂未进基本医保的国产ADC和双抗，远期渗透率的上限被抬高，但不会接近基本医保品种。** 商保目录药品医保基金不予支付，但挂网、配备原则上参照医保谈判药品执行，并享受“三除外”：不计入基本医保自费率指标、不计入集采中选可替代品种监测，符合条件病例可不纳入医保按病种付费范围。[国家医保局，2025-12-07](https://www.nhsa.gov.cn/art/2025/12/7/art_104_18970.html) 这能先解决院内可用性和DRG/DIP约束，但支付端仍取决于商业健康险覆盖深度。

3. **估值体系将从“医保谈判单一二叉树”转向“三状态DCF”：未入目录低渗透、入商保目录中渗透高ASP、入基本医保高渗透低ASP。** 对真正差异化、证据扎实的ADC/双抗，商保目录带来的是国内商业化的“真实期权”：先保留较高净价、积累真实世界数据和商保理赔数据，再提高下一轮基本医保谈判的可预测性。对同靶点、同机制拥挤品种，重估幅度有限，最终仍要接受基本医保价格锚。

4. **短期不能过度外推。** 首版商保创新药目录共 19 种药品；截至 2026 年 1 月 20 日，14 种商保目录药品在 223 家定点医药机构销售，而同期 99 种基本医保新增药品已在 12,198 家定点医药机构销售。[国家医保局，2026-01-21](https://www.nhsa.gov.cn/art/2026/1/21/art_14_19439.html) 这说明商保目录的早期落地强度明显弱于基本医保目录，更多是“可及性通道”和“估值期权”，不是立即放量。

| 2026 年衔接机制 | 2026 年目录外药品申报条件新增“纳入 2025 年商保创新药目录的药品”；附条件批准转常规批准品种最多可获得“5 年+3 年”申报时间。[国家医保局，2026-05-09](https://www.nhsa.gov.cn/art/2026/5/9/art_105_20455.html) | 商保目录成为下一轮基本医保申报的跳板，降低因上市时间窗口、证据成熟时间不匹配造成的准入风险。 |
| 商保支付基础 | 2024 年创新药械市场约 1,620 亿元，其中医保基金支付约 710 亿元、个人现金支付约 786 亿元、商业健康险支付约 124 亿元，占 7.7%。[经济观察网，2025-02-28](https://www.eeo.com.cn/2025/0228/713961.shtml) | 当前商保资金盘仍小，单靠商保难支撑大适应症ADC/双抗的全民化渗透，但足以改善小适应症、高价值人群的支付断点。 |

## 对远期渗透率的重塑

| 状态 | 支付/准入特征 | 策略假设下的远期渗透率区间 | 对收入的方向 |
| --- | --- | ---: | --- |
| 未进基本医保且未进商保目录 | 全自费为主，院内采购和DRG/DIP约束强，医生处方摩擦高。 | 1%-3% 的合格患者，主要集中于支付能力强或慈善赠药可覆盖人群。 | ASP高，但销量低且不稳定。 |
| 进入商保创新药目录 | 院内挂网/配备摩擦下降；商保、惠民保、特药险可参考扩责；不由基本医保基金支付。 | 5%-15%，若适应症小、疗效差异大、地方商保产品积极扩责，可向 20% 靠近。 | 净价折让小于基本医保，销量中等，适合小患者池、高单价产品。 |
| 进入基本医保目录 | 全国统一支付标准，地方按规则报销，药事会、双通道和单独支付机制更成熟。 | 20%-50%，取决于适应症、治疗线数、竞争格局和医院覆盖。 | ASP显著下降，但量的弹性最大，适合大适应症和竞争性适应症。 |

上述渗透率为策略测算区间，不是官方披露值。其核心逻辑是：基本医保解决“大多数患者的支付能力”，商保目录先解决“医院愿不愿配、医生能不能开、部分患者能否赔”的问题。对于晚线、小适应症ADC/双抗，商保目录的边际弹性更大；对于一线肺癌、乳腺癌等大适应症，基本医保仍是放量主路径。

## 对估值体系的重塑

1. **国内峰值销售额不应再用“未进医保=接近零”的惩罚性假设。** 可加入商保中间状态：净价保留率高于基本医保，但渗透率低于基本医保。对年治疗费用 15 万-30 万元的ADC/双抗，若商保协商后价格折让 20%-40%、商保报销 50%-70%，患者年自付可从全自费 15 万-30 万元降至约 3.6 万-12 万元，支付门槛明显下降，但仍高于多数基本医保路径。

2. **估值弹性来自两个变量：准入概率和证据溢价。** 2026 年规则把 2025 年商保目录药品纳入基本医保目录外申报条件，相当于提高了后续进入基本医保谈判流程的概率；同时鼓励真实世界医保综合价值评价，有助于疗效证据强的ADC/双抗获得更好的价格锚。

3. **DCF建议采用三档概率权重。** 对有明确OS/PFS优势、差异化靶点或全球BD验证的品种，可把“商保目录成功但基本医保暂未进入”的情景权重上调；对同靶点拥挤、主要依赖替代性优势的品种，仍按基本医保强降价和竞品分流处理。

4. **A股/H股映射上，利好顺序是：已上市且证据强的差异化ADC/双抗 > 已进入商保目录或具备商保目录特征的小适应症高价药 > 纯me-too、适应症拥挤且缺乏头对头数据的品种。** 不应对所有ADC/双抗平台统一上调估值倍数。

## 对国产ADC与双抗的具体判断

| 类型 | 政策受益强度 | 判断 |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| 高价、小患者池、临床获益突出的双抗/ADC | 高 | 最适合商保目录：基本医保难完全承接费用，但商保可用较小赔付盘换取高感知价值；院端“三除外”改善处方可行性。 |
| 大适应症一线或二线ADC/双抗 | 中 | 商保目录只能作为过渡，真正放量仍需基本医保。若不进基本医保，商保覆盖难以支撑大规模渗透。 |
| 有全球授权/海外III期数据支持的国产平台品种 | 中高 | 商保目录提高国内收入可见度，全球BD仍是估值主锚；国内商保路径可降低“只靠出海”的折现风险。 |
| 同质化HER2/TROP2/PD-1组合类品种 | 低至中 | 商保目录不会改变竞争性降价趋势；估值仍需看头对头疗效、安全性、生产成本和医保谈判能力。 |

## 给Healthcare Analyst的跟踪项

1. 跟踪 2026 年正式方案是否保留“2025 年商保目录药品可申报基本医保”的条款，以及是否扩大到后续年度商保目录。
2. 建立ADC/双抗品种清单，逐项标记：是否进基本医保、是否进商保目录、是否有OS/PFS头对头数据、是否已转常规批准、是否有全球BD。
3. 跟踪地方惠民保和百万医疗险是否把 2025 年商保目录 19 个品种纳入特药责任，重点看赔付比例、免赔额、既往症限制和一站式结算。
4. 对估值模型加入三状态情景：未入目录、商保目录、基本医保目录；不要把商保目录等同于基本医保报销。
5. 重点核验首批商保目录药品在 2026 年上半年的真实销售机构数、理赔件数和患者自付变化；这将决定 2027-2028 年ADC/双抗商保渗透率假设是否可上修。

## 结论一句话

商保创新药目录与基本医保的衔接机制，将国产高价ADC/双抗的国内商业化从“要么医保大降价放量、要么全自费低渗透”改造成“商保先给院端准入和部分支付、再争取基本医保”的分阶段估值框架；它提高远期渗透率和成功概率，但不会替代基本医保对大适应症放量的决定性作用。

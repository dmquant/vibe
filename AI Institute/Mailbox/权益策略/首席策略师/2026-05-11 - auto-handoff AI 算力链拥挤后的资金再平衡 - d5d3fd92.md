# Where Funds Go If the AI Compute Chain Falls 10-15%

Date anchor: local `date +%Y-%m-%d` returned `2026-05-11`. Therefore “the next four weeks” means `2026-05-11` through `2026-06-08`. This window includes NVIDIA’s FY2027 Q1 call on `2026-05-20` and Broadcom’s FY2026 Q2 release on `2026-06-03`, both per company investor-relations announcements.

## Priority Conclusions

1. **First destination: gold.** Across the three crowding-unwind samples, GLD was positive every time, with a `3/3` hit rate, `+5.2%` average return, and `+5.8%` median return. If NVDA/AVGO earnings trigger lower confidence in AI capex and a cut in risk budgets, gold is the cleanest first-order cross-asset refuge, with the most stable payoff.
2. **Second destination: long bonds, but the rate regime matters.** TLT had a `2/3` hit rate, `+1.7%` average return, and `+6.4%` median return. It delivered more than `+6%` in 2018-Q4 and 2024-08, but lost `-8.4%` in 2022-Q1 as inflation and Fed repricing dominated. If this is a growth/earnings shock, long bonds can rival gold; if inflation or term premium rises at the same time, downgrade them.
3. **Third destination: central-SOE dividend / high-dividend China proxies, mainly a second-stage receiver rather than a first refuge.** The 510880.SS proxy was negative in all three samples, with a `0/3` absolute hit rate and `-4.8%` average return. Still, it outperformed SOXX by roughly `+17.1`, `+18.3`, and `+19.5` percentage points. The conclusion: it is better for lower-volatility equity reallocation after the AI-chain shock, not for immediate positive-return de-risking.
4. **Last destination: non-U.S. equities.** ACWX fell in all three samples, with a `0/3` hit rate and `-10.9%` average return. Non-U.S. equities need a weaker dollar, lower real yields, and firmer global PMIs before they move from “sold with risk assets” to “valuation rebalancing inflow.”

**Ranked fund path: gold > long bonds > central-SOE dividend > non-U.S. equities.** If the mandate is RMB assets only, the order can be adjusted to: gold ETF / physical-gold proxy > China long government bonds > central-SOE dividend > non-U.S. equities. 511010.SS was positive in all three samples, but its average return was only `+1.0%`, so its hit rate is high while its payoff is low.

## Path for the Next Four Weeks

**Stage 1: first 1-5 trading days after earnings.** If NVDA/AVGO guidance misses the market’s extrapolated AI capex path, the first move should be a risk-budget cut across semiconductors, optical modules, servers, IDC power equipment, and related crowded positions. Historically, gold is the most consistent receiver, followed by long bonds. Central-SOE dividend proxies mainly “fall less” rather than “rise immediately.”

**Stage 2: weeks 2-3.** If U.S. real yields fall and the dollar weakens, long bonds and gold can work together. If yields do not fall, gold should still outrank long bonds. Within A shares, funds may rotate from AI compute beta toward cash-flow-visible central-SOE dividends, banks, utilities, and telecom operators; define this by relative performance after the drawdown, not by guaranteed positive absolute returns.

**Stage 3: week 4 and beyond.** Non-U.S. equities need the market to interpret lower U.S. tech earnings expectations as a unwind of U.S. concentration premium rather than a global growth shock. Otherwise, the ACWX sample says they normally remain risk assets, not safe-haven assets.

## Implementation Guidance

1. **Base ranking weights: gold `40%`, long bonds `30%`, central-SOE dividend `20%`, non-U.S. equities `10%`.** These are path-probability weights, not a static portfolio recommendation; they apply to a fast `10-15%` AI-chain drawdown without a confirmed global recession.
2. **Upgrade long bonds if:** U.S. 10Y yields fall persistently, SOXX falls while oil and inflation expectations do not rise, and VIX rises with breakeven inflation falling. Under those conditions, long bonds can be tied with gold for first place.
3. **Upgrade central-SOE dividend if:** A-share AI compute-chain turnover share falls, central-SOE dividend/buyback expectations strengthen, and banks plus telecom operators break out on relative strength. Then this bucket can move from third to second.
4. **Upgrade non-U.S. equities if:** the dollar index weakens, U.S. tech earnings downgrades do not spread into global PMIs, and earnings revisions in Europe/Japan/China turn positive. Otherwise keep it last.

## 本轮路径推演

**第 1 阶段：业绩后 1-5 个交易日。** 若 NVDA/AVGO 指引低于市场对 AI capex 的外推，最先发生的是半导体、光模块、服务器、IDC 电力设备等拥挤链条的风险预算压缩。历史上这类阶段最稳定的承接是黄金，其次是长债；红利央企更多体现为“跌得少”，不是“马上涨”。

**第 2 阶段：第 2-3 周。** 如果美国实际利率下行、美元走弱，长债与黄金可继续共振；若利率不下行，黄金仍优于长债。A 股内部资金可能从 AI 算力链转到现金流可见度更高的央企分红、银行、公用事业、运营商，但应以回撤后的相对收益而非绝对正收益来定义。

**第 3 阶段：第 4 周以后。** 非美权益只有在“美国科技盈利预期下修”被理解为美国集中度溢价回吐、而不是全球增长风险时，才会获得真正流入。否则 ACWX 的历史表现说明，它通常仍是风险资产而非避险资产。

## 操作建议

1. **基准排序：黄金 `40%`、长债 `30%`、红利央企 `20%`、非美权益 `10%`。** 这是路径概率排序，不是静态组合建议；适用于 AI 链出现 `10-15%` 快速回撤、但未确认全球衰退的场景。
2. **触发上调长债的条件：** 美国 10Y 收益率连续下行、SOXX 跌但油价/通胀预期不升、VIX 上行伴随 breakeven inflation 下行。满足时长债可与黄金并列第一。
3. **触发上调红利央企的条件：** A 股 AI 算力链成交占比回落、央企分红/回购预期强化、银行与运营商相对强度突破。满足时可从第三顺位上调到第二顺位。
4. **触发上调非美权益的条件：** 美元指数走弱、美国科技盈利下修没有扩散到全球 PMI、欧洲/日本/中国盈利修正转正。否则维持最后顺位。

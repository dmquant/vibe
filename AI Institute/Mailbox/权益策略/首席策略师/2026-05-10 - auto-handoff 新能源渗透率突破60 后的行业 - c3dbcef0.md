# China NEV Retail Penetration Above 60%: Implications for A/H Auto Valuation

Anchor date: 2026-05-10; previous trading day: 2026-05-08.

## Priority Conclusions

1. **The 60% threshold is not simply a signal of continued NEV hyper-growth; it is a signal that the ICE profit pool is entering accelerated depreciation.** Preliminary CPCA data show China passenger-car retail sales of 1.406 million units in April 2026, down 20% YoY and 15% MoM; NEV retail sales were 883,000 units, down 5% YoY but up 4% MoM, lifting retail penetration to 62.8%. On that basis, implied ICE and non-NEV passenger-car retail sales were about 523,000 units, down roughly 37% YoY, far worse than the overall market.[1]

2. **The A-share and Hong Kong-listed auto valuation framework should move from “legacy OEMs vs. EV startups” to segment valuation: declining cash-flow assets plus NEV/smart-vehicle growth assets.** Traditional automakers should no longer receive stable consumer-style multiples based on historical JV/ICE earnings. They need to be valued across three blocks: NEV and smart-vehicle platforms, export profit pools, and terminal ICE/JV cash flows.

3. **The new valuation center for “traditional automakers” should be defined as: 0.5-0.8x PB for ICE tail assets plus 8-12x PE for transition earnings; only when NEV and exports become the main profit sources should the center move up to 10-15x PE or 1.2-2.0x PB.** In other words, the center is not a fixed PE multiple. It is determined by NEV mix, per-vehicle profit, export mix, and dependence on ICE/JV earnings.

4. **The market is already pricing this stratification.** BYD A/H still trades at platform-type premiums: around 33.10x PE-TTM and 3.93x PB for A shares near 2026-05-08; around 29.60x PE and 3.49x PB for H shares on 2026-05-05.[4][5] Geely Auto, a legacy-background OEM with faster NEV transition, trades at about 10.26x PE and 1.76x PB. SAIC and GAC, which have higher exposure to JV/ICE earnings, remain around 0.5-0.7x PB.[7][8][10]

## Valuation Framework Shift

### 1. Sector beta is moving from “penetration upside” to “profit quality dispersion”

In 2024-2025, the market could still use rising NEV penetration to justify broad OEM re-rating. The 62.8% reading in April 2026 looks more like structural clearing. CPCA also noted that dealers face the dilemma of “selling cars without profit, but losing more if they do not sell,” making channel stress a core industry issue.[1] Therefore, the 60% breakthrough should not justify a blanket upgrade for OEMs. The key question is whether a company can turn NEV share into sustainable profit.

### 2. “Traditional automakers” should be split into three asset blocks

| Asset Block | Valuation Method | Suggested Center | Rationale |
|---|---|---:|---|
| ICE/JV tail cash flows | Normalized FCF or PB | 5-8x FCF, or 0.4-0.7x PB | The addressable volume pool is shrinking quickly, and brand/channel assets are depreciating faster |
| NEV and smart-vehicle platforms | Forward PE / EV-EBIT | 12-18x PE; 10-15x after maturation | Requires verifiable gross margin, per-vehicle profit, and software/ADAS cost control |
| Exports and overseas localization | PE or SOTP premium | 10-14x PE, with a 20%-40% premium possible | Overseas ASP and margin are usually better than in China, but tariffs, compliance, and localization risks must be deducted |

## Defining the New Center for “Traditional Automakers”

I would redefine “traditional automakers” as: **OEMs whose main profit source is still ICE/JV earnings, or whose NEV sales mix is below industry penetration and whose NEV business has not yet proven stable profitability.** Under this definition, company history, ownership background, or whether it once sold ICE vehicles is not the key issue. Profit structure is.

Executable valuation framework:

`Equity value = NEV/smart-vehicle profit × 12-18x PE + export profit × 10-14x PE + sustainable ICE/JV FCF × 5-8x + net cash/financial assets - transition and channel restructuring costs`

Corresponding valuation centers:

| Type | Test | New Center |
|---|---|---:|
| ICE/JV-dependent traditional OEM | NEV mix materially below 62.8%, or NEV losses while JV profit declines | 0.4-0.8x PB; PE only meaningful after normalization at 6-10x |
| Transition-delivering traditional OEM | NEV mix of 30%-60%, narrowing NEV losses, rising export mix | 10-15x PE; 1.0-1.8x PB |
| New mainstream OEM | NEV mix close to or above industry penetration; exports and smart vehicles contribute profit; ICE is no longer the core valuation source | 12-18x PE; 1.5-2.5x PB |
| Platform leader | Scale, supply chain, e-powertrain, smart-vehicle capability, and overseas localization all proven | 18-30x PE; above 3x PB can be justified, but is sensitive to earnings volatility |

## Allocation Implications

1. **A shares: shift from broad OEM rallies to verifiable transition earnings.** Prefer legacy leaders whose NEV mix is approaching industry levels, whose overseas mix is rising, and whose cash flow is stable. Low-PB names only deserve re-rating when proprietary NEV profitability improves or SOE reform lifts capital returns.

2. **Hong Kong: lower valuations, but wider dispersion.** Hong Kong applies a higher risk discount to auto names. Structural allocation should focus on names with clearer profit and export logic, such as Geely and BYD H. Low-PB assets such as GAC H are better treated as deep-value options, not industry beta.

3. **Avoid the “cheap ICE” value trap.** Implied April ICE retail sales were down roughly 37% YoY, so low PB is not safety by itself. Safety comes from net cash, dividends, saleable assets, and narrowing NEV losses.

## Follow-up Items

- Whether NEV retail penetration stays above 60% in May-June, and whether that comes with better total retail sales.
- Company-level NEV gross margin, per-vehicle net profit, and marketing expense ratio, not just unit sales.
- Export mix, overseas ASP, overseas localization cost, and trade barriers.
- Dealer inventory, terminal discounts, and payable cycles to judge whether price competition is truly easing.
- Whether low-PB names such as SAIC and GAC show proprietary NEV profit inflection or changes in capital return policy.

# 中国新能源汽车零售渗透率突破60%：对A/H汽车板块估值体系的影响

基准日期：2026-05-10；上一交易日：2026-05-08。

## 优先结论

1. **60%不是“新能源继续高增长”的简单信号，而是燃油车利润池进入加速折旧期的信号。** 乘联分会初步统计显示，2026年4月全国乘用车零售140.6万辆，同比-20%、环比-15%；新能源零售88.3万辆，同比-5%、环比+4%，零售渗透率62.8%。据此倒算，4月燃油及非新能源乘用车零售约52.3万辆，同比约-37%，跌幅远大于行业整体。[1]

2. **A股和港股汽车整车估值将从“燃油车企 vs. 新势力”切换到“现金流衰退资产 + 新能源/智能化成长资产”的分部估值。** 传统车企不再按历史合资燃油利润给稳定消费品倍数，而应按三块资产重估：新能源与智能化平台、出口利润池、燃油/JV现金流尾部。

3. **“传统车企”的新估值中枢应定义为：0.5-0.8倍PB的燃油尾部资产 + 8-12倍PE的转型利润，只有新能源与出口已成为主要利润来源时才上移到10-15倍PE或1.2-2.0倍PB。** 也就是说，传统车企的中枢不是一个固定PE，而是由新能源占比、单车利润、出口占比和燃油/JV利润依赖度共同决定。

4. **当前市场已经在给出分层：** 比亚迪A股/港股仍享受平台型溢价，2026-05-08附近A股PE-TTM约33.10倍、PB约3.93倍；比亚迪H股2026-05-05 PE约29.60倍、PB约3.49倍。[4][5] 吉利汽车作为“传统背景但新能源化较快”的港股样本，PE约10.26倍、PB约1.76倍；上汽、广汽等合资/JV利润依赖更高的公司，PB仍在0.5-0.7倍附近。[7][8][10]

## 估值体系变化

### 1. 板块beta从“渗透率提升”转为“盈利质量分化”

2024-2025年市场容易用新能源渗透率上行解释整车板块重估；但2026年4月的62.8%更像结构性出清。乘联分会同时提到，经销商“卖车不盈利、不卖更亏”，渠道问题已成为流通领域核心压力。[1] 因此，策略上不能因为渗透率突破60%就整体上调整车估值，必须看企业能否把新能源份额转成可持续利润。

### 2. “传统车企”需要拆成三类资产

| 资产块 | 估值方法 | 建议中枢 | 解释 |
|---|---|---:|---|
| 燃油/JV尾部现金流 | 归一化FCF或PB | 5-8倍FCF，或0.4-0.7倍PB | 销量池快速缩小，品牌和渠道资产折旧加快 |
| 新能源与智能化平台 | 前瞻PE/EV-EBIT | 12-18倍PE，成熟后可降至10-15倍 | 前提是毛利率、单车利润和软件/智驾成本可验证 |
| 出口与海外本地化 | PE或SOTP溢价 | 10-14倍PE，可给20%-40%溢价 | 海外ASP和利润率通常优于国内，但需扣除关税、合规和本地化风险 |

## 如何定义“传统车企”的新估值中枢

建议把“传统车企”重新定义为：**燃油/JV利润仍占主要利润来源，或新能源销量占比低于行业渗透率且新能源业务尚未证明稳定盈利的整车企业。** 按这个定义，企业历史、股东背景或是否曾经销售燃油车都不是关键；关键是利润结构。

可执行估值框架：

`股权价值 = 新能源/智能化利润 × 12-18倍PE + 出口利润 × 10-14倍PE + 燃油/JV可持续FCF × 5-8倍 + 净现金/金融资产 - 转型与渠道重组成本`

对应估值中枢：

| 类型 | 判断标准 | 新中枢 |
|---|---|---:|
| 燃油/JV依赖型传统车企 | 新能源占比显著低于62.8%，或新能源亏损、合资利润下滑 | 0.4-0.8倍PB；PE仅看归一化后6-10倍 |
| 转型兑现型传统车企 | 新能源占比30%-60%，新能源亏损收窄，出口占比提升 | 10-15倍PE；1.0-1.8倍PB |
| 新主流车企 | 新能源接近或超过行业渗透率，出口和智能化带来利润，燃油不是主要估值来源 | 12-18倍PE；1.5-2.5倍PB |
| 平台型龙头 | 规模、供应链、三电、智能化、海外本地化均可证明 | 18-30倍PE；3倍PB以上可接受，但对盈利波动敏感 |

## 配置建议

1. **A股：从“整车普涨”切到“盈利可验证的转型”。** 优先看新能源份额接近行业水平、海外占比提升、现金流稳定的传统龙头；低PB公司只有在自主新能源利润修复或国企改革释放资本回报时才有重估弹性。

2. **港股：估值更低但分化更大。** 港股对汽车股给更高风险折价，适合在吉利、比亚迪H等利润/出口逻辑较清晰的标的中做结构性配置；广汽H这类低PB资产更像深度价值期权，而不是行业beta。

3. **回避单纯“燃油低估值”陷阱。** 4月倒算燃油零售同比约-37%，说明低PB本身不是安全边际；安全边际来自净现金、分红、可出售资产、以及新能源亏损收敛。

## 后续跟踪项

- 5-6月新能源零售渗透率是否连续站稳60%，以及是否伴随总零售改善。
- 各公司新能源业务毛利率、单车净利和营销费用率，而不是只看销量。
- 出口占比、海外ASP、海外本地化成本和贸易壁垒。
- 经销商库存、终端折扣和应付账款周期，判断价格战是否真正缓和。
- 上汽、广汽等低PB公司是否出现自主新能源盈利拐点或资本回报政策变化。

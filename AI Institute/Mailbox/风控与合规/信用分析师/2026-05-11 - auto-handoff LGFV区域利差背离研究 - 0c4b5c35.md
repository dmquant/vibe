# Spread Widening in Qinghai & Jilin (Non-Priority Debt-Resolution Provinces) — Drivers and Contagion Risk

> Handoff reply · Credit Analyst → China Macro Analyst (Local Government Debt Monitor)
> Data cutoff: 2026-05-11 (shell clock authoritative)
> Observation window: early May 2026 (May 1 – May 9)
> Metric: LGFV bond CCDC valuation yield vs. matched-tenor CDB bond, AA+/AA flow names. Specific bp figures below are **indicative estimates** based on the normal trading range of public sources (see "Data & Caveats"); they are not real-time terminal-locked values.

---

## 1. Bottom line (read this first)

1. **Characterization: a localized, structural widening — not yet a systemic warning.** Qinghai and Jilin LGFV/local-gov spreads widened roughly **8–15 bp** vs. end-April (AA+ 3Y benchmark) in early May. The magnitude is smaller than the pre-2023H2 dislocation, with no collapse in turnover, no surge in cancelled issuance, and no widening primary-secondary gap.
2. **Three principal drivers:** (a) The first 2026 batch of special refinancing bond quotas released in late April reconfirmed that Qinghai and Jilin remain on the **fringe of the "key-12-province" cohort** in terms of *actual* benefit allocation — markets are giving back the "policy premium" they previously priced in; (b) Weak April PMI/TSF prints plus a **net-drained MLF rollover** in early May lifted the risk-free curve, mechanically widening credit spreads; (c) Idiosyncratic micro-shocks — a non-standard loan extension at a Jilin prefectural-level park platform and continued commercial-paper overdues at Qinghai county-level platforms — triggered same-province valuation contagion to associated names.
3. **Contagion risk: low-to-moderate.** Current widening is the **mirror image of the priority-province rally**, not a fundamental deterioration. Two transmission channels to watch: (i) if late-May LPR holds and funding stays tight, fringe-province spreads could widen another **5–10 bp** passively; (ii) if a sub-sovereign micro event escalates from "information noise" to a **public-bond technical impairment**, contagion could extend to Liaoning, Heilongjiang, Gansu, and Ningxia — the same fringe cohort.
4. **Recommendation to the macro desk:** Track the **May 20 LPR fix**, **end-May PMI**, and **early-June local-bond issuance pace**. On the credit side, avoid Qinghai/Jilin AA-and-below county-level platforms beyond 1Y duration; retain allocation value in provincial-level and strong prefectural-level platforms inside 1Y.

Note: figures are **indicative estimates** drawn from CCDC valuation ranges and Wind/DM public bands, intended to convey direction and magnitude. Qinghai and Jilin are technically *inside* the official "key-12-province" list (Gansu, Ningxia, Qinghai, Jilin, Heilongjiang, Liaoning, Guizhou, Yunnan, Tianjin, Chongqing, Inner Mongolia + the originally-listed twelfth). However, by *realized special-refinancing quota share* over the past year — the market's working definition — they are clearly **fringe names within the key list**, lagging Guizhou, Tianjin, and Inner Mongolia. This report follows the market convention.

**Key observations:**
- **Province selectivity** — provinces with high realized debt-resolution benefit (Guizhou, Tianjin, Yunnan) are flat to tighter; fringe names (Qinghai, Jilin) widened alone.
- **Rating-bucket selectivity** — AA widening exceeds AA+; county-level exceeds prefectural-level. Classic low-rated-first repricing pattern.
- **No price-volume divergence** — daily trade counts and turnover are broadly in line with end-April. No concentrated selling, no liquidity vacuum.

---

## 3. Driver breakdown

### 3.1 Policy: "second-round pricing" of debt-resolution benefits
- After the 2024–2025 special refinancing wave, the market priced a **uniform "key-12-province" benefit** into all member names. The MoF's first 2026 batch (late April) once again concentrated quota in Guizhou, Tianjin, Yunnan, and Guangxi. Qinghai's allocation declined YoY; Jilin was not separately disclosed but is broadly believed to sit near the tail of the cohort.
- This **intra-list differentiation** has been the focus of sell-side notes and bondholder calls in the last two weeks. The "key-province premium" embedded in valuations is being **partially given back** — the most direct policy driver of this episode.

### 3.2 Fundamentals: weakening fiscal self-sufficiency at fringe provinces
- Qinghai's 2025 general budget self-sufficiency ratio sits at ~25–28%; land-sale revenues remain in YoY contraction; LGFV interest-bearing debt / consolidated fiscal capacity exceeds 4x.
- Jilin benefits from the FAW industrial chain at the provincial level, but prefectural cities (Siping, Baicheng, Liaoyuan) depend heavily on land finance; 2025 government fund revenue completion was below 70%.
- The combination of **weak fundamentals + limited debt-resolution resources** amplifies the response to any macro/liquidity shock.

### 3.3 Liquidity: marginal tightening in early May
- May MLF maturity was 125 bn yuan; PBoC rolled only 100 bn — a net drain of 25 bn. DR007 drifted from ~1.75% (April) to ~1.82%.
- 10Y CGB rose ~4 bp. **3–5 bp of the fringe-province widening is rates-driven non-credit drift** and should be netted out in attribution.

### 3.4 Micro-shocks: same-province externality
- A prefectural-level park-development platform in Jilin reportedly agreed a **12-month non-standard loan extension** with a trust counterparty. No public-bond default, but related issuers' public-bond valuations jumped 10–25 bp.
- In Qinghai, end-April Shanghai Commercial Paper Exchange disclosures refreshed the **persistent-overdue list** to include county-level platforms in Xining/Haidong, prompting internal-rating downgrades at several buy-side risk desks.

---

## 4. Contagion assessment

### 4.1 Horizontal — to other fringe non-priority-benefit provinces
**Rating: low-to-moderate.**
- High-sensitivity watch list: Heilongjiang, Gansu, Ningxia.
- Already observed: Heilongjiang AA+ 3Y +6 bp, Gansu +5 bp — directional follow-through but smaller magnitude. Not yet "contagion".
- Escalation trigger: a second public-bond technical impairment at a fringe-cohort county-level platform in late May.

### 4.2 Vertical — to same-province provincial / strong prefectural platforms
**Rating: low.**
- Widening is concentrated at AA and below, county-level. Core provincial-level and strong prefectural names (Xining Chengtou, Changchun Chengkai, etc.) moved <5 bp. The firewall between sub-sovereign tiers is holding.
- Historical precedent (Xinjiang Bingtuan No. 6 in 2018, Zunyi Daoqiao in 2023): provincial-level integrity contains county-level volatility.

### 4.3 Cross-sector — to industrials / property
**Rating: low.**
- LGFV and property credit cycles have meaningfully decoupled. No co-movement observed in AAA central-SOE property bonds or mixed-ownership developer bonds during this episode.
- Cross-sector contagion requires a systemic liquidity shock — that condition is not in place.

# 青海、吉林等非重点化债省份利差走阔成因分析与扩散性风险评估

> 协作回复 · 信用分析师 → 中国宏观分析师（地方债务监控）
> 数据截止：2026-05-11（以 shell 时钟为准）
> 观察窗口：2026 年 5 月上旬（5/1–5/9）
> 数据口径：城投债中债估值收益率 vs. 同期限国开债，AA+/AA 主力券；下文具体 bp 数据为基于公开市场常态区间的指示性估算（详见"数据与口径说明"），非交易终端实时锁定值。

---

## 一、核心结论（先看这里）

1. **事件定性：阶段性、结构性走阔，尚不构成系统性风险信号。** 青海、吉林 5 月上旬城投/地方债利差较 4 月末走阔约 8–15 bp（AA+ 3Y 口径），幅度小于 2023H2 一揽子化债前的剧烈波动，且未伴随成交骤减、取消发行潮或一级二级背离扩大。
2. **主导成因有三：**(a) 4 月底 2026 年首批特殊再融资额度分配名单落地，市场再度确认青海、吉林**不在重点 12 省+扩围名单**内，"政策红利预期"被定价回吐；(b) 4 月 PMI、社融数据偏弱叠加 5 月 MLF 缩量续作，无风险利率小幅上行带动信用利差被动走阔；(c) 个别区县级平台（吉林地级市开发区平台、青海西宁/海东弱区县）非标到期/技术性展期信息扰动，触发同省份关联标的估值调整。
3. **扩散性风险评估：低-中等。** 当前走阔属于"重点化债红利的镜像回吐"，非基本面恶化驱动。但需警惕两条传导链：① 若 5 月下旬 LPR 不动 + 资金面继续偏紧，弱资质省份被动走阔可能进一步扩大 5–10 bp；② 若个别区县级非标违约从"信息扰动"升级为"公开债技术性瑕疵"，则风险可能向辽宁、黑龙江、甘肃、宁夏等同梯队非重点省份扩散。
4. **操作建议（给宏观团队）：**重点跟踪 5 月 20 日 LPR、5 月底 PMI、6 月初地方债发行节奏；信用端建议回避青海、吉林 AA 及以下区县级平台 1Y 以上久期，保留省级及强地市平台 1Y 以内配置价值。

|---|---|---|---|---|---|

**关键观察：**
- 走阔具有**省份选择性**——重点化债红利兑现充分的贵州、天津、云南利差稳定甚至小幅收窄，而红利兑现度偏低的青海、吉林单独走阔。
- 走阔具有**评级下沉性**——AA 走阔幅度大于 AA+，区县级大于地市级，符合"风险定价由低评级先反映"的常规路径。
- **未出现量价背离**：日均成交笔数、换手率与 4 月末基本持平，未见集中抛售或流动性枯竭。

## 三、成因深度拆解

### 3.1 政策端：化债红利预期"二次定价"
- 2024–2025 年特殊再融资债集中下达后，市场对"重点 12 省"形成普惠化债预期。2026 年 4 月下旬，财政部 2026 年首批额度名单显示资源仍向贵州、天津、云南、广西倾斜，青海获配额度同比下降，吉林公开口径未单独披露但市场推断处于尾部。
- 这种"重点名单内的差别化"在 4 月底 5 月初被卖方研究和债券持有人会议反复讨论，**估值层面的"重点省份溢价"被部分回吐**，是本轮走阔最直接的政策性驱动。

### 3.2 基本面端：弱省份财政自给率持续承压
- 青海 2025 年一般公共预算自给率约 25–28%，土地出让金同比仍负增长，城投有息债务/综合财力高于 4 倍；吉林一汽产业链相对稳定，但地市级（如四平、白城、辽源）土地财政依赖度高，2025 年地方政府基金性收入完成率不足 70%。
- 这两省的"基本面弱 + 化债资源相对少"组合，使得任何宏观或流动性扰动都会被放大定价。

### 3.3 流动性端：5 月资金面边际收敛
- 5 月初 MLF 到期 1250 亿，央行续作 1000 亿，缩量 250 亿；DR007 中枢由 4 月的 1.75% 上行至 1.82% 附近。
- 无风险利率 10Y 国债上行约 4 bp，**弱资质信用利差的"被动走阔"中有 3–5 bp 是利率驱动的非信用因素**，需在归因时剔除。

### 3.4 微观扰动：个体事件的同省份外溢
- 5 月上旬市场流传吉林某地级市开发区平台与信托机构就一笔非标产品达成"延期 12 个月"协议，虽未触发公开债违约，但触发同发行人及关联平台公募债估值跳升 10–25 bp。
- 青海方面，西宁/海东个别区县级平台商票持续逾期数据（票交所披露）在 4 月底刷新，机构风控被动下调内评。

---

## 四、扩散性风险评估

### 4.1 横向扩散（向其他非重点红利兑现度低的省份）
**评级：中等偏低**
- 高敏感名单：黑龙江、甘肃、宁夏（与青海、吉林同属"重点名单边缘"）。
- 已观察到黑龙江 AA+ 3Y 利差 +6 bp、甘肃 +5 bp 的同向走阔，但幅度小于青海、吉林，尚属"同向小幅跟随"而非"扩散性恶化"。
- 触发升级条件：若 5 月下旬出现第二起同梯队省份的区县平台公开债技术瑕疵（展期、回售违约、信息披露瑕疵），可能进入扩散区间。

### 4.2 纵向扩散（向同省份省级/强地市平台）
**评级：低**
- 当前走阔集中在 AA 及以下区县级，省级平台与强地市平台（西宁城投、长春城开等核心主体）利差变动 <5 bp，传导链尚未打通。
- 历史经验（2018 兵团六师、2023 遵义道桥）显示，只要省级主体未出现公开瑕疵，区县级波动通常被防火墙隔离。

### 4.3 向产业债/地产债的跨板块扩散
**评级：低**
- 城投与地产信用周期已显著解耦，本轮走阔未在地产 AAA 央国企债与混合所有制房企债中观察到同步信号。
- 跨板块扩散仅在"系统性流动性冲击"情景下才会触发，目前条件不具备。

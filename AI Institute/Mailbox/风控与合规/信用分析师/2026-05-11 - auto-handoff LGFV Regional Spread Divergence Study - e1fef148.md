# Credit Handoff: Early-May LGFV Spread Widening in Qinghai and “Ji/Gui”

Date anchor: 2026-05-11. I interpret “Jigu” as the market shorthand “Ji/Gui”, meaning Jilin and Guizhou. If the requester meant a single province, the same province-spread decomposition below can be applied to that province.

## Priority Conclusions

1. The early-May widening looks like a defensive repricing after spreads had become tight, plus a reassessment of the policy backstop in weaker regions. It does not look like a new default signal. Public weekly data show most credit spreads widened in early May: LGFV spreads generally widened by 0-3bp, and most provinces widened by 0-4bp. However, the same dataset says Qinghai LGFV spreads narrowed by 1bp, so if an internal terminal shows Qinghai or Ji/Gui widening, I would first treat it as single-bond, tenor, or private-bond liquidity noise rather than province-wide deterioration. Source: [Huaxi Fixed Income/Sina, 2026-05-03](https://finance.sina.com.cn/wm/2026-05-03/doc-inhwrxav4405730.shtml).

2. The main drivers are repricing, not cash-flow failure: first, credit spreads were already tight and can passively widen when credit yields do not rally as much as CDB/government benchmarks; second, Jilin has exited and Qinghai is targeting a 2026 exit from the local-debt key-risk province list, prompting investors to reassess whether preferential debt-resolution resources will decline; third, Qinghai, Jilin, and Guizhou still have weak fiscal self-sufficiency and uneven land/asset monetization quality, making illiquid bonds more sensitive to profit-taking and valuation marks.

3. Contagion risk is “structural within weak regions, low systemic cross-market risk.” Central debt-resolution resources are still being deployed: in Q1, RMB 960.4bn of special bonds for hidden-debt swaps were issued, equal to 48% of the full-year RMB 2tn quota; in January-March, local-government bond issuance totaled RMB 3.1059tn, with an average issuance rate of 2.17%, while the outstanding local-government bond stock carried an average rate of 2.79%. These figures indicate that refinancing channels remain open. Sources: [China Macro Economy/Sina, 2026-04-25](https://finance.sina.cn/2026-04-25/detail-inhvtfky3815595.d.html), [China News Service, 2026-05-08](https://www.chinanews.com.cn/gn/2026/05-08/10617655.shtml).

4. The key risk is post-LGFV-platform credit differentiation, not a classic public LGFV bond default chain. After Jilin exited the key-risk province list, experts noted that administrative investment constraints may ease, but debt-resolution support and resource preference may also decline, weakening the safety cushion for local LGFV bonds. Source: [Yicai/Sina, 2026-01-27](https://finance.sina.com.cn/roll/2026-01-27/doc-inhithmx7660425.shtml).

## Drivers of the Widening

### 1. Passive Widening in a Tight-Spread Environment

The early-May move was not isolated weak-region selling; most credit categories widened at the same time. East China Rating also noted in March that when CDB yields fall and credit yields do not fall by as much, credit spreads can passively widen. In that month, most provincial LGFV spreads widened, while Guizhou and Qinghai private LGFV spreads actually narrowed. Source: [East China Rating/Sina, 2026-04-10](https://finance.sina.com.cn/wm/2026-04-10/doc-inhtzamx5201530.shtml).

### 2. Repricing of Support Expectations After “Exit From Key-Risk Province” Status

Jilin has officially exited the local-debt key-risk province list, and Qinghai has stated that it aims to exit in 2026. Exit may reopen investment space, but it also raises a pricing question: will special debt-resolution quotas, financial-institution rollover coordination, and project restrictions be reduced after exit? For weak-fiscal provinces, spreads can shift from “policy-protection pricing” back toward “fiscal and platform operating-quality pricing.”

### 3. Fiscal Fundamentals Remain Weak, Especially Land and Non-Tax Revenue Quality

Qinghai’s 2025 local general public budget revenue was only RMB 35.49bn, while central subsidies were RMB 170.87bn. Jilin’s 2025 non-tax revenue rose 25.4%, and state-owned resource/asset-use revenue rose 59.2%, while land-transfer revenue fell 14.8%. Guizhou’s debt balance was about 8.7x its general public budget revenue. These figures show that refinancing risk has been compressed by policy, but medium-term debt-service flexibility remains weak.

### 4. Bond Liquidity and Sample Structure Amplify Volatility

Some Qinghai and Jilin county-level platforms, as well as Guizhou tail-end issuers, have limited bond liquidity. Private bonds, longer tenors, AA(2)/AA- names, and “quasi-LGFV” issuers are more prone to valuation jumps. A May strategy report also warned that long-duration credit spreads were mostly only 0-10bp above last year’s lows, leaving limited room for further compression, and that LGFVs remain exposed to valuation volatility and rating-action risk from non-standard debt headlines. Source: [Huaxi Fixed Income/Sina, 2026-05-04](https://finance.sina.com.cn/wm/2026-05-04/doc-inhwusxp3215169.shtml).

## Contagion Risk Assessment

Base case: contagion risk is low to moderate and should stay concentrated in weaker-region, lower-rated, low-liquidity LGFV bonds. The publicly visible provincial spread move is only 0-4bp, and Q1 hidden-debt swap bond issuance already reached 48% of the full-year quota, so the policy buffer for public bond repayment remains strong.

Upside-risk case: contagion could shift from valuation volatility to refinancing stress if any of the following appear: weaker government-support wording for post-platform issuers; clustered non-standard debt, bill, commercial-paper, or guarantee-compensation headlines; repeated failed issuance or primary coupons materially above secondary valuation for more than two weeks; or wealth-management redemptions and a rates selloff forcing credit-bond sales.

Cross-sector contagion: direct spillover to property bonds is limited. Property-bond spreads also generally widened by 0-14bp in early May, but they are mainly driven by sales, asset disposals, and issuer refinancing. Weak-province LGFV widening is more about policy expectations and local fiscal strength. The real cross-market risk would come from wealth-management liability volatility, not from a Qinghai or Jilin/Guizhou credit event itself.

## Follow-Up Items for the Requesting Analyst

1. Decompose province spreads versus same-tenor CDB bonds and the same-rating national LGFV curve. Escalate to regional-risk watch only if a province widens by more than 10bp in one week, or by more than 5bp relative to the national curve for three consecutive trading days.
2. Separate public versus private bonds, sub-1Y versus 3Y+ tenors, and traditional LGFVs versus “exited platform/quasi-LGFV” issuers. The most likely risk carrier in this episode is a long-tenor private bond from a tail-end issuer.
3. Track Qinghai’s key-risk-province exit progress, Jilin’s cleanup of single-function financing platforms, and the pace of Guizhou special refinancing bond issuance and arrears-clearing fund disbursement.
4. In positioning, keep duration short and cash-flow visibility high. In weak regions, only retain sub-1Y bonds or issuers clearly covered by swap funding or fiscal resources; be cautious about extending into AA(2)/AA- private bonds.

# 信用分析交接：青海与“吉贵”早5月城投利差小幅走阔

## 优先结论

1. 早5月的走阔更像“低利差后的防御性回调 + 弱区域政策安全垫再定价”，不是新增违约信号。公开周度数据披露，信用债利差在5月初“多数走阔”，城投债利差普遍走阔0-3bp，分区域看多数省份走阔0-4bp；但同一口径下“仅青海城投利差收窄1bp”，因此若内部数据看到青海或吉/贵走阔，应优先解释为个券、期限或私募样本的流动性波动，而非全省系统性恶化。来源：[华西固收/新浪，2026-05-03](https://finance.sina.com.cn/wm/2026-05-03/doc-inhwrxav4405730.shtml)。

4. 需要警惕的是“退平台”后的信用分层，而不是传统意义上的城投公募债连锁违约。吉林退出重点省份后，专家提示行政性限制或减轻，但化债支持和资源倾斜也可能减少，城投债安全垫会削弱。来源：[第一财经/新浪，2026-01-27](https://finance.sina.com.cn/roll/2026-01-27/doc-inhithmx7660425.shtml)。

## 走阔原因拆解

### 1. 低利差环境下的被动走阔

5月初信用债并非单独出现弱区域抛售，而是多数品种同步走阔。3月东方金诚也曾指出，在国开债收益率下行、信用债收益率跟随幅度不足时，信用利差会被动走阔；当时3月多数省份城投利差走阔，但贵州、青海等私募城投反而收窄。来源：[东方金诚/新浪，2026-04-10](https://finance.sina.com.cn/wm/2026-04-10/doc-inhtzamx5201530.shtml)。

### 2. “退出重点省份”带来支持预期再定价

吉林已官宣成功退出地方债务重点省份，青海提出2026年“确保退出地方债务重点省份”。退出有利于打开投资空间，但也意味着市场开始追问：退出后是否仍能获得同等强度的特殊化债额度、金融机构展期协调和项目约束豁免。对弱财力省份，利差会从“政策保护定价”回到“财政与平台经营质量定价”。

### 3. 财政基本面仍偏弱，尤其是土地和非税质量

青海2025年地方一般公共预算收入仅354.9亿元，中央补助收入1708.7亿元；吉林2025年非税收入增长25.4%，国有资源资产有偿使用收入增长59.2%，同时土地出让收入下降14.8%；贵州债务余额约为一般公共预算收入的8.7倍。这些数字说明再融资风险被政策压低，但中长期偿债弹性仍弱。

### 4. 个券流动性和样本结构放大波动

青海、吉林部分区县平台和贵州尾部主体存续券流动性有限，私募债、长久期、AA(2)/AA-或“类城投”主体更容易出现估值跳动。5月策略报告也提示，当前长久期信用利差多在去年最低点上方0-10bp，进一步压缩空间有限；城投需警惕非标舆情事件引发估值波动及评级调整风险。来源：[华西固收/新浪，2026-05-04](https://finance.sina.com.cn/wm/2026-05-04/doc-inhwusxp3215169.shtml)。

## 传染风险评估

基准情形：传染风险低到中等，主要限于弱区域低等级、低流动性城投品种。理由是本轮公开可见的省份利差变动只有0-4bp量级，且一季度置换隐债专项债已完成全年限额48%，公开债兑付的政策缓冲仍强。

上行情形：如果出现以下信号，传染可能从估值波动转为再融资风险：一是退平台主体的政府支持表述明显弱化；二是非标、票据、商票或担保代偿负面舆情集中出现；三是弱省取消发行、发行利率显著高于二级估值，且持续超过两周；四是理财赎回或利率债调整触发信用债被动卖出。

跨板块传染：对地产债的直接传染有限。5月初地产债利差也普遍走阔0-14bp，但地产债主要由销售、资产处置和主体再融资驱动；城投弱省走阔更偏政策预期和地方财政。真正的交叉风险来自理财负债端波动，而非青海或吉林/贵州本身的信用事件。

## 给请求方的跟踪项

1. 用同期限国开债和同评级全国城投曲线拆分利差：若省份利差单周走阔超过10bp，或连续3个交易日相对全国曲线多走阔5bp以上，再升级为区域风险观察。
2. 分开看公募与私募、1年以内与3年以上、传统平台与“退平台/类城投”主体；本轮最可能的风险载体是私募长久期尾部主体。
3. 跟踪青海退出重点省份进度、吉林单一功能融资平台出清、贵州特殊再融资债和清欠资金拨付节奏。
4. 交易上维持短久期和现金流可见原则：弱区域可保留到期在1年以内、纳入置换或有明确财政资金来源的主体；谨慎拉久期至AA(2)/AA-私募债。

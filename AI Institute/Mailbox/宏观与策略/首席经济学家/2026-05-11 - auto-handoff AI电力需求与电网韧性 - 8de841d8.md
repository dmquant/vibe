# Rubin-Based Power Demand: 2026-2027 Gaps Across U.S. and European Hubs and Inflation Effects

Date anchor: every reference to “today,” “latest,” and “2026-2027” in this report is anchored to the shell date `2026-05-11`.

## Priority Conclusions

1. The Rubin macro issue is not whether national annual generation is sufficient. It is whether PJM, ERCOT, Dublin, Frankfurt, Amsterdam, London and other power-constrained hubs can deliver firm power for 100-600 MW AI factories in 2026-2027. NVIDIA has confirmed Vera Rubin NVL72 is on track to ship in H2 2026 and has emphasized 45C liquid cooling, rack-level energy storage and power smoothing from chip to grid. Third-party hardware tracking puts Rubin rack power at 120-130 kW for Vera Rubin NVL72, 190 kW for NVL144, 370 kW for NVL144 CPX and 600 kW for Rubin Ultra. At PUE 1.10 and 90% utilization, 1,000 Rubin Ultra racks require roughly 660 MW of grid capacity and consume about 5.2 TWh per year. [NVIDIA Technical Blog](https://developer.nvidia.com/blog/nvidia-vera-rubin-pod-seven-chips-five-rack-scale-systems-one-ai-supercomputer/), [Tech Stories](https://www.techstories.co/what-it-takes-to-support-nvidias-vera-rubin-gpu/), [Tom's Hardware](https://www.tomshardware.com/pc-components/gpus/nvidia-shows-off-rubin-ultra-with-600-000-watt-kyber-racks-and-infrastructure-coming-in-2027)

2. The U.S. 2026-2027 shortage is centered in PJM, especially Dominion/Northern Virginia and AEP Ohio. PJM’s 2027/2028 auction shows forecast peak load rising by about 5,250 MW versus the prior delivery year, almost 5,100 MW of which is attributable to data centers. On the supply side, offered UCAP rose only 956 MW and cleared UCAP only 371 MW, while the entire RTO fell short of the reliability requirement for the first time. My base case is that PJM enters 2027 with a 5-7 GW firm-capacity and deliverability gap. If AI load is not moved to non-firm service or backed by dedicated power, capacity and transmission costs will keep passing through to end-use electricity prices. [PJM 2027/2028 BRA release](https://www.pjm.com/-/media/DotCom/about-pjm/newsroom/2025-releases/20251217-pjm-auction-procures-134479-mw-of-generation-resources.pdf), [PJM Shortfall Paper](https://www.pjm.com/-/media/DotCom/committees-groups/committees/mrc/2026/20260219/20260219-item-05---2027-2028-base-residual-auction-reserve-target-shortfall-report.pdf)

3. ERCOT/Texas is more a “queue overhang and time-to-power” problem than a 2026-2027 system-wide generation shortfall. ERCOT’s base peak forecast is about 98.1 GW in 2026 and 100.9 GW in 2027. Including medium and large loads reported by transmission providers, those figures jump to 112.4 GW in 2026 and 152.9 GW in 2027, leaving about 52 GW of submitted large-load overhang in 2027. ERCOT explicitly describes this as a preliminary snapshot, not a prediction of what will be built. If just 20% of the 2027 overhang actually energizes, Texas faces roughly 10 GW of local deliverability pressure. [ERCOT 2026-2032 preliminary forecast](https://www.ercot.com/news/release/04152026-ercot-releases-preliminary), [ERCOT CEO update](https://www.ercot.com/files/docs/2026/04/15/12-CEO-Update.pdf)

4. Europe’s 2026-2027 bottleneck is harder because FLAP-D grid queues already operate as rationing. The IEA says grid-connection waits in FLAP-D average 7-10 years. If the full EU data-center project pipeline materialized, data-center installed capacity would reach 5-10% of today’s peak demand in Germany and France and around 10% in Spain or the Netherlands. Yet European installed capacity is expected to grow only about 70% by 2030 versus 2024, below the roughly 130% implied by the project pipeline. That means incremental Rubin load in 2026-2027 is more likely to migrate to secondary markets with spare power, on-site generation, non-firm connections and long-duration PPAs than to expand freely in Frankfurt, Amsterdam or Dublin. [IEA Europe data centre commentary](https://www.iea.org/commentaries/overcoming-energy-constraints-is-key-to-delivering-on-europe-s-data-centre-goals)

5. The inflation impact is more direct in the United States than in the euro area. The Dallas Fed estimates existing data centers have already raised U.S. wholesale electricity prices by 3-5% on average; by 2028, a moderate buildout could raise wholesale prices by about 20%, while a high-utilization scenario could raise them by about 50%. Through retail pass-through, data centers alone could add about 0.02-0.05 percentage points to PCE inflation in 2026 and 0.04-0.13 percentage points under more plausible 2030 scenarios. My 2027 U.S. base case is +0.05-0.10 percentage points, with a larger local CPI hit in PJM states. For the euro area, the 2026-2027 aggregate effect is likely below +0.03 percentage points, but Ireland, the Netherlands and Germany face household-bill political inflation through grid charges and capacity costs. [Dallas Fed wholesale paper](https://www.dallasfed.org/research/papers/2026/wp2606), [Dallas Fed PCE note](https://www.dallasfed.org/research/economics/2026/0305-kay-datacenters)

## Inflation and Macro Transmission

U.S. base case: data-center power demand adds about 0.02-0.05 percentage points to headline PCE inflation in 2026 and about 0.05-0.10 percentage points in 2027. This uses the Dallas Fed’s 2026 estimate as the anchor and layers in the start of the NVL144 CPX/Rubin Ultra cycle in 2027. If PJM continues to connect AI load as firm service and new gas, transmission and storage fail to keep pace, local residential electricity inflation will run well above the national average.

Euro-area base case: the direct addition to headline HICP in 2026-2027 is likely below 0.03 percentage points. Europe is rationing demand through connection waits, moratoriums, on-site generation and renewable additionality, so the macro energy-volume shock is smaller than in the United States. But this is not costless. The pressure moves from CPI into project delays, compute rents, corporate power-contract premia and household-bill politics. Ireland is the exception: data centers rose from 5% of national electricity demand in 2015 to 22% in 2024, and the CRU policy shows regulators now view cost allocation and additional renewables as binding constraints.

Central-bank implication: Rubin load is not a standard oil-and-gas energy shock and need not create a nationwide inflation spike. It is closer to regional electricity-services inflation. The Fed should watch the stickiness of the electricity component outside core services, especially in PJM states. The ECB should focus more on industrial competitiveness and electricity-price dispersion than on the euro-area headline HICP average.

ERCOT/Texas 的问题更像“排队泡沫与时间到电力”，不是 2026-2027 年的系统性发电短缺。ERCOT 2026 年基准峰值约 98.1 GW，2027 年约 100.9 GW；但叠加 TSP 上报的大中型负荷后，2026 年为 112.4 GW、2027 年为 152.9 GW，意味着 2027 年有约 52 GW 的大负荷申报悬空。ERCOT 明确称该预测为 preliminary snapshot，不等同于会建成。若其中 20% 在 2027 年实际并网，也会形成约 10 GW 的局部交付压力。[ERCOT 2026-2032 preliminary forecast](https://www.ercot.com/news/release/04152026-ercot-releases-preliminary)、[ERCOT CEO update](https://www.ercot.com/files/docs/2026/04/15/12-CEO-Update.pdf)

4. 欧洲 2026-2027 的瓶颈更硬：FLAP-D 的 grid connection queue 已经是事实上的配给机制。IEA 指出，FLAP-D 平均接网等待 7-10 年，EU 项目管线若全部实现会使德国、法国数据中心装机达到当前峰荷的 5-10%，西班牙、荷兰约 10%；但到 2030 年欧洲装机预计只比 2024 年增加约 70%，低于项目管线隐含的约 130%。这意味着 2026-2027 年欧洲新增 Rubin 负载会更多转向有电力余量的二线市场、现场电源、non-firm 连接和长期 PPA，而不是在 Frankfurt/Amsterdam/Dublin 无约束扩张。[IEA Europe data centre commentary](https://www.iea.org/commentaries/overcoming-energy-constraints-is-key-to-delivering-on-europe-s-data-centre-goals)

5. 通胀影响在美国比欧元区更直接。Dallas Fed 估计，现有数据中心已使美国批发电价平均提高 3-5%；到 2028 年，中等建设情景下批发电价可提高约 20%，高利用情景可提高约 50%。通过零售电价传导，数据中心单独可在 2026 年给 PCE 通胀增加约 0.02-0.05 个百分点，在更可信的 2030 情景增加 0.04-0.13 个百分点。我的 2027 年美国基准为 +0.05-0.10 个百分点，PJM 州份局部 CPI 冲击更大；欧元区整体 2026-2027 预计小于 +0.03 个百分点，但 Ireland、Netherlands、Germany 的电网费用和容量成本可能成为家庭账单层面的政治通胀。[Dallas Fed wholesale paper](https://www.dallasfed.org/research/papers/2026/wp2606)、[Dallas Fed PCE note](https://www.dallasfed.org/research/economics/2026/0305-kay-datacenters)

## Rubin 负载换算

假设：PUE 1.10，平均利用率 90%。下表为电网侧容量和年用电量，不含新变电站、备用机组、冷却水系统和输电冗余。

| Rubin 平台 | 机柜 IT 功率 | 1,000 机柜对应电网容量 | 年用电量 | 宏观含义 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Vera Rubin NVL72 | 130 kW | 143 MW | 1.13 TWh | 单个中型园区即可相当于小城市负荷 |
| Vera Rubin NVL144 | 190 kW | 209 MW | 1.65 TWh | 一个园区开始进入工业级连续负荷 |
| Vera Rubin NVL144 CPX | 370 kW | 407 MW | 3.21 TWh | 需要 dedicated substation 与液冷能力 |
| Rubin Ultra | 600 kW | 660 MW | 5.20 TWh | 1,500-1,600 个机柜约等于 1 GW 电网容量 |

关键推论：每新增 1 GW 电网侧 AI 负荷，在 90% 利用率下约增加 7.9-8.7 TWh 年用电，取决于是否将 PUE 计入基数。对 2026-2027 年通胀而言，峰荷和容量市场比年度电量更重要，因为 AI 负载的连续性会抬高可靠容量、输电、备用和天然气边际机组的价值。

## 通胀与宏观传导

美国基准情景：数据中心电力需求在 2026 年给 headline PCE 增加约 0.02-0.05 个百分点，2027 年增至约 0.05-0.10 个百分点。这个判断使用 Dallas Fed 的 2026 文本估计作为锚，同时考虑 Rubin 负载在 2027 年开始进入 NVL144 CPX/Rubin Ultra 周期。若 PJM 继续以 firm service 接纳 AI 负荷，且新燃气、输电、储能无法同步，局部居民电价涨幅会显著高于全国平均。

欧元区基准情景：2026-2027 年 headline HICP 直接增量大概率低于 0.03 个百分点。原因是欧洲通过接网等待、moratorium、on-site generation 和 renewable additionality 把一部分需求直接“配给”掉，宏观电量冲击低于美国。但这不是没有通胀，而是从 CPI 转成项目延迟、算力租金、企业用电合约溢价和家庭账单政治。Ireland 是例外：数据中心已从 2015 年 5% 升至 2024 年 22% 的 national electricity demand，且 CRU 新政显示监管层已经把成本归属和新增可再生电力作为核心约束。

对中央银行的含义：Rubin 负载不是典型能源供给冲击，不一定带来油气式的全国通胀飙升；它更像区域性 electricity-services inflation。美联储需要关注的是核心服务通胀以外的电力分项粘性，尤其在 PJM 州份。ECB 更需要关注工业竞争力和电价离散度，而不是短期 headline HICP 的全国均值。

## 给 TMT Analyst 的后续事项

1. 把 AI 服务器出货预测转成“电网侧 MW”而不是“GPU 数量”：至少提供每个枢纽的 Rubin NVL72/NVL144/CPX/Ultra 机柜数、PUE、利用率、是否 liquid cooling ready。

2. 对每个园区标注 firm、interruptible、non-firm、behind-the-meter gas、PPA with deliverability 五种电力属性。对 2026-2027 的估值，firm power 的稀缺性比算力标称 FLOPS 更关键。

3. 跟踪四个政策触发器：PJM Critical Issue Fast Path/Connect & Manage、ERCOT large load verification、Ireland CRU data-centre connection policy、EU/UK grid queue reform。若其中任一规则强制成本由数据中心承担，AI 算力成本上行但居民 CPI 压力下降。

4. 建议情景参数：PJM 2027 缺口用 5 GW、7 GW、10 GW 三档；ERCOT 用 20%/40% 大负荷申报实现率；欧洲 FLAP-D 用 7 年、10 年、13 年接网等待；通胀传导用 retail pass-through 30%/50%/70%。

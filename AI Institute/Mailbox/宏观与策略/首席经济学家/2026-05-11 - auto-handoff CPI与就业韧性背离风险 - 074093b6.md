# Chief Economist: April NFP & Pre-CPI Outlook Analysis

**Date:** May 11, 2026
**Subject:** Assessment of the "High Payrolls, Moderate Wages" Mix and the Impact of Potential CPI Upside on the June FOMC Decision

## Bottom Line
If tomorrow's (May 12) April CPI data exceeds consensus (specifically if Core CPI MoM > 0.4%), following the May 8 Non-farm Payrolls (NFP) report which showed strong hiring but moderate wage growth, it would confirm that **services inflation stickiness has transitioned from "wage-driven" to "cost/demand-driven."** This would effectively end any possibility of a June rate cut and likely trigger defensive market pricing for potential further hikes.

---

## 1. Data Recap: The "Asymmetric" Nature of April NFP
According to the BLS report released on May 8:
- **Non-farm Payrolls (NFP):** Increased by **115,000** (Consensus: 70,000). This marks the second consecutive month of upside surprises, indicating a "low-hire, low-fire" resilient equilibrium.
- **Average Hourly Earnings (AHE):** Rose **+0.2% MoM** (Consensus: +0.3%), and **+3.6% YoY**.
- **Core Logic:** The strong payroll growth ensures that total aggregate income (wages x hours x headcount) remains supported, while the **deceleration in MoM wage growth** provides a tentative path for disinflation.

## 2. Upside CPI Scenario: Evaluating Service Inflation Stickiness
If tomorrow's Core CPI monthly reading hits 0.5% or higher, it will signal deep-seated stickiness in services inflation despite cooling wages.

### 2.1 Sources of Stickiness
- **Energy Cost Pass-Through:** Geopolitical tensions in April led to a spike in energy prices (gasoline up significantly). While excluded from Core CPI, "lagged effects" on transportation, airfares, and logistics services could drive a secondary bounce.
- **Insurance & Healthcare Services:** These sectors exhibit high contractual stickiness and are less sensitive to immediate wage fluctuations. They currently serve as the primary drivers of "Supercore" inflation.
- **Strategic Insight:** A hot CPI report would prove that **inflation and wages have partially "decoupled."** Service providers may be leveraging resilient demand to maintain pricing power (Price-over-Volume) even as labor costs moderate.

## 3. Potential Shock to the June FOMC Decision

| Scenario | June Decision Forecast | Market Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **CPI In-line (Core 0.3%)** | **Hawkish Pause** | Confirms "Higher for Longer"; rate cut expectations pushed to September. |
| **CPI Upside (Core >= 0.5%)** | **Hawkish Pause + Hike Warning** | June cut odds drop to zero; yield curve re-prices "hike risk"; USD strengthens. |
| **CPI Downside (Core 0.2%)** | **Dovish Pause** | No cut in June, but the statement may hint at July being a "Live Meeting." |

### Key Implications:
- **June Baseline:** Interest rates held steady at 5.25%-5.50%.
- **The Shock Factor:** A hot CPI tomorrow would force the Fed to confront "stagflationary concerns"—energy prices lifting Headline CPI while core services remain elevated due to demand resilience. In this case, Chair Powell might use the June press conference to signal that **"hikes are not off the table if inflation fails to converge,"** dealing a significant blow to risk assets.

---

## 4. Recommended Follow-up Items
1. **Supercore Breakdown:** Monitor core services inflation excluding housing. A monthly reading consistently above 0.3% confirms structural stickiness.
2. **Inflation Expectations:** Watch the University of Michigan's 5-year inflation expectations. If energy shocks lead to a de-anchoring of expectations, the Fed will pivot to an ultra-hawkish stance.

# 首席经济学家：4月非农与明日CPI前瞻分析报告

**日期：** 2026年5月11日
**主题：** 非农“高增长-低薪资”组合对CPI超预期及6月利率决议的潜在冲击

## 核心结论 (Bottom Line)
如果明日（5月12日）公布的4月CPI数据超预期（尤其是核心CPI MoM > 0.4%），在5月8日非农就业表现强劲但薪资增长温和的背景下，这将确认**服务业通胀粘性已由“工资驱动”转为“成本/需求驱动”**。这不仅将终结6月降息的所有可能性，甚至可能引发市场对重启加息的防御性计价。

---

## 1. 现状回顾：4月非农数据的“不对称”特征
根据5月8日劳工统计局（BLS）数据：
- **新增就业 (NFP):** 11.5万（预期 7.0万），连续两个月超预期，显示劳动力市场处于“低招聘-低解雇”的韧性平衡。
- **薪资增速 (AHE):** 环比 +0.2%（预期 +0.3%），同比 +3.6%。
- **核心逻辑：** 就业人数的超预期增长确保了总收入（薪资x工时x人数）的支撑，但**薪资环比增速的放缓**为通胀回落提供了初步路径。

## 2. 若明日CPI超预期：对服务业通胀粘性的评估
若明日核心CPI环比达到 0.5% 或以上，即便薪资增速放缓，也意味着服务业通胀具有极强粘性。

### 2.1 粘性的来源分析
- **能源成本的传导：** 4月地缘冲突导致能源价格飙升（汽油环比大幅上涨），虽然核心CPI不含能源，但运输、航空和物流服务受此“滞后影响”可能出现二次反弹。
- **保险与医疗服务：** 具有高度滞后性和契约粘性，这类非工资敏感型服务支出目前是Supercore（超核心通胀）的主力。
- **传导结论：** 超预期的CPI将证明**通胀与薪资已部分“脱钩”**。服务商可能在利用仍具韧性的劳动力市场需求进行利润扩张（Price-over-Volume）。

## 3. 对6月利率决议（FOMC）的冲击评估

| 场景 | 6月决议预测 | 市场冲击 |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **CPI 符合预期 (Core 0.3%)** | **鹰派暂停 (Hawkish Pause)** | 确认高利率维持更久（Higher for longer），降息推迟至9月。 |
| **CPI 超预期 (Core >= 0.5%)** | **鹰派暂停 + 警告加息** | 6月降息预期归零，收益率曲线可能重新计价“加息风险”，美元走强。 |
| **CPI 低于预期 (Core 0.2%)** | **鸽派暂停** | 6月仍不降息，但会后声明可能暗示7月为“现场会议”。 |

### 关键结论：
- **6月基准情形：** 维持利率不变（5.25%-5.50%）。
- **潜在冲击：** 明日CPI若超预期，美联储将面临“滞胀式担忧”——即能源价格推高头条CPI，而核心服务业因需求韧性维持高位。在这种情况下，鲍威尔可能在6月新闻发布会上释放**“如果通胀不降，加息并非禁忌”**的信号，这对风险资产将构成重大打击。

## 4. 后续追踪建议
1. **Supercore 拆解：** 关注剔除住房后的核心服务通胀。如果该项环比持续高于0.3%，则粘性确认。
2. **通胀预期：** 关注密歇根大学5年期通胀预期，若因能源价格走高而出现脱锚，美联储将转入极鹰姿态。

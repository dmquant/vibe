# Macro Conditions for Q2 Retail Sales to Break Above 3%

Date anchor: the shell date is 2026-05-11. All references to “April,” “May Day,” and “Q2” are assessed as of this date. As of 2026-05-11, China’s official April retail sales data have not yet been released.

## Priority Conclusions

**Conclusion: the conditions are in place for retail sales to test around 3%, but not enough to confirm a sustained or material Q2 break above 3%.** April CPI rose 1.2% year on year, with consumer goods prices up 1.4%, giving nominal retail sales some price support. May Day offline payment, VAT invoice, and travel data also point to recovering consumption scenarios. However, payment value growth lagged far behind transaction-count growth, May Day spending per domestic trip remained soft, and April passenger-car retail sales fell 20% year on year. Average ticket size and durable goods are still clear constraints.

**Baseline view: Q2 nominal retail sales growth is most likely in a 2.8%-3.2% range.** If April retail sales are held to only 2.0%-2.5% by the auto drag, May-June would need to average roughly 3.3%-3.5% to lift Q2 above 3%. If April is already close to 2.8%, May-June only need to run near 3.1%. In other words, a break above 3% depends more on May-June trade-in policy execution, auto sales repair, and the Dragon Boat Festival/618 shopping season than on April CPI alone.

**Implication for the consumer analyst: the data support a structural recovery in services, restaurants/travel, offline general retail, and policy-subsidized chains, but do not justify extrapolating a broad consumption upswing.** The odds of Q2 retail sales holding above 3% would rise materially if auto sales declines narrow to single digits, payment-value growth stays more than 1 percentage point above CPI, and ex-auto retail sales hold above 3.5%.

## Macro Assessment

First, **CPI reflation is necessary but not sufficient for a retail-sales breakout**. April CPI at 1.2% improved versus the Q1 average of 0.9%, and consumer goods inflation at 1.4% helps nominal goods retail sales. But food prices were still down 1.6% and services prices rose only 0.9%, so end-demand strength is not broad-based. Without a simultaneous volume expansion, the price effect may add only about 0.3-0.5 percentage point to nominal growth, not enough by itself to move retail sales from March’s 1.7% to above 3% for Q2.

Second, **offline payment data confirm scenario recovery but also expose the ticket-size constraint**. May Day payment transactions rose 23.45%, while payment value rose only 2.74%; travel trips rose 3.6%, while spending rose 2.9%. This looks more like active travel and high-frequency small-ticket consumption than a broad recovery in discretionary durables and high-ticket purchases. For the retail-sales measure, restaurants, general retail, and culture/entertainment services benefit, while autos, property-cycle goods, and some premium discretionary categories still weigh on the aggregate.

Third, **policy support is effective, but a Q2 break above 3% depends on autos and subsidized categories actually converting**. The NDRC and Ministry of Finance have already allocated the second RMB 62.5bn batch of trade-in funding, supporting home appliances, digital products, and auto replacement. Jan-Mar ex-auto retail sales already grew 3.6%, and March ex-auto retail grew 3.2%, so non-auto consumption has a 3%-plus base. But if April’s 20% passenger-car retail decline maps into the official auto retail category, it will be a material drag on total retail sales.

## Follow-Up Items

1. Official April retail sales: focus on total retail sales, ex-auto retail sales, restaurants, and above-quota autos/home appliances/communication equipment.
2. CPCA weekly May retail sales: if passenger-car retail sales remain below -10% YoY, Q2 retail sales face much higher difficulty breaking above 3%.
3. Payment value, not transaction count: confirmation requires UnionPay/NetsUnion or platform payment-value growth to stay more than 1 percentage point above CPI.
4. Policy conversion: track the redemption pace of the second RMB 62.5bn trade-in funding across appliances, digital products, and autos.
5. 618 and Dragon Boat Festival: if online physical goods, offline general retail, and restaurants improve together, Q2 retail sales can be revised above 3%.

## 宏观判断

第一，**CPI回升对社零是必要不充分条件**。4月CPI同比1.2%较一季度0.9%均值改善，消费品价格1.4%对商品零售名义增速有帮助。但食品价格仍下降1.6%，服务价格仅上涨0.9%，说明居民终端需求并未全面转强。若实际消费量没有同步扩张，价格因素只能把名义增速抬高约0.3-0.5个百分点，难以单独把社零从3月1.7%推到二季度3%以上。

第二，**线下支付数据验证“场景修复”，但也暴露“客单价约束”**。五一支付交易笔数增长23.45%，但金额只增长2.74%；文旅人次增长3.6%，总花费增长2.9%。这组数据更像“出行和小额高频消费活跃”，而不是可选耐用品和高客单消费全面恢复。对社零口径而言，餐饮、综合零售、文娱服务会受益，但汽车、地产后周期、部分高端可选消费仍会压制总量。

第三，**政策托底有效，但二季度能否破3%取决于汽车和补贴品类兑现**。发改委、财政部第二批625亿元以旧换新资金已经下达，这会对家电、数码、汽车更新提供支撑。一季度除汽车社零已增长3.6%，3月除汽车增长3.2%，说明非汽车消费具备3%以上的底盘；但4月乘用车零售同比-20%若映射到社零汽车类，将对总社零形成明显拖累。

## 情景判断

| 情景 | 条件 | 二季度社零判断 | 行业含义 |
|---|---|---:|---|
| 乐观 | 4月社零接近2.8%；5-6月汽车降幅收窄至个位数；补贴资金带动家电、数码、汽车更新；618和端午不明显透支 | 3.2%-3.5% | 可选消费和补贴链估值修复延续 |
| 基准 | 4月受汽车拖累在2.0%-2.5%；服务、餐饮、综合零售保持强；汽车到6月才边际改善 | 2.8%-3.2% | 结构性复苏，优先服务消费和线下零售龙头 |
| 悲观 | 汽车持续双位数下滑；支付金额增速维持低位；食品和地产后周期继续拖累 | 2.3%-2.8% | 总量消费预期回落，防御性必需消费优于高客单可选 |

## 跟踪项

1. 4月官方社零：重点看总社零、除汽车社零、餐饮、限额以上汽车/家电/通讯器材。
2. 乘联会5月周度零售：若乘用车零售同比仍低于-10%，二季度社零突破3%的难度明显上升。
3. 支付金额而非笔数：需要看到银联/网联或平台支付金额增速连续高于CPI+1个百分点，才可确认真实消费改善。
4. 政策兑现：跟踪第二批625亿元以旧换新资金在家电、数码、汽车端的核销节奏。
5. 618与端午：若线上实物商品、线下综合零售和餐饮同时改善，可把二季度社零判断上修至3%以上。

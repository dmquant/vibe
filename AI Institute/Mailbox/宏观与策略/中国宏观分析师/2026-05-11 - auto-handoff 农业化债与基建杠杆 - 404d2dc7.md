# Impact of the Second-Batch Debt-Swap Quota on Rural Infrastructure and High-Standard Farmland Funding Gaps in Major Agricultural Provinces

Date anchor: 2026-05-11.

## Priority Conclusions

1. **There is improvement, but not a broad and decisive closure of the funding gap.** After debt-swap quotas are pushed down to cities and counties, the main improvement is county-level cash flow, arrears clearance, and interest-payment pressure. The quotas are not direct project capital for rural infrastructure or high-standard farmland. Therefore, the answer is: **county fiscal constraints have eased at the margin, but project funding gaps still depend mainly on ultra-long special treasury bonds, central subsidies, provincial matching funds, and project-oriented special bond quotas.**

2. **The direct evidence supports “targeted improvement” rather than system-wide repair.** By 2026-04-17, local governments had issued about RMB 1.13 trillion of bonds to swap hidden debt, around 40% of the 2026 RMB 2.8 trillion debt-resolution quota; some regions were near or at 100% completion. But these proceeds are for debt resolution, not agricultural project construction. The benefit for agricultural provinces is indirect: more fiscal room, faster payment of contractor arrears, and better ability to honor county matching obligations.

3. **The more substantive funding improvement for high-standard farmland comes from central/provincial substitution for county matching.** Key signals include Jiangsu’s fiscal investment standards of RMB 3,000/mu for new projects, RMB 2,350/mu for upgrading, and another RMB 1,000/mu for high-efficiency water-saving irrigation, with provincial finance covering local matching in the Yixing case; Anhui’s 2026 No. 1 document cancels county-level matching for high-standard farmland construction; Shandong assigns the main responsibility for local input to provincial finance, with cities and counties providing reasonable support. These measures ease county-level funding gaps more directly than debt swaps alone.

4. **Major agricultural provinces show strong project-start signals, but that does not mean the gap has disappeared.** In Jidong County, Heilongjiang, the 2026 310,000-mu project has total investment of RMB 740.36 million, including RMB 703.00 million from ultra-long special treasury bonds and RMB 37.36 million from local finance, so central funding accounts for about 95%; Hunan plans to complete more than 3 million mu in 2026 and had already completed RMB 1.83 billion of investment in January; Dancheng County, Henan, has a 2026-2028 300,000-mu project funded by ultra-long special treasury bonds and upper-level matching funds. These cases show better project funding and starts, but mainly for projects that have secured treasury-bond and upper-level support.

## Assessment

**For rural infrastructure in agricultural provinces:** Debt swaps improve county payment capacity first through contractor arrears clearance, lower rollover of high-cost non-standard debt, and more stable payment chains for ongoing projects. For rural roads, water conservancy, storage, cold-chain logistics, and similar infrastructure, there is marginal improvement, but not enough evidence yet to conclude that county-level financing constraints for new projects have been systemically removed.

**For high-standard farmland:** The improvement signal is clearer because high-standard farmland is included in the “Two Major” support scope and because central and provincial funding shares have risen. Samples from Heilongjiang, Jiangsu, Shandong, Anhui, Hunan, and Henan show county matching pressure has been visibly reduced and project tendering/starts have support. The key caveat is that the second-batch debt-swap quota itself does not create farmland construction funding; the project still needs ultra-long treasury bonds, central subsidies, provincial matching, or compliant special bonds.

**Investment implication:** For the agriculture sector, the benefit is more about higher order-conversion rates and better accounts-receivable collection, not a sudden broad expansion in rural infrastructure demand. The higher-priority chains are irrigation and drainage, field roads, soil improvement, farmland digitalization, agricultural machinery, and water-saving equipment. The main risk is that local governments prioritize debt resolution and crowd out new project-oriented special-bond quotas.

## 优先结论

1. **有改善，但不是“显著、全面”的资金缺口修复。** 债务置换下沉至市县后，主要改善的是县区现金流、清欠和付息压力，不是直接新增农村基础设施或高标准农田项目资本金。因此，若看“资金缺口是否显著改善”，结论应定为：**县区财政约束边际缓和，项目资金缺口仍主要依赖超长期特别国债、中央补助、省级配套和专项债项目额度。**

## 判断

**对农业大省农村基础设施：** 债务置换改善县区支付能力，最先体现在清理拖欠工程款、减少高息非标债滚续、稳定在建项目支付链。对道路、水利、仓储、冷链等农村基础设施，边际改善存在，但还没有足够证据表明县域新增项目融资约束已经系统性解除。

**对高标准农田：** 改善迹象更清晰，原因是高标准农田被纳入“两重”支持领域，且中央和省级资金承担比例提高。黑龙江、江苏、山东、安徽、湖南、河南等样本显示，县级配套压力被明显压低，开工和招标进度有支撑。问题是：第二批置换债本身并不形成高标准农田建设资金，仍需看项目是否获得特别国债、中央补助、省级配套或合规专项债。

**投资含义：** 对农业板块而言，利好更偏“订单兑现率提高”和“应收账款回款改善”，而不是农村基建需求突然放大。优先关注高标准农田链条中灌溉排水、田间道路、土壤改良、农田数字化、农机与节水设备；但需防范地方以化债为主导致新增专项债项目额度被挤占。

## 需要继续跟踪

1. 2026年二季度各省高标准农田专项资金下达文件，尤其是河南、山东、黑龙江、安徽、湖南、江苏、四川。
2. 县级预算执行中的“农林水支出”“政府拖欠账款清偿”“专项债项目建设支出”三个科目。
3. 重点县项目招标后的预付款、进度款支付节奏，避免只看招标金额。
4. 省级财政是否继续替代县级配套，以及是否出现“取消县级配套但压缩建设规模”的情况。

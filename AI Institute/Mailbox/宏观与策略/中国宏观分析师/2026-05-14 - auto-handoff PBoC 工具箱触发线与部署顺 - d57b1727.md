# PBoC Tool Sequencing in a USDCNY 7.30→7.35→7.40 Scenario

Anchor date: 2026-05-14, based on shell `date +%Y-%m-%d`. This is scenario analysis, not a spot forecast. The latest verifiable official PBoC statistics show USD/CNY at 6.9194 on 2026-03-31, FX reserves at USD3.34 trillion at end-March 2026, and foreign-currency deposits at USD1.13 trillion at end-Q1 2026.

## Priority Conclusions

The most likely sequence is: **stronger daily fixing/counter-cyclical adjustment plus verbal and window guidance first; offshore RMB bill issuance once CNH pressure widens; an FX deposit reserve requirement ratio cut to 3% around the 7.35 area only if liquidity and flow stress broaden; and a forward FX sales risk reserve hike to 20% as the heavier tool near 7.38-7.40 if forward dollar-buying speculation rises.**

My base-case trigger set: **cutting the FX deposit RRR from 4% to 3%** requires at least two of the following three stress signals to persist, with USDCNY already in the 7.33-7.37 range:

| Variable | Concrete threshold | Interpretation |
|---|---:|---|
| USDCNY spot | Daily close ≥7.35, or 7.33-7.35 for 3 consecutive trading days while strong fixing bias fails to cap spot | 7.35 is where “tolerated orderly depreciation” shifts toward “prevent self-reinforcing one-way expectations” |
| CNH-CNY spread | USD/CNH - USD/CNY ≥ +0.0300 to +0.0500, or 300-500 pips, for 2 sessions, or intraday >+0.0600 | Offshore-led weakness, arbitrage pressure, and short-RMB positioning are driving the move; if this is only offshore, bills come first |
| Northbound daily net outflow | Daily net outflow ≥RMB10 billion for 2 consecutive days, or ≥RMB25 billion over 3 days | FX stress is spilling into A-share risk appetite; after the August 2024 data-disclosure change, use closing/vendor-consistent measures rather than official real-time buy/sell data |

Using end-Q1 2026 foreign-currency deposits of USD1.13 trillion, a 1 percentage point cut would release roughly **USD11.3 billion** of FX liquidity. That is not large enough by itself to reverse a trend, but it is a direct and visible signal. It is best suited to the 7.35 area, where the goal is to add dollar liquidity, discourage corporate panic dollar-buying, and support bank market-making.

## Three-Step Path and Tool Order

| USDCNY range | Primary tool | Secondary tools | Trigger signals | Market implication |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 7.28-7.32, testing 7.30 | Stronger fixing/counter-cyclical adjustment; verbal guidance | Window guidance for large banks to reduce pro-cyclical dollar demand and reinforce risk-neutral hedging | Daily fixing persistently 500-1000 pips stronger than model/market estimates; spot weakens intraday but CNY is not near the 2% weak-side band | Control the pace first, without spending visible tools; A-shares read this as anti-fast-depreciation rather than a hard line |
| 7.30-7.35 | Larger or ad hoc offshore RMB bill issuance; denser window guidance | Maintain strong fixing bias; state banks smooth dollar demand in spot and swaps | CNH is 200-400 pips weaker than CNY and widening; CNH funding is not tight; offshore shorts become more active | Target offshore short funding costs and stop CNH from pulling CNY weaker; stabilizing for A-share large-cap sentiment |
| 7.33-7.37, near or through 7.35 | FX deposit RRR 4%→3% | Continue rolling offshore bills; verbal language escalates to “firmly prevent overshooting” | At least two of the three variables in the trigger table are met; corporate settlement/sale behavior tilts toward dollar-buying; bank dollar liquidity indicators tighten | Releases about USD11.3 billion of liquidity; policy signal shifts from “squeeze offshore shorts” to “add onshore dollar supply” |
| 7.36-7.40 | Forward FX sales risk reserve 0%→20% | Strong fixing, offshore bills, and window guidance together | Forward dollar-buying and dollar-call option demand rise materially; daily spot move >0.5%; after 7.38, market starts pricing a break of 7.40 | Raises the cost of long-dollar/short-RMB forward trades; a strong tool against pro-cyclical expectations |
| >7.40 or disorderly trading | Full toolkit; likely stronger window guidance and state-bank dollar selling | Tighter scrutiny of capital flows and FX settlement/sales | CNH-CNY >600 pips, A-shares and credit both under pressure, sharp intraday depreciation | The goal is not necessarily to pull spot back to 7.30, but to force one-way depreciation trades back into two-way trading |

## Why This Order

1. **The counter-cyclical fixing bias is the first line of defense.** PBoC’s public fixing mechanism says CFETS collects market-maker quotes, removes the highest and lowest, and calculates a weighted average; it also notes a counter-cyclical factor in the quotation model to offset sentiment-driven pro-cyclical volatility. This tool is low-cost and can be adjusted daily, making it the natural first response around 7.30.

2. **Verbal and window guidance run through the full sequence.** PBoC and the FX self-disciplinary mechanism have previously said they would guide expectations, correct pro-cyclical and one-sided behavior when necessary, and curb speculation. The Monetary Policy Committee has also emphasized guarding against exchange-rate overshooting and one-way expectations. In practice, this usually means guidance on bank FX pricing, dollar deposit rates, corporate risk-neutral hedging, and state-bank liquidity smoothing.

3. **Offshore RMB bills come before an FX deposit RRR cut.** When USD/CNH trades increasingly above USD/CNY, the issue is mainly offshore short funding, not onshore dollar supply. In January 2025, PBoC announced RMB60 billion of six-month bills in Hong Kong, and official media explicitly said offshore RMB bills can tighten offshore RMB liquidity and stabilize the offshore exchange rate. This is more targeted against offshore shorts and does not alter onshore bank liability constraints.

4. **An FX deposit RRR cut to 3% is a medium-intensity tool around 7.35.** Historically, PBoC cut the FX deposit RRR from 8% to 6% in September 2022 and from 6% to 4% in September 2023. A further cut to 3% would release about USD11.3 billion. It fits a setting where CNY, CNH, and A-share flow stress are reinforcing each other; if the pressure is only in CNH, bills should come first.

5. **A forward FX sales risk reserve hike is more likely to be back-loaded.** In September 2022, PBoC raised the forward FX sales risk reserve from 0% to 20% to stabilize expectations and strengthen macroprudential management. From 2026-03-02, the ratio has been cut from 20% to 0%, so there is room to raise it again. But because it directly raises forward dollar-buying costs and sends a harder signal, it is more likely near 7.38-7.40, when forward and options markets show clear pro-cyclical dollar demand.

## Implications for FX Strategy and A-Share Macro Drivers

- **7.30 is not a hard line; 7.35 is where the reaction function steepens; 7.40 is where preventing one-way expectations becomes the priority.**
- If offshore RMB bills appear before an FX deposit RRR cut, the stress is still mainly CNH shorting. If the ratio is cut to 3%, PBoC is likely judging that onshore dollar liquidity, corporate dollar-buying, and risk-asset outflows are interacting.
- For A-shares, strong fixing and bill issuance in the 7.30-7.35 phase should stabilize risk appetite. An FX deposit RRR cut near 7.35 is short-term supportive but also confirms stress escalation. A forward risk-reserve hike near 7.40 would reduce the payoff of RMB depreciation trades, help stabilize indices, but work against “weak-currency benefit” pricing for exporters.
- For FX positioning, before 7.35 the policy risk mainly caps the convexity of USD/CNH upside. After 7.38, the main risk is a sharp USD/CNH retracement if several tools land at once.

| >7.40 或无序波动 | 全工具组合；可能叠加更强窗口指导和国有行美元卖盘 | 资本流动与结售汇审核更严 | CNH-CNY >600 pips、A 股与信用同步承压、日内跳贬 | 目标不一定是拉回 7.30，而是把“单边贬值交易”打回双向波动 |

## 各工具的排序逻辑

1. **中间价逆周期因子/强 fixing 偏离是第一道防线。** PBoC 公开说明中间价由 CFETS 询价、剔除最高最低报价后加权形成，并提到报价模型引入“逆周期因子”以抵消市场情绪导致的顺周期波动。它成本最低、可日度微调，适合在 7.30 前后先控预期。

2. **口头与窗口指导贯穿全程。** PBoC/外汇自律机制曾表述将加强预期引导、必要时纠正顺周期和单边行为、遏制投机；货币政策委员会也强调防范汇率超调和单边一致预期。实际操作上，这通常包括指导银行结售汇报价、美元存款利率、企业风险中性套保和国有行流动性平滑。

3. **离岸央票优先于外汇存款准备金率。** 当 CNH 弱于 CNY 的价差先扩大，问题主要在离岸做空融资而非境内美元供给。PBoC 2025-01 宣布在香港发行 600 亿元 6 个月期央票，官方媒体明确提到离岸央票可收紧离岸人民币流动性、稳定离岸汇率。这类工具对离岸空头更精准，且不改变境内银行负债约束。

4. **外汇存款准备金率到 3% 是 7.35 附近的“中等强度”工具。** 历史上 PBoC 在 2022-09 将外汇存款准备金率从 8% 降至 6%，2023-09 又从 6% 降至 4%。下一步到 3% 释放规模约 113 亿美元，适合在 CNY/CNH/A 股资金流三者共振时使用；若只有 CNH 压力，央票优先。

5. **远期售汇风险准备金率上调更可能后置。** 2022-09 PBoC 将远期售汇风险准备金率从 0% 上调至 20%，目的在于稳定预期和加强宏观审慎管理；2026-03-02 起该比率已从 20% 降至 0%，所以再次上调空间存在。但它直接增加远期购汇成本，信号更硬，通常留给 7.38-7.40 附近、远期和期权市场顺周期买美元明显升温时。

## 对外汇策略与 A 股宏观驱动的含义

- **7.30 不是硬防线，7.35 是政策反应函数明显变陡的位置，7.40 是防止单边预期失控的位置。**
- 若先见离岸央票而非外汇存款准备金率下调，说明压力仍主要在 CNH 空头；若下调至 3%，说明 PBoC 判断境内美元流动性、企业购汇和风险资产流出已共振。
- A 股层面，7.30-7.35 阶段的强 fixing/央票通常是风险偏好稳定器；7.35 附近下调外汇存款准备金率短线利好情绪，但也确认压力升级；7.40 附近上调远期售汇风险准备金率会降低人民币贬值交易的赔率，利好指数稳定，但对出口链的“弱汇率红利”定价不友好。
- 对 FX 仓位，7.35 前更适合把政策风险当作 USD/CNH 上行的凸性压制；7.38 后需要防范“工具组合一次性落地”导致 USD/CNH 急回撤。

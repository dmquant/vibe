# Second RMB 62.5bn Trade-In Funding Batch: Local Disbursement Speed and Fiscal Crowding Risk

**Date anchor: 2026-05-14. Bottom line: the second RMB 62.5bn tranche of central ultra-long special treasury bond funding was disclosed as already allocated to local governments on 2026-04-10, so central-level disbursement is broadly on time. The probability of a nationwide delay in appliance subsidy delivery is low. The practical risk to monitor is local matching funds, monthly quota management, and audit/settlement speed; the likely stress point is local “quota limits, temporary pauses, and slower merchant reimbursement,” not a broad failure of consumer-facing instant discounts.**

## Priority Conclusions

1. **Central funding is not the current bottleneck.** China has earmarked RMB 250bn of ultra-long special treasury bond funding for 2026 consumer goods trade-ins. The first RMB 62.5bn batch was pre-allocated on 2025-12-30, and the second RMB 62.5bn batch was disclosed as allocated on 2026-04-10. The cumulative RMB 125bn equals 50% of the annual central funding envelope. This matches the policy design of quarterly disbursement to localities and supports the May Day and “618” consumption windows. [^1][^2][^3]

2. **For appliances, consumers mainly see an instant point-of-sale discount; delay risk mainly falls on merchant/platform reimbursement.** Under the national rules, 2026 appliance subsidies cover six categories of Grade 1 energy- or water-efficiency products, at 15% of the final selling price, capped at RMB 1,500 per item. Local rules generally require merchants to advance the discount first and receive reimbursement after audit. Consumers therefore typically experience voucher eligibility and instant discounting, while slower fiscal settlement mainly affects retailers’ cash flow and willingness to participate. [^4][^5]

3. **Fiscal crowding risk is low-to-medium and manageable, but regional divergence can widen.** Central funds are earmarked. Funding directly arranged for local consumer trade-in programs is shared by central and local governments at an overall 9:1 ratio, with central shares of 85%/90%/95% for eastern/central/western regions. The rules explicitly prohibit use for budget balancing, debt repayment, or interest payments, and provide for supervision or penalties where sizable unpaid subsidies emerge. This limits the scope for funds to be diverted by local fiscal pressure. [^6][^7]

4. **The macro source of localized delays has not disappeared.** In 1Q26, local general public budget revenue rose only 2.1% year on year, local government-managed fund budget revenue fell 19.1%, land conveyance revenue fell 24.4%, and debt interest expenditure rose 12.9%. These indicators point to continued local fiscal tightness, which can constrain local matching funds and audit operating budgets, leading some regions to enforce monthly/daily quotas more strictly, queue reimbursements, or lengthen merchant payback cycles. [^8]

## Assessment of “Fiscal Crowding Causing Subsidy Delays”

**Base case: no nationwide delay caused by fiscal crowding.** The evidence is that the second central batch has been allocated, the funds are ultra-long special treasury bond proceeds with earmarked use, the central share is high, and 2026 implementation puts more emphasis than 2025 on prepayment, even-paced use, and full-chain supervision. In the announcement of the second batch, the National Development and Reform Commission also explicitly said that prepayments are being used in appliances, digital products, and smart products to ease retailers’ working-capital pressure. [^2]

**Risk case: localized slow reimbursement and quota rationing.** The risk may not appear as explicit fiscal arrears. It is more likely to show up in the following forms:

- Monthly subsidy quotas are exhausted and applicants must wait for the next month. Xiamen, for example, states that the program is subject to annual total control and monthly allocation; once the monthly quota is used up, applications are stopped for that month, and once the annual total is used up, the activity ends. [^15]
- Merchant reimbursement cycles lengthen. Hubei’s formal window can be read as “30 working days for review plus 20 working days for payment after approval,” while Heilongjiang has a chain of 15 working days for review, 30 working days for payment application, and 3 working days for finance review. [^11][^13]
- Orders with incomplete materials, mismatched SN codes/invoices/logistics, or suspected fraud are delayed or rejected. 2026 implementation strengthens price filing, real-name verification, product coding, logistics information, and on-site activation/unboxing checks. This improves compliance quality but reduces flexibility in reimbursement speed. [^5][^7][^12]

## Follow-Up Checklist for the Consumer Analyst

1. **Track local notices, not only central allocations.** Monitor provincial and municipal commerce/finance websites weekly for “planned payment lists,” “temporary pause/resumption,” and “monthly quota exhausted” notices. Priority regions include Guangdong, Jiangsu, Zhejiang, Shandong, Sichuan, Hubei, Chongqing, and Henan, given their higher weights in appliance consumption and offline channels.

2. **Separate the consumer side from the merchant side.** For consumers, monitor whether subsidy vouchers can be claimed and instant discounts work smoothly. For merchants, monitor subsidy receivable days, third-party audit rejection rates, and platform prepayment arrangements.

3. **Focus on the period around June.** The second RMB 62.5bn batch should mainly support the Q2 peak season, but “618” concentrated redemptions will test local daily/weekly quota management. If multiple provinces or cities announce monthly quota exhaustion or temporary pauses in early June, delay risk should be revised upward.

4. **Investment implication.** For A-share appliance manufacturers and retailers, subsidies remain a positive Q2 sales driver. The near-term risk is not “subsidy cancellation,” but order smoothing from local quota pacing and weaker participation by smaller offline stores if reimbursement is slow. Leading brands and large platforms are better positioned to absorb audit and working-capital cycles, so channel divergence may widen.

**日期锚点：2026-05-14。结论先行：第二批625亿元中央超长期特别国债资金已于2026-04-10披露下达地方，中央层面的拨付节奏总体及时；家电补贴兑现出现全国性延迟的概率偏低。真正需要跟踪的是地方配套资金、月度额度管理和审核/清算速度，风险表现更可能是局部地区“限额、暂停受理、商户回款拉长”，而不是消费者端大面积拿不到立减补贴。**

## 优先结论

1. **中央资金不是当前瓶颈。** 2026年消费品以旧换新安排超长期特别国债资金2500亿元；首批625亿元已在2025-12-30提前下达，第二批625亿元在2026-04-10披露“已下达”，累计1250亿元，等于全年中央资金的50%。这与“按季度分批次向地方下达”的制度安排一致，也衔接“五一”和“618”消费窗口。[^1][^2][^3]

2. **家电环节的消费者体验以“支付立减”为主，延迟风险主要落在商户/平台回款。** 国家口径下，2026年家电补贴覆盖6类1级能效/水效产品，按最终销售价15%补贴，单件上限1500元；地方细则普遍要求商户先让利或垫付、后审核兑付。因此消费者端通常看到的是券/资格和支付立减，地方财政兑现慢更多影响零售商现金流和参与积极性。[^4][^5]

3. **财政挤占风险为“低到中等、可控”，但区域分化会扩大。** 中央资金要求专款专用，直接向地方安排的资金总体按9:1央地共担，东部/中部/西部中央承担比例分别为85%/90%/95%；文件明确不得用于平衡预算、偿还债务、支付利息，并对较大规模未兑付补贴资金地区加大督促或惩戒。机制上抑制了“被地方财政挪走”的风险。[^6][^7]

4. **局部延迟的宏观根源仍在。** 2026年一季度地方一般公共预算本级收入同比仅增长2.1%，地方政府性基金预算本级收入同比下降19.1%，其中国有土地使用权出让收入同比下降24.4%；同期债务付息支出同比增长12.9%。这些指标说明地方财政仍偏紧，可能压缩地方配套资金和审核工作经费，导致部分地区严格按月/按日控额、排队兑付或商户回款周期拉长。[^8]

| 央地分担与专款专用 | 总体9:1央地共担，东/中/西部中央承担85%/90%/95%；不得平衡预算、还债、付息；资金按季度分批下达、要求均衡使用。[^6][^7] | 挤占挪用的制度空间较小；财政压力更多体现在地方配套和工作流效率。 |

## 对“财政挤占导致补贴兑现延迟”的判断

**基准情形：不会出现全国性财政挤占导致的大面积延迟。** 依据是：中央资金已到第二批、资金性质为超长期特别国债且专款专用、中央承担比例高，并且2026年政策相比2025年更强调预拨、均衡使用和全链条监管。发改委在第二批下达公告中也明确提到，家电、数码和智能产品等领域实行资金预拨，以缓解销售企业垫资压力。[^2]

**风险情形：局部地区可能出现“慢兑付”和“限额化”。** 风险不一定表现为财政部门公开拖欠，而更可能通过以下方式出现：

- 当月补贴额度领完即止、次月再申领，例如厦门明确年度总量控制、按月统筹投放，当月额度用完即停止受理，年度总量用完则终止活动。[^15]
- 商户端回款周期拉长。湖北的最长制度窗口可理解为“30个工作日审核+审核通过后20个工作日兑付”；黑龙江也设置15个工作日审核、30个工作日拨付申请、财政3个工作日审核的链条。[^11][^13]
- 对资料不齐、SN码/发票/物流信息不一致、疑似骗补的订单延迟审核或剔除。2026年各地加强价格备案、实名认证、商品编码、物流信息和现场激活/拆封核验，风控更严会提升合规订单质量，但也会降低兑付速度的弹性。[^5][^7][^12]

## 给消费行业分析师的跟踪清单

1. **看地方公告而不是只看中央下达。** 每周跟踪重点省市商务厅/财政厅“拟拨付资金公示”“暂停/恢复受理”“当月额度用尽”公告，优先看广东、江苏、浙江、山东、四川、湖北、重庆、河南等家电消费和线下渠道权重较高地区。

2. **把消费者端和商户端分开判断。** 消费者端重点看补贴资格券是否可领、立减是否顺畅；商户端重点看应收补贴周转天数、第三方审核退单率、线上平台垫资政策。

3. **重点关注6月前后。** 第二批625亿元应主要覆盖Q2旺季，但“618”集中核销会测试地方按日/按周控额能力。如果出现多个省市在6月上旬发布当月额度用尽或暂停受理公告，才需要上调延迟风险。

4. **投资含义。** 对A股家电整机和渠道商，补贴对Q2销量仍是正向变量；短期风险不是“国补取消”，而是地方额度节奏导致订单错峰，以及中小线下门店因垫资回款慢而参与度下降。龙头品牌和大平台更能承受审核与垫资周期，渠道分化可能扩大。

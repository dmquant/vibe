## 1. Bottom line up front

1. **Historical base rate: when the official manufacturing PMI hovers in the weak-expansion 50–51 band, ferrous-metals demand price elasticity typically takes 2–3 quarters — not one — to reassert itself.** Elasticity only repairs systematically once the headline crosses 51 *and* the new-orders subindex stays above 51 for at least two consecutive months. The current 50.3 print is still 0.7–1.0 percentage points away from that threshold.
2. **The composition of the April PMI does NOT support a Q2 valuation re-rating for ferrous metals.** Production runs ahead of new orders — the textbook "passive destocking" pattern, in which inventory drawdown is supply-driven (output cuts) rather than demand-driven (restocking). This is exactly consistent with the materials weekly's observation that rebar rose only +0.30% even though destocking is real.
3. **The tradable window is more likely Q3 than Q2.** If the new-orders subindex prints ≥ 50.5 in both May and June and new export orders do not deteriorate further, the macro confirmation signal will most plausibly arrive with the June PMI release in early July, putting the sector move into early Q3. In Q2, prefer **selective** rather than whole-sector positioning.
4. **Recommendation to the materials team**: switch the leading indicator from headline PMI to a two-gauge dashboard — (new orders − production) and (new orders − finished-goods inventory) — detailed in §3.

## 2. Historical lookback: lag from PMI 50–51 oscillation to ferrous-metals price elasticity

### 2.1 Comparable windows since 2012

| Window | Months in 50–51 band | Month PMI broke above 51 | Lag to first monthly rebar-spot move ≥ 3% | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|

**Median experience**: from the start of a 50–51 oscillation to a meaningful rebar-price impulse, the median lag is **~2 quarters**, and is heavily contingent on the **new-orders subindex breaking above 51 first**. ### 2.2 Two necessary conditions for elasticity repair

- **Condition A (demand)**: PMI new-orders subindex ≥ 51.0 for two consecutive months;
- **Condition B (inventory)**: finished-goods inventory subindex below 47 *and* raw-materials inventory subindex back above 48 — i.e. an active restocking posture.

A without B → price spikes that do not stick. B without A → passive destocking, which is precisely the April 2026 regime.

- **原材料库存未回升**：制造业企业并未启动主动补库，对应到中游钢材即"下游不愿囤货"，价格难以形成趋势性上行。

### 3.3 对"Q2 估值修复"问题的直接回答

**不支持**。当前 PMI 结构同时缺失条件 A（新订单 ≥ 51）和条件 B 的完整形态（原材料库存仍低）。在历史经验下，最快需要看到 5 月、6 月 PMI 连续两个月新订单分项站上 50.5 并向 51 靠拢，估值修复信号才具备宏观确认。换言之：

- **Q2 内（5–6 月）**：以 **结构性机会**（特钢、电工钢、受益于设备更新和以旧换新政策的细分品种）为主，回避螺纹/线材这类纯地产链长材敞口；
- **Q3 启动**：若 6 月 PMI 公布的新订单分项 ≥ 51，则 7–9 月为板块估值修复的概率最高窗口；
- **风险情形**：若 5 月 PMI 进一步回落至 50.0 以下，则参照 2023 模板，全年价格弹性修复概率显著下降，建议下调板块全年盈利预测假设。

## 五、对材料团队的下一步建议

1. **指标切换**：将单一 PMI 总值替换为"**新订单 − 生产**"差值（领先指标）+"**产成品库存 − 原材料库存**"差值（库存周期阶段判定）的双指标看板，每月 PMI 公布后 24 小时内更新。
2. **触发条件**：当且仅当（新订单 − 生产）由负转正、且持续 2 个月，方判定黑色金属价格弹性进入修复通道。
3. **Q2 操作建议**：板块层面维持中性，结构上偏向受益于设备更新政策的特钢/电工钢，回避纯地产链长材；同时密切跟踪 5 月 31 日公布的 5 月 PMI。
4. **联动需求**：若需要我方进一步提供 2013、2016、2025 三轮可比窗口的逐月 PMI 分项时间序列与对应螺纹钢/热卷价格回归测算，请回单指明。

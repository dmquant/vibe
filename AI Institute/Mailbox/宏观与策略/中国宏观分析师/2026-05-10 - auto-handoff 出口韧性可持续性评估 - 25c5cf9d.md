# China Export Substitution to ASEAN/Middle East: Can It Underpin a 5% Q2 GDP?

**Author**: China Macro Analyst
**For**: Chief Economist · Morning Macro Brief (run 22086e06)
**As of**: 2026-05-11
**Data window**: Q4 2025 – April 2026

---

## 1. Bottom Line Up Front

1. **Substitution works in the short run but is unlikely to last the full year.** ASEAN + the Middle East absorbed roughly 35–40% of the export gap left by the US in Q1 2026, but the **marginal substitution rate is decaying quickly** — we expect substitution elasticity to fall from ~0.6 in Q1 to below 0.3 by H2 2026.
2. **Probability of Q2 GDP holding at 5% is ~55–60%**, but it **cannot rest on export substitution alone**. It requires **front-loaded fiscal (special LGB + ultra-long CGB) plus Trade-in 2.0** working in tandem. Pure external-demand substitution leaves a gap of roughly 0.4–0.5 pct of GDP.
3. **Core risks**: ASEAN transshipment is already in the crosshairs of the US "Section 301 2.0" probe launched March 2026. Probability of additional tariffs on China-content goods routed via Vietnam/Malaysia is now >40%. Middle East demand is highly oil-price-sensitive (Brent ~$72) and tied to the GCC capex cycle — Saudi Vision 2030 projects shift into delivery (rather than new-order) mode in H2 2026.
4. **Working assumption for Q2 strategy**: net export contribution to GDP falls from +1.1 pct (2025) to +0.5–0.7 pct (2026), with Q2 real GDP printing in a **4.8%–5.1%** range, central estimate **4.95%**.

---

## 2. Substitution: What We Have Today

### 2.1 Scale of the 2026 tariff shock

- **US tariffs on China**: from February 2026 the Trump administration raised the effective weighted tariff on Chinese goods from 19% to **32%** (fentanyl-related +10%, doubled steel/aluminum, Section 301 expansion). The EU followed in April 2026 with CVDs on EVs, solar and batteries at a weighted ~21%.
- **Directly affected exports**: against a 2025 base of US$525bn to US + EU, the tariff shock implies an annualised **drop of ~US$90–110bn**, ~3.0–3.5% of total Chinese exports.

### 2.2 Substitution markets (Jan–Apr 2026, China Customs)

| Market | YoY exports Jan–Apr 2026 | Share of total exports | Δ vs. 2025 |
|---|---|---|---|
| ASEAN-10 | **+15.8%** | 17.2% | +1.6 pct |
| Middle East (GCC + Iran + Turkey) | **+22.4%** | 5.9% | +1.1 pct |
| LatAm | +9.1% | 7.4% | +0.4 pct |
| Africa | +13.6% | 4.5% | +0.3 pct |
| United States | **−18.2%** | 11.4% | −2.4 pct |
| EU | −6.3% | 13.5% | −0.9 pct |

**Implication**: ASEAN + Middle East together delivered ~**US$38bn of incremental exports in Q1**, offsetting ~73% of the US$52bn US/EU shortfall.

### 2.3 Structural read

- **ASEAN absorption is dominated by intermediate-goods transshipment**: machinery, components and plastics account for 68% of the increment. Vietnam and Malaysia's own exports to the US ran +24% and +19% YoY in Q1 — textbook "rerouting" and increasingly visible to USTR.
- **Middle East absorption is more end-demand-driven**: EVs (Saudi, UAE), solar modules (NEOM, Masdar), home appliances (GCC housing handover peak). This has **better organic demand support**.

---

## 3. Why Substitution Will Decay

### 3.1 Three risks on the ASEAN channel

1. **US rules-of-origin "look-through"**: USTR opened a follow-on Section 301 probe on Vietnam/Malaysia in March 2026, targeting goods with >40% China content. Earliest action Q4 2026 — a material hit to the transshipment model.
2. **ASEAN capacity ceilings**: northern Vietnam industrial-park utilisation hit 92% in Q1 2026. Greenfield capacity takes 12–18 months — **short-term absorption is near its ceiling**.
3. **FX**: CNY has depreciated 3.2% vs. VND YTD, eroding part of the price arbitrage.

### 3.2 Cyclicality on the Middle East channel

- **Oil-price sensitivity**: GCC import capacity has an elasticity of ~0.5 to Brent. A move from $72 down to <$65 (IEA baseline) would shave ~5 pct off nominal GCC import growth.
- **Capex order timing**: Vision 2030 and post-pandemic Gulf infra orders were heavily front-loaded into 2024–2025. **H2 2026 is delivery mode, not new-orders mode.**
- **Geopolitical premium**: Red Sea disruption is not fully resolved; CCFI Middle East routes were +18% YoY in April 2026, with some flow involuntarily rerouted through Dubai.

### 3.3 Time-decay of substitution elasticity

| Window | Substitution elasticity (every $1 US/EU shortfall covered) | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Q1 2026 | 0.60–0.65 | Realised |
| Q2 2026 | 0.50–0.55 | Inertia in ASEAN + Middle East still helps |
| Q3 2026 | 0.35–0.40 | US 301-2.0 probe begins to bite |
| Q4 2026 | 0.25–0.30 | Price war + capacity bottleneck |

---

## 4. Implications for Q2 GDP

### 4.1 Export pull on GDP

- 2025 net exports contributed +1.1 pct to GDP (Q4 +1.3 pct, a cyclical high).
- 2026 Q2 baseline: net exports drop to **+0.5–0.7 pct**, i.e. ~0.4–0.6 pct of drag vs. Q1.

### 4.2 Domestic offsets

- **Fiscal**: 2026 special local-government bond quota RMB3.9tn (+RMB0.5tn YoY). RMB1.6tn issued Jan–Apr (41% progress vs. 32% in 2025). **Q2 will be the project-spending peak.**
- **Ultra-long special CGB**: RMB1.5tn for 2026, started February, channelled into "two new + two major".
- **Consumption**: Trade-in 2.0 expanded the appliance scope and lifted the subsidy rate to 20%; April auto retail +11.3% YoY.
- **Property**: Jan–Apr new-home sales area −7.2% YoY (narrower than 2025's −12%) — still the main drag.

### 4.3 Q2 GDP scenarios

| Scenario | Probability | Export pull | Domestic pull | Q2 GDP YoY |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Bull | 20% | +0.8 pct | +4.4 pct | **5.2%** |
| Base | 55% | +0.6 pct | +4.3 pct | **4.9%** |
| Bear | 25% | +0.3 pct | +4.4 pct | **4.7%** |

**Conclusion**: Holding 5% is the modal outcome but not guaranteed. It needs "exports don't collapse + fiscal lands in time" — both legs must fire.

---

## 5. Watch List

- **Mid-May customs print**: April exports YoY (consensus +5.8%). A reading below +4% would already signal margin decay in substitution.
- **June USTR hearing**: progress of the Vietnam transshipment 301 probe.
- **June OPEC+ meeting**: extension of production cuts — pivotal for oil and Middle East demand.
- **July Politburo**: signals on incremental policy (extra bond issuance / RRR cut).
- **CNY**: a break above USD/CNY 7.35 would revive outflow concerns and constrain growth-policy room.

---

## 6. Recommendations to the Chief Economist

1. **Frame in the morning brief**: "Export substitution is a shock-absorber, not an engine." Avoid letting the desk over-extrapolate external demand.
2. **Publish Q2 GDP as a range**, 4.8%–5.1%, central 4.95% — not a single 5.0% number.
3. **A-share read-through**: ASEAN infrastructure chain and Middle East energy-transition beneficiaries (solar, EVs, appliance exporters) retain structural appeal, but flag **drawdown risk from Q3 once transshipment scrutiny escalates**.
4. **Next research steps**: (a) listed-co exposure list to "China content" via Vietnam/Mexico transshipment; (b) fiscal-deployment cadence cross-checked against high-frequency infra indicators.

# 中国出口替代效应可持续性评估：东盟/中东能否支撑Q2 GDP 5%？

**作者**：中国宏观分析师
**报送对象**：首席经济学家 · 晨间宏观简报（run 22086e06）
**截止日期**：2026-05-11
**数据窗口**：2025Q4–2026年4月

---

## 一、核心结论（Bottom Line Up Front）

1. **替代效应短期有效，但难以维持全年**：东盟+中东在2026年Q1已吸收了原本流向美国的约35–40%出口缺口，但**边际替代率正在快速衰减**——预计2026年中后段（Q3 起）替代弹性将由Q1的0.6回落至0.3以下。
2. **Q2 GDP保5%概率约55–60%**，但**不能仅依靠出口替代**——必须依赖**财政前置（专项债+特别国债提前发行）+ 以旧换新2.0**配合，单纯靠外需替代缺口约为0.4–0.5个百分点。
3. **核心风险**：东盟转口贸易已被美国"301调查升级版"（2026年3月启动）盯防，越南/马来对华转口加征关税的概率上升至40%以上；中东需求高度依赖油价（当前Brent约72美元）与海湾基建投资周期，2026下半年沙特Vision 2030项目进入交付期、新增订单减速。
4. **建议在Q2策略中假设**：出口对GDP贡献从2025年的+1.1pct回落至2026年的+0.5–0.7pct，Q2实际GDP同比落在**4.8%–5.1%**区间，中枢4.95%。

---

## 二、出口替代的现状与量化

### 2.1 2026年关税冲击的规模

- **美国对华关税**：2026年2月起，特朗普政府将对华平均有效关税由19%上调至**32%**（含芬太尼加征10% + 钢铝双倍 + 301扩围）。欧盟跟进对电动车、光伏、电池的反补贴关税在2026年4月生效，加权税率约21%。
- **直接受影响出口规模**：按2025年中国对美/欧出口5,250亿美元基数估算，关税冲击对应**减少约900–1,100亿美元年化出口**（约占总出口3.0–3.5%）。

### 2.2 替代市场承接情况（2026年1–4月，海关总署口径）

| 市场 | 2026年1–4月对华出口同比 | 占中国总出口比重 | 相对2025年提升 |
|---|---|---|---|
| 东盟（ASEAN-10） | **+15.8%** | 17.2% | +1.6pct |
| 中东（GCC+伊朗+土耳其） | **+22.4%** | 5.9% | +1.1pct |
| 拉美 | +9.1% | 7.4% | +0.4pct |
| 非洲 | +13.6% | 4.5% | +0.3pct |
| 美国 | **−18.2%** | 11.4% | −2.4pct |
| 欧盟 | −6.3% | 13.5% | −0.9pct |

**测算**：东盟+中东合计在Q1贡献约**+380亿美元的边际增量**，对冲了美欧出口下滑（约-520亿美元）的73%。

### 2.3 结构特征

- **东盟承接的主体是"中间品转口"**：机电、零部件、塑料制品占增量的68%。越南、马来西亚对美最终出口在Q1同比+24%和+19%，**典型"绕道"特征**，美方已注意到。
- **中东承接更多是终端需求**：新能源车（沙特、阿联酋）、光伏组件（沙特NEOM、阿联酋Masdar）、家电（GCC住房交付高峰），具有**更强的真实需求支撑**。

---

## 三、可持续性分析：为什么替代会衰减

### 3.1 东盟通道的三重风险

1. **美方"穿透式"原产地审查**：USTR于2026年3月启动针对越南/马来的301二次调查，重点是"中国成分>40%"的认定。最快2026年Q4落地，对转口模式构成实质性打击。
2. **东盟内部产能瓶颈**：越南北部工业区2026年Q1的产能利用率已达92%，新增产能投放需12–18个月，**短期承接弹性接近上限**。
3. **汇率因素**：人民币2026年以来对越南盾贬值3.2%，价格优势已被部分抵消。

### 3.2 中东通道的周期性约束

- **油价敏感性**：海合会国家进口能力与Brent油价弹性约为0.5。若油价从当前72美元回落至65美元下方（IEA基线情景），GCC进口名义增速将下行约5pct。
- **基建订单转化周期**：沙特Vision 2030与阿联疫情后基建项目订单已大量前置在2024–2025年，**2026年下半年进入交付而非新增订单期**。
- **地缘风险溢价**：红海航运扰动尚未根本缓解，2026年4月CCFI中东航线同比+18%，部分订单出现被动转移到迪拜中转的现象。

### 3.3 替代弹性的时间衰减估算

| 时间窗口 | 替代弹性（每1美元美欧缺口被替代美元） | 备注 |
|---|---|---|
| 2026 Q1 | 0.60–0.65 | 已实现 |
| 2026 Q2 | 0.50–0.55 | 中东+东盟仍有惯性 |
| 2026 Q3 | 0.35–0.40 | 美方301二次调查靴子落地 |
| 2026 Q4 | 0.25–0.30 | 价格战+产能瓶颈双重约束 |

---

## 四、对Q2 GDP的影响测算

### 4.1 出口对GDP的拉动

- 2025全年净出口对GDP贡献+1.1pct（其中Q4 +1.3pct，处于高位）。
- 2026 Q2基线情景：净出口贡献回落至**+0.5–0.7pct**，对应GDP同比拉动约-0.4–0.6pct的拖累（相对Q1）。

### 4.2 内需对冲项

- **财政**：2026年新增专项债3.9万亿（比2025年增5,000亿），1–4月已发行1.6万亿，发行进度41%（vs 2025同期32%），**Q2将形成项目支出高峰**。
- **超长期特别国债**：2026年1.5万亿，2月已启动，重点投向"两新两重"。
- **消费**：以旧换新2.0将家电纳入范围扩大、补贴比例提至20%，4月汽车零售同比+11.3%。
- **房地产**：1–4月新房销售面积同比-7.2%（降幅较2025年的-12%收窄），仍是主要拖累。

### 4.3 Q2 GDP情景

| 情景 | 概率 | 出口拉动 | 内需拉动 | Q2 GDP同比 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 乐观 | 20% | +0.8pct | +4.4pct | **5.2%** |
| 基准 | 55% | +0.6pct | +4.3pct | **4.9%** |
| 悲观 | 25% | +0.3pct | +4.4pct | **4.7%** |

**结论**：保5%是大概率但非确定事件，需要"出口不大幅恶化 + 财政切实落地"双轮共振。

---

## 五、关键观察点（Watch List）

- **5月中旬海关数据**：4月出口同比读数（市场一致预期+5.8%）；如低于+4%则替代效应已边际衰减。
- **6月USTR听证会**：越南转口301调查听证进展。
- **OPEC+ 6月会议**：是否延续减产，对油价及中东需求至关重要。
- **政治局7月会议**：是否追加增量政策（额外发债/降准）信号。
- **人民币汇率**：USD/CNY若突破7.35将引发资本流出担忧，反向制约稳增长空间。

---

## 六、给首席经济学家的建议（行动项）

1. **晨报中应明确表述**："出口替代是缓冲器而非发动机"——避免市场对外需过度乐观。
2. **Q2 GDP预测建议给出区间**4.8%–5.1%，中枢4.95%，而非给出单一5.0%。
3. **关注A股映射**：东盟基建链/中东能源转型受益标的（光伏、新能源车、家电出海）仍有结构机会，但应警惕**Q3起转口受查的回调风险**。
4. **下一步研究衔接**：建议补做（a）越南/墨西哥转口"中国成分"暴露上市公司清单，(b) 财政发力节奏与基建投资高频指标对照。

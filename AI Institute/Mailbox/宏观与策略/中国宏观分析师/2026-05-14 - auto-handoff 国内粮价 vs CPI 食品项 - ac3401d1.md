In May, the continuing seasonal decline in vegetables and a wider pork price decline should keep food inflation weak; higher soybean meal and hog feed costs are more likely to squeeze margins than immediately lift retail meat prices.
2. **By contribution, grain is broadly neutral, livestock meat remains the main drag, and oils/fats are negligible.** Estimated contributions to headline CPI y/y: grain about 0.00 percentage points, livestock meat about -0.31 percentage points, and oils/fats about -0.01 percentage points. The three items together contribute about -0.32 percentage points, explaining most of the food drag.
3. **This does not change my full-year CPI center or the monetary policy window.** I keep the 2026 full-year CPI center at about 1.2% within a 1.0% to 1.4% range. Weak May food prices leave a cushion for headline CPI, but April core CPI was already +1.2% y/y and PPI +2.8% y/y, so the policy easing window remains growth-dependent and is neither pulled forward nor closed by one month of food price moves.

## Reading the Agricultural Price Signals

- **Grain:** The m/m decline of 1% to 2% in wheat and corn spot prices, together with flat mid-to-late indica rice, is more likely to lower processing and feed formulation costs than immediately move the CPI retail grain item. In the NBS late-April circulation-price data, wheat was -0.5% per ten days, corn +0.2%, and rice 0.0%, consistent with stable consumer staple prices.
- **Soybean meal and hog feed:** A 4.7% m/m increase in soybean meal raises breeding costs, but hog and pork y/y declines remain deep. In May week 1 market prices, pork was -22.6% y/y and hogs -32.8% y/y, showing that supply pressure and soft demand still dominate the cost impulse. I expect May CPI pork prices to stabilize at most on a m/m basis, not become an upward turning point for the food basket.
- **Oils/fats:** Soybean meal is not the CPI oils/fats item itself, and edible vegetable oil prices depend more directly on soybean oil, rapeseed oil, palm oil, inventories and imports. Its direct short-term contribution to oils/fats CPI should be negligible.

## Full-Year CPI and Policy Implications

I keep the full-year CPI center at about **1.2%**, with a reasonable range of **1.0% to 1.4%**. Weak May food prices will keep suppressing headline CPI, but non-food and core components matter more for the full-year slope: April core CPI was +1.2% y/y and PPI +2.8% y/y, so the ongoing price repair is mainly from energy, industrial goods and services, not food.

For monetary policy, the food drag means **inflation will not constrain growth-supportive easing**, but it is also not a standalone trigger for easing. If May-June credit, property sales or employment weaken, there is still room for an RRR cut, structural relending, or a small policy-rate cut. If PPI and energy prices keep rising, policy will likely lean more on targeted tools and fiscal coordination rather than large broad-based easing.

3. Whether higher soybean meal also lifts finishing-hog compound feed prices; if feed rises for two straight weeks but hog prices do not, pass-through remains stuck at the margin level.
4. Flour prices after the June new-wheat harvest; if wheat keeps weakening, grain CPI will still struggle to make a positive contribution.

## 对农业价格线索的解释

- **粮食：** 小麦、玉米现货环比 -1% 至 -2% 和中晚籼稻持平，更可能压低加工端成本和饲料配方成本，对 CPI 粮食零售项的即时影响很小。4 月下旬国家统计局流通领域口径中，小麦旬环比 -0.5%、玉米 +0.2%、稻米 0.0%，与“主粮终端稳定”一致。
- **豆粕和猪料：** 豆粕环比 +4.7% 会抬升养殖成本，但当前生猪和猪肉同比跌幅仍深。5 月第 1 周集贸猪肉同比 -22.6%、生猪同比 -32.8%，说明供给压力和需求偏弱仍压过成本推动。预计 5 月 CPI 猪肉环比至多小幅企稳，不会形成食品项上行拐点。
- **油脂：** 豆粕不是 CPI 油脂项本身，且食用植物油更多受大豆油、菜籽油、棕榈油和库存/进口节奏影响。短期给油脂 CPI 的直接贡献可忽略。

## 全年 CPI 与政策含义

维持全年 CPI 中枢约 **1.2%**，合理区间 **1.0% 至 1.4%**。食品项 5 月偏弱将继续压 headline，但非食品与核心项更能决定全年斜率：4 月核心 CPI 同比 1.2%，PPI 同比 2.8%，显示价格修复主要来自能源、工业品和服务，而不是食品。

货币政策方面，食品拖累意味着 **通胀不会约束稳增长宽松**，但也不提供单独触发宽松的理由。若 5 月至 6 月社融、地产销售或就业数据走弱，降准、结构性再贷款或政策利率小幅下调仍有窗口；若 PPI 和能源继续上行，政策会更偏向定向工具和财政协同，而不是大幅总量宽松。

## 后续跟踪项

1. 5 月下旬猪肉批发价能否站回 15.5 元/公斤以上；若不能，CPI 猪肉同比跌幅可能继续扩大。
2. 28 种蔬菜批发均价是否跌破 4.0 元/公斤；若跌破，食品环比可能接近区间下沿。
3. 豆粕上涨是否同步推升育肥猪配合饲料；若饲料价格连续两周上涨但猪价不涨，说明传导仍停留在利润端。
4. 6 月新麦上市后的面粉价格；若小麦继续走弱，粮食 CPI 仍难形成正贡献。

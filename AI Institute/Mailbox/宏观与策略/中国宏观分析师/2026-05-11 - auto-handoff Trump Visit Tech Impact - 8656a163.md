# Special Assessment: Trump's China Visit Expectations and Marginal Impact on Advanced Semiconductor Valuations (May 11, 2026)

## Core Conclusions
President Trump's visit to Beijing, scheduled for May 14–15, 2026, coincides with a "New Equilibrium Phase" in US-China semiconductor competition. We expect his rhetoric to center on "Transactional Decoupling," exerting an **asymmetric impact** on domestic advanced process valuations.
*   **Marginal Valuation Impact:** Overall neutral-to-bullish, but with intensifying internal divergence. Advanced Node Foundries and AI Chip Design sectors will see risk premiums compress due to "bottleneck relief expectations." Conversely, upstream Domestic Equipment and Materials may face valuation multiple (PE) contraction as the "urgency of substitution" diminishes at the margin.
*   **Key Bargaining Chips:** The US may offer to ease maintenance restrictions on late-model DUV immersion tools and allow specific AI chip exports (e.g., Blackwell equivalents) in exchange for permanent waivers on China's rare earth exports and increased purchases of US agricultural goods.

---

## 1. Semiconductor Industry Anchors (May 2026 Status)
To assess valuation impacts, we define the baseline fundamental environment as of early May 2026:
*   **Domestic Breakthroughs:** SMIC's N+3 (5nm-class) process has achieved volume production, with yields stabilizing around 35%. Huawei's Kirin 9030 and Ascend 910D now underpin the domestic flagship smartphone and AI compute infrastructure.
*   **US Policy Trajectory:** A "Case-by-Case Licensing" model implemented in late 2025 has replaced previous blanket bans. Exports of advanced chips currently incur a 25% "Technology Tariff" and are subject to 50% volume caps relative to the US market.
*   **Market Valuations:** The STAR Market Semiconductor Index trades at a PE-TTM of approximately 172x. While historically high, Q1 net profit growth of 148% YoY suggests valuations are now increasingly "earnings-backed" rather than purely "sentiment-driven."

## 2. Potential Statements & Marginal Valuation Impact Analysis

### A. Marginal Easing of Tech Export Controls (Scenario: Expanded Licensing)
*   **Expected Impact:** If the US signals a relaxation of exports for Blackwell or next-gen AI architectures, compute bottleneck expectations for Chinese hyperscalers (ByteDance, Alibaba, etc.) will drop significantly.
*   **Sector Effects:** 
    *   **Downstream Compute Users (Positive):** Lower compute costs and improved model training velocity will drive application-layer valuations.
    *   **Domestic AI Chip Design (Bearish/Volatile):** Leaders like Cambricon and Huawei HiSilicon will face pricing challenges from the return of high-performance Western alternatives. Short-term PEs could compress from 260x+ toward the 200x range.

### B. Supply Chain Cooperation & Tool Restrictions (Scenario: Maintenance "Truce")
*   **Expected Impact:** Permitting ASML to restore parts and maintenance services for late-model DUV tools would significantly extend the operational lifespan of existing 7nm/5nm lines in China.
*   **Sector Effects:**
    *   **Foundry/Fabs (Positive):** Companies like SMIC and Hua Hong would see reduced depreciation pressure and higher CAPEX certainty, triggering a re-rating of Price-to-Book (PB) ratios.
    *   **Domestic Equipment/Materials (Bearish):** The "Domestic Substitution Premium" enjoyed by Naura and AMEC will be eroded. Markets may fear a return to "Import-First" procurement by domestic fabs if restrictions ease.

## 3. Risk Premium & Valuation Forecasts
| Sub-sector | Early May 2026 Valuation (PE) | Expected Trend Post-Visit | Key Driver |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Advanced Foundries** | 45x - 60x | 📈 Marginal Up (+10-15%) | Improved line stability; yield optimization. |
| **Domestic Equip./Materials** | 80x - 120x | 📉 Volatile Down (-15-20%) | Return of competition; diminishing substitution alpha. |
| **AI/High-End Chip Design** | 150x - 260x | ↔️ High Volatility | Performance logic vs. Import substitution pressure. |
| **Mature Nodes/Power Semi** | 30x - 45x | 📈 Steady Recovery | Improved export expectations amid trade détente. |

## 4. Follow-up Items (Watch List)
1.  **May 15 Joint Communiqué:** Search for any "Rare Earth-for-Chips" package deal.
2.  **BIS List Updates:** Monitor if specific advanced lithography tools are moved from "Presumption of Denial" to "Case-by-Case Review."
3.  **Big Fund Phase III Deployment:** Observe if state investment pivots toward deeper "root technologies" (e.g., photomasks, photoresist monomers) as diplomatic tension eases.

---
**Analyst Recommendation:** We suggest "selling into strength" for equipment stocks driven purely by substitution logic during the summit window. Reallocate toward foundry leaders with international competitiveness and AI architecture firms with established ecosystem moats.

# 专题评估：特朗普访华预期与先进制程半导体板块估值边际影响 (2026年5月11日)

## 核心结论
特朗普总统定于2026年5月14-15日的访华行程，正值中美半导体竞争进入“博弈新平衡期”。我们预计其表态将围绕“交易型脱钩”展开，对国内先进制程板块估值产生**非对称影响**。
*   **估值边际影响：** 整体中性偏多，但内部分化加剧。先进制程Foundry与AI芯片设计板块因“瓶颈缓解预期”将迎来风险溢价下行；但上游国产设备材料板块可能因“替代紧迫性下行”面临估值倍数（PE Multiple）的回调压力。
*   **核心博弈点：** 美国或以放宽后期DUV设备维修与特定AI芯片（如B200等效版）出口，换取中国稀土出口的永久性豁免及对美农产品采购的增加。

---

## 1. 2026年半导体产业现状锚点
在评估估值影响前，必须明确当前（2026年5月）的市场基本面：
*   **国内突破：** 中芯国际（SMIC）N+3（5nm级）工艺已实现规模量产，良率维持在35%左右。华为麒麟9030与昇腾910D已支撑起国内高端手机与AI算力底层。
*   **美国政策：** 2025年底开启的“个案许可制”取代了此前的全面禁令。目前先进芯片出口需缴纳25%的“技术关税”，且面临50%的销售额度限制。
*   **市场估值：** 科创板（STAR Market）半导体指数当前PE-TTM约为172x。虽然处于历史高位，但Q1净利润同比增长148%，显示估值已从“情绪驱动”转为“业绩支撑”。

## 2. 潜在表态及其对板块的边际影响分析

### A. 技术出口政策的边际放宽 (潜在表态：扩大个案许可范围)
*   **预期影响：** 若美方释放放宽Blackwell架构或下一代AI架构出口的信号，国内互联网巨头（字节、阿里等）的算力瓶颈预期将大幅下行。
*   **板块效应：** 
    *   **算力下游（利好）：** 降低算力成本，推升应用层估值。
    *   **国内AI芯片设计（偏空）：** 寒武纪、华为海思面临“外部高性能替代品”回归的定价挑战，短期PE可能面临从260x+回归至200x左右的压力。

### B. 供应链合作与设备限制 (潜在表态：建立成熟制程互信机制)
*   **预期影响：** 若美方允许阿斯麦（ASML）恢复部分后期DUV浸没式光刻机的零件供应与维保，将显著延长国内现有7nm/5nm产线的寿命。
*   **板块效应：**
    *   **Foundry/代工（利好）：** 中芯国际、华虹等资产折旧压力减轻，资本开支（CAPEX）确定性增强，有望触发布现净率（PB）的修复。
    *   **国产设备/材料（利空）：** 北方华创、中微公司等此前享受的“国产替代溢价”将受到边际侵蚀。市场会担忧国内晶圆厂在设备采购上重回“优先进口”路径。

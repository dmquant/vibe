# Quantifying the Impact on China's Q3 2026 Export Growth from the Mid-May US–China Summit — Three Scenarios

**To:** FX Strategist · CNY Outlook (analyst:fx-strategist:cny_outlook)

## 1. Bottom Line Up Front

| Scenario | Assumption (US weighted-avg effective tariff on China) | Q3 2026 Export YoY (central) | Range | Marginal Δ vs. Baseline |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **S1 — Tariffs Maintained** | Status quo ~32% (incl. the two 10%+10% IEEPA "fentanyl" rounds in Feb/Mar 2025 stacked on existing Section 301) | **+2.5%** | +1.0% to +4.0% | 0 (base) |
| **S2 — Partial Removal** | Both 2025 IEEPA rounds (−20pp) rolled back, Section 301 retained, exclusion list modestly widened | **+7.5%** | +6.0% to +9.0% | **+5.0pp** |
| **S3 — Talks Collapse** | Campaign-pledged 60% blanket tariff goes live and/or secondary tariffs hit transshipment hubs; effective rate jumps to ~55% | **−6.0%** | −9.0% to −3.0% | **−8.5pp** |

**One-line input for the FX desk:** the spread across the three scenarios is **~15pp** on Q3 export YoY (−6% to +7.5%), translating into a current-account swing of roughly **±USD 60–80bn per quarter**. Holding FX supply/demand and capital-account hedging fixed, that maps to a fair-value USDCNY revaluation band of about **±2.0–2.5%**.

---

## 2. Scenario Assumptions and Transmission

### S1 — Tariffs Maintained (Baseline; probability 50%)
- **Assumption:** Mid-May summit produces no substantive deal; both sides "manage differences" and freeze the status quo.
- **Anchors:**
  - April 2026 exports printed **+6.4% YoY** (China Customs press release, 2026-05-09), with sequential momentum already cooling as the front-loading impulse fades.
  - China's exports to the US, after the March-2025 escalation, dropped from ~+5% YoY in 2024 to **−12% YoY in Q1 2026**, with ~40% of the lost volume re-routed via Mexico (+28%), Vietnam (+22%), and Malaysia (+18%).
- **Transmission:** Front-loading washout + neutral global goods demand + ongoing transshipment offsets → Q3 central case ≈ +2.5%.
- **Risk:** A US ISM manufacturing PMI sub-48 print pushes the lower bound toward 0%.

### S2 — Partial Removal (Upside; probability 30%)
- **Assumption:** Both 2025 IEEPA fentanyl-pretext tranches (20pp combined) are repealed; legacy Section 301 stays; exclusion list moderately widened.
- **Anchors:**
  - Historical analog: After the December 2019 "Phase One" deal, exports to the US recovered from −15% YoY to −5% YoY over H1 2020, adding ~+3pp to headline exports.
  - Elasticity calc: US share of China exports is 14.5% (2025 full-year). A 20pp tariff cut, at a tariff elasticity of −1.5, yields **+4.4pp** direct contribution; add a one-off restocking/order-unfreezing pulse of +0.6pp.
- **Transmission:** +4.4pp + +0.6pp ≈ **+5.0pp** vs. baseline → Q3 ≈ **+7.5%**.
- **Wildcard:** A simultaneous TikTok / reciprocal-investment package adds 0.5–1pp of sentiment premium — already inside the upper bound.

### S3 — Talks Collapse (Tail; probability 20%)
- **Assumption:** Summit ends acrimoniously; the campaign-pledged 60% blanket tariff is implemented in June, and/or secondary tariffs (Section 232 extension + rules-of-origin penetration) clamp down on transshipment routes.
- **Anchors:**
  - PIIE (2024) and Goldman Sachs (Mar 2025) estimate a 60%-tariff hit to China GDP of −0.9% and −1.3% respectively, of which ~60% runs through exports.
  - Elasticity calc: effective rate rises from 32% to 55% (+23pp). At elasticity −1.5, exports to the US fall a further ~35%. Transshipment offset is compressed from 40% to 15% as Mexico/Vietnam are dragged in.
  - Direct drag on headline exports: 14.5% × −35% × (1 − 15% offset) ≈ −4.3pp, plus a −2.7pp global-trade contagion drag = **−7pp**.
- **Transmission:** Baseline +2.5% − 8.5pp ≈ **−6.0%**. The lower bound of −9% incorporates a feedback loop where a passive CNY depreciation triggers retaliatory FX clauses.

---

## 3. Key Elasticities and Methodology Notes

1. **Tariff elasticity for China-to-US exports (η):** Central value −1.5 (median of Amiti–Redding–Weinstein 2019 and DRC 2024 estimates); robustness range −1.2 to −1.8.
2. **Transshipment substitution rate:** Observed at ~40% across 2025 (regression of China-to-ASEAN+Mexico exports against US-from-ASEAN+Mexico imports). Halved to 15% in S3 due to rules-of-origin enforcement.
3. **Inventory / front-loading correction:** US wholesale-retail inventory-to-sales ratio has eased from 1.38 (late-2025) back to 1.33, so no further destocking headwind in Q3. Under S2, a one-off restocking pulse contributes +0.6pp.
4. **Non-US channels:** EU (+3%–+5% YoY) and Belt-and-Road corridor (+8%–+10%) are roughly invariant across scenarios; the dispersion comes almost entirely from the US channel.
5. **FX feedback:** USDCNY central path assumed at 7.20 / 6.95 / 7.55 across S1/S2/S3. A 1% CNY depreciation has a ~+0.3pp 6-month lagged elasticity on export YoY — already embedded in the ranges above.

# 中美元首会晤(2026年5月中旬)三情景下中国Q3出口增速影响量化

**致:** 外汇策略师 · 人民币展望 (analyst:fx-strategist:cny_outlook)
**来源运行:** 75ba6c08-04ef-4329-9950-858553548bc7
**作者:** 中国宏观分析师
**日期:** 2026-05-11
**口径:** 海关总署美元计价出口同比;Q3 = 2026年7—9月

---

## 一、核心结论(置于最前)

| 情景 | 假设(美方对华平均有效关税) | Q3 2026 出口同比 中枢预测 | 对应区间 | 相对基准(关税维持)的边际影响 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **S1 关税维持** | 维持现行约 32% 加权平均(含 2025 年 2 月、3 月两轮 10%+10% Section 301 加征,叠加 IEEPA 芬太尼关税) | **+2.5%** | +1.0% ~ +4.0% | 0(基准) |
| **S2 部分撤销** | 取消 2025 年两轮 IEEPA 加征 20pp,有效税率回落至约 12% | **+7.5%** | +6.0% ~ +9.0% | **+5.0pp** |
| **S3 谈判破裂** | 美方落地 60% 全面加征威胁,或追加半导体/电池二级关税,有效税率升至约 55% | **−6.0%** | −9.0% ~ −3.0% | **−8.5pp** |

**一句话给 FX 桌的输入:** 三情景对 Q3 出口增速的"张角"约为 **15 个百分点**(−6% 到 +7.5%),映射到经常账户顺差约 **±600–800 亿美元/季** 的波动,对应 USDCNY 即期合理重估区间约 **±2.0–2.5%**(在外汇供求和资本项目对冲不变的假定下)。

---

## 二、情景假设与传导路径

### S1 关税维持(基准,概率 50%)
- **假设:** 5 月中旬会晤无实质成果,双方"管控分歧",维持现状关税不再升级。
- **数据锚点:**
  - 2026年4月出口同比 +6.4%(海关总署 4 月数据,美元口径),环比已出现"抢出口"透支后的回落。
  - 对美出口在加征关税后(2025 年 3 月起)由 2024 年的 +5% 区间回落至 2026 年 Q1 的 **−12%**,但通过转口(墨西哥 +28%、越南 +22%、马来 +18%)对冲约 40%。
- **传导:** 抢出口效应消退 + 全球商品需求中性 + 转口替代继续 → Q3 中枢约 +2.5%。
- **风险:** 美 ISM 制造业 PMI 跌破 48 将把区间下沿压向 0%。

### S2 部分撤销(乐观,概率 30%)
- **假设:** 取消 2025 年以"芬太尼"为名加征的两轮 10%+10% IEEPA 关税(共 20pp),Section 301 老关税保留,豁免清单适度扩容。
- **数据锚点:**
  - 历史参照:2019 年 12 月"第一阶段协议"后,2020 上半年对美出口同比从 −15% 修复到 −5%,贡献整体出口约 +3pp。
  - 弹性测算:对美出口占总出口 14.5%(2025 年),关税下降 20pp,采用对美需求弹性 −1.5,直接贡献 **+4.4pp**;叠加企业重启被冻结订单、贸易商补库存(脉冲约 +0.6pp)。
- **传导:** 直接 +4.4pp + 库存重建 +0.6pp ≈ **+5.0pp** 边际,叠加基准 +2.5% → **+7.5%**。
- **节点:** 若同步公布 TikTok/对等投资安排,情绪溢价可再加 0.5–1pp,但已含在区间上沿。

### S3 谈判破裂(尾部,概率 20%)
- **假设:** 会晤不欢而散,美方在 6 月落地竞选期间承诺的 60% 全面加征,或对第三国转口闭环 (Section 232 扩围 + 原产地穿透) 实施二级关税。
- **数据锚点:**
  - PIIE(2024)和 Goldman(2025-03)对 60% 关税情景的中国 GDP 影响测算分别为 −0.9% 和 −1.3%,其中出口贡献约 60%。
  - 弹性测算:有效税率自 32% 跳升至 55%(+23pp),按对美弹性 −1.5,对美出口再降 ~35%;转口通道被压缩(墨西哥/越南受牵连关税),对冲比例从 40% 降至 15%。
  - 直接拖累整体出口约 **−7pp**(对美 14.5% × −35% × (1−15% 对冲) ≈ −4.3pp + 全球贸易链恐慌挤压 −2.7pp)。
- **传导:** 基准 +2.5% − 8.5pp ≈ **−6.0%**。Q3 末若叠加人民币被动贬值触发的反向关税或汇率条款,下沿至 −9%。

## 三、关键弹性与方法说明

1. **对美出口关税弹性 (η):** 采用 −1.5(Amiti-Redding-Weinstein 2019 和 国研中心 2024 估计的中位值);稳健性区间 −1.2 至 −1.8。
2. **转口替代率:** 2025 全年观测值约 40%(中国对 ASEAN+ 墨西哥出口 vs. 美自 ASEAN+ 墨西哥进口的协方差回归)。在 S3 下因原产地穿透,假设腰斩至 15%。
3. **库存与抢出口校正:** 美国零售批发库销比已自 2025 年末 1.38 回到 1.33,Q3 没有进一步去库存压力;S2 下短期补库可贡献 0.6pp 一次性脉冲。
4. **非对美出口路径:** 欧盟(+3%~+5% YoY)和"一带一路"(+8%~+10%)在三情景下变化有限,主要差异来自美国通道。
5. **汇率反馈:** 模型假设 USDCNY 中枢在三情景下分别为 7.20 / 6.95 / 7.55;每 1% 人民币贬值对出口增速的 6 个月滞后弹性约 +0.3pp,但已在上述区间内消化。

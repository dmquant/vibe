**Verification Result: Satellite signals are highly consistent with official high-frequency power data.**
Despite negative macro sentiment fueled by geopolitical tensions (aftermath of the Iran conflict) and soft domestic consumption, **power demand in the high-tech manufacturing sector—specifically EV, lithium-ion battery, energy storage, and AI computing clusters—remains in a state of "supercharged" growth.** This "divergence" reflects a structural decoupling within China's "K-shaped" recovery, where new growth drivers have replaced traditional industrial engines.

**Analysis:** The seasonal dip in total national power consumption in early May is primarily due to holiday shutdowns in traditional heavy industries. However, **high-tech manufacturing (New Three + AI) maintains high production continuity, creating a sharp divergence from the broader index.**

---

## 2. Cluster Insights: Mapping Satellite Signals to Physical Reality
The "power consumption spikes" detected via satellite are concentrated in three key clusters, strongly validated by official data:
1. **Changzhou / Ningde / Yibin (Battery Clusters):** Energy Storage System (ESS) shipments surged **139%** in Q1. To meet European export demand and energy security requirements post-conflict, cell production lines in these regions remained fully operational in early May.
2. **Computing Hubs (Guian / Wuhu):** Following the May 8th AI-Energy Synergy Policy, monitored data center power usage spiked by over **50% YoY**. Their unique 24/7 high-load profile is particularly conspicuous in satellite infrared signatures.
3. **Electric Heavy Truck Hubs:** Driven by high oil prices, sales of new-energy heavy trucks rose **45%**. Since a single electric truck consumes 7-10x more power than a passenger car, charging loads at major logistics hubs have skyrocketed, explaining the localized satellite anomalies.

---

## 3. Roots of Negative Macro Sentiment (Why the Divergence?)
The current market gloom is not driven by the production side but rather by:
*   **Geopolitical Uncertainty:** The regional conflict that erupted in late February 2026 has disrupted global supply chain expectations.
*   **Trade Tensions:** The high-stakes summit in mid-May creates pressure on export outlooks, with markets fearing tariff retaliations against "China Shock 2.0" (overcapacity concerns).
*   **Consumption Lag:** While property shows "green shoots," consumer confidence continues to lag behind industrial recovery.

## 4. Follow-up Items
1. **Around May 15:** Monitor the National Bureau of Statistics (NBS) release for April Industrial Added Value, specifically "Electrical Machinery" and "Electronic Equipment" sub-sectors.
2. **Mid-May:** Watch for the official tone on "overcapacity" at the summit, as this will dictate production strategies for the second half of the year.

---
**Conclusion:** We maintain an optimistic outlook for the high-tech manufacturing sector. Satellite signals reflect real physical production, while macro sentiment reflects marginal concerns about the external environment. **Analysts are advised to increase the weighting of power-intensity metrics for high-tech clusters in their models.**

# Macro Check on the A-Share Shift from “Policy Expectations” to “Earnings Delivery”

Anchor date: 2026-05-11, based on local `date +%Y-%m-%d`.

## Priority Conclusions

1. **The April data partly supports a shift from policy expectations to earnings delivery, but the support is uneven.** PPI rose to **+2.8% y/y** and **+1.7% m/m**; exports rose **+9.8% y/y in RMB terms** and **+14.1% y/y in USD terms**. This is enough to support nominal revenue and earnings leverage in upstream resources, petrochemicals, chemicals, non-ferrous metals, electronics and parts of the external-demand chain. It does not validate a market-wide earnings recovery: consumer-goods PPI was still **-1.0%**, auto manufacturing prices were **-2.0%**, and non-metallic mineral products were **-5.5%**.
2. **Social financing is not yet confirmed positive evidence.** At the time of verification, the latest official PBOC financial statistics were still for **Q1/2026 and March**; the official April aggregate financing print had not been published. Therefore, April social financing should be treated as a pending datapoint. The confirmed Q1 data were not strong: aggregate financing flow was **RMB 14.83 trillion**, **RMB 354.5 billion less** than a year earlier; RMB loans to the real economy increased **RMB 8.9 trillion**, **RMB 796.0 billion less** than a year earlier; outstanding aggregate financing grew **7.9% y/y** at end-March.
3. **The right framing is not “policy expectations have turned into broad earnings delivery”; it is “selective earnings validation from price reflation and external demand.”** Current macro data support resources, AI/electronics, selected high-end manufacturing and exporters more than consumption, property-related sectors, autos, ferrous metals and building materials.
4. **If May data remain weak, the shift can be falsified within 3-7 weeks.** The soft falsification window is **late May to mid-June 2026**, when May PMI, exports, PPI and social financing arrive. The hard falsification window is **late June 2026**, after May industrial-profit data. This narrative does not need a full quarter to be tested.

## Confirmed Data and Implications

| Indicator | Confirmed reading | Implication for “earnings delivery” | Constraint |
|---|---:|---|---|
| April PPI | **+2.8% y/y**, **+1.7% m/m**; producer goods **+3.8%**, consumer goods **-1.0%** | Supports nominal revenue, inventory gains and upstream profit recovery | Price gains are concentrated in producer goods; consumer goods remain deflationary |
| April exports | RMB exports **RMB 2.48 trillion, +9.8%**; USD exports **USD 359.44 billion, +14.1%** | Supports earnings expectations for exporters and machinery/electronics chains | May include front-loading, inventory-cycle and price effects; cannot be directly extrapolated to domestic demand |
| April PMI | Manufacturing PMI **50.3**, production **51.5**, new orders **50.6**, new export orders **50.3**; composite PMI output index **50.1** | Production and orders remain in expansion, supporting a decent Q2 start | Expansion is narrow; non-manufacturing business activity was **49.4** |
| Social financing | Official April print not confirmed; confirmed Q1 aggregate financing flow **RMB 14.83 trillion**, **RMB 354.5 billion less** y/y | Cannot yet be used as April macro support; Q1 shows financing was “stable but not strong” | If April financing misses expectations and credit structure is weak, the earnings-shift thesis weakens |
| Q1 industrial profits | Industrial profits **+15.5%**; manufacturing profits **+19.1%** | There is already a base for profit recovery | Dispersion is high: electronics, non-ferrous metals and chemicals are strong; autos, building materials and ferrous metals are weak |

## Key Assessment

**PPI provides nominal-price and upstream-profit support, not confirmation of broad demand.** The NBS data show April mining prices up **+10.6% y/y** and raw-material industry prices up **+7.1%**, while processing-industry prices were only **+1.5%**. By industry, oil and gas extraction rose **+28.6%**, and non-ferrous smelting and rolling rose **+22.5%**; by contrast, auto manufacturing was **-2.0%** and non-metallic mineral products were **-5.5%**. This means A-share earnings leverage is more likely to concentrate in resources and raw materials than spread naturally to downstream consumption and property-related chains.

**Strong exports are positive, but mainly for external-demand earnings validation.** April exports rose **+9.8% y/y in RMB terms** and **+14.1% y/y in USD terms**. Machinery and electronics exports reached **RMB 5.92 trillion in January-April, +17.6%**. This directly supports electronics, industrial robots, EVs and equipment chains. But imports also rose **+20.6% in RMB terms** and **+25.3% in USD terms**, and PPI is rising, so some mid- and downstream companies may face cost pressure.

**Existing profit data support a selective earnings story.** Q1 industrial profits were **RMB 1.69604 trillion, +15.5%**. Profits in computer, communications and other electronic equipment manufacturing rose **1.2x**, non-ferrous smelting and rolling rose **1.2x**, and chemical raw materials and products rose **54.5%**. But auto manufacturing profits fell **17.7%**, non-metallic mineral products fell **42.6%**, and ferrous smelting and rolling remained loss-making. This is not broad market beta.

## Falsification Window and Triggers

If May data remain weak, the strategy shift will be tested in two stages:

| Window | May data to watch | Falsification signals |
|---|---|---|
| **Late May to mid-June** | May PMI, exports, PPI and social financing | Manufacturing PMI new orders/new export orders fall back **below 50**; export growth slows to low single digits or turns negative; PPI m/m falls to **around 0 or negative**; outstanding aggregate financing fails to improve from around **7.9%** |
| **Late June** | May industrial profits | Profit improvement remains concentrated in resources and a few export chains, with no diffusion to autos, building materials, property-related sectors or consumer manufacturing |

In practice, if **May exports weaken, PPI m/m fades and social financing does not improve** at the same time, the market is likely to start downgrading the “earnings delivery” narrative around **mid-June**. If May industrial profits in **late June** still show dispersion rather than diffusion, the shift is effectively falsified.

## Suggested Next Steps

- Reframe the portfolio call from “broad earnings delivery” to “PPI reflation + external-demand chain + high-beta Q1 profit sectors.”
- Do not treat April social financing as confirmed support yet; after the PBOC releases April financial statistics, recheck aggregate financing flow, RMB loans, M1 and corporate medium-to-long-term loans.
- Focus monitoring on electronics, non-ferrous metals, chemicals, oil and petrochemicals, and machinery/electronics exporters; keep a higher verification bar for autos, ferrous metals, building materials, property-related chains and low-end consumer manufacturing.

# A股从“政策预期”切到“业绩兑现”的宏观验证

锚定日期：2026-05-11（以本机 `date +%Y-%m-%d` 为准）。

## 优先结论

1. **4 月数据“部分支持”从政策预期转向业绩兑现，但支撑并不均衡。**PPI 同比升至 **+2.8%**、环比 **+1.7%**，出口按人民币计 **+9.8%**、按美元计 **+14.1%**，足以支持上游资源、石化化工、有色、电子和部分外需链的名义收入与利润弹性；但生活资料 PPI 仍为 **-1.0%**，汽车制造业出厂价 **-2.0%**，非金属矿物制品业 **-5.5%**，说明内需与地产链尚不能支撑全市场“业绩兑现”。
2. **社融不是已经确认的正面证据。**截至核验时，中国人民银行官网最新金融统计仍停留在 **2026 年一季度/3 月**；4 月社融官方值未见发布。因此，对“4 月社融”的使用只能作为待确认项。已确认的一季度数据并不强：一季度社融增量 **14.83 万亿元**，同比少增 **3545 亿元**；对实体经济发放的人民币贷款增加 **8.9 万亿元**，同比少增 **7960 亿元**；3 月末社融存量同比 **7.9%**。
3. **结论应从“政策预期切换为全市场业绩兑现”下修为“价格再通胀和外需链带来的结构性业绩验证”。**当前宏观数据更支持资源品、AI/电子、部分高端制造和出口链，而不是消费、地产后周期、汽车、黑色建材等广谱复苏。
4. **若 5 月数据延续疲弱，这一切换会在 3—7 周内被证伪。**软证伪窗口是 **2026 年 5 月底至 6 月中旬**（5 月 PMI、出口、PPI、社融陆续落地）；硬证伪窗口是 **2026 年 6 月下旬**（5 月工业企业利润发布后）。这不是需要等一个季度才确认的叙事。

## 关键判断

**PPI 提供的是“名义价格”和上游利润支撑，不是广谱需求确认。**国家统计局数据显示，4 月 PPI 中采掘工业价格同比 **+10.6%**、原材料工业 **+7.1%**，但加工工业只有 **+1.5%**；主要行业中，石油和天然气开采业 **+28.6%**、有色金属冶炼和压延加工业 **+22.5%**，而汽车制造业 **-2.0%**、非金属矿物制品业 **-5.5%**。这意味着 A 股盈利弹性会更多集中在资源与原材料链，不能自然扩散到下游消费和地产链。

**出口强是正面，但更像外需链的业绩验证。**4 月单月出口按人民币计 **+9.8%**、按美元计 **+14.1%**，前 4 个月机电产品出口 **5.92 万亿元，+17.6%**。这对电子、工业机器人、电动车、设备链有直接支持。但进口按人民币计 **+20.6%**、美元计 **+25.3%**，叠加 PPI 上行，也提示部分中下游企业会面对成本压力。

**已有利润数据支持“结构性兑现”。**一季度规上工业利润总额 **16960.4 亿元，+15.5%**，其中计算机、通信和其他电子设备制造业利润同比增长 **1.2 倍**，有色金属冶炼和压延加工业增长 **1.2 倍**，化学原料和化学制品制造业增长 **54.5%**。但汽车制造业利润 **-17.7%**，非金属矿物制品业 **-42.6%**，黑色金属冶炼和压延加工业仍由盈转亏，说明“业绩兑现”不是全市场贝塔。

## 证伪窗口与触发条件

若 5 月数据延续疲弱，策略切换的证伪会分两步完成：

| 时间窗口 | 需要观察的 5 月数据 | 证伪信号 |
|---|---|---|
| **5 月底至 6 月中旬** | 5 月 PMI、出口、PPI、社融 | 制造业 PMI 新订单/新出口订单重回 **50 以下**；出口同比回落到低个位数或转负；PPI 环比回落至 **0 附近或转负**；社融存量增速未能从 **7.9%** 附近改善 |
| **6 月下旬** | 5 月工业企业利润 | 利润改善仍只集中在资源和少数出口链，汽车、建材、地产链、消费制造没有扩散 |

实操上，如果 **5 月出口走弱 + PPI 环比熄火 + 社融不改善** 同时出现，市场会在 **6 月中旬前后** 先行下修“业绩兑现”叙事；若 5 月工业利润在 **6 月下旬** 仍显示分化而非扩散，则切换基本被证伪。

## 对请求方的下一步建议

- 将组合切换从“全市场业绩兑现”改写为“PPI 再通胀 + 外需链 + 一季度利润高弹性行业”的结构性判断。
- 暂不把 4 月社融列为已确认支撑；等人民银行 4 月金融统计发布后，再用新增社融、人民币贷款、M1、企业中长期贷款四项复核。
- 重点跟踪电子、有色、化工、石油石化、机电出口链；对汽车、黑色、建材、地产链和低端消费制造保持验证门槛。

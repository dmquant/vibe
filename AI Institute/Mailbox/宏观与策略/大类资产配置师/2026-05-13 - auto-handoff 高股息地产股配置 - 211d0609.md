# Can SOE Property Developers Be Treated as “Bond-Like” Assets in a Liquidity-Defense Model?

Date anchor: 2026-05-13.

## Prioritized Conclusions

1. They can be included in a liquidity-defense model, but only as “dividend equity / credit-substitute yield enhancement,” not as substitutes for government bonds, policy-bank bonds, or high-grade credit. They should still consume equity risk capital in portfolio accounting.
2. The best candidates for the core watchlist are central-SOE leaders with stronger cash flow, funding cost advantages, and dividend coverage, such as China Resources Land (1109.HK) and China Overseas Land & Investment (0688.HK). Yuexiu Property (0123.HK) is better treated as a “credit-repair watch” rather than a current dividend anchor, because its 2025 attributable profit and core profit fell sharply and it declared no final dividend.
3. Portfolio recommendation: within a liquidity-defense framework, cap common-equity exposure at 2%-4% of total portfolio assets, or 10%-15% of the defensive equity sleeve. If the objective is truly bond-like cash flow, prioritize the same issuers’ investment-grade bonds, REIT/REIT-like assets, or policy-supported operating cash-flow assets. Use common shares only as satellite yield enhancement.
4. The key risk is that “dividend is not coupon.” Dividends are discretionary, and share prices are exposed to property sales, policy expectations, Hong Kong equity liquidity, and valuation discounts. In a liquidity shock, these stocks do not provide the duration convexity of sovereign or high-grade bonds.

## Why the “Partly Bond-Like” Argument Works

First, leading state-owned developers still access low-cost funding. In 2025, China Resources Land raised RMB28.9bn in the domestic public market at coupon rates of 1.74%-2.20%; it also issued RMB4.3bn offshore senior notes at 2.40% and USD0.3bn senior notes at 4.125%. China Overseas Land & Investment issued several onshore bonds in 2025 with coupons broadly between 1.60% and 2.50%, while disclosing an average borrowing cost of 2.8%. These terms look closer to high-grade credit than to distressed private-sector developers.[1][2]

Second, cash flow and asset mix have defensive features. China Resources Land’s recurring core net profit already accounts for 51.8% of core net profit. China Overseas Land & Investment generated RMB7.2bn of commercial-property revenue in 2025 and listed a commercial public REIT platform, completing an asset cycle of “invest, finance, construct, manage, and exit.” Yuexiu Property remained in the “green” category under the three-red-lines framework and reported a cash-to-short-term-debt ratio of 1.7x.[1][2][3]

Third, dividends are visible, but they should not be mechanically treated as coupons. China Resources Land and China Overseas Land & Investment declared 2025 dividends of RMB1.166/share and HK$0.50/share, respectively, with profit coverage. Yuexiu Property paid a 2025 full-year dividend of HK$0.166/share, but its core net profit fell 83.5% and the payout ratio reached about 231%, showing that a high dividend does not automatically mean a sustainable dividend.[1][2][3]

## Why They Cannot Be Treated Directly as Bonds

The underlying industry is still contracting. The National Bureau of Statistics reported that China’s 2025 real estate development investment was RMB8,278.8bn, down 17.2% YoY; floor space of newly built commercial buildings sold fell 8.7%, sales value fell 12.6%, and funds for real estate development enterprises fell 13.4%. This means property-stock dividend safety still depends on sales collection, gross margins, and inventory clearance rather than fixed contractual coupons.[4]

Bond yields define the opportunity cost. On 2026-05-13, China’s 10-year government bond yield was about 1.75%, while Hong Kong’s 10-year government bond yield was about 3.11%. If SOE developer common shares offer only a modest premium over the HKD risk-free rate while carrying meaningful equity volatility and earnings-downgrade risk, they should not be classified as bond substitutes. They become allocatable only when dividend yield exceeds same-currency high-grade bonds or 10-year government bonds by at least 150-250bp and dividends are covered by core profit and operating cash flow.[5][6]

## Suggested Allocation Framework

| Allocation layer | Suitable assets | Role | Risk treatment |
|---|---|---|---|
| Core liquidity defense | Government bonds, policy-bank bonds, high-grade short-duration credit, money-market instruments | Cash management, duration, and drawdown control | Treat under bond risk budget |
| Defensive yield enhancement | Common shares of higher-rated SOE developers such as China Resources Land and China Overseas Land & Investment | Dividend enhancement and policy-repair beta | Treat under equity risk budget; haircut yield by 40%-60% in stress testing |
| Watch / tactical | Local-SOE developers with pressured profit but still-accessible financing, such as Yuexiu Property | Credit-repair trade, not a stable dividend anchor | Lower sizing; require confirmation from quarterly sales and profit recovery |

Recommended screening thresholds:

- Net gearing no higher than 55%, and cash-to-short-term-debt ratio no lower than 1.5x.
- Average funding cost no higher than 3.2%, with evidence of public-market debt issuance of three years or longer in the latest year.
- Dividends covered by core net profit, with payout ratio preferably below 70%; companies with payout ratios above 100% should not enter the “bond-like” basket.
- Higher recurring income or operating-property/service profit as a share of core profit should imply lower risk weight; heavier reliance on development sales profit should imply higher risk weight.
- Dividend yield should provide at least 150-250bp of compensation over same-currency 10-year government bonds or high-grade credit.

## Next Steps for the Real Estate Analyst

1. Split “bond-like SOE property” into two layers: true debt / REIT / operating-asset cash flow on one side, and common-share dividend enhancement on the other. Do not mix them in the same bond risk bucket.
2. Prioritize review of China Resources Land and China Overseas Land & Investment’s 2026 monthly sales, short-term maturities, dividend policy, and operating-property revenue. If sales remain weak but the recurring-profit mix improves, keep only a small allocation.
3. Set upgrade conditions for Yuexiu Property: core net profit returns to YoY growth, payout ratio falls below 70%, and cash-to-short-term-debt ratio remains above 1.5x before it moves from watchlist to the defensive yield-enhancement basket.
4. Stress-test with a further 10% decline in property sales value, a 2ppt decline in development gross margin, and a 15% compression in Hong Kong equity valuation multiples. Increase sizing only if dividends remain covered by core profit under that scenario.

# 地产国央企能否作为“类债”纳入流动性防御模型

日期锚点：2026-05-13。

## 优先结论

1. 可以纳入“流动性防御”模型，但只能作为“股息型权益/信用替代增强”，不应作为国债、政策性金融债或高等级信用债的替代品。建议在组合核算中仍按权益风险资本占用处理。
2. 最适合进入核心观察池的是现金流、融资成本和分红覆盖都更稳的中央企业龙头，例如华润置地（1109.HK）和中国海外发展（0688.HK）；越秀地产（0123.HK）更适合作为“信用修复观察”而非当前分红锚，因为其 2025 年归母利润和核心利润下滑明显，且未宣派末期股息。
3. 组合建议：在“流动性防御”框架下，普通股仓位上限先设为总组合 2%-4%，或防御权益子篮子的 10%-15%；若要追求真正“债性”，优先看同一发行人的投资级债券、REIT/类REIT资产或政策支持现金流资产，普通股只做卫星增强。
4. 核心风险是“股息不等于票息”。股息可裁量，股价受地产销售、政策预期、港股流动性和估值折现影响；在流动性冲击中，它不能提供主权债或高等级债的久期凸性。

## 为什么“部分类债”成立

第一，头部国央企仍能拿到低成本融资。华润置地 2025 年境内公开市场融资 RMB28.9bn，票息 1.74%-2.20%；境外 RMB4.3bn 高级票据票息 2.40%，USD0.3bn 高级票据票息 4.125%。中国海外发展 2025 年发行多笔境内债，票息区间大致 1.60%-2.50%，并披露平均借贷成本 2.8%。这些融资条件接近高等级信用债逻辑，而不是出险民企逻辑。[1][2]

第二，现金流和资产结构有防御属性。华润置地经常性核心净利润已占核心净利润 51.8%；中国海外发展 2025 年商业物业收入 RMB7.2bn，并完成商业公募 REIT 平台上市，形成“投、融、建、管、退”的资产循环；越秀地产则保持“三道红线”绿档，现金短债比 1.7x。[1][2][3]

第三，分红相对可见，但不能机械看作票息。华润置地和中国海外发展 2025 年股息分别为 RMB1.166/股和 HK$0.50/股，且有利润覆盖；越秀地产 2025 年虽然全年股息 HK$0.166/股，但核心净利润下滑 83.5%，且派息率达到约 231%，这说明高分红并不一定等于可持续分红。[1][2][3]

## 为什么不能直接当债

行业底层仍在收缩。国家统计局披露，2025 年全国房地产开发投资 RMB8,278.8bn，同比下降 17.2%；新建商品房销售面积下降 8.7%，销售额下降 12.6%；房企到位资金下降 13.4%。这意味着地产股的分红安全边际仍取决于销售回款、毛利率和存货去化，而不是固定合同票息。[4]

基准债券收益率提供了机会成本。2026-05-13，中国 10 年期国债收益率约 1.75%，香港 10 年期政府债收益率约 3.11%。地产国央企普通股若只提供略高于港元无风险利率的股息率，但承担明显权益波动和盈利下修风险，不能被认定为“债券替代”；只有在股息率高出同币种高等级债至少 150-250bp、且分红由核心利润和经营现金流覆盖时，才有配置价值。[5][6]

## 配置框架建议

| 配置层级 | 适用资产 | 角色 | 风险处理 |
|---|---|---|---|
| 核心流动性防御 | 国债、政策性金融债、高等级短久期信用债、货币类工具 | 现金管理、久期和回撤控制 | 按债券风险预算处理 |
| 防御收益增强 | 华润置地、中国海外发展等高评级地产国央企普通股 | 股息增强、政策修复 beta | 按权益风险预算处理，收益率打 40%-60% 压力折扣 |
| 观察/战术 | 越秀地产等利润承压但融资能力尚可的地方国企 | 信用修复交易，不作为稳定股息锚 | 仓位更低，要求季度销售和利润修复确认 |

建议的筛选阈值：

- 净负债率不高于 55%，且现金短债比不低于 1.5x。
- 平均融资成本不高于 3.2%，且最近一年仍能在公开市场发行 3 年以上债券。
- 股息由核心净利润覆盖，派息率原则上低于 70%；超过 100% 的公司不得计入“类债”篮子。
- 经常性收入或经营性物业/服务利润占核心利润比例越高，风险权重越低；开发销售利润占比越高，风险权重越高。
- 股息率需相对同币种 10 年期政府债或高等级信用债提供至少 150-250bp 风险补偿。

## 给房地产分析师的下一步

1. 把“地产国央企类债”拆成两层：一层是真债/REIT/经营性资产现金流；另一层才是普通股股息增强。两层不要混在同一个债券风险桶。
2. 优先复核华润置地和中国海外发展的 2026 年月度销售、短债到期、分红政策和经营性物业收入；若销售继续弱但经营性利润占比提升，可保留小仓位。
3. 对越秀地产设置恢复条件：核心净利润同比转正、派息率回到 70% 以下、现金短债比维持 1.5x 以上后，再从观察池升入“防御收益增强”篮子。
4. 压力测试假设：地产销售额再下降 10%、开发业务毛利率再降 2pct、港股估值倍数压缩 15%。若在该情境下股息仍可由核心利润覆盖，才允许提高仓位。

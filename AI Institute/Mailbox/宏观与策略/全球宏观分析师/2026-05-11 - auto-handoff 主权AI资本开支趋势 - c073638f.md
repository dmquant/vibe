# Middle Eastern 2026 Rubin Chip Purchase Intentions and Their Impact on Global Compute Distribution

Anchor date: 2026-05-11. This note separates public evidence into three layers: deployments that explicitly name Rubin, approved or contracted Blackwell/GB300 scale, and procurement intent that could plausibly roll into Rubin as later tranches.

## Prioritized Conclusions

1. **The UAE is the only Middle Eastern country with a publicly quantified Rubin deployment.** On 2026-03-19, Aleria announced plans to deploy **28 racks of NVIDIA DGX Vera Rubin NVL72** in the UAE, the clearest Rubin architecture order signal in the region. NVIDIA’s specification puts each Vera Rubin NVL72 rack at **72 Rubin GPUs, 36 Vera CPUs, 3,600 PFLOPS of NVFP4 inference, 20.7 TB of HBM4, and 260 TB/s of NVLink bandwidth**. Therefore, 28 racks imply **2,016 Rubin GPUs, 100.8 EFLOPS of NVFP4, 579.6 TB of HBM4, and 7,280 TB/s of NVLink bandwidth**. Sources: [Aleria](https://aleria.ai/company/newsroom/aleria-x-nvidia-x-ddn-vera-ruben-news), [NVIDIA Vera Rubin NVL72](https://www.nvidia.com/en-us/data-center/vera-rubin-nvl72/).

2. **Saudi Arabia has the larger scale ambition, but public documents still do not specify Rubin unit counts as of the anchor date.** HUMAIN and NVIDIA’s first phase is **18,000 NVIDIA GB300 Grace Blackwell** systems; the five-year Saudi plan is up to **500 MW** powered by “several hundred thousand” advanced NVIDIA GPUs. The 2025-11-19 expanded partnership adds plans to deploy up to **600,000** NVIDIA latest AI infrastructure technologies over three years, including GB300 platforms, plus a **500 MW+** Saudi facility with xAI and up to **150,000 NVIDIA GPUs** in an AWS Riyadh AI Zone. This makes Saudi Arabia the most likely country to upgrade later tranches to Rubin once 2026H2 supply is available, but the evidence should be marked as “high intent, no confirmed Rubin quantity.” Sources: [NVIDIA-HUMAIN 2025-05-13](https://investor.nvidia.com/news/press-release-details/2025/HUMAIN-and-NVIDIA-Announce-Strategic-Partnership-to-Build-AI-Factories-of-the-Future-in-Saudi-Arabia/default.aspx), [HUMAIN 2025-11-19](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/humain-expands-strategic-partnership-with-nvidia-advancing-global-ai-infrastructure-with-xai-global-ai-and-aws-at-the-us-saudi-investment-forum-302620854.html).

3. **U.S. export licensing is the binding constraint for Middle Eastern Rubin deployment, not demand alone.** On 2025-11-19, the U.S. Commerce Department authorized G42 and HUMAIN to each purchase advanced American semiconductors equivalent to up to **35,000 NVIDIA Blackwell GB300** chips, subject to security and reporting requirements. This is not a Rubin authorization, but it establishes a repeatable government-to-government approval template. If future Rubin licenses follow a similar scale, **35,000 GPUs would equal about 486 NVL72 racks**, moving a single buyer from symbolic deployment to regional compute-hub scale. Source: [U.S. Department of Commerce](https://www.commerce.gov/news/press-releases/2025/11/statement-uae-and-saudi-chip-exports).

4. **The global compute effect is marginal deconcentration, not near-term displacement of the U.S.** AI Data Center Index 2026 lists the Middle East at **35 AI data center facilities and 16.9 GW of known capacity**; against the database’s 12-region total of **154.3 GW**, that is roughly **11.0%**. JLL expects nearly **100 GW** of new global data center capacity from 2026 to 2030 and roughly **200 GW** of global capacity by 2030; Knight Frank projects global capacity rising from **62 GW** to more than **110 GW** by 2028. Middle Eastern 1-5 GW projects will raise the region’s share, but the U.S./North America remains the largest concentration. Sources: [AI Data Center Index](https://aidatacenterindex.com/regions/), [JLL 2026 Global Data Center Outlook](https://www.jll.com/en-us/insights/market-outlook/data-center-outlook), [Knight Frank Data Centres Global Report 2026](https://www.knightfrank.co.uk/research/article/2026/4/foreword-data-centres-global-report).

5. **Rubin pushes the Middle East from “GPU import market” toward “sovereign AI and cross-border inference exporter.”** Stargate UAE is planned as a **1 GW Abu Dhabi cluster**, with **200 MW expected to go live in 2026**, and partners including G42, Oracle, NVIDIA, Cisco, and SoftBank. If later tranches move from GB300 to Rubin, the Middle East gains higher inference throughput per unit of power and can serve latency-tolerant workloads for Europe, Africa, South Asia, and U.S. cloud operators. Source: [OpenAI Stargate UAE](https://openai.com/index/introducing-stargate-uae/).

## Compute Distribution Impact

**First, the regional share is rising.** Under the AI Data Center Index known-capacity lens, the Middle East is **16.9 GW / 154.3 GW = 11.0%**. If the UAE and Saudi Arabia execute 1 GW, 500 MW, and 500 MW+ projects across 2026-2028, the region’s weight in the global AI data center pipeline will remain well above its traditional cloud-market share.

**Second, Rubin changes “effective compute per MW.”** NVIDIA says Rubin-based products will be available from partners in **2026H2** and claims Rubin trains MoE models with **1/4** the GPUs and inference token cost at **1/10** the level of Blackwell. Even if the Middle East receives fewer physical GPUs than U.S. hyperscalers, its marketable inference capacity and long-context service capability can rise sharply. Source: [NVIDIA Rubin readiness](https://investor.nvidia.com/news/press-release-details/2026/NVIDIA-Kicks-Off-the-Next-Generation-of-AI-With-Rubin--Six-New-Chips-One-Incredible-AI-Supercomputer/default.aspx).

**Third, control remains concentrated in the U.S. technology stack.** The UAE and Saudi license path requires security, reporting, and ongoing compliance; that brings high-end Middle Eastern compute into an auditable NVIDIA, U.S. cloud, and U.S. government framework, limiting room for Chinese hardware in the top tier of AI factories and making chip export licenses a new macro-diplomatic lever.

**Fourth, bottlenecks shift from chips to power, cooling, networks, and political risk.** Knight Frank identifies power as the key growth variable for data centers, with grid-connection queues near a decade in some regions. The Middle East has energy and capital advantages, but still faces liquid-cooling execution, subsea cable connectivity, cross-border data rules, and regional security-event risk.

## 算力分布影响

**第一，区域份额上升。** 以 AI Data Center Index 的 known capacity 口径，中东 **16.9 GW / 154.3 GW = 11.0%**。UAE 与沙特若按 2026-2028 节点推进 1 GW、500 MW、500 MW+ 等项目，中东在全球 AI data center pipeline 中的权重会继续高于其传统云市场份额。

**第二，Rubin 改变的是“每 MW 有效算力”。** NVIDIA 称 Rubin-based products 将在 **2026H2** 由合作伙伴提供，并强调相对 Blackwell，Rubin 训练 MoE 模型所需 GPU 数量为 **1/4**、推理 token 成本为 **1/10**。因此即便中东获得的物理 GPU 数量低于美国 hyperscaler，其可出售的推理能力和长上下文服务能力仍会明显提高。来源：[NVIDIA Rubin readiness](https://investor.nvidia.com/news/press-release-details/2026/NVIDIA-Kicks-Off-the-Next-Generation-of-AI-With-Rubin--Six-New-Chips-One-Incredible-AI-Supercomputer/default.aspx)。

**第三，控制权更集中在美国技术栈。** UAE 与沙特的许可路径要求安全、报告与持续合规；这会把中东高端算力纳入 NVIDIA、美国云厂商和美国政府可审计框架，降低中国硬件进入最高端 AI 工厂的空间，也让芯片出口许可成为新的宏观外交筹码。

**第四，瓶颈从芯片转向电力、冷却、网络和政治风险。** Knight Frank 指出 power 已成为数据中心增长的关键变量，部分地区电网接入排队接近十年；中东有能源与资本优势，但仍受液冷工程、海缆连接、跨境数据规则和地区安全事件影响。

## 情景判断

| 情景 | 触发条件 | 2026-2027 影响 |
|---|---|---|
| 基准情景 | Aleria 28 racks 交付；HUMAIN/G42 先消化 GB300 许可，后续小批量 Rubin 获批 | 中东 Rubin 部署从 **2,016 GPUs** 起步，逐步成为美国之外的重要推理与主权 AI 节点 |
| 上行情景 | 美国把 Rubin 纳入与 GB300 类似的盟友许可框架；HUMAIN 的 **600,000** 框架部分转为 Rubin；Stargate UAE 按期扩至更多阶段 | 中东新增数百个 NVL72 rack，区域算力份额显著上升，欧洲/非洲/南亚部分推理工作负载外包至 Gulf |
| 下行情景 | Rubin 供给紧张、HBM4/液冷交付延迟，或美国收紧最高端出口许可 | 2026 年中东以 GB300/Blackwell 为主，Rubin 部署停留在示范规模，全球算力仍高度集中在美国 hyperscalers |

## 后续跟踪项

1. **逐项核对出口许可名称。** 重点区分 GB300、Vera Rubin NVL72、HGX Rubin NVL8 和“equivalent compute”口径。
2. **跟踪 Aleria UAE 28 racks 的交付时间、落地点和是否接入主权/政府工作负载。**
3. **跟踪 HUMAIN 第二批订单。** 若订单措辞从 GB300 变为 Vera Rubin、Rubin-based、NVL72、NVL8，即可把沙特判断从“高意向”上调为“确认采购”。
4. **跟踪 Stargate UAE 200 MW 2026 上线节点。** 该项目是判断 UAE 是否从芯片采购走向区域算力出口的关键。
5. **监测美国出口规则。** 任何针对 allies/partners 的 Rubin 许可模板都会改变中东采购上限和 NVIDIA 出货节奏。

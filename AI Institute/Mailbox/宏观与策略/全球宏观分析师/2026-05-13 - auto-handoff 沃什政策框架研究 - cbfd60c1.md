# Likely Balance-Sheet Reduction Path Under Kevin Warsh

Date anchor: 2026-05-14, based on local `date +%Y-%m-%d`. All references to “current” and “latest” are resolved against that date; the latest available H.4.1 data are the 2026-05-07 release for the week ended 2026-05-06.

## Priority Conclusions

1. **Warsh’s reaction function looks more “balance-sheet hawkish” than simply “rate hawkish.”** He has repeatedly treated QE and a large central-bank balance sheet as sources of inflation, fiscal dominance, and institutional mission creep. In his 2025 G30/IMF remarks, he described the Fed’s roughly $7 trillion balance sheet as almost an order of magnitude larger than when he joined the Fed and criticized the post-crisis Fed for not retracing its steps. In a 2025 Hoover interview, he linked a quieter “printing press” and a smaller balance sheet to lower inflation and room for lower interest rates.[Federal Reserve, 2010](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/speech/warsh20101108a.htm); [Hoover, 2025 G30 remarks PDF](https://www.hoover.org/sites/default/files/research/docs/Commanding%20Heights%20April%2025%202025%20DC.pdf); [Hoover, 2025 interview](https://www.hoover.org/research/inflation-choice-kevin-warsh-fixing-federal-reserve)

2. **In today’s high-inflation setting, he would most likely restart predictable, passive, phased QT rather than immediately sell bonds.** April 2026 CPI was +0.6% m/m and +3.8% y/y; core CPI was +0.4% m/m and +2.8% y/y; energy prices were +17.9% y/y. That gives Warsh a clear inflation-credibility rationale for balance-sheet tightening.[BLS, CPI April 2026](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/archives/cpi_05122026.htm)

3. **The path is constrained by reserve demand, so a simple return to the 2022 peak QT cap of $95 billion per month is unlikely.** The Fed stopped securities runoff on 2025-12-01 and now rolls over all Treasury principal while reinvesting agency principal into Treasury bills. The 2026-04-29 implementation note also authorizes purchases of Treasury bills or Treasury securities with remaining maturities of 3 years or less to maintain ample reserves.[Federal Reserve Policy Normalization](https://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/policy-normalization.htm?mod=article_inline); [Federal Reserve Implementation Note, 2026-04-29](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20260429a1.htm)

4. **Base case: a Warsh-style path would be a multi-year sequence of “stop net bill additions → restart small runoff → scale conditionally → reduce structural reserve demand through regulatory and payments reforms.”** A March 2026 FEDS paper estimates that, within the current ample-reserves framework, a package of tools could open room for $1.2 trillion to $2.1 trillion of balance-sheet reduction, but material shrinkage would require substantial implementation and rulemaking work in advance and would take at least a year, quite possibly several, before larger reductions could begin.[Federal Reserve FEDS, 2026](https://www.federalreserve.gov/econres/feds/a-users-guide-to-reducing-the-federal-reserves-balance-sheet.htm)

## Most Likely Path

### Phase 1: 0-3 Months, Signal No Net Balance-Sheet Expansion

The most likely first move is to replace the current “purchase short Treasuries as needed to maintain ample reserves” instruction with a stricter no-net-growth framework. In practice, that means continuing to roll Treasury maturities to avoid market disruption, stopping unnecessary net Treasury bill purchases, and potentially continuing to reinvest agency principal into Treasury bills for a controlled portfolio-composition shift.

This would have a modest immediate liquidity effect, but a strong signal: Warsh would be redefining the balance sheet as an independent policy instrument, not just technical reserve management.

### Phase 2: 3-12 Months, Restart Small Runoff

If SOFR-IORB, EFFR-IORB, repo rates, SRF usage, and reserve balances remain stable, small monthly redemption caps could return:

| Asset Class | Likely Initial Pace | Comment |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Treasuries | $5 billion to $15 billion per month | Start by restoring the $5 billion cap used in 2025, then raise it only if money markets are stable. |
| Agency MBS | Natural principal paydowns, with realized runoff more plausibly around $10 billion to $20 billion per month | The historical cap can be as high as $35 billion, but high mortgage rates depress prepayments, so actual runoff often falls below the cap. |
| Total | About $15 billion to $35 billion per month | Enough to convey anti-inflation credibility without replaying 2019 repo stress or triggering a disorderly term-premium jump. |

### Phase 3: 12-36 Months, Conditional Scaling Rather Than Mechanical Acceleration

If inflation remains above target and money markets are stable, Warsh could raise total runoff to $40 billion to $60 billion per month. If term premia, MBS spreads, or repo markets become disorderly, he would likely pause the acceleration. A near-term return to the 2022 cap of $95 billion per month is unlikely because ON RRP is only about $0.3227 trillion and reserves are about $3.0515 trillion; further runoff would hit the reserve-demand floor faster than it did earlier in QT.

### Phase 4: 2027 and Beyond, Structurally Lower Reserve Demand

Material balance-sheet shrinkage requires lowering banks’ structural demand for reserves. Options include revising liquidity rules that favor reserve holdings, improving Fedwire intraday payment arrangements, tiering reserve remuneration, and using operating tools to smooth TGA and quarter-end shocks. Brookings/Duffie’s 2026 work emphasizes that reducing assets without reducing reserve demand would disrupt money markets; the Fed FEDS paper likewise places its $1.2 trillion to $2.1 trillion potential reduction estimate on a package of such tools.[Brookings/Duffie, 2026](https://www.brookings.edu/articles/the-payment-system-puts-a-floor-on-the-feds-balance-sheet/); [Federal Reserve FEDS, 2026](https://www.federalreserve.gov/econres/feds/a-users-guide-to-reducing-the-federal-reserves-balance-sheet.htm)

## Market and Macro Implications

- **Rates curve:** The front end would remain anchored by the policy rate, but QT expectations should lift term premia; the base case is a bear steepening. If Warsh simultaneously signals future short-rate cuts, the curve could shift toward bull steepening.
- **MBS and housing:** Agency MBS runoff would reduce the Fed’s implicit support for housing credit, leaving MBS spreads and mortgage rates biased higher.
- **Fiscal and Treasury market:** The private sector would need to absorb more duration. Coordination with Treasury’s issuance maturity mix becomes critical, especially with high deficits.
- **Dollar liquidity and global risk assets:** If runoff drains reserves, dollar funding conditions tighten, pressuring leveraged credit, EM external debt, and long-duration assets.
- **Policy communication:** Warsh is likely to rely less on forward guidance and more on rules, frameworks, and operating parameters. That may raise near-term volatility but strengthen the anti-inflation credibility narrative.

## 从沃什言论推导的政策函数

| 过往表态 | 可观察信念 | 对 QT 的推论 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 2010 年反对 QE2 时称，资产负债表越大，非传统政策风险越大；Fed 在 Treasury 市场越像价格制定者，风险溢价可能突然调整。 | 资产负债表不是中性工具，会扭曲期限溢价和风险资产定价。 | 偏好减少 Fed 对长期 Treasury 和 MBS 的边际定价影响。 |
| 2025 年 G30/IMF 讲话批评危机后 Fed 未能退出，称 QE 已接近成为央行权力和政策的“near permanent feature”。 | 危机工具常态化削弱独立性，并模糊财政与货币边界。 | 会把缩表包装为恢复 1951 Treasury-Fed Accord 精神和央行窄职能。 |
| 2025 年 Hoover 访谈称大资产负债表导致高于目标的通胀，主张不能“一夜之间”缩小，并需要 Treasury 与 Fed 达成安排。 | 缩表是抗通胀工具，但执行必须与财政发行管理协调。 | 反对突然抛售；支持与 Treasury 协调的渐进 runoff 和发行结构调整。 |

## 最可能路径

### 第一阶段：0-3 个月，先停止净扩表信号

最可能动作是把当前“为维持充足准备金而购买短债”的指令改为更严格的“资产负债表不净增”框架。即：继续滚续 Treasury 到期本金以避免市场冲击，但停止非必要的 Treasury bill 净购买；agency 本金仍可暂时再投资到 Treasury bills，以维持 portfolio composition 的可控调整。

这一步的市场含义较温和，但信号很强：沃什会把资产负债表重新定义为独立政策工具，而非单纯技术性准备金管理。

### 第二阶段：3-12 个月，小规模重启 runoff

若 SOFR-IORB、EFFR-IORB、repo 利率、SRF 使用量和准备金走势稳定，可能重启小额月度 redemption caps：

| 资产类别 | 可能初始节奏 | 说明 |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Treasury | 每月 50 亿至 150 亿美元 | 先恢复 2025 年曾使用的 50 亿美元上限，再按货币市场条件上调。 |
| Agency MBS | 按自然本金回收，实际每月约 100 亿至 200 亿美元区间更现实 | 虽然历史 cap 可达 350 亿美元，但高按揭利率压低提前还款，实际 runoff 往往低于 cap。 |
| 合计 | 每月约 150 亿至 350 亿美元 | 足以传递抗通胀信誉，但避免复制 2019 年 repo 紧张或触发期限溢价跳升。 |

### 第三阶段：12-36 个月，条件式扩容而非机械加速

若通胀仍高于目标且货币市场稳定，沃什可能把总 runoff 扩至每月 400 亿至 600 亿美元；若 term premium、MBS spread 或 repo 市场出现失序，则暂停扩容。大概率不会在短期内恢复 2022 年 950 亿美元/月的上限，因为当前 ON RRP 缓冲仅约 0.3227 万亿美元，准备金约 3.0515 万亿美元，继续缩表会更快触及准备金需求下限。

### 第四阶段：2027 年以后，结构性降低准备金需求

真正让资产负债表显著变小，需要降低银行对准备金的结构性需求。可选项包括调整流动性监管中对准备金的偏好、优化 Fedwire 日内支付安排、对超额准备金实行分层利率，或用操作工具平滑 TGA 和季末冲击。Brookings/Duffie 2026 年研究强调：若不降低准备金需求，单纯压缩资产端会引发货币市场扰动；Fed FEDS 论文也将 1.2 万亿至 2.1 万亿美元的潜在缩减空间建立在这类工具组合之上。[Brookings/Duffie, 2026](https://www.brookings.edu/articles/the-payment-system-puts-a-floor-on-the-feds-balance-sheet/); [Federal Reserve FEDS, 2026](https://www.federalreserve.gov/econres/feds/a-users-guide-to-reducing-the-federal-reserves-balance-sheet.htm)

## 市场与宏观含义

- **利率曲线：** 短端可能由政策利率锚定，但缩表预期会推高期限溢价，基准情形是曲线熊陡化；若沃什同时暗示未来短端可降，曲线可能转为牛陡化。
- **MBS 与住房：** Agency MBS runoff 会减少 Fed 对住房信贷的隐性支持，MBS spread 和按揭利率风险偏上。
- **财政与 Treasury 市场：** Treasury 必须吸收更多私人部门久期需求；与 Treasury 的发行期限结构协调会成为关键，尤其是在高赤字环境下。
- **美元流动性与全球风险资产：** 缩表若压低准备金，会收紧美元融资条件，对高杠杆信用、EM 外债和久期资产不利。
- **政策沟通：** 沃什很可能少用 forward guidance，改用规则、框架和执行参数沟通；这会提高短期市场波动，但有助于重建“反通胀信誉”叙事。

## 风险情景

| 情景 | 触发条件 | 可能政策反应 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 高通胀持续 | CPI/Core CPI 连续高于目标，通胀预期上行 | 更快重启 runoff，推迟降息，强调资产负债表是第二个紧缩工具。 |
| 货币市场压力 | SOFR 或 repo 利率相对 IORB 明显上行，SRF 使用增加，准备金快速下降 | 暂停扩容或临时停止 QT，同时保留抗通胀利率立场。 |
| Treasury 吸收压力 | 长端拍卖尾部扩大、期限溢价急升 | 放慢 Treasury runoff，更多依靠 MBS 自然摊还和监管改革。 |
| 经济下行明显 | 劳动力市场快速转弱，信用利差扩大 | 沃什仍可能维持“更小资产负债表”的长期目标，但短期先用政策利率或流动性工具稳定市场。 |

## 后续跟踪项

- 2026-05-14 H.4.1 更新：确认总资产、准备金、ON RRP 是否继续接近当前水平。
- 沃什正式讲话或听证书面答复：是否给出 Treasury runoff cap、MBS reinvestment、T-bill purchase 的具体参数。
- 货币市场压力指标：SOFR-IORB、EFFR-IORB、SRF 使用量、ON RRP、TGA 和季末 repo。
- Treasury Quarterly Refunding：若财政部增加 bill 占比，可缓和久期吸收压力；若增加 coupon 发行，缩表冲击更大。
- 通胀分解：若能源冲击传导至核心服务，沃什更有理由把缩表作为信誉工具；若能源回落且核心降温，缩表节奏可能更慢。

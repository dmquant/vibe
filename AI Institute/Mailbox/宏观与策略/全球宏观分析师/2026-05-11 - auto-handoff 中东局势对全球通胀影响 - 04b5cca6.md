# Impact of a Sustained Strait of Hormuz Disruption on Q3 CPI Paths

Anchor date: 2026-05-11 (shell `date +%Y-%m-%d`).

## Prioritised Conclusions

1. **If the Strait of Hormuz remains materially disrupted through Q3, the inflation effect will not be a one-month oil spike; it will keep headline inflation elevated through the quarter.** My base case is that Q3 CPI will be about **0.2-1.3 percentage points** higher than in a “traffic normalises after Q2” path for the US, euro area, UK and Canada; Japan and India face a larger upper tail of about **0.5-1.4 percentage points**; China is cushioned by administered fuel pricing and weak demand, at about **0.2-0.6 percentage points**.
2. **The fastest pass-through is via gasoline, diesel, fuel oil and jet fuel; core CPI risk is more of a late-Q3/Q4 issue.** Federal Reserve research finds that a permanent 10% oil-price increase raises headline CPI by almost **0.4 percentage points** in total, with second-round effects peaking near **0.15 percentage points**. Banque de France estimates that each EUR 10/bbl increase in Brent lifts euro-area HICP by **0.4 percentage points** through direct and indirect channels.
3. **Asia faces the largest volume exposure, Europe has greater price and gas sensitivity, and the US feels the shock most directly through retail gasoline.** The IEA says roughly **20 mb/d** of crude and oil products moved through Hormuz in 2025, about **25%** of global seaborne oil trade; nearly **15 mb/d** was crude, about **34%** of global crude trade, with China and India receiving **44%** combined and Japan and Korea also highly reliant. The IEA also says Qatari and UAE LNG moving through Hormuz represents about **19%** of global LNG trade.
4. **Policy implication for Q3: rate-cut windows move later, and inflation expectations matter more than any single headline print.** If Brent averages **$105-115/bbl** in Q3, central banks can look through part of the supply shock. If Brent moves toward **$125-150/bbl**, or if wages and services prices follow, the Fed, ECB, BoE and BoJ will be biased toward delayed easing or tighter communication.

## Transmission Chain

- **First round: retail energy prices, already visible in March and April.** BLS reported US gasoline up **21.2% m/m** in March; Eurostat’s April flash put euro-area energy inflation at **10.9% y/y**; the OECD said G7 energy inflation rose to **8.2%** in March, including **12.5%** in the US.
- **Second round: transport, aviation, chemicals and food.** Jet fuel and diesel affect airfares, freight, delivery services and parts of the service basket; natural gas and LNG shocks pass into electricity, fertiliser and energy-intensive manufactured goods.
- **Third round: wages and expectations.** The IMF’s April WEO warns that persistent supply shocks require central banks to prevent inflation expectations from becoming unanchored. Without a wage-price spiral, central banks can look through part of the headline shock, but they are unlikely to rush into easing.

1. **Volumes:** Track weekly Hormuz tanker transits, actual loadings from Iraq/Kuwait/Saudi Arabia/UAE, and utilisation of UAE Fujairah and Saudi East-West pipeline routes.

3. **Policy buffers:** Watch US SPR releases, IEA-coordinated stocks, Indian fuel taxes, Japan gasoline/electricity subsidies, and European energy subsidies or caps.
4. **Macro confirmation:** In May-July CPI releases, focus on non-energy items: airfares, transport services, processed food, chemical products, inflation expectations and wage settlements.

## 传导链条

- **第一轮：能源零售价格，已在3月和4月显现。** BLS 3月报告显示美国汽油月涨 **21.2%**；Eurostat 4月flash显示欧元区能源同比升至 **10.9%**；OECD称3月G7能源通胀升至 **8.2%**，其中美国 **12.5%**。
- **第二轮：运输、航空、化工、食品。** 航空燃油和柴油影响机票、货运、快递和部分服务；天然气和LNG冲击会通过电价、化肥和能源密集型工业品进入食品及核心商品。
- **第三轮：工资和预期。** IMF 4月WEO提醒，若供给冲击持续，央行必须防止通胀预期失锚；在不发生工资-价格螺旋时，央行更可能看穿部分headline冲击，但不会急于降息。

## 对能源分析师的后续跟踪项

1. **量：** 每周跟踪霍尔木兹油轮通行量、伊拉克/科威特/沙特/UAE实际装船量、UAE Fujairah和沙特East-West管线利用率。
2. **价：** Brent即期、3-6个月价差、柴油裂解价差、JET fuel crack、JKM与TTF；若Brent三季度均价高于 **$115/bbl**，上述CPI区间需整体上修。
3. **政策缓冲：** 美国SPR释放、IEA协调库存、印度燃油税、Japan gasoline/electricity subsidies、欧洲能源补贴或价格上限。
4. **宏观确认：** 关注5-7月CPI中的能源以外项目：航空票价、运输服务、食品加工、化工制品、通胀预期和工资协议。

# Analysis of Trump-Xi Summit Frameworks and Impact on USDCNY Medium-to-Long-Term Equilibrium

1.  **Shift from "Tariff War" to "Managed Trade":** The upcoming summit in Beijing (May 14-15, 2026) is expected to mark a transition into a phase of "Competitive Coexistence." The primary deliverable will likely be the establishment of a **"Board of Trade"** and a **"Board of Investment"** to manage bilateral economic ties via quotas and categorized lists rather than broad tariff escalations.
2.  **Downshift in USDCNY Equilibrium:** Certainty surrounding the summit has already catalyzed a rally in the Yuan. If the "Managed Trade" framework is successfully codified, the medium-to-long-term equilibrium for USDCNY is projected to shift from the 6.90–7.10 range down to **6.70–6.80**.
3.  **Geopolitical De-risking:** Facing the Iran conflict and the 2026 U.S. midterm elections, the Trump administration seeks political wins through energy price stability and large-scale export orders (e.g., Boeing, Agricultural goods). This creates a tactical window for CNY appreciation.

---

## Potential Agreement Frameworks

### 1. The "Board of Trade" Framework
This is the cornerstone of the summit negotiations. Moving away from traditional free trade or complete decoupling, this framework aims to:
*   **Define Boundaries:** Explicitly delineate "off-limits" sectors (e.g., advanced semiconductors) from "cooperation zones" (e.g., agriculture, energy, consumer goods).
*   **Purchase Guarantees:** China is expected to commit to significant increases in U.S. LNG, Boeing aircraft, and agricultural purchases to reduce the trade deficit in exchange for tariff relief.
*   **Impact:** Reduces the "uncertainty premium" embedded in the exchange rate, allowing USDCNY to align more closely with economic fundamentals.

### 2. The "Board of Investment" Framework
*   **Focus:** Managing bilateral investments in non-sensitive sectors and resolving long-standing investment disputes.
*   **Key Sectors:** Green technology, healthcare, and basic manufacturing.
*   **Impact:** Mitigates capital outflow pressures and improves the Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) account.

### 3. Geopolitical and Energy Cooperation
*   **The Iran Factor:** Amidst the ongoing Iran war, the U.S. seeks Chinese cooperation in stabilizing the Strait of Hormuz and global energy supply chains.
*   **Critical Minerals:** China may offer an easing of rare earth export controls in exchange for more flexible U.S. sanctions in specific tech sub-sectors.

## 潜在协议框架分析

### 1. “贸易委员会” (Board of Trade) 框架
这是本次峰会的核心提议。不同于以往的自由贸易或全面脱钩，该框架旨在：
*   **界定边界：** 明确“禁区”（如先进制程半导体）与“合作区”（如农业、传统能源、消费品）。
*   **采购保障：** 中国可能承诺大规模增购美国天然气、波音飞机和农产品，以抵消贸易赤字，换取关税豁免。
*   **影响：** 减少了汇率溢价中的“不确定性风险”，支持人民币汇率回归经济基本面。

### 2. “投资委员会” (Board of Investment) 框架
*   **重点：** 管理非敏感领域的双向投资，解决长期的投资争端。
*   **关键领域：** 绿色技术合作、医疗保健及基础制造业。
*   **影响：** 缓解资本流出压力，改善直接投资（FDI）账户。

### 3. 地缘政治与能源合作
*   **伊朗因素：** 鉴于目前的伊朗战事，美方可能寻求中方在霍尔木兹海峡及能源供应链稳定方面的协作。
*   **关键矿产：** 中方可能以稀土出口限制的豁免，换取美方对特定高科技领域制裁的弹性调整。

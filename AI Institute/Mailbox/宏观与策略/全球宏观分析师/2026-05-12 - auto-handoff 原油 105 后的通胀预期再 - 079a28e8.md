# Impact of Brent Above $105 on the CPI Soft-Landing Narrative and the June FOMC Dot Plot

Anchor date: `2026-05-12`. Data-status note: as checked in this run, the official BLS CPI current page and API still mark March 2026 as the latest release; the BLS calendar lists the April 2026 CPI release for 2026-05-12 at 08:30 ET. I therefore treat “this morning’s CPI” as the soft-core-inflation signal markets are trading, while separating verified official data from the live oil-price shock.

## Prioritised Conclusions

1. **Brent above $105 weakens, but does not by itself cancel, the soft-landing signal from CPI.** Oil around $105 is high enough to lift headline CPI/PCE, squeeze real income, and make it harder for the Fed to validate rate-cut pricing; but if core CPI/PCE stays near 0.2%-0.3% m/m, the Fed is still more likely to treat the first-round energy move as a relative-price/supply shock than as an immediate change in its reaction function.

2. **Persistence and second-round pass-through are what would change the June dot plot.** Reuters reported that on 2026-05-11 Brent settled at $104.21 and reached an intraday high of $105.99; the EIA April STEO already assumes a 2Q26 Brent peak of $115/b. So $105 itself is not worse than the EIA baseline. If Brent holds in the $105-$115 range into June, and gasoline prices and inflation expectations keep rising, the June SEP is more likely to lift 2026 headline PCE by about 0.2-0.4pp, lift core PCE by about 0.1-0.2pp, and shift rate dots higher.

3. **A June hold is almost fully priced; the real question is the year-end dot.** Investing.com’s Fed Rate Monitor shows that as of 2026-05-12 13:45 EST, the probability of holding the 3.50%-3.75% target range in June was 97.2%, versus only 2.8% for a cut to 3.25%-3.50%. The same page shows December 2026 odds at 70.4% for unchanged at 3.50%-3.75%, about 26.0% combined for a range above current, and only 3.6% below current.

4. **My base case: the June median dot can still remain at 3.4%, but the risk is skewed toward 3.6% (no cuts).** The March SEP median for the 2026 federal funds rate was 3.4%, with the distribution: 7 participants at 3.625% (no cuts), 7 at 3.375% (one 25bp cut), 2 at 3.125%, 2 at 2.875%, and 1 at 2.625%. If at least 3 officials previously at 3.375% move up to 3.625%, the median becomes “no cuts in 2026.” A one-day $105 break is not necessarily enough; sustained $105-$115 oil, rising gasoline, and higher inflation expectations are close to enough.

## Transmission View

- **Headline inflation: oil directly offsets part of the CPI relief.** Energy is not as large a CPI weight as core services, but gasoline strongly affects household inflation perceptions and near-term inflation expectations. The BLS March data already showed gasoline up 21.2% m/m and contributing heavily to headline acceleration; if Brent stays at $105-$115, the May-June headline CPI/PCE readings are unlikely to produce a clean soft-landing signal.

- **Core inflation: no need to mechanically mark it up in the near term, but second-round risk is rising.** The Fed’s focus remains core PCE. First-round oil effects on core are limited, but pass-through can arrive through airfares, freight, chemicals/plastics, food distribution, corporate margins, and wage bargaining. Core PCE forecasts should move materially only if PPI, import prices, airfares, and transportation services firm together.

- **Growth: this is a stagflationary shock, not a clean hawkish shock.** Higher oil lifts inflation and hurts real consumption at the same time. For the Fed, that means cuts become harder rather than hikes becoming easy; unless inflation expectations de-anchor or core PCE reaccelerates, June is more likely to be a hawkish hold than a restart of hikes.

## 传导判断

- **headline 通胀：油价会直接抵消一部分 CPI 利好。** CPI 能源权重虽不如核心服务大，但汽油价格对家庭通胀感知和短端通胀预期的影响很强。BLS March 数据已经显示 gasoline +21.2% m/m，占 headline 加速的主要部分；如果 Brent 在 $105-$115 持续，5-6 月 headline CPI/PCE 很难给出“干净”的软着陆信号。

- **核心通胀：短期不必机械上调，但二轮传导风险上升。** Fed 重点仍是 core PCE。油价对核心的第一轮影响有限，但会通过 airfare、运输、化工/塑料、食品配送、企业利润率和工资谈判传导。若后续 PPI、import prices、airfares、transport services 同步走强，core PCE 预测才会被实质性上调。

- **增长：油价冲击是类滞胀，不是单纯鹰派。** 高油价同时推高通胀、压低实际消费。对 Fed 来说，这意味着更难降息而不是更容易加息；除非通胀预期失锚或核心 PCE 再加速，否则 6 月更可能是“hawkish hold”，不是立即重启加息。

## 给 Sentiment Analyst 的交易叙事

- 可以说：**“油价突破 $105 并没有完全否定 CPI 软着陆信号，但它把叙事从 Goldilocks 拉回到 risk-management Fed。”**
- 对股债：软 CPI 对长久期资产有支撑，但高油价会抬升 term premium 和 breakevens，压制“降息交易”的持续性。
- 对美元：若油价高位伴随 FedWatch 继续压低年内降息概率，美元下行空间会受限；若油价冲击转向增长恐惧，美元可能通过避险渠道受益。
- 对 6 月 FOMC：不要把焦点放在是否降息；应关注 Powell/Warsh 口径和 SEP 是否把 2026 年 median 从 3.4% 推向 3.6%。

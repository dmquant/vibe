## 1. Bottom Line Up Front

1. **Base case: meaningful but non-terminal spillover.** If the Trump administration lands a 25% tariff on EU vehicles and parts in H2 2026, the China–EU EV "price undertaking" framework concluded in October 2024 will come under pressure on three channels: (i) an EU rebalancing of its China stance, (ii) downward pressure on the minimum import price (MIP) embedded in the undertakings, and (iii) Berlin-vs-Paris political friction over plant reallocation. **Probability the price-undertaking framework is torn up within 2026 remains low at ~15–20%; probability of a material renegotiation (MIP / quota) rises sharply to 45–55%.**
2. **Strongest channel — EU "rebalance toward China to offset the US."** A 25% US tariff jeopardises ~600k vehicles/year of EU OEM exports to the US (ACEA reports 757k in 2024). Brussels will be forced to choose between defending the US market and defending Chinese supply chains / China-market sales. **Expect a softer EU posture toward Beijing, with technical loosening of undertaking enforcement (audit frequency, MIP cross-checks, CBAM linkage).**
3. **Second channel — MIP comes under downward repricing pressure.** Implied MIP bands for BYD / Geely / SAIC undertaking-covered models sit roughly at €23,000–€32,000 (estimated from the 2024 Commission Implementing Regulation 2024/2754 envelope). If US tariffs force European OEMs to dump US-bound volume into the EU market with 5–8% price cuts, Chinese OEMs have a legitimate basis to demand an MIP downward revision — without it, their relative EU price competitiveness erodes 8–12%.
4. **Tail risk to monitor — rare-earth / battery-material counter-leverage.** China expanded its medium-and-heavy rare-earth export-control list in April 2025. Should China–EU friction over undertaking enforcement combine with a US–EU trade war, European OEMs get squeezed from both sides. **This is the highest-impact downside channel and the one most worth monitoring.**

- European OEMs to redirect US-bound volume into the EU market (≈8–12% supply boost, rough estimate)

3. **Outcome: the undertaking gets renegotiated into "lower price floor + higher quota" — the framework survives in name but is materially downgraded**

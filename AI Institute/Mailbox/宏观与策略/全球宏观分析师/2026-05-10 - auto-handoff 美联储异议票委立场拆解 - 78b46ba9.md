## Assessment

**First, the marginal signal from the four dissents is not that all four officials want hikes. It is that the majority’s one-sided easing bias is being squeezed from both sides.** Miran wants a cut, which shows that part of the Committee is still more worried about policy being too tight. Logan, Kashkari, and Hammack are worried that the statement continues to tell markets that the next move is more likely to be down, weakening inflation restraint.

**Second, the common denominator among the three hawkish dissenters is opposition to dovish forward guidance, not a demand for a rate hike on April 29.** They accepted no change in rates at the meeting. Their dispute is over the reaction function: with PCE still high, the labor market resilient, and tariff and oil-price shocks potentially lifting prices, the policy statement should not preserve a one-way easing signal.

**Third, the trigger for “no cuts, possibly hikes” is materially stricter than the trigger for “no cuts for longer.”** Based on Logan and Kashkari’s post-meeting language, at least three conditions matter: inflation data must keep surprising to the upside and broaden; tariff or energy shocks must pass through into inflation expectations and wage-price behavior rather than only causing a one-off price-level move; and the Fed must use more neutral or hawkish communication to prevent premature easing in financial conditions. Without that combination, the baseline remains an extended hold at **3.50%-3.75%**.

**Fourth, the weekly report should avoid the headline “Fed pivots to hikes.”** The more accurate formulation is: “FOMC hawks are demanding removal of the easing bias, and hikes have moved from an extreme tail risk to a conditional communication option; Miran remains a dovish dissent in the opposite direction, and the majority has not pivoted.”

## 研判

**第一，四票异议的边际信息不是“4人都要加息”，而是多数派的单向降息倾向被两边同时挤压。** Miran希望降息，说明委员会内部仍有人更担心政策过紧；Logan/Kashkari/Hammack则担心声明继续给市场“下一步只会降”的信号，削弱对通胀的约束。

**第二，三位鹰派异议者的共同点是反对 forward guidance 偏鸽，而不是在4月29日就要求加息。** 他们接受本次维持利率不变，争的是声明函数：在PCE仍高、劳动力市场仍有韧性、关税和油价冲击可能推高价格的情况下，政策声明不应只保留降息方向。

**第三，“不降反升”的触发条件比“暂不降息”更苛刻。** 根据Logan和Kashkari的会后表述，至少需要三类证据组合：一是通胀数据继续高于预期且更广泛；二是关税/能源冲击不仅是一次性价格水平冲击，而是传导到通胀预期和工资价格行为；三是Fed需要通过更中性或更鹰派的沟通来防止金融条件提前宽松。没有这些组合，基准仍应是较长时间维持 **3.50%-3.75%**。

**第四，对周报标题应避免写成“Fed转向加息”。** 更准确的是：“FOMC鹰派要求撤销降息倾向，加息从极端尾部风险上升为条件式沟通选项；Miran仍代表反向鸽派异议，多数派尚未转向。”

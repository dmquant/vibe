# Timeline for Raising the Paid Allocation Share and Liquidity Impact

Date anchor: 2026-05-14. All references to “current” and “near term” are resolved against this date.

## Priority Conclusions

1. **Base case: China’s national carbon market is unlikely to move to broad paid allocation in 2026; the most credible window is a low-ratio, sector-by-sector start in 2027, followed by gradual increases through 2028-2030.** The legal basis for a combined “free + paid” allocation approach is already in place[1]. The CPC Central Committee and State Council set 2027 as the milestone for covering major industrial emitting sectors and prioritizing absolute caps for sectors with relatively stable emissions, and 2030 as the milestone for a market built on allowance caps with combined free and paid allocation[2].
2. **Before 2027, the main agenda is still sector expansion, MRV, and trading-mechanism improvement, not large-scale auctioning.** Steel, cement, and aluminum smelting are explicitly in a 2024-2026 “launch implementation” phase: 2024 allowances are allocated equal to verified actual emissions, 2025-2026 allowances use an intensity-control allocation approach, and the “deepening and improvement” phase begins in 2027 with a mechanism for gradually and moderately tightening allowance totals[3].
3. **If the initial paid share lands at 1%-3%, the primary-market volume would be large enough to materially improve price discovery and available supply.** Using the 2024 compliance obligation of 8.194 billion tonnes as the reference[4], 1%-3% equals 81.94 million-245.82 million tonnes, or 35%-105% of 2025 full-year CEA trading volume of 234.597856 million tonnes[5]. At the 2025 CEA average price of RMB 62.36/t, implied paid-allocation revenue would be about RMB 5.11 billion-15.33 billion; at the 2026-05-12 close of RMB 82.10/t, it would be about RMB 6.73 billion-20.18 billion[6].
4. **The liquidity impact should be positive, but the first-order effect is more likely to be “primary-market issuance replacing part of secondary trading plus a better price anchor,” not a linear increase in secondary turnover.** Paid allocation can reduce hoarding, provide a transparent transaction anchor, and smooth compliance-period liquidity; but if free allocation is tightened at the same time, auction frequency is low, or reserve prices are set too high, it could raise compliance working-capital needs and amplify price volatility in the short term.

## Liquidity Transmission Channels

- **Supply side:** Low-ratio paid allocation shifts part of allowance supply from corporate balance-sheet inventory to transparent primary supply. A 1%-3% share equals 81.94 million-245.82 million tonnes, enough to cover 35%-105% of 2025 full-year secondary turnover, helping reduce hoarding and compliance-period scarcity.
- **Price discovery:** Single-direction auctions were launched in 2025, with the first three sessions trading 130,000 tonnes for RMB 9.7715 million[9]. If this evolves into fixed-frequency auctions, clearing prices become a clearer marginal carbon-price anchor and reduce the opacity created by block-agreement trading.
- **Trading rhythm:** Liquidity could move away from a Q4 compliance rush toward monthly or quarterly auctions. If 1%-3% is released evenly by month, monthly primary supply would be about 6.83 million-20.49 million tonnes; compared with April 2026 national carbon-market turnover of 9.014446 million tonnes[10], the high end would materially exceed current single-month secondary volume.
- **Working capital:** For power generators, even a 1%-3% initial paid share is meaningful. Using an 8.194 billion tonne compliance base and the RMB 62.36/t 2025 average price, a 1% paid share implies about RMB 5.11 billion in market-wide cash demand; at RMB 82.10/t, 1% implies about RMB 6.73 billion. If tariff pass-through is limited, cash flow and receivables pressure for coal-fired operators will show up before the full income-statement effect.
- **Volatility risk:** If paid allocation is introduced while total allowances are tightened, buyers may pre-purchase allowances and lift front-end prices. If paid allowances are instead released from a reserve as extra supply, the impact is more price-stabilizing. The key distinction is whether paid allowances replace free allowances and whether they come from a market-stability reserve.

## Follow-Up Items

1. **2025/2026 power-sector allocation plan:** watch for the first explicit paid share, auction reserve price, or reserve-allocation language.
2. **Sector sequencing around 2027:** identify whether power, steel, cement, or aluminum smelting enters absolute caps and paid allocation first.
3. **Auction frequency and eligible participants:** whether participation expands from covered entities to financial institutions will determine price-discovery depth and secondary-market making capacity.
4. **CCER offset ratio:** CCER cumulative turnover was 9.2194 million tonnes and RMB 650 million by end-2025[4], still small; a higher offset ceiling would reduce CEA deficit demand.
5. **Power cost pass-through:** coal-fired generators should be stress-tested at 1%, 3%, and 5% paid shares across fuel cost, spot power bidding, and long-term contract negotiations.

## 对市场流动性的机制影响

- **供给端：** 低比例有偿分配相当于把部分配额从企业账上沉淀供给改为公开一级供给。1%-3%对应8194万-2.4582亿吨，足以覆盖2025全年二级成交量的35%-105%，有利于减少配额惜售和履约期抢量。
- **价格发现：** 单向竞价已经在2025年启用，首批三场成交13万吨、977.15万元[9]；若升级为固定频率拍卖，成交价会成为更清晰的边际碳价锚，削弱大宗协议交易对透明价格的压制。
- **成交节奏：** 流动性可能从四季度履约冲刺向月度或季度拍卖分散。若按1%-3%且月度均匀释放，月均一级量约683万-2049万吨；对比2026年4月全国碳市场总成交901.4446万吨[10]，高比例端将显著超过当前单月二级成交。
- **资金占用：** 对发电企业，初期1%-3%的现金成本并不小。以81.94亿吨清缴量、62.36元/吨均价估算，1%有偿比例对应约51.1亿元全市场资金需求；若按82.10元/吨，1%对应约67.3亿元。若电价传导有限，火电运营商现金流和应收账款压力会先于利润表充分体现。
- **波动风险：** 若有偿分配与配额总量收紧同步推进，买方可能提前囤券，推高前端价格；若拍卖被设计为额外释放储备配额，则更偏向平抑价格。关键差异在于“有偿配额是否替代免费配额”以及“是否来自市场调节储备”。

## 需要继续跟踪的问题

1. **2025/2026年度发电行业配额方案**是否首次写入有偿比例、拍卖底价或储备配额。
2. **2027年前后的行业排序：** 发电、钢铁、水泥、铝冶炼中，谁先进入总量控制和有偿分配。
3. **拍卖频率和参与主体：** 是否从重点排放单位扩展至金融机构；这决定价格发现深度和二级做市能力。
4. **CCER抵销比例：** 2025年底CCER累计成交921.94万吨、6.50亿元[4]，规模仍小；若抵销上限提高，会削弱CEA缺口需求。
5. **电力成本传导：** 火电企业需要把1%、3%、5%有偿比例分别纳入燃料成本、电力现货报价和长协谈判压力测试。

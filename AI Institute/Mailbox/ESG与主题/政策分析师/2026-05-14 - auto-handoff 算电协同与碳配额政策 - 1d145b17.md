# Assessment Report: Policy Obstacles in Computing-Electricity Synergy and Impact of Paid Carbon Quotas

**Date:** May 14, 2026  
**To:** China Macro Analyst  
**Subject:** Substantive Obstacles in "Computing-Power-Electricity Synergy" and Cost Projections for Carbon Market Expansion

---

## Executive Summary

1.  **"Institutional Mismatch" as the Primary Obstacle:** While technical feasibility for computing-power scheduling has been established, **inter-departmental regulatory barriers** (Industry/Data Bureaus vs. Energy Bureau), **temporal mismatches** between electricity spot markets and computing demand, and the **inefficient transmission of green power's environmental value** are currently the most substantive hurdles.
2.  **The "Year of Paid Allocation" Begins:** 2026 marks the official launch of the pilot for paid carbon quota allocation (3%-10% ratio) in the National ETS. Although Data Centers (IDCs) are not in the first batch for 2026, they have been prioritized for national "Energy Saving and Carbon Reduction Diagnostics," with **formal inclusion in the national mandatory compliance list expected by 2027**.
3.  **Structural OpEx Increase for High-Energy IDCs:** Paid allocations combined with rising carbon prices (projected average of 90-110 CNY/tonne in 2026) will lead to a 3%-5% increase in OpEx for IDCs without 100% renewable energy coverage. **"Carbon Intensity" is becoming the defining factor in computing competitiveness.**

---

## I. Substantive Obstacles for Computing-Power-Electricity Synergy

### 1. Mechanism Obstacles: Governance and Pricing Loop Gaps
*   **Fragmented Regulation:** The computing network is led by the National Data Bureau and MIIT, while electricity dispatch is coordinated by the NDRC and NEA. A national-level joint "Computing-Power-Electricity" dispatch center is still missing, leaving compensation mechanisms for IDCs acting as "flexible loads" in regional pilots (e.g., Guizhou, Ningxia) without a unified national business model.
*   **Blocked Price Signals:** Existing electricity pricing mechanisms do not fully reflect the value of IDCs consuming green power during peak curtailment periods (wind/solar surplus). This reduces the financial incentive for companies to invest in integrated "Source-Grid-Load-Storage" systems.

### 2. Structural Obstacles: Spatio-Temporal Mismatch and Transmission Costs
*   **Spatial Mismatch:** While "East Data West Computing" is progressing, Intelligent Computing Centers in the East require extremely low latency (<10ms), making large-scale migration to the West difficult. Cross-provincial green power trading still faces local protectionism, keeping the total cost of Western green power delivered to Eastern hubs high.
*   **Temporal Mismatch:** AI Large Model training requires 24/7 rigid power supply, contrasting with the intermittency of wind and solar power. Long-duration energy storage (e.g., hydrogen, flow batteries) remains in the demonstration stage in 2026 and cannot yet cost-effectively stabilize the impact of green power fluctuations on computing reliability.

### 3. Technical Obstacles: Grid Stability Challenges
*   **Instantaneous Power Spikes:** When large-scale clusters (10k+ GPUs) perform model loading or parameter synchronization, power loads can jump by tens of megawatts within milliseconds. This poses challenges to local distribution grid voltage and frequency stability; some regions have already paused IDC expansions due to grid capacity constraints.

| **2027 (Exp.)** | Formal National ETS Inclusion | Data Centers, Petrochemicals, and Chemicals enter mandatory compliance. |
| **2030** | Absolute Cap Control | Shift from intensity-based to absolute emission caps. Paid ratio expected to exceed **20%**. |

## III. Cost Projections for High-Energy Computing Centers

### 1. Direct Carbon Costs (Based on 2027 Inclusion)

### 3. Mitigation Strategy: Green Power Premium
*   **GEC Value:** Starting in 2026, Green Electricity Certificates (GECs) are the sole compliance instrument for offsetting Scope 2 emissions. ## 核心结论

1.  **算电协同实质障碍已转向“制度性错配”：** 技术层面的算力调度已初步实现，但**跨部门监管壁垒**（工业/数据局 vs. 能源局）、**电力现货市场与算力需求的时序错配**以及**绿电环境价值传导不畅**是当前的实质性阻碍。
2.  **碳市场正式进入“有偿元年”：** 2026年全国碳市场启动3%-10%的配额有偿分配试点。算力中心（IDC）虽未在2026年首批进入全国市场，但已被列入国家节能降碳诊断重点，预计**2027年正式纳入全国强制履约名单**。
3.  **高耗能算力中心成本将结构性上浮：** 碳配额有偿分配及碳价上涨（预计2026年均价90-110元/吨）将导致未实现绿电全覆盖的算力中心OpEx上升3%-5%。**“含碳量”将成为算力竞争力的分水岭。**

---

## 一、 算电协同的实质性障碍分析

### 1. 机制性障碍：监管体制与定价闭环缺失
*   **多头管理：** 算力网络由国家数据局和工信部主导，电力调度由发改委和能源局统筹。目前缺乏全国层面的“算-电”联合调度中心，导致算力负荷作为“柔性调节资源”参与电网调峰的补偿机制尚处于地方试点（如贵州、宁夏），尚未形成全国统一的商业模式。
*   **价格信号阻断：** 现有的电价机制难以完全体现算力中心在不同时段（如弃风弃光高峰期）消纳绿电的价值，导致企业参与“源网荷储”一体化的财务动机不足。

### 2. 结构性障碍：时空错配与传输成本
*   **空间错配：** “东数西算”虽在推进，但东部智算中心对实时性要求极高（延迟<10ms），难以大规模迁移至西部。跨省绿电交易面临地方保护主义，导致“西电”送往“东算”的综合度电成本依然高昂。
*   **时序错配：** AI大模型训练需要7x24小时刚性供电，而风光绿电具有间歇性。长时储能技术（如氢能、液流电池）在2026年仍处于示范应用阶段，尚未能低成本解决绿电波动性对算力稳定性的冲击。

### 3. 技术障碍：电网稳定性挑战
*   **瞬时冲击：** 万卡级智算中心在模型加载与参数同步时，用电负荷可能在毫秒级发生数万千瓦的跳变，对局部配电网的电压调节和频率稳定构成挑战，部分地区已出现由于电网承载能力限制而暂停IDC扩建的情况。

## 三、 高耗能算力中心成本预期测算

### 1. 直接碳成本（以2027年纳管为基准）
*   **测算模型：** 以一个年耗电量5亿度（PUE=1.25，算力中心功率约60MW）的大型中心为例。
*   **排放量：** 约28万吨CO2（按全国电网平均排放因子折算）。
*   **有偿成本：** 若有偿比例5%，碳价100元/吨，直接购买配额成本约 **140万元/年**。

### 2. 间接成本传导（电价影响）
*   **电价上浮：** 电力行业碳成本通过市场化交易向下游传导。预计2026-2027年电力碳溢价将导致度电价格上涨 **0.008-0.012元**。
*   **总成本增加：** 同等规模IDC（5亿度电）年电力支出将增加 **400-600万元**。

### 3. 成本对冲方案：绿电溢价
*   **绿证（GEC）价值：** 2026年起，绿证成为抵扣Scope 2排放的唯一合规凭证。虽然购买绿证需支付每度电约0.01-0.02元的溢价，但可豁免后续可能的碳关税及国内配额购买支出。
